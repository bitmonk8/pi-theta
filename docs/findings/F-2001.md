## Finding

`writeText(path, contents): Promise<void>` is the only `FileSystem` member with no `.code` rejection note, yet the prose calls the seam the load-bearing surface for "every other normative file read or write." No concrete `writeText` consumer is enumerated in the corpus.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Either (a) give `writeText` the same Node-style `.code` rejection note its read siblings carry (`EACCES`/`EPERM`/`ENOSPC`/read-only volume; other I/O failures surface their underlying `.code` unchanged) plus a surfacing rule for how a write rejection reaches a loom-observable outcome, or (b) narrow the "or write" claim to match the absence of any current writer until a writing consumer is specified. Prefer (a) if any 1.0 write path exists; otherwise (b).

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Each unstated disposition lets two reasonable implementations diverge on an error path: a `compile()` throw, a `writeText` rejection, or an `estimate()` throw could variously crash the runtime, surface a loom-observable Err, or be silently swallowed. The `writeText` gap is the mildest while no consumer exists, but it leaves a "load-bearing write surface" with no rejection mapping; the `estimate()`-throw gap sits on a path that runs on every `bind_context: session` binder call.

## Finding

The drain-state contract uses the noun "arm" for two distinct concepts on the same page, with no disambiguating qualifier at most use sites. First, it names the members of the closed two-valued `drainStateTag` set: the `"shutting-down"` arm and the `"degraded-needs-reload"` arm (e.g. "either arm of sub-step 1's closed two-arm tag set", "uniform across the `"shutting-down"` and `"degraded-needs-reload"` arms"). Second, it names the three `readDrainState` dispatch branches the slash handler chooses between — arm (a) dispatch, arm (b) shutting-down note, arm (c) degraded-needs-reload note (PIC-29: "The three arms are mutually exclusive…"; PIC-30: "MUST NOT introduce a fourth arm"; the acceptance-criteria heading "→ arm (b)").

These two namespaces are not in one-to-one correspondence, which is what makes the overload a real hazard rather than a cosmetic clash. Dispatch arm (b) is fed by three field-tuples — `(false, "shutting-down")`, `(true, "shutting-down")`, and `(true, undefined)` — so the `(true, undefined)` tuple routes to dispatch arm (b) while matching **no** tag-set arm (its tag is `undefined`, which the spec is explicit is not a member of the two-arm tag set). A reader who collapses "the shutting-down arm" (a tag-set value) with "arm (b)" (a dispatch branch) will wrongly conclude that arm (b) requires `tag === "shutting-down"`, and a fixture author can assert on the wrong predicate. The companion finding "routes through arm (b) subject ambiguous" is a direct symptom of the same collision.

The fix is terminological, not behavioural: reserve one word per concept. PIC-29 already gives the dispatch branches stable letter labels (a)/(b)/(c) and describes them as a decision the handler routes through, so "branch" is the natural reserved word for the dispatch enumeration, leaving "arm" for the tag-set values where "the `"shutting-down"` arm" reads idiomatically. The renaming must sweep every dispatch-sense use of "arm" — PIC-29's "three arms"/"fourth arm", PIC-30's "fourth arm", the `(drained: true, tag: undefined) → arm (b)` acceptance-criteria heading, the read-failure-fallback prose ("short-circuit on arm (c)"), and the arm-(c)/arm-(b) fixture references in `session-only-degraded-state.md` — while leaving the tag-set "arm" usages untouched.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Reserve **"arm"** for the closed two-value `drainStateTag` set (the `"shutting-down"` arm and the `"degraded-needs-reload"` arm) and rename the three `readDrainState` dispatch options to **"branch (a)/(b)/(c)"** throughout the drain-state surface.

Concrete edit sites:

- `drain-state-contract.md` — PIC-29: "The three arms are mutually exclusive…" → "three branches"; the tuple-to-arm map "(a)/(b)/(c)" labels stay but are described as branches; PIC-30: "fourth arm" → "fourth branch" and "(a) dispatch, (b) shutting-down note, (c) degraded-needs-reload note" reworded as branches; the `readDrainState` Methods bullet "choose between three behaviours: (a)…(b)…(c)" → branches; the slash-dispatch prose "route through arm (a)"/"short-circuit on arm (c)" → "branch (a)"/"branch (c)"; the read-failure-fallback clause "short-circuit on arm (c)" → "branch (c)".
- The acceptance-criteria heading `## Acceptance criteria — `(drained: true, tag: undefined)` → arm (b)` → `… → branch (b)`, and its body bullets ("Arm-(b) routing", "routes through arm (b)") → branch.
- `session-only-degraded-state.md` — fixtures (j)/(l)/(m): "arm (c)"/"arm-(c)" and the arm-(b)/arm-(c) cross-references → "branch (c)"/"branch (b)".

Leave the tag-set "arm" usages (`"shutting-down"` arm, `"degraded-needs-reload"` arm, "two-arm tag set", "either arm of sub-step 1's closed two-arm tag set") unchanged — those are the reserved sense. The arm/branch distinction must read consistently in the prose that crosses both namespaces (e.g. the read-failure fallback that "short-circuits on branch (c) returning the `"degraded-needs-reload"` arm note"). Watch the anchor IDs: PIC-29/PIC-30 anchors and the `#read-failure-fallback` anchor are unaffected by the prose rename, so no inbound cross-reference breaks.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader who conflates dispatch arm (b) with the tag-set `"shutting-down"` arm mis-models the `(true, undefined)` → arm-(b) route and can write a fixture asserting the wrong predicate (`tag === "shutting-down"` instead of the full arm-(b) tuple set). PIC-29's explicit six-tuple→arm map lets a careful implementer recover the correct behaviour, so a working system is still reachable; the cost is reader friction and an elevated chance of an off-target test.

## Problem

The drain-state contract uses the noun "arm" for two distinct concepts on the same page. It names the members of the closed two-value `drainStateTag` set (the `"shutting-down"` arm and the `"degraded-needs-reload"` arm) and, separately, the three `readDrainState` dispatch options (arm (a) dispatch, arm (b) shutting-down note, arm (c) degraded-needs-reload note). The two namespaces are not one-to-one: dispatch arm (b) is fed the `(true, undefined)` tuple, whose tag is `undefined` and so matches no tag-set arm. A reader who collapses "the shutting-down arm" (a tag-set value) into "arm (b)" (a dispatch branch) mis-models the `(true, undefined)` route and can assert the wrong predicate in a fixture.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit-introduction
**Introducing commits:** `52b9101` ("pi-loom spec: resolve \"T24 — Fork-reason watcher closure leaves the extension in an unspecified, silently degraded state\"")
**History:** `git log -S` confirms neither `"degraded-needs-reload"` (the second tag-set arm) nor the three-arm `readDrainState` dispatch enumeration existed before `52b9101`; that commit introduced the degraded-state branch and coined "arm" for both the closed two-arm `drainStateTag` set and the three dispatch branches in the same edit, so the overload was present from the moment the surface entered the corpus. Later commits only propagated it without introducing a second sense: `f5e89f4` (the 100KB/30KB spec-split) relocated the content from `pi-integration-contract.md` into `drain-state-contract.md`, `3137321` coined the PIC-29..32 anchors, and `8008b91` (F-1217) added the acceptance-criteria/fixture blocks carrying the "→ arm (b)" heading.

## Solution approach

Rename every dispatch-sense use of "arm" to "branch" across the drain-state surface, leaving the tag-set-value uses of "arm" (`"shutting-down"` arm, `"degraded-needs-reload"` arm, "two-arm tag set") unchanged. The sweep covers PIC-29 and PIC-30, the `readDrainState` *Methods* enumeration, and the [`#read-failure-fallback`](PIC-31) prose in `drain-state-contract.md`; the `## Acceptance criteria — … → arm (b)` heading and its body bullets; and the arm-(b)/arm-(c) fixture references in `session-only-degraded-state.md` fixtures (j)/(l)/(m).

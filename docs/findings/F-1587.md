## Finding

In `docs/spec_topics/binder/defaulting-system-note-echo.md`, the closing paragraph of `## Echo policy` says:

> The echo channel is also used for the binder's `needs_info` and `ambiguous` outputs, which *replace* execution rather than precede it (both shaped by [System-note rendering](#system-note-rendering)):

The term **"echo channel"** is `bind_echo`-specific: the echo is the one-line success note appended *before* the loom starts, and it is suppressed by `bind_echo: false` (and auto-suppressed under the single-string bypass). The `needs_info` and `ambiguous` outputs are not echo content ‚Äî they are binder *failure* notes that fire regardless of `bind_echo:` and *replace* execution rather than precede it. Naming their delivery surface the "echo channel" wrongly implies they ride the same on/off switch and reuse the same prefix grammar.

The shard already provides the correct umbrella term: the section above this sentence is `## System-note rendering`, and the in-shard parenthetical even points at it. The same shard also distinguishes the two grammars normatively in rule 3 ‚Äî failure notes use `loom /<name>: <fixed-phrase> ‚Äî <sanitised-suffix>` while the success echo uses `Running /<name>: <formatted-args>` ‚Äî so calling failure delivery "echo" contradicts that demarcation in the same file. The fix is a one-sentence rewording to use "system note" as the delivery noun.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/spec_topics/binder/defaulting-system-note-echo.md`, replace the sentence

> The echo channel is also used for the binder's `needs_info` and `ambiguous` outputs, which *replace* execution rather than precede it (both shaped by [System-note rendering](#system-note-rendering)):

with

> System notes are also the delivery surface for the binder's `needs_info` and `ambiguous` outputs, which *replace* execution rather than precede it and are shaped by [System-note rendering](#system-note-rendering); unlike the success echo above, these notes fire regardless of the `bind_echo:` setting (which governs the success echo only):

The added "unlike the success echo above ‚Ä¶ fire regardless of the `bind_echo:` setting" half-sentence pre-empts the reader inference the original wording invites ‚Äî that `bind_echo: false` also suppresses these failure notes ‚Äî and aligns the noun with the section title and with rule 3's prefix demarcation. No other site needs to change: the failure-mode templates table in `determinism-cancellation-failure.md` already uses the `loom /<name>: ‚Ä¶` failure-note grammar verbatim, and the page-local cross-reference resolves to the same anchor.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A first-time reader can reasonably infer that disabling `bind_echo:` also suppresses the `needs_info` / `ambiguous` notes, or that those notes share the `Running /<name>:` prefix ‚Äî both incorrect. Two conforming implementers would still produce the same observable behaviour because the failure-mode templates table and System-note rendering rule 3 are the load-bearing specifications; this sentence is summary prose, not the contract.

## Finding

The Edge-cases paragraph requires teardown-handler diagnostics to use `console.error` directly; the actual requirement is a sink that does not call back into `pi.*` (stale at teardown).

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** f5e89f4 — docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set (2026-06-04, Thomas Andersen); 14424b4 — pi-loom spec: resolve "ActiveInvocationRegistry disposeBarrier resolver + stale below refs" (2026-06-06, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** `active-invocation-registry.md` was carved into its own page by the spec-set size-cap split f5e89f4 (2026-06-04); the `Set<{…}>` entry shape, the "V8 `Set` insertion-order invariant" justification, the `console.error` teardown sink, and the "or immediately (prompt mode)" `disposeBarrier` wording were all present in that first commit (`git log -S` on each token resolves to f5e89f4). The fourth facet — the setup wrap's `Promise.withResolvers()` construction step — was added later by 14424b4 (2026-06-06), which broadened the already-prescriptive "Dispatch-site setup wrap" bullet while introducing the `disposeBarrier` resolver-storage rule; the wrap itself (with its `try`/`catch` and `Set.add` mechanics) existed at inception.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Reword the constraint to require that teardown-handler diagnostics emit through a sink that does not re-enter `pi.*` (and thereby bypass the persistent-diagnostic channel), and demote `console.error` to a non-normative example of such a sink. Preserve the enumerated five-code allowlist and the "no other diagnostic code" rule unchanged.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The over-prescribed container and `console.error` sink do not change observable behaviour but bind implementers more tightly than the conformance surface requires and are inconsistent with the corpus's seam-injection convention, inviting needless friction in implementation and tests. The unpinned "immediately" prompt-mode settle timing is the only facet that could let two implementers diverge on `Promise.allSettled` ordering during teardown; the effect is bounded because the cap (`SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS`) backstops it.

## Finding

Clusters 1 and 2 are cohesive: the build-aside-publish swap *is* registration step 5, and the failure-injection seam injects directly onto that swap path. Cluster 3, by contrast, is an orthogonal load-time concern â€” model resolution â€” that shares the load pass with registration only incidentally. The leaf's binding obligations remain precise (the five `Tests.` bullets pin the behaviour), so this is not a correctness gap. It is a granularity defect: a single leaf carrying an unrelated third concern enlarges the implementation diff and the fix-loop critique surface, and couples model-resolution wiring to the registration/reload leaf.

## Consequence

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Extract the model-reference-matcher production-wiring cluster out of `V9b`/`V9b-T` into a new paired leaf `<new>`/`<new>-T`:

- Move from `V9b`'s `Adds.` the "model-reference-matcher production wiring point" sentence (construction of loom's own exact-match resolver over `ctx.modelRegistry.getAvailable()` at the load pass and its injection into the `V6a` parser seam), and move from both `V9b`'s and `V9b-T`'s `Tests.` the "model-reference-matcher production wiring" bullet (the single-source-of-construction / instance-identity observable) into the new leaf. Carry the same spec citations (`host-interfaces-core.md#model-registry-pin`, `#binder-model-matcher-inputs`).
- Give `<new>` a `Deps.` on `V9b` (the load pass it hooks into) and `V6a` (the parser seam it injects into); give `<new>-T` the matching tests-task deps.
- Retarget the references in `V11a` and `V11a-T` that currently read "the matcher instance `V9b` constructs and injects at the load pass" so they name `<new>` as the construction site.
- Leave `V9b` with registration steps 1â€“5 (`PIC-36 â€¦ PIC-39`) and the `ReloadFailureInjector` seam (registry-swap and `.loom`/`.warp` re-parse arms). Strike the model-matcher line from `V9b`'s `Ships when.` and add the corresponding gate to `<new>`'s `Ships when.`

The construction must stay at a load-pass-owning leaf. The `PIC-36 â€¦ PIC-39` coverage-matrix row stays mapped to `V9b`; the extracted cluster has no numbered REQ-ID and adds no new row.

## Problem

`V11b` / `V11b-T` ("Bind context, truncation, and transcript renderer") bundle two independently-verifiable units in a single leaf pair: the byte-exact compact-transcript renderer (`BNDR-7` renderings 7aâ€“7i, `BNDR-8`, `BNDR-9`, and the `loom/parse/bind-context-session-on-subagent` warning) and the session-context truncation walk (four `FakeTokenEstimator` boundary vectors pinning the 8000-token / 20-turn whole-turn cut). The two are different recognition problems exercised through different fixtures â€” a pure-function byte-equality contract over the `AgentMessage` union versus per-message token arithmetic that selects which turns the renderer is handed â€” bridged only at the `BNDR-7i` zero-turn seam the spec already treats as separable. To confirm `V11b` is correct a reviewer must hold the full byte-discipline of nine reference renderings and the boundary arithmetic of four token/turn vectors in one pass.

## Solution approach

Split the `V11b` / `V11b-T` pair along the renderer/truncation seam. Keep `V11b` / `V11b-T` scoped to the compact-transcript renderer (`BNDR-7`, `BNDR-8`, `BNDR-9`, and the `loom/parse/bind-context-session-on-subagent` warning), narrowing the renderer leaf's `Adds.` and `Ships when.` and striking the truncation walk and its four vectors from both Tests blocks. Add a new paired leaf in the `V11` phase owning the session-context truncation walk, naming `V8e` (the `TokenEstimator` / PIC-16 seam the walk binds, which `V11b` does not currently declare) and the retained `V11b` in its `Deps.` Link the new implementation leaf into `docs/plan.md`'s `### V11 â€” Binder` listing, leave the `BNDR-7, BNDR-8, BNDR-9` coverage-matrix row pointed at the renderer leaf, and add the new truncation leaf alongside `V11b` in `H7a`'s `Deps.` and its two permitted-code-list / derived-golden prose bullets.

## Finding

The provider-error, forced-tool, and provider seed-field re-validation obligations (`version-bump-step2.md` checklist items; `version-bump-triggers.md` step 6) re-run fixtures against "a maintained corpus" whose maintenance owner is named but whose initial-population owner and existence are assumed; a re-run against an empty corpus passes vacuously.

**Spec edits:** State corpus existence as a precondition of each re-run obligation and name the initial-population owner (the plan leaf / implementation step that first captures each corpus), and add a non-empty / minimum-coverage guard so a re-validation gate cannot go green against an empty or partial corpus (e.g. the fixture suite MUST assert at least one captured body per supported provider before reporting the re-run green). Apply consistently across the provider-error fixtures (item i), forced-tool fixtures (item u), retry/cancellation fixtures (items aa/ab), and the step-6 provider seed-field fixtures.

**Relationship:** same-cluster — resolves independently across `version-bump-step2.md` items and `version-bump-triggers.md` step 6.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

State corpus existence as a precondition of each re-run obligation and name the initial-population owner (the plan leaf / implementation step that first captures each corpus), and add a non-empty / minimum-coverage guard so a re-validation gate cannot go green against an empty or partial corpus (e.g. the fixture suite MUST assert at least one captured body per supported provider before reporting the re-run green). Apply consistently across the provider-error fixtures (item i), forced-tool fixtures (item u), retry/cancellation fixtures (items aa/ab), and the step-6 provider seed-field fixtures.

**Relationship:** same-cluster — resolves independently across `version-bump-step2.md` items and `version-bump-triggers.md` step 6.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Each precondition admits silent divergence or a vacuous pass: two implementers disagree on what an absent `engines.node` field does at the floor gate; a runtime reference under a future top-level root is silently elided by the step-5 sweep; the step-2(b) tie-break apparatus is inert under contributor self-merge; and an empty fixture corpus turns a re-validation gate green without exercising anything. None blocks the procedure from being implemented, but each lets a real regression land undetected.

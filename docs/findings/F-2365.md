## Finding

`V12b` ("Top-level `Err` formatting and chain attribution") delivers two mechanisms with disjoint data paths and disjoint dependency footprints in a single leaf pair:

- **Per-kind note-template rendering** â€” `SLSH-3` (single-line top-level `Err`, sole subagent-mode surface) and `SLSH-4` with its inline `SNK-aâ€¦k` labels (one verbatim note per `QueryError` kind, plus a catch-all total over the union). This is a closed dispatch table over the `QueryError` kind set with no external seam; it needs only `V12a` (slash dispatch) and `V4d` (the `QueryError` variant schema).
- **Recursive chain attribution** â€” `SLSH-5` appends ` from <callee> invoked at <parent>:<line>` per `invoke_callee` hop, leaf-first, rendering the per-hop provenance from `V15g`'s per-frame invocation record. This mechanism cannot begin until `V15g` lands and is the sole reason `V12b`/`V12b-T` carry a `V15g` dependency.

Because the two are bundled, the entire `V12b` leaf is unpickable until `V15g` ships, even though the template-rendering half is fully implementable and testable from `V12a`/`V4d` alone. The leaf-format rule that "each leaf inside a slice is the smallest feature that can ship and be tested independently" (`conventions.md` Â§3) is not met: the template dispatch table and the chain-attribution suffix are independently shippable features with independent failure modes.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Extract the `SLSH-5` recursive chain-attribution suffix into its own leaf pair and leave the per-kind note dispatch table in `V12b`:

- In the new `<new>`/`<new>-T` leaf pair, place the `SLSH-5` bullet (the ` from <callee> invoked at <parent>:<line>` per-hop, leaf-first suffix rendered from `V15g`'s per-frame invocation record). This leaf carries the `V15g` dependency (alongside `V12b`/`V4d` as needed for the rendered `Err` it appends to). Cite the same spec anchor `slash-invocation.md#slsh-5` and keep the V15g-producer / V12-consumer relationship intact.
- In `V12b`/`V12b-T`, keep `SLSH-3`, `SLSH-4`, and the `SNK-aâ€¦k` inline labels, and remove `V15g` from the `Deps.` field of both files (leaving `V12a`, `V4d`, and `V12b-T` on the impl leaf).
- In `coverage-matrix.md`, split the `| SLSH-3, SLSH-4, SLSH-5 | V12b |` row so `SLSH-3, SLSH-4` map to `V12b` and `SLSH-5` maps to `<new>`.
- Add the new `SLSH-5` closing leaf to `H5b`'s `Deps.` per How-to-use step 2 (it closes a coverage-matrix-mapped numbered REQ-ID), keeping the V12 range contiguous.

`V15g`'s producer seam and its `consumed by V12b` cross-reference must be retargeted to the new leaf ID once the implementer allocates it. The leaf-ID scheme is the plan's existing `V<slice><letter>` form; do not pin a final ID here â€” use the implementer-allocated ID in place of `<new>`.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The closed-set note dispatch table (`SLSH-3`/`SLSH-4`/`SNK-aâ€¦k`), which depends only on `V12a` and `V4d`, is forced to wait on `V15g` before `V12b` becomes pickable, because the leaf also carries the `V15g`-dependent `SLSH-5` chain-attribution suffix. An implementer cannot land or red-green the template renderer independently of the invoke-provenance seam; the bundling also enlarges the leaf's review surface. Behaviour does not diverge between implementers, so this degrades build sequencing rather than correctness.

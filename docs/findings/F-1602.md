## Finding

`docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/patch-skew-degradation.md` is named, opened, and scoped by its `Patch-skew degradation contract` paragraph — the patch-skew window definition, the widening / narrowing / mixed sub-rules, and the `(P-a)/(P-b)/(P-c)` patch-skew fixture obligations. Yet the same page carries the entire normative `session_shutdown` teardown contract that the runtime runs on *every* shutdown, regardless of `event.reason`: the introductory "The handler runs in fixed order regardless of `event.reason`" framing, the numbered five sub-steps (drain + tag-init, abort, bounded await, watcher close, listener detach), the `Per-step isolation` paragraph, the closed normative `details.call` label set across sub-steps 1/4/5, and the `interstitial seam` swallow rule.

These rules are universal teardown contract, not patch-skew contract. The patch-skew window only ever invokes them (via the `loom/host/session-shutdown-reason-unknown` fallback "runs the full five-sub-step teardown" sentence in the opening paragraph); it does not author them. Their sibling page `session-shutdown-semantics.md` — already the home of the `Session-swap behaviour for in-flight invocations` paragraph, the `Per-invocation operator visibility (clean-cancel path)` rule, the `Partial-append fate during teardown` rule, the `invoke`-parent observation rule, the `Factory-ordering pin`, and the `<a id="session-shutdown-semantics"></a>` anchor — is the natural and consistent home for the teardown sequence and per-step-isolation rules.

The misfiling has two concrete failure modes. (1) An implementer or reviewer working on session-shutdown semantics consults `session-shutdown-semantics.md`, finds only the in-flight-invocation supplements that explicitly cite "the five-sub-step sequence above", and is left to discover that the sequence itself lives elsewhere — on a page named for a transient skew window. The `V9g` plan leaf already lists both files in its `**Spec.**` line precisely because the contract is split this way. (2) An implementer working on patch-skew handling sees the universal teardown sequence as in-scope for the patch-skew page, conflating "what we run on a no-snapshot reason" (which is patch-skew's concern) with "what running the teardown means" (which is not).

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** f5e89f4 — docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set (2026-06-04, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The five-sub-step teardown sequence and `Per-step isolation` rules were authored as a single normative block inside the monolithic `spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` by 5e8b499 (2026-05-06, "resolve 'Hot-reload teardown contract for the loom extension instance is unspecified'") and elaborated in place by later commits (6d1a949, a7d2a80). The misfiling was introduced by f5e89f4, the `100KB hard / 30KB recommended` cap split: it carved the monolithic PIC file into the present per-topic layout and chose to home the universal teardown sequence in the newly-created `patch-skew-degradation.md` (where the patch-skew window prose then naturally accreted around it) rather than in the sibling `session-shutdown-semantics.md` created in the same commit to hold the session-swap supplements. Both target pages were born of one commit; the split picked the wrong home for the teardown sequence.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Relocate the teardown sequence and per-step isolation block from `patch-skew-degradation.md` to `session-shutdown-semantics.md` in a single edit:

- Move, verbatim and in this order, from `patch-skew-degradation.md` to `session-shutdown-semantics.md` (immediately after the `<a id="session-shutdown-semantics"></a>` anchor and the existing `Session-swap behaviour for in-flight invocations` opening paragraph): the introductory sentence `The handler runs in fixed order regardless of event.reason:`, the five numbered sub-steps (`Stop accepting new work` … `Detach forwarding listeners`), and the entire `**Per-step isolation.**` paragraph including its `<a id="interstitial-seam-tag-write-swallow"></a>` anchor and its closed `details.call` enumeration.
- Preserve every `<a id="…">` anchor on the moved content byte-exact (`substep-1-drain-flag`, `substep-1-init-drain-state-tag`, `substep-1-act-order`, `substep-1-shutting-down-arm`, `substep-1-unset-tag-fallback`, `substep-1-degraded-needs-reload-arm`, `interstitial-seam-tag-write-swallow`). Anchors are wire surface used by every cross-referencing page; the move must not rename them.
- Leave behind, in `patch-skew-degradation.md`, the `Patch-skew degradation contract` opening paragraph (window definition, widening/narrowing/mixed sub-rules, `(P-a)/(P-b)/(P-c)` patch-skew fixture obligations) and the `Fixture-obligation categories` paragraph that immediately follows. Where the `(P-a)`/`(P-b-1)`/`(P-c-1)` fixture bullets cite "the full five-sub-step teardown still runs", retarget the phrase's cross-reference to the relocated home on `session-shutdown-semantics.md`.
- Sweep every other intra-corpus reference to the moved content. Update link targets (path or path-plus-anchor) in the read-only spec files listed in **Spec Documents** above. Since the anchors are preserved, the sweep is path-only for anchored links and is a path-plus-section-name update for unanchored phrasings (`patch-skew-degradation.md` §`session_shutdown` sub-step N → `session-shutdown-semantics.md` §`session_shutdown` sub-step N).
- Sweep plan-side citations. `V9g`, `V9g-T`, `V9a`, `V17a`, and `V18c` each reference `patch-skew-degradation.md` for material that moves; update their spec-pointer prose. Update the `cka-31` row in `docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md` and the corresponding `// allow: cka-31 — patch-skew-degradation.md` source-side exemption comment string to point at the new home in the same commit so the coverage gate remains coherent.
- The relocation is content-preserving; no normative rule, anchor, label set, or fixture obligation is added, removed, or rephrased.

Edge cases for the implementer: the moved `Per-step isolation` paragraph contains forward references ("the **Unknown-reason rule** above", "the [**Diagnostic-emission isolation**] rule defined under *Edge cases* below", "the **`LoomRegistry` drain-state contract** below") that are already cross-page links to PIC siblings — they relocate as-is. The opening sentence of the moved block (`The handler runs in fixed order regardless of event.reason:`) currently follows the `Fixture-obligation categories` paragraph; after the move it should follow the `Session-swap behaviour for in-flight invocations` framing paragraph on `session-shutdown-semantics.md`, which already names "the five-sub-step sequence above" and will then sit immediately above its referent.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Two reasonable implementers do not produce divergent runtime behaviour from this misfiling — every rule remains normative and locatable — but they pay a recurring documentation tax: every `V9g`-class change has to consult one page named for the contract and one named for a transient skew window, and every cross-reference to a sub-step or `details.call` label has to point at a page whose title disclaims it. Review and audit costs compound: lens passes that scope by page name (and the existing review-finding above flagging "patch-skew teardown fixtures over-prescribe spy mechanisms") inherit the same scope confusion.

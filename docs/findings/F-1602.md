## Problem

`docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/patch-skew-degradation.md` is named, opened, and scoped by its `Patch-skew degradation contract` paragraph (the patch-skew window definition, the widening/narrowing/mixed sub-rules, and the `(P-a)/(P-b)/(P-c)` fixture obligations), yet it also carries the entire universal `session_shutdown` teardown contract the runtime runs on every shutdown regardless of `event.reason`: the `The handler runs in fixed order regardless of event.reason:` opener, the five numbered sub-steps, and the `Per-step isolation` paragraph with its closed `details.call` label set and `interstitial seam` swallow rule. These are universal teardown contract, not patch-skew contract — the patch-skew window only invokes them, it does not author them. The natural home is the sibling page `session-shutdown-semantics.md`, which already holds the in-flight-invocation supplements (`Session-swap behaviour for in-flight invocations`, `Per-invocation operator visibility`, `Partial-append fate during teardown`, the `Factory-ordering pin`) and the `<a id="session-shutdown-semantics"></a>` anchor.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** f5e89f4 — docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set (2026-06-04, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The five-sub-step teardown sequence and `Per-step isolation` rules were authored as a single normative block inside the monolithic `spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` by 5e8b499 (2026-05-06, "resolve 'Hot-reload teardown contract for the loom extension instance is unspecified'") and elaborated in place by later commits (6d1a949, a7d2a80). The misfiling was introduced by f5e89f4, the `100KB hard / 30KB recommended` cap split: it carved the monolithic PIC file into the present per-topic layout and chose to home the universal teardown sequence in the newly-created `patch-skew-degradation.md` (where the patch-skew window prose then naturally accreted around it) rather than in the sibling `session-shutdown-semantics.md` created in the same commit to hold the session-swap supplements. Both target pages were born of one commit; the split picked the wrong home for the teardown sequence.

## Solution approach

Move the teardown block — the `The handler runs in fixed order regardless of event.reason:` opener, the five numbered sub-steps, and the `Per-step isolation` paragraph — from `patch-skew-degradation.md` to `session-shutdown-semantics.md`, landing it after the `<a id="session-shutdown-semantics"></a>` anchor and the `Session-swap behaviour for in-flight invocations` paragraph that already names "the five-sub-step sequence above". Leave the `Patch-skew degradation contract` and `Fixture-obligation categories` paragraphs on `patch-skew-degradation.md`, and retarget their `(P-a)/(P-b-1)/(P-c-1)` fixture bullets' "the full five-sub-step teardown still runs" cross-references to the relocated home. Sweep the remaining intra-corpus and plan-side citations (the spec pages that link to the moved content, and the `cka-31` coverage-matrix row with its source-side exemption comment) to point at the new home.

## Solution constraints

- Preserve every `<a id="…">` anchor on the moved content byte-exact (`substep-1-drain-flag`, `substep-1-init-drain-state-tag`, `substep-1-act-order`, `substep-1-shutting-down-arm`, `substep-1-unset-tag-fallback`, `substep-1-degraded-needs-reload-arm`, `interstitial-seam-tag-write-swallow`) — they are cross-referenced wire surface and the move must not rename them.
- The relocation is content-preserving: no normative rule, anchor, label set, or fixture obligation may be added, removed, or rephrased (the sub-step-1 prose and the `Per-step isolation` paragraph are resolved independently by F-1775 and F-1621).

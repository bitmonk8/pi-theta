## Finding

`conventions.md` uses the "close" word-family in two unrelated senses, and they sit in consecutive bullets of the same `§Leaf format` block:

- **Sense A — implementation / gate completion.** "the **implementation** leaf (`<id>`) that closes it (its green tests are the **closure** evidence)", "A leaf that **closes** a GOV-16 stable-inline-label family", and the pervasive "**closing** gate" / "**closing** leaf" / "**closing**-leaf column" vocabulary throughout `§REQ-ID discipline`. Here "close" means *the leaf/test discharges an obligation*.
- **Sense B — graph reachability over spec pages.** The `§Leaf format` **Spec** bullet: "The **Spec** field MUST be **closed under normative cross-link**: **closure** runs from each listed topic outbound, applies transitively to a fixed point". Here "closed/closure" is the set-theoretic *closure under a relation* — a static reachability property over `spec_topics/**` pages, nothing to do with implementation being finished.

The collision is sharp because the **Spec** bullet (sense B) is immediately followed by the **Tests** bullet (sense A) and the coverage-matrix sub-bullet's "closure evidence" (sense A); the single word "closure" appears in both senses within the one section. `leaf-template.md`'s **Spec** field note repeats sense B verbatim ("The field MUST be closed under normative cross-link").

A reader scanning the **Spec** bullet can mis-read "closed under normative cross-link" as an implementation-completion claim — i.e. "every REQ-ID reachable from the listed pages must already be implemented/closed" — rather than the intended "the **Spec** field must *list* every page reachable by following normative cross-links to a fixed point." That misreading would either over-restrict which leaves are pickable or inflate a leaf's perceived scope.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A leaf author reading the **Spec** bullet may mis-read "closed under normative cross-link" as an implementation-completion obligation rather than a page-listing (graph-reachability) obligation, and either under-list the **Spec** field (treating not-yet-implemented reachable pages as out of scope) or over-state the leaf's scope. Implementers can still produce a working leaf, so this degrades guidance clarity rather than blocking pickup or a gate.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Disambiguate by renaming sense B (the graph-reachability property) so the "close/closes/closing/closure" word-family is reserved for sense A (implementation / gate completion). In `docs/plan_topics/conventions.md` `§Leaf format` **Spec** bullet and the `docs/plan_topics/leaf-template.md` **Spec** field note, replace the sense-B verb/noun with a term that (i) does not reuse `close`/`closes`/`closing`/`closure`, and (ii) does not reuse `complete`/`completeness`/`transitive-completeness` — that family is already taken by the `H5d` coverage-set gate and the `<id>-complete` tags, so reusing it would create a second collision rather than fix the first.

Concretely, restate the **Spec**-field requirement around "cross-link expansion to a fixed point". For example, change the `conventions.md` text "The **Spec** field MUST be closed under normative cross-link: closure runs from each listed topic outbound, applies transitively to a fixed point, and excludes narrative cross-links per `governance.md` GOV-3" to "The **Spec** field MUST be **transitively expanded along normative cross-links**: expansion runs from each listed topic outbound to a fixed point, and excludes narrative cross-links per `governance.md` GOV-3", and make the corresponding substitution in the `leaf-template.md` **Spec** field note ("The field MUST be **transitively expanded along normative cross-links** … expansion runs from each listed page outbound … to a fixed point"). Keep the existing "normative cross-link" / "narrative cross-link" definitions unchanged.

Leave the sense-A occurrences ("closing gate", "closing leaf", "the implementation leaf that closes it", "closure evidence") as they are — sense A is the retained meaning of the word-family. The exact replacement term is content the fixer may adjust as long as it avoids both reserved word-families ("close*"/"complete*") and reads as a graph-reachability property rather than an implementation-completion claim.

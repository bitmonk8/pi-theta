## Finding

`V13c` (Query tool loop and typed two-phase) carries six distinct verification points in one leaf: (1) free-phase tool-loop mechanics (`CIO-4` — round advance, parallel-batch single-slot, forced-respond exemption); (2) the `max_rounds:0` forced-respond boundary (`QRY-14`); (3) untyped exhaustion surfacing `ToolLoopExhaustedError` (`QRY-16`); (4) the typed depth-6 co-fire vector, where a depth-6 typed-query response trips the loom-owned depth walk (`V5e`) before AJV and ceiling #4 surfaces as a `validation`/`maxDepth` `Err` while the co-satisfied ceiling #2 is enumerated as `masked:["ceiling#2"]` on the operator-facing `RuntimeEvent`; (5) the `cka-47` `@`-query-dispatch cancellation checkpoint facet distributed off `V17c`; and (6) the `ERR-13` completed-callee-finality live-carrier witness delegated from `V4f`.

Point (4) is qualitatively heavier than the others: it is a multi-system integration scenario that only exercises its target behaviour when both `V5e` (depth enforcement) and `V16a` (cross-ceiling arbitration seam) are operative, exercising the depth walk, AJV ordering, and `masked`-enumeration arbitration in a single fixture. Points (5) and (6) are independently-owned facets distributed onto `V13c` from other leaves (`V17c`'s checkpoint set; `V4f`'s no-rollback obligation), each red-testable in isolation against the `V8a` `Checkpoint` seam and the live query-tool-loop surface respectively.

The plan's own leaf-grouping principle is that "each leaf inside a slice is the smallest feature that can ship and be tested independently." `V13c` aggregates a core query-tool-loop feature, a cross-ceiling integration scenario, and two distributed resilience facets into a single TDD pass and a single `Ships when` gate.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer picking `V13c` must hold six verification points — free-phase loop mechanics, a cross-ceiling depth-6 integration scenario gated on `V5e`+`V16a` being operative, a cancellation checkpoint, and the `ERR-13` live-carrier witness — in one TDD red-green pass behind a single `Ships when` gate. The integration scenario is categorically harder to isolate than the core mechanics, so the leaf is slow to drive red-for-the-right-reason and a partial implementation can land with one facet (e.g. the `masked` enumeration or the `ERR-13` witness) never independently observed. A diligent implementer can still produce a working leaf, so the cost is review/verification friction rather than a build break.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Three-way split (core loop / ceiling co-fire / distributed resilience)

- **Approach:** Reduce `V13c` to the query-tool-loop core and peel the heavier and distributed facets into two new leaves.
- **Plan edits:** Keep in `V13c`/`V13c-T` the `CIO-4` free-phase mechanics, the `QRY-14` `max_rounds:0` boundary, and the `QRY-16` untyped exhaustion path. Move the typed depth-6 co-fire vector (`QRY-16` worked example; `masked:["ceiling#2"]`) into a new `<new>`/`<new>-T` leaf carrying `Deps` on `V13c`, `V5e`, `V16a`. Move the `cka-47` `@`-query-dispatch checkpoint facet and the `ERR-13` completed-callee-finality witness into a second `<new>`/`<new>-T` leaf carrying `Deps` on `V13c`, `V8a`, `V4f`. Link both new leaves under `docs/plan.md` §V13. Re-point the `coverage-matrix.md` `cka-47` and `ERR-13` closing-leaf cells from `V13c` to the new owning leaf. Re-target `V18d.Deps` from `V13c` to the new `ERR-13`-owning leaf, and `H7a.Deps` from `V13c` to the new ceiling-co-fire-owning leaf.
- **Spec edits:** None.
- **Pros:** Each resulting leaf tests one concern cluster; the integration scenario the finding flags as hardest gets its own gate; aligns leaf ownership with the already-distributed facet sources (`V17c` checkpoint set, `V4f` no-rollback).
- **Cons:** Two new leaf pairs plus consumer/coverage-matrix re-targeting — the largest edit of the options.
- **Risks:** Mis-targeting the `coverage-matrix` cells or the `V18d`/`H7a` `Deps` would orphan a closing leaf or break the pickability chain; verify each moved cell resolves to its new owner.

### Option B — Two-way split (peel only the cross-ceiling depth-6 scenario)

- **Approach:** Address only the facet the finding flags as categorically hardest, leaving the distributed resilience facets with the core.
- **Plan edits:** Move the typed depth-6 co-fire vector (`QRY-16` worked example) into a new `<new>`/`<new>-T` leaf with `Deps` on `V13c`, `V5e`, `V16a`. Keep the `cka-47` checkpoint facet and `ERR-13` witness in `V13c`. Link the new leaf under `docs/plan.md` §V13, and re-target `H7a.Deps` from `V13c` to the new ceiling-co-fire leaf. `coverage-matrix.md` `QRY` rows for the depth-6 example move to the new leaf; `cka-47`/`ERR-13` cells and `V18d.Deps` stay on `V13c`.
- **Spec edits:** None.
- **Pros:** Smallest edit; isolates the multi-system integration test the finding names without disturbing the `cka-47`/`ERR-13` ownership or the `V18d` edge.
- **Cons:** `V13c` still carries five points including two distributed resilience facets; the leaf remains larger than its siblings.
- **Risks:** Same coverage-matrix/Deps re-targeting risk, smaller surface.

### Recommendation

Take Option A. The `cka-47` checkpoint facet and the `ERR-13` witness entered `V13c` as late per-site distributions (`849d926`, `a63786f`) from leaves that already own those obligations, so peeling them into their own leaf restores `V13c` to the query-tool-loop feature it began as and gives each distributed facet an independently-observable gate. Watch the `coverage-matrix.md` `cka-47`/`ERR-13` cells and the `V18d`/`H7a` `Deps` edges: each must resolve to the new owning leaf after the move, or a closing leaf is orphaned. Use real `V<N><letter>`-scheme IDs for the new leaves at authoring time in place of the `<new>` placeholders.

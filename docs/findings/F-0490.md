## Finding

`H7a` (terminal integration-acceptance run) carries two structurally independent verification scenarios in a single leaf. The first scenario is the primary multi-feature fixture run: one representative fixture `.loom` driven through the `H4a` harness exercising the typed-query → tool-loop → code-tool-invoke → schema-lowering/validation → binder → cancellation pipeline, gated by a committed golden transcript, a golden diagnostics list, and a `golden ⊆ permitted` containment check (Tests bullets 1–4). The second scenario is a co-occurring ceiling-#3 (binder-retry-class, `V11f`) / ceiling-#2 (`tool_loop.max_rounds` runtime-class, `V13c`) breach driven through the live pipeline to witness `CIO-5` arbitration order with `masked` enumeration (Tests bullet 5) — a different fixture stimulus and a different verification target (cross-ceiling arbitration, not pipeline composition).

The two scenarios share only the `H4a` harness. They use different fixtures, assert against different artefacts, and certify different spec concerns. Because both live under one leaf's `Ships when`, a reviewer must hold both in a single pass and either scenario's failure reds the whole gate, masking which concern regressed. The second scenario is also the leaf's `CIO-5` closure contribution: `coverage-matrix.md` lists `H7a` as the `CIO-5` co-witness, and `V16a` defers its `CIO-5` cross-site "never-interleaves" property end-to-end to `H7a`. That coupling means any decomposition of the leaf must also re-home those `CIO-5` references onto whichever leaf carries the arbitration scenario.

The second scenario entered later that day at `b73a11a`, which appended the ceiling-#3/#2 arbitration Tests bullet, added `V16a` to Deps, and extended `Ships when` — resolving a different finding (`CIO-5` verified only in isolation) by bolting the arbitration witness onto the existing integration-acceptance leaf rather than onto a dedicated leaf. The bundling is therefore not present-since-inception; it is the residue of a single later finding-fix that chose co-location.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

This finding is bimodal: the split route both adds a new leaf and retargets the `CIO-5` references that `coverage-matrix.md` and `V16a` pin to `H7a`. The two obligations cannot be discharged by one leaf edit, so the options below decompose them, and the recommendation fixes their order.

### Option A — Split the arbitration scenario into its own leaf

**Approach.** Move Tests bullet 5 (the ceiling-#3/#2 co-occurring-breach arbitration witness) out of `H7a` into a new leaf, leaving `H7a` as the primary pipeline-composition fixture gate.

**Plan edits.**
- In `docs/plan_topics/H7a-integration-acceptance.md`: remove the ceiling-arbitration Tests bullet and the `Ships when` clause covering it; drop `V16a` from Deps if no remaining `H7a` bullet needs it; remove the `CIO-5` closure language from the leaf.
- Create the new leaf (`<new>`; the source suggested `H7b`) carrying the ceiling-#3/#2 arbitration live-site witness, with `Deps` covering the new leaf's prerequisites (the source proposed `H7a`, `V16a`, `V11f`, `V13c`).
- In `docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md`: change the `CIO-5` co-witness from `H7a` to `<new>`.
- In `docs/plan_topics/V16a-ceiling-order-masked.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V16a-T-ceiling-order-masked.md`: retarget the "`CIO-5` witnessed end-to-end by `H7a`" cross-references to `<new>`.
- In `docs/plan.md`: link the new leaf into the Horizontal phases / Release-gate index.

**Spec edits.** None.

**Pros.** Each leaf's `Ships when` certifies one concern; a regression points at the right scenario. `CIO-5` closure is owned by a leaf whose sole job is the arbitration witness.

**Cons.** Multi-file change touching the coverage matrix and `V16a` cross-references; introduces a new leaf and a new Deps edge into the release-gate sequence.

**Risks.** A missed `CIO-5` reference retarget would leave `coverage-matrix.md`/`V16a` pointing at a leaf that no longer carries the witness — a traceability break. Mitigated by doing the reference retarget after the new leaf exists (see Recommendation order).

### Option B — Keep one leaf; record the dual scope explicitly

**Approach.** Leave `H7a` carrying both scenarios, but annotate in the Adds that the leaf intentionally bundles the pipeline-composition fixture gate and the cross-ceiling arbitration witness as two distinct verification targets under one terminal gate.

**Plan edits.**
- In `docs/plan_topics/H7a-integration-acceptance.md`: add a one-sentence Adds note that the leaf carries two verification targets and why they share the terminal harness; no Deps or coverage-matrix change.

**Spec edits.** None.

**Pros.** Minimal churn; no coverage-matrix or `V16a` cross-reference edits; no new release-gate Deps edge.

**Cons.** The two concerns stay entangled at gate time; a reviewer still cannot tell which scenario reds the gate.

**Risks.** None beyond leaving the bundling in place.

### Recommendation

Given the advisory severity and that both scenarios already run through the same `H4a` harness at the terminal position, Option B is the lower-risk default; the bundling is a reviewability cost, not a correctness or pickability defect. If reviewability of the `CIO-5` closure is judged to outweigh the cross-file churn, take Option A and resolve its two obligations in order: first create the new leaf and move the arbitration Tests bullet plus its Deps (the scope-bounding edit), then — on that stable baseline — retarget the `CIO-5` co-witness in `coverage-matrix.md` and the `V16a`/`V16a-T` cross-references to the new leaf. Either way, watch the interaction with the `CIO-5`-overclaim finding below: under Option A the soften lands on the new leaf, not `H7a`.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The leaf is buildable as written, so this does not block pickup. The cost is at gate time: the primary pipeline-composition regression and the cross-ceiling arbitration regression are entangled under one `Ships when`, so either failure reds the terminal gate and a reviewer cannot tell from the gate result which of the two unrelated concerns regressed. It also forces the `CIO-5` closure for the entire corpus to ride on a sub-bullet of an otherwise pipeline-composition leaf.

## Problem

`grammar.md` is registered as a non-narrative page under the `GRAM` prefix but carries zero `GRAM-N` REQ-ID anchors. Its grammar productions ‚Äî the closed continuation-trigger table, the type-grammar productions, the `///`-placement production, and the statement-block tail-`?` carve-out ‚Äî are therefore uncitable by `#prefix-n` from the lexer, parser, type-system, and descriptions pages that depend on them. GOV-9's `#prefix-n` cross-link contract is consequently unsatisfiable for every defining obligation on the page.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception-with-progressive-extension

**Introducing commits:**

- `c1e5d64` (2026-05-05, "spec: REQ-ID infrastructure + Phase 12a heading promotion on five named pages (Decision #12, partial)") ‚Äî the foundational commit. It introduced the REQ-ID prefix table on `spec.md` with rows for `LEX`, `SCHM`, `DESC`, `FN`, `TOOL`, `BNDS` (then `BNDG`), `RET`, `RVM`, `IMPL`, and `GRAM`, and explicitly deferred the per-rule coinage pass: *"Phase 12b (per-rule REQ-ID assignment) ‚Ä¶ remain as the mechanical sweep across the rest of spec_topics/ and plan_topics/."* The Phase 12a heading-promotion pass touched only five named pages (`binder.md`, `query.md`, `schemas.md`, `expressions.md`, `frontmatter.md`); the eleven pages in this finding were registered but not anchored, by design.
- `1c7f550` (2026-05-07, "Hard ceilings block does load-bearing definitional work inside informative orientation") ‚Äî extended the same pattern when the new `hard-ceilings.md` page was registered under `CEIL` without coining `CEIL-N` IDs.
- `c4ad105` (2026-05-08, "HC3-a‚Ä¶HC3-e and NOCEIL-N labels escape GOV-3/GOV-8") ‚Äî re-registered `hard-ceilings.md` to additionally own `CIO`, and `CIO-1‚Ä¶CIO-6` were subsequently coined while `CEIL` remained empty.
- `4f588e0` (2026-06-03, "Non-narrative pages with zero coined REQ-IDs non-referenceable") ‚Äî recognised the residue and *codified* the progressive-drainage discipline by adding GOV-22 to `governance.md`. GOV-22 explicitly pins the per-commit, on-edit coinage trigger and disclaims any corpus-wide coinage commit.

**History:** The gap is structurally intentional ‚Äî the eleven prefixes were registered up front (`c1e5d64`) as a placeholder commitment that per-page coinage would land later, page-by-page, as edits to those pages flow through. `4f588e0` later codified that intention as GOV-22, which means the current state is not a regression but the steady-state operating mode of the rule. The finding's force is that "later" has never arrived for these eleven pages despite ~13 months of spec evolution, leaving GOV-9 cross-links permanently unsatisfiable for the bulk of the normative corpus.

## Solution approach

Coin `GRAM-N` REQ-IDs at each defining obligation site on `grammar.md` ‚Äî sites carrying an RFC-2119 modal outside fenced code, inline code spans, and HTML comments, plus each closed-set production (which is itself the normative obligation). Author each in dual-form per GOV-1 and allocate the numeric tail per GOV-3 under the already-registered `GRAM` prefix. Rewrite every page that currently cites a `grammar.md` section heading anchor to cite the new `#gram-n` fragment instead.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: per-rule coinage on the sibling non-narrative pages (`lexical.md`, `schemas.md`, `bindings.md`, `return.md`, `functions.md`, `tool-calls.md`, `runtime-value-model.md`, `descriptions.md`, `implementation-notes.md`, and the `hard-ceilings/` subtree) is owned by F-1268‚Ä¶F-1277 ‚Äî this finding coins only `GRAM` IDs on `grammar.md`.

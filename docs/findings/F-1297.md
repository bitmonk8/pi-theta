## Problem

The `options.signal` bullet in *Binder inference call* asserts loom forwards `loomAbort.signal` and the cancellation paragraph relies on `complete()` aborting the in-flight provider call. That behavioural property is pinned upstream at `conversation-drive.md#complete-retry-and-cancellation-presupposition` (property 2), re-validated through version-bump checklist item `(ab)`, but `binder-inference.md` carries no cross-reference to it. A reader of the binder page alone sees an unbacked behavioural assertion rather than a named, version-bump-tracked host presupposition.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `3683345` (2026-06-06) introduced the *`complete()` retry-timing and cancellation behavioural presupposition* block in `conversation-drive.md`; `803106d` (2026-06-07) extended it with the *Authoritative source* sub-paragraph and version-bump (aa)/(ab) routing. Neither commit added a back-reference from the `options.signal` bullet in `binder-inference.md`.
**History:** `git log -S'complete-retry-and-cancellation-presupposition'` shows the property pin landed in `conversation-drive.md` while the binder page's local assertion remained un-cross-linked. The finding is one facet of a larger `complete()`-call-shape under-pinning.

## Solution approach

Add a forward-link from the `options.signal` bullet in *Binder inference call* to `conversation-drive.md#complete-retry-and-cancellation-presupposition` (property 2) and to version-bump checklist item `(ab)` at `version-bump-step2.md#bump-checklist-complete-signal-honouring`. The presupposition already exists upstream; the link makes the binder page's local assertion a named, re-validated host presupposition.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: do not relocate the presupposition into `binder-inference.md` — that placement decision is owned by F-1143.
- MUST NOT add a second normative pin of the cancellation property on the binder page; the upstream presupposition is the sole authoritative source.

## Finding

The **Failures** section of `docs/spec_topics/tool-calls.md` opens with an unqualified universal: *"Tool-call failures surface as the `CodeToolError` variant of `QueryError`"*, then enumerates a four-member `cause` enum (`validation` / `execution` / `cancelled` / `unknown_tool`). The section heading and the surrounding **Argument shape** / **Return type** / **Concurrency** prose treat Pi tools and `.loom` callables uniformly under "tool-call", so a reader naturally reads the opening sentence as covering *every* `<name>(args)` failure regardless of callee kind.

A later paragraph in the same section flatly contradicts that reading: *"For a `.loom` callable, failures the callee returned cascade through the standard `InvokeCalleeError` variant […]; failures from the loom infrastructure itself […] cascade through `InvokeInfraError`. The only situation where `CodeToolError` arises for a `.loom` callable is loom 1.0's `"unknown_tool"` safety net."* The same paragraph further pins `InvokeInfraError { cause: "validation", ... }` as the input-validation arm on the `.loom`-callable side. So a `.loom`-callable failure is `CodeToolError` only in the single `"unknown_tool"` race; the three primary causes (`validation`, `execution`, `cancelled`) live on `InvokeCalleeError` / `InvokeInfraError`.

The contradiction is load-bearing because the **Failures** section is the entry point for authors writing `match` arms over tool-call failures. An author who reads only the opening sentence and the `cause` enum writes a single `CodeToolError` arm and silently mis-handles every primary failure mode of a `.loom` callable they happen to call as a tool — the arm never matches and the `Err` either propagates unhandled through `?` or falls into a catch-all the author thought was unreachable. The fix is to scope the opening sentence to the Pi-tool arm and forward-reference the `.loom`-callable cascade paragraph before the `cause` enum, so the reader meets the kind-split before reading rules they will mis-apply.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Rewrite the **Failures** opening sentence to scope it to Pi tools and forward-reference the `.loom`-callable cascade paragraph *before* enumerating the `cause` values, so the reader meets the kind-split before reading rules they would otherwise mis-apply to `.loom`-callable failures.

Concretely, replace the current opening:

> **Failures.** Tool-call failures surface as the `CodeToolError` variant of `QueryError`. The schema lives in [Errors and Results — QueryError variants](./errors-and-results/queryerror-variants.md#queryerror-variants); the cause enum is `validation` (arguments failed input-schema validation), `execution` (tool's `execute()` threw or returned `isError: true`), `cancelled` (AbortSignal fired), or `unknown_tool` (callable was lost across a file-watcher reload — cannot occur during the lifetime of a single invocation, since the load-time `tools:` table holds strong references to resolved callables; see [Parameters and Frontmatter](./frontmatter.md)).

with a kind-scoped opening that names the split up front, e.g.:

> **Failures.** Tool-call failure surfacing splits on callee kind: a **Pi-tool** failure surfaces as the `CodeToolError` variant of `QueryError`; a **`.loom`-callable** failure surfaces through `InvokeCalleeError` / `InvokeInfraError` per the paragraph below, with `CodeToolError` arising only in the `"unknown_tool"` safety-net case. The `CodeToolError` schema lives in [Errors and Results — QueryError variants](./errors-and-results/queryerror-variants.md#queryerror-variants); its `cause` enum (`validation` / `execution` / `cancelled` / `unknown_tool`) applies to the Pi-tool arm and to the `.loom`-callable `"unknown_tool"` arm — `validation` (arguments failed input-schema validation), `execution` (tool's `execute()` threw or returned `isError: true`), `cancelled` (AbortSignal fired), or `unknown_tool` (callable was lost across a file-watcher reload — cannot occur during the lifetime of a single invocation, since the load-time `tools:` table holds strong references to resolved callables; see [Parameters and Frontmatter](./frontmatter.md)).

Edge cases the implementer must preserve:

- The `cause`-enum descriptions and the `unknown_tool` race-impossibility parenthetical are normative content and must survive the rewrite verbatim — only their framing changes.
- The cross-link to `errors-and-results/queryerror-variants.md#queryerror-variants` is the canonical schema home and must remain a live anchor.
- The "Pi tool" / "`.loom` callable" terminology must match the page's existing usage (the **Argument shape** and **Return type** paragraphs already use exactly those two labels — do not introduce "registered loom" or "subagent-mode callable" synonyms at this site).
- The *Outcome enumeration (normative)* sub-bullet immediately below the opening, the `CodeToolError` vs `ModelToolError` paragraph, and the `.loom`-callable cascade paragraph itself all remain unchanged; the rewrite is strictly to the opening sentence + `cause`-enum sentence.
- Do not weaken the `CodeToolError` definition — the Pi-tool arm is still load-bearing and still uses the same closed enum; the rewrite only narrows the *scope claim*, not the variant itself.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two reasonable implementers would diverge: one writes a single `CodeToolError` `match` arm over `<name>(args)` failures (taking the opening sentence at face value); the other writes both a `CodeToolError` arm and `InvokeCalleeError` / `InvokeInfraError` arms (having read the later paragraph). The first implementation silently mis-handles every primary failure mode of a `.loom` callable called as a tool — `validation`, `execution`, and `cancelled` all route off the arm the author wrote — with no diagnostic, because the `Err` is well-typed and merely matches a different arm than the author expected.

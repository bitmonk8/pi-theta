## Problem

The **Failures** section of `docs/spec_topics/tool-calls.md` opens with an unqualified claim — *"Tool-call failures surface as the `CodeToolError` variant of `QueryError`"* — followed by a four-member `cause` enum. Because the section treats Pi tools and `.loom` callables uniformly under "tool-call", a reader takes the opening as covering every `<name>(args)` failure regardless of callee kind. A later paragraph in the same section contradicts that reading: a `.loom`-callable failure cascades through `InvokeCalleeError` / `InvokeInfraError`, and `CodeToolError` arises for a `.loom` callable only in the `"unknown_tool"` safety-net case. An author who reads only the opening writes a single `CodeToolError` `match` arm and silently mis-handles the primary `.loom`-callable failure modes (`validation`, `execution`, `cancelled`), which route off the arm the author wrote.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commit:** `7cd9cc6` (2026-05-04) — *spec: unify tools and loom-as-tool callables under tools:; add ToolCallError*
**History:** The contradiction has been present since the **Failures** section was first added. Commit `7cd9cc6` introduced both the opening sentence (then *"Tool-call failures surface as a new `QueryError` variant"* followed by the `ToolCallError` schema) and the later `.loom`-callable cascade paragraph (*"For a registered loom callee, failures the callee returned cascade through the standard `InvokeCalleeError` variant […]. The only situation where `ToolCallError` arises for a loom callee is V1's `"unknown_tool"` safety net."*) in the same diff, so the kind-split was authored alongside the unqualified opening rather than retrofitted. The 2026-05-05 rename pass (`6750c81`) rewrote `ToolCallError` → `CodeToolError` throughout but left both sentences structurally as authored; the spec-topics split (`fecb504`) moved the section verbatim into `spec_topics/tool-calls.md`; the size-cap pass (`f5e89f4`) only re-pointed the schema cross-link; and `8ad3d33` extended the `.loom`-callable paragraph with the `InvokeInfraError { cause: "validation" }` clarification — none of these touched the opening sentence. `git log -L` over the `**Failures.**` line on `docs/spec_topics/tool-calls.md` confirms `7cd9cc6` as the only commit that has ever authored its content.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the **Failures** opening sentence and its `cause`-enum sentence so the callee-kind split is stated up front: scope the `CodeToolError` claim to the Pi-tool arm and forward-reference the `.loom`-callable cascade paragraph before the `cause` enum, so the reader meets the kind-split before reading rules that otherwise mis-apply to `.loom`-callable failures. Carry the `cause`-enum members forward as the contract surface for the Pi-tool arm and the `.loom`-callable `"unknown_tool"` arm.

## Solution constraints

- Edits are confined to the **Failures** opening sentence and its `cause`-enum sentence; the *Outcome enumeration (normative)* sub-bullet, the `CodeToolError` vs `ModelToolError` paragraph, and the `.loom`-callable cascade paragraph must remain unchanged.
- The `cause`-enum member descriptions and the `unknown_tool` race-impossibility parenthetical are normative; their content must be preserved (only their framing may change).

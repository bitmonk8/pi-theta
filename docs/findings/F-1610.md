## Finding

`unknown-reason-rule.md` introduces two handler-local variables by spelled-out identifier — `capturedEventReason` (pre-initialised to the literal `"<unreadable>"`, overwritten by the `event.reason` read on the success arm) and `pinnedConstantReadOk` (pre-initialised to `false`, set to `true` on the snapshot lookup-and-`literals` success arm) — and then uses those exact names as cross-document binding handles rather than role descriptions. The names are quoted verbatim in adjacent normative prose as if they were wire-side identifiers:

- `session-only-degraded-state.md` quotes `pinnedConstantReadOk && capturedEventReason ∈ {"new","resume","fork"}` as the narrow diagnostic-emission predicate and `capturedEventReason ∉ {"quit","reload"}` as the broad tag-transition predicate in the *Predicate split* table; the *Seam minimality (normative)* clause forbids "any handler-scoped state seam beyond `pinnedConstantReadOk` and `capturedEventReason`"; acceptance branches (d) and (e) assert `pinnedConstantReadOk = true` / `false` as fixture preconditions.
- `drain-state-contract.md`'s *Handler control-flow ordering* clause names the two variables in carve-out (b) of the pre-sequence enumeration and again in the `markRuntimeDegraded` *Methods* paragraph as the seam the broader predicate `capturedEventReason ∉ {"quit", "reload"}` consumes.
- `version-bump-triggers.md`'s step-5 inbound-reference sweep lists `capturedEventReason` in the second-stage grep anchor set `event.reason|SessionShutdownEvent|capturedEventReason|entry.shutdownReason` as a required search token on each Pi bump.
- `docs/plan_topics/V9h-unknown-reason-rule.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V9l-session-only-degraded-branch.md` cite "the unknown-reason rule's `pinnedConstantReadOk` seam" as the normative-cross-link-closure justification in their **Spec** fields.

The observable contract behind these names is purely behavioural: a captured-reason slot whose default is the literal `"<unreadable>"`, a snapshot-read-succeeded flag whose default is `false`, and two predicates over those two pieces of state. Nothing outside the runtime sees either identifier — the operator-visible wire surface is `details.event.reason`, `details.observed`, `details.failure`, and the per-entry `shutdownReason` stamp; the handler's local variable bindings are private to the module that implements them. By naming the locals in spec prose and then re-quoting them across four PIC pages and two plan leaves, the spec elevates internal identifier spellings into contract surface, in tension with its own observable-only discipline. A conformant implementation that named the variables `lastReason` and `snapshotOk` would diverge from spec text without diverging from spec semantics, and a future handler-side rename would trigger a multi-page inbound-reference sweep that produces no behavioural change. The defect has the same shape as the sibling `active-invocation-registry.md` finding (internal container / field spellings pinned where only wire-side identifiers and iteration order are observable) and the `drain-state-contract.md` `drainStateTag`-vs-`tag` naming finding.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Demote both handler-local identifiers from spec-pinned names to spec-described roles, in one editorial pass across the cited files:

1. In `unknown-reason-rule.md`'s pre-init clause, replace the named-variable introductions with role descriptions — for example, "the handler-scoped *captured-reason slot*, pre-initialised at handler entry to the literal string `"<unreadable>"`, overwritten by the success arm of operation (2)'s `event.reason` read" and "the handler-scoped *snapshot-read-succeeded flag*, pre-initialised at handler entry to `false`, set to `true` on the success arm of operation (1)'s snapshot lookup-and-`literals` read." Preserve the behavioural pins verbatim — the `"<unreadable>"` literal default, the pre-init-before-either-read ordering, the success-arm transitions, the unchanged-on-snapshot-failure invariant, and the obligation that both slot and flag survive the handler-entry `try`/`catch` discipline are the contract; only the spelled-out names go.
2. In `session-only-degraded-state.md`'s *Predicate split* table, restate both predicates in role terms — "the snapshot-read-succeeded flag is set **and** the captured-reason slot's value is a member of `{"new","resume","fork"}`" for the narrow predicate; "the captured-reason slot's value is not a member of `{"quit","reload"}`" for the broad predicate. The *Seam minimality (normative)* clause's two-seam ceiling is preserved by phrasing it as "no handler-scoped state seam beyond the snapshot-read-succeeded flag and the captured-reason slot" — the prohibition is on adding a *third* piece of handler-scoped state, not on a particular identifier spelling, and it survives the rename. Acceptance branches (d) and (e), the harmlessness paragraph, and the *Co-emission rules* sub-step-2 stamp note switch from `pinnedConstantReadOk = true / false` and `capturedEventReason ∈ …` to "snapshot-read-succeeded flag set / unset" and "captured-reason slot holds …".
3. In `drain-state-contract.md`, restate carve-out (b) of the *Handler control-flow ordering* clause's pre-sequence enumeration in role terms (the handler-entry preamble pre-initialises the two slots described by `unknown-reason-rule.md`), and rephrase the `markRuntimeDegraded` *Methods* paragraph's tag-transition predicate the same way. The cross-reference invariant requiring both pages to sweep in the same commit on any predicate-text edit is preserved.
4. In `version-bump-triggers.md`, re-ground the step-5 inbound-reference sweep. Drop `capturedEventReason` from the second-stage grep anchor list and replace it with anchors keyed on shapes the spec does pin observably — the per-entry stamped field `entry.shutdownReason` (already in the list) and the diagnostic payload site `details.event.reason` — together with the prose description "the handler-scoped capture site of the session-shutdown reason, whatever the implementation has named it." The closed-enumeration discipline the paragraph imposes is preserved; what changes is that the enumeration documents observable reference shapes rather than grepping for a private token the spec invented. The leaf-owned build-time audit pass the paragraph mandates re-keys on the same observable shapes.
5. In the V9h and V9l plan-leaf **Spec** fields' normative-cross-link-closure notes, replace "the unknown-reason rule's `pinnedConstantReadOk` seam" with "the unknown-reason rule's snapshot-read-succeeded flag seam" (or equivalent role phrasing).

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- The two roles MUST remain distinguishable as exactly two separate seams — the *Seam minimality* prohibition keys on the two-piece state model (one captured-reason slot, one snapshot-read-succeeded flag), not on identifier spellings, and the restated predicates must continue to reference exactly those two pieces with no collapse into one.
- The `version-bump-triggers.md` rewrite is load-bearing: the paragraph currently uses `capturedEventReason` as a contributor-facing grep token, so demoting the name without re-grounding the recipe would weaken the inbound-reference sweep. Re-ground on `entry.shutdownReason` (the per-entry stamped field) and `details.event.reason` (the emission payload site), both of which the spec pins observably, *before* dropping the literal identifier from the anchor set.
- Preserve the argument the *Seam minimality* clause makes for why the narrow predicate's first conjunct must consult the snapshot-read-succeeded flag rather than re-reading `event.reason` or doing a `"<unreadable>"` sentinel-string comparison — it pins the snapshot-failure-with-closed-set-`event.reason` exclusion against an alternative implementation that re-reads at the discriminator site. That argument is about *what state the predicate consumes*, not *what the variable is spelled*, and it survives the rename in role form.
- A spec-side reader may search the corpus for the literal `capturedEventReason` token after this edit and find it absent; add a short note at the top of `unknown-reason-rule.md`'s pre-init clause indicating that the role labels are spec-owned and the implementation chooses the identifiers, so a future reader does not re-introduce the pin to "restore" the cross-reference grep.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A conformant implementation that picks different identifier names diverges from spec text without diverging from spec semantics, and a contributor renaming the variables inside the handler module triggers a multi-page inbound-reference sweep with no observable effect. The misalignment also lays a contributor-side trap on the next Pi version bump: `version-bump-triggers.md`'s step-5 grep recipe treats the literal token `capturedEventReason` as a required search anchor, so an implementation that named its handler-scoped capture differently would silently miss its own emission site during the bump audit, weakening the sweep that the recipe exists to enforce.

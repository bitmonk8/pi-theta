## Finding

The normative exclusion "Pi's own `LoadExtensionsResult.errors` field is **not** used" lives only in the one-paragraph intro of `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics.md` (the hub index). Its sibling — the redirection to `loom/load/extension-bootstrap-failed` for the same class of factory-time bootstrap-registration failures Pi would otherwise push into `LoadExtensionsResult.errors` — also sits there. Neither statement appears anywhere in `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md`, which is the page that owns the "Persistent diagnostics (default)" delivery-channel contract and enumerates the `loom/parse/*`, `loom/load/*`, and `loom/runtime/*` namespaces routed through `pi.sendMessage({ customType: "loom-system-note", … })`.

An implementer asking "which Pi-side channel does loom use for `loom/load/*` diagnostics, and is `LoadExtensionsResult.errors` part of that surface?" will land on `diagnostic-shape.md` (linked from the hub Contents and referenced from every code-registry shard). That page enumerates exactly two delivery sinks (the `loom-system-note` persistent channel plus the `ctx.ui.notify` transient-toast auxiliary) and lists five carved-out `console.error` exceptions, but never mentions `LoadExtensionsResult.errors` — leaving the reader to infer from omission rather than from a positive normative statement on the page that owns the contract.

The placement defect is split-induced: prior to commit `f5e89f4` (the 100 KB / 30 KB size-cap split), the exclusion paragraph and the delivery-channel paragraphs sat in the same monolithic `diagnostics.md`, so the exclusion was de-facto co-located with the channel rules. The split kept the exclusion on the index page and moved the channel rules to the new child shard without re-stating the exclusion on the child, so the co-location was lost.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-by-later-commit
**Introducing commit:** `f5e89f4` — "docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set" (2026-06-04)
**History:** The `LoadExtensionsResult.errors`-not-used exclusion entered the corpus at commit `ac8c3af` ("pi-loom spec: resolve 'LoadExtensionsResult.errors is the wrong delivery channel for loom diagnostics'", 2026-05-04), authored into the then-monolithic `spec_topics/diagnostics.md` as the intro paragraph immediately above the delivery-channel prose — i.e. co-located with the channel contract. The link to `loom/load/extension-bootstrap-failed` was added in-place at commit `6829a79` ("Pi-SDK hidden-assumption cluster …"). The size-cap split commit `f5e89f4` moved the delivery-channel prose into the new child page `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md` while leaving the exclusion paragraph behind on the index page, severing the co-location without restating the exclusion on the child. `git log -S 'LoadExtensionsResult' -- docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md` returns no commits, confirming the exclusion was never carried into the child shard.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Restate the exclusion on `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md`, immediately above (or as the opening sentence of) the **Persistent diagnostics (default)** paragraph, in roughly this form:

> Pi's own `LoadExtensionsResult.errors` field is **not** a loom delivery channel: that field is owned by Pi's extension loader and is populated only while Pi is `import()`-ing the loom extension's entry point. Loom emits its own [`loom/load/extension-bootstrap-failed`](./code-registry-load.md) for the factory-time bootstrap registration / subscription failures Pi would otherwise push into `LoadExtensionsResult.errors`; loom 1.0's full Pi-side delivery surface is the two channels enumerated in this section plus the five `console.error` carve-outs below.

Keep a one-sentence pointer on the hub `diagnostics.md` so a reader landing on the hub still sees that the exclusion exists, but make `diagnostic-shape.md` the normative site. Two implementer-relevant edge cases the wording should not blur: (a) the exclusion is about `LoadExtensionsResult.errors` as a *channel for loom-emitted diagnostics*, not about loom never appearing in that field at all (Pi may itself populate it if the loom extension's `import()` fails); (b) the cross-link target is `./code-registry-load.md` (relative to the child page), not `./diagnostics/code-registry-load.md` (which is the hub-relative path).

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader consulting the delivery-channel contract page in isolation does not see the `LoadExtensionsResult.errors` exclusion, and so cannot confirm from that page alone that the enumerated `loom-system-note` + `console.error` + `ctx.ui.notify` set is exhaustive for Pi-side delivery. Implementers are unlikely to invent a route through `LoadExtensionsResult.errors` (Pi populates that field, not the extension), but a tester writing a "no loom diagnostic appears on `LoadExtensionsResult.errors`" assertion has no anchor on the delivery-channel page to cite.

## Problem

The taxonomy-extension sentence in `audit-target-categories.md` (the "Satisfying the three in-scope categories MUST extend `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY`'s entry-kind taxonomy" sentence) has an ambiguous qualifier, "with at minimum one new entry kind per category not already covered". Reading A binds "not already covered" to *category* — a new kind is owed only for categories with no existing entry kind at all, yielding two new kinds. Reading B binds it to *surface* — each of the three categories owes a kind for whatever surface its existing kinds do not yet enumerate, yielding three new kinds. The colon-trailing enumeration commits to Reading B (it lists a category-(2) kind for the un-covered peer-package named imports), but a Reading-A implementer reading the lead clause first can take the enumeration as illustrative and ship a two-kind inventory. The ambiguity propagates into the downstream MUSTs that key on what "the new kinds" enumerates.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the lead clause of the taxonomy-extension sentence so the "not already covered" qualifier binds to *surface* rather than to *category*, committing the sentence to Reading B (each of the three categories owes at least one new kind for the surface its existing kinds do not enumerate). Keep the colon-trailing enumeration and its parenthetical naming `type-union-snapshot` and `namespace-function` as the two existing-coverage cases — they are already correct under Reading B, and the category-(2) clause's `where not already covered by an existing entry kind` qualifier remains accurate.

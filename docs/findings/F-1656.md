## Finding

The taxonomy-extension obligation in `audit-target-categories.md` reads:

> Satisfying the three in-scope categories MUST extend `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY`'s entry-kind taxonomy beyond the existing kinds, with at minimum one new entry kind per category not already covered: a category (3) kind …; a category (1) kind …; and a category (2) kind for named imports from the four `@earendil-works/*` peer packages where not already covered by an existing entry kind …

The qualifier *"per category not already covered"* binds two ways:

- **Reading A** (per-category): "one new kind per [category not already covered]" — a new kind is required only for categories with no existing entry kind at all. Under this reading category (2) already has `type-union-snapshot` and `namespace-function`, so no new category-(2) kind is owed; only category (1) and category (3) gain new kinds.
- **Reading B** (per-uncovered-surface): "one new kind per category, [covering the surface in that category] not already covered" — every one of the three categories owes at least one new kind for whatever surface in that category existing kinds do not yet enumerate. Category (2) owes a kind for the peer-package named imports that `type-union-snapshot` and `namespace-function` do not cover.

The colon-trailing enumeration commits to Reading B (it explicitly lists a category-(2) kind for the un-covered peer-package named imports, and the parenthetical names the two existing-coverage cases that the new kind is *added alongside*, not *substituted for*). But a Reading-A implementer reading the lead clause first can take the enumeration as a non-exhaustive illustration of the categories where coverage happens to be needed — and conclude that the category-(2) entry simply notes that one such kind already exists. The two implementers ship divergent inventories: Reading A produces two new kinds, Reading B produces three.

The same ambiguity propagates into the downstream MUSTs in the paragraph — "The new kinds MUST be coined …", "the literal-read assertion's branch table MUST gain an arm for each new kind", and the family-(4) failure-routing — all key on what "the new kinds" enumerates.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Rewrite the lead clause to bind the "not already covered" qualifier to *surface*, not to *category*, and to commit to Reading B explicitly. Replace:

> … with at minimum one new entry kind per category not already covered: a category (3) kind …; a category (1) kind …; and a category (2) kind … where not already covered by an existing entry kind …

with:

> … with at minimum one new entry kind for each of the three categories, covering the surface in that category not already covered by any existing entry kind: a category (3) kind …; a category (1) kind …; and a category (2) kind … not already covered by an existing entry kind …

Keep the colon-trailing enumeration and its parenthetical naming `type-union-snapshot` and `namespace-function` as the two existing-coverage cases — that enumeration is already correct under Reading B; the edit only retunes the lead clause so it agrees with the enumeration. Do not delete the `where not already covered by an existing entry kind` qualifier on the category-(2) clause — it remains correct (and is now consistent with the lead clause's matching qualifier on each of the three categories), and is what scopes the new category-(2) kind to the surface the two existing kinds do not enumerate.

Implementer edge case: the parenthetical "(the existing `type-union-snapshot` entry for `SessionShutdownEvent.reason` and the existing `namespace-function` entries for the nine factory-probable function-member entries are the two existing-coverage cases)" stays attached to the category-(2) clause unchanged; it documents existing coverage rather than constraining the new kind's scope.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two reasonable implementers diverge on whether a new category-(2) entry kind is owed at all. Under Reading A, the audit ships with no kind covering peer-package named imports beyond the two existing entries, and any new in-runtime peer-package named import (e.g. a future named import not matching `type-union-snapshot` or `namespace-function`) routes to the unrecognised-`kind` arm or to family (1) "unknown reference" rather than to the intended category-(2) coverage. Reading B is the corpus-intended one; the lead clause should not leave it as a guess.

## Finding

`H6a`'s **Ships when** clause attaches the adjective "falsifiable" to the evidence-record artefact twice: `"Recorded" obligates a falsifiable evidence record committed in this leaf's **gate-activation commit message**` and, later, `capturing the falsifiable record of the run`. This is a category slip. Throughout the corpus "falsifiable" is reserved for *assertions and obligations* — claims a test or inspection could disprove (e.g. `runtime-event-channel.md` "This makes the obligation falsifiable", and the `BNDR-N` "falsifiable, code-keyed assertion" usage). An evidence *record* is a static artefact; it is not the thing that is true-or-false. What the gate actually requires is a record that is concrete and **independently verifiable after the fact** — which is exactly what the surrounding prose then describes ("a skipped, mis-recorded, or stale-pin run is detectable after the fact by reading the commit message").

The word therefore imposes no content constraint and reads as borrowed from the originating finding's title rather than as a deliberate term of art at this site. The substance of the record — the Pi-SDK pin literal, named owner, run date, and observed result — is already fully enumerated in the same clause, so the fix is purely a wording correction that re-aligns the adjective with the verifiable-after-the-fact intent.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/H6a-live-corpus-activation.md` §**Ships when**, correct both uses of "falsifiable" applied to the record artefact:

- Replace `"Recorded" obligates a falsifiable evidence record committed in this leaf's **gate-activation commit message**` with `"Recorded" obligates a concrete, independently verifiable evidence record committed in this leaf's **gate-activation commit message**`.
- Replace `capturing the falsifiable record of the run:` with `capturing the independently verifiable record of the run:`.

Leave the enumerated record contents (Pi-SDK pin literal, named owner, run date, observed result against the `real-host-smoke-gate.md` criterion) and the "detectable after the fact by reading the commit message" sentence unchanged — they already state the verifiable-after-the-fact intent the corrected adjective names.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

"falsifiable" mis-attributes a true/false property to a static artefact and could be misread as imposing a content constraint the clause does not actually impose. Because the required record fields are enumerated immediately afterward, no implementer behaviour diverges; the defect is confined to wording precision.

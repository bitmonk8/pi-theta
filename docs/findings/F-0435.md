## Finding

The `## Blocked obligations` section of `docs/plan.md` is an index of *what is blocked and why* — each entry states a blocking condition and names the leaf that owns the deciding resolution. The `V9l` entry exceeds that role. Beyond the blocking condition (host-prerequisites clause (a) open contradiction) and the resolution owner (`V18c`'s Deps edge), it embeds two pieces of non-index prose:

1. **Spec-uncertainty commentary** — "The spec records that the resolution 'may find the branch unreachable as written' and require `SM-4`/`SM-5`/`SM-6`/`SM-3b` to be reworked." This names internal state-machine identifiers whose currency cannot be verified from the plan corpus, and describes a *spec-side* rework that only becomes actionable once the block resolves.

2. **A conditional "Pre-designed fallback:" paragraph** — implementation direction (collapse to full teardown + `/reload` with no degraded tag write; rework the degraded-branch obligations spec-side) that applies only *after* `V18c` resolves clause (a) as *branch unreachable*. At that point the correct vehicles are a spec rework plus a fresh plan edit, not pre-staged prose in the plan index.

Both pieces are speculative until the block resolves, and the conditional implementation direction is leaf-scoped rather than index-scoped. `V9l`'s own `Ships when` field already points back at this paragraph ("the pre-designed fallback in `plan.md` §Blocked obligations governs what ships"), so whichever way the prose is dispositioned, that cross-reference must be reconciled in the same change.

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**
- `55ed974` (2026-06-12, "pi-loom plan: resolve \"degraded-state obligations green over open spec contradiction\"") — created `## Blocked obligations` with the `V9l` entry, introducing the `SM-4`/`SM-5`/`SM-6`/`SM-3b` spec-commentary and a brief fallback pointer.
- `9c42f2d` (2026-06-16, "pi-loom plan: resolve \"V9l fallback path belongs in §Blocked obligations\"") — moved the detailed fallback *out of* the `V9l` leaf's `Adds` field and *into* the §Blocked obligations entry, expanding it to its current verbose form and repointing `V9l`'s `Ships when` at the index.
**History:** `git log -S` on `Pre-designed fallback` / `SM-4` / `Blocked obligations` over `docs/plan.md` isolates the two commits above. The defect is the combined result: the speculative content entered at `55ed974`, and a deliberate placement consolidation at `9c42f2d` concentrated the verbose fallback in the plan index — the exact location this finding flags. Note that `9c42f2d` was itself a review-driven resolution that moved the fallback *to* §Blocked obligations; the placement option below would reverse it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Relocate the conditional direction to the V9l leaf (placement)

**Approach.** Move the "Pre-designed fallback" sentence out of `docs/plan.md` §Blocked obligations and onto `docs/plan_topics/V9l-session-only-degraded-branch.md`, where conditional implementation direction for the leaf naturally lives, leaving only the blocking condition + resolution owner in the index entry.
**Plan edits.** Strike the fallback sentence from the §Blocked obligations `V9l` entry; restore a leaf-local fallback statement on `V9l` (its `Adds` field previously carried one); repoint `V9l`'s `Ships when` parenthetical at the leaf-local statement instead of `plan.md` §Blocked obligations.
**Spec edits.** None.
**Pros.** Co-locates conditional direction with the leaf that will act on it; keeps §Blocked obligations a lean index.
**Cons.** Directly reverses commit `9c42f2d`, which deliberately moved the fallback the other way; does not address the stale `SM-4`/`SM-5`/`SM-6`/`SM-3b` spec-commentary, which remains speculative wherever it lives.
**Risks.** Re-opening a settled placement decision invites oscillation across fix-loop iterations.

### Option B — Delete the speculative content (cruft)

**Approach.** Strike both the spec-uncertainty commentary and the "Pre-designed fallback" sentence from the §Blocked obligations `V9l` entry, leaving the blocking condition (host-prerequisites clause (a) open contradiction) and the deciding-resolution owner (`V18c` clause (a)). When `V18c` resolves, the spec rework plus a fresh plan edit re-introduce whatever disposition is then correct.
**Plan edits.** In `docs/plan.md` §Blocked obligations `V9l` entry, strike the sentence beginning "The spec records that the resolution…" and the sentence beginning "Pre-designed fallback:", retaining the blocking-condition and resolution-owner text. In `docs/plan_topics/V9l-session-only-degraded-branch.md`, reconcile the `Ships when` parenthetical so it no longer references a deleted fallback paragraph — state plainly that if clause (a) resolves *branch unreachable*, the leaf's disposition is settled spec-side at resolution time.
**Spec edits.** None.
**Pros.** Removes stale/speculative prose; does not re-open the leaf-vs-index placement decision; smallest durable surface.
**Cons.** Loses the pre-staged contingency until `V18c` resolves (re-derivable from the spec at that time).
**Risks.** The `V9l` `Ships when` cross-reference must be reconciled in the same change or it dangles.

### Recommendation

Take **Option B**. The fallback is conditional direction that only applies after `V18c` resolves clause (a), and the `SM-4`/`SM-5`/`SM-6`/`SM-3b` commentary is unverifiable stale detail; both are speculative today. Deleting them and reconciling the `V9l` `Ships when` reference is the durable fix. Option A re-litigates the placement decision recorded in commit `9c42f2d` and invites fix-loop oscillation. Watch two edge cases: the §Blocked obligations entry must retain the blocking condition and the `V18c` resolution-owner edge, and `V9l`'s `Ships when` parenthetical must not be left pointing at the deleted fallback paragraph.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Speculative planning prose in the plan index does not block any leaf from being picked up, but it is stale-by-construction: the `SM-4`/`SM-5`/`SM-6`/`SM-3b` reference drifts silently as the spec evolves, and the fallback direction reads as committed plan when it is contingent on a resolution that has not happened. A reader auditing the blocked list cannot tell the durable facts (blocking condition, resolution owner) from the conditional pre-staging.

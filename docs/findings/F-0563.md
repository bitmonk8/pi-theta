## Finding

`V13f` instruments the `@`-query provider's abandonable Promise — it asserts the swallowing handler attaches at the Promise-construction site and that a late settlement is suppressed along all three side channels. That construction site does not exist until the `@`-query construct and its dispatch land: `V13a` (Query render) introduces the `@`-template query construct, and `V13c` (Query tool loop) owns the model provider-call loop that produces the abandonable provider Promise. `V13f`'s Deps are `V13f-T`, `V9n`, `V8a`, `H4b` — and `V13f-T`'s Deps are `V9n`, `V8a`, `H4b` — neither names any V13 leaf.

Every sibling per-site swallowing-handler-routing leaf declares its site-owner: `V14f → V14a` (code-side `execute()`), `V15h → V15a` (invoke-child execution Promise), `V9o → V9i` (subagent-mode `AgentSession.abort()`), and the adjacent `V13g → V13a`. `V13f` is the lone exception.

Because pickability is decided purely on the presence of each direct Deps completion tag, `V13f` (and `V13f-T`) become pickable as soon as `V9n`, `V8a`, `H4b` (and `V13f-T`) are complete — potentially before `V13a`/`V13c`. An implementer who picks it then has no `@`-query to dispatch and cannot construct the late-settlement test the leaf is defined around.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** `eaa5000` (2026-06-16, "pi-loom plan: resolve \"V13c bundles four independent spec obligations into one leaf\"")
**History:** `V13f-query-cancellation-routing.md` and `V13f-T-query-cancellation-routing.md` were both created in `eaa5000` (verified via `git log --follow --diff-filter=A`). At creation the Deps were `V13f-T, V9c, V8a, H4b` — already carrying no V13 site-owner (`V9c` is the prompt-mode conversation-drive leaf, not the `@`-query construct). A later commit `0c3a2a2` (2026-06-17) retargeted that single non-V13 edge `V9c → V9n` during the V9c split but did not add a V13 dependency. The omission has therefore been present from the leaf's first commit; no later edit introduced or removed it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add the `@`-query site-owner to the `Deps` field of both `docs/plan_topics/V13f-query-cancellation-routing.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V13f-T-query-cancellation-routing.md`.

Add `V13c` — it owns the model provider-call tool loop that constructs the abandonable provider Promise, and it transitively reaches `V13a` (the `@`-query construct) through `V13c → V13b → V13a`, so a single edge orders both `V13f` and `V13f-T` after the construct and its dispatch exist. Concretely:

- In `V13f`, change `**Deps.** \`V13f-T\`, \`V9n\`, \`V8a\`, \`H4b\`` to include `\`V13c\``.
- In `V13f-T`, change `**Deps.** \`V9n\`, \`V8a\`, \`H4b\`` to include `\`V13c\``.

If the abandonable Promise being instrumented is the bare `@`-query render/dispatch rather than the tool-loop provider call, add `\`V13a\`` instead of (or in addition to) `\`V13c\``. Match whichever V13 leaf actually owns the Promise-construction site `V13f`'s tests attach to; the goal is that `V13f`/`V13f-T` become pickable only after that site exists.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

The pickability predicate will mark `V13f`/`V13f-T` ready in a state where the `@`-query construct and provider dispatch do not yet exist, so the implementer either stalls (no Promise site to instrument, no late-settlement test constructible) or invents a stand-in surface that diverges from the real `V13a`/`V13c` provider Promise. The per-site cancellation witness can silently land against the wrong surface.

## Finding

`PIC-2. Prompt-mode sequential execution` is authored as the final paragraph of `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/subagent.md` (line 90), appended after `PIC-22` (subagent parallel spawn initiation) and the `concurrent-invocation-isolation` block whose closing sentence references it by contrast ("the three potential sources of in-session overlap therefore produce genuine concurrency only between subagent-mode invocations"). The rule itself is wholly about *prompt-mode* bodies — that within one user session at most one prompt-mode body holds an open `pi.setActiveTools` snapshot/restore window — and is *derived* from three premises, none of which lives on `subagent.md`: (i) Pi's per-session slash-handler serialisation at `tool-registration-lifetime.md#snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions`, (ii) the load-time rejection of prompt-mode `.loom` callees in `tools:` at `frontmatter-fields-a.md`, and (iii) the parent-suspending `invoke(...)` rule and closed `subagent → prompt` fan-out in `invocation.md`.

A reader navigating the prompt-mode invocation surface — opening `conversation-drive.md` (which already hosts `PIC-18`, the prompt-mode turn-lifecycle event subscription), `tool-registration-lifetime.md` (which already hosts `PIC-17` / `PIC-19`, the snapshot/restore protocol PIC-2 derives from), or `invocation.md` (cross-mode semantics and the parallel-`invoke` non-surface) — finds no PIC-2 on any of those pages and no forward pointer to it. The rule is reachable only by anchor from the orientation aggregator `session-model-and-appendix.md` (`SM-7c`), from `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` (GOV-30's enumeration), and from the four plan leaves `V9c` / `V9c-T` / `V15d` / `V15d-T`; topic-proximity navigation misses it entirely.

## Solution Space

### Recommendation

Move the `<a id="pic-2">` (PIC-2 prompt-mode sequential execution) paragraph from `subagent.md` to `tool-registration-lifetime.md`, placing it immediately after `PIC-19` (the snapshot/swap-install-failure protocol), so the derived cross-body non-overlap property sits on the same page as the snapshot/restore contract (`PIC-17` / `PIC-19`) it is derived from.

Spec edits to make:

- Cut the PIC-2 paragraph from `subagent.md` (line 90) and insert it on `tool-registration-lifetime.md` after `PIC-19`. Preserve the `<a id="pic-2">` anchor string verbatim across the move so the inbound links continue to resolve under the new path.
- Inside the moved paragraph, rewrite the closing back-reference from "per **Concurrent-invocation isolation and concurrency disposition** above" (a same-page reference on `subagent.md`) to the cross-file form `[Subagent — Concurrent-invocation isolation](./subagent.md#concurrent-invocation-isolation)`; leaving it as a bold-text same-page reference would produce a dead reference under the new path.
- Inside the moved paragraph, rewrite the link to `snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions` from its current cross-file form to a same-page link, since that precondition now lives on the same page as PIC-2.
- Rewrite the closing sentence of `subagent.md`'s `concurrent-invocation-isolation` block from its same-file reference to PIC-2 into the cross-file form `[PIC-2](./tool-registration-lifetime.md#pic-2)`.
- Repoint the six inbound links — `session-model-and-appendix.md`'s `SM-7c`, GOV-30's enumeration in `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` (the canonical aggregator pointer), and the **Spec** lines of plan leaves `V9c` / `V9c-T` / `V15d` / `V15d-T` — from `subagent.md#pic-2` to `tool-registration-lifetime.md#pic-2`. The four plan-leaf **Spec** lines spell the path explicitly and must be updated in the same commit as the spec move; a grep for `subagent.md#pic-2` across `docs/` after the edit must return zero hits.
- Add a one-sentence forward pointer near `PIC-18` on `conversation-drive.md` so a reader on the prompt-mode driver surface still finds the rule (e.g. "see PIC-2 at `tool-registration-lifetime.md` for the cross-body non-overlap derivation").

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

Navigability defect only. The rule is fully stated and operative — every inbound citation reaches it by anchor — but a reader who arrives at the prompt-mode surface by topic does not find it where they would expect, and a future contributor editing the snapshot/restore protocol or the prompt-mode driver has no local reminder that PIC-2 depends on those rules. No observable behaviour changes; no two reasonable implementers would diverge.

## Finding

The *Slash-command entry* bullet under **Forwarding into `loomAbort`** in `docs/spec_topics/cancellation.md` justifies its `MUST tolerate ctx.signal being undefined at slash-command entry` rule with the inline parenthetical "Pi documents `ctx.signal` as `undefined` in idle, non-turn contexts (which is exactly when the slash-command handler fires)". That sentence is stated as a bare fact about Pi with no citation — no Pi-SDK pin reference, no JSDoc quotation, no anchor link.

The fact itself is correct and is already pinned elsewhere in the corpus. `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-core.md` §*Cancellation source* (anchor `#cancellation-source`) pins both the type — `ExtensionContext.signal: AbortSignal | undefined` in `dist/core/extensions/types.d.ts` at the loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin — and the verbatim JSDoc *"current abort signal, or undefined when the agent is not streaming"*, and draws the same "slash-command handler fires from idle" conclusion the cancellation page relies on. The defect is therefore a missing cross-reference at the cancellation-page site, not a missing piece of analysis: a reader of `cancellation.md` has no way to audit the inline assertion against the PIC pin that owns it, and a future Pi-SDK bump that altered the JSDoc would update the PIC page without touching this dangling parallel claim, drifting them silently.

The defensive tolerance MUST itself is independently sound and not in dispute — the cancellation page can keep that obligation unconditionally without depending on the behavioural claim.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/spec_topics/cancellation.md`, in the *Slash-command entry* bullet under **Forwarding into `loomAbort`**, replace the bare parenthetical "Pi documents `ctx.signal` as `undefined` in idle, non-turn contexts (which is exactly when the slash-command handler fires)" with a cross-reference to the PIC pin that owns the claim:

> The runtime MUST tolerate `ctx.signal` being `undefined` at slash-command entry — per the JSDoc pinned at [Pi Integration Contract — Cancellation source](./pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-core.md#cancellation-source) (*"current abort signal, or undefined when the agent is not streaming"*), and a slash-command handler fires from idle — and MUST NOT depend on its truthiness for any pre-turn checkpoint.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- Keep the MUST unconditional. The tolerance is a defensive correctness invariant in its own right; do not rewrite it to be conditional on the PIC claim ("MUST tolerate iff Pi documents undefined…"), which would create a brittle dependency on the JSDoc wording.
- Use the existing anchor `#cancellation-source` on `host-interfaces-core.md`; do not introduce a new anchor.
- Do not move the JSDoc quotation into `cancellation.md` as a second verbatim copy — the version-bump checklist re-audits the JSDoc against the pinned `.d.ts` at one site (the PIC page); a second verbatim copy would re-introduce the drift hazard this finding is fixing.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Implementers can still build the correct behaviour from the unconditional MUST. The cost is auditability and drift: a reviewer who notices the inline assertion cannot trace it to its source, and a Pi-SDK bump that altered the JSDoc wording (e.g. removed the idle-context guarantee) would update `host-interfaces-core.md` and the version-bump checklist while leaving the parallel claim in `cancellation.md` silently stale.

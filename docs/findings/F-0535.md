## Problem

`V9h`'s **Adds** field lists "the four-arm routing" with no antecedent naming which four arms are meant, one sentence away from the prose splitting the `new`/`resume`/`fork` degraded branch out to `V9l`. A reader scanning the field can reconcile the unqualified term with the adjacent session-reason prose and conclude the four arms route over the session-shutdown reason set — and that `V9h` therefore owns only three of them, the fourth having moved to `V9l`. That reading is wrong: the four arms are the snapshot-lookup-failure → `details.failure` discriminator arms (per `unknown-reason-rule.md` *Lookup-failure-to-discriminator routing*), all owned by `V9h`.

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** c6a664e — pi-loom plan: build/update plan for spec.md + review (2026-06-10, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The V9h leaf was created in c6a664e with the unqualified "the four-arm routing" already in its **Adds** parenthetical, adjacent to session-reason prose ("…and the session-only degraded state for `new`/`resume`/`fork`…"). The leaf-over-bundling fix cb8737a (2026-06-12) split the degraded branch out to V9l and rewrote the surrounding sentence, and b28e62f (2026-06-16) renamed the file, but neither changed the unqualified term or its proximity to the session-reason text, so the collision has persisted since inception.

## Solution approach

Qualify the bare phrase "the four-arm routing" in `V9h`'s **Adds** field so it unambiguously names the snapshot-lookup-failure → `details.failure` discriminator routing rather than a routing over session reasons, citing the `unknown-reason-rule.md` *Lookup-failure-to-discriminator routing* clause that pins those four arms.

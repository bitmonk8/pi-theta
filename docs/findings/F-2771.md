## Finding

`V7d` (and its paired `V7d-T`) carries three distinct deliverables in one leaf. The first is the core `loom-system-note` delivery surface: the `sendMessage` envelope with its renderer registration, the per-`.loom` multi-error batching (one `sendMessage` carrying the full `Diagnostic[]` assembled by `V7a`), the re-scan re-emit behaviour, and the producer-facing **diagnostic-emission seam** that parse/load/runtime producers (e.g. the `V1a` lexer) call. The second is the `PIC-21` renderer-throw trap: when the renderer body throws, the throw must not escape the `MessageRenderer` invocation and a minimal `Component` is returned instead. The third is the `PIC-54` `system-note-delivery-failed` fallback chain ‚Äî `sendMessage` ‚Üí `ctx.ui.notify` ‚Üí terminal `console.error` ‚Äî with at least five sub-cases (display/empty-content skip, throwing-`ctx.ui.notify` catch-and-proceed, terminal-`console.error` self-wrapped swallow, no-abort continuation).

The two resilience deliverables are red-testable in complete isolation from the core delivery path: the `PIC-21` trap exercises only the renderer body throwing, and the `PIC-54` chain exercises only `sendMessage` throwing on emission. Neither depends on the batching/seam machinery being exercised in the same leaf. The `conventions.md` ¬ß3 granularity rule states each vertical leaf is "the smallest feature that can ship and be tested independently"; `V7d` aggregates three such features. The coverage matrix already tracks the two resilience concerns as their own rows (`PIC-21 ‚Üí V7d`, `PIC-54 ‚Üí V7d`), confirming they are distinct closing obligations rather than facets of the core delivery.

Every existing consumer of `V7d` (`V1a`, `V9a`, `V9d`, `V9k`, `V9p`, `V9q`, and the `H5b` canary set) depends on it solely for the producer-facing diagnostic-emission seam ‚Äî i.e. the core delivery path, not the resilience internals ‚Äî so narrowing `V7d` to the core and peeling the resilience paths onto a new leaf leaves those Deps edges valid.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A faithful implementer can still build all three deliverables, but the bundled leaf produces a larger single TDD increment whose `Ships when` gate conflates core delivery with two resilience paths, weakening the per-leaf red-then-green discipline `conventions.md` ¬ß3 prescribes. The granularity defect degrades reviewability and increases diff size; it does not block pickup or cause divergent behaviour.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Keep `V7d` as the core-delivery leaf and peel the two resilience paths onto a new leaf.

- In `docs/plan_topics/V7d-system-note-channel.md`, narrow `Adds` to the `loom-system-note` envelope + renderer registration, the per-`.loom` multi-error batching, the re-scan re-emit, and the producer-facing diagnostic-emission seam; strike the `PIC-21` renderer-throw trap and the `sendSystemNote ‚Üí ctx.ui.notify ‚Üí console.error` fallback-chain clauses. Strike the corresponding `PIC-21` and `system-note-delivery-failed` (`PIC-54`) `Tests` bullets, leaving the multi-error-batch-delivery and re-scan-re-emit bullets. Narrow `Ships when` to the batched-envelope delivery criterion only (drop "exercises the `PIC-21` renderer-throw trap and the delivery-failure fallback chain").
- Make the matching `Tests` deletions in `docs/plan_topics/V7d-T-system-note-channel.md`.
- Add a new leaf (`<new>`, with its paired `<new>-T`) under ¬ßV7 in `docs/plan.md` owning the `PIC-21` renderer-throw trap and the `PIC-54` fallback chain. Its `Deps` include `V7d` (it consumes the delivery surface it traps/falls-back around) and `H4a`. Move the struck `PIC-21` and `system-note-delivery-failed` `Tests` bullets verbatim onto it.
- In `docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md`, retarget the `PIC-21` and `PIC-54` closing-leaf cells from `` `V7d` `` to the new leaf's ID.
- Add the new leaf to `H5b`'s coverage-producing Deps set, per `conventions.md` How-to-use step 2 (a new leaf closing coverage-matrix-mapped REQ-IDs joins `H5b.Deps`).

The existing `V7d` consumers (`V1a`, `V9a`, `V9d`, `V9k`, `V9p`, `V9q`) depend on the producer-facing seam that stays on `V7d`, so their `Deps` edges need no change.

## Finding

`active-invocation-registry.md` specifies that all three insertion sites populate the entry's `loom` field via the **same canonical-name resolver**, which *reads* the callee's final-derived slash-name from the resolved catalog entry — "the slash-name field the [Invocation — Static resolution] cache entry and the [Parameters and Frontmatter — `tools`] snapshot entry **already carry** on those entries". For slash-registered looms this grounds out cleanly against the `LoomRegistry` `Map<slashName, parsedLoom>` key. For the two callee-only arms it does not: neither cited owner declares such a field.

- `invocation.md` §Static resolution defines the per-load-pass parse cache as holding the callee's parsed/lowered source (for argument-type checking, return-type inference, cycle detection) and its aggregated diagnostics. No slash-name / canonical-key field is declared on a cache entry.
- `frontmatter-fields-a.md` §`tools` defines the resolution snapshot's entry **name** as the in-loom callable name — the basename with hyphens replaced by underscores (`./code-review.loom` → `code_review`), or the `as` override. That is *not* the slash-name the registry needs: the Discovery final-derived form takes the stem **verbatim** (hyphens preserved, no leading `/`). The only name the snapshot declares is the underscore-remapped callable name.

So the registry asserts a field exists on two entry shapes whose owning pages either declare no such field (the parse cache) or declare a *different* name under the only name field they do carry (the `tools:` snapshot). Compounding the gap, the resolver pins a mechanism — "the `loom` field is **read** from the resolved catalog entry's slash-name field rather than constructed at the insertion site" — even though it concedes re-derivation "would produce the identical canonical form". The only observable contract is that all three arms set `details.event.loom` to the same final-derived canonical form; the read-vs-construct choice is not observable.

**History:** The `ActiveInvocationRegistry` symbol predates this commit, but commit 52b9101 authored the `loom`-field canonical-name resolver block — introducing both the assertion that the static-resolution cache entry and the `tools:` resolution snapshot entry "already carry" the slash-name field (pickaxe on "same canonical-name resolver" and "already carry" first lands here) and the "read … rather than constructed at the insertion site" prescription. The later size-cap split (f5e89f4, 2026-06-04) relocated the block verbatim into `active-invocation-registry.md`; the cited owner pages (`invocation.md` §Static resolution, `frontmatter-fields-a.md` §`tools`) were never updated to declare the presupposed field.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Declare the canonical slash-name field on the cited owner entries

- **Approach:** Make the assertion true. Add a normative slash-name (final-derived) field to the static-resolution cache entry shape and to the frozen `tools:` resolution snapshot entry, declared distinct from the underscore-remapped callable name; keep the registry's read-the-field mechanism grounded against those declarations.
- **Spec edits:** `invocation.md` §Static resolution — state each cache entry carries the callee's final-derived slash-name (post-`pi.looms` mapping, stem verbatim, no leading `/`). `frontmatter-fields-a.md` §`tools` — state the resolution snapshot entry carries the same field, explicitly separate from the in-loom callable name. `active-invocation-registry.md` — leave the "read" wording, now pointing at declared fields.
- **Pros:** Preserves the existing resolver mechanism; makes the cross-reference truthful at the owner sites.
- **Cons:** Adds a field to two entry shapes no other consumer needs; the `tools:` snapshot already carries an underscore-remapped name, so a second verbatim-stem field invites name confusion. Wide surface (three pages, two leaf entry shapes).
- **Risks:** The new field must be specified as the verbatim stem (no hyphen→underscore remap), sitting directly beside the callable-name remap rule it contradicts — easy to mis-implement as the same value.

### Option B — Reframe the resolver contract behaviourally and demote "read, not construct"

- **Approach:** State only the observable obligation — all three insertion arms set `details.event.loom` to the same final-derived canonical form per Discovery — and have the resolver obtain that form from the resolved callee (the `LoomRegistry` key for slash-registered looms; the Discovery final-derived form of the resolved callee for callee-only `.loom` files) rather than presupposing a field on the cache/snapshot entries. Demote "read, not construct" to a non-normative note.
- **Spec edits:** `active-invocation-registry.md` — replace the "both of which carry the same canonical-key field" / "already carry on those entries" / "read … rather than constructed" assertions with a single behavioural contract (all arms' `details.event.loom` equal the final-derived canonical form per Discovery), and move the read-vs-construct preference into a non-normative implementation note. No edits to `invocation.md` or `frontmatter-fields-a.md`.
- **Pros:** Removes the dependency on undeclared fields; confines the change to the page that owns the resolver; yields a directly testable contract (equality of the wire value across the three arms).
- **Cons:** Leaves the per-arm derivation source implicit — implementers must apply the Discovery final-derived rule themselves.
- **Risks:** The non-normative note must still forbid arm-specific re-derivation that diverges on `pi.looms` mapping order, or the dedup-bucket split re-emerges.

### Recommendation

Option B. The only observable property is that all three arms agree on the final-derived canonical form — which the page already asserts — and the page itself concedes re-derivation "would produce the identical canonical form"; pinning a read-mechanism on a field three owners must carry adds coupling the contract does not need and that two of those owners do not honour. Keep a non-normative note requiring every arm to apply the identical Discovery final-derived rule (post-`pi.looms` mapping, stem verbatim, no leading `/`) so two implementations cannot converge on different forms.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two reasonable implementers diverge on the `details.event.loom` wire value for the callee-only arms: one looks for the asserted slash-name field and, finding only the `tools:` snapshot's underscore-remapped callable name, emits that (`code_review`); another applies the Discovery final-derived rule and emits the verbatim stem (`code-review`). The registry exists to keep `details.event.loom` identical across all three arms so operators can dedup on it; an undeclared/mis-identified source field splits those dedup buckets between conforming implementations — exactly the failure the resolver was written to prevent.

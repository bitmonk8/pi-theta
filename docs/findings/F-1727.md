## Finding

The glossary entry for `respond-repair` in `docs/spec_topics/glossary.md` (line 21) coins the canonical hyphenation `respond-repair` ("Hyphenated as `respond-repair` in prose; underscored as a YAML key") and then immediately violates it in its own last sentence: "the response-repair loop above is the only 'repair' mechanism the spec defines." The variant has an extra `e` (`response-` rather than `respond-`), which is the precise spelling the entry exists to forbid. The defect is one occurrence of the wrong spelling inside the entry that defines the rule.

The same trailing clause is the only place in `docs/spec_topics/glossary.md` and the only place in `docs/spec.md` that uses the misspelt variant, but it is the most visible one: it sits inside the definition that grep gates and other naming references treat as authoritative. A second, separate occurrence lives in `docs/spec_topics/frontmatter/frontmatter-fields-b-and-templates.md` ("The block configures **respond-repair** — the response-repair mechanism specified in [Query — Schema-validation respond-repair](...)"), surfaced as its own finding (see Related Findings). They share a fix shape but resolve at independent sites.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In the `respond-repair` glossary entry in `docs/spec_topics/glossary.md`, change the trailing clause "the response-repair loop above is the only 'repair' mechanism the spec defines" to "the respond-repair loop above is the only 'repair' mechanism the spec defines." (drop the `e` from `response-`). No other text in the entry needs to change; the canonical-hyphenation sentence and the YAML-key contrast already stand. Do not touch the `frontmatter-fields-b-and-templates.md` occurrence in this fix — it is a sibling finding (see Related Findings) and resolves on its own page.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader following the entry's own MUST-style hyphenation rule encounters the forbidden spelling one clause later; the contradiction is recognisable on sight and forces no implementation divergence, but it weakens the glossary's authority as the source of truth for the term.

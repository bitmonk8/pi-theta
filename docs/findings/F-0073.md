## Finding

The third closing-gate surface — a normative MUST/MUST-NOT on a non-narrative `spec_topics/**` page carrying neither a numbered `PREFIX-N` REQ-ID nor a `loom/...` registry code — is named two different ways across the two cross-cutting plan files that define it. `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline calls it the "un-anchored-obligation **class** [`governance.md` GOV-22] names"; `coverage-matrix.md` §"Code-keyed obligation areas (no numbered REQ-IDs)" calls it the "un-anchored-obligation **residue** under [`governance.md` GOV-22]". No inline equivalence statement links the two phrasings.

Both attributions point at the same GOV-22 anchor, and GOV-22 itself uses neither coinage as a defined term — its prose says the rule "drains that **residue**", so "residue" both matches the spec's own language and is the prevalent form across the corpus (the GOV-22 closing-gate rows in `coverage-matrix.md`, plus H5a/V9f/V9l, all say "GOV-22 residue"). The lone "class" usage in `conventions.md` is the outlier. A reader cross-referencing the two files cannot mechanically confirm "class" and "residue" denote one surface rather than two.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-by-change (single commit)
**Introducing commits:** `0603eb4` — "pi-loom plan: resolve \"un-anchored normative MUSTs invisible to closing gate\"" (2026-06-10)
**History:** `git log -S "un-anchored-obligation class" -- docs/plan_topics/conventions.md` and `git log -S "un-anchored-obligation residue" -- docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md` each return exactly `0603eb4` and no later edit. Both coinages were authored in the same commit that introduced the third closing-gate surface; the divergent terminology ("class" in `conventions.md`, "residue" in `coverage-matrix.md`) was born together in that change rather than drifting apart over time.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline, replace the single occurrence of `un-anchored-obligation class` with `un-anchored-obligation residue`, so the sentence reads "… is the un-anchored-obligation residue [`governance.md` GOV-22](../spec_topics/governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md#gov-22) names." `coverage-matrix.md` already carries the canonical "residue" form and the spec's GOV-22 prose uses "residue"; leave both untouched. This converges the corpus on one term without adding an equivalence sentence.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader of `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline sees "un-anchored-obligation class" while `coverage-matrix.md` and the rest of the corpus say "residue", with no equivalence sentence; momentary ambiguity over whether one or two GOV-22 surfaces exist. Gate behaviour and leaf acceptance are unaffected — the operative obligation is fully specified by the surrounding text in both files.

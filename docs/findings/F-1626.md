## Finding

The *Authoritative source* sentence of each behavioural-presupposition paragraph closes with a near-verbatim parenthetical of the form:

> (**known-fragile evidence**: this bundler-output path is a diffable artefact at the pin rather than a load-bearing claim about its stable shape across patches; a rename or re-bundle routes item (X)'s textual-diffability failure to the established version-bump recovery path).

The clause appears ~8 times across the PIC presupposition surface, differing only in the checklist letter and in `path` / `paths` agreement:
- `host-prerequisites.md` — 7 sites, items (k), (s), (ao), (l), (z), (ap), (h).
- `conversation-drive.md` — 2 sites, items (u) and (aa)/(ab).
- `host-interfaces-services.md` — 1 site, item (w).
- `subagent.md` — 1 site, item (y).

(Three further sites — the original carrier at `tool-registration-lifetime.md#snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions`, the renderer-resolution narration at `extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md#renderer-registration`, and the pre-bind-throw-closure narration at `registration-steps.md#pre-bind-throw-closure-evidence` — already inline the framing in their own bespoke shapes and are not part of the copy-paste cluster.)

The posture this clause encodes — that a cited `dist/*.js` path is loom-1.0-pin diffable evidence, not a stable-shape contract, and that a textual-locatability failure of any such path routes the matching checklist item through the established version-bump recovery path — is one rule, stated identically at every site. Restating it inline at each presupposition makes already-large paragraphs harder to skim and obscures the per-paragraph differentiator (the checklist letter, the named SDK symbol).

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Define the known-fragile-evidence posture **once** as a named, anchored paragraph and replace each per-site parenthetical with a one-line backreference.

Concretely:

1. Author a short, anchored paragraph in `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/host-prerequisites.md` (recommended; it is the canonical home of the presuppositions that carry the clause, and `version-bump-step2.md` already cross-references this page). Give it an `<a id="known-fragile-evidence-posture"></a>` anchor and state the rule once, in something close to the following shape — wording is editorial, the load-bearing content is the two clauses already inlined verbatim today:

   > **`dist/*.js` path citations — known-fragile evidence posture.** Any `dist/*.js` path or bundled-internal symbol the presuppositions on this page (and elsewhere in PIC) cite is **known-fragile evidence** at the loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin: a diffable artefact at the pin, not a load-bearing claim about its stable shape across patches. A Pi patch that renames, re-bundles, minifies, or relocates such a citation routes the failure of the corresponding editorial-review checklist item's textual-diffability check to the recovery path that item already enumerates under [Pi version bump procedure](./version-bump-intro.md#pi-version-bump-procedure) — it does not invalidate the loom-normative observable rule the citation accompanies.

2. At each of the ~8 sites that today carry the verbatim parenthetical, **replace the parenthetical** with a terse one-liner that names the citation and the routed checklist item, e.g.

   > … is observed at `ModelRegistry.getAvailable()` in `dist/core/model-registry.js` (known-fragile evidence per the [posture above](#known-fragile-evidence-posture); textual-locatability failure routes through item (k) of the editorial-review checklist).

   The per-site one-liner keeps three pieces of differentiating information — the SDK symbol, the bundler-output path, and the checklist letter — and drops everything that is identical across sites.

3. Do **not** also rewrite the three pre-existing bespoke carriers (`tool-registration-lifetime.md#snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions`, the renderer-resolution narration under `extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md#renderer-registration`, and the pre-bind-throw-closure narration under `registration-steps.md#pre-bind-throw-closure-evidence`). They are not copy-paste sites: each inlines the posture as part of a larger argument (multi-symbol evidence trail, observable-rules-hold-independent framing, per-driver audit recipe), so collapsing them to the one-liner would lose load-bearing content. The new anchored paragraph backreferences these three sites as fellow consumers of the same posture; they backreference it in return for the routing-rule clause.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- Each per-site one-liner MUST still name **its own checklist letter** ((k), (s), (ao), (l), (z), (ap), (h), (u), (aa)/(ab), (w), (y)); the version-bump-step2 audit recipe is keyed on those letters and dropping them breaks the routing.
- The wording around `path is` / `paths are` (singular vs plural — items (l) and (h) cite two paths each, item (aa)/(ab) covers two checklist letters) must be preserved per-site; the consolidated definition is written so the per-site one-liner can stay neutrally singular ("the citation routes …").
- The relocation does not affect the *Authoritative source* sentence's own load-bearing content (the named SDK symbol, the bundler-output path); only the trailing parenthetical is replaced.

## Consequence

Nothing observable to an implementer changes: the routing rule is correctly stated everywhere it appears.

## Finding

The `V4e` and `V4g` leaves name the same concept two ways. Each leaf heading and `Adds.` opener uses the canonical term **pre-evaluation** (`# `V4e` — Pre-evaluation failures`, `# `V4g` — Pre-evaluation reload-failure integration`), and the authoritative spec page `errors-and-results/error-model.md` uses **pre-evaluation** exclusively (four occurrences; the abbreviation "pre-eval" never appears there). But the `Tests.` bullets and `Ships when.` lines of both leaves — and their paired `-T` tests files — abbreviate to **pre-eval** ("routes pre-eval", "load-time pre-eval routing causes", "the load-time pre-eval routing surface").

The two terms denote one concept, so there is no semantic defect, but the inconsistency is a divergence surface: a reader or a future cross-referencing leaf cannot rely on a single grep token to find every pre-evaluation routing assertion, and the abbreviation is not anchored anywhere as an accepted synonym.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** `c6a664e` (V4e), `324813d` (V4g)
**History:** The plan corpus is git-tracked. `git log -S"pre-eval"` shows `V4e-pre-evaluation-failures.md` carried the mixed spelling from its creation in `c6a664e` ("pi-loom plan: build/update plan for spec.md + review", 2026-06-10). `V4g-pre-evaluation-reload-failure.md` was created later in `324813d` ("pi-loom plan: resolve 'split ERR-7 reload scenario out of V4e routing checks'", 2026-06-18), inheriting the abbreviated prose from V4e at the moment it was split out. The defect was thus present at each file's inception rather than introduced by a later targeted edit.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In each of the four files, replace the bare abbreviation "pre-eval" with "pre-evaluation" so the body prose matches the leaf headings and the `error-model.md` spelling. Concretely:

- `V4e-pre-evaluation-failures.md` and `V4e-T-pre-evaluation-failures.md` — in the `Tests.` bullets change "host-incompatible pre-eval failure routes" → "host-incompatible pre-evaluation failure routes" and each "routes pre-eval" → "routes pre-evaluation" (ERR-1…ERR-6, ERR-16); in `Ships when.` change "all seven load-time pre-eval routing causes" → "all seven load-time pre-evaluation routing causes".
- `V4g-pre-evaluation-reload-failure.md` and `V4g-T-pre-evaluation-reload-failure.md` — change "the load-time pre-eval routing surface" → "the load-time pre-evaluation routing surface" (in `Adds.` and `Deps.`) and "routes pre-eval to `loom-system-note`" → "routes pre-evaluation to `loom-system-note`" (in `Tests.` and `Ships when.`).

Leave the hyphenated compound modifier "pre-evaluation" intact where it already appears. The `-T` partner files mirror the implementation leaves' prose, so sweep both halves of each pair in the same change to keep the paired tasks consistent.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

Pure wording inconsistency; no implementer diverges on behaviour. The only cost is a weaker grep/cross-reference surface — searches keyed on "pre-evaluation" miss the abbreviated bullets, and the abbreviation could propagate further without a single anchored spelling.

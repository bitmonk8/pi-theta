## Finding

`host-prerequisites.md` carries a sub-section, anchored `#clause-a-contradiction-narrative` and headed **"Clause (a) contradiction narrative (skippable decision-log detail)"**, whose own opening sentence classifies it as "decision-log narrative, not load-bearing normative text — the gate obligation lives in clause (a) and the recording contract in item (a)'s authoritative-determination recording shape, so a reader tracking only the contract may skip it." It then traces the evidentiary investigation behind clause (a)'s open-contradiction classification: Pi's `docs/extensions.md` "Session replacement lifecycle and footguns" behaviour, the closed-over `LoomRegistry` survival argument, and the CLAUDE.md no-globals rule as an escape-hatch foreclosure.

This is expository decision-log prose living in the middle of a normative host-prerequisites contract page. The normative load is already carried elsewhere: the gate obligation is stated in clause (a) of the *Host prerequisites for the degraded-state branch* block above, and the recording contract is owned by `version-bump-step2.md#bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions`. The narrative contributes no requirement an implementer must satisfy — it is rationale for why clause (a) is classified an open contradiction rather than an unpinned presupposition. Its presence in a normative file forces every reader of the prerequisites contract to recognise and skip it, exactly the cost the page's own "may skip it" caveat concedes.

## Solution Space

### Recommendation

In `host-prerequisites.md`, condense the `#clause-a-contradiction-narrative` sub-section in place: delete the expository investigation walk-through and replace it with a one- or two-sentence evidentiary note under the same anchor. The note states that clause (a) is an open contradiction because Pi's documented teardown-and-rebind lifecycle (`@earendil-works/pi-coding-agent` `docs/extensions.md`, "Session replacement lifecycle and footguns") rebinds a fresh `LoomRegistry` on a session-only swap, and the CLAUDE.md no-globals rule forecloses any surviving handle — for example: *"Clause (a) is an open contradiction because Pi's documented teardown-and-rebind lifecycle (`@earendil-works/pi-coding-agent` `docs/extensions.md`, \"Session replacement lifecycle and footguns\") rebinds a fresh `LoomRegistry` on session-only swap, and the no-globals rule forecloses any surviving handle."*

The condensed note must preserve the `docs/extensions.md` "Session replacement lifecycle and footguns" citation and the "fresh non-drained `LoomRegistry`" conclusion — those are the evidentiary kernel an editorial reviewer relies on at each Pi minor bump. Retain the existing anchor so inbound cross-references continue to resolve. Do not introduce a separate decision-log or ADR file; keep the note inline on the page.

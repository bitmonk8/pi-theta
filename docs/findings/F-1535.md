## Finding

The host-prerequisites clause-(a) open question — whether loom's package-loaded `extensions/index.ts` survives a session-only `session_shutdown` — is recorded with substantial investigation prose at two normative sites and re-cited at a third:

1. **`host-prerequisites.md` — *Host prerequisites for the degraded-state branch*, clause (a)** (≈600 words inside a single bullet): the body narrates the contradiction against `@earendil-works/pi-coding-agent` `docs/extensions.md` ("Session replacement lifecycle and footguns"), enumerates Pi's documented teardown-and-rebind verdicts, names the no-globals rule that rules out an escape hatch, lays out the two dispositive verdicts (`governed-by-rebind` / `provably-differs`), and points at `version-bump-step2.md#bump-checklist-instance-survival` for the recording shape. Most of this is decision-log narrative, not a normative requirement an implementer or reviewer needs to read at each visit.
2. **`version-bump-step2.md` — checklist item (a)** carries an "**Open at the loom 1.0 pin, not merely a future-minor re-audit**" preamble (≈150 words) that re-states the contradiction inside what is otherwise a uniform-shape MUST/SHOULD per-bump checklist row, then defers the actual recording shape to the *Outcome recording conventions* block at the bottom of the same file. Item (a)'s row is thereby ~10× the size of its sibling rows and breaks the per-bump audit-row reading pattern.
3. **`session-only-degraded-state.md`** opens with an *Implementation gate* blockquote that cites both anchors again to enumerate which clauses on the page are gated.

The substantive recording-shape contract (two verdicts, evidentiary standard, acceptance criterion) has already been factored into the *Outcome recording conventions* block at the foot of `version-bump-step2.md` (the `bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions` anchor), so the two consuming sites no longer need to carry it inline. What remains in clause (a) and item (a) is now narrative explanation rather than load-bearing normative text: the only thing those sites need to convey to a reader is the gate ("the degraded-state branch MUST NOT be implemented until item (a)'s authoritative-determination record resolves to `provably-differs`") and a single pointer to the canonical investigation home.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Pick `host-prerequisites.md` as the single canonical home for the contradiction narrative (it is where the open question semantically lives, and `version-bump-step2.md` already houses the recording-shape contract in *Outcome recording conventions*). Then:

1. In `host-prerequisites.md`, replace clause (a)'s body with a 1–3 sentence normative paragraph that:
   - States the gate: "the degraded-state branch MUST NOT be implemented until item (a)'s authoritative-determination record resolves to `provably-differs`";
   - Cites `docs/extensions.md` ("Session replacement lifecycle and footguns") once as the source of the contradiction;
   - Points to a clearly-titled sibling sub-section (e.g. `<a id="degraded-state-clause-a-open-question"></a>`*Open question — extension-instance survival across session-only `session_shutdown`*) immediately below the (a)–(d) list, into which the existing ~600-word investigation prose moves verbatim (no content lost, just relocated out of the normative bullet body and into a distinct sub-section a reader can skip).
2. In `version-bump-step2.md`, replace item (a)'s "**Open at the loom 1.0 pin, not merely a future-minor re-audit**" preamble with one sentence that names the item as carrying the *one-shot authoritative-determination recording shape*, points at *Outcome recording conventions* → *Item (a) authoritative-determination recording shape* for the verdict / evidentiary-standard / acceptance-criterion contract, and points at the new `host-prerequisites.md#degraded-state-clause-a-open-question` sub-section for the contradiction narrative. The recording-conventions block already covers the rest.
3. Leave `session-only-degraded-state.md`'s *Implementation gate* blockquote alone — its two cross-refs continue to resolve to the same anchors and the blockquote is itself the page's normative gate, not a third citation of the investigation prose.

Edge cases for the implementer:
- Preserve the `bump-checklist-instance-survival` anchor verbatim — `plan.md` §Blocked obligations, `V9l-session-only-degraded-branch.md`, and the `session-only-degraded-state.md` blockquote all cite it.
- Preserve the `degraded-state-host-prerequisites` anchor verbatim for the same reason.
- The new sub-section's anchor (`degraded-state-clause-a-open-question` above is illustrative) is added — no existing site cites it, so the name is free.
- Do not change the gate's semantics: clause (a) remains MUST-resolve-before-SM-4/5/6, item (a) remains a one-shot authoritative-determination record (not the recurring per-bump pass/fail row shape), and the two-verdict / evidentiary-standard / acceptance-criterion contract in *Outcome recording conventions* is unchanged.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader of `host-prerequisites.md` scanning the four-clause (a)–(d) list hits a 600-word decision-log paragraph where the other three clauses are crisp 1–2 sentence presuppositions; a reader of the (a)–(ap) bump checklist hits one 10×-sized row that breaks the uniform row shape. Both are readability/maintenance gaps rather than implementability blockers — `V9l` and `V18c` already encode the gate correctly — but every future revision of the decision-log narrative (e.g. when item (a) resolves) has to be applied twice and kept in sync with the *Outcome recording conventions* third site.

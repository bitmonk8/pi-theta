## Finding

`runtime-event-channel.md` carries a real operator-facing observability NFR: every `loom-system-note` `CustomMessage` — including `display: false` notes — is converted by Pi's `convertToLlm` transformer into a `{ role: "user", content }` entry on every subsequent provider call, with no opt-out under the loom 1.0 SDK pin. The practical consequence for operators is that parse errors, binder failures, panic notes, and always-log runtime events durably consume context budget (they feed `ctx.getContextUsage()` and compaction) for the remainder of the session. This is an operator/observability obligation, not a wire-call detail.

It is currently stated inside the `## Runtime event channel` page interleaved with implementation-facing call-shape prose. The substance lives in the `Custom-message channel persistence and LLM-context entry` paragraph wedged between the `**Delivery surface.**` paragraph (which describes the single `pi.sendMessage` envelope) and the `**Empty `content` is legal.**` paragraph, plus a non-normative restatement further down in the `**Runtime event channel.**` block. Both occurrences sit under headings that advertise SDK call mechanics, not observability. An operator scanning the page for "what do I need to know about context growth" has no heading or subsection that names the concern; the durable-context budget consequence and the no-opt-out fact are reachable only by reading SDK-transform prose end to end.

The information is present and correct — this is an organization defect, not a content gap. Collecting the durable-context / no-opt-out / budget-growth material under a clearly-labelled observability (operator-notes) subsection, separated from the `pi.sendMessage` envelope text, makes the obligation discoverable to the audience it addresses.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add a dedicated, clearly-labelled observability/operator-notes subsection on `runtime-event-channel.md` (e.g. `### Operator-facing context-budget note` or under a `## Observability` heading) and gather the durable-context material there: every `loom-system-note` (regardless of `display`) enters the LLM context as a `user`-role entry on every subsequent provider call, with no opt-out at the loom 1.0 pin, and therefore contributes durably to `ctx.getContextUsage()` and compaction. State the operator expectation (budget growth from emitted diagnostics/notes) in that subsection.

Keep the `convertToLlm` SDK-mechanism detail (the transformer reference, `triggerTurn: false` not excluding context, the `excludeFromContext` analogy and re-audit hook) as the mechanism backing the subsection — either inside it or demoted to a non-normative implementation note — so the `**Delivery surface.**` paragraph and the per-variant `display`/`content` matrix carry only call-shape text.

Preserve the existing `<a id="custom-message-context-entry-presupposition">` anchor on whichever paragraph retains the `convertToLlm` mechanism text — `version-bump-step2.md` checklist item (r) links to it. Cross-reference the new subsection from the `display: false` gating rule rather than restating the transform there.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

An operator-facing observability obligation (durable context-budget growth from every system note, no opt-out) is reachable only by reading SDK-mechanism prose, reducing its discoverability for the operator audience it targets. No implementer diverges and no behaviour changes; the content is fully present (and partly restated as a non-normative operator note).

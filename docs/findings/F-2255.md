## Problem

Within step 2 of the Pi version-bump procedure (`version-bump-step2.md`), parenthesised Latin letters label two distinct, independently-coined schemes: the editorial-review checklist items `(a)`–`(aq)`, and the two SDK surface-inventory test sub-steps `(a)` positive-direction / `(b)` negative-direction (anchors `bump-step-2-positive` / `bump-step-2-negative`). The `step 2(a)` shorthand — the spec's reference to the seven-capability literal-read assertion — recurs throughout the checklist items themselves, so a reader inside the checklist meets bare `(a)`/`(b)` (a checklist item) and `step 2(a)`/`step 2(b)` (an SDK-test sub-step) as two unrelated referents drawn from the same alphabet under the same step number. The checklist carries no step number of its own, so the "step 2" prefix does not separate the two schemes. The collision imposes interpretive burden and a standing mis-citation risk as the checklist grows.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `e5cd514` (2026-05-09, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit\""), `57d8235` (2026-05-11, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"T23 per-session slash-handler serialisation citation\"")
**History:** `git log -S` traces the two label schemes to two commits two days apart. `e5cd514` first introduced the SDK surface-inventory test's `(a)` *Positive direction* / `(b)` *Negative direction* lettering and the `step 2(a)`/`step 2(b)` shorthand (`git log -S 'Positive direction' --reverse` and `-S 'bump-step-2-positive'` both bottom out there). `57d8235` then introduced the *Editorial-review checklist for unpinned host presuppositions* with lettered items `(a)`–`(e)` in the same step-2 context (`git log -S 'Editorial-review checklist' --reverse`; `git show 57d8235` confirms the lettered checklist preamble was added there). Neither commit alone is defective: each label scheme is locally coherent. The collision is the interaction — both reuse parenthesised Latin letters inside step 2 without a disambiguating prefix. Later commits (down to `27a023e`, 2026-06-27) grew the checklist to `(aq)` and multiplied the `step 2(a)` references to 112 across ten files, aggravating the shared-namespace overlap but not introducing it.

## Solution approach

Relabel one of the two colliding schemes so they no longer share step 2's parenthesised-Latin-letter namespace; the SDK surface-inventory sub-steps are the smaller (two-item) scheme and the natural relabel target, reserving Latin letters for the editorial-review checklist. Propagate the relabelling to every `step 2(a)` / `step 2(b)` reference across the files named in the location list, preserving the Arabic `branch (N)` sub-structure cited on the `step 2(b)` references.

## Solution constraints

- Preserve the `bump-step-2-positive` and `bump-step-2-negative` HTML anchors unchanged; cross-page references resolve against them.
- Out of scope: assigning the editorial-review checklist its own step number in the overall 1–7 procedure (owned by F-2252 / F-2256).

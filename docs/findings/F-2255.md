## Finding

Within the single "step 2" context of `version-bump-step2.md`, parenthesised Latin letters label two distinct, independently-coined things:

1. The **editorial-review checklist** items, lettered `(a)` through `(aq)`. Item `(a)` is the *Extension-instance survival across session-only `session_shutdown`* open question (anchor `bump-checklist-instance-survival`); item `(b)` is *Multi-`session_shutdown` delivery permitted to a single instance*; and so on.
2. The **SDK surface-inventory test** sub-steps under the numbered "2. Re-run the SDK surface-inventory tests" item, which PIC pins as `(a)` *Positive direction — literal-read of the imported namespace* (anchor `bump-step-2-positive`) and `(b)` *Negative direction* (anchor `bump-step-2-negative`).

The phrase `step 2(a)` is the spec's shorthand for the seven-capability literal-read assertion (concept 2). It occurs 112 times across ten spec files, almost always inside the very checklist items `(f)`–`(aq)` whose own labels belong to scheme 1 (e.g. each item closes with "escalates to a build-time pin … on the same footing as the seven-capability literal-read assertion under step 2(a) above"). A reader sitting inside the checklist therefore encounters bare `(a)`/`(b)` (a checklist item) and `step 2(a)`/`step 2(b)` (an SDK-test sub-step) in the same paragraph, two unrelated referents drawn from the same parenthesised-letter alphabet under the same step number. The checklist itself carries no step number of its own, so the "step 2" prefix does not disambiguate scheme 2 from scheme 1 — both are step-2 content.

The defect is a naming collision in a shared label namespace, not an undefined reference: nearly every `step 2(a)` site spells out "the seven-capability literal-read assertion", and the checklist items are cited as "item (a)" rather than "step 2(a)", so context resolves the intended referent in practice. The cost is interpretive burden and a standing risk of mis-citation as the checklist grows.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `e5cd514` (2026-05-09, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit\""), `57d8235` (2026-05-11, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"T23 per-session slash-handler serialisation citation\"")
**History:** `git log -S` traces the two label schemes to two commits two days apart. `e5cd514` first introduced the SDK surface-inventory test's `(a)` *Positive direction* / `(b)` *Negative direction* lettering and the `step 2(a)`/`step 2(b)` shorthand (`git log -S 'Positive direction' --reverse` and `-S 'bump-step-2-positive'` both bottom out there). `57d8235` then introduced the *Editorial-review checklist for unpinned host presuppositions* with lettered items `(a)`–`(e)` in the same step-2 context (`git log -S 'Editorial-review checklist' --reverse`; `git show 57d8235` confirms the lettered checklist preamble was added there). Neither commit alone is defective: each label scheme is locally coherent. The collision is the interaction — both reuse parenthesised Latin letters inside step 2 without a disambiguating prefix. Later commits (down to `27a023e`, 2026-06-27) grew the checklist to `(aq)` and multiplied the `step 2(a)` references to 112 across ten files, aggravating the shared-namespace overlap but not introducing it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Relabel the two SDK surface-inventory test sub-steps in `version-bump-step2.md` from `(a)`/`(b)` to Roman numerals `(i)`/`(ii)`, reserving Latin letters exclusively for the editorial-review checklist. Change the two sub-step headings under "2. Re-run the SDK surface-inventory tests" so the positive-direction sub-step reads `(i)` and the negative-direction sub-step reads `(ii)`, keeping the `bump-step-2-positive` / `bump-step-2-negative` anchors unchanged.

Rewrite every `step 2(a)` reference to `step 2(i)` and every `step 2(b)` reference to `step 2(ii)` across all ten spec files in the location list (not just `version-bump-step2.md`): `version-bump-step2.md`, `version-bump-step2b.md`, `version-bump-triggers.md`, `version-bump-intro.md`, `inventory-audit-intro.md`, `audit-target-categories.md`, `audit-wire-and-canary.md`, `registration-steps.md`, `subagent.md`, and `overview-and-orientation.md`. After this change directions use Roman numerals `(i)`/`(ii)`, checklist items use Latin letters `(a)`–`(aq)`, and step-2(ii) branches use Arabic `(1)`–`(5)`, so the three coexisting label kinds are distinguishable by alphabet alone.

`step 2(b)` carries a `branch (N)` sub-structure cited extensively (e.g. `step 2(b) branch (2)`, `branch (5)`): rewrite the prefix to `step 2(ii) branch (N)` while leaving the Arabic branch numbers intact. Sweep every listed file so no `step 2(a)`/`step 2(b)` spelling survives anywhere in the corpus.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A version-bump contributor reading the editorial-review checklist meets two unrelated referents for `(a)` and `(b)` in the same step-2 namespace, raising the risk of citing or resolving the wrong one. Because nearly every `step 2(a)` site also names "the seven-capability literal-read assertion" and the checklist items are cited as "item (a)", a careful implementer can still build the correct gates; the harm is interpretive burden and citation fragility rather than divergent behaviour.

## Problem

The `#session-shutdown-details-conventions` block in `diagnostic-shape.md` is a single unbroken paragraph of roughly 1,400 words bundling five independently-enforceable normative sub-topics for the `session_shutdown` event payload: the `{reason}` field-set closure at loom 1.0.0, the per-code `details.event.reason` value spaces, the runtime-constructed sibling carve-out for `details.event.loom` / `details.event.invocation_id`, the presence-entitlement scope distinguishing structured emissions from the fallback-path token shapes, and the runtime construction obligation. These MUST-bearing rules are demarcated only by inline italic run-in labels embedded mid-paragraph, whereas the surrounding file uses anchored bold run-in leads as its sub-section device — so a reader or auditor scanning by document structure sees this entire wire-contract specification as one opaque paragraph behind a single lead. The construction obligation in particular (whose violation would silently leak future SDK fields onto the wire under a spread-style refactor) is buried at the far end of the paragraph where structure-scanning will not surface it.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** commit-tracked
**Introducing commits:** `52b9101` (2026-05-11) — `pi-loom spec: resolve "T24 — Fork-reason watcher closure leaves the extension in an unspecified, silently degraded state"`
**History:** The *Runtime construction obligation* paragraph was authored in `52b9101` as part of the T24 fix, which introduced the `loom/host/session-shutdown-runtime-degraded` row and the new `details.event.{reason}` nesting convention; the paragraph was added together with the closed-`{reason}` strictness pin it defends, on what was then `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics.md`. The forbidden-construct enumeration ("no `...event` spread, no `Object.assign(..., event, ...)`, no per-key copy loop over `Object.keys(event)`") and the "explicit static property name" positive prescription were present at first appearance, not introduced by a later refactor. The paragraph was carried over to `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md` unchanged in content (only the path changed) by `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04) when the 100KB / 30KB size cap split `diagnostics.md` into an index plus child pages. The misplacement therefore predates the split: the rule was authored on the shape page in `52b9101` while the sibling *Construction-site wrap* / *Hoist obligation* clauses landed on the PIC `diagnostic-emission-isolation` page, and the over-prescriptive framing is the form the rule has had since its single authoring commit.

## Solution approach

Split the `#session-shutdown-details-conventions` paragraph in `diagnostic-shape.md` into discrete sub-sections, one per the five normative sub-topics currently demarcated by inline italic run-in labels, using the file's existing anchored bold run-in lead device. Retain the `<a id="session-shutdown-details-conventions"></a>` anchor on the field-set-closure sub-section so the existing inbound cross-references continue to resolve onto the material they cite. Surface the construction-obligation MUST as its own structure-scannable sub-section rather than at the far end of a paragraph.

## Solution constraints

- Restructure within `diagnostic-shape.md` only; move no content to other pages.
- Preserve every MUST-bearing rule verbatim.
- The file's ~37 KB size relative to the 30 KB recommended cap is out of scope (handled as a separate size-cap pass).

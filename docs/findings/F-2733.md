## Finding

`V12b` (and its paired `V12b-T`) carries two qualitatively different concerns in a single leaf. The first is a flat, stateless rendering surface: the top-level one-line `Err` render at the slash-dispatch boundary (`SLSH-3`) and the per-kind verbatim note templates (`SLSH-4` plus the `SNK-a … SNK-i` per-kind bullets and the `SNK-k` total catch-all). Each of these is closed by a byte-exact "renders verbatim" assertion against a single `QueryError` value — trivial, independent fixtures.

The second concern is `SLSH-5`: a recursive chain-attribution suffix that walks the `invoke_callee` hop chain leaf-first and, per hop, consumes the per-frame invocation record produced by `V15g` (parent post-`realpath` path + call-site source line) to append ` from <callee> invoked at <parent>:<line>`. This is a recursive walk requiring multi-hop fixtures and a live dependency on `V15g`'s provenance seam — structurally distinct from rendering a single template string. It is the sole reason `V15g` sits in `V12b`'s `Deps`.

The two halves share no fixture machinery: the template half needs one `QueryError` per kind; the chain half needs a multi-frame invocation-record fixture wired through `V15g`. Splitting `SLSH-5` into its own leaf lets the recursive walk red-green in isolation and confines the `V15g` dependency to the leaf that actually consumes it.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

If shipped unfixed the leaf remains buildable, but its implementer must hold both the flat per-kind verbatim-template surface and the recursive `V15g`-consuming multi-hop chain walk in one red-green TDD pass, mixing trivial single-value template fixtures with multi-frame invocation-record fixtures. No two implementers diverge on observable behaviour; the cost is a coarser-than-necessary build unit and a `V15g` dependency that the template half does not need.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Extract the `SLSH-5` recursive chain-attribution concern into a new leaf, leaving `V12b` as the flat `Err`-rendering leaf.

- In `docs/plan_topics/V12b-top-level-err-chain.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V12b-T-top-level-err-chain.md`, keep `SLSH-3`, `SLSH-4`, and the `SNK-a … SNK-i` / `SNK-k` template bullets; remove the `SLSH-5` bullet. Drop `V15g` from both leaves' `Deps.` (it is consumed only by `SLSH-5`); the remaining `Deps.` are `V12a`, `V4d` (plus `V12b-T` on the impl leaf). Narrow the **Adds.** to the per-kind templates and **Ships when.** to the per-kind notes rendering.
- Add a new leaf `<new>` (impl) and its paired `<new>-T` tests leaf under `plan_topics/`, owning the `SLSH-5` chain-attribution rendering. Move the existing `SLSH-5` bullet text verbatim (` from <callee> invoked at <parent>:<line>` per `invoke_callee` hop, leaf-first, rendering from `V15g`'s per-frame record without deriving source positions). The new leaf's `Deps.` include the (now template-only) `V12b` and `V15g`; **Spec.** carries `slash-invocation.md` (and `queryerror-variants.md` as needed for the chain hop kinds).
- Link the new impl leaf under the **V12 — Slash invocation** section of `docs/plan.md`.
- In `docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md`, change the row currently mapping `SLSH-3, SLSH-4, SLSH-5 | V12b` so `SLSH-3, SLSH-4` map to `V12b` and `SLSH-5` maps to the new `<new>` leaf.

`V15g`'s own Adds/Tests prose names `V12b` as the consumer of its provenance record; on touch, retarget that reference to the new chain leaf. The implementer picks the final leaf ID for `<new>` per the plan's `V<slice><letter>` scheme and the no-invented-ID / pickability rules in `conventions.md`.

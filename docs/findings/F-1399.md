## Finding

The *Compact-transcript format (normative)* sub-section of
`docs/spec_topics/binder/binder-model-and-context.md` opens with a
long rationale paragraph (`**Why the transcript bytes are pinned.**`)
that explains *why* the BNDR-7 reference renderings are pinned
byte-exact: it walks through the input-reproducibility contract, the
`temperature: 0` plus FNV-1a seed setting from *Determinism*, the
provider-side caveats around the seed field, and the alias-resolution
add-on dependency. The paragraph carries no `Note (non-normative)`
label, no blockquote, no italics — visually it sits at the same level
as the numbered rendering rules and the `MUST`-bearing BNDR-7
preamble that follow it.

The reproduce-exactly obligation itself is already carried by the
BNDR-7 anchor's own preamble (`Reference renderings (normative;
conforming implementations MUST reproduce these exactly)`), so the
rationale paragraph adds no normative content — it is pure
justification for an obligation stated immediately below it. The same
page already uses the `> **Note (non-normative):**` blockquote
pattern twice on neighbouring material (the binder-model
cost-economics note and the `bind_context` authoring guidance), so a
labelling convention is established and locally consistent.

Because the paragraph is unlabelled and structurally indistinguishable
from the rules around it, a reader cannot tell at a glance which
sentences are obligations and which are background — exactly the
ambiguity the page's `Note (non-normative)` discipline exists to
prevent.

when the void-truncation rendering was added. None of those edits
revisited the non-normative-labelling question.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Relabel the paragraph in place as a non-normative blockquote,
matching the convention the same page already applies on lines 7
and 25. Concretely, in
`docs/spec_topics/binder/binder-model-and-context.md` under the
`<a id="compact-transcript-format-normative"></a>` heading,
replace the bare `**Why the transcript bytes are pinned.** …`
paragraph with a `> **Note (non-normative):**` blockquote whose
body is the existing seven-sentence rationale verbatim. Keep
every sentence, every cross-reference (`#determinism`,
`#binder-model`, *Provider seed-field mapping*,
`#binder-model-parse-rule`), and the closing alias-resolution
caveat — none of the content is at fault, only the absence of a
non-normative label.

Edge cases for the implementer:

- Do **not** move the paragraph out of the section. It justifies
  BNDR-7's reproduce-exactly preamble and was deliberately
  co-located there by `3cd40c5`; moving it to
  `implementation-notes.md` or a separate rationale file would
  separate the rationale from the rule and defeat the prior
  finding's intent.
- Do **not** weaken or drop the rationale text. The
  `temperature: 0` + FNV-1a-seed framing and the
  provider-seed-field caveat both anchor concepts cited
  elsewhere on the page.
- Preserve the trailing alias-resolution sentence (`Alias-resolved
  binder models … only when the resolved concrete model stays
  stable across runs.`) inside the same blockquote — it is part
  of the same rationale arc.
- The BNDR-7 preamble itself (line 59, `<a id="bndr-7"></a>
  **BNDR-7.** Reference renderings (normative; conforming
  implementations MUST reproduce these exactly) …`) stays
  unchanged: it is the load-bearing MUST and is already
  correctly placed.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

Implementers and reviewers must read the seven-sentence paragraph
in full and reason about it to conclude it carries no obligation
beyond what BNDR-7's preamble already says. No conformance
outcome shifts. The cost is reader-time and the local violation of
the page's own non-normative-labelling convention.

## Finding

PIC-54 (runtime-event-channel.md, at the `sendSystemNote` best-effort fallback chain's definition site) is a named-hazard MUST: the terminal `console.error` step â€” reached when both the `pi.sendMessage` emission and the step-2 `loom/runtime/system-note-delivery-failed` diagnostic have already failed â€” MUST be wrapped in `try`/`catch`, and a throw from it MUST be silently swallowed and MUST NOT propagate out of the fallback chain, regardless of the reach-path that entered the chain (the per-invocation `finally` clean-cancel path, the extension-bootstrap SDK-failure path, or the capability-probe `host-incompatible` path). The stated purpose is to keep a throwing `console.error` from unwinding the factory body (PIC-6: MUST NOT throw), the `session_start` handler, or the per-invocation `finally`.

This is a non-observable safety obligation with no conformance vector: nothing in the corpus pins a fixture that injects a throwing `console.error` on the chain's final step and asserts the throw is absorbed. By contrast, the sibling construction-site swallow obligations on the same surface (PIC-26, building on PIC-25) carry explicit acceptance criteria in `diagnostic-emission-isolation.md` â€” fixtures (a)/(b)/(c), including a self-wrapped-inner-throw fixture. PIC-54 is left with no equivalent, even though `diagnostic-emission-isolation.md` imports the PIC-54 obligation by reference into the same paragraph that owns those fixtures.

Without a fixture, an implementation that omits the terminal `try`/`catch` (or that catches but does not cover all three reach-paths) passes every existing test while still permitting a throwing `console.error` to unwind the factory / `session_start` / per-invocation `finally` â€” the exact failure PIC-54 exists to prevent.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add one acceptance fixture for PIC-54 that injects a throwing `console.error` stub on the fallback chain's terminal step (after both `pi.sendMessage` and the step-2 `loom/runtime/system-note-delivery-failed` diagnostic have failed) and asserts, for each of the three named reach-paths:

- per-invocation `finally` clean-cancel path,
- extension-bootstrap SDK-failure path,
- capability-probe `host-incompatible` path,

that the throw does not propagate out of the `sendSystemNote` fallback chain (the factory body, `session_start` handler, and per-invocation `finally` complete normally), and that no further diagnostic is emitted after the swallow (the terminal `console.error` is the bottom of the stack; no retry, no second emission).

Place the fixture in the `## Acceptance criteria` section of `diagnostic-emission-isolation.md`, adjacent to the existing PIC-25/PIC-26 fixtures, since that section already owns the swallow-obligation fixtures for this surface and imports PIC-54 by reference; key it on PIC-54 and introduce no new diagnostic code or wire field. Implementer note: the three reach-paths must each be exercised â€” a single fixture covering only the per-invocation `finally` path leaves the broadened PIC-54 scope (bootstrap and capability-probe paths) unverified.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The normative MUST is unambiguous, so two implementers will not diverge on intended behaviour; but the swallow is non-observable and untested, so a regression that lets a throwing terminal `console.error` unwind the factory body, `session_start` handler, or per-invocation `finally` would ship undetected. The asymmetry with the fixture-backed sibling obligation PIC-26 makes the omission look deliberate when it is not.

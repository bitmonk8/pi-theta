## Problem

The `## Session Model` bullet list in `docs/spec_topics/session-model-and-appendix.md` enumerates SM-1 … SM-8 as a single flat sequence in which the four gated obligations (SM-3b, SM-4, SM-5, SM-6) are interleaved with the active siblings (SM-1, SM-2, SM-3a, SM-7a–SM-7e, SM-8). The only signal that SM-3b/4/5/6 are gated on the open contradiction at `#degraded-state-host-prerequisites` is a trailing italicised `*Implementation gate:*` clause buried inside each bullet's body. An implementer scanning the list for what to build next has no scannable structural separator distinguishing pickable obligations from blocked ones, and the umbrella claim that the contract "decomposes into the eight sub-obligations below" gives no hint that half of them are blocked.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit

**Introducing commit:** `b8f004f` (2026-06-19) — *pi-loom spec-plan: fix F-0719 — SM-4/5/6/3b degraded-state implementation-gate contingency markers*.

**History:** The eight-bullet SM-N decomposition was introduced in `40ffdc5` (2026-05-27, "T19a — Replace session-model paragraph with eight SM-N sub-units") inside `docs/spec.md`, with no implementation-gate markers — at that point no SM-N was gated, so a flat list raised no scannability concern. `81bf52a` (2026-06-11) relocated the list verbatim into `docs/spec_topics/session-model-and-appendix.md` as part of the spec-topics split. `b8f004f` then resolved F-0719 by appending the `*Implementation gate:*` clauses to SM-3b/4/5/6 inline, retaining the flat-list shape; this is the commit at which the SM-N list first contained a mix of gated and active obligations without structural separation. `80fcd63` (2026-06-20, F-0718+F-0823) later refined SM-4's marker to cite the `provably-differs` verdict and the authoritative-determination recording shape, but did not alter the list's structure. The defect is therefore the structural choice made in `b8f004f` — adding gate markers in-place rather than extracting a sub-section — rather than an interaction across commits.

## Solution approach

Restructure the `## Session Model` list so the gated obligations are visually partitioned from the active ones in place. Move the SM-3b, SM-4, SM-5, SM-6 bullets — each carrying its existing `<a id="sm-…">` anchor and `*Implementation gate:*` clause — under a new editorial sub-heading that groups the clause-(a)-gated obligations, and add a forward-link from SM-3's umbrella prose to SM-3b's relocated position so SM-3's two-leg decomposition stays discoverable from the active list. Name the active-vs-gated partition in the umbrella paragraph at the top of `## Session Model`.

## Solution constraints

- Preserve every `sm-3b-…`, `sm-4-…`, `sm-5-…`, `sm-6-…` anchor on its bullet under [GOV-23](./governance/anchor-scheme-and-retired.md#gov-23): the anchors move with their content; do not renumber or reuse them (they are inbound-cited from `host-prerequisites.md`, `version-bump-step2.md`, `model-changes-and-non-goals.md`, and `plan.md`).

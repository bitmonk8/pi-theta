## Finding

The "Signal source" and "Forwarding into `loomAbort`" paragraphs in `cancellation.md` specify runtime↔SDK signal-chain wiring (per-invocation `AbortController`, entry-point forwarding shapes, `ctx.signal`-undefined handling, prompt-mode bidirectional `ctx.abort()`, subagent abort listener) that duplicates the authoritative `host-interfaces-core.md#cancellation-source` section.

Separately, 033b8e3 introduced the Checkpoint edge-case bullet's blanket "Production wiring is a no-op (an already-resolved promise per checkpoint) … purely a test surface" claim; 8cd3c4a later added the `loop-iter` macrotask yield to the Granularity rule (mirrored by PIC-10's precise scoping), falsifying that blanket claim without updating the stale bottom bullet.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Consolidate the SDK-wiring mechanics in `host-interfaces-core.md#cancellation-source` (which already carries the same material — fold in any detail unique to `cancellation.md`, e.g. the prompt-mode `ctx.abort()` linkage and the spawn-then-immediate-cancel ordering, that PIC does not already state). Replace the two `cancellation.md` paragraphs with a one-paragraph loom-observable summary (every invocation runs under a single runtime-owned `loomAbort.signal`; cancellation is forwarded into it from the three entry points) plus a cross-reference to `host-interfaces-core.md#cancellation-source`. Keep CNCL-4 (abort-reason propagation) as a behavioural rule where it currently lives; only the wiring mechanics relocate.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

An implementer reading the closing edge-case bullet in isolation would wire every checkpoint — including `loop-iter` — as an already-resolved promise, defeating cancellation of compute-bound loops (the exact defect the macrotask-yield rule was added to fix); the page contradicts itself on observable production behaviour. The duplicated SDK-wiring prose additionally invites drift between `cancellation.md` and `host-interfaces-core` as either copy is edited.

## Finding

The `cka-27` row in `coverage-matrix.md` maps `session-only-degraded-state.md`'s degraded-state MUSTs (Recovery-path prohibition set, state-independent drain-state tag write, *Predicate split*, *Seam-minimality*, *Inline triplet is normative*) to closing leaf `V9l`. `V9l` is blocked: its `Deps` field names `V18c` as the clause-(a) resolution owner, and the leaf is not pickable until the version-bump editorial-review checklist item (a) authoritatively resolves the host-prerequisites clause (a) open contradiction. The dependency is mechanically tracked — `V9l` is a coverage-producing leaf, so it sits in `H5b`'s `Deps`, and `H6a` (which depends on `H5b`) cannot activate the live-corpus release gate while `V9l` is unpicked — so this is not a silent matrix gap.

The asymmetry is the defect. `V9l` points *to* `V18c` (the leaf records its blocker), and `plan.md` §Blocked obligations records both possible dispositions of the contradiction. But `V18c` — the leaf that owns the deciding resolution — carries no reciprocal pointer: nothing in its editorial-review checklist item (a) tells the bump contributor that resolving clause (a) dispositions the `cka-27` coverage row and unblocks (or retires the degraded MUSTs behind) `V9l`. A contributor working `V18c` item (a) in isolation has no signal that their resolution has a downstream coverage consequence.

Because the contradiction's two outcomes (branch reachable → implement `V9l`; branch unreachable → the §Blocked obligations pre-designed fallback governs, `V9l` ships collapsed and the degraded obligations are reworked spec-side) both keep `cka-27` closed by `V9l`, the release-gate machinery does not let anything ship wrong. The cost is purely traceability: the resolution owner does not name the obligation it gates, so the disposition step can be overlooked, stalling the release until someone reconstructs the link.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V18c-version-bump-checklist.md`, extend the description of editorial-review checklist item (a) so it names the coverage obligation that item (a)'s resolution gates: state that resolving the host-prerequisites clause (a) contradiction dispositions the `cka-27` coverage row whose closing leaf `V9l` is blocked on it, and cross-reference `plan.md` §Blocked obligations for the two governed outcomes (clause (a) resolves *branch reachable* → `V9l` is unblocked and implemented; clause (a) resolves *branch unreachable* → the §Blocked obligations pre-designed fallback governs, `V9l` ships collapsed to full teardown + `/reload` and the degraded-state obligations are reworked spec-side). The literal cross-reference should name `V9l` and `cka-27` and link `plan.md` §Blocked obligations, so the bump contributor reading item (a) sees the downstream disposition without reconstructing it.

The spec edits (reworking the degraded-state MUSTs) belong to the future resolver under the *branch unreachable* outcome, not to this plan fix; this fix only adds the cross-reference. The `cka-27` row in `coverage-matrix.md` stays mapped to `V9l` under both outcomes and needs no edit.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The dependency is mechanically tracked, so the release gate cannot silently activate with `cka-27` unresolved. The residual risk is that the bump contributor resolving clause (a) in `V18c` has no pointer reminding them to disposition `cka-27` / `V9l`, so the disposition can be overlooked and the resolution-to-coverage link must be reconstructed by hand, stalling the release.

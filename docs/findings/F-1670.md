## Finding

`docs/spec_topics/query/query-escapes-stringification.md` carries the title *"Query escapes stringification"* and the same label in the parent `query.md` Contents list, but the file holds **four** `##` sections, not two: `Escapes` (QRY-17), `Stringification of interpolated values` (QRY-18), `Discarded query results are a parse error (loom/parse/discarded-query-result)` (QRY-19), and `Observability of discarded results` (QRY-20) — the latter with `Panics during interpolation are not caught by let _ =` (QRY-21) as a subsection. Two of the four `##` headings and three of the five anchored REQ-IDs concern the discard contract and the runtime panic propagation rule, neither of which is plausibly findable under a title that names only escapes and stringification.

The cost is navigational: a reader looking up the discard parse rule, the discard-observability runtime event, or the interpolation-panic rule has no surface signal that this page owns them. The parent index in `query.md` reinforces the mis-cue by listing the page under the same understated label, and grep-by-title against the corpus misses the discard surface entirely. This is a sibling of the two placement findings (*"QRY-19 ... on the wrong page"* and *"QRY-20/QRY-21 ... on the wrong page"*); their resolution determines whether this finding needs a rename at all or simply dissolves when the misplaced REQ-IDs move out.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit introduction
**Introducing commits:** `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04, "docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set")
**History:** Before this commit, the discard parse rule, the discard-observability event, and the interpolation-panic subsection lived in the monolithic `docs/spec_topics/query.md` (see `git show 31ff060:docs/spec_topics/query.md` lines 134–189: `## Escapes`, `## Stringification of interpolated values`, `## Discarded query results are a parse error`, `## Observability of discarded results`, `### Panics during interpolation are not caught by let _ =`), where the page H1 was simply `# Query` and no naming mismatch existed. The `f5e89f4` splitter (`docs/_tools/split_spec.py`) carved `query.md` into four children keyed by a slug table; the entry `("query-escapes-stringification", 136, 192)` mechanically swept all four sections — Escapes, Stringification, Discarded, Observability (plus the Panics subsection) — into a single child whose slug, H1, and parent-Contents label were derived from only the first two section titles. The QRY-19 / QRY-20 / QRY-21 anchors did not yet exist at split time; they were later coined in place by `716a13b` (2026-06-25, "pi-loom spec-plan: fix F-1286 — coin QRY-1..21 across query subtree + repoint V13 leaves/coverage"), formalising the misplacement but not introducing it. The naming defect therefore enters at `f5e89f4`'s slug choice — no earlier commit ever named this page — and is a pure artefact of the mechanical split.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Defer to the placement findings

**Approach.** Treat this as a downstream consequence of the two sibling findings (*"QRY-19 ... on the wrong page"* moves QRY-19 to `query-forms.md`; *"QRY-20/QRY-21 ... on the wrong page"* moves QRY-20 and the QRY-21 subsection to `query-failure-and-repair.md`). With those edits landed, the page reduces to its two on-title sections (Escapes / Stringification) and the current title is accurate; no rename is needed.

**Spec edits.** None on this page. The `Spec.` citation in `docs/plan_topics/V13g-query-discard-observability.md` and its `-T` sibling should be updated when the QRY-20/21 placement finding lands (out of scope for this finding's edit).

**Pros.**
- Single edit pass; no need to rename, no churn on cross-references that already grep-resolve.
- Aligns the page's *contents* with its *title* by curating contents rather than re-labelling.
- Future readers encountering `Discarded query results ...` find it under the failure-and-repair page, which is also where `QueryError` variants live.

**Cons.**
- Resolution is gated on two other findings landing; if either is rejected (e.g. for splitter-stability reasons), the title mismatch persists.
- Adds an ordering constraint: this finding is a no-op only *after* the placement edits commit.

**Risks.**
- If a placement finding is rejected after this one is resolved as a no-op, the mismatch silently survives.

### Option B — Rename in place to "Query template syntax"

**Approach.** Rename the H1 to `# Query template syntax` (the umbrella covering escapes, stringification, the discard-result discipline, and the interpolation-panic carve-out), update the parent index entry in `docs/spec_topics/query.md` Contents, and update the `Spec.` citation labels in the four V13a / V13g plan-topic files. File path stays the same (`query-escapes-stringification.md`) to avoid disturbing the splitter's slug table, the 1571 cross-reference rewrites it performed in `f5e89f4`, and the F-1286 anchor inventory; only the rendered title and the human-facing label change.

**Spec edits.**
- `docs/spec_topics/query/query-escapes-stringification.md` line 1: `# Query escapes stringification` → `# Query template syntax`.
- `docs/spec_topics/query.md` Contents bullet: rename label to match.
- `docs/plan_topics/V13a-query-render.md`, `V13a-T-query-render.md`, `V13g-query-discard-observability.md`, `V13g-T-query-discard-observability.md`: refresh the bracketed link label in the `Spec.` line.

**Pros.**
- Independent of any other finding's resolution; lands as a single coherent edit.
- Page title accurately covers all five anchored REQ-IDs on the page as it stands today.
- Preserves the page slug, leaving the splitter's slug table, cross-reference rewrites, and grep-by-path traceability undisturbed.

**Cons.**
- "Query template syntax" is a broader umbrella than the page's current contents warrant — it would, in principle, also cover query forms (`query-forms.md`), which is the dedicated grammar page; the title becomes accurate-but-overlapping.
- If a placement finding subsequently lands, the new title becomes *over*-broad for the slimmer remaining contents and a second rename is needed.

**Risks.**
- Future readers may assume `Query template syntax` is the canonical grammar page and miss `query-forms.md`.

### Recommendation

Take **Option A**. The sibling placement findings are stronger than this one — they argue from REQ-ID *content*, not page *labelling* — and if they land, this finding becomes a self-resolving no-op with no rename and no cross-reference churn. Land Option A by deferring the edit until the two placement findings reach a verdict; if either is rejected, fall back to Option B and rename the H1 (and the parent Contents label) only. Edge case for the implementer: if Option A is taken, also confirm post-placement that the page H1 and the `query.md` Contents entry still read naturally with only Escapes + Stringification remaining — both already do, so no further edit is expected.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The damage is confined to corpus navigability — readers searching for the discard contract by topic, page title, or Contents-list entry are misled — and to the recurring drag the mismatch puts on future cross-referencing work that has to override the title with explicit anchor text.

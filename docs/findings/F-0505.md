## Finding

`V4c`'s `ERR-12` Tests bullet states that ERR-8 holds inside a subagent loom, exercised via the `H4a` harness modelling a subagent-mode callee — and then appends a forward-reference clause: "the live `V9i` subagent surface carries the delegated ERR-8 live-surface re-assertion (cancellation does not mutate the real subagent `AgentSession`'s committed turns) as a delegated live-carrier witness." That appended clause describes what a *different* leaf (`V9i`) is obligated to test; it imposes nothing testable on the `V4c` implementer, whose surface is the `H4a` double, not the live subagent session.

The obligation the clause narrates already has an authoritative, testable home. `V9i`'s Tests carry a dedicated bullet — "`ERR-8` (delegated live-carrier witness for `V4c`'s ERR-8/ERR-12 deferral): a mid-stream cancellation driven inside the real subagent `AgentSession` does not mutate the subagent session's committed turns" — and `V9i` lists `V4c` in its `Deps`. The paired tests leaf `V4c-T` already scopes `ERR-12` to the `H4a` harness explicitly ("— not the live `V9i` surface"), so the implementation leaf's bullet is the only place that re-states the live-surface re-assertion.

The clause is therefore duplicated rationale: the same delegated-witness obligation now exists both as narrative prose on `V4c` and as a concrete test bullet + `Deps` edge on `V9i`. If `V9i`'s witness obligation later changes, the `V4c` narration becomes a stale, divergent description with no mechanical guard.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V4c-terminal-outcomes.md`, strike the forward-reference clause from the `ERR-12` Tests bullet — delete everything from `; the live \`V9i\` subagent surface carries` through `as a delegated live-carrier witness.` so the bullet ends after `modelling a subagent-mode callee`. This restores parity with `V4c-T`'s already-correct scoping and leaves the live-surface re-assertion owned solely by `V9i`'s `ERR-8` delegated-witness bullet (which retains its `V4c` `Deps` edge). Do not edit `V9i-subagent-isolation.md` or `V4c-T-terminal-outcomes.md` — both are already correct; the `V9i` test bullet and `Deps` edge are the authoritative home and must remain.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

If shipped unfixed, the `V4c` implementer reads a sentence that imposes no obligation on their leaf and could mistakenly believe they must exercise the live `V9i` subagent surface (which `V4c-T` explicitly excludes). The duplicated rationale is also a divergence surface: a future change to `V9i`'s delegated-witness obligation leaves the `V4c` narration silently stale.

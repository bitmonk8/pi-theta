## Finding

`V4g`'s `Deps.` field is `` `V4g-T`, `V4e`, `V9b`, `V10d` `` followed by a long parenthetical that describes the seam contract rather than the dependency relationship: it states that `V9b` declares the `ReloadFailureInjector` interface (`injectReloadFailure`) that `V4g` imports, enumerates the three arms exercised through it (registry-swap, `.loom`/`.warp` re-parse, settings-re-merge), and re-narrates that `ERR-7`'s synthetic failure is injected through that one interface.

Per the leaf format in `conventions.md` §Leaf format, `Deps.` is "Other leaf IDs that must be complete first" plus the pickability predicate — a bare ID list. Interface-contract substance — naming a cross-leaf seam and the behaviour exercised against it — belongs in `Adds.`, where a Class-2 named cross-leaf seam binds when a consuming leaf names it. `V4g`'s `Adds.` already carries this substance: it names the `ReloadFailureInjector.injectReloadFailure` seam, the registry-swap and `.loom`/`.warp` re-parse arms, `V10d`'s settings-re-merge arm, and the `V4e` load-time routing surface `V4g` builds on.

The result is a second, non-authoritative home for the seam-contract prose. The `Deps.` parenthetical duplicates what `Adds.` already states and can drift from it, with no mechanical guard reconciling the two.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V4g-pre-evaluation-reload-failure.md`, reduce the `Deps.` field to the bare ID list — `` **Deps.** `V4g-T`, `V4e`, `V9b`, `V10d` `` — striking the entire trailing parenthetical. The contract substance it carries already appears in `Adds.` (the `ReloadFailureInjector.injectReloadFailure` seam owned by `V9b`; the registry-swap, `.loom`/`.warp` re-parse, and `V10d` settings-re-merge arms; and the `V4e` load-time routing surface), so this is a deletion rather than a move. If on a close read any clause in the struck parenthetical is not already covered by `Adds.`, fold that clause into `Adds.` rather than leaving it in `Deps.`. This fix is internal to the `V4g` leaf file; the spec is read-only for it, and no other leaf needs editing.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The `Deps.` parenthetical duplicates the `ReloadFailureInjector` seam-contract prose already present in `Adds.`, creating a divergence surface: a future change to the seam contract leaves the two narrations silently inconsistent, and the seam's binding home (per leaf format, `Adds.`) is not the place a reader is pointed to. No implementer is currently misled — the dependency IDs are listed first and pickability is unaffected.

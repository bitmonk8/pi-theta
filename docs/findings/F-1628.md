## Problem

The `literal-read/<symptom>` discriminator family — explicitly a peer to the
audit's `audit/<class>/<family>/<symptom>` shape, outside the `audit/`
namespace and carrying no `<class>` segment — is defined inside the *Target
surface categories* paragraph (`id="audit-target-surface-categories"`) of
`audit-target-categories.md`, while the audit's *Failure-surface contract*
(`id="audit-failure-surface-contract"` in `audit-failures.md`) is the single
home for every audit-time wire failure discriminator. A contributor reading
`audit-failures.md` to enumerate the audit's wire discriminators sees only the
`audit/...` shape and concludes the three-class partition is exhaustive,
missing the `literal-read/...` peer entirely. There is no cross-reference from
the *Failure-surface contract* back to the literal-read family.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception (cross-file structure)
**Introducing commits:**
- `e5cd514` (2026-05-09) — *pi-loom spec: resolve "T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit"* — introduced the `literal-read/<symptom>` discriminator family (the namespace contrast vs `audit/<class>/<family>/<symptom>`, the floor `<symptom>` tokens, the per-`<symptom>` fixture floor, the `unknown-entry-kind` token) and authored it inside the *Target surface categories* paragraph of the then-monolithic `pi-integration-contract.md`.
- `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04) — *docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set* — split `pi-integration-contract.md` into the `audit-target-categories.md` / `audit-failures.md` siblings. The split preserved the content's home unchanged, which is when the embedded placement became a sibling-file misplacement (the *Failure-surface contract* now lives in a separate file from its peer literal-read discriminator family).

**History:** `git log --all -S "literal-read/<symptom>"` over the spec corpus surfaces only the two commits above as content authors (and `23d28a1` as a basename-cross-reference sweep that left the placement unchanged). No subsequent commit has touched the literal-read family's home location.

## Solution approach

Move the `literal-read/<symptom>` discriminator family from the *Target
surface categories* paragraph (`id="audit-target-surface-categories"`) of
`audit-target-categories.md` to the *Failure-surface contract* paragraph
(`id="audit-failure-surface-contract"`) of `audit-failures.md`, framed as a
sibling to the `audit/<class>/<family>/<symptom>` shape. At the vacated site,
retain the minimum-entry-shape obligation and the obligation that each shape
failure surfaces under its own `literal-read/<symptom>` discriminator, and add
a forward-link to `audit-failures.md` for the token floor, namespace contrast,
and fixture obligations rather than restating them. Preserve the existing
cross-references from `version-bump-step2.md` step 2(a) and
`inventory-audit-intro.md` by keeping the relevant anchor IDs resolvable,
re-creating any moved `<a id>` anchor at the relocation site.

## Finding

`audit-failures.md` owns the audit's *Failure-surface contract* — the structural
discriminator shape `audit/<class>/<family>/<symptom>`, the closed three-class
partition (`violation` / `infra` / `canary`), the per-`<symptom>` fixture floor,
the family→step-2(b) routing table, and the per-family record-shape table.
That paragraph is the single home for "what kinds of audit-time failure
discriminators exist, what they look like on the wire, and what each must
carry".

The *peer* `literal-read/<symptom>` discriminator family — explicitly framed in
the spec as "a peer to the inventory-closure audit's `audit/<class>/<family>/<symptom>`
shape … not under the `audit/` namespace … carries no `<class>` segment, since
it never participates in the three-class partition the audit pins" — is defined
instead inside the *Target surface categories* paragraph of
`audit-target-categories.md`. The category paragraph homes the full
literal-read failure surface: the namespace contrast against
`audit/<class>/<family>/<symptom>`, the floor `<symptom>` tokens an
audit-participating entry must produce on a malformed entry shape
(`missing-path-on-audit-kind`, `empty-path`, `empty-leftmost-segment`,
`missing-kind-field`, `non-string-kind`, `unknown-entry-kind`), the
per-`<symptom>` fixture floor that mirrors the audit-side floor in
`audit-failures.md`, and the recovery routing back to `version-bump-step2.md`
step 2(a).

A contributor reading `audit-failures.md` to enumerate the audit's wire
discriminators sees only the `audit/...` shape and concludes the three-class
partition is exhaustive; they miss the `literal-read/...` peer namespace
entirely. Symmetrically, a contributor reading *Target surface categories*
expects category-detection rules and finds an embedded sibling
failure-discriminator contract that belongs with the audit's failure-surface
contract. The two halves of one wire surface live on two different pages with
no cross-reference from `audit-failures.md` back to the literal-read family.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Move the `literal-read/<symptom>` discriminator family from the
*Target surface categories* paragraph of `audit-target-categories.md` to the
*Failure-surface contract* paragraph of `audit-failures.md`, placing it as a
sibling sub-paragraph immediately after the three-class partition.

The relocated content is the contiguous span beginning with "The SDK
surface-inventory literal-read assertion … MUST additionally enforce the
minimum-entry-shape obligation by surfacing each shape failure under its own
`literal-read/<symptom>` discriminator …" through the illustrative
`<symptom>` tokens and per-fixture floor, plus the later "The literal-read
assertion's failure-discriminator shape is `literal-read/<symptom>` (a peer to
the inventory-closure audit's `audit/<class>/<family>/<symptom>` shape …" span
and the `unknown-entry-kind` recovery routing.

At the relocation site in `audit-failures.md`, frame the family as a sibling
to the `audit/<class>/<family>/<symptom>` shape: cite the three-class
partition above explicitly when stating that `literal-read/<symptom>` is
outside the `audit/` namespace and carries no `<class>` segment, so the
exhaustiveness scope of the three-class partition is unambiguous on its face.
Restate the per-`<symptom>` fixture floor against the literal-read tokens in
parallel form with the audit-side floor so the two floors are visibly siblings
rather than two pages' independent obligations.

At the vacated site in `audit-target-categories.md`, retain the minimum-entry-shape
obligation (`kind: string`, `path: string`, the audit-participating-kind
exemption for the four positive-direction-only kinds) and the obligation that
each shape failure surfaces under its own `literal-read/<symptom>`
discriminator, but cross-reference `audit-failures.md` for the token floor,
namespace contrast, and fixture obligations rather than restating them.
Preserve the existing cross-references from `version-bump-step2.md` step 2(a)
and `inventory-audit-intro.md` by leaving the anchor IDs intact at whichever
file they now resolve into (re-create the relevant `<a id>` anchor at the
relocation site if the anchor currently sits on the moved span).

Implementer edge cases: the recovery routing of `literal-read/unknown-entry-kind`
back to step 2(a)'s "add a recognising branch to the literal-read assertion's
branch table for the new `kind`" obligation must remain visible from the
relocated site; the per-`<symptom>` fixture floor in `audit-failures.md`
currently keys on the audit's family/`<symptom>` set only — extend its
introductory framing to scope the floor over both the audit-side and the
literal-read-side `<symptom>` enumerations rather than introducing a
second-floor paragraph.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Two cooperating contributors who read different pages first will produce
conformant implementations whose wire-discriminator inventory differs in which
namespaces they expect to emit and exercise: an `audit-failures.md`-first
reader who relies on the three-class closure overlooks the
`literal-read/<symptom>` peer entirely; a `audit-target-categories.md`-first
reader treats the audit-side `<symptom>` floor and the literal-read-side
`<symptom>` floor as two unrelated obligations. The defect does not block
implementation — both halves are stated in full somewhere — but it raises the
floor on contributor diff-reading effort every time the literal-read
discriminator set is extended or audited.

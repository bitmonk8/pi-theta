## Finding

`docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/unknown-reason-rule.md` now has a dedicated `## Acceptance criteria — \`"throw:<String(error)>"\` discriminator grammar` section listing per-fixture obligations (a)–(d) for the throw-discriminator family. The sibling fixture floor for the `"literals-shape-invalid"` discriminator — "A conformance test suite MUST cover each of these four sub-cases with its own fixture: each fixture constructs a synthetic snapshot value … and asserts the handler emits exactly one `loom/host/session-shutdown-pinned-constant-unreadable` with `details.failure === "literals-shape-invalid"` and that no `loom/host/session-shutdown-reason-unknown` fires" — instead sits inline at the end of the single multi-thousand-word **Unknown-reason rule** body paragraph, immediately after the enumeration of the four structural sub-cases (1)–(4).

The two are peer obligations against the same closed `details.failure` discriminator-literal set ([PIC-47] / substring (d) of [PIC-48]) and the same fixture-shape contract (leaf owns mechanic; branch coverage + discriminator literal are normative). A reader (or a downstream plan-leaf author such as `V9h`/`V9h-T`) scanning the file for fixture obligations will find the throw-grammar fixtures under the visible `## Acceptance criteria` heading and silently miss the `"literals-shape-invalid"` ones buried in the body — exactly the failure mode the Acceptance criteria section was carved out to prevent for the throw-grammar family. The page is also the only one of the four Shard-08 pages that touched in commit `8008b91` (the F-1217 fix-loop) where one Acceptance criteria section was added while a parallel pre-existing fixture floor on the same page was left in its body paragraph.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `a7d2a80` (Sun Jun 7 2026, `pi-loom spec: resolve "patch-skew teardown / literals-shape-invalid fixture enumeration"`) — added the four-sub-case fixture floor inline at the end of the **Unknown-reason rule** body paragraph; `8008b91` (Thu Jun 25 2026, `pi-loom spec-plan: fix F-1217 — add acceptance-criteria/fixture blocks for four Shard-08 MUSTs`) — added the `## Acceptance criteria — \`"throw:<String(error)>"\` discriminator grammar` section as a sibling of the body paragraph but did not migrate (or mirror) the pre-existing `"literals-shape-invalid"` four-sub-case fixture floor into a parallel section.
**History:** `git log -S'"literals-shape-invalid"' -- docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/unknown-reason-rule.md` and `git log -L8,16:docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/unknown-reason-rule.md` together pin the timeline: the `"literals-shape-invalid"` clause and its conformance-suite obligation entered the body paragraph in `a7d2a80` (`docs/spec.md` had been split out of by `f5e89f4` already), and the file had no `## Acceptance criteria` section until `8008b91` added the throw-discriminator one as a peer of the body paragraph. The inconsistency is the cross-product of the two and surfaced the moment `8008b91` landed.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add a second `## Acceptance criteria — \`"literals-shape-invalid"\` discriminator sub-cases` section to `unknown-reason-rule.md`, peer to the existing throw-discriminator one, listing the four sub-cases as bullets `(a)`–`(d)` (`literals` not an array; array with a non-string element; empty array; array of strings with at least one empty-string element). Each bullet's body is the per-fixture obligation already in the body paragraph: construct a synthetic snapshot whose `literals` field exhibits exactly that one sub-case's structural defect (via the same test-only static-import-preserving substitution mechanic the (P-b)/(P-c-1) `patch-skew-degradation.md` fixtures establish), dispatch a `session_shutdown` event, and assert the handler emits exactly one `loom/host/session-shutdown-pinned-constant-unreadable` with `details.failure === "literals-shape-invalid"` and that no `loom/host/session-shutdown-reason-unknown` fires. Open the section with the same one-paragraph framing the throw-discriminator section uses ("decomposes into the fixture obligations below, all of which the implementation MUST satisfy (the leaf owns fixture-shape; branch coverage and the exact discriminator string are normative)") and cite [PIC-47] / substring (d) of [PIC-48] identically.

Mirror rather than move: leave the four-sub-case enumeration and the one-sentence conformance-suite obligation in the body paragraph as the load-bearing definition of the discriminator's structural reach, and have the new Acceptance criteria section open with "The four structural sub-cases of `"literals-shape-invalid"` enumerated in the **Unknown-reason rule** body above decompose into the fixture obligations below" so the body paragraph remains self-contained and the Acceptance criteria section is the canonical fixture-survey anchor. Moving the entire passage out would leave a now-orphaned "per the four sub-cases of the catch-arm-discriminator paragraph below" forward-reference in the *Lookup-failure-to-discriminator routing* arm (4) clause earlier in the same paragraph; mirroring preserves that anchor's resolvability.

Give the new section its own stable anchor (e.g. `<a id="literals-shape-invalid-acceptance"></a>` on its heading) and update `V9h`'s and `V9h-T`'s `Tests` bullet to cite it alongside the existing `#pic-47` cite, so the plan-leaf reader lands directly on the fixture-list anchor.

Watch out for [PIC-48]'s anchor-stable-contract-surface rule: substring (d) of [PIC-48] names `"literals-shape-invalid"` itself as anchor-stable wherever it appears at inbound-reference sites, and the **Unknown-reason rule** paragraph carries a same-paragraph carve-out for restatements. A new `## Acceptance criteria` section is structurally outside the **Unknown-reason rule** paragraph, so its uses of the `"literals-shape-invalid"` literal are inbound references under PIC-48 and any future spelling drift in the new section MUST sweep the existing body-paragraph site (and the V9h/V9h-T citations) in the same commit — which the existing PIC-48 inbound-reference-sweep rule already governs without further edit.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The normative obligation is present and unambiguous; an implementer who reads the body paragraph end-to-end picks it up. The cost is a discoverability inconsistency on a page where one Acceptance criteria section already exists: a fixture-survey pass keyed on `## Acceptance criteria` headings under-counts the page's fixture floor by four, and the V9h-T author has to read the body paragraph to confirm the same four sub-cases the plan leaf already names. No implementer divergence is plausible.

## Finding

`V8b` and `V8b-T` carry, as the second clause of their `PIC-13` Tests bullet, "no `src/**` module reads `process.env`/`process.cwd` directly" (and `V8b`'s Ships-when restates it as "the no-direct-`process.env`/`process.cwd` ambient-access ban"). This is a bare-glob restatement: it neither names the leaf that owns the canonical ambient-primitive scan nor the document that declares `src/**` the production-code root.

The canonical ambient-primitive scan over `src/**` is owned by `H3a`, whose architectural test already asserts "no `src/**` module *directly references* `process.env`, `process.cwd`, `crypto.randomUUID`, …" over a superset of the primitives `V8b` names. The `src/**` scope itself is canonically fixed in `conventions.md` §"Cross-cutting rules (every leaf)", which declares `src/**` "the single production-code root" that the `H3a` ambient-primitive scan (among other gates) reads, and explicitly routes the one out-of-tree module (`extensions/index.ts`) to release-time residue inspection (checklist item 8). So the "a production module could live outside `src/**`" concern is itself a documented, owned design decision rather than an unexamined assumption.

What remains is a duplication / ownership ambiguity rather than a coverage hole: `V8b`'s `PIC-13` Tests bullet asserts the same `process.env`/`process.cwd` ban that the `H3a` architectural scan already owns, but cites neither `H3a` nor the `conventions.md` scope clause. A fresh implementer of `V8b-T` cannot tell whether to stand up a second, independent ambient-access scan (duplicating `H3a`'s) or to assert that `PIC-13`'s ambient arm is closed by the existing `H3a` scan. Two reasonable implementers diverge on test placement, and the glob is restated as a free-floating fact that drifts out of sync if the canonical scope is ever revised.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V8b-filesystem-seam.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V8b-T-filesystem-seam.md`, rewrite the `PIC-13` Tests bullet's ambient-access clause so it references the canonical owner instead of restating the `src/**` glob as a free-standing fact. The clause should make clear that (a) the ambient-access ban is scanned over `src/**`, the single production-code root declared in `conventions.md` §"Cross-cutting rules (every leaf)", and (b) the `process.env`/`process.cwd` ban is the `H3a` ambient-primitive scan's obligation — `V8b` does not stand up a second independent scan, it relies on (or cross-references) the `H3a`-owned scan for `PIC-13`'s ambient arm. Apply the same cross-reference to `V8b`'s Ships-when sentence ("the no-direct-`process.env`/`process.cwd` ambient-access ban").

Concretely: replace the trailing clause "no `src/**` module reads `process.env`/`process.cwd` directly" with text that points at the `H3a` scan and the `conventions.md` scope declaration (e.g. names `H3a` as the owning scan and `conventions.md` §"Cross-cutting rules" as the source of the `src/**` scope), so the glob is not asserted as a bare fact in the `V8b` leaf pair. `H3a` and `conventions.md` are read-only for this fix — do not move the scan ownership or re-scope `src/**`; only `V8b`/`V8b-T` change.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The substantive ban (no direct `process.env`/`process.cwd` in `src/**`) ships regardless, because `H3a`'s architectural scan asserts it independently. The exposure is a divergence in `V8b-T`: an implementer may either duplicate the `H3a` scan as a second `V8b`-local test, or leave `PIC-13`'s ambient arm leaning silently on `H3a` — and the restated glob can drift out of step with the canonical `conventions.md` scope if it is ever changed.

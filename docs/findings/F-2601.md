## Finding

`diagnostics/placeholder-rendering-b.md` §8 carries a *"Carve-out: `<failure>` is not a §8 placeholder"* block for the `loom/host/session-shutdown-pinned-constant-unreadable` row. The block opens correctly — it explicitly states that the `<failure>` grammar is *"pinned at the diagnostic-construction site (per [Pi Integration Contract — Unknown-reason rule], which owns the grammar)"* — and then proceeds to re-state that grammar in full:

- the three discriminator forms (`"missing-entry"`, `"literals-shape-invalid"`, and the `"throw:<…>"` template family, including the 256-UTF-16-code-unit truncation and control-character escaping done at construction);
- a canonical membership predicate, `d === "missing-entry" || d === "literals-shape-invalid" || d.startsWith("throw:")`, presented as something *"operator tooling MUST use"*;
- the unreachability claim (*"the empty case is unreachable by construction — every discriminator value is non-empty"*);
- the `"throw:<unreadable>"` coercion-failure fallback.

Every one of those is already normatively owned by PIC's Unknown-reason rule (`pi-integration-contract/unknown-reason-rule.md`): the three forms and their construction by PIC-47 (`#unknown-reason-rule-handler-trycatch`), and the discriminator literals + `"throw:<String(error)>"` template are designated *anchor-stable contract surface* by PIC-48 (substring (d), `#unknown-reason-rule-substring-d`) — meaning any future edit MUST sweep every inbound reference in the same commit. The carve-out's restatement is exactly such an inbound reference, so the duplication directly enlarges the sweep burden that PIC-48 was written to bound.

The duplication is more than redundant. PIC's rule says operator tooling **MAY** type-narrow via `startsWith("throw:")`; the placeholder-rendering carve-out promotes this to a **MUST** and authors a specific predicate expression — a normative operator-tooling obligation that a *rendering* page has no business owning and that is strictly stronger than what the owning page states. A reader who treats placeholder-rendering-b as authoritative could over-constrain tooling against the actual contract.

The carve-out does own legitimate, rendering-layer content that must stay: that `<failure>` is not a §8 placeholder, that the renderer emits it verbatim (no re-truncate, no re-escape, no `<no message>` substitution, no §8 first-line truncation), that §8 prefix/suffix anchoring does not apply to this row, and that the build-time strict-equality prohibition enumerates only rows containing an actual §8 placeholder. The defect is confined to the embedded grammar restatement, which should be a cross-reference rather than a second normative copy.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Reduce the §8 `<failure>` carve-out to the rendering-layer facts placeholder-rendering-b owns, and defer the discriminator grammar to PIC's Unknown-reason rule by cross-reference.

Retain in the carve-out:

- the statement that `<failure>` is **not** a §8 placeholder and is rendered verbatim from the loom-constructed discriminator;
- the renderer obligations: MUST NOT re-truncate or re-escape the payload, MUST NOT substitute `<no message>`, MUST NOT apply §8's first-line truncation, and §8 prefix/suffix anchoring does not apply to this row;
- the test-conformance note that the two literal forms MAY use byte-identical equality and the throw-template family MAY use a `startsWith("throw:")` partial match — phrased as "matching the throw-template family pinned by PIC's Unknown-reason rule", not as a re-derived predicate;
- the exemption from the build-time strict-equality prohibition (that prohibition enumerates only rows whose Message templates contain an actual §8 placeholder).

Replace with a single pointer to `unknown-reason-rule.md` (PIC-47 `#unknown-reason-rule-handler-trycatch`, PIC-48 `#unknown-reason-rule-substring-d`):

- the enumeration of the three discriminator forms and their 256-UTF-16 / control-character construction;
- the `d === "missing-entry" || d === "literals-shape-invalid" || d.startsWith("throw:")` membership predicate (do not re-author it here, and do not promote PIC's operator-tooling `MAY`-narrow to a `MUST`);
- the empty-case-unreachable claim and the `"throw:<unreadable>"` coercion-fallback restatement.

The net carve-out reads roughly: *"`<failure>` is rendered verbatim — its grammar is owned by [PIC — Unknown-reason rule]; §8's truncation/anchoring rules and the strict-equality prohibition do not apply to this row."* Implementer edge case: confirm the `<failure>` row's *Message* template (`session_shutdown pinned-constant read failed: <failure>`) and the closure-bullet (d) entry in §8's *Closure* paragraph still cross-reference PIC after the trim, so no orphaned mention of the removed predicate survives.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The `<failure>` discriminator grammar is normatively stated in two places, so any future tightening of the wire grammar (forms, escaping, truncation, fallback) must be hand-mirrored across PIC's Unknown-reason rule and the placeholder-rendering carve-out or the copies silently diverge — and PIC-48 already designates these literals anchor-stable surface requiring an inbound-reference sweep, which the duplicate enlarges. The copies agree today, so no implementer currently diverges; the live hazard is the carve-out's `MUST`-strength membership predicate, which over-states PIC's `MAY`-narrow allowance and could mislead a reader into treating the rendering page as the operator-tooling authority.

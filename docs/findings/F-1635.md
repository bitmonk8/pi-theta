## Finding

`docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-failures.md` — the *Malformed-marker discriminator* paragraph closes with: "The audit MUST surface every malformed marker under **family (5)'s discriminator**, AND MUST additionally surface the underlying out-of-inventory reference (if any) under its own family (1) / (2) / (3) discriminator on the same audit run." The phrasing routes every malformed marker — irrespective of which clause `(a)`–`(g)` of the discriminator it failed — to one singular "family (5) discriminator". Read on its own, the paragraph permits an audit implementation that emits one shared `<symptom>` token for every malformed marker (e.g. `audit/violation/<family>/malformed`).

The *Failure-surface contract* paragraph two bullets below then pins a per-`<symptom>` fixture floor that, in passing, enumerates "nine tokens total: both stale sub-kinds (s1) and (s2) plus all seven malformed-marker clauses (a)–(g); one fixture exercises one token" — i.e. each malformed-clause is meant to surface under its own distinct `<symptom>` token. The obligation that each clause `(a)`–`(g)` gets its own stable `<symptom>` token therefore lives only in a counting aside inside a fixture-floor paragraph, on the strength of a single hyphenated "sub-kind/clause" phrase. An implementer reading the *Malformed-marker discriminator* paragraph in isolation, or reading the *Failure-surface contract* paragraph without registering that the bracketed count is normative, can satisfy the singular-discriminator wording while leaving the per-clause routing collapsed — exactly the failure mode the per-`<symptom>` floor was added to prevent.

The defect is local to the *Malformed-marker discriminator* paragraph: it states the obligation at family-discriminator granularity when the failure-surface contract requires it at `<symptom>` granularity. Tightening that one sentence to route each malformed clause `(a)`–`(g)` to its own stable `<symptom>` token under family (5) closes the singular-vs-plural gap and removes the implementer's reliance on the downstream counting aside.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Rewrite the sentence in `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-failures.md`'s *Malformed-marker discriminator* paragraph that currently reads "The audit MUST surface every malformed marker under family (5)'s discriminator, AND MUST additionally surface the underlying out-of-inventory reference …" to pin per-clause routing at family-(5) granularity. Suggested wording:

> "The audit MUST surface every malformed marker under family (5), routing each violating clause (a)–(g) above to its own stable `<symptom>` token (one `<symptom>` per clause, no collapsing of clauses onto a shared token), AND MUST additionally surface the underlying out-of-inventory reference (if any) under its own family (1) / (2) / (3) discriminator on the same audit run, so the contributor sees both which malformed-marker clause failed and which underlying surface remains unauthorised."

The "no collapsing of clauses onto a shared token" parenthetical is what closes the singular-discriminator reading. The implementer still owns the literal `<symptom>` strings — PIC only pins that each clause gets a distinct one, on the same footing as the family-(5) stale sub-kinds `(s1)` and `(s2)` per *Stale sub-kinds* above. Edge cases to keep aligned: (1) the *Failure-surface contract* paragraph's parenthetical "the family has nine tokens total: both stale sub-kinds (s1) and (s2) plus all seven malformed-marker clauses (a)–(g)" must continue to read consistently with the rewritten malformed-marker paragraph — if the rewrite is landed first, leave the counting aside as-is; if a future edit rephrases either side, keep the nine-token count and the seven-clause enumeration in lock-step. (2) The *Malformed-marker dual-emission co-commit obligation* in `version-bump-step2b.md` step 2(b) branch (4) refers back to "the *Malformed-marker discriminator* above"; the rewrite does not change that anchor, so no cross-file edit is required.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A conformant audit can read the malformed-marker paragraph as authorising a single `<symptom>` token for every malformed marker, emit (e.g.) `audit/violation/<family>/malformed` for all seven clauses, and only fail the per-`<symptom>` fixture floor once an implementer notices the counting aside two paragraphs later. The downstream floor catches the collapse mechanically — so this is not a correctness gap — but the paragraph as written invites the wrong reading and makes the per-clause routing depend on the implementer cross-reading two paragraphs to extract the obligation.

## Finding

`V3a`'s **Adds** field promises two behaviours drawn from `expressions.md` §Identifier resolution: bare-identifier resolution in the four-tier shadow order `local > fn > import > callable` (first match wins, with local bindings shadowing everything lexically), and the no-match outcome `loom/parse/unknown-identifier`. The spec defines both precisely — the four numbered tiers in `expressions.md` §Identifier resolution, and `loom/parse/unknown-identifier` in `diagnostics/code-registry-parse.md` ("Bare identifier in call or value position resolves to nothing in scope").

`V3a`'s **Tests** field, however, covers only operator precedence/associativity, evaluation order and short-circuit observability, equality/ordering/arithmetic widening, and the `loom/parse/non-boolean-condition` truthiness reject. No bullet asserts the four-tier shadow ordering and no bullet asserts the `loom/parse/unknown-identifier` emission. Because `V3a` is the closing leaf for the `expressions.md` (EXPR) area under `cka-3`, the leaf can ship green with resolution mis-ordered (e.g. a `fn` or callable shadowing a local binding) or with the unknown-identifier diagnostic never emitted, and no gate witnesses the gap.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

`V3a` is the sole closing leaf for the EXPR identifier-resolution obligation, so its `cka-3` coverage entry passes vacuously for these two behaviours: two good-faith implementers could ship divergent resolution orders (or omit the `unknown-identifier` emission entirely) and both pass `npm test`. Every downstream leaf that resolves bare identifiers (bindings, functions, calls, interpolation) builds on an unverified resolution core.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add two **Tests** bullets to `V3a` (with the corresponding failing assertions authored in `V3a-T`), each citing `expressions.md` §Identifier resolution:

- A bullet asserting the four-tier shadow order: a local binding shadows a same-named top-level `fn`, import, and callable (tier 1 over 2–4), and among `fn` / import / callable the first match wins in the documented `(2) → (3) → (4)` order. Cite `expressions.md#identifier-resolution`.
- A bullet asserting that a bare identifier matching none of the four tiers fires `loom/parse/unknown-identifier`. Cite `expressions.md#identifier-resolution` and the registry row in `diagnostics/code-registry-parse.md`.

Extend `V3a`'s **Ships when** so it names these two behaviours alongside the existing precedence/short-circuit/arithmetic/`non-boolean-condition` proofs. Keep `cka-3`'s closing-leaf mapping unchanged — the obligation already belongs to `V3a`; only its asserting tests are missing.

## Problem

In `binder-bypass-and-envelope.md`, `### Binder system prompt` sits as an `H3` under `## Binder envelope`, alongside `### Binder envelope schema (constructed dynamically from params:)`. The envelope is the structured output the binder model returns; the system prompt is the input the runtime sends to that model, so nesting the input under the output inverts the producer/consumer relationship and presents the system-prompt contract as a sub-aspect of the envelope rather than a peer concern. The deep `#### System-prompt structure (normative)` heading and its eight numbered structural obligations are consequently buried two levels under "Binder envelope" in the rendered TOC, despite being cited as top-level surfaces by `V11d`/`V11d-T`, `binder-model-and-context.md`, and `pi-integration-contract/binder-inference.md`.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `c1e5d64` ("spec: REQ-ID infrastructure + Phase 12a heading promotion on five named pages", 2026-05-05); `f5e89f4` ("docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set", 2026-06-04)
**History:** Before `c1e5d64`, the binder page used bold-prefixed paragraphs throughout (`**Binder envelope.**`, `**Binder system prompt** —`, `**Binder envelope schema (constructed dynamically from params:).**`); there was no heading hierarchy to invert. `c1e5d64` promoted these flat bold prefixes to headings as part of Phase 12a, and in doing so chose `## Binder envelope` as an `H2` and grouped `### Binder envelope schema`, `### Session-context truncation`, and `### Binder system prompt` underneath it as `H3` siblings — the moment the producer/consumer inversion entered the corpus. `f5e89f4` split the monolithic `binder.md` into four child pages under `binder/` and inherited the nesting verbatim; the split also moved `### Session-context truncation` to `binder-model-and-context.md`, leaving the system-prompt heading as the lone non-envelope `H3` under the envelope umbrella and making the misgrouping more visible. The defect is the *interaction*: the heading-promotion grouping decision plus the later split that removed the sibling that gave the grouping a (weak) topical excuse.

## Solution approach

Promote `### Binder system prompt` to an `H2` sibling of `## Binder envelope`, kept after it so the producer/consumer reading order (runtime constructs and sends the prompt, binder returns the envelope) is preserved. Promote `#### System-prompt structure (normative)` to `### System-prompt structure (normative)` so its two-level subordination survives within the new sibling section.

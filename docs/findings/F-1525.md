## Finding

In `binder-bypass-and-envelope.md` the heading `### Binder system prompt` (line 81) sits as an `H3` under `## Binder envelope` (line 15), alongside its true siblings `### Binder envelope schema (constructed dynamically from params:)` (line 33). The envelope is the structured response the binder model *returns*; the system prompt is the input the runtime *sends* to that model. Nesting the input under the output inverts the producer/consumer relationship and tells the TOC reader that the system-prompt contract is a sub-aspect of the envelope rather than a peer concern.

The misnesting is also load-bearing for cross-references: `V11d`/`V11d-T`, `pi-integration-contract/binder-inference.md`, and `binder-model-and-context.md` all cite `#system-prompt-structure-normative` and `#binder-system-prompt` as if these were top-level surfaces of the page. The deep `H4` system-prompt-structure heading and the eight numbered structural obligations beneath it — the contract that `V11d` implements — are buried two levels under "Binder envelope" in the rendered TOC, making the system-prompt contract harder to discover than its centrality warrants.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `c1e5d64` ("spec: REQ-ID infrastructure + Phase 12a heading promotion on five named pages", 2026-05-05); `f5e89f4` ("docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set", 2026-06-04)
**History:** Before `c1e5d64`, the binder page used bold-prefixed paragraphs throughout (`**Binder envelope.**`, `**Binder system prompt** —`, `**Binder envelope schema (constructed dynamically from params:).**`); there was no heading hierarchy to invert. `c1e5d64` promoted these flat bold prefixes to headings as part of Phase 12a, and in doing so chose `## Binder envelope` as an `H2` and grouped `### Binder envelope schema`, `### Session-context truncation`, and `### Binder system prompt` underneath it as `H3` siblings — the moment the producer/consumer inversion entered the corpus. `f5e89f4` split the monolithic `binder.md` into four child pages under `binder/` and inherited the nesting verbatim; the split also moved `### Session-context truncation` to `binder-model-and-context.md`, leaving the system-prompt heading as the lone non-envelope `H3` under the envelope umbrella and making the misgrouping more visible. The defect is the *interaction*: the heading-promotion grouping decision plus the later split that removed the sibling that gave the grouping a (weak) topical excuse.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Promote `### Binder system prompt` (line 81) to `## Binder system prompt`, immediately after the existing `## Binder envelope` block, and promote `#### System-prompt structure (normative)` (line 110) to `### System-prompt structure (normative)` so the two-level subordination is preserved within the new sibling section. The "Type display", "Default-literal rendering", and "Parameter-line reference renderings" subsections remain as italic-titled paragraph blocks under the promoted `### System-prompt structure (normative)` heading — they have no heading level to adjust.

Edge cases for the implementer:

- The GitHub-derived slug anchors `#binder-system-prompt` and `#system-prompt-structure-normative` are unchanged by heading-level promotion (they derive from the heading text, not the level), so the four inbound citations from `V11d`/`V11d-T`, `binder-model-and-context.md`, and `pi-integration-contract/binder-inference.md` continue to resolve without rewrite.
- The explicit `<a id>` anchors elsewhere in the section (none on these two headings; the `bypass-cases`, `slash-argument-whitespace`, `bndr-1`, `bndr-2`, `bndr-3`, and `loom-1-0-seam-binder-refinement-loop` anchors all sit inside the unchanged `## Binder bypass` and `## Binder envelope` sections) are unaffected.
- Do not re-order the H2s; place `## Binder system prompt` after `## Binder envelope` so the producer/consumer reading order (the runtime constructs the prompt, sends it, the binder returns the envelope) is preserved — the inversion was only in the nesting, not the file order.
- Do not relocate the section to `binder-model-and-context.md`. That page owns the binder model resolution chain and the session-context truncation walk that *feeds* the prompt body; moving the prompt-rendering contract there would re-create a producer/consumer mismatch in the opposite direction.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

No behaviour diverges — every normative obligation under the system-prompt heading still binds — but every fresh reader of the binder subtree must mentally re-parent the system-prompt contract before reasoning about it, and the rendered TOC misleads navigators looking for input-side rules. Implementers reading top-down from the page outline are pushed past the system-prompt contract on the assumption it is a detail of the envelope schema.

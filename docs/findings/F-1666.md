## Finding

`docs/spec_topics/overview-and-orientation.md` opens with an `## Overview`
subsection composed of three paragraphs: the intro ("`pi-loom` is a Pi
Coding Agent extension…"), the `<a id="terminal-outcomes-aggregator">`
paragraph, and the `<a id="file-extension-grammar">` paragraph. Of those
three, the latter two have grown into dense architecture-level prose
that recites — not just orients toward — material owned by canonical
topic pages:

- the closed three-outcome (success / fail / cancelled) classification
  with the ceiling-#4 per-arm exclusion list (sub-clauses (a) and (b),
  including the slash-load `params` cross-routing through ceiling #3
  and the `invoke(...)` `params` arm carve-out that surfaces as
  `Err(InvokeInfraError { cause: "validation", … })`);
- the `loomAbort.signal` forwarding contract over Pi's per-handler
  `ctx.signal`, the tool-exposed `signal` parameter, and parent-`invoke`
  signals;
- the partial-append contract;
- the pre-evaluation-vs-evaluation outcome split;
- the `.loom` / `.warp` invocability and discovery rules with
  diagnostic-code routing forward-links.

Each clause carries an explicit owner forward-link, so the paragraphs
*function* as aggregators — but they are not framed as one. Every other
aggregator on the same page (the *Pi SDK and capabilities* bullet list,
*Host runtime*, *Scope*, *Non-functional requirements*, *Hard ceilings*)
opens with `*Orientation aggregator (per [Governance — GOV-30](./governance.md)).*`
and is enumerated in GOV-30's *multi-source-page* `currently:` list
([governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md#gov-30](./governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md#gov-30)).
The terminal-outcomes and file-extension-grammar paragraphs carry
neither the orientation tag nor a GOV-30 registration, so they are
governed by no aggregator-vs-source lock-step and read as primary
normative text at the most-visited entry point.

The result is a primary-entry-point Overview that the reader must walk
through clause by clause, with two anchors (`terminal-outcomes-aggregator`,
`file-extension-grammar`) advertising that other pages cite *into* this
prose. That cuts against the section's role as conceptual orientation
and creates a dual-authority drift hazard against the canonical owners
([errors-and-results/error-model.md](./errors-and-results/error-model.md),
[cancellation.md](./cancellation.md),
[pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-core.md](./pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-core.md#cancellation-source),
[imports.md](./imports.md), [discovery.md](./discovery.md)).

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Demote to orientation, push detail to topic pages

**Approach.** Trim the two Overview paragraphs to a 2–4 sentence
conceptual statement each, keep only the names of the surfaces (the
three terminal outcomes; the `.loom` / `.warp` distinction), and move
every normative-shaped clause to its canonical owner page.

**Spec edits.**

- Rewrite the `<a id="terminal-outcomes-aggregator">` paragraph to one
  sentence naming the success / fail / cancelled trichotomy and one
  forward-link to
  [Errors and Results — Terminal outcomes](./errors-and-results/error-model.md#terminal-outcomes).
  Move the ceiling-#4 exclusion sub-clauses (a) and (b), the
  `invoke(...)` `params` carve-out, and the cross-routing-through-#3
  detail into the `## Terminal outcomes` section on
  `errors-and-results/error-model.md` (if any of those clauses are not
  already restated there verbatim — most are; verify per-clause before
  deletion). Move the partial-append clause to the existing
  `#partial-append-contract` anchor on the same page. Move the
  `loomAbort.signal` forwarding clause into the matching paragraph on
  [Cancellation — Forwarding into `loomAbort`](./cancellation.md) and
  the PIC cancellation-source anchor, where the operative rules already
  live.
- Rewrite the `<a id="file-extension-grammar">` paragraph to one or two
  sentences: `.loom` is invocable as a slash command; `.warp` is a
  library module. Forward-link to
  [Imports — Permitted top-level forms](./imports.md#permitted-top-level-forms),
  [Discovery — File-extension namespace](./discovery/discovery-sources.md#file-extension-namespace),
  and the invocation page. Move the five-form enumeration parenthetical
  and the diagnostic-code-routing note to those owner pages (if not
  already present).
- Preserve both `<a id>` anchors so inbound citations from
  `errors-and-results/error-model.md`, `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md`,
  and others continue to resolve. The detached duplicate anchors on the
  same compound line (`#no-additional-ceilings`,
  `#ceiling-interaction-order`) are a separate finding's surface and
  are not touched here.

**Pros.** Restores the Overview to its labelled role; eliminates the
dual-authority drift hazard; brings the two paragraphs into structural
parity with their five GOV-30-registered siblings on the same page;
each affected clause ends up on the page whose readers most need it.

**Cons.** Inbound citations that pin to the *paragraph* anchor and
expect to find a particular clause there (e.g. a reviewer following
`#terminal-outcomes-aggregator` to read the closed enumeration) land on
a thinner orientation paragraph and must follow one extra link.
Per-clause verification that each removed sentence is already present
on the destination page is a real, manual workload, and any clause not
yet there must be transcribed before deletion.

**Risks.** A clause removed from the Overview but never landed on the
destination page becomes a silent omission; the audit must be
clause-by-clause, not paragraph-by-paragraph. Inbound link-rot — the
anchors are preserved, but a per-clause grep
(`grep -rn '#terminal-outcomes-aggregator\|#file-extension-grammar' docs/`)
must confirm no citation depends on a removed sentence.

### Option B — Promote to registered GOV-30 aggregators

**Approach.** Accept that these two paragraphs are *de facto*
aggregators and bring them under the existing GOV-30 discipline rather
than relocating their content.

**Spec edits.**

- Add the `*Orientation aggregator (per [Governance — GOV-30](./governance.md)).*`
  tag to both paragraphs, mirroring the *Host runtime*, *Scope*, and
  *Hard ceilings* siblings.
- Register the two paragraphs in GOV-30's *multi-source-page*
  `currently:` enumeration on
  [governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md#gov-30](./governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md#gov-30),
  naming each by anchor (the terminal-outcomes paragraph cites
  `errors-and-results/error-model.md`'s terminal-outcomes and
  partial-append-contract anchors, `cancellation.md`, the PIC
  cancellation-source anchor, and the ceiling-#3 / ceiling-#4 surfaces;
  the file-extension-grammar paragraph cites `imports.md`,
  `discovery.md`, `invocation.md`, and `slash-invocation.md`). The
  GOV-30 lock-step MUST then binds the spec corpus to keep them in
  parity with their owner pages.
- Trim duplicative phrasing where the owner page already states the
  rule; leave the orientation and the forward-links.

**Pros.** Preserves the citation-target anchors and current inbound
links verbatim; minimal disruption to other pages; the GOV-30 tag is a
1-line edit per paragraph; the lock-step trigger becomes the long-term
drift defence.

**Cons.** The "orientation" frame still has to absorb 6–8 clauses per
paragraph, so reader cognitive load at the entry point only partially
improves; the section title is still `## Overview`, which connotes
"conceptual model" rather than "aggregator hub". GOV-30 lock-step is
reviewer-enforced (no mechanical witness), so the drift hazard is
mitigated but not eliminated. Adding two more aggregators to GOV-30's
already-long `currently:` enumeration grows the review surface every PR
that touches an owner page must consider.

**Risks.** Registering two paragraphs whose source-set spans 6+ topic
pages each makes the GOV-30 1:1 referent-identity criterion harder for
reviewers to apply on PR; under pressure this devolves to "looks
roughly right" and silent drift continues.

### Recommendation

**Option A.** The defect is "architecture-level rules at the primary
entry point", and Option B leaves the rules at the primary entry point.
Option A's per-clause migration is the more invasive edit but it is the
one that resolves the finding rather than annotating around it, and the
five GOV-30 sibling aggregators on the same page demonstrate the
1-sentence-plus-link form the surviving paragraphs should take.

Implementer must watch:

- The `terminal-outcomes-aggregator` and `file-extension-grammar`
  anchors stay where they are; the paragraphs *behind* them shrink, but
  inbound citations are not repointed.
- Before deleting any clause from the Overview, confirm with a literal
  grep that the destination page carries that clause; transcribe it
  there in the same commit if not.
- The ceiling-#4 sub-clauses (a) and (b) belong on
  `errors-and-results/error-model.md#terminal-outcomes` and
  `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md#ceiling-4-table` per the existing
  ownership pattern; the carve-out for `invoke(...)`'s `params` arm
  surfacing as `Err(InvokeInfraError { cause: "validation", … })` is
  owned by [Invocation — Failures](./invocation.md) and the destination
  there must be checked too.
- The "Failures that occur before evaluation begins" sentence
  is content that `errors-and-results/error-model.md#terminal-outcomes`
  already owns (its ERR-N enumeration); keep one orientation sentence
  pointing there and delete the inline restatement.
- The retained Overview prose should not carry MUSTs; if any survives
  the trim, it belongs on a topic page.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The implementation is reachable through the forward-links, so no
implementer is blocked. The costs are reader load at the spec's primary
entry point, an asymmetry against the sibling aggregators on the same
page (which a reviewer is likely to spot and re-raise), and a drift
hazard: the terminal-outcomes paragraph already inlines normative-shaped
prose (the ceiling-#4 sub-clauses (a) and (b)) that, untagged and
unregistered under GOV-30, has no lock-step trigger when the canonical
owners edit those rules.

## Problem

`V5d-T`'s Tests block lists five bullets, but three describe behaviour `V5d` does not implement: the `loom/load/schema-slug-collision` detection bullet, the SHA-256 canonical-hash → 16-hex-slug bullet, and the per-`$defs` two-map sidecar bullet. These three are byte-identical to `V5f-T`'s Tests, absent from `V5d`'s own Tests, and explicitly disowned by `V5d`'s Adds (lowering and the canonical-hash recipe are owned by `V5f`). The lowering/hash work that would satisfy them lands in `V5f`, which is sequenced after `V5d` and `V5f-T`, so `V5d` can never turn those bullets green at its exit gate while `V5f-T` carries the same rows as its own legitimate red-then-green target. Both TDD exit gates become mutually unsatisfiable and the rows are duplicated across two `-T` leaves with no single owner.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-by-commit
**Introducing commit:** `cb8737a` — "pi-loom plan: resolve \"Systemic leaf over-bundling across the plan corpus\"" (Thomas Andersen, 2026-06-12)
**History:** The slug-collision and canonical-hash bullets were authored in `V5d-T` at corpus inception (`c6a664e`, 2026-06-10), and the per-`$defs` sidecar bullet was added by `ba7da00` (2026-06-10, "V5d lowering-pass outputs bound by no Tests bullet"); at that point `V5d` still owned the lowering/hash work, so the bullets were correctly placed. The defect was introduced by `cb8737a`, the over-bundling split: it created the new `V5f` / `V5f-T` leaves from `V5d`'s lowering/hash content, rewrote `V5d`'s Adds/Tests/Ships-when to disown lowering and hashing, and copied the three bullets into `V5f-T` — but did not strip them from `V5d-T` (that file is absent from `cb8737a`'s change set). The bullets became stale at the moment ownership moved, leaving the two `-T` leaves duplicated.

## Solution approach

In `docs/plan_topics/V5d-T-subset-lowering.md`, delete the three Tests bullets `V5d` disowns: the `loom/load/schema-slug-collision` detection bullet, the SHA-256 canonical-hash → first-16-hex-slug bullet, and the per-`$defs` two-map sidecar bullet (the schema-subset.md Lowering Algorithm step 5 bullet). The remaining reject-gate bullet and the `Result`-in-schema-position / array-element-order bullet then align 1:1 with `V5d`'s Tests and Ships-when, making `V5d`'s exit gate satisfiable. The `V5d-T` heading's lowering/hash over-claim is the naming root cause and is tracked by a related finding (see Relationships).

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: `V5f-T` keeps these three bullets — it is their correct owner, so do not edit it.
- The spec pages are read-only for this fix; do not move any obligation into the spec.

## Finding

`V6c`'s `loom/load/unresolvable-loom-path` test rejects a `tools:` `.loom` entry "whose path does not exist or is not readable" at load time. Determining existence/readability is a filesystem probe, and the *No globals, statics, singletons* convention bans direct ambient filesystem access in `src/**` — every file read must be routed through the `V8b` `FileSystem` seam (`docs/plan_topics/conventions.md` §"No globals, statics, singletons"; the only legitimate references live inside the seam-adapter modules).

`V6c`'s `Deps` are `V6c-T, V6a, V15a, V9f`. It never names `V8b`, and its `Adds`/`Tests` prose does not state that another leaf performs the probe on its behalf — the diagnostic is attributed to `V6c` ("rejected at load time"), implying `V6c` itself does the filesystem touch. This is unlike `V1a`, whose `Adds` names "the `V8b` `FileSystem` byte-read seam" and lists `V8b` in `Deps`, satisfying the Class-2 seam-binding discipline in `docs/plan_topics/conventions.md` §"Leaf format" (**Deps**).

The plan leaves the locus of the probe undetermined. `V15a` (already in `V6c`'s `Deps`) owns the static-resolution per-pass walk that "walks transitively from the entry loom across literal `invoke` paths and `.loom` `tools:` entries, parsing and lowering each visited file," and `V15a` lists `V8b` in its own `Deps` — so the filesystem touch that surfaces an unresolvable `.loom` path could naturally live there, with `V6c` only observing the result. But nothing in either leaf states this. Two reasonable implementers diverge: one adds a direct probe inside `V6c` (which then has no seam in `Deps` and risks an ambient-access architectural violation), the other routes it through `V15a`'s resolution and treats `V6c` as a pure observer.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two good-faith implementers produce divergent architectures for the same diagnostic: one performs an undeclared filesystem probe inside `V6c` (no `V8b` seam in `Deps`, risking a direct ambient-access violation the `H3a` scan or release-time residue inspection then flags), the other routes it through `V15a` and leaves `V6c` as an observer. The `unresolvable-loom-path` behaviour can ship attributed to a leaf that holds no seam to perform it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — `V6c` performs the probe directly

**Approach.** Treat the existence/readability probe as `V6c`'s own work, performed through the `V8b` `FileSystem` seam.
**Plan edits.** Add `V8b` to `V6c`'s `Deps` (and `V6c-T`'s `Deps`); add a clause to `V6c`'s `Adds` naming the `V8b` `FileSystem` seam as the surface through which the `.loom`-path existence/readability check runs, mirroring `V1a`'s "the `V8b` `FileSystem` byte-read seam" phrasing.
**Spec edits.** None.
**Pros.** Keeps the `unresolvable-loom-path` probe co-located with the leaf the diagnostic is attributed to; the seam binding is explicit and satisfies the Class-2 *names the seam* discipline directly at `V6c`.
**Cons.** Duplicates a filesystem touch `V15a`'s static-resolution walk already performs over the same `.loom` `tools:` entries, raising the possibility of two probe sites for the same path resolution.
**Risks.** If `V15a`'s walk and `V6c`'s probe disagree on resolution semantics (relative-root base, `realpath` containment), the same path could resolve differently in the two passes.

### Option B — `V15a`'s static-resolution surfaces it; `V6c` observes

**Approach.** Locate the probe in `V15a`'s static-resolution walk (which already reads each `.loom` `tools:` entry through its `V8b` dependency) and have `V6c` observe the result.
**Plan edits.** State in `V6c`'s `Adds`/`Tests` that the `unresolvable-loom-path` rejection is surfaced by `V15a`'s static-resolution of `.loom` `tools:` entries and `V6c` observes that result (a Class-2 prose reference that resolves to `V15a`'s resolution mechanism); add to `V15a`'s `Adds`/`Tests` that its static-resolution pass over `.loom` `tools:` entries surfaces `loom/load/unresolvable-loom-path` for a `.loom` literal that resolves to no readable file. The existing `V6c → V15a` `Deps` edge and `V15a → V8b` edge already carry the seam transitively.
**Spec edits.** None.
**Pros.** Single filesystem-probe site shared with the transitive parse/lower walk that already visits each `.loom` `tools:` path; no new `Deps` edge needed; aligns with `V15a`'s stated ownership of `.loom`-path resolution.
**Cons.** Splits the `unresolvable-loom-path` diagnostic's authoring across two leaves (`V15a` raises it, `V6c` observes it).
**Risks.** `V6c-T`'s test must drive the path through the `V15a` resolution surface rather than a direct probe, so the tests task depends on `V15a`'s resolution being exercisable in isolation.

### Recommendation

Take Option B. `V15a`'s `Adds` already declares the static-resolution per-pass walk that visits each `.loom` `tools:` entry through its `V8b` seam; the `unresolvable-loom-path` failure is the natural product of that read, so concentrating the probe there avoids a second filesystem touch and a new `Deps` edge. Add to `V15a` a Tests/Adds statement that its static-resolution surfaces `loom/load/unresolvable-loom-path`, and reword `V6c`'s `unresolvable-loom-path` bullet to observe `V15a`'s resolution result rather than imply `V6c` probes the disk; mirror the reword in `V6c-T`. Watch the edge case where a `tools:` `.loom` path is syntactically valid and discovery-root-contained but unreadable (permissions): both the `INV-1` containment check and the readability check must surface as `loom/load/unresolvable-loom-path` from the same `V15a` pass, distinct from the `InvokeInfraError{load_failure}` containment-escape channel.

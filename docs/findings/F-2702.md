## Finding

The `## Blocked obligations` entry for `V9l` carries the full contingent
implementation contract for the "branch unreachable as written" resolution
outcome: *"`V9l` instead collapses to full teardown + `/reload` with no degraded
tag write, and its degraded-branch obligations are reworked spec-side
(`session-only-degraded-state.md` and `SM-4`/`SM-5`/`SM-6`/`SM-3b`) before the
leaf ships."* That is a statement of what code/spec rework ships under one
resolution branch — leaf-level acceptance content. `## Blocked obligations` is an
index/tracking surface: it records which leaves are gated, on what open question,
and who owns the deciding resolution.

The `V9l` leaf (`docs/plan_topics/V9l-session-only-degraded-branch.md`) currently
carries no fallback contract of its own; its **Ships when** field only bounces the
reader back to the index (*"the pre-designed fallback in plan.md §Blocked
obligations governs what ships"*). An implementer who picks `V9l` once the block
resolves reads the leaf, then has to follow an indirection into the plan index to
learn what the unreachable-branch outcome requires.

Note the history (see `## Issue introduction`): the current placement is the
deliberate output of a prior plan-review fix that resolved a finding arguing the
*opposite* placement. This finding and that prior finding are direct inverses, so
a naive "move it all back to the leaf" reversal would simply re-fire the finding
the prior fix closed. The durable fix splits the content by role rather than
relocating it wholesale.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The leaf is reachable only after `V18c` resolves the block, and its Ships-when
already cross-references the index, so no implementer is hard-blocked. The cost is
indirection: the index/tracking surface carries a multi-clause implementation
contract that the picking implementer must follow out of the leaf to read, and the
leaf cannot be read self-contained for "what ships under each resolution outcome."

## Issue introduction

- `9c42f2d` (2026-06-16, "pi-loom plan: resolve 'V9l fallback path belongs in §Blocked obligations'") — moved the fallback contract off the leaf into `plan.md` `## Blocked obligations`, leaving the leaf with only a back-pointer. This established the placement the present finding flags.

The present finding asserts the opposite ("contract belongs on the leaf"). The two findings oscillate; a wholesale reversal re-introduces the exact defect `9c42f2d` closed, so the fix must allocate content by role (block-tracking vs leaf-acceptance) rather than relocate the whole paragraph.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Split the contingency content by role so that both the index's tracking concern
and the leaf's self-containment concern are satisfied, ending the oscillation.

- In `docs/plan.md` `## Blocked obligations`, the `V9l` entry retains the
  block-tracking content only: the block description, the host-prerequisites
  clause (a) open contradiction, the deciding-resolution owner
  (`version-bump-step2.md#bump-checklist-instance-survival`, tracked by `V18c`'s
  **Deps** edge), and a one-line statement that the resolution may find the branch
  unreachable as written, in which case `V9l`'s leaf-level fallback contract
  governs what ships. Strike the concrete implementation specifics (the "collapses
  to full teardown + `/reload` with no degraded tag write, and its degraded-branch
  obligations are reworked spec-side …" clause) from the index entry.

- In `docs/plan_topics/V9l-session-only-degraded-branch.md`, restore the concrete
  fallback contract as a contingent note on the leaf (the Adds field is its prior
  home per `55ed974`): if clause (a) resolves *branch unreachable as written*,
  `V9l` collapses to full teardown + `/reload` with no degraded tag write, and the
  degraded-branch obligations are reworked spec-side
  (`session-only-degraded-state.md` and the session-model identifiers the index
  names) before the leaf ships. The unknown-reason / closed-set / snapshot-failure
  obligations stay in `V9h`.

- Update the leaf **Ships when** so the *branch unreachable* clause references the
  leaf's own fallback note rather than bouncing to `plan.md` §Blocked obligations.

The index stays the authoritative tracking surface for the block (honoring the
intent of `9c42f2d`); the leaf becomes self-contained for "what ships under each
outcome" (honoring this finding). The `plan.md` §Blocked obligations entry is the
single owner of the block-tracking prose; the `V9l` leaf is the single owner of the
fallback implementation contract — neither restates the other beyond a one-line
cross-reference.

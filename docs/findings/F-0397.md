## Finding

`V15a` (Invocation core) carries four concerns that are each independently observable and testable: (1) path + `realpath` discovery-root containment (`INV-1`, checked at both load-time and invocation-time on two channels), (2) the static-resolution per-pass parse cache and its transitive parse/lower walk (depended on by `V15b`, `V15c`, `V15e`, `V15f`), (3) the fresh-vs-attach cross-mode matrix selection, and (4) propagation of the callee's produced final value to the `invoke` caller on success (absent on fail/cancel). Its **Tests** and **Ships when** correspondingly stack `INV-1`/`INV-2`/`INV-3`, the static-resolution walk, the `return.md` final-value contract, and the cross-mode matrix into one impl + `-T` pair.

A clean split of (1)–(3) is impractical: the cross-mode matrix (3) presupposes a loaded, cached callee AST (2), which presupposes containment-validated paths (1) — they form a causal chain that cannot be picked up in isolation. Only facet (4), final-value propagation, is cleanly peelable: it sits at the success boundary, depends on the `V3d` function-result seam, and nothing else in the slice gates on it via a `Deps` edge (the downstream `V15*` leaves depend on `V15a` for the parse cache and invoke core, not for final-value propagation).

This is a borderline, advisory observation, not a defect: `V15a` is comprehensible and buildable as written. The residue is what remains after earlier splits already peeled prompt-suspend, the six parse/load diagnostics, the provenance seam, and the ceiling surfaces out of the original (larger) invoke-core leaf.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Peel final-value propagation into a thin leaf

**Approach.** Move facet (4) out of `V15a` into a new leaf (the source suggests `V15a-ret`; the implementer picks the final ID). The peeled leaf owns the `return.md` final-value contract against the `V3d` function-result seam.

**Plan edits.**
- `V15a` **Adds.** — strike the clause `propagating the callee's produced final value to the invoke caller on success (absent on fail/cancel)`.
- `V15a` **Tests.** — remove the `return.md — final-value contract` bullet.
- `V15a` **Ships when.** — remove the trailing `and asserts the callee's produced final value propagates … and is absent on fail/cancel` clause.
- `V15a-T` — mirror the same removal of the `return.md` final-value bullet.
- New leaf `<new>` (+ its `<new>-T` partner) — owns the final-value contract test; **Deps** include `V15a` and `V3d`.
- `docs/plan.md` — add the new leaf's link under `### V15 — Invocation and imports`.
- `docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md` — re-point the `return.md` (RET) final-value facet from `V15a` to the new leaf so its closing-gate mapping stays satisfied.

**Spec edits.** None.

**Pros.** Each remaining leaf is independently testable; the final-value facet gets its own observable completion tag; smaller per-leaf surface.

**Cons.** Adds a paired leaf + `-T` partner + `Deps` wiring + a coverage-matrix re-point for a low-severity gain; final-value propagation is a thin slice whose paired-leaf overhead may exceed the atomicity benefit.

**Risks.** If the `return.md` final-value coverage-matrix facet is not re-pointed to the new leaf, the closing-gate sweep flags an uncovered RET facet.

### Option B — Accept `V15a` as-is

**Approach.** Leave the four concerns in `V15a`. The (1)→(2)→(3) chain cannot be cleanly peeled, and the leaf is buildable and comprehensible as written.

**Plan edits.** None.

**Spec edits.** None.

**Pros.** Zero churn; no new `Deps` wiring or coverage-matrix edits; the four concerns are tightly coupled at runtime.

**Cons.** `V15a` stays the largest invoke-slice leaf; per-facet partial completion is not separately observable.

**Risks.** None material.

### Recommendation

Take **Option B** unless a concrete divergence emerges. The finding is advisory; facets (1)–(3) are a causal chain that resists a clean split, and final-value propagation — though peelable — is a thin slice whose paired-leaf overhead exceeds the atomicity benefit at this severity. Revisit Option A only if the unnamed callee-mode/path-readability resolution seam (related finding below) lands new content on the cross-mode-matrix facet and pushes `V15a` materially larger; at that point peel final-value first so the seam work lands on a smaller baseline. If Option A is taken, re-point the `return.md` final-value coverage-matrix facet to the new leaf in the same change.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

If shipped unsplit, `V15a` remains the largest leaf in the invoke slice and the partial completion of any single facet (e.g. final-value propagation) is not separately observable through its own `<id>-complete` tag — the whole bundle lands or none of it does. No implementer is blocked and no spec rule goes uncovered; the cost is a larger per-leaf review/critique surface, not a correctness gap.

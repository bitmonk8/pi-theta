## Finding

`V18b` (Inventory-closure audit gate) carries two concerns with distinct verification paths inside one leaf. The first is bounded scanner construction: the build-time surface-set-closure audit, the category-(1)/(2)/(3) recogniser detection, the non-exemptible family-(4) out-of-scope discriminator, the `audit/<class>/<family>/<symptom>` record grammar, and the fail-closed non-empty canary. These are verified by red/green assertions against seeded fixtures (`V18b-T`). The second is an open-ended retrofit: the `Adds` bullet also makes `V18b` own "the land-green `src/` sweep the spec assigns to the audit-introducing commit" — every existing `src/` Pi-side reference the new audit would surface must be deleted, promoted to an `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` entry, marked `// allow-pi-surface:`, or rewritten, so the audit lands green rather than red on `main`.

The sweep's scope is not fixed by `V18b`'s own design; it grows with every prior runtime leaf that introduces Pi-side references (`V13c`, `V14a`, `V15a`, `V17a`, and others). `V18b`'s `Ships when` reflects this coupling: it requires `npm test` to run the closure audit **green on `main`**, which cannot hold until the full codebase-wide retrofit is complete. The leaf therefore mixes a self-contained, fixture-testable scanner with a whole-tree green bar whose surface area is owned by the rest of the plan.

The spec genuinely mandates that the audit and its land-green sweep land in the same introducing commit (`inventory-audit-intro.md` §Inventory-closure audit: "an implementation whose first `npm test` invocation against `main` is red on this audit violates this paragraph"). The atomicity concern is about the plan's leaf granularity, not about the commit grouping: a single leaf carries two verification paths and an open-ended, externally-scoped completion bar.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 402bdbe — pi-loom plan: resolve "V18b Adds omits broader-inventory population, taxonomy, and land-green sweep" (2026-06-11, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The audit gate itself has been in `V18b` since the plan corpus's first commit (c6a664e, 2026-06-10), where the `Adds` bullet covered only the scanner, recogniser detection, and canary. The land-green `src/` sweep clause was appended to `V18b`'s `Adds` in 402bdbe, a prior plan-review resolution that broadened the leaf to also own the spec-assigned same-commit retrofit; that edit is what coupled the bounded scanner with the open-ended sweep in a single leaf.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Split scanner construction from the land-green sweep

**Approach.** Keep `V18b`/`V18b-T` as the scanner leaf — recogniser detection, family-(4) discriminator, record grammar, and canary, exercised against seeded fixtures only, with the audit **not yet wired to run over the real `src/` tree** in `npm test`. Add a new leaf that, in one commit, wires the audit over `src/**/*.ts` in `npm test` and performs the land-green retrofit of every existing `src/` Pi-side reference. The new sweep leaf depends on the scanner leaf.

**Plan edits.** Narrow `V18b`/`V18b-T` to the fixture-tested scanner; add `<new>` (sweep) and `<new>` (sweep tests); retarget the `Deps` of `V18c` and `V18c-T` from `V18b` to the sweep leaf — the one that lands the audit green on `main`.

**Spec edits.** None. The split stays inside `inventory-audit-intro.md`'s land-green mandate provided the scanner leaf does not enable the audit over the live `src/` tree; the audit goes live over `src/` only in the sweep commit, alongside the retrofit.

**Pros.** Each leaf has one verification path — fixture red/green for the scanner, full-codebase green for the sweep. The externally-scoped sweep is isolated from scanner construction; both estimation and review improve.

**Cons.** Requires care that the scanner leaf does not wire the audit over real `src/` (else it lands red on `main` and violates the spec). Adds two leaves and a `Deps` retarget through `V18c`/`V18c-T`.

**Risks.** A careless split lands the audit red on `main` (spec violation under `inventory-audit-intro.md` §Inventory-closure audit). A missed `Deps` retarget leaves `V18c` gated on the scanner leaf rather than the green-on-main sweep leaf.

### Option B — Keep one leaf; surface the two verification paths in its acceptance criteria

**Approach.** Leave `V18b` bundled — it mirrors the spec's same-commit land-green obligation directly — but make the two distinct signals explicit so the implementer does not under-scope the leaf: (1) the scanner fires red against a seeded off-inventory fixture and green against recognised shapes; (2) `npm test` runs the closure audit green on `main` after the retrofit.

**Plan edits.** `V18b`'s `Ships when` already names both signals ("green on `main` and red against a seeded off-inventory reference"); reinforce in `Adds` that the sweep's surface is the whole audited `src/` tree, not the scanner's fixtures.

**Spec edits.** None.

**Pros.** Honors the spec's single-commit land-green obligation with no dependency churn; smallest diff.

**Cons.** The leaf stays large and its completion bar remains coupled to an open-ended sweep whose surface grows with prior runtime leaves; one leaf still carries two verification paths.

**Risks.** An implementer estimates `V18b` as scanner-only and is surprised by the sweep's codebase-wide scope.

### Recommendation

Take Option B unless the team has independent reason to want the split. The spec binds the audit and its land-green sweep to the same introducing commit, so splitting buys atomicity at the cost of a real spec-violation hazard (a scanner leaf that wires the audit live lands red on `main`). Importance is low and Option B carries no dependency churn.

If the split is preferred for reviewability, resolve the scanner leaf first — scanner against seeded fixtures only, audit not yet live over `src/` — so the sweep lands on a stable scanner baseline; then the sweep leaf wires the audit over `src/` and performs the retrofit in one commit; then retarget `V18c`/`V18c-T` `Deps` onto the sweep leaf. Edge case to watch: the scanner leaf's `Ships when` must not assert a green `npm test` over real `src/`, or it inherits the same open-ended bar the split was meant to remove.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The leaf is implementable, but its completion bar is decoupled from its own content: an implementer scoping `V18b` as scanner construction is then obligated to retrofit every Pi-side reference across `src/`, a surface that other leaves continue to grow, before `npm test` goes green on `main`. This degrades estimation and review (two unrelated verification paths in one diff) without producing a spec-divergent or unpickable leaf.

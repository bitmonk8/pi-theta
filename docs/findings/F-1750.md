## Finding

SLSH-4 declares the per-`kind` system-note shapes in the SNK-a..SNK-k table
**normative templates** that conformance tests MAY assert on byte-for-byte,
and pins the surrounding template text as verbatim. Inside that surround,
ten distinct `<…>` placeholders appear across the rows — `<name>`, `<n>`,
`<message>`, `<tool_name>`, `<cause>`, `<rounds>`, `<last_tool_name>`,
`<callee_path>`, `<inner.kind>`, `<kind>` — but the section binds none of
them to a `QueryError` field, and gives no rendering rule for the four
placeholders whose source field is a snake_case wire enum (`<cause>` on
SNK-g/SNK-i, `<inner.kind>` on SNK-j, `<kind>` on SNK-k). The umbrella
preamble's "free-form content sourced from a model" carve-out names only
`<message>`, leaving the other placeholders' rendering provenance silent.

The cross-file defaults do not close the gap: `cause` on `CodeToolError`
and `InvokeInfraError` is declared as a closed string union of snake_case
literals (`"load_failure"` | `"parse_failure"` | `"validation"` | …) and
[`queryerror-variants.md` — `kind` discriminator] pins the `kind` field as
the snake_case wire form, but neither says whether the renderer for SNK-g
emits `(validation)` verbatim or rephrases it as `(validation error)` /
`("validation")`, nor whether SNK-i emits `(load_failure)` or
`(load failure)`. Two conforming implementations will therefore produce
divergent byte-strings — and divergent passes against conformance — for
the same `QueryError`, defeating the normative-template MUST.

The sibling pages this section is the structural analogue of do bind their
placeholders: `binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md` pins
`<provider>` to `TransportError.provider` and `<ajv-summary>` to a stable
join over `ValidationIssue` entries, and
`diagnostics/placeholder-rendering-a.md` enumerates every diagnostic
placeholder by category with one rendering rule per category. SLSH-4 is
out of step with its peers.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `53f5831` (2026-05-03), `907c029` (2026-05-04),
`b3bc4ce` (2026-05-04)
**History:** `53f5831` (spec: rewrite query primitive…) introduced the
five-row per-`kind` table inside `spec.md`'s slash-invocation section with
unbound placeholders `<n>`, `<message>`, `<tool_name>`. `907c029` (resolve
"QueryError union has three conflicting authoritative-shaped definitions")
added three further rows extending the same unbound-placeholder pattern
(`<cause>`, `<reason>`, `<callee_path>`, `<inner.kind>`). `b3bc4ce`
(2026-05-04, spec: pin failure-observability surface) then promoted the
table to a **normative template** with a byte-verbatim MUST and a
conformance-test hook, sharpening the defect: every prior row's
unspecified rendering became a conformance question without ever being
answered. Subsequent edits (`98ba4fe` splitting `validation` into SNK-a /
SNK-b; `9bd29ab` renaming `<reason>` to `<cause>`; `36cc8d9` renaming
`<iterations>` to `<rounds>`; `27b2576` adding the SNK-a..SNK-k inline
labels) all touched the same rows without ever introducing per-placeholder
field bindings or the enum-rendering rule. The defect is an interaction
between the placeholder set's organic growth and the late normativity
tightening — no single commit is at fault, and the durable fix addresses
the missing binding subsection, not any one of those commits.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

After the SNK table and before the existing "The table is exhaustive…"
paragraph, add a short normative subsection (label-free or a single SNK
label, at the author's discretion) titled "Placeholder bindings" with two
clauses.

**Clause 1 — per-placeholder field source.** A bullet list, one entry per
placeholder used in the table, naming the `QueryError` field whose value
fills it. Concretely:

- `<name>` — the slash-invoked loom's command name (filename without
  `.loom`), per the section's opening sentence.
- `<n>` — `ValidationError.attempts` (per the SNK-a row's wording, the
  number of respond-repair follow-up turns; cross-reference to
  `queryerror-variants.md` §Query-time variants).
- `<message>` — the `message` field of the leaf `QueryError`
  (`TransportError.message` on SNK-c, `ModelToolError.message` on SNK-d,
  `CodeToolError.message` on SNK-g, the catch-all variant's `message` on
  SNK-k), rendered verbatim. State the empty-string fallback explicitly
  (emit the surrounding template with `<message>` as the empty string —
  the runtime never substitutes a placeholder string of its own; the same
  rule the binder page applies to `<provider>` empties).
- `<tool_name>` — `ModelToolError.tool_name` on SNK-d,
  `CodeToolError.tool_name` on SNK-g.
- `<cause>` — `CodeToolError.cause` on SNK-g, `InvokeInfraError.cause`
  on SNK-i (rendering rule in clause 2).
- `<rounds>` — `ToolLoopExhaustedError.rounds`, rendered as the shortest
  decimal integer (defer to `diagnostics/placeholder-rendering-a.md` §4
  for the numeric rendering form to avoid duplicating the rule).
- `<last_tool_name>` — `ToolLoopExhaustedError.last_tool_name`, with the
  existing `null` → literal `respond` carve-out kept in its current
  location.
- `<callee_path>` — `InvokeInfraError.callee_path` on SNK-i,
  `InvokeCalleeError.callee_path` on SNK-j; cross-reference to SLSH-5's
  `<callee_path>` derivation paragraph (post-`realpath` absolute path
  recorded at the invocation site) so a single derivation is canonical.
- `<inner.kind>` — `InvokeCalleeError.inner.kind` on SNK-j (rendering
  rule in clause 2).
- `<kind>` — the leaf `QueryError.kind` on SNK-k (rendering rule in
  clause 2).

**Clause 2 — wire-enum verbatim.** Enum-valued placeholders — explicitly
`<cause>`, `<kind>`, and `<inner.kind>` — render the wire literal
verbatim, as the snake_case string carried by the corresponding
`QueryError` field. The renderer MUST NOT humanise (no
`load_failure` → `"load failure"`, no `invoke_callee` → `"invoke callee
error"`, no quoting beyond what the surrounding template's backticks
provide). The justification is one sentence: `kind` is pinned as the wire
form at [`queryerror-variants.md` — `kind` discriminator], and the
`cause` fields are declared as closed snake_case literal unions in their
respective variant schemas; both are stable wire contract, and the
byte-verbatim SLSH-4 MUST cannot be satisfied if the renderer is free to
rephrase them.

Edge case the implementer must handle: a forward-compatible `kind` value
not in the loom 1.0 closed set arrives on SNK-k (catch-all). Clause 2's
"verbatim wire literal" rule applies unchanged — the renderer emits
whatever the field carries, in line with the variant-of-`QueryError`
type-openness seam (ERR-15).

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two reasonable implementers will produce divergent byte-strings for the
same `QueryError` — most visibly on SNK-g (`<cause>` rendering),
SNK-i (`<cause>` rendering), SNK-j (`<inner.kind>` rendering), and
SNK-k (`<kind>` rendering) — and either implementation can claim to
satisfy SLSH-4's MUST. The byte-verbatim conformance contract that SLSH-4
exists to establish is unenforceable for these rows, and `V12b`'s
per-row test bullets cannot be authored against the spec as written.

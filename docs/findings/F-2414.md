## Finding

The inventory-audit corpus mixes two phases of obligation on the same pages without a legible boundary. `inventory-audit-intro.md` opens with the loom-1.0 **PIC-15 SDK capability inventory** (a 1.0 MUST), then flows — with no visible divider — into the **Inventory-closure audit** paragraph (an explicit post-1.0 hardening the implementation only *MAY* publish), and then into the **Strict-capability absence-under-the-probed-name arm**, which is again a loom-1.0 MUST ("MUST additionally fail …") relocated onto this page. `audit-target-categories.md` carries a top-of-page blockquote stamping the whole page as "post-1.0 hardening", yet the same page states two loom-1.0 step-2(a) obligations as normative MUSTs buried inside multi-hundred-word paragraphs: the **minimum-entry-shape enforcement** ("The SDK surface-inventory literal-read assertion … MUST additionally enforce the minimum-entry-shape obligation …") and the **branch-table-arm precondition** ("The … branch table MUST gain an arm for each new kind … before the first `npm test` invocation that runs the audit on `main`").

The page does carry one carve-out sentence ("the positive-direction step-2(a) literal-read-assertion obligations referenced on this page … are loom-1.0 gates and are not affected"), so the phase ownership is *recoverable* by a careful reader. But the loom-1.0 normative text remains physically interleaved with post-1.0 audit-detection mechanics under a post-1.0 banner, with no per-obligation phase label, no visual separator, and no consolidated index of what is deferred. There is no Non-goals section on `inventory-audit-intro.md` itself (only `version-bump-intro.md` carries one, scoped to the bump procedure), so the post-1.0 deferral of the automated audit is discoverable only by reading the inline prose.

The net effect is that the loom-1.0/post-1.0 phase boundary across the inventory-audit pages is not legible at a glance, and the loom-1.0 gates that must ship for 1.0 are the hardest part of the corpus to extract from the post-1.0 mechanics they are tangled with.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — In-place phase separation

- **Approach:** Leave the loom-1.0 step-2(a) obligations where they are, but make the phase boundary legible: insert a visible phase divider between PIC-15 and the post-1.0 Inventory-closure audit paragraph on `inventory-audit-intro.md`; lift each loom-1.0 gate (minimum-entry-shape enforcement, branch-table-arm precondition, strict-capability absence arm) out of its surrounding 2000-word paragraph into its own marked sub-block tagged `loom-1.0 gate`; and add a short Non-goals index on `inventory-audit-intro.md` (mirroring `version-bump-intro.md` §Non-goals — a scannable list of cross-links, not a restatement) naming the deferred post-1.0 items and forward-linking the project-wide `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md` §loom 1.0 non-goals.
- **Spec edits:** `inventory-audit-intro.md` (phase divider, sub-block extraction of the absence arm, Non-goals index); `audit-target-categories.md` (extract the minimum-entry-shape MUST and branch-table-arm MUST into their own `loom-1.0 gate` sub-blocks, keeping them on-page next to the post-1.0 mechanics they reference).
- **Pros:** Minimal cross-reference churn; the obligations stay adjacent to the audit context that motivates them; the existing bidirectional step-2(a)↔audit cross-references stay intact.
- **Cons:** The loom-1.0 gates remain physically on pages whose banner reads "post-1.0 hardening"; legibility improves but the structural smell (1.0 MUSTs on post-1.0 pages) persists.
- **Risks:** A future reader could still treat the page banner as authoritative over the per-block tags; the phase tags must be unambiguous to avoid that.

### Option B — Relocate loom-1.0 obligations to their step-2(a) owner

- **Approach:** Move the minimum-entry-shape enforcement, the branch-table-arm precondition, and the strict-capability absence arm out of the audit pages and into `version-bump-step2.md` §step 2(a) (the literal-read assertion that already owns these obligations by cross-reference), leaving the audit pages to carry only the post-1.0 negative-direction detection mechanics under their post-1.0 banner. Add the same Non-goals index on `inventory-audit-intro.md`, plus a back-reference from the audit pages to the relocated gates' new home.
- **Spec edits:** `version-bump-step2.md` (absorb the three relocated MUSTs); `inventory-audit-intro.md` and `audit-target-categories.md` (delete the relocated MUSTs, leave a one-line cross-reference, add the Non-goals index); update the `V18b`/`V18c` **Spec**-field cross-references.
- **Pros:** Clean phase separation — the post-1.0-banner pages become genuinely post-1.0; the loom-1.0 gates live with the rest of the step-2(a) mechanical-gate prose.
- **Cons:** Heavy cross-reference rework (the obligations are bidirectionally cross-referenced today); risk of orphaning the audit-context the obligations are currently stated against; `version-bump-step2.md` is already very dense.
- **Risks:** A relocation that drops the surrounding context (e.g. why the minimum-entry-shape obligation exists, which kinds it scopes) can degrade the obligation into an unmotivated MUST; the move must carry the scoping context with it.

### Recommendation

Take **Option A**. The loom-1.0 gates are already bidirectionally cross-referenced to step 2(a) and the page already carries explicit carve-out sentences, so the defect is one of legibility, not of missing or misplaced normative content; an in-place phase divider plus per-gate `loom-1.0 gate` sub-blocks plus a Non-goals index resolves the legibility gap at far lower cross-reference risk than relocation. Add the Non-goals index on `inventory-audit-intro.md` regardless of option. Watch two edge cases: the phase divider and per-block tags must be unambiguous enough to override the page-level "post-1.0 hardening" banner in a quick read, and the Non-goals index must stay a scannable cross-link list (not a restatement) so it does not become a second drift surface against `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md`.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer skimming the post-1.0 banner on `audit-target-categories.md` (or the inline "post-1.0 mechanization" framing on `inventory-audit-intro.md`) can mis-scope the buried loom-1.0 MUSTs — either deferring the minimum-entry-shape enforcement and branch-table arms as post-1.0, or over-building the post-1.0 audit detector as a 1.0 merge gate. The explicit carve-out sentences make the correct scoping recoverable, so a careful reader can still produce a working 1.0 system; the cost is discoverability and the elevated risk of mis-scoping under a quick read.

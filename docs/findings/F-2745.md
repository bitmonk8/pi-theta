## Finding

`V18c`'s editorial-review acceptance criterion ranges only over checklist items `(a)`–`(aq)`. Both the leaf's `Adds.` field ("the contributor's editorial-review checklist (items `(a)`–`(aq)`)") and its `Ships when.` field (clause (1) "records a per-item outcome for every item `(a)`–`(aq)`"; clause (2) "no MUST-level item `(a)`–`(e)` carries a `fail` outcome") stop at `(aq)`.

The spec checklist they gate against — [`version-bump-step2.md`](../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md) — enumerates two further items the leaf never names:

- **`(ar)` — Step-5 inbound-reference enumeration-delta reconciliation.** This is a MUST-level per-bump confirmation: "The contributor MUST confirm, on each bump, that the documented inbound-reference enumeration was swept and that every positive result-delta … was reconciled in the same edit." It is the loom-1.0 enforcement point for the grep over the `{"new","resume","fork"}` session-only triplet and its `{"quit","reload"}` teardown complement; an unreconciled delta there can silently invert the session-only-vs-teardown predicates. The spec preamble explicitly distinguishes `(ar)` as "not a candidate-minor presupposition audit but a mandatory per-bump reconciliation confirmation routed to this checklist by step 5."
- **`(as)` — Triggering slash-command user-turn commit posture.** This is a SHOULD-level-audit-but-MUST-record item (same footing as `(f)`–`(aq)`); the spec preamble names it alongside that range ("SHOULD additionally audit it against items (f) through (aq) and item (as)").

Because the leaf's gate does not require a recorded outcome for `(ar)` or `(as)`, the acceptance check passes while a MUST-level reconciliation (`(ar)`) was never recorded, and the leaf's blocking set (`(a)`–`(e)` with a `fail` blocking the bump) omits `(ar)` even though `(ar)` is MUST-level. The spec also closes the checklist with a maintenance rule binding future editorial-review items to be added "in the same edit," but no analogous rule keeps the `V18c` leaf's enumerated range in lockstep, so the leaf has drifted behind the spec.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

An implementer authoring `V18c`'s editorial-review confirmation follows the leaf's explicit `(a)`–`(aq)` range and never enforces item `(ar)` — a MUST-level per-bump reconciliation — so a Pi minor bump can merge with no recorded `(ar)` outcome and an unreconciled inbound-reference delta silently inverting the `{"new","resume","fork"}` / `{"quit","reload"}` session-only-vs-teardown predicates. Item `(as)`'s mandatory recording is likewise dropped. The acceptance gate the leaf specifies does not match the spec checklist it is meant to enforce.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Bring `V18c`'s editorial-review acceptance range into line with the spec checklist's full enumeration in [`version-bump-step2.md`](../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md):

- In `Adds.`, replace the parenthetical "items `(a)`–`(aq)`" range with the full `(a)`–`(as)` range, and reflect the spec's normative levels: MUST-level for `(a)`–`(e)` and `(ar)`, SHOULD-level-audit-but-MUST-record for `(f)`–`(aq)` and `(as)`.
- In `Ships when.` clause (1), require a recorded per-item outcome for every item the checklist enumerates, `(a)`–`(as)` (item `(e)` recording its two sub-outcomes). Phrase the range so a future enumerated item is covered without a further edit — e.g. "every item the checklist in `version-bump-step2.md` enumerates" rather than a hardcoded terminal letter.
- In `Ships when.` clause (2), add item `(ar)` to the bump-blocking MUST-level set: a `fail` outcome or a missing `(ar)` row blocks the bump, alongside the existing `(a)`–`(e)` blocking condition. Item `(as)` stays SHOULD-audit-MUST-record — a recorded `fail` / `not-performed` on `(as)` is a conformant non-blocking outcome, identical to the `(f)`–`(aq)` treatment already stated.

The spec is read-only for this fix; the leaf is the only edited surface.

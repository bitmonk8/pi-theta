## Finding

The best-effort delivery chain that backs every `loom-system-note` emission â€” `pi.sendMessage` â†’ `ctx.ui.notify` â†’ `loom/runtime/system-note-delivery-failed` diagnostic â†’ terminal `console.error`, defined at `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/runtime-event-channel.md` under the **System notes** paragraph â€” is referenced by two distinct surface names across the seven Pi-integration-contract sub-files that cite it. `capability-probe.md`, `extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md`, and `host-interfaces-core.md` write "**System notes** fallback chain"; `active-invocation-registry.md`, `runtime-event-channel.md` (PIC-54 itself), `session-shutdown-semantics.md`, and `diagnostic-emission-isolation.md` write "`sendSystemNote` fallback chain". (The finding's "named three ways across three files" framing undercounts â€” the drift is two surface names across seven files.)

Compounding the naming drift, the two phrasings each carry a different inline expansion of the chain itself: the `**System notes**` form abbreviates to three steps (`sendSystemNote` â†’ `ctx.ui.notify` â†’ `console.error`, dropping the `loom/runtime/system-note-delivery-failed` diagnostic step), while the `` `sendSystemNote` `` form lists all four steps. `sendSystemNote` is never introduced as a defined term â€” it appears only as the chain's name and as the first token of one of the inline expansions, with no glossary entry, no canonical definition site, and no statement of whether it denotes a spec concept, an SDK export, or an internal implementation helper. PIC-54 (`runtime-event-channel.md`) and the `**Diagnostic-emission isolation**` import-by-reference clause in `diagnostic-emission-isolation.md` both pin obligations to "the `sendSystemNote` fallback chain" without anchoring that name to its definition site.

The chain is the durable spec-pinned reach-path for system-note delivery across every reach-path PIC-54 enumerates (per-invocation `finally`, extension-bootstrap SDK-failure, capability-probe `host-incompatible`), so the drift forces a reader resolving any cross-PIC-sub-file reference to learn at each site that the two names point at the same construct and that the abbreviated three-step expansion is the same chain as the four-step one.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Adopt **`` `sendSystemNote` fallback chain ``** as the canonical name (it is the longer-form name already used by PIC-54 â€” the chain's only normative anchor â€” and by the `**Diagnostic-emission isolation**` import-by-reference, and the `` `sendSystemNote` `` token gives readers a single grep target), and:

1. **Coin the name and define `sendSystemNote` at the chain's definition site.** In the **System notes** paragraph of `runtime-event-channel.md`, immediately above the existing step-1 list, add a one-sentence definition introducing the name â€” e.g. *"The chain defined by steps 1â€“2 below, together with the terminal `console.error` of step 2, is referred to throughout the Pi-integration contract as **the `sendSystemNote` fallback chain**. `sendSystemNote` is a spec-coined label for the chain as a whole; it names no SDK export and prescribes no implementation helper."* Anchor it (`<a id="sendsystemnote-fallback-chain"></a>`) so cross-file references can link precisely.

2. **Add a glossary entry** to `docs/spec_topics/glossary.md` pointing at the new anchor on `runtime-event-channel.md`, on the same footing as existing chain/concept entries.

3. **Sweep the seven sub-files** and replace every occurrence of "**System notes** fallback chain" with "`sendSystemNote` fallback chain" (linked to the new anchor at first use in each file). Sites: `capability-probe.md` item (ii); `extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md` `pi.registerMessageRenderer` failure bullet and `loom/load/extension-bootstrap-failed` routing paragraph; `host-interfaces-core.md` `ExtensionUIContext.ui` inline comment.

4. **Reconcile the inline expansions.** Strike the abbreviated three-step inline expansion (`sendSystemNote` â†’ `ctx.ui.notify` â†’ `console.error`) wherever it appears (`capability-probe.md` item (ii); `extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md` routing paragraph), since it omits the `loom/runtime/system-note-delivery-failed` diagnostic step the definition site pins. After the name is defined and linked, an inline expansion is no longer needed on the citing side; the link to the definition site carries the chain shape. If an inline expansion is retained anywhere for reader convenience, it MUST be the four-step form (`pi.sendMessage` â†’ `ctx.ui.notify` â†’ `loom/runtime/system-note-delivery-failed` diagnostic â†’ `console.error`) that matches `session-shutdown-semantics.md`'s expansion and the step list at the definition site.

5. **Re-point the four plan leaves** (`V7d`, `V7d-T`, `V9p`, `V9p-T`) so their prose uses the new canonical name. No acceptance-criteria edit is required â€” the same delivery-failure-fallback test still exercises the same chain.

Edge cases for the implementer: the bare token `sendSystemNote` (without the "fallback chain" suffix) appears only inside the inline expansion of the three-step abbreviated form and inside the chain's name itself; step (4) above eliminates the former site, and step (1) defines the term at the latter, so after the sweep `sendSystemNote` has exactly one definition site and is referenced only as part of the canonical chain name or via the anchor. Do not also rename the construct at its definition site to use the `**System notes**` form â€” `**System notes**` is already the paragraph heading at `runtime-event-channel.md` for the broader system-notes contract (the call shape, the four `details` variants, the always-log set, etc.) and overloading it with the chain's name reintroduces the drift in the other direction.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Two implementers reading the same PIC sub-file pair (e.g. `extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md` + PIC-54) must independently verify that "**System notes** fallback chain" and "`sendSystemNote` fallback chain" are the same construct, and reconcile the three-step vs four-step inline expansions. The construct itself is well-defined at its definition site; nothing prevents a working implementation. The cost is reviewer-side: every PIC cross-reference traversing the chain pays an extra mapping step, and PIC-54 / the `**Diagnostic-emission isolation**` import-by-reference pin obligations to an undefined symbol (`sendSystemNote`) whose scope (spec concept vs SDK export vs implementation helper) the reader cannot read off the surface text.

## Finding

The glossary entry for **`surface-set closure`** (`docs/spec_topics/glossary.md`) states the property is "enforced at loom 1.0 by the *positive-direction* SDK surface-inventory literal-read assertions and the editorial-review checklist". The qualifier "positive-direction" is load-bearing terminology — it distinguishes the step-2(a) literal-read assertions (which assert that inventory entries exist and have the required shape) from the *negative-direction* inventory-closure audit (which scans the source tree for surfaces absent from the inventory). That positive/negative distinction is defined and contrasted on the Pi-Integration-Contract pages (`inventory-audit-intro.md`, `version-bump-step2.md`, the `audit-*` pages), but the glossary entry uses the bare adjective with no inline gloss and no anchor to where the distinction is drawn. A reader consulting the glossary — whose purpose is definitional self-containment — cannot tell from the entry what makes the literal-read assertions "positive-direction" as opposed to the audit, and the entry's lone `See:` link points at the audit's introductory anchor rather than at the positive/negative contrast.

The fix is a one-line edit at the glossary entry: either gloss "positive-direction" inline (e.g. "the *positive-direction* literal-read assertions that assert each inventory entry exists with the required shape, as opposed to the negative-direction audit that scans for unlisted surfaces") or anchor the qualifier to its defining site (`version-bump-step2.md#bump-step-2-positive` / `inventory-audit-intro.md`).

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In the `surface-set closure` glossary entry, make the "positive-direction" qualifier self-explaining at the point of use. Either:

- add a parenthetical gloss inline — e.g. "the *positive-direction* literal-read assertions (which assert each `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` entry is present with the required shape, as distinct from the *negative-direction* inventory-closure audit that scans the audited source tree for surfaces absent from the inventory)"; or
- link the qualifier to its defining site, `[PIC — step 2(a)](./pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md#bump-step-2-positive)`, where the positive-direction literal-read assertions are pinned.

Keep the edit confined to the glossary entry; the PIC pages already define the distinction and need no change. Match the wording the canonical pages already use ("positive-direction … literal-read assertions" / "negative-direction … audit") so the gloss does not coin a third phrasing.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A glossary reader meets an undefined load-bearing adjective and must hunt across the PIC pages to recover the positive-vs-negative-direction distinction. No implementer would build divergent behaviour from this; it is a definitional-clarity gap in a page whose contract is self-containment.

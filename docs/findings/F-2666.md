## Finding

PIC-52 bundles a review/architecture-verified architectural prohibition (no added sandbox / capability-filter / mediated-proxy channel in any 1.x release) with a runtime-testable denial-routing observable under one undifferentiated MUST, and the denial clause's bound party is left implicit by the passive "silent success on denial is forbidden."

**Spec edits:** In `trust-boundary.md`, break PIC-52 into two anchored obligations: (1) the design invariant — no interposed privilege layer; the runtime MUST NOT add additional access channels in 1.x; major-version concern — explicitly tagged as verified by architectural review (not runtime-testable); and (2) the runtime-testable denial-routing observable that names the **loom runtime** as the bound party: a thrown / `isError: true` host return MUST surface to loom code as `Err(QueryError { kind: "code_tool", cause: "execution", … })` and MUST NOT be converted to `Ok`. Repoint `V14d` / `V14d-T` to the new denial-routing sub-anchor.

**Relationship:** same-cluster (resolves at PIC-52's prohibition and denial sentences, independently of split 2).

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The runtime behaviour PIC-52 mandates (no interposed privilege layer; reported denials surface as `Err`) is unambiguous, so a working system is still producible — the gaps are verification-mode and citation clarity. A test author may waste effort attempting to test the unobservable channel prohibition, or conversely dismiss the whole rule — including the testable denial clause — as unverifiable; and a reader of PIC-52 alone may over-trust the grant-model claim that the orientation page deliberately hedges.

## Problem

Step 7 of the version-bump procedure (`version-bump-triggers.md`) closes with "If pi-ai exposes the field under the probed name `strictCapable`, this step is a no-op." That treats the field-present-under-the-probed-name case as a no-op, but the *Strict-capability absence-under-the-probed-name arm* (`inventory-audit-intro.md#strict-capability-absence-under-probed-name`) extends step 2(a)'s `strict-capability-probe` literal-read assertion to fail **red** whenever any reachable `Model<Api>` exposes a `strictCapable` member under the probed name. A contributor hitting that red is therefore sent by step 7 to look in the wrong place. Step 7 also names no resolution branch for the field-present case: no documented path to retract the `strict-capability-absence-pin` claim, rework the binder's universal-`W` narrative, and clear the build so the bump can merge green.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `b0cdf1b` (May 6 2026, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Re-validating obligation has no defined output and no enforcement gate\"") authored the original "If no such indicator has been added, this step is a no-op" sentence; `6829a79` (June 6 2026, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Pi-SDK hidden-assumption cluster (strictCapable absence, decode steps, pi.on origin)\"") later introduced the absence-under-the-probed-name arm that the sentence now contradicts.
**History:** `git log -S "this step is a no-op"` shows `b0cdf1b` first lands the step (then numbered 6) with the "field absent → no-op" arm only; at that point the procedure had no symmetric red gate for the field-present case, so the sentence's enumeration was exhaustive. `git log -G "absence-under-the-probed-name"` shows the *absence-under-the-probed-name arm* (the build-time literal-read red triggered when a reachable `Model<Api>` declaration exposes `strictCapable` under the probed name) is introduced in `6829a79`, which adds it adjacent to the `strict-capability-absence-pin` anchor and explicitly names "the rename-detection arm owned by bump-procedure step 7 (which catches the field present under a *different* name)" — but does not edit step 7 to name the new arm or retract the no-op claim. The subsequent moves (`bfe08cf` fixing the strictCapable package attribution, `bf92fe6` relocating the absence-arm MUST from `audit-target-categories.md` to `inventory-audit-intro.md`) preserved the contradictory wording on both sides. The defect is therefore the **interaction**: the no-op sentence was correct at authoring and became incorrect when the symmetric red arm was added.

## Solution approach

Rewrite step 7's trailing no-op sentence so it distinguishes the field-absent arm (the loom 1.0 baseline, genuinely a no-op) from the field-present-under-the-probed-name arm, which reddens step 2(a) via the absence-under-the-probed-name gate and is not a no-op. For the field-present arm, name a resolution branch that retracts the `strict-capability-absence-pin` claim at `audit-target-categories.md#strict-capability-absence-pin`, re-validates the strict-capability requirement at `binder-model-and-context.md#strict-capability-requirement` against the now-present value, and clears step 2(a). Forward-link the absence-under-the-probed-name arm at `inventory-audit-intro.md#strict-capability-absence-under-probed-name`. Preserve the existing rename arm unchanged.

## Solution constraints

- The field-present resolution is a contract-level edit; do not route it through or merge it into step 5's snapshot-edit-only sweep (step 5 trigger (ii) owns a different edit shape).
- Out of scope: the absence-arm gate's implementation-owned discriminator and the V18c plan leaf and its fixtures — the spec edit pins only the behavioural contract.

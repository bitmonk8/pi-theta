## Finding

The *Out of scope: `loom/typecheck/*`* paragraph in `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md` (anchor `loom-typecheck-namespace`) closes with an *Audit-gate carve-out* sub-clause that imposes a normative MUST on build-time audit tooling: "Audit gates that scan source or spec for `loom/...` literals and reconcile them against the Code registry below MUST carve out `loom/typecheck/` as a prefix-disjoint namespace — a `loom/typecheck/*` literal MUST NOT be reported as a missing registry entry."

The host file's subject matter is the diagnostic envelope shape, the delivery-channel rules, and the closed Code registry (DIAG-2/3/4) — i.e. the runtime-observable diagnostic surface. The audit-gate MUST is about a build-time CI tool's reconciliation logic, a different surface owned elsewhere in the corpus (the SDK surface-inventory audit family in `pi-integration-contract/inventory-audit-intro.md` … `audit-wire-and-canary.md`, and the governance-side namespace-discipline rules). The host paragraph's job is to inform a reader auditing the `loom/...` surface that `loom/typecheck/*` matches are out of scope for the Code registry — a perfectly in-scope declarative claim — but the MUST that binds an audit-gate implementation is a stranded normative obligation a reader looking for diagnostic-shape rules will not expect, and a reader implementing or reviewing the audit gate cannot find via the audit-family pages.

The MUST is real and load-bearing — `H5a`'s closing-gate arm relies on a prefix-disjoint `loom/typecheck/*` exclusion, and `V18b`'s inventory-closure audit relies on the same exclusion to prevent its non-empty-scan canary from miscounting brand strings as in-scope references. Both leaves cite the rule via the spec without an authoritative single home: `H5a` describes the carve-out as a `Convention:` of its own, and `conventions.md` paraphrases the rule rather than citing the diagnostics page. The obligation is therefore both authoritative-in-the-wrong-place and silently restated downstream.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Relocate the *Audit-gate carve-out* sub-clause's MUST to `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/inventory-audit-intro.md`, immediately after the *Inventory-closure audit* paragraph that introduces the build-time audit tooling and the `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` source of truth. Phrase the relocated obligation as a normative MUST on the inventory-closure audit (and any other build-time audit gate that scans `loom/...` literals against the diagnostics Code registry): the audit MUST treat `loom/typecheck/` as a prefix-disjoint namespace, and a `loom/typecheck/*` literal MUST NOT be reported as a missing registry entry — preserving the wording verbatim so downstream paraphrases (`H5a`'s `Convention:` bullet, `V18b`'s *Tests* bullet, `conventions.md`'s narrative restatement) keep matching the authoritative text. Add a parallel anchor (e.g. `loom-typecheck-prefix-disjoint`) so plan leaves can link to the MUST directly.

In `diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md` keep the rest of the *Out of scope: `loom/typecheck/*`* paragraph unchanged (the namespace claim, the *Naming convention* and *Definition site* sub-clauses, and the reader-orientation sentence about how to read `loom/typecheck/*` matches when auditing the `loom/...` surface). Replace the relocated MUST with a one-sentence informative pointer: a build-time audit gate reconciling `loom/...` literals against this registry MUST honour the prefix-disjoint carve-out pinned at the new anchor, with an explicit cross-link. The reader is informed that the carve-out exists; the gate-implementer obligation is owned by the page that owns the gate.

Update `H5a-closing-gate-automation.md`'s `Convention:` bullet, `V18b-inventory-audit.md`'s *Tests* bullet, `V18b-T-inventory-audit.md`'s *Tests* bullet, and `conventions.md`'s prose restatement to cite the new authoritative anchor in `inventory-audit-intro.md` rather than the diagnostics-shape page. Implementer edge case: keep `loom/typecheck/<kebab-case-name>` naming-convention rule and the no-`<...>`-placeholder rule in `diagnostic-shape.md` — those constrain the brand-string surface itself rather than the audit-gate reconciliation behaviour, and the audit-gate rule's correctness does not depend on them.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader implementing the SDK surface-inventory audit or the closing-gate automation has no in-place reason to consult the diagnostics shape doc, so they may produce an audit gate that reports every `loom/typecheck/*` literal as a missing registry entry — a noise floor that drowns the canary's signal and breaks `V18b` / `H5a`'s green state. Conversely, a reader maintaining the diagnostics-shape page sees a MUST about audit tooling and may wrongly conclude that diagnostics owns the audit-reconciliation policy.

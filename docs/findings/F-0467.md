## Finding

The **Activation window** paragraph of the manual real-host smoke governance note states that a trigger event occurring before `H7a` lands is deferred, and that "*its owner* records the deferral in the triggering commit message." At this point in the note the term "owner" is not yet defined — the named-owner mapping ("the bump contributor for trigger (1) and the merging contributor for trigger (2)") appears only later, in the **Live-host precondition** sentence and again in the trigger-enumeration at the end of the note. A reader hitting "its owner" therefore has only the grammatical referent "a trigger event," which admits two distinct readings: the actor who caused the trigger event, versus the author of the triggering commit.

The two readings coincide for trigger (1) (the bump contributor is the bump-commit author) but can diverge for trigger (2) in a collaborative workflow: the merging contributor who runs the smoke is not necessarily the author of the commit(s) inside the merge, and the "triggering commit message" for trigger (2) is the merge commit. Because the `H6a` release-gate auditor locates the recorded run by reading the triggering commit message, a deferral recorded by the wrong party (or in the wrong commit) is not where the auditor looks.

The note already fixes the intended reading elsewhere — "the smoke's two named owners (the bump contributor for trigger (1) and the merging contributor for trigger (2))" — so the fix is to make the **Activation window** sentence use that same named-owner phrasing at point of first use rather than the bare pronoun.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/real-host-smoke-gate.md`, in the **Activation window** sentence, replace the clause `its owner records the deferral in the triggering commit message` with a clause that pins the actor to the trigger's named owner — for example `the named owner of that trigger (the bump contributor for trigger (1), the merging contributor for trigger (2)) records the deferral in the triggering commit message`. This phrasing matches the named-owner mapping the note already uses in the **Live-host precondition** sentence ("the bump contributor for trigger (1) and the merging contributor for trigger (2)") and in the trigger-enumeration clauses ("owned by the contributor performing the bump" / "owned by the contributor of that merge"), so the recorded-deferral evidence the `H6a` auditor reads lands in the commit message the auditor inspects.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A contributor reading "its owner" before the note defines its named owners must guess the referent; for a collaborative trigger-(2) merge the trigger actor and the triggering-commit author can differ, so two contributors could record the deferral against different commit messages. The `H6a` auditor, which locates the recorded run by reading the triggering commit message, may then look in the wrong place. Contributors can still act because the named-owner mapping is recoverable later in the same note, so this degrades clarity rather than blocking the gate.

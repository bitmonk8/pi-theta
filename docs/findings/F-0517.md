## Finding

`V5d` (Schema-subset reject gate) declares `V2d` (Canonical integer/number renderer) in its `Deps` field, but nothing in `V5d` consumes a number renderer. `V5d`'s own `Adds` states it is an allowlist / reject-by-default gate that accepts the permitted JSON-Schema subset and rejects everything else, and explicitly delegates the work that would need numeric rendering: "The lowering pass and the canonical-hash recipe that operate on the accepted subset are owned by `V5f`." The reject gate only fires `loom/parse/unsupported-feature` and `loom/parse/result-in-schema-position` diagnostics — it produces no canonical numeric form.

`V2d`'s own `Adds` names exactly two consumers — the schema canonical-hash recipe (`V5f`) and the binder argument echo (`V11h`). `V5f` (and its `-T`), `V11h` (and its `-T`), and `V11d` (and its `-T`) all legitimately carry `V2d` in `Deps`. `V5d` and its paired `V5d-T` are the outliers: they retain the edge but exercise no rendering path.

The edge is a stale artefact of an earlier leaf split (see Issue introduction): `V5d` once owned the lowering + canonical-hash pass, for which `V2d` was a real prerequisite. When that responsibility moved to `V5f`, the `V2d` edge stayed on `V5d`. The surviving edge falsely tightens the dependency graph — it makes `V5d` un-pickable until `V2d-complete` even though `V5d` needs nothing from `V2d`.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `c6a664e` (initial plan build — created the leaf, then named `V5d-subset-lowering.md`, with `Deps: V5d-T, V5a, V5b, V2d`); `cb8737a` ("resolve Systemic leaf over-bundling across the plan corpus" — the commit at which the edge became stale)
**History:** The plan corpus is git-tracked. `git log -L` over `V5d`'s `Deps` line shows it was authored verbatim at inception (`c6a664e`) as `V5d-T, V5a, V5b, V2d`. At that time the leaf was titled "Schema-subset gate, lowering, and canonical hash" and owned the binder-number-rendered canonical-hash recipe, so `V2d` was a legitimate prerequisite. `git show cb8737a -- docs/plan_topics/V5d-subset-lowering.md` shows that commit retitled the leaf to "Schema-subset reject gate", stripped the lowering pass and the canonical-hash recipe from its `Adds`/`Tests`/`Ships when`, and moved them to the newly-created `V5f` — but left the `**Deps.** ... V2d` line untouched. A later rename (`09293d2`) moved the files to `V5d-reject-gate.md` / `V5d-T-reject-gate.md`, confirming the lowering responsibility had departed. The defect is therefore the interaction: an originally-correct edge that `cb8737a` failed to retire when it relocated the only number-rendering work to `V5f`.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Remove the `V2d` token from the `Deps` field of both `docs/plan_topics/V5d-reject-gate.md` (currently `V5d-T, V5a, V5b, V2d` → `V5d-T, V5a, V5b`) and `docs/plan_topics/V5d-T-reject-gate.md` (currently `V5a, V5b, V2d` → `V5a, V5b`). The reject gate emits only diagnostics and performs no numeric rendering; the canonical-hash recipe that consumes `V2d` is owned by `V5f`, which retains its own `V2d` edge. `V2d` remains reachable from `H5b`'s `Deps` transitively through `V5f` (and `V11h`/`V11d`), so dropping the `V5d` edge does not strand the BNDR-4 / BNDR-5 coverage row (`V2d`) — no coverage-matrix or transitive-completeness change is required.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

`V5d` declares a dependency it does not use, so `V5d` is reported un-pickable until `V2d-complete` even though the reject gate needs nothing from the number renderer. No leaf ships wrong, but the dependency DAG misrepresents the true critical path and may serialise `V5d` behind unrelated work.

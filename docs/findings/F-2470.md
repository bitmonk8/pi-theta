## Finding

A typed query's two-phase loop transitions from the free phase to the forced respond turn the moment "the model emits a free-phase plain text turn (provider stop reason `end_turn` / `stop`)" — the trigger is stated in `conversation-drive.md`'s typed-query bullet and in `query-tool-loop.md` QRY-14 step 2. The runtime therefore depends on a behavioural property of `@earendil-works/pi-ai` and each loom 1.0-supported provider (`anthropic-messages`, `openai-completions`, `mistral`, `amazon-bedrock`): that a plain-text (non-tool-use) final turn reliably normalises to `AssistantMessage.stopReason` `end_turn` or `stop`, distinct from `tool_use`. This normalised value is what distinguishes "loop the free phase again" from "dispatch the forced respond turn"; it is not part of pi-ai's typed surface, so a provider or pi-ai change that surfaced a plain-text final turn under some other stop reason would not be caught by the SDK surface-inventory test or the pi-ai version pin.

Every other host-behavioural dependency on this page carries an explicit presupposition block plus a routed editorial-review item under the *Editorial-review checklist for unpinned host presuppositions* (`version-bump-step2.md`, items (a)–(as)): the `complete()` forced-tool behaviour (item (u)), the `complete()` retry/cancellation behaviour (items (aa)/(ab)), driven-turn commit ordering (item (ac)), user-visible streaming (item (ak)), and `waitForIdle()` no-rejection (item (al)). The transport-failure `stopReason: "error"` member is itself pinned by item (m). The free-phase `end_turn`/`stop` trigger normalisation — equally a behavioural presupposition with no build-time signal — has no presupposition block and no checklist item. A silent drift (a provider whose plain-text terminator maps to a stop reason the trigger does not recognise) would not fire the trigger: the typed query would instead loop to `max_rounds` and degenerate through CIO-4's `max_rounds`-final branch, or the unrecognised reason would route through `provider-error-mapping.md`'s Stop-reason classification to a `TransportError(retryable: false)` — in both cases a wrong outcome with no editorial-review step scheduled to catch the regression.

(The companion concern from the original framing — that an untyped query's indefinite hang has no loom-side deadline — is already pinned: the *Hang handling* clause states "`waitForIdle()` has no internal deadline; hangs are bounded only by the cancellation path." The live gap is the free-phase trigger presupposition above.)

**History:** The free-phase plain-text-turn trigger has named provider stop reasons `end_turn` / `stop` since `query-tool-loop.md` and `conversation-drive.md` were carved out of the monolithic spec (f5e89f4, 2026-06-04), and in the predecessor monolith before that; no presupposition or editorial-review hook was ever attached to that normalisation. The asymmetry the finding flags deepened as sibling host-behavioural presuppositions were retrofitted around the same surface — the `complete()` forced-tool hook (e63ba6c, 2026-06-05) and, through 2026-06-25, the second forced-respond entry point (b119f5b) alongside the `stopReason: "error"` / commit-ordering / streaming / no-rejection postures — each gaining its own editorial-review item while the free-phase trigger normalisation was left uncovered.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add a `complete()`-style presupposition block to `conversation-drive.md` (adjacent to the existing `complete()` forced-tool presupposition, since both bear on the typed-query loop), stating that the free-phase→forced-respond transition presupposes that across all four loom 1.0-supported providers a plain-text (non-tool-use) final turn normalises to `AssistantMessage.stopReason` `end_turn` or `stop` (distinct from `tool_use`), that this is a behavioural property of `@earendil-works/pi-ai`'s per-provider adapters not on its typed surface, and that a silent regression would misfire the free-phase termination with no SDK surface-inventory signal. Route detection to a new SHOULD-level editorial-review item in `version-bump-step2.md`'s items-(a)–(as) checklist (next free letter), parallel to item (m), instructing the contributor to re-run the per-provider plain-text-turn fixtures and confirm the `end_turn` / `stop` normalisation holds on each Pi minor bump; carry the standard escalation clause ("escalates to a build-time pin once Pi exposes a typed contract step 2(a) can verify"). Cross-reference the new item from the presupposition block. Reuse the `stopReason` field pin already established by item (m) rather than re-pinning the field; this block adds only the `end_turn` / `stop` value-space presupposition the trigger relies on.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

For the immediate implementation two reasonable implementers do not diverge — QRY-14 names `end_turn` / `stop` explicitly. The gap is a missing re-validation hook: if a future pi-ai minor or a supported provider surfaces a plain-text final turn under a stop reason other than `end_turn` / `stop`, the free-phase→forced-respond transition silently fails (the typed query loops to `max_rounds` or the reason misclassifies to `TransportError`), and no editorial-review checklist item is scheduled to detect it — unlike every sibling host presupposition on the same page.

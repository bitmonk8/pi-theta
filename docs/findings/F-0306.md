## Problem

The `Adds.` field rule in `conventions.md` (§Leaf format) classifies a mechanism named in a leaf's `Adds.` as **Class-2 binding** when "a leaf listing this leaf in its `Deps.` names the seam in its own `Adds.` or `Tests.`", and otherwise leaves it illustrative (Class-3). The criterion turns entirely on the verb *names*, which is never defined: two incompatible readings are open — (a) the consumer must cite the seam by an exact identifier token drawn from the upstream `Adds.`, or (b) any unambiguous prose reference to the seam suffices. Which reading applies decides, for every cross-leaf seam, whether the upstream leaf is obligated to provide a stable Class-2 contract or may treat the seam as illustrative shape. No mechanical gate consumes the Class-2 classification, so the ambiguity propagates directly into implementer and self-review judgement.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-by-later-edit (single commit)
**Introducing commit:** `dd28dbb` — "pi-loom plan: resolve 'Adds. clause (ii) seam-binding test undecidable from Deps.'" (2026-06-10)
**History:** The seam-binding clause (ii) is present since the initial plan build (`c6a664e`, 2026-06-10) in a vaguer form: "(ii) a named cross-leaf **seam** that another leaf's **Deps.** relies on." Commit `dd28dbb` rewrote that clause to make the binding test decidable from `Deps.` — "a leaf listing this leaf in its `Deps.` **names the seam** in its own `Adds.` or `Tests.`. A seam named in `Adds.` with no such consumer remains illustrative (non-binding)." That rewrite closed the "undecidable from `Deps.`" gap but introduced the new sub-ambiguity this finding flags: it pins the binding to a `Deps.`-consumer that *names* the seam without defining what *names* requires (exact-identifier match vs. prose reference). The Class-1/2/3 labels were named later (`058ac36`, 2026-06-11) but did not define the *names* precision. The precision gap therefore entered with `dd28dbb`.

## Solution approach

Define what *names the seam* requires in the Class-2 clause of the `Adds.` bullet in `conventions.md` (§Leaf format) so the binding/illustrative call is decidable, and keep the parallel gloss in `leaf-template.md` consistent. The recommended resolution is the unambiguous-prose-reference reading: a seam referenced by a `Deps.`-consumer's `Adds.`/`Tests.` that names both the seam and its owning leaf-ID is Class-2 binding — this leaves the existing `V16a`-seam consumers (`V4e`, `V5e`, `V11f`, `V13c`, `V15b`) already compliant. If the exact-identifier-token reading is chosen instead, declare the canonical token in `V16a` before retargeting the five consumers, so the propagation lands on a stable upstream definition.

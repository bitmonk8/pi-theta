## Problem

Step 5 of the version-bump procedure (`version-bump-triggers.md`) carries a `MUST`-level build-time audit gate — a standing enumeration pass over the `src/`, `test/`, and `docs/` grep targets and second-stage anchor filter that fails `npm test` red on an unreconciled enumeration delta — distinct from the per-bump constants edit the step is nominally about. That deliverable is buried in the interior of the *Enumeration mechanism — grep target* sub-paragraph and is surfaced nowhere a reader scans for scope: not in the `#pi-version-bump-procedure` intro (`version-bump-intro.md`), not in its `### Non-goals` index, and not in the steps 5–7 output summary, which enumerates the procedure's outputs as (a) a green build-time run, (b) a single bump commit, and (c) green runtime evidence. A contributor scoping the bump from the step heading or the output summary will not see that a CI gate must be authored, so the gate introduced to remove reliance on contributor discipline can itself be silently skipped.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** single-commit

**Introducing commit:** `c0259ca` — *"pi-loom spec: resolve 'Version-bump procedure: four obligations have no verifiable acceptance criterion'"* (2026-06-07)

**History:** The build-time audit clause first entered the corpus at `52b9101` (2026-05-11, *"resolve T24 — Fork-reason watcher closure..."*), where it was an optional, appropriately-scoped adornment: *"The implementation MAY additionally implement a build-time audit pass over the same three grep targets ... PIC does not require the audit."* As a `MAY`, it carried no obligation to surface it as a deliverable. Commit `c0259ca` upgraded that `MAY` to a `MUST` (*"The implementation MUST implement a build-time audit pass ... fails `npm test` red"*) and inserted the forward-reference *"at loom 1.0 a build-time audit gate (pinned later in this step) re-runs that enumeration on each `npm test`"*, in order to give the inbound-reference sweep a verifiable acceptance criterion. That modality flip converted an optional nicety into a mandatory engineering deliverable, but `c0259ca` made no corresponding edit to the procedure's introduction, its `Non-goals` index, or the step-7 output summary — leaving the now-required gate un-surfaced. The scope defect therefore dates entirely to `c0259ca`; the prior `MAY` form was not defective.

## Solution approach

Surface the build-time inbound-reference audit gate as a named deliverable at the procedure's scoping surfaces. Add it to the steps 5–7 output summary in `version-bump-triggers.md` as an enumerated implementation deliverable (a standing CI gate the implementation builds). Add a mention of the gate to the `#pi-version-bump-procedure` intro in `version-bump-intro.md` so it is visible before step 5. Demote step 5's interior statement of the gate in the *Enumeration mechanism — grep target* sub-paragraph to a back-reference to the now-named deliverable.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: deleting or weakening step 5's normative enumeration mechanics (the `src/`/`test/`/`docs/` grep targets and second-stage anchor filter) in the *Enumeration mechanism — grep target* sub-paragraph — they remain the gate's definition.

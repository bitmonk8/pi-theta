## Problem

PIC-2 (Prompt-mode sequential execution) is a prompt-mode-only MUST constraining the `pi.setActiveTools` snapshot/restore window, but its sole anchor `id="pic-2"` sits on the Subagent chapter at `subagent.md#pic-2`. The authoritative prompt-mode driver page `conversation-drive.md` carries no statement of the sequential-execution invariant and no link to PIC-2. A prompt-mode implementer entering through `conversation-drive.md` (the V9c entry point) sees neither the invariant nor a pointer to the page that pins it; the inbound cross-references all forward in from outside the prompt-mode page. The rule's normative substance is prompt-mode-only, so its current placement is a topical mismatch as well as a discoverability gap.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `7d7bfe2` (2026-06-01, "pi-loom spec: resolve SM-7c/SM-8 authoritative-in-place relocation (T08/T10/T11)") and `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04, "docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set")
**History:** Commit `7d7bfe2` minted PIC-2 inline in `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` immediately after the **Concurrent-invocation isolation and concurrency disposition** block — the placement followed naturally from PIC-2's framing as a contrast to subagent-mode concurrency ("The three potential sources of in-session overlap therefore produce genuine concurrency only between subagent-mode invocations, per **Concurrent-invocation isolation and concurrency disposition** above"). In the single-file form, a prompt-mode reader scrolling the **Conversation drive — prompt mode** section would still encounter PIC-2 a few paragraphs further down, so its proximity to the subagent block was tolerable. Commit `f5e89f4` then split the 576KB `pi-integration-contract.md` into thirty per-topic child pages under `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/`; the split table (in `docs/_tools/split_spec.py`) routed the byte range containing PIC-2 into `subagent.md` (entries `("conversation-drive", 240, 291)` and `("subagent", 352, 399)`). The split preserved every anchor but made PIC-2 invisible to a reader of `conversation-drive.md`, because the in-file proximity that masked the topical mismatch in the single-file form vanished at chapter-page boundaries. Neither commit individually authored the defect: `7d7bfe2` made a defensible local placement choice given the surrounding contrast prose, and `f5e89f4` was a mechanical size-cap split that preserved anchors faithfully. The interaction between the two — the placement choice followed by the page-boundary split — is what stranded the prompt-mode-only obligation on the subagent chapter without a cross-reference.

## Solution approach

Move the `pic-2` block from `subagent.md` to `conversation-drive.md`, the page that owns the prompt-mode driver contract, and repoint the inbound URLs (SM-7c in `session-model-and-appendix.md`, the `spec.md` Session Model aggregator, GOV-30's enumeration in `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md`, and the V9c plan leaves `V9c-conversation-drive.md` / `V9c-T-conversation-drive.md`) from `subagent.md#pic-2` to `conversation-drive.md#pic-2`. Rewrite the block's internal backreference to **Concurrent-invocation isolation and concurrency disposition** — currently an in-file "above" reference — into an explicit cross-file link to `subagent.md#concurrent-invocation-isolation`. Add a cross-reference on the subagent side that preserves the prompt-mode/subagent-mode contrast PIC-2's original adjacency carried.

## Solution constraints

- The `id="pic-2"` anchor MUST survive the move byte-for-byte, so the inbound URLs (SM-7c, the `spec.md` aggregator, GOV-30, the V9c leaves) remain resolvable.

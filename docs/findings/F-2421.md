## Finding

The Binder bypass section closes with: *"All other shapes — multiple fields, non-string types, defaults present, optional or nullable types — go through the binder."* This is an illustrative enumeration of what the single-string bypass excludes, but `optional` is not a loom type-system concept and pairs confusingly with `nullable`.

Loom has no separate notion of an optional field. `schemas.md` states every declared field is **required** (the lowered schema's `required` lists every property), and "Optional fields are expressed as `T | null` — there is no `field?: T` shorthand"; `grammar.md` likewise says "each field is required by default, optionality is written `T | null`". In loom, therefore, *optional* and *nullable* denote the same surface form (`T | null`). Listing them as two distinct disqualifiers ("optional or nullable types") implies a distinction the type system does not have, and invites a reader to hunt for a `?`-modifier or a `required`-list semantics that loom does not provide. The remaining candidate reading — "optional" meaning "has a default" — is already covered separately by the preceding "defaults present" item, making the term redundant under that reading too.

The defect does not corrupt the actual bypass decision: the normative single-string-bypass condition is precisely stated ("exactly one field, that field's type is `string`, and the field has no default"), so a single nullable field has type `string | null ≠ string` and correctly routes through the binder regardless of how a reader interprets "optional". The issue is confined to the illustrative prose using a term that contradicts loom's own type vocabulary.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In the "All other shapes" sentence, drop the standalone "optional" term and name only the structural disqualifier loom actually has: replace "defaults present, optional or nullable types" with "defaults present, or nullable types (`T | null`)". Optionally add a half-clause noting loom expresses optionality only as `T | null` (cross-referencing `schemas.md` Object schema), to pre-empt the reader who expects a `?`-modifier. Leave the normative single-string-bypass condition untouched — it is already exact.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader of the illustrative list may infer loom supports an "optional" field marker distinct from `T | null`, contradicting the type system. The normative bypass condition is unaffected, so no two implementers diverge on behaviour; the harm is purely a misleading term in non-binding prose.

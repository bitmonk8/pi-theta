## Problem

Sub-step 4 of the `session_shutdown` teardown sequence in `session-shutdown-semantics.md` calls `discoveryWatcher.close()` and `settingsWatcher.close()` without awaiting them, while the **Per-step isolation** paragraph wraps each call in a synchronous `try`/`catch` that routes throws to `loom/host/session-shutdown-teardown-step-failed`. chokidar's `FSWatcher.close()` returns `Promise<void>` (v3 and v4, and the chokidar version is deliberately unpinned per `host-prerequisites.md`), so an async close rejection escapes the synchronous catch, becomes an unhandled rejection on the invalidated runtime, and the diagnostic is never emitted for that call site. The `drain-state-contract.md` idempotent, Read-failure fallback, and all-three-throw branches depend on that per-call routing firing on a double-close throw, so they are also unsound. Sub-step 4 names chokidar's `close()` directly rather than the synchronous `Unsubscribe = () => void` teardown of the `FileWatcher` seam (PIC-14), and the spec never states whether sub-step 4's watcher teardown is synchronous or asynchronous.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `5e8b499` (2026-05-06, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Hot-reload teardown contract for the loom extension instance is unspecified\""); `6ce416d` (2026-05-07, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Teardown sub-steps 1, 4, and 5 lack a per-step isolation rule\"")
**History:** `git log -S` over the spec corpus shows the defect arose from the interaction of two independently-landed edits, not a single faulty commit. `5e8b499` first introduced sub-step 4 ("Close watchers. Call `discoveryWatcher.close()` … and `settingsWatcher.close()`") with no statement of whether the calls are awaited or whether `close()` is synchronous. `6ce416d` then added the **Per-step isolation** rule wrapping each sub-step in a synchronous `try`/`catch` that routes throws to `loom/host/session-shutdown-teardown-step-failed` — at which point the latent synchronous-throw assumption became load-bearing: a synchronous catch over an un-awaited asynchronous `close()` can only ever capture a synchronous throw. Neither commit alone is the sole cause; the unsound assumption emerges only from their combination. Both blocks were later relocated verbatim into `session-shutdown-semantics.md` by `c3de47a` (F-1602), which preserved the defect without re-introducing it.

## Solution approach

Clarify in sub-step 4 whether watcher teardown is synchronous or asynchronous, routing it through the synchronous `Unsubscribe` seam at PIC-14 (`#pic-14`) so the **Per-step isolation** synchronous `try`/`catch` only ever needs to catch synchronous throws. State how an async `close()` rejection from the production chokidar adapter is handled — e.g. routing to the watcher's PIC-55 (`#pic-55`) error / transient-toast surface rather than escaping as an unhandled rejection or reaching `loom/host/session-shutdown-teardown-step-failed`. Reword the double-close throw routing in `drain-state-contract.md`'s idempotent, Read-failure fallback, and all-three-throw clauses to reference the synchronous seam teardown.

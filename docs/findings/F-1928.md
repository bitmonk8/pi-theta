## Finding

`V9g`'s `session_shutdown` handler realises its sub-step-3 bounded await directly against the injected `Clock` seam. The spec (`session-shutdown-semantics.md` §`session_shutdown` sub-step 3) pins the mechanism concretely: at handler entry the runtime captures `deadline = Clock.now() + SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS`, the await resolves on the earlier of `Promise.allSettled(...)` settling or `Clock.setTimeout(SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS, ...)` firing, and the pending `Clock.setTimeout` handle is cleared on the success path. Sub-step 4 likewise calls `Clock.clearTimeout` (its closed `details.call` set even names the literal label `"Clock.clearTimeout(debounce)"`). The `Clock` seam (`now` / `wallNow` / `setTimeout` / `clearTimeout` via the `WallClock` adapter) is owned by `V8d`.

`V9g`'s Deps are `V9g-T, V9a, V9e, V9h, V17a`, and `V9g-T`'s are `V9e, V9h, V17a`. Neither names `V8d`. The leaf's only edge to anything timing-related is `V17a`, but `V17a` does **not** supply a Clock-bounded await primitive: its Adds carries `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS` only as "the cancellation-runtime *semantics* … as a conceptual consumer", with the constant physically residing in `V9a`'s pinned-constants block, and `V17a`'s own Deps (`V17a-T, V8a, V4d, V9a, H4a, H4b`) do not include `V8d` either. So the timing primitive the bounded await needs is reachable for `V9g` only by going to the `Clock` seam directly — and that seam's owning leaf is not a declared edge.

The defect is a missing direct-consumer Deps edge, not an ordering break: `V8d` is transitively reachable through `V9g → V17a → V8a → V8d` (`V8a.Deps` = `V8a-T, H3a, V8d`), so `V8d` is complete by the time `V9g` becomes pickable. This is the same class of omission as `V9g`'s undeclared direct use of `V9m`'s drain-state API in sub-step 1 and of `V9b`'s step-4 subscription it back-fills — a directly-consumed contract surfaced only transitively.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** `c6a664e` (2026-06-10) — `pi-loom plan: build/update plan for spec.md + review`
**History:** `V9g`/`V9g-T` were authored in the initial plan-build commit `c6a664e` already carrying the `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS` cap and the "fires at the cap" assertion, with Deps `V9g-T, V9e, V9h, V17a` (impl) and `V9e, V9h, V17a` (tests) — `V8d` absent from the first version. The later edit `a6e2ecb` added the `V9a` Deps edge but never the `Clock` seam. The defect — a deterministic cap assertion with no enumerated `Clock` seam and no `FakeClock` test-mechanism statement — has been present since the leaf pair first existed; it was not introduced by any later interaction.


## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add `V8d` to the **Deps** field of both `docs/plan_topics/V9g-session-shutdown.md` (current `V9g-T, V9a, V9e, V9h, V17a`) and `docs/plan_topics/V9g-T-session-shutdown.md` (current `V9e, V9h, V17a`), so the leaf pair declares its direct dependency on the `Clock` seam its sub-step-3 bounded await and sub-step-4 timer cleanup consume. Do not instead claim `V17a` supplies the Clock-bounded primitive: `V17a` owns only the `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS` constant's semantics and does not itself depend on `V8d`, so that framing would be false. The spec (`session-shutdown-semantics.md` sub-step 3, `host-interfaces-services.md` Clock interface) and `V8d` are read-only for this fix — the edit is confined to the two `V9g` Deps lines. Honour the project's existing leaf-ID scheme and do not invent new IDs.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer wiring `V9g` from its declared Deps does not have the `Clock` seam in scope as a direct edge, even though the handler must call `Clock.now()` / `Clock.setTimeout()` / `Clock.clearTimeout()` to realise the sub-step-3 deadline and the sub-step-4 timer cleanup. Because `V8d` is transitively complete and the `H3a`/`PIC-12` architectural gate flags any direct ambient timing reference outside the `WallClock` adapter, the leaf can still be produced correctly; the cost is a discoverability gap (the implementer must locate the seam owner that the Deps list should have named), not a broken gate or a wrong-by-construction implementation.

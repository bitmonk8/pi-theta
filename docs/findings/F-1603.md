## Problem

The *Generated-output-in-src limitation (acknowledged soft surface)* sub-clause in the `audit-scope` paragraph does double duty: it pins the in-scope contributor-discipline prohibition (generated `.ts` build outputs MUST NOT be emitted into `src/`, which the inventory-closure audit has no mechanical surface for detecting) and, in the same breath, sketches an optional sibling check the same sentence disclaims requiring. The optional half names a concrete discriminator token (`scope-audit/generated-source-in-tree`), a detection heuristic (a `// @generated` leading-comment scan on every walked file), and a namespace constraint, forward-binding the shape of a check PIC defers past loom 1.0. Parallel soft-surface paragraphs (*Substitution limitation*, *Stale-citation limitation*) confine themselves to the in-scope acknowledgement and route deferred work to a one-line "out of scope for loom 1.0" framing; this one forces a reader to extract the prohibition from the optional-feature design sketch around it.

## Solution approach

Narrow the *Generated-output-in-src limitation (acknowledged soft surface)* sub-clause in `audit-scope` to its in-scope content only: the contributor-discipline prohibition, the acknowledgement that the inventory-closure audit walks `src/**/*.ts` blindly and so has no mechanical surface for detecting violations, and a one-line deferral sentence on the model of the *Stale-citation limitation* closing sentence. Delete the optional-feature detail — the `scope-audit/generated-source-in-tree` discriminator token, the `// @generated` leading-comment heuristic, and the namespace-constraint sentence. Retain the closure-protection sentence guarding the *Failure-surface contract* three-class `violation` / `infra` / `canary` enumeration, but re-anchor it to "any sibling scope-violation check" rather than the deleted example.

- Out of scope: the "Future change … MUST extend either the include glob or the exclusion list" sentence at the end of the *Audit scope* paragraph — do not modify it.

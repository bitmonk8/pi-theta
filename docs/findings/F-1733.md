## Finding

Clause (ii) of the *Routing obligation for future ceilings* paragraph on
`docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings/ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md` reads:
*"(ii) fall through to a documented `NOCEIL-N` — the per-axis entry is
rewritten to point at the existing `NOCEIL-N` claim's documented
fall-through … ; where no existing `NOCEIL-N` applies, this form coins
a new `NOCEIL-N` entry in the `NOCEIL-1` … `NOCEIL-4` closure list above
… and points the per-axis entry at it as the special case of (ii)."*
The verb *"applies"* carries no mechanical test, and the surrounding
prose admits at least two incompatible readings:

- *Axis-membership reading.* `NOCEIL-N` "applies" iff its row in the
  four-axis closure list at the head of the paragraph names the same
  axis as the retired ceiling. On this reading clause (ii)'s
  coin-a-new-`NOCEIL` branch is unreachable for any retirement on one
  of the four enumerated axes, because the four-axis closure list
  already pairs every axis with exactly one `NOCEIL-N`; the branch
  could fire only on an axis the *introduction* clause's *new-axis
  case* should have added.
- *Prose-fit reading.* `NOCEIL-N` "applies" iff the editor judges that
  the natural-language claim under `NOCEIL-N` (e.g. "no wall-clock
  timeout per query / tool-call / invoke", "no runtime-value memory
  ceiling") covers the retired ceiling's resource shape. On this
  reading the branch fires whenever the editor finds the existing
  `NOCEIL-N`'s wording too narrow, even when the axis matches.

The two readings hand the GOV-15 release-gate reviewer opposite
decisions on the same retirement (does the retirement need a new
`NOCEIL-5` coined under GOV-16's inline-label allocation discipline, or
does the existing per-axis `NOCEIL-N` discharge clause (ii)?), and the
spec gives the reviewer no apparatus for choosing between them.

The same ambiguity propagates to the *Release gate* paragraph below,
whose retirement bullet relies on "the per-axis entry … updated to one
of the four forms (i)–(iv)" — form (ii)'s applicability is decided by
the same undefined "applies".

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace the undefined verb with an axis-membership test that ties
clause (ii) to the *Four-axis closure list* at the head of the
paragraph. Concretely, rewrite the clause's conditional from:

> where no existing `NOCEIL-N` applies, this form coins a new
> `NOCEIL-N` entry in the `NOCEIL-1` … `NOCEIL-4` closure list above
> (extending the list per GOV-16's inline-label allocation discipline)
> and points the per-axis entry at it as the special case of (ii).

to wording of the shape:

> where no existing `NOCEIL-N` is the four-axis closure list's
> `NOCEIL-N` for the retired ceiling's axis (i.e. no row of the
> *Four-axis closure list* above pairs that axis with an existing
> `NOCEIL-N`), this form coins a new `NOCEIL-N` entry in the
> `NOCEIL-1` … `NOCEIL-4` closure list above (extending the list per
> GOV-16's inline-label allocation discipline) and points the per-axis
> entry at it as the special case of (ii); whenever the four-axis
> closure list already pairs the retired ceiling's axis with an
> existing `NOCEIL-N`, that `NOCEIL-N` is the routing destination and
> the coining sub-branch does not fire.

Implementer-relevant edge cases the rewording must cover:

- The "axis the retired ceiling was on" is the axis named on the
  per-axis entry being rewritten by clause (ii); the rewording must
  pin that source explicitly so a reviewer cannot read "axis" as the
  retired ceiling's editorial subject.
- The *new-axis case* in the *introduction* clause is the only
  remaining path that grows the four-axis closure list; the
  *retirement* clause's coining branch should therefore be reachable
  only when introduction earlier added an axis without a `NOCEIL-N`
  row, which is a closure defect on its own. Either state that
  invariant explicitly in clause (ii) or strike the coining sub-branch
  entirely and require the editor who introduced the axis to coin the
  `NOCEIL-N` at introduction time. The recommended wording above keeps
  the sub-branch for defensiveness; striking it is the cleaner
  alternative if the introduction clause is tightened in the same
  edit.
- The *Release gate* paragraph's retirement bullet inherits the new
  test by reference ("one of the four forms (i)–(iv)") and does not
  need separate wording, but the reviewer-inspection prose should be
  re-read after the edit to confirm no second copy of the undefined
  verb has crept in.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two GOV-15 reviewers reading the same retirement diff can reach
opposite verdicts: one accepts the retirement on the axis-membership
reading because the per-axis `NOCEIL-N` row already maps the axis; the
other rejects it on the prose-fit reading and demands a coined
`NOCEIL-5` under GOV-16 before clearing the release. The release-gate
decision is supposed to be a mechanical check; today it is a
prose-judgement coin-flip that can either block a release editorially
or silently widen the `NOCEIL-1` … `NOCEIL-4` closure list with an
entry the four-axis enumeration did not actually need.

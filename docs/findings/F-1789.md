## Finding

The orientation-aggregator section on `docs/spec_topics/language-and-architecture.md` is dual-anchored — `<a id="loom-1-0-non-goals-aggregator"></a><a id="v1-non-goals"></a>` immediately precedes `### V1 non-goals` — yet the heading text itself still spells the legacy token. Every prose use beneath it is canonical (`loom 1.0 non-goals list`, `loom 1.0 scope decisions`, `loom 1.0 ships`, link text `Future Considerations — loom 1.0 non-goals`), so a reader meets the legacy spelling exactly once at this site: in the heading they navigate by. The owning page on `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md` already uses `## loom 1.0 non-goals`, so the aggregator and the source it points at disagree at the heading level.

GOV-20 permits this state — heading prose is a governed callsite still in alias scope until converted — but the conversion is now both safe and pre-anticipated by governance: the site is already dual-anchored (so GOV-26 alias permanence preserves inbound `#v1-non-goals` resolution after the heading is renamed) and GOV-25-incidental-auto-id explicitly uses this exact heading (`### loom 1.0 non-goals`) as its worked example for the rule that the rendered GFM auto-id of the post-rename heading is *not* a citable anchor — both authored arms remain the citable fragments. The rename therefore lands as a one-line text edit with no anchor authoring, no cross-reference sweep, and no governance amendment.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction

**Introducing commits:**
- `0e12140` (2026-05-08) — `pi-loom spec: resolve "Non-goals are not consolidated into a single section"` — coined the `### V1 non-goals` aggregator heading (then on `docs/spec.md`) with body prose that already used `V1` throughout.
- `a6d82db` (2026-05-29) — `pi-loom spec: resolve "Prose V1 / V1.0 / V1.x rename to canonical spelling at non-closure callsites"` — converted the surrounding body prose at this aggregator (and across the corpus) from `V1` to `loom 1.0` but left the ATX heading text on this section unconverted, producing the present heading-vs-body mismatch. The companion dual-anchor commit `2e0dad2` (same day) added `<a id="loom-1-0-non-goals-aggregator"></a>` alongside the existing `<a id="v1-non-goals"></a>`, so the site is dual-anchored and the heading rename is governance-ready.

**History:** The defect is not present-since-inception of the aggregator: at `0e12140` the heading and body were internally consistent (both `V1`). It is the partial prose-rename in `a6d82db` — which deliberately scoped itself to non-closure prose callsites and stopped short of ATX heading text — that introduced the visible inconsistency. The intervening relocation commits `f5e89f4` (size-cap split) and `81bf52a` (collapse `spec/` into `spec_topics/`) only moved the section across files without editing the heading or its body. The fix therefore discharges work that the May-29 rename pass deliberately deferred.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Rename the heading on `docs/spec_topics/language-and-architecture.md` line 6 from `### V1 non-goals` to `### loom 1.0 non-goals`. Leave both authored anchor arms (`loom-1-0-non-goals-aggregator` and `v1-non-goals`) in place — GOV-26 (alias permanence) requires the `v1-` arm to stay, GOV-29 (cross-page canonical-arm uniqueness) is the reason the canonical arm carries the `-aggregator` tail rather than the bare `loom-1-0-non-goals` slug owned by the source page. Make no edits to anchors, cross-references, or the body paragraphs beneath the heading.

Edge cases:
- The post-rename heading's GFM-rendered auto-id (a third fragment such as `loom-10-non-goals`) is governed by GOV-25-incidental-auto-id: it MUST NOT be authored or cited as a stable anchor. Citers continue to use the two authored arms.
- No corpus-wide grep is required for cross-references: every inbound link to this site already targets one of the two authored arms, both of which remain.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader scanning the spec sees mixed version vocabulary at the section title — the single most prominent location for a naming inconsistency — and may briefly doubt whether the aggregator heading and the `## loom 1.0 non-goals` source page are the same concept. No normative content drifts and no link breaks; this is pure surface presentation.

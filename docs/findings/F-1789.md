## Finding

The orientation-aggregator section in `docs/spec_topics/language-and-architecture.md` carries the heading `### V1 non-goals` (line 6), but everything around it has been converted to the canonical `loom 1.0` spelling. The two `<a id>` anchors immediately above the heading (line 5) are `loom-1-0-non-goals-aggregator` (canonical arm) and `v1-non-goals` (back-compat alias arm); the section body (line 8) refers to "the consolidated loom 1.0 non-goals list"; the owning page heads its section `## loom 1.0 non-goals` (`future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md`); and every inbound cross-reference — in `overview-and-orientation.md`, `version-bump-intro.md`, `conversation-drive.md`, `session-model-and-appendix.md`, and the `host-prerequisites.md` presupposition prose — uses the words "loom 1.0 non-goals". This is the only `V1`-spelled non-goals heading remaining in the corpus.

The mismatch is purely a spelling lag, not a governance violation: GOV-20 (`governance/release-version-naming.md`) makes `V1` a governed alias for "loom 1.0" and permits mixed spellings during incremental conversion, and GOV-25's incidental-auto-id prohibition uses `### loom 1.0 non-goals` as its worked example, confirming the dual-anchor pair is the stable citation surface. The heading text is therefore the lone unconverted callsite for a section whose surroundings have all been converted, leaving a reader who lands on the heading staring at `V1 non-goals` while the anchor, body, and inbound links all say "loom 1.0".

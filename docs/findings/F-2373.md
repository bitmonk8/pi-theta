## Finding

`V18c` (`docs/plan_topics/V18c-version-bump-checklist.md`) reads two constants from `V9a`'s single source-of-truth pinned-constants block at build time: `FACTORY_PROBABLE_CAPABILITIES` (the partition-reconciliation symbol) and `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS` (the cancellation-runtime cap, consumed by V18c's literal-read assertion). Both are exported by `V9a` per its Adds field.

V18c's `Deps` field is `` `V18c-T`, `V18a`, `V18b` `` â€” it does not name `V9a`. The ordering edge to `V9a` is satisfied only transitively, because `V18a` declares `` `V18a-T`, `V9a` `` in its own Deps. The plan is therefore executable today: build order reaches `V9a` before `V18c` through `V18c â†’ V18a â†’ V9a`.

The gap is that V18c's *direct* consumption of `V9a`-owned symbols is undeclared. The declared dependency graph does not reflect the actual symbol-consumption edge, so the ordering guarantee for V18c's V9a reads rests on V18a continuing to depend on V9a rather than on V18c's own Deps.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V18c-version-bump-checklist.md`, add `` `V9a` `` to the `**Deps.**` field so the direct constant-consumption edge is declared. The field currently reads `` **Deps.** `V18c-T`, `V18a`, `V18b` ``; it should also list `` `V9a` ``. The existing transitive chain through `V18a` is unaffected; this makes V18c's own dependence on `V9a`'s `FACTORY_PROBABLE_CAPABILITIES` and `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS` explicit and robust against future restructuring of `V18a`'s Deps. No other field of V18c changes, and no spec edit is required.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Nothing breaks at present: the `V18c â†’ V18a â†’ V9a` chain orders the build correctly and V18c's literal-read assertions resolve their `V9a` symbols. The latent risk is that V18c's direct dependence on `V9a` is invisible in its Deps, so a future edit that drops `V9a` from `V18a`'s Deps would silently remove V18c's only ordering guarantee for `FACTORY_PROBABLE_CAPABILITIES` / `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS`, exposing a compile-time red.

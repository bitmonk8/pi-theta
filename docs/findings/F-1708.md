## Finding

`docs/spec_topics/query/query-escapes-stringification.md` currently anchors two normative rules — `QRY-20` (operator-facing observability of a discarded `Err`: the always-log runtime event delivered on the `loom-system-note` channel with `display: false`, plus the `RuntimeEvent.discard_site` source-position stamp) and its subsection `QRY-21` (runtime panics raised inside a `${expr}` interpolation propagate before the enclosing `let _ =` binding completes, so the discard form does not contain them). Neither rule is about *escapes* or about how a value of a given Loom static type renders into prompt text. `QRY-20` is a failure-observability rule sited on the operator-facing always-log channel; `QRY-21` is a discard-form / runtime-panic propagation rule that happens to fire from an interpolation site but says nothing about how interpolation renders a value.

Page placement carries a load-bearing reader contract: a reviewer or implementer consulting `query-escapes-stringification.md` is asking *"how is the rendered prompt text shaped?"*. They will not consult it for the operator-facing event surface that preserves discarded `Err`s, nor for how runtime panics inside interpolated expressions skip the `let _ =` discard. Conversely, an implementer wiring up the discard event or auditing what does and does not escape a `let _ =` binding will go to the failure-and-repair surface and find nothing.

This is the residual of a cluster: a sibling finding moves `QRY-19` (the parse-time discarded-query-result contract) off the same page to `query-forms.md`; the page-title finding observes the whole umbrella mis-scope. Resolving this one closes the runtime/observability half of the same cluster.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Move `## Observability of discarded results` (containing `QRY-20`) and its subsection `### Panics during interpolation are not caught by ` `` `let _ =` `` (containing `QRY-21`) out of `docs/spec_topics/query/query-escapes-stringification.md` into `docs/spec_topics/query/query-failure-and-repair.md`, carrying the `<a id="qry-20">` and `<a id="qry-21">` anchors verbatim so existing inbound links continue to resolve. Insert the relocated section after the `## Failure modes` block (which establishes `QueryError` variants and the always-log surface vocabulary `QRY-20`'s body references) and before `### Forced respond turn non-compliance` — that position makes the relocated rules the natural continuation of "what happens to an `Err` the runtime produces" before the page narrows to schema-validation repair. Update the relative path in `QRY-20`'s body from `../pi-integration-contract.md` to the same form already used elsewhere in `query-failure-and-repair.md` (the path depth is identical, so the existing href text is preserved). Update the `**Spec.**` lines in `docs/plan_topics/V13g-query-discard-observability.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V13g-T-query-discard-observability.md` to swap `query-escapes-stringification.md` for `query-failure-and-repair.md`. In `docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md`, move `QRY-21` out of the `V13a` row and add it to the `V13g` row, leaving the `V13a` row as `QRY-1, QRY-5, QRY-6, QRY-7, QRY-17, QRY-18` and the `V13g` row as `QRY-19, QRY-20, QRY-21`.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- The page-title finding (`Page title understates scope (QRY-19/20/21)`) recommends renaming `query-escapes-stringification.md`. Resolving the current finding first removes `QRY-20` and `QRY-21` from the page's contents, which then reduces that sibling to a `QRY-19`-only question — once `QRY-19` is also moved (the other sibling finding), the page-title scope concern is fully discharged. Do not pre-empt that finding's edit here.
- The `QRY-19` sibling finding moves the parse rule to `query-forms.md`, not to `query-failure-and-repair.md`. Resist the temptation to co-locate all three rules on a single new page: `QRY-19` is a parse-time contract that belongs with the other query forms; `QRY-20`/`QRY-21` are runtime failure-handling rules that belong with the other failure modes. The split is intentional.
- The `QRY-20` body cross-references the *Runtime event channel* on the pi-integration-contract page; the link target is unaffected by the move (the channel definition is owned by `pi-integration-contract/runtime-event-channel.md`, not by `query-escapes-stringification.md`).

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer of the discard-observability event and of the `let _ =` panic-containment carve-out will look on the failure/repair surface and not find the rules; an auditor reviewing the escapes/stringification surface will be distracted by failure-observability and runtime-panic prose that has no bearing on prompt rendering. The rules themselves are well-formed, so two reasonable implementers will converge on the same behaviour, but discoverability — the load-bearing property of topical placement — degrades.

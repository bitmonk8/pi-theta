## Finding

The bullet in `query-failure-and-repair.md` that pins "a follow-up that fails for a non-validation reason **does not consume an `attempts` slot**" trails a `Rationale: …` clause explaining *why* the rule was chosen ("`attempts` is the bound on *respond-repair*, not on incidental infrastructure failure; consuming a slot for a transport blip would silently shorten the repair budget on retry"). The normative obligation — the slot debits only when re-validation *runs* — is fully expressed by the two preceding sentences; the trailing `Rationale:` clause is design-log motivation about why the rule was preferred over the alternative, not a constraint an implementer must honour.

This is one of two surviving `Rationale:`-prefixed clauses in the spec corpus (the other lives in `query-forms.md`'s empty-template short-circuit bullet) and the only one in the respond-repair page. Inline design rationale in a normative bullet inflates the surface a reader has to scan to extract the obligation, and it sets a pattern that invites further "Rationale: …" sentences to accrete next to other MUSTs.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-by-fix
**Introducing commits:** `06491ee` (2026-05-04, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Coercion follow-up failure modes unspecified\"")
**History:** The bullet — including the `Rationale: …` clause verbatim — was added to the then-single-file `spec_topics/query.md` in `06491ee` while resolving a different finding ("Coercion follow-up failure modes unspecified"). The clause has survived the `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04) split into `query/query-failure-and-repair.md` and the `6406600` (2026-06-25) rewording of the bullet's first sentence (which sharpened "was then re-validated (whether successfully or not)" into the current "the slot debits when that re-validation *runs*…" formulation) without itself being touched. No commit between `06491ee` and the review point intentionally addressed the inline-rationale-in-normative-bullet pattern.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Delete the `Rationale: …` clause from the bullet, leaving the two normative sentences intact. After the edit the bullet reads:

> A follow-up that fails for a non-validation reason **does not consume an `attempts` slot**. `attempts` counts only follow-ups that produced an assistant response which the runtime then re-validated; the slot debits when that re-validation *runs*, regardless of whether it passes or fails.

No new ADR / Background block is required — the surrounding section (the "Non-validation failures during a respond-repair follow-up" prose plus the three sibling bullets and the Edge-cases paragraph) already conveys the operative scope of `attempts` without recourse to the deleted sentence. Do not touch the sibling bullets, the `context_overflow` short-circuit rule, or the Edge-cases paragraph; the change is one-sentence scoped.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader still arrives at the correct obligation; the bullet is merely two sentences longer than it needs to be and normalises decision-log prose adjacent to normative bullets. Leaving it in costs nothing per-bullet but compounds across the corpus.

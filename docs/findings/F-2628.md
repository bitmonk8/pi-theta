## Finding

The session-only degraded-state branch and the `LoomRegistry` drain-state contract each fence their behaviour with prohibitions phrased against *internal state representation* rather than against observable behaviour. In `session-only-degraded-state.md`, the *Seam minimality (normative)* clause states the runtime "MUST NOT introduce any handler-scoped state seam beyond `pinnedConstantReadOk` and `capturedEventReason` — no `branchTakenWasClosedSet` flag, no per-sub-step success roll-up …". In `drain-state-contract.md`, PIC-30 states the runtime "MUST NOT introduce a third boolean drain-state field, an arm-specific 'post-failed-handler' gate on `LoomRegistry.readDrainState`, or any other suppression mechanism …".

Several of the enumerated prohibitions have no observable correlate. A `branchTakenWasClosedSet` local computed as `capturedEventReason ∈ {"new","resume","fork"}`, a "sub-step 4 completed without a throw" roll-up (which the spec itself notes is vacuous because **Per-step isolation** already guarantees sub-step 4 returns), or an internal third field that does not change `readDrainState`'s three-arm return — each would produce byte-identical observable behaviour yet is forbidden by the literal text. A conformant runtime that merely carries an extra intermediate variable is thereby mislabelled non-conformant, and the corresponding plan-leaf acceptance test ("no handler-scoped state seam is introduced") asserts an unobservable property.

The observable contract these clauses actually defend is narrower and correct, and must survive any reframing: (1) the diagnostic-emission predicate must be `pinnedConstantReadOk && capturedEventReason ∈ {"new","resume","fork"}` and the tag-transition predicate the value-independent complement `capturedEventReason ∉ {"quit","reload"}`; (2) the discriminator must consume the *captured* reason value, not a fresh `event.reason` re-read at the discriminator site (a fresh read either re-throws or reads an already-classified value, bypassing the catch-arm classification — an observable divergence); and (3) `readDrainState` must expose exactly the three mutually-exclusive arms of PIC-29, with no fourth arm or alternative gating field that would suppress dispatch on the post-handler `(drained: false, tag: undefined)` tuple. The defect is purely the framing: the genuinely-internal prohibitions over-constrain implementations without protecting any observer.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Restate both clauses as invariants on observable values and the closed dispatch surface, and demote the representation-only prohibitions to non-normative implementation notes.

- In `session-only-degraded-state.md` *Seam minimality (normative)*: keep the normative requirements that (a) the diagnostic-emission predicate is `pinnedConstantReadOk && capturedEventReason ∈ {"new","resume","fork"}`, (b) the tag-transition predicate is the value-independent complement `capturedEventReason ∉ {"quit","reload"}` covering all three arms, and (c) the discriminator reads the captured reason value rather than re-reading `event.reason` at the discriminator site (this last is genuinely observable — a fresh read re-throws or bypasses the catch-arm classification). Move the "no `branchTakenWasClosedSet` flag / no per-sub-step success roll-up / no extra handler-scoped seam" sentence into a non-normative note explaining that the two seams already exhaust both predicates' information requirements, so any additional internal variable is redundant rather than forbidden.
- In `drain-state-contract.md` PIC-30: keep the normative requirement that `readDrainState` exposes exactly the three mutually-exclusive arms of PIC-29 and that no mechanism conditionally suppresses slash dispatch on the post-handler `(drained: false, tag: undefined)` tuple (the all-three-throw tuple is observationally identical to steady-state and MUST route through arm (a)). Demote the "no third boolean drain-state field" enumeration to a non-normative note clarifying that a field with no effect on the three-arm return is simply redundant.
- Update the `V9l` and `V9m` acceptance-test prose so the "no seam introduced" / "no third boolean field" tests assert the observable predicate values and the three-arm dispatch behaviour rather than the absence of an internal field.

Edge case for the fixer: do **not** drop the captured-value-vs-fresh-reread requirement or the closed-three-arm / no-dispatch-suppression requirement — those are the observable core and removing them would re-open the divergences `52b9101` was written to close.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A conformance check taken literally rejects a correct runtime that carries a redundant intermediate variable (or any internally-equivalent representation), and `V9l`'s "no handler-scoped state seam is introduced" / `V9m`'s "no third boolean drain-state field" acceptance criteria assert unobservable properties. Implementers still produce a working system because the observable behaviour is fully pinned by the two predicate forms and the closed three-arm `readDrainState` dispatch, but the over-constraint invites spurious non-conformance flags and obscures which obligations are actually testable.

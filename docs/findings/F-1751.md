## Finding

SM-2's parenthetical reads: *"…the SDK `SessionShutdownEvent['reason']` type is the source of truth for both the payload path and the enumeration of the closed reason set, so a future SDK pin bump diff-audits against that interface"*. The verb "diff-audits" is a coined term: it appears nowhere else in the spec corpus, has no glossary entry, and the clause names neither the agent (human reviewer? `tsc`? a test?), nor the mechanism (a `git diff`? a build-time assertion? a checklist item?), nor the obligation (what triggers, what passes, what fails).

The implied obligation is in fact fully owned downstream. Three distinct surfaces in `pi-integration-contract/` already pin it: the build-time `SessionShutdownEvent['reason']` type-equality assertion gated by the `loom/typecheck/session-shutdown-reason-snapshot` brand string (`version-bump-intro.md` step 1 + `version-bump-triggers.md` step 5); the literal-read SDK surface-inventory test at `version-bump-step2.md#bump-step-2-positive`; and the editorial-review checklist items (a)–(ap) at `version-bump-step2.md`. SM-2 sits adjacent to this machinery and would be the natural inbound link, but the "diff-audits" clause cites none of it, so the reader cannot tell whether SM-2 imposes a fresh obligation (it does not) or merely gestures at one of the three downstream surfaces (and if so which).

The clause is a parenthetical inside a normative bullet; the routing partition SM-2 actually owns (closed-set member vs. unknown reason, delegated to the Unknown-reason rule) is unaffected. The defect is purely clarity: undefined jargon plus a missing forward link to the procedure that owns the obligation the clause hand-waves toward.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace the closing clause of the SM-2 parenthetical — *"so a future SDK pin bump diff-audits against that interface"* — with a forward link to the procedure that owns the obligation. Concretely, drop "diff-audits" and rewrite the clause to point at the two existing surfaces that together discharge it: the build-time `SessionShutdownEvent['reason']` brand-string type-equality assertion (`version-bump-intro.md` step 1 / `version-bump-triggers.md` step 5) and the SDK surface-inventory literal-read assertion (`version-bump-step2.md#bump-step-2-positive`). A drop-in replacement is:

> …the SDK `SessionShutdownEvent['reason']` type is the source of truth for both the payload path and the enumeration of the closed reason set; the obligation to re-verify that source-of-truth claim on each Pi minor bump is owned by [Pi version bump procedure — step 1's `SessionShutdownEvent['reason']` type-equality assertion](./pi-integration-contract/version-bump-intro.md) and [step 2(a)'s SDK surface-inventory literal-read](./pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md#bump-step-2-positive).

Edge cases for the implementer: keep the rewrite inside the existing parenthetical (do not promote it to a sibling bullet — the routing partition is what SM-2 normatively owns); preserve the existing "reproduced here for illustration" framing immediately before the rewritten clause (that framing pins the non-normative status of the inlined union literals and is independently load-bearing); leave the SM-2 anchor `sm-2-closed-shutdown-reason-set` unchanged; and verify the two link targets still resolve after any concurrent edits to `version-bump-intro.md` / `version-bump-step2.md`.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader of SM-2 cannot determine whether "diff-audits" denotes an existing build-time gate, an editorial-review item, or an unstated new obligation. The downstream version-bump procedure already covers the underlying concern, so no implementer behaviour changes; the cost is reader confusion and a missing inbound link from SM-2 to the procedure that owns the obligation.

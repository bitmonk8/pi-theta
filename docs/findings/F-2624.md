## Finding

`schema-subset.md` and `descriptions.md` carry five fragments of non-normative rationale and editorial commentary interleaved with their normative prose:

1. **Subset-derivation provenance** (`schema-subset.md` intro) — the `*(Non-normative provenance.)*` clause explaining that the emitted subset "was chosen at loom 1.0 authoring time to sit within what the loom 1.0-supported providers' strict modes … could enforce."
2. **Depth-ceiling derivation** (`schema-subset.md`, **Depth** bullet) — "The value `5` … was chosen at loom 1.0 authoring time to stay at or below the stricter of the supported providers' published nesting caps." (Same provenance prose, without the explicit marker.)
3. **Draft-choice provenance** (`schema-subset.md`, **Draft** bullet) — the `*(Non-normative provenance.)*` clause explaining 2020-12 "was chosen … as the draft both supported providers' strict modes accepted."
4. **HTML comment** (`schema-subset.md`, Canonical schema hash, step 2) — `<!-- default and the integer-valued structural keywords … are deliberately absent here … -->`, an editorial note explaining why certain keywords never reach the lowered fragment.
5. **Provider-Markdown-rendering claim** (`descriptions.md`, **No transformation** bullet) — the `*(Non-normative provenance.)*` sentence asserting "OpenAI and Anthropic schema consumers at loom 1.0 authoring time render description text as Markdown."

Each fragment is either decision-log rationale (why a normative choice was made) or, in item 5, an empirical claim about external provider behaviour that loom does not own. They are correctly hedged and explicitly flagged non-normative, but they sit inline in the normative subset enumeration, the depth-ceiling definition, the canonical-hash recipe, and the descriptions lowering rules, where they enlarge the reading surface and invite a reader to treat design history as a spec obligation.

Each fragment carries a genuinely normative kernel that must be retained when the cruft is stripped: the *loom-owned, does-not-track-provider-drift* stance (items 1–3), the rejected-keyword guarantee already pinned by the top-of-file enumeration (item 4), and the byte-for-byte emission rule (item 5). The defect is the rationale/history wrapping around those kernels, not the kernels themselves.

No single commit is the origin; the cruft is the cumulative byproduct of three independently-reasonable fixes, each adding explanatory hedging to normative prose.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Strip the historical rationale from each fragment while preserving its normative kernel; the cleanup is one coherent pass over the two pages.

- **Subset intro, Depth bullet, Draft bullet (`schema-subset.md`).** Keep the loom-owned / does-not-track-provider-drift assertion in each (it is a normative stance: implementers must not re-derive the subset, the depth-5 ceiling, or the 2020-12 draft from current provider capabilities). Remove the `*(Non-normative provenance.)*` markers and the "chosen at loom 1.0 authoring time because provider X/Y accepted it" history. If the project keeps a decision log or ADR, relocate the provider-history rationale there; otherwise delete it — it is already explicitly non-normative and adds no implementer value.
- **Canonical schema hash, step 2 (`schema-subset.md`).** Delete the HTML comment. Its content — that `default` and the integer-valued structural keywords never reach the lowered fragment — is already guaranteed by the top-of-file rejected-keyword enumeration and the binder's no-lowering rule; the comment is redundant editorial commentary.
- **No transformation bullet (`descriptions.md`).** Keep the normative rule ("Loom emits description text byte-for-byte … no escaping, dedenting, or wrapping … beyond the multi-line join and common-leading-whitespace strip"). Delete the `*(Non-normative provenance.)*` sentence asserting OpenAI/Anthropic render descriptions as Markdown — it is an empirical claim about external provider behaviour loom neither controls nor verifies. If authors need that hint, relocate it to a non-goal or decision-log entry rather than the normative lowering rules.

Edge case for the implementer: do not delete the decoupling clauses in items 1–3. Removing "it is loom-owned and does not track later changes to any provider's cap" (and its subset/draft siblings) would reintroduce the very provider-coupling defect that commit 29e4faa fixed.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

If shipped unfixed, the spec carries decision-log rationale and an unowned provider claim inside normative sections. No implementer-observable behaviour changes and no two implementers diverge; the cost is a larger reading surface and a standing risk that a future reader mistakes design history (or provider rendering behaviour loom cannot guarantee) for a loom obligation.

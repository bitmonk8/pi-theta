## Problem

The final-value-contract Tests bullet in `V3d` and `V3d-T` asserts the function body's produced value "is its final value at the function-result seam on success and is absent on fail/cancel," and `V3d`'s **Ships when** repeats the absent-on-fail/cancel clause. Per the spec (`functions.md` §"Final value (language definition)"), the *absence* of a final value on fail/cancel is observable only at a caller surface — an `invoke` parent's `Ok` payload or the subagent-boundary return — not at `V3d`'s own scope. None of those surfaces, nor the `V17a` cancellation primitive or the `V4c` cancelled-outcome envelope, is reachable from `V3d`'s declared **Deps** (`V3d-T`, `V3a`, `V4a`); `V15a` and `V9i` depend on `V3d`, not the reverse. A `V3d-T` cancel test therefore cannot red out for the intended reason, so an implementer invents a local seam abstraction and cancel sentinel that may diverge from the real `V15a`/`V9i` return surfaces and the `V17a`/`V4c` model.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-by-specific-commit
**Introducing commit:** `7a8565a` (2026-06-11) — "pi-loom plan: resolve \"V3d-T over-asserts final-value propagation\""
**History:** At plan inception (`c6a664e`, 2026-06-10) `V3d-T`'s final-value bullet read "the final value propagates to an `invoke`/subagent caller on success and is absent on fail/cancel" — i.e. the absence claim was anchored to the caller surface where it is observable, and `V3d`'s Ships when said only "final-value propagation." Commit `7a8565a`, a prior review-fix targeting the over-assertion of final-value *propagation* at `V3d`, removed the caller-surface anchoring and replaced it with the "function-result seam" coinage at `V3d`'s own scope, while retaining the "absent on fail/cancel" clause and adding it to `V3d`'s Ships when. `git log -S "function-result seam"` confirms that commit is the sole origin of the coinage across `V3d`, `V3d-T`, `V15a`, and `V9i`. The over-correction stripped the only construct that made the absence assertion observable, producing the present unreachable-at-dep-scope defect.

## Solution approach

Narrow the final-value-contract Tests bullet in both `docs/plan_topics/V3d-functions-and-return.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V3d-T-functions-and-return.md` to assert only the success-path production reachable at `V3d`'s dep scope — including the `?`/Err early-return short-circuit that `V4a` makes reachable — and drop the "absent on fail/cancel" clause. Rewrite `V3d`'s **Ships when** to assert the function-result-seam final value produced on success only. Leave the absent-on-fail/cancel observation to the `V15a` and `V9i` delegated witnesses that already carry it.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the absent-on-fail/cancel observation is owned by the `V15a` (invoke return) and `V9i` (subagent return) witnesses; do not add `V15a`/`V9i` to `V3d`'s **Deps**, since they depend on `V3d` and that edge would be circular.
- Keep the `cka-7` row in `docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md` jointly closed after the trim (Plan rules require the coverage matrix to stay consistent on leaf edits).

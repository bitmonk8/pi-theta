## Problem

The seven audit-cluster pages under `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/` carry no consolidated Non-goals / out-of-scope section, even though the subsystem ships five explicit scope dispositions (capabilities the audit deliberately does not require, capabilities the implementation MAY add, and capabilities deferred past loom 1.0). Each disposition is currently buried in one normative paragraph among many, so a reviewer assessing scope creep, an implementer asking whether a capability is MUST or MAY, and a future PIC editor evaluating a "should we widen the audit?" proposal must grep across the seven pages to reconstruct the set. The Pi version-bump procedure already uses the scannable-index idiom (`version-bump-intro.md` `### Non-goals`); the audit subsystem missed the retrofit. The omission is organisational, not normative — the five dispositions are an existing closed set drawn from text already in the corpus.

## Solution approach

Add a consolidated Non-goals / out-of-scope section at the top of `inventory-audit-intro.md`, before the `sdk-capability-inventory` anchor, following the `version-bump-intro.md` `### Non-goals` idiom — a scannable index where each entry forward-links to its authoritative paragraph rather than restating it. Index the five existing audit-cluster scope dispositions: citation-resolution against a REQ-ID / PIC-anchor registry (deferred past loom 1.0), the `scope-audit/generated-source-in-tree` discriminator (MAY), type-aware tooling (MAY), a non-empty-scan canary floor above `> 0` (MAY), and mechanical detection of substitution-induced stale exemption markers (contributor-discipline-only).

## Solution constraints

- Editorial only: do not move normative content, change MUST/MAY/SHOULD modals, or rename anchors; the inline disclaimers at each cited paragraph remain the governing statements and the new section must not restate them or introduce a disposition not already present in the cited paragraphs.

## Finding

The seven audit-cluster pages under `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/` (`inventory-audit-intro.md`, `audit-target-categories.md`, `audit-recognised-shapes.md`, `audit-resolution.md`, `audit-failures.md`, `audit-wire-and-canary.md`, `capability-inventory-items.md`) carry no Non-goals / Out-of-scope section. The subsystem nonetheless ships several explicit scope dispositions: capabilities the audit deliberately does not require, capabilities the implementation MAY add but PIC does not mandate, and capabilities deferred past loom 1.0. Each disposition is currently buried in one normative paragraph among many. The five concrete dispositions a reader has to reconstruct by scanning the cluster are:

1. **Citation-resolution against a registry of valid REQ-IDs / PIC anchors** — deferred past loom 1.0 (`audit-resolution.md` *Citation-token grammar*; restated in `audit-failures.md` *Stale-citation limitation*). Markers citing a non-existent `REQ-9999` or `PIC#nonexistent-anchor` parse as well-formed and authorise the surface.
2. **`scope-audit/generated-source-in-tree` discriminator (a `// @generated` heuristic on `src/**/*.ts`)** — MAY, not required (`audit-resolution.md` *Generated-output-in-src limitation*). Widening the inventory-closure audit's family-set to subsume it is deferred past loom 1.0.
3. **Type-aware tooling (TypeScript-program symbol-table walk as an alternative to a static-AST walker)** — MAY, not required (`inventory-audit-intro.md` introductory paragraph; `audit-recognised-shapes.md` closing sentence). The audit's behavioural contract is invariant under tooling choice.
4. **Per-counter floor above `> 0` for the non-empty-scan canary** — MAY, not required (`audit-wire-and-canary.md` *Non-empty-scan canary*). PIC pins only the `> 0` floor; a higher pinned floor requires a baseline-snapshot mechanism the implementation owns.
5. **Stale exemption marker introduced by a substitution refactor** (the *Substitution limitation* under `audit-failures.md` *Stale-marker discriminator*) — contributor-discipline-only, no audit discriminator owed in loom 1.0.

The Pi version-bump procedure already follows this idiom: `version-bump-intro.md` opens with a `### Non-goals` section that is a scannable index of (a)–(d) items, each one a one-line forward link to the authoritative paragraph rather than a restatement (the inline disclaimers remain governing). A reviewer assessing scope creep, an implementer asking whether they MUST or MAY ship a given capability, and a future PIC editor evaluating a "should we widen the audit?" proposal all currently lack the same scannable surface for the audit subsystem and have to grep across the seven pages to enumerate it. The five items above are an existing, closed set drawn from text already in the corpus; the omission is organisational, not normative.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add a `## Non-goals / Out of scope for loom 1.0` section at the top of `inventory-audit-intro.md`, immediately after the H1 and before `## SDK capability inventory`. Follow the `version-bump-intro.md` `### Non-goals` format exactly: each entry is a one-sentence forward-link to the authoritative paragraph rather than a restatement, and the entry's normative disposition (`MAY` / `deferred past loom 1.0` / `contributor-discipline-only`) is taken verbatim from the cited paragraph. The opening sentence explicitly states that the inline dispositions on the cited paragraphs remain the governing statements; the new section is an index under GOV-12, not a normative copy. Enumerate exactly these five entries, in this order:

- (a) **Citation-resolution against a REQ-ID / PIC-anchor registry.** Deferred past loom 1.0; see [`audit-resolution.md` — *Citation-token grammar*](./audit-resolution.md#audit-exemption-mechanism) and [`audit-failures.md` — *Stale-citation limitation*](./audit-failures.md).
- (b) **`scope-audit/generated-source-in-tree` discriminator (`// @generated` heuristic on `src/**/*.ts`).** MAY, not required; widening the inventory-closure audit's family-set to subsume it is deferred past loom 1.0. See [`audit-resolution.md` — *Generated-output-in-src limitation*](./audit-resolution.md#audit-scope).
- (c) **Type-aware tooling (TypeScript-program symbol-table walk).** MAY, not required; the audit's behavioural contract is invariant under tooling choice. See the introductory paragraph of this file and [`audit-recognised-shapes.md` closing sentence](./audit-recognised-shapes.md#audit-recognised-shapes).
- (d) **Non-empty-scan canary per-counter floor above `> 0`.** MAY, not required; PIC pins only the `> 0` floor. A higher pinned floor requires a baseline-snapshot mechanism. See [`audit-wire-and-canary.md` — *Non-empty-scan canary*](./audit-wire-and-canary.md#audit-non-empty-scan-canary).
- (e) **Mechanical detection of substitution-induced stale exemption markers.** Contributor-discipline-only, no audit discriminator owed in loom 1.0. See [`audit-failures.md` — *Substitution limitation*](./audit-failures.md).

Implementer notes for the fixer:

- The section is editorial — no normative content moves, no MUST/MAY/SHOULD modal changes, no anchor renames. The inline disclaimers at each cited paragraph remain the governing statements; the new section MUST NOT restate them or invent a new disposition.
- The list is closed at exactly the five items above. Adding a sixth item silently widens the loom 1.0 disposition surface; any candidate sixth item must instead extend the cited normative paragraph and then be forward-linked from here.
- Place the section before `## SDK capability inventory` so it precedes any normative content the file owns, mirroring the `version-bump-intro.md` placement.
- Use the same one-line-per-item shape as `version-bump-intro.md`; do NOT introduce a sub-bullet structure. The section is a scannable index, not a discussion.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A conformant audit can be built without the section — every disposition is present somewhere in the normative text. The cost is review-time and onboarding friction: a reviewer auditing whether a proposed sixth `<family>` discriminator is in or out of loom 1.0 scope, an implementer asking whether the higher canary floor is mandatory, and a future PIC editor evaluating "should we promote scope-audit/generated-source-in-tree to a hard gate?" all currently have no canonical disposition list to argue against and must grep across seven pages to reconstruct it.

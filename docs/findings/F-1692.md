## Finding

PIC-52 in `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/trust-boundary.md` states that the runtime "MUST NOT introduce additional access channels (sandbox, capability filter, mediated proxy) in any loom 1.x release; widening or narrowing this rule is a major-version concern." Because the underlying rule is a *prohibition*, the phrase "widening or narrowing this rule" has two opposite readings:

- Reading A — *narrowing the rule's scope = relaxing it*: the rule now forbids fewer channel categories, so a sandbox is allowed in loom 1.x. Under this reading "narrowing this rule" is the major-version trigger.
- Reading B — *narrowing the rule's scope = strengthening it*: the rule now forbids strictly more channel categories. Under this reading "widening" is the relaxation that needs a major bump and "narrowing" is the strengthening that needs one too.

The wording leaves a future spec editor unable to tell which class of follow-on edit forces a loom 2.0 bump and which is permissible in a minor release. This matters because PIC-52 is the explicit governance hook the trust-boundary topic uses to time-bind the no-extra-mediation guarantee: if the trigger condition is ambiguous, neither direction is reliably guarded.

The fix is to name the two edit shapes directly in terms of the prohibition itself rather than via a meta-reference to "this rule," so the trigger is unambiguous to a reader applying the version-bump rules without re-deriving them.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** `52219c4` ("Address all 41 spec-review findings", 2026-05-06) added the "widening or narrowing this rule is a major-version concern" clause as part of the original *No additional access channels* NFR text under the trust-boundary cluster fix.
**History:** The clause has carried verbatim through `e4017a8` (2026-06-25, F-1260 relocation to `trust-boundary.md`) and `1acdc55` (2026-06-25, F-1344 PIC-52 anchor coinage). Neither relocation nor anchor coinage changed the wording. The ambiguity has therefore existed for the entire lifetime of the rule itself; no later edit caused the defect.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/trust-boundary.md`, replace the trailing clause of the PIC-52 sentence:

> ...in any loom 1.x release; widening or narrowing this rule is a major-version concern.

with text that names both directions explicitly, e.g.:

> ...in any loom 1.x release. Relaxing this prohibition to permit any of the listed channel categories (sandbox, capability filter, mediated proxy), or extending it to cover additional categories not listed here, is a major-version concern.

Implementer guidance:

- Keep the "(sandbox, capability filter, mediated proxy)" enumeration as the closed list the relax-direction references; do not introduce a separate enumeration.
- Use "extending" (not "widening") for the strengthening direction and "relaxing" (not "narrowing") for the loosening direction, so the trigger language matches the verbs already used elsewhere in the version-bump topics.
- The sentence remains a single MUST-NOT plus a governance follow-on; no new normative obligation is added, no anchor changes, and PIC-52 retains its current shape and citers (`coverage-matrix.md`, `V14d`, `V14d-T`).

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A future spec editor proposing a 1.x change that touches the prohibition (e.g. adding a mediated proxy for a specific Pi-API surface) cannot tell from PIC-52 alone whether the change requires a major bump or is admissible in a minor release. The defect does not stop implementation of loom 1.0 but undermines PIC-52's stated purpose of binding the no-extra-mediation guarantee across the 1.x line.

## Finding

`V10c` (Settings reads and merge) carries three units that exercise different
machinery and verify against different spec surfaces:

1. **Settings reads / deep-merge / validation** â€” the five settings keys, the
   project-over-global deep-merge precedence, and the malformed-settings
   load-phase diagnostic. This is the unit the coverage matrix anchors at
   `V10c` via `DISC-7`.
2. **Reload debounce** â€” the 250 ms `Clock`-driven drop-and-reschedule window,
   verified by a `FakeClock` virtual-time vector (a burst of N events in one
   window produces exactly one reload; a later event across the boundary
   produces a second).
3. **The settings-re-merge sub-arm of the watcher-time reload
   failure-injection seam** â€” contributed against the test-only
   `ReloadFailureInjector` interface that `V9b` declares, routing a synthetic
   re-merge failure onto the `loom-system-note` path with `triggerTurn:false`.
   Its REQ-ID closure (`ERR-7`) lives at `V4e`, not at `V10c`.

Units 1 and 2 are independently buildable and reviewable; unit 3 stands up the
failure-injection plumbing against a separate seam owner. A reviewer cannot
confirm deep-merge correctness, debounce coalescing arithmetic, and the
failure-injection arm in one pass.

A second-order consequence sharpens the split: unit 2 (the reload debounce) has
no coverage-matrix row of its own today â€” it rides on the `DISC-7`-anchored
`V10c` leaf. Carving units 2 and 3 into a new leaf produces a leaf with no
coverage-matrix entry, the same untraceable-leaf class flagged for `V11g` /
`V15d` / `V17b` elsewhere in this review. The split therefore carries two
obligations: decompose the leaf, and restore traceability of the carved-out
leaf.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

If `V10c` ships as one leaf, a reviewer must verify deep-merge precedence,

blocker; implementers can still produce a working leaf.

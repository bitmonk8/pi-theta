## Problem

The `V10c` leaf (Settings reads and merge) carries three units that exercise
different machinery and verify against different spec surfaces: the settings
reads / deep-merge precedence / validation (the `DISC-7` closure); the 250 ms
`Clock`-driven reload debounce; and the settings-re-merge sub-arm of the
watcher-time reload failure-injection seam, contributed against `V9b`'s
`ReloadFailureInjector` interface with its `ERR-7` closure homed at `V4e`. A
reviewer cannot confirm deep-merge correctness, debounce coalescing arithmetic,
and the failure-injection arm in one pass. Compounding the split, the reload
debounce has no coverage-matrix row of its own — it rides the `DISC-7`-anchored
`V10c` row — so carving it out produces an untraceable leaf.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction (accretion atop inception bundling)
**Introducing commits:** `c6a664e` (2026-06-10, initial plan build) co-located units 1+2; `66acde6` (2026-06-11) introduced the failure-injection arm (unit 3); `d64dce5` (2026-06-11) and `fdce2a2` (2026-06-12) named/re-homed the `ReloadFailureInjector` interface.
**History:** `git log --follow` on `docs/plan_topics/V10c-settings-merge.md` shows the leaf was born (`c6a664e`) bundling settings reads, deep-merge precedence, validation, and the reload debounce — units 1 and 2 have been co-located since inception. The settings-re-merge sub-arm of the failure-injection seam was not present at inception: `git log -S 'failure-injection seam'` first surfaces it at `66acde6` ("resolve 'ERR-7 test injects at an undefined channel seam'"), with `d64dce5` ("Watcher-time reload failure-injection seam under-specified and ungated") naming the `ReloadFailureInjector` interface and `fdce2a2` ("ReloadFailureInjector seam has two owners") moving the single declaration site to `V9b` and leaving `V10c` to contribute the arm. The leaf crossed the atomicity threshold when unit 3 accreted while a chain of ERR-7 / failure-injection findings were being resolved.

## Solution approach

Split `V10c` into two paired leaves: reduce `V10c` (and `V10c-T`) to the
settings reads, deep-merge precedence, validation, and malformed-settings
diagnostic, keeping the `V10c` ID on the merge leaf so `DISC-7` and the
merged-settings consumers stay pinned without edits. Carve the `Clock`-driven
reload debounce and the settings-re-merge failure-injection arm into a new
paired leaf, linked from the `docs/plan.md` "V10 — Discovery and settings"
listing and added to the `H5b` coverage-producing-set Deps. Repoint the `ERR-7`
settings-re-merge contributor reference and the `V10c` Deps edge in `V4e` /
`V4e-T` to the new leaf. Restore traceability of the carved-out leaf by
anchoring the reload-debounce obligation
(`discovery/package-and-settings.md#caching-and-reload`) to a falsifiable
coverage-matrix entry.

## Solution constraints

- The spec is read-only for this fix; `package-and-settings.md
  §caching-and-reload` already states the reload obligation and is cited
  read-only.
- New leaf IDs and their `-T` pair follow the plan-leaf ID scheme in
  `## Plan rules` (next free letter in the `V10` phase, no ID reuse).

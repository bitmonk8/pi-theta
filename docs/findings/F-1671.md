## Finding

The *Parameter-line reference renderings* block in `binder-bypass-and-envelope.md` pins a fourth reference rendering for the description-omitted form (`  language (string) required`) and closes the sentence with the qualifier "the line ends after `required`, with no trailing space, no em-dash, and no `\n`-internal whitespace." The "no `\n`-internal whitespace" qualifier is unparsable as written:

- Read one way ("no whitespace inside / preceding the `\n`"), it forbids any whitespace anywhere on the line — directly contradicting the immediately-following sentence that mandates the two leading U+0020 SPACE characters, and contradicting the table rows above that include intra-line spaces (e.g. between `language` and `(string)`).
- Read another way ("no `\n` character embedded inside the line as whitespace"), it is trivially true (the `\n` is the terminator, not a content character) and constrains nothing.
- The intended meaning — given that the preceding clauses already forbid a trailing space and a trailing em-dash — is "no trailing whitespace of any kind between `required` and the terminating `\n`" (i.e. the rule also rejects a trailing tab, U+00A0, or other non-space whitespace that the explicit "no trailing space" clause leaves open).

A test author targeting the literal text will pick whichever reading they hit first; an implementation that emits `  language (string) required\t\n` is conforming under the "embedded `\n`" reading and non-conforming under the intended one.

The clause has carried the ambiguity unchanged since its first authoring; no subsequent edit revisited it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace the trailing qualifier `"no trailing space, no em-dash, and no \`\n\`-internal whitespace"` with an unambiguous closed enumeration, e.g.:

> the line ends after `required` — no trailing whitespace of any kind (no U+0020 SPACE, U+0009 TAB, U+00A0 NO-BREAK SPACE, or other whitespace character) between `required` and the terminating `\n`, and no em-dash.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- The two **leading** U+0020 SPACE characters remain mandatory (per the next sentence); the "no trailing whitespace" rule applies only between `required` and the line-terminating `\n`.
- The terminating byte is `\n` (U+000A), not `\r\n`; the rule forbids `\r` immediately before `\n` as well (a `\r` is whitespace and is between `required` and the `\n`).
- The byte-exact reference-rendering MUST in the sentence above this one already implies this rule for the four enumerated input shapes; the qualifier exists to extend the rule to the description-omitted form in general, which is why the unambiguous phrasing matters.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The reference-rendering table immediately above pins the byte sequence `  language (string) required` and the surrounding prose mandates byte-exact reproduction, so a strict conformance check against the table already catches a trailing tab. The ambiguity is in the *prose qualifier* that explains *why* the line ends where it does; an implementer reading only the qualifier (without cross-checking the byte-exact MUST) could rationalise emitting trailing whitespace, and a documentation reader hits a sentence that does not parse coherently.

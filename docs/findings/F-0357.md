## Finding

`V5b` (`docs/plan_topics/V5b-disc-unions-recursion.md`) packs two algorithmically-independent schema analyses into a single leaf:

1. **Discriminator detection** — implicit detection (present-in-every-variant / single string-literal / unique value) plus the explicit `by <field>` override on `=` alias forms, carrying the diagnostic set `loom/parse/non-string-discriminator`, `ambiguous-discriminator`, `missing-discriminator`, `duplicate-discriminator-value`, `nested-discriminator`, and `by-on-object-schema`.
2. **Recursion / cycle detection** — auto `$defs`/`$ref` lowering for self- and mutual recursion plus the type-alias-cycle detector (object-hop cycles legal), carrying `loom/parse/type-alias-cycle`.

These are unrelated passes over the schema-declaration AST: the first is a field-matching analysis across union variants; the second is a reachability/cycle walk over the named-type graph. They share no diagnostic codes and no analysis state. The leaf exposes a single `Ships when` gate ("`npm test` detects implicit/explicit discriminators and the legal/illegal recursion cases"), so the two halves cannot be shipped or verified independently — partial completion produces no observable state, and a single-pass review must hold both analyses in view at once.

The severity is modest: the spec (`docs/spec_topics/schemas.md` §Discriminated unions, §Recursion) defines both behaviours unambiguously, so an implementer is not at risk of diverging on correctness — the cost is review burden and unobservable partial progress, not a coverage or contradiction defect.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Split into two leaves

**Approach:** Carve `V5b` into a discriminator-detection leaf (implicit detection + explicit `by <field>`, with the six discriminator diagnostics) and a recursion/cycle-detection leaf (`$defs`/`$ref` lowering + `type-alias-cycle`), each with its paired `-T`. The implementer allocates the two real IDs (use `<new>` placeholders until then).

**Plan edits:** Create the two leaf files and their `-T` partners; relink the V5 slice list in `docs/plan.md`; split the `schemas.md (SCHM)` row in `coverage-matrix.md` so each new leaf is the closing leaf for the REQ-IDs it owns; retarget the `Deps.` references to `V5b` in `V5d`/`V5d-T`/`V5f`/`V5f-T` to the new leaf(s) each actually consumes; update `H5b`'s `Deps.` (the contiguous `V5a`–`V5f` range no longer expresses the set once the IDs change).

**Spec edits:** None.

**Pros:** Each analysis ships and is reviewed independently; partial progress becomes observable.

**Cons:** Propagation touches ≥8 files (two new leaf pairs, four dependent `Deps.` fields, the coverage-matrix row, the H5b range, the V5 listing) for a low-severity gain.

**Risks:** The `V5a`–`V5f` range in `H5b.Deps` and any other range-style references must be re-derived against the new IDs; a missed reference silently drops a closing leaf from the transitive-completeness set.

### Option B — Keep one leaf, regroup its contents

**Approach:** Leave `V5b` and `V5b-T` as single leaves but reorganise their `Adds.`/`Tests.` so the discriminator-detection material and the recursion/cycle-detection material read as two clearly-named, self-contained groups within the leaf.

**Plan edits:** Edit `V5b` and `V5b-T` only — re-shape the `Adds.` and `Tests.` prose into the two named groups. No DAG, coverage-matrix, or dependent-`Deps.` changes.

**Spec edits:** None.

**Pros:** Resolves the single-pass review-burden concern with a one-file edit; no propagation; no ID allocation.

**Cons:** The two halves still share one `Ships when` gate, so partial completion remains unobservable.

### Recommendation

Take **Option B**. The two analyses are genuinely independent, but the leaf is small (one extra diagnostic beyond the discriminator set) and importance is low, so the split's propagation cost across `V5d`/`V5f`, the coverage-matrix row, and `H5b`'s dependency range is not warranted; regrouping the leaf's `Adds.`/`Tests.` into two named cohesive areas addresses the review-burden concern at the cost of one file. Reserve Option A for the case where the recursion arm is later judged to warrant independent shipping. Edge case for whichever option is taken: keep the explicit `by <field>` form grouped with implicit discriminator detection (the spec treats them as one rule with a shared string-literal constraint), not with recursion.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

If shipped as-is, the single `Ships when` gate means neither the discriminator nor the recursion half can be marked complete or reviewed in isolation; a reviewer must absorb two unrelated analyses in one pass and partial implementation yields no observable green-state milestone. No spec rule goes uncovered and no implementer-divergence risk exists, so the leaf still produces a correct result — this is an organisation/review-cost concern, not a correctness or coverage gap.

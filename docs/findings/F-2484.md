## Finding

The "Transient toasts (auxiliary)" paragraph in `diagnostic-shape.md` correctly prohibits all three toast-surface arms from writing the `LoomRegistry` drain-state tag. Alongside that prohibition it gives an inline rationale that restates PIC's `session_shutdown` handler invariant: the tag "machinery is reserved for the `session_shutdown` teardown path, whose handler-entry short-circuit predicate assumes the tag is written only there … so a toast-surface write would cause a later `session_shutdown` to short-circuit and skip teardown."

That short-circuit predicate — `snapshot.drained === true || snapshot.tag !== undefined`, evaluated once at handler entry — and its skip-teardown semantics are owned and fully specified by the PIC drain-state contract (the idempotent-handler clause and PIC-29 in `drain-state-contract.md`). Embedding a paraphrase of the predicate's behaviour in `diagnostic-shape.md` creates a second normative-adjacent surface for the same invariant. The two are not bound by any lockstep/sweep rule, unlike the several "a future edit MUST sweep both" couplings PIC maintains internally. PIC has already broadened this predicate once (from a tag-only check to the drained-or-tag disjunction); the next change to the predicate updates PIC but silently leaves the `diagnostic-shape.md` restatement behind, at which point a reader who consults only `diagnostic-shape.md` gets a stale account of *why* the prohibition exists.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Keep the prohibition on this page — it is the toast surface's own contract: "None of the three arms writes the `LoomRegistry` drain-state tag; that machinery is reserved for the `session_shutdown` teardown path." Replace the inline predicate rationale ("whose handler-entry short-circuit predicate assumes the tag is written only there … so a toast-surface write would cause a later `session_shutdown` to short-circuit and skip teardown") with the existing `(see [PIC's `LoomRegistry` drain-state contract](../pi-integration-contract/drain-state-contract.md#loom-registry-drain-state-contract))` cross-reference as the single pointer to *why*, optionally framed as "writing it out-of-band would corrupt that contract's short-circuit behaviour." Do not restate the predicate expression or the skip-teardown consequence in `diagnostic-shape.md`; the PIC drain-state contract is the source of truth for both.

Edge case for the editor: the prohibition sentence and the three per-arm "writes no drain-state tag" statements (arms (1)–(3)) stay normative on this page — only the predicate-mechanism justification moves to the cross-reference.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Two surfaces describe the same `session_shutdown` short-circuit invariant with no enforced lockstep, while PIC is the sole owner of the predicate and has already broadened it once. If PIC's predicate changes again, the `diagnostic-shape.md` restatement drifts and an implementer reasoning from `diagnostic-shape.md` alone works from a stale invariant. No behaviour is wrong today; the prohibition itself is unambiguous.

## Finding

`docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings/ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md` is titled
`# Ceiling invariants and audit`, and the parent index
`docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings.md` links to it under the same label. But
the page's `##` sections are `Worked consequences` and `No additional loom
1.0 runtime ceiling applies` (the `NOCEIL-1 â€¦ NOCEIL-4` claims plus the
*Audit methodology*, *Routing obligation*, *Rejected-candidate record*, and
*Release gate* paragraphs). The ceiling-set invariants the title promises â€”
the *Audience-coverage invariant* and the *Ceiling-#1 panic-uniqueness
invariant* â€” are not on this page; they are on its sibling
`hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md` under `## Ceiling-set invariants`
(anchor `#ceiling-set-invariants`), and every cross-page reference to those
invariants in the corpus correctly resolves to that sibling file. The H1
therefore systematically misdescribes the file: a reader who follows the
parent-index link looking for "ceiling invariants" lands on a page whose
body opens with worked consequences and never defines an invariant.

The mismatch is purely an H1/TOC-label defect â€” the file's actual content
(NOCEIL claims + audit methodology + worked consequences) is coherent on
its own and is correctly cross-linked from the rest of the corpus by
anchor (`#audit-methodology`, `#four-axis-closure-list`,
`#no-additional-ceilings`, `#release-gate`). No anchor, REQ-ID, or
inline-label registration is affected.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Rename the page heading and the parent-index TOC label to describe what
the page actually contains. Concretely:

- In `docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings/ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md`,
  replace the H1 `# Ceiling invariants and audit` with a heading that names
  the page's two top-level sections â€” for example
  `# NOCEIL claims and audit methodology` (the NOCEIL block carries the
  "worked consequences" as an introductory section, so the title need not
  itemise both).
- In `docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings.md` `## Contents`, update the link
  label to match the new H1.

Leave the filename (`ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md`) and every anchor on
the page (`#audit-methodology`, `#four-axis-closure-list`,
`#no-additional-ceilings`, `#release-gate`) unchanged: every existing
cross-reference in the corpus resolves through those anchors, the
`NOCEIL` inline-label per-page registration in
`governance/stable-inline-labels.md` keys off the file path, and plan
leaves `V16a` / `V16a-T` cite the path. Renaming the file would force
edits across `governance/stable-inline-labels.md`, the parent index, every
inbound anchor reference listed under *Spec Documents*, and both plan
leaves, with no offsetting benefit â€” the navigation defect lives in the
displayed label, not the URL.

The implementer must verify, after the H1 edit, that no on-page paragraph
self-references the page by its old title text (a `grep -n "ceiling
invariants and audit"` on `docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings/` is
sufficient).

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader navigating the `Hard Runtime Ceilings` index for the ceiling-set
invariants follows the obviously-named link and lands on a page that does
not contain them, then has to discover that the invariants live on the
sibling page. Nothing breaks; navigation cost increases and the
`hard-ceilings/` subtree's titles cease to describe its layout, eroding
trust in the index.

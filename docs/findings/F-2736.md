## Finding

`V14a` (and its paired `V14a-T`) bundle several distinct verification surfaces in a single leaf: the three parse-time argument codes (`tool-arg-not-literal` / `tool-arg-arity` / `tool-arg-type-mismatch`), the closed `CodeToolError` enum, the two return-type lowering rows, the `.loom`-callable failure surface, the five runtime `execute()` envelope-lowering mechanics (text-filter + single-`"\n"` join with silent non-text discard, `Ok("")` empty result, `isError:true` → `Err(CodeToolError{cause:"execution"})` with 4096-byte code-point-boundary truncation, the fixed no-text message, and the `execute()`-throw coercion), the `cka-47` tool-call cancellation-checkpoint facet, and the `ERR-13` completed-callee-finality witness. These span the parse/type-contract surface and the runtime execution-and-lowering surface — implementation areas that are written and verified differently and cannot be turned red→green in one coherent increment.

The `conventions.md` vertical-slice rule states each leaf should be "the smallest feature that can ship and be tested independently." The V14 slice already split off `V14b`–`V14f` (parallel batch, off-surface routing, host denial, depth ceiling, swallowing handler), and the sibling live-surface leaves `V13c` and `V15a` each carry only their core feature plus one `cka-47` facet and one `ERR-13` witness. `V14a` additionally carries the five-mechanic runtime envelope-lowering block on top of that baseline, putting it materially above its siblings in distinct verification surface.

Peeling the runtime `execute()` envelope-lowering surface (with the `cka-47` and `ERR-13` facets that ride on the live tool-call execution surface) into its own paired leaf brings `V14a` back in line with the V14 slice and the `V13c`/`V15a` pattern, while leaving the parse-time/type-contract surface in `V14a`.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

If `V14a` ships unsplit, the implementer must turn red→green across parse-time argument validation, the closed enum, two return-type lowerings, the `.loom`-callable failure surface, five runtime envelope-lowering mechanics, a cancellation checkpoint, and the `ERR-13` finality witness in one commit — a broad increment spanning the parse/type-contract and runtime-execution surfaces that is hard to review in a single pass and is out of line with the smallest-shippable-leaf convention and with sibling leaves `V14b`–`V14f`, `V13c`, and `V15a`. No spec rule goes unimplemented and no leaf is unpickable, so the impact is verification ergonomics, not correctness.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Peel the runtime `execute()` envelope-lowering surface out of `V14a` into a new paired leaf (`<new>` / `<new>-T`). The new leaf owns the five accepted-path `execute()` envelope-lowering mechanics, and carries the `cka-47` tool-call-checkpoint facet and the `ERR-13` completed-callee-finality witness with it — those two facets ride on the live tool-call execution surface, the same way `V13c` and `V15a` co-locate their `cka-47` facet and `ERR-13` witness with the live-surface leaf that establishes the call. `V14a` retains the parse-time argument checks, the closed `CodeToolError` enum, the return-type rows, and the `.loom`-callable failure surface.

Concrete edits:

- Move the `execute()` envelope-lowering `Tests` bullet, its `Ships when` clause, and the `cka-47`-checkpoint and `ERR-13`-witness bullets from `V14a-tool-calls.md` / `V14a-T-tool-calls.md` into the new `<new>` / `<new>-T` files; leave the parse-code, enum, return-row, and `.loom`-failure bullets in place.
- In `coverage-matrix.md`, retarget the `V14a` facet of the `cka-47` row, the `cka-46` row's `execute()`-lowering reference, and the `ERR-13` row's `V14a` co-witness entry to the new leaf ID.
- Add the new leaf's link to the `### V14 — Tool calls` list in `plan.md`.
- Set the new leaf's `Deps` to the live-execution-surface inputs the moved bullets need (the `V8a` `Checkpoint` seam, `V14a` for the call grammar, `V4f` for the `ERR-13` deferral, plus the `cka-46`/lowering inputs already on `V14a`), and audit the `Deps` of `V14b`–`V14f` to point at whichever leaf owns the surface each consumes — most consume the live `<name>(args)` call grammar that stays in `V14a`, so those edges likely remain on `V14a`.

Constraints: the spec topics are read-only for this fix — the spec rules are unchanged, only the closing leaf for the runtime-execution subset moves. Use the placeholder `<new>` until the implementer allocates a real ID under the plan's `V14<letter>` scheme; do not invent a final ID. Honour the no-invented-ID policy and the leaf-ID scheme in `## Plan rules`.

Edge cases to watch: the return-type rows and the `.loom`-callable failure surface describe the *lowered* accepted/failure result and are coupled to execution; keep them with whichever leaf establishes the result-lowering surface so the boundary stays coherent, and keep the live `<name>(args)` call grammar identifiable in `V14a` so the `V14b`–`V14f` dependency edges stay valid.

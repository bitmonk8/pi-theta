## Finding

The **Closure** paragraph in `placeholder-rendering-a.md` declares that introducing, retiring, or recategorising a placeholder "is a spec-versioned breaking change governed by **GOV-7** and **GOV-8** in [`spec.md` — Governance](../../spec.md)". That link resolves to `docs/spec.md`, which is a nine-line table-of-contents stub: it carries only an `<a id="overview">` anchor and three forward links to topic pages, with no Governance section and no GOV-7/GOV-8 content of any kind. The link also has no `#` fragment, so there is no anchor for GitHub's slug fallback to land on — following it drops the reader on a three-link index page.

GOV-7 (*mutation procedures*) and GOV-8 (*REQ-ID lifecycle*) are defined on `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md`, each behind an explicit anchor (`<a id="gov-7"></a>` and `<a id="gov-8"></a>`). The closure citation is the load-bearing pointer from the placeholder-rendering surface to the rules that make its closure a versioned contract; a reader or audit following it cannot reach either rule. This also fails GOV-9's cross-link contract, which requires every cross-page reference to a REQ-ID to resolve as a `#prefix-tail` URL fragment pointing at the depended-upon anchor.

The bolded "GOV-7 / GOV-8 governance posture" mention earlier in the same page's intro paragraph is unlinked prose and is not itself defective; the single broken target is the `[`spec.md` — Governance](../../spec.md)` link in the Closure paragraph.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single introducing commit (refactor-induced)
**Introducing commit:** `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04) — *docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set*
**History:** The closure cross-reference was authored at `61ea1a5` (2026-05-06, *pi-loom spec: resolve "Diagnostic message placeholders have no defined rendering"*) inside the then-monolithic spec, as `[`spec.md` — Governance](../spec.md)` on what was `spec_topics/diagnostics.md`. At that time the link was correct: `git show 61ea1a5:spec.md` is a 177-line document whose Governance appendix defined `GOV-7 (mutation procedures)` and `GOV-8 (REQ-ID lifecycle)` inline, and `../spec.md` resolved to it. The defect entered at the `f5e89f4` size-cap split, which in one commit (a) extracted the Governance appendix out of `spec.md` into the `spec_topics/governance/` subtree — GOV-7/GOV-8 landing in the newly created `req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` — and (b) carved the placeholder-rendering closure paragraph into the new `placeholder-rendering-a.md`, mechanically rewriting its link to `../../spec.md` for the deeper path. `git show ab2b669:docs/spec.md` (the prior state) still contained the GOV-7/GOV-8 definitions, while `git show f5e89f4:docs/spec.md` shows zero governance lines remaining. The link target's content moved away in the very commit that carried the link forward, but the citation was adjusted only for relative depth, not repointed to the new owner page — leaving it aimed at a `spec.md` that had become a stub.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace the single `[`spec.md` — Governance](../../spec.md)` target in the Closure paragraph with two explicit ID-anchored links to the rules' owner page, resolving as GOV-9 `#prefix-tail` fragments:

- `[GOV-7](../governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md#gov-7)`
- `[GOV-8](../governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md#gov-8)`

The relative path is `../governance/…` — `placeholder-rendering-a.md` sits in `spec_topics/diagnostics/`, so one `../` reaches `spec_topics/` and then `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md`. Render the two as separate links (the prose already names "GOV-7 and GOV-8" as distinct rules) rather than a single combined link, so each REQ-ID citation resolves to its own anchor. Leave the unlinked intro-paragraph mention as plain prose; only the Closure-paragraph link is changed.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader or reviewer who follows the citation to learn the versioning rules governing the placeholder surface lands on a contentless TOC stub and cannot reach GOV-7 or GOV-8. The placeholder-rendering rules themselves remain fully specified and implementable, so no two implementers diverge on rendering behaviour; the damage is a navigation/traceability dead end at the point where the surface's breaking-change discipline is asserted.

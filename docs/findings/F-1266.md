## Problem

`CTRL-1` on `control-flow.md` is a single REQ-ID whose paragraph asserts three independently observable obligations on the `for ... in` iterand: once-evaluation at loop entry, once-commit on the empty-array path, and mutation non-interference under a mid-body `let mut` reassignment. A red-first test can falsify any one of the three while the other two hold. Because all three share one anchor, the coverage-matrix `| CTRL-1 | V3c |` row and the `V3c` / `V3c-T` *Tests* bullet are the finest grain the closing gate can cite, so closing any one obligation closes `CTRL-1` as a whole and silently certifies the unexercised facets.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-in-single-commit
**Introducing commits:** `cff564e` ("pi-loom spec: resolve \"for in iterand evaluation cardinality\"", 2026-06-05)
**History:** Before `cff564e` the `**`for` ... `in`**` paragraph on `control-flow.md` carried no cardinality obligation at all. `cff564e` resolved spec-review finding T15 by inserting a single new paragraph anchored `<a id="ctrl-1"></a> **CTRL-1.**` that, in one sentence-plus-rider, asserted all three facets (once-evaluation, empty-array once-commit, mutation non-interference). The bundling was baked in at the moment of coinage — the prior text did not state any of the three obligations, and no subsequent edit touched the `CTRL-1` paragraph. `git log -S 'evaluated exactly once, at loop entry'` returns only `cff564e` (the introducing commit) and `330eb0a` / the current review file (back-references in review prose). The defect is a single-commit authoring miss: the T15 fix authored three observable obligations into one anchor instead of coining one anchor per obligation.

## Solution approach

Apply a GOV-8 *Split* to `CTRL-1`: retire its anchor on `control-flow.md` and coin one fresh `CTRL`-prefix REQ-ID per obligation (once-evaluation at entry, once-commit on the empty-array path, mutation non-interference), each paragraph stating exactly one obligation. Replace the `| CTRL-1 | V3c |` coverage-matrix row to cite the new IDs, and split the single `CTRL-1` *Tests* bullet on `V3c-control-flow.md` and `V3c-T-control-flow.md` into one bullet per new ID. The mutation-non-interference paragraph names its referent (the iterand binding) explicitly, co-resolving the sibling `let mut`-referent finding.

## Solution constraints

- Retired `CTRL-1`'s ID and numbering position MUST NOT be reused (GOV-8 *Deletion*); allocate the new IDs as the next free numerics under the registered `CTRL` prefix per GOV-3.
- The `control-flow.md` split, the `coverage-matrix.md` `CTRL-1` row, and the `V3c` / `V3c-T` *Tests* bullets MUST update in lock-step (GOV-30) so no surface retains a dangling `CTRL-1` reference.

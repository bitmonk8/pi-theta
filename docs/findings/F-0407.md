## Problem

`V17a` — Cancellation core carries two independently-verifiable mechanisms in one leaf: the `loomAbort` forwarding contract (three-entry-point forwarding, abort-reason propagation with the one-shot guard, downward-only propagation, the `CNCL-1..6` discard rules, and the swallowing-handler substrate-suppression assertion) and the checkpoint-granularity surface (the fixed checkpoint set firing before each of the five sites with its negative arm, plus the loop-iteration macrotask-yield property). These split cleanly along the `V8a` `Checkpoint` seam boundary: the granularity surface is observable through the seam alone, while the forwarding contract is assertable without driving the five-site checkpoint set. The result is a roughly eleven-assertion **Ships when** gate that the paired `V17a-T` tests task must red-author all at once, against `conventions.md` §3's "smallest feature that can ship and be tested independently."

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception (amplified by later granularity-hardening commits)
**Introducing commits:** `c6a664e` (`pi-loom plan: build/update plan for spec.md + review`, 2026-06-10) established the composite; `319b277` (`resolve "Cancellation checkpoint granularity set unverified"`, 2026-06-10) and `dc56e9a` (`resolve "V17a Ships-when gate observes only a subset of cancellation obligations"`, 2026-06-10) raised the property count to its current level.
**History:** `git log --follow` on `docs/plan_topics/V17a-cancellation-core.md` shows the file was created in `c6a664e` already bundling the `loomAbort` forwarding contract, the fixed checkpoint set, and the discard rules in its **Adds** field. At creation the **Tests** listed only `CNCL-1..3` plus a single combined forwarding bullet (~6 assertions). `git log -S "five sites"` / `git log -S "macrotask"` confirm the explicit five-site checkpoint-granularity bullet and the loop-iteration macrotask-yield bullet were added in `319b277`, and the **Ships when** gate was widened to enumerate them in `dc56e9a`. The two-mechanism composite is therefore original to the leaf; the ~11-assertion volume that surfaces the atomicity defect is the cumulative result of those two later verification-hardening commits.

## Solution approach

Split `V17a` along the `V8a` `Checkpoint` seam. Keep the `loomAbort` forwarding contract — `CNCL-1..6`, three-entry-point forwarding, abort-reason propagation, downward-only propagation, late-settlement discard rules, and the swallowing-handler substrate-suppression assertion — in `V17a`, and extract the checkpoint-granularity surface (the five-site fixed checkpoint set, its best-effort negative arm, and the loop-iteration macrotask-yield property) into a new vertical leaf with its paired `-T` tests task, `Deps: V17a`. Wire the extracted leaf into the V17 slice list in `docs/plan.md`, and update `H5b`'s `V17a`–`V17b` Deps coverage range to admit the inserted leaf.

## Solution constraints

- Allocate the extracted leaf's ID under the plan's `V<N><letter>` scheme (no-invented-ids); do not mint an arbitrary identifier.
- Keep `coverage-matrix.md` and `conventions.md` consistent with the split: `CNCL-1..6` and the swallowing-handler row stay mapped to `V17a`.

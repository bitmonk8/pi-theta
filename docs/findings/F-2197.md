## Problem

`docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/provider-error-mapping.md` defines two surfaces on opposite sides of the request/response divide: a response-path provider error-response classification (`#provider-error-mapping`, `#transport-error-retryable`, `#stop-reason-classification`) and a request-path provider seed-field mapping (`#provider-seed-field-mapping`). The two share an organizing principle (both are per-provider tables version-coupled to `@earendil-works/pi-ai`) but carry no normative interdependency, and the seed-field mapping is the request-side companion to a binder requirement whose seed-value derivation lives in the binder cluster. The page presents the two sections back-to-back with no seam, scope note, or non-goals statement, so a reader cannot tell the co-location is intentional rather than incidental.

**Verdict:** present-since-inception (of the file)
**Introducing commits:** `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04, "docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set")
**History:** The corpus is git-tracked. `git log --follow` on `provider-error-mapping.md` shows the file was created by the size-cap split `f5e89f4`, which carved 30 child pages out of the monolithic `pi-integration-contract.md`. `git show f5e89f4^:docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` confirms the `provider-seed-field-mapping` section (`<a id="provider-seed-field-mapping">`) and the provider error-mapping content already co-existed in the pre-split monolith; the split moved both into `provider-error-mapping.md` together rather than separating the request-path table. The file has therefore bundled both surfaces since the moment it existed. The follow-up `6932599` (2026-06-05, "provider-error-mapping.md H1 names only the first third of its content") only widened the H1 to `Provider error and seed-field mapping` â€” it acknowledged the bundling but did not add a scope boundary or non-goals. The general absence of non-goals sections is itself a corpus-wide present-since-inception trait, not specific to this file.

## Solution approach

Add a scope-and-non-goals statement after the H1 and before `#provider-error-mapping` that declares the page deliberately owns both the response-path error-response classification (`#provider-error-mapping`, `#transport-error-retryable`, `#stop-reason-classification`) and the request-path seed-field mapping (`#provider-seed-field-mapping`), and names what the page does not cover. Add a forward-link from `#provider-seed-field-mapping` to the binder seed-value derivation at `binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md#determinism`, identifying the seed-field table as the request-side companion to that requirement.

## Solution constraints

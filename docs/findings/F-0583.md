## Finding

The INV-4 depth-bound sentence in `docs/spec_topics/invocation.md` reads: "counting **both** direct `invoke(...)`, `.loom` callable calls through `tools:`, and cross-file `.warp` `fn` calls". The coordinator "both" grammatically promises a two-item list, but three coordinated items follow, producing a momentary mis-parse on first read.

The defect is confined to this one sentence. The paragraph immediately below already enumerates the same three countable-frame kinds in correct three-item form ("any direct `invoke(...)` call, any `.loom` callable call dispatched through a `tools:` entry, or any cross-file `.warp` `fn` call"), so the intended meaning is unambiguous from context and no downstream artefact is mis-specified.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/spec_topics/invocation.md`, INV-4 "Invocation depth bound" (the first paragraph), strike "both" from "counting both direct `invoke(...)`, …" — either delete the word outright ("counting direct `invoke(...)`, `.loom` callable calls through `tools:`, and cross-file `.warp` `fn` calls") or replace it with "all three of". The countable-frame enumeration in the second paragraph already uses the correct three-item form and needs no change.

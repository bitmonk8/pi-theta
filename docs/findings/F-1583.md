## Finding

The *System-prompt structure (normative)* subsection of `binder/binder-bypass-and-envelope.md` enumerates eight independent obligations as a plain numbered list (items 1–8). Each item carries its own RFC-2119 `MUST` and is a defining obligation site the page owns, yet none of the eight has a co-located REQ-ID or page-local anchor. The only stable identifier covering them is the section-heading anchor `#system-prompt-structure-normative`, which addresses all eight as a unit.

Because the items have no individual anchors, every inbound reference to a single item resolves it by ordinal position rather than by a stable id. `binder-model-and-context.md` does this in at least three places — "*System-prompt structure (normative)* item 6 below" (session-context render-rule cross-link), "item 5" (User-arguments-line convention), and the BNDR-7 reference renderings that cite "[System-prompt structure (normative) item 6]" by number — and the section's own preamble names "items 2, 3, 4, and 6" as the conditional-presence set. Positional citation is brittle: inserting or reordering an item silently invalidates every such reference, and four of the eight items (2 Description, 3 Argument-hint, 4 Parameters block, 6 Session-context block) each govern an independent conditional-presence rule that a conformance test, coverage-matrix facet, or failure report needs to cite individually.

The corpus already acknowledges this gap: the coverage matrix row `cka-45` annotates the section "(un-anchored; GOV-22 residue)", and GOV-22 (progressive coinage of un-anchored obligations) plus GOV-9 (cross-link form) jointly require these MUST-bearing sites to carry `#prefix-n` REQ-ID anchors so cross-links resolve to a specific obligation. The same page already follows the right pattern for its sibling reference-rendering block: BNDR-7 publishes an umbrella REQ-ID with per-rendering sub-letter anchors `bndr-7a`…`bndr-7j` (the GOV-23 sub-letter scheme) precisely so a test can cite one rendering without naming the others. Items 1–8 lack that treatment.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Coin a single `BNDR-N` umbrella REQ-ID for the *System-prompt structure (normative)* subsection (next free tail under the `binder/` → `BNDR` prefix per GOV-3, in the GOV-1 dual-form layout), and assign page-local sub-letter anchors `bndr-Na`…`bndr-Nh` to items 1–8 in list order — mirroring the BNDR-7 `bndr-7a`…`bndr-7j` precedent on the same page and the GOV-23 sub-letter scheme. The umbrella REQ-ID is the load-bearing identifier for citations of the section as a unit; the sub-letters let a single item be cited individually.

Then replace the positional paraphrase cross-references with anchor links: in `binder-model-and-context.md`, point "item 6 below", "item 5", and the BNDR-7 "item 6" reference at the corresponding `bndr-Nf` / `bndr-Ne` (etc.) anchors; in the section's own preamble, the "items 2, 3, 4, and 6" conditional-rule list may keep its prose ordinals but should anchor to the sub-letters where it cross-links.

Edge cases to watch: per GOV-23/GOV-22 anchor-stability, a future split or insertion appends a new sub-letter rather than renumbering existing siblings, and a retired sub-letter's anchor is not recycled — so the ordinal "item N" prose and the `bndr-N?` sub-letter must not be assumed to stay numerically aligned after any later edit. Do not reuse the existing `BNDR-1`/`BNDR-2`/`BNDR-3` ids (those name the envelope arms, a different obligation set).

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Implementers can still build a conforming system from the item content, which is unambiguous. But every inbound positional reference breaks if an item is inserted or reordered, and tests/coverage facets cannot cite the four independent conditional-presence rules by stable id — leaving GOV-9's `#prefix-n` cross-link contract unsatisfiable for this section and the gap as standing GOV-22 residue.

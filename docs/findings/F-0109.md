## Finding

The forwarding-listener throw-trap Tests bullet — byte-identical in both `V17b` and its paired `V17b-T` — instructs the implementer to drive the throw injection "through the entry-point harness so the throw is raised inside the real listener boundary rather than a bespoke double." "Bespoke double" is not a defined term anywhere in the plan corpus or the index; it appears only in these two leaf files. The phrase names the rejected alternative the test must avoid (a hand-rolled stand-in listener that bypasses the real wiring), but it leaves that alternative's boundary unspecified.

The positive obligation — inject through the entry-point harness so the throw is raised inside the real listener — is stated clearly, so the bullet is implementable. The defect is that the negative contrast ("rather than a bespoke double") adds no precise constraint: an implementer cannot tell from the text exactly what construction is being forbidden, only that "real" is preferred over "fake." The normative weight is "a custom stub listener wired outside the entry-point harness," which the prose should state directly instead of behind an undefined adjective.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In both `docs/plan_topics/V17b-forwarding-listener-throw-trap.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V17b-T-forwarding-listener-throw-trap.md`, in the `Tests.` "Forwarding-listener throw-trap" bullet, replace the fragment `rather than a bespoke double` with `rather than a stub listener wired directly to `loomAbort.abort()` outside the entry-point harness`. The two leaves carry this bullet byte-identical today; apply the same replacement to both so they stay identical. No other field changes; `cancellation.md` and `host-interfaces-core.md` are the read-only referents and are not touched.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The test bullet remains implementable because the positive instruction (inject through the entry-point harness, raise inside the real listener) is clear; the undefined contrast term only weakens reviewer confidence that two implementers will reject the same constructions. Left unfixed, the bullet carries jargon the rest of the corpus never defines, inviting a reader to over- or under-scope the forbidden stub.

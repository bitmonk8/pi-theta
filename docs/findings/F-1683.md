## Problem

PIC-22 (*Parallel subagent spawn initiation*) on `subagent.md` is a behavioural spawn-initiation MUST, but its body also carries a multi-sentence resource-ownership NFR (the run beginning "Beyond the absence of an admission cap and scheduler pinned above…") stating that the loom adds no per-class threshold or cross-sibling aggregation/throttling/storm-detection, and that runtime-value heap, OS file-descriptor/socket/child-process, and provider rate-limit/quota limits are owned by the host JavaScript runtime, the host OS, and the LLM provider respectively. This resource-ownership boundary is a system-boundaries NFR, not part of the spawn-initiation behaviour PIC-22 names. Bundling it into the behavioural MUST buries the NFR where an operator reasoning about resource limits is unlikely to look and inflates the PIC-22 requirement body with content that does not constrain the spawn-initiation behaviour.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `a389af0` (2026-05-13), `7a3b907` (2026-06-10)
**History:** The resource-ownership prose was authored at `a389af0` ("rewrite T20 as positive ownership-boundary statement"), which resolved the T20 finding about undisclaimed non-memory resource exhaustion. At that point it lived in the `no-invocation-cap` concurrency-disposition paragraph in the then-`implementation-notes.md`, attached to the no-cap/no-scheduler disposition — not yet a distinct anchored MUST. The embedding defect crystallised at `7a3b907` ("subagent parallel-initiation MUST has no closing leaf"), which carved the `<a id="pic-22">` anchor out of the combined concurrency paragraph: it split the spawn-initiation MUST into the standalone PIC-22 requirement but carried the resource-ownership sentences into the new PIC-22 body rather than leaving them on the `concurrent-invocation-isolation` / `no-invocation-cap` paragraph that owns the no-cap disposition. The subsequent `f5e89f4` 100KB-cap split only relocated the page; it did not introduce the bundling.

## Solution approach

Move the resource-ownership NFR run out of the PIC-22 requirement body into the `concurrent-invocation-isolation` / `no-invocation-cap` block immediately above, continuing that block's no-cap/no-scheduler disposition. Leave PIC-22 purely behavioural, repointing its "does not promise resource unboundedness" pointer to the relocated note.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: PIC-22's spawn-initiation behaviour, diagnostics, and predicates — the relocation is organisational and changes no normative content.
- Keep the `no-invocation-cap` anchor stable; inbound forward-links (e.g. the GOV-12 aggregator's inventory) resolve to it.

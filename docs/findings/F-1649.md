## Problem

The second bullet of *Stop-reason classification* in `provider-error-mapping.md` (anchor `#stop-reason-classification`) opens with "Any other non-terminal stop reason", but its own example — a content-filter terminator — is terminal by construction, and the parent paragraph has already framed the entire bullet list as classifying *terminators* (HTTP-200 responses ending on a stop reason outside `end_turn` / `stop` / `tool_use`). The "non-terminal" qualifier therefore contradicts the bullet's own framing. A reader can recover two divergent partitions: that "non-terminal" is a slip covering all non-`length` terminators, or that it carves out a domain-specific sub-class; the readings agree on the named examples but diverge for any future stop reason that is a clean turn boundary yet off the `{end_turn, stop, tool_use, length}` list — the exact residual class this bullet exists to handle.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit

**Introducing commits:** `ef4cfb8` — "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Stop-reason → QueryError variant mapping is undefined\"" (2026-06-07).

**History:** The *Stop-reason classification* paragraph and both of its bullets — including the offending "Any other non-terminal stop reason" wording — were added in a single commit (`ef4cfb8`) that resolved a prior finding about the stop-reason → `QueryError` mapping being undefined. `git log -L` over the section shows no subsequent edits to these lines; the only earlier touch (`f5e89f4`) is the file's initial split-out of the surrounding anchors and added no stop-reason text. The contradiction was therefore present on first emission of the rule — a wording slip the resolving commit introduced rather than a drift between later edits.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the second bullet's leading clause under `#stop-reason-classification` to state the residual class positively against the sibling `length` bullet — "Any stop reason other than `length`" — keeping the remainder of the sentence intact. This makes the partition grammatically explicit and aligns it with the paragraph's framing of all non-`end_turn`/`stop`/`tool_use` stop reasons as terminators.

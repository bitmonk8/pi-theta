## Finding

The second bullet of *Stop-reason classification* in `provider-error-mapping.md` reads "Any other non-terminal stop reason — including a content-filter terminator, or any stop reason the runtime does not otherwise recognise — maps to `TransportError` with `retryable: false`". The qualifier "non-terminal" is self-contradicting on its own example: a content-filter "terminator" is by construction terminal (it ends the response), and the parent paragraph has just established that the entire bullet list classifies *terminators* (HTTP-200 responses that end on a stop reason outside `end_turn` / `stop` / `tool_use`). The intended set is "every terminator other than `length`", not "non-terminal" anything.

A reader trying to recover the intent has two equally-plausible partitions: (a) the word "non-terminal" is a slip and the rule covers all non-`length` terminators (which the surrounding context implies), or (b) "non-terminal" is meant in a domain-specific sense ("not a normal turn-boundary terminator like `end_turn`/`stop`/`tool_use`"), in which case a content-filter stop reason is being labelled "non-terminal" relative to the *tool-loop's* notion of a clean boundary. The two readings produce identical behaviour for the named examples but diverge for any future stop reason that is itself a clean turn boundary yet not on the `{end_turn, stop, tool_use, length}` short list — exactly the open-ended residual class this bullet is meant to fix.

The defect is purely lexical; the surrounding rule (everything other than `length` ⇒ `TransportError{retryable:false}`, with the *Classifier input surface* note pinning the input) is already complete.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit

**Introducing commits:** `ef4cfb8` — "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Stop-reason → QueryError variant mapping is undefined\"" (2026-06-07).

**History:** The *Stop-reason classification* paragraph and both of its bullets — including the offending "Any other non-terminal stop reason" wording — were added in a single commit (`ef4cfb8`) that resolved a prior finding about the stop-reason → `QueryError` mapping being undefined. `git log -L` over the section shows no subsequent edits to these lines; the only earlier touch (`f5e89f4`) is the file's initial split-out of the surrounding anchors and added no stop-reason text. The contradiction was therefore present on first emission of the rule — a wording slip the resolving commit introduced rather than a drift between later edits.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace the bullet's leading clause "Any other non-terminal stop reason" with "Any stop reason other than `length`", keeping the rest of the sentence intact. The polished bullet reads:

> Any stop reason other than `length` — including a content-filter terminator, or any stop reason the runtime does not otherwise recognise — maps to `TransportError` with `retryable: false` (the terminator is a definite outcome of the request, not a transient condition — the same rationale the **`TransportError.retryable` population** rule above applies to an HTTP-200 body-envelope error).

This restates the residual class positively against the only stop reason the sibling bullet handles (`length`), so the partition is grammatically obvious and matches the paragraph's own framing of all non-`end_turn`/`stop`/`tool_use` stop reasons as *terminators*. No other text in the section needs to move.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- The `end_turn` / `stop` / `tool_use` set is *not* a third hidden bullet — those reasons never reach this classifier because the introducing paragraph explicitly excludes them as normal turn boundaries handled upstream. Do not synthesise a `TransportError` for them.
- The classifier consumes `AssistantMessage.stopReason` per the *Classifier input surface* note above; do not branch on HTTP status or `errorMessage` wording in this bullet — those branches belong to the overflow table and the *Provider error mapping* prose.
- `stopReason: "error"` is *not* covered here; it is a body-envelope error routed by `PIC-50`/`PIC-51` in `conversation-drive.md` and the *Provider error mapping* prose above. The polished bullet's "any stop reason other than `length`" is bounded by the introducing paragraph's "successful HTTP-200 provider response … other than `end_turn` / `stop` / `tool_use`" scope, which already excludes `"error"` because it rides the error envelope rather than a normal 200.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Two careful implementers can still arrive at the right behaviour by reading the surrounding paragraph, but they will write different fixture matrices: one will treat the bullet as "non-`length` terminators ⇒ TransportError" and cover content-filter explicitly; another will read "non-terminal" literally and assume a content-filter stop reason is *also* a terminator already handled elsewhere, leaving the content-filter case untested. The behaviour-divergence risk is low; the test-coverage-divergence risk is real.

## Problem

`H1a`'s final **Tests** bullet (the `tsc-toolchain-floor` literal-read assertion) and the matching **Adds** `typescript` provider clause both assert the declared `typescript` range "satisfies the TypeScript floor of `>=5.4.0`", but the verb "satisfies" is not operationalized into a concrete semver comparison. Three natural readings ŌĆö exact-string, lower-bound, and range-overlap ŌĆö diverge on real `package.json` ranges (e.g. `^5.4.0` passes lower-bound and overlap but fails exact-string; `>=5.0.0` passes overlap but provisions a sub-floor `tsc`). Two reasonable implementers would therefore encode tests with different pass/fail behaviour. The sibling literal-read assertions in the same leaf (`engines.node`, `pi.extensions`) use the unambiguous verb "equals", leaving only the `typescript` bullet with an implicit comparison operator.

The defect has been present since the assertion was first authored; no intervening commit narrowed or operationalized the predicate.

## Solution approach

Operationalize "satisfies" into an explicit lower-bound semver predicate in both the final **Tests** bullet and the matching **Adds** `typescript` clause of `docs/plan_topics/H1a-scaffold-and-toolchain.md`: the declared `typescript` range must admit the floor version `5.4.0` but reject the version just below it (`5.3.9`), so idiomatic ranges like `^5.4.0` pass while sub-floor ranges like `>=5.0.0` fail. State the **Tests** bullet's fail condition in the same terms (absent `typescript`, floor not admitted, or sub-floor admitted). Keep the **Adds** wording consistent with the operationalized predicate.

- Out of scope: the sibling `engines.node`, `pi.extensions`, and peer-dep `Tests` assertions in the same leaf.

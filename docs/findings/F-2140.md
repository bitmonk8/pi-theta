## Problem

The `session_shutdown` teardown contract relies on `chokidar` tolerating a second `.close()` on an already-closed watcher: `drain-state-contract.md`'s `#idempotent-handler-clause` all-three-throw corner case and its `#read-failure-fallback` (PIC-31) re-entry, plus acceptance branches (j) and (m) in `session-only-degraded-state.md`, all re-enter sub-step 4 ("Close watchers", `session-shutdown-semantics.md`) against already-closed handles and accept whatever the second `.close()` does. The spec names this "chokidar's acknowledged-unpinned double-close behaviour", and `implementation-notes.md` declares the dependency without a version. Unlike loom's TypeScript build-toolchain floor (`host-prerequisites.md#tsc-toolchain-floor`) — version-pinned and gated against silent regression by a build-time assertion and a per-bump re-confirmation obligation — this load-bearing chokidar behaviour carries no version floor, no behaviour-locking test, and no re-confirmation entry. A `chokidar` upgrade that changed double-close semantics, e.g. an asynchronous rejection escaping the synchronous per-step `try`/`catch`, would invalidate the re-entry acceptance with no signal.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** single introducing commit
**Introducing commit:** `52b9101` (2026-05-11, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"T24 — Fork-reason watcher closure leaves the extension in an unspecified, silently degraded state\"")
**History:** The corpus is git-tracked. `git log -S 'double-close' -- docs/` and `git log -G 'unpinned double-close'` trace the earliest spec-body occurrence (excluding spec-review store commits) to `52b9101`, which introduced the *idempotent* clause and the *all-three-throw corner case* into the then-monolithic `pi-integration-contract.md`. `git show 52b9101` confirms that commit coined both "chokidar's unpinned double-close behaviour" and "chokidar's acknowledged-unpinned double-close behaviour," establishing the load-bearing reliance and simultaneously labelling it unpinned, with no accompanying pin, behaviour-locking test, or re-confirmation gate. Later commits (`2775382` adding throwing-`readDrainState` acceptance criteria; `f5e89f4` the size-cap split into `drain-state-contract.md` / `session-only-degraded-state.md`) propagated the same phrasing but did not introduce the gap. The reliance has been unguarded since `52b9101`.

## Solution approach

Add a normative behaviour-locking fixture obligation to the teardown-contract test surface that double-closes a real `chokidar` watcher through sub-step 4's close path and asserts the accepted outcome, covering both the synchronous-throw and asynchronous-rejection escape paths. Reference it from the `#read-failure-fallback` (PIC-31) and all-three-throw arms in `drain-state-contract.md` and from branches (j)/(m) in `session-only-degraded-state.md`. Add a forward-link from the `chokidar` dependency clause at `implementation-notes.md#loom-package-implementation-dependencies-loom-1-0`, and a per-bump re-confirmation entry on the version bump checklist (`version-bump-intro.md#pi-version-bump-procedure`), mirroring the `#tsc-toolchain-floor` guard.

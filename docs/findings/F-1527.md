## Problem

Step 2(b)'s branch tie-break reserves branch (3) (declared exemption) for cases where the offending reference resolves to exactly one callsite, expressed as a single `<path>:<line>:<symbol>` triple "mechanically checkable on the audited source tree at bump time". This is a normative branch-eligibility gate — a contributor MUST pick the branch whose criterion the violation satisfies and a reviewer MUST confirm the fit on the bump-commit diff — yet `<symbol>`, the component that determines what counts as "one" callsite, is never defined at the gate. The triple notation is emitted inline with no cross-reference to the `symbol` field defined in the *Per-family record-shape table* in `audit-failures.md`. Two plausible readings diverge: an SDK surface aliased to two local names collapses to one symbol under the SDK-member reading but counts as two under the local-binding reading, so two reasonable contributors can reach different exempt-vs-promote selections for the same violation.

## Solution approach

At the branch (3) "single callsite" tie-break in `version-bump-step2b.md`, bind `<symbol>` to the `symbol` field defined in the *Per-family record-shape table* in `audit-failures.md` via cross-reference, and state that the callsite-counting unit is the resolved SDK surface rather than the local binding name. Make explicit the consequence that an SDK surface reached through two distinct local aliases counts as the same `<symbol>` (so aliasing alone does not multiply callsites), while two import lines of the same surface remain two `<path>:<line>` pairs and thus two callsites regardless of the `<symbol>` reading.

## Solution constraints

- Cross-reference the *Per-family record-shape table* `symbol` row in `audit-failures.md`; do not re-define `symbol` independently in `version-bump-step2b.md`.

## Problem

`V4f` delegates the live-surface re-assertion of its `ERR-13`
completed-callee-finality vector to three production-surface carriers —
`V13c`, `V14a`, and `V15a` — and each impl leaf carries the witness as a
Tests bullet. But the witness is wired into neither half of the TDD gate:
the paired test-first leaves `V13c-T`, `V14a-T`, and `V15a-T` do not carry
the `ERR-13` bullet, so the per-phase red-first obligation never covers it,
and none of the three impl leaves' **Ships when** conditions names the
finality assertion. A reviewer can observe every Ships-when condition green
and tag `V13c`/`V14a`/`V15a` complete with the live-surface finality
assertion never written.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 7bd65a7 — pi-loom plan: resolve "V4c/V4f defer ERR-8 to ERR-13 live-surface confirmation to downstream owners that never re-assert it" (2026-06-18, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** `git log -S "delegated live-carrier witness"` over the three impl leaves reports 7bd65a7 as the first and only commit to introduce the term. That commit added the `ERR-13` live-carrier witness bullet to the Tests blocks of `V13c`, `V14a`, and `V15a` and rewrote `V4f` to delegate the live-surface re-assertion to those three production surfaces, but it did not add the corresponding bullet to the paired `-T` leaves nor name the assertion in any impl-leaf Ships-when. The witness thus entered the corpus red-first-unbacked and ungated in the same edit that created it; the term is absent from `V13c-T` / `V14a-T` / `V15a-T` at every revision.

## Solution approach

Add the `ERR-13` delegated live-carrier witness bullet to the **Tests**
field of `V13c-T`, `V14a-T`, and `V15a-T`, matching the surface-specific
wording already present in each paired impl leaf (query-tool-loop callee for
V13c, code-side tool call for V14a, `invoke` child for V15a). Add a clause
naming the `ERR-13` completed-callee-finality assertion to the **Ships
when** field of `V13c`, `V14a`, and `V15a` so the witness becomes an
observable gate condition rather than an ungated Tests-block entry. The `-T`
leaves' existing "fail red for the intended reason" Ships-when already
covers the new bullet.

## Finding

The `**REQ-ID discipline.**` bold-term bullet in `conventions.md` (under "## Cross-cutting rules (every leaf)", line 65) is the single authoritative home for five distinct plan-governance surfaces:

1. the numbered-`PREFIX-N`-REQ-ID ↔ coverage-matrix mapping gate (and the per-prefix numbering-hole check),
2. the diagnostic-registry-code ↔ asserting-test parity gate,
3. the GOV-22 un-anchored normative-MUST closing-gate surface (the `H5e` arm),
4. the GOV-16 stable-inline-label family closure semantics (the two closure shapes), and
5. the *Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance* obligation (the `H5d` arm and the `H5b.Deps` trigger).

The bullet's lead term, however, signals only "REQ-ID." A contributor scanning the cross-cutting-rules list for the diagnostic-code parity rule, the un-anchored-MUST rule, the inline-label-family closure rule, or the transitive-completeness rule gets no signal from the rule term that those obligations live inside the "REQ-ID discipline" bullet. The body is correct and complete; only the term that indexes it is too narrow.

This is the load-bearing index term for these rules across the plan: `H5a`, `H5b`, `H5d`, `H5e`, `H5f`, and `H6a` all cite "*REQ-ID discipline*" by name in their **Convention.** fields, and `coverage-matrix.md` and `plan.md` reference it as well. The term therefore functions as a discoverability anchor, and an anchor that names one of five surfaces undersells what it points at.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A contributor scanning the cross-cutting-rules list for the diagnostic-code parity, un-anchored-MUST, inline-label-closure, or transitive-completeness rule sees only a bullet labelled "REQ-ID discipline" and may not realise those obligations live there — risking authoring a leaf that overlooks one of those closing-gate obligations or restating the rule elsewhere. No implementer is blocked and no gate misfires: the rules are stated correctly and the gates (`H5a`/`H5d`/`H5e`/`H5f`) are wired regardless of the term; only discoverability degrades.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/conventions.md` (line 65), replace the `**REQ-ID discipline.**` bold lead term with one that names the surfaces the bullet governs — e.g. `**Closing-gate discipline (REQ-IDs, diagnostic codes, and un-anchored MUSTs).**` — leaving the bullet body unchanged.

Propagate the new term to every site that cites it by name so no reference dangles:

- the in-file `§REQ-ID discipline` cross-references within `conventions.md` itself (the *Tests* and *Deps* leaf-format bullets, the *Transitive-completeness* sub-clause's self-reference, and release-time residue-inspection items 5 and 7);
- the **Convention.** field of `H5a`, `H5b`, `H5d`, `H5e`, `H5f`, and `H6a`, and the `(*REQ-ID discipline* — …)` Tests-bullet annotations inside those leaves;
- the `*REQ-ID discipline*` citations in `coverage-matrix.md` (intro and *Code-keyed obligation areas* enumeration) and the `plan.md` "How to use this plan" §2 reference.

The new term is content; choose it to read naturally where it is cited inline (the `(*…* — …)` annotation form recurs across the H5* leaves). The bullet body, the gate wiring, and every leaf's Deps/Tests/Ships-when stay as they are.

## Finding

The *Doc updates* cross-cutting rule in `conventions.md` tells a contributor where to
record what they learn while building a leaf:

> The plan itself is updated only when the **plan** changes; non-plan discoveries go
> to `notes.md`.

The routing test ("when the plan changes") is defined in terms of itself: a "plan
change" is whatever causes a plan-file edit, and a plan-file edit is whatever a plan
change calls for. The rule supplies no observable criterion that distinguishes a
discovery requiring a plan-file edit from one that belongs in `notes.md`.

Concrete cases sit squarely in the gap: a contributor who finds that a leaf needs an
additional **Deps** entry, or that a leaf's **Ships when** gate is mis-stated, or that
a coverage-matrix mapping is wrong, cannot tell from the rule whether to edit the plan
files (the index plus the affected `plan_topics/` leaf) or to log the observation in
`notes.md`. Both routings are defensible under the circular phrasing. The adjacent
*Spec drift* rule already carves out the "spec is wrong" case and routes it to a
dedicated spec-fix commit, so *Doc updates* is left governing exactly the plan-vs-notes
boundary it never draws.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A contributor who, mid-leaf, discovers a needed structural correction (an extra
**Deps** edge, a mis-stated **Ships when**, a wrong coverage-matrix mapping) gets no
guidance on whether to amend the plan files or merely note it: under one reading the
correction is silently logged in `notes.md` and never applied to the plan, leaving the
plan stale; under the other, trivial implementation observations churn the plan files.
Implementers can still produce a working leaf, so the defect degrades plan-maintenance
discipline rather than blocking any leaf.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `conventions.md`, in the *Doc updates* bullet, replace the circular clause

> The plan itself is updated only when the **plan** changes; non-plan discoveries go to `notes.md`.

with a clause that enumerates the structural triggers that constitute a plan change,
for example:

> The plan files — the plan index and any `plan_topics/` leaf page — are updated only
> when the plan's *structure* changes: adding, modifying, splitting, merging, removing,
> or re-sequencing a leaf; correcting a leaf's **Deps** or **Ships when**; or adjusting
> a [`coverage-matrix.md`](./coverage-matrix.md) mapping. An implementation-time
> observation that needs no such structural change goes to `notes.md`.

Keep the existing *Spec drift* routing intact — a discovery that the spec is wrong
still stops and fixes the spec first; the *Doc updates* clause governs only the
plan-files-vs-`notes.md` boundary. The enumeration above is illustrative of the
intended distinction; the binding requirement is that the rule state an observable
criterion for "plan change" (structural plan edits) rather than restating the term.

## Finding

The version-bump procedure has a single canonical noun for "a build-time test that fails (`npm test` / `npm run typecheck`) until the corresponding bump step is completed": **mechanical gate**. `version-bump-intro.md` defines it directly ("the build-time tests cited at each step are the mechanical gates that fail loudly until the corresponding step is completed"), `version-bump-step2.md` uses it throughout, and within `version-bump-triggers.md` itself step 6 ("the build-time `Api`-coverage assertion is the mechanical gate that fails until this step has been completed") and step 7 ("the SDK surface-inventory assertion … is the mechanical gate that fails until this step has been completed") both conform.

Step 5's inbound-reference audit — a structurally identical build-time gate that reddens `npm test` on a result-delta against the documented enumeration — does not use the canonical term. It is introduced as "a **build-time audit gate** (pinned later in this step)" and later restated as "the implementation MUST implement a **build-time audit pass** … so that a result-delta … fails `npm test` red". A reader cross-referencing the "mechanical gate" guarantees that the rest of the procedure (and the intro's definition) attach to every step's gate cannot tell, from the label alone, that step 5's audit carries the same fail-loud-until-complete contract; the two non-canonical labels read as if they might name a different mechanism.

(The original finding also listed "build-time gate" as a third drifting label. On inspection the two "build-time gate" occurrences in step 5 do not name the step-5 audit: one is a cross-reference to the *Patch-skew degradation contract*'s gate, and the other — "no build-time gate fires when the §7 `<reason>` row is left unedited" — is the intro's own generic phrasing for the *absence* of a gate. Those are correct as written; the genuine drift is the step-5 audit's own label.)

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Standardise the step-5 inbound-reference audit on the canonical noun **mechanical gate**. On its first mention in step 5, introduce it as "a mechanical gate (a build-time audit pass over `src/`, `test/`, and `docs/`)" so the descriptive "audit pass" qualifier is preserved while the canonical term is established; refer to it as "the mechanical gate" thereafter, matching step 6 and step 7. Replace the bare "build-time audit gate" label accordingly. Leave the two "build-time gate" occurrences that reference the Patch-skew degradation contract's gate and the generic "no build-time gate fires" §7 phrasing unchanged — they name a different gate and the absence of a gate, respectively, and the latter matches `version-bump-intro.md`'s own wording.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The step-5 gate's behaviour is fully specified regardless of the label (it explicitly "fails `npm test` red rather than relying on contributor-side discipline"), so no implementer builds the wrong thing. The cost is reader friction: a reviewer auditing which steps have a "mechanical gate" may not recognise the step-5 audit as one of them, or may suspect a distinct mechanism.

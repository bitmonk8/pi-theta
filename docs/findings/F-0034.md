## Problem

In `docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md`, the `## Governance REQ-IDs (GOV-*) — corpus governance, not runtime obligations` section opens by stating that `GOV-*` IDs are corpus invariants that "map to no runtime leaf," then pivots into a dense block of *runtime* and *ceiling* closure mappings: the `CEIL` prefix derivation, the `CIO-2 … CIO-4`/`CIO-6 → V16a` closures, the `CIO-1`/`CIO-5` co-witness rationale, `HC3-a … HC3-e → V11f`, and the `NOCEIL-1 … NOCEIL-4` distributed-seam enumeration. That content is runtime-closure metadata, so the heading's "not runtime obligations" disclaimer is false for the bulk of the paragraph. A maintainer reading the `CIO` rows in the `## Numbered REQ-IDs (runtime obligations)` table has no pointer to the precedence/co-witness rationale, and the `HC3`/`NOCEIL`/`CEIL` notes have no home near the runtime obligations they explain.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction (seed present since inception, aggravated by later coverage fixes)
**Introducing commits:**
- `c6a664e` ("pi-loom plan: build/update plan for spec.md + review") — established the `## Governance REQ-IDs (GOV-*) — corpus governance, not runtime obligations` heading and embedded the seed runtime-closure clause ("its cross-ceiling content is carried by the `CIO` IDs and the `HC3`/`NOCEIL` inline labels, all closed by `V16a` and `V11f`") inside the corpus-governance paragraph.
- `2565ddd` ("pi-loom plan: resolve \"V16a NOCEIL Adds. claim with no backing Tests\"") — expanded the seed clause into the full `CIO-1 … CIO-6`/`HC3-a … HC3-e`/`NOCEIL-1 … NOCEIL-4` distributed-seam closure enumeration, deepening the pivot.
- `8ef7055` ("pi-loom plan: resolve \"V16a over-claims all CIO bullets provable at stateless seam\"") — split the `CIO` rows in the numbered table and added the `CIO-1`/`CIO-5` decision-vs-temporal and seam-local-vs-cross-site co-witness rationale into the same governance paragraph.

**History:** The placement contradiction was latent at inception (`c6a664e`) as a one-clause runtime-closure note under the corpus-governance heading. Two later commits resolving unrelated `V16a` coverage findings (`2565ddd`, then `8ef7055`) accreted the bulk of the runtime/ceiling closure prose into that same paragraph rather than siting it near the numbered `CIO` rows, which is what made the pivot conspicuous. `git log -S`/`git show` over the cited file confirm the heading text is unchanged since `c6a664e` while the runtime-closure body grew across those two fixes.

## Solution approach

In `docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md`, move the runtime/ceiling closure prose — the `CEIL` prefix derivation, the `CIO-2 … CIO-4`/`CIO-6 → V16a` closures, the `CIO-1`/`CIO-5` co-witness rationale, `HC3-a … HC3-e → V11f`, and the `NOCEIL-1 … NOCEIL-4` enumeration with its `hard-ceilings.md` Audit cross-reference — out of the `## Governance REQ-IDs (GOV-*)` section to a location adjacent to the `## Numbered REQ-IDs (runtime obligations)` table, so a reader of the `CIO` rows can reach it. Leave only the genuine corpus-governance text (the `GOV-*` enumeration, the "arm-(b) corpus invariants … map to no runtime leaf" statement, and the `H5a` operationalisation sentence) under the governance heading, making its disclaimer true.

## Solution constraints

- Preserve the relocated closure mappings verbatim: moving the prose must not alter any `CIO`/`HC3`/`NOCEIL`/`CEIL` → leaf closure.

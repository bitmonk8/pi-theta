## Finding

The single paragraph anchored by `<a id="strict-capability-requirement">` / `<a id="binder-model-parse-rule">` in `docs/spec_topics/binder/binder-model-and-context.md` (the *Binder model* section) is one un-numbered block of prose that pins at least five separately-conformance-testable obligations:

1. **Two-step resolution to a model** — the resolved free-form binder-model string is matched at the load-time pass by loom's own exact-match resolution over `ctx.modelRegistry.getAvailable()`.
2. **Reference-form matching** — a bare `modelId` matches against `Model<Api>.id`; a `provider/modelId` reference matches a model whose `Model<Api>.provider` (short provider-id form) and `Model<Api>.id` both equal the reference's components.
3. **Ambiguity / no-match → no model** — a bare `modelId` matching models under more than one provider is ambiguous and resolves to no model (not pick-first); any no-match or ambiguous case fails the load with `loom/load/binder-model-unresolved`.
4. **Three-valued `strictCapable` probe** — `true` admits with no diagnostic, `false` emits `loom/load/binder-model-not-strict-capable` (E) and refuses registration, `undefined` emits `loom/load/binder-model-strict-capability-unknown` (W) and registers; plus the MUST to short-circuit the probe when resolution yields no model.
5. **Bypass exemption** — bypass-eligible looms (no-params and single-string bypass) skip both checks because they never call the binder.

None of these carries a per-obligation REQ-ID or page-local sub-anchor. A conformance test, a coverage-matrix cell, or a failure report that exercises one obligation cannot cite a stable identifier for it — it can only point at the whole `#binder-model-parse-rule` anchor, which equally names the other four. GOV-8 ID-lifecycle tracking therefore tracks the block as one unit and cannot record the individual obligations' status. The defect is conspicuous on this very page: the neighbouring rules BNDR-8, BNDR-9, BNDR-10, and BNDR-11 each carry their own REQ-ID, and BNDR-7's reference renderings were given page-local sub-letter anchors (`bndr-7a`…`bndr-7j`) for exactly the cited reason — "so a test, coverage-matrix leaf, or failure report can cite a single rendering without naming the others" — while the binder-model resolution obligations were left as un-numbered prose. Seven sites across the corpus already deep-link `#binder-model-parse-rule` as a unit (code-registry-load, package-and-settings, frontmatter-fields-a, host-interfaces-core, host-prerequisites, binder-inference), and the plan's `V11a` test leaf enumerates obligations 1–4 by re-paraphrasing the prose rather than citing IDs.

Inspecting that commit's diff, the paragraph already bundled all the obligations present today — two-step resolution, bare-vs-`provider/modelId` matching, the ambiguity→no-model rule, the three-valued `strictCapable` probe with its short-circuit, and the bypass exemption — under the single `binder-model-parse-rule` anchor with no per-obligation REQ-ID; the missing-identifiers defect was therefore present in full at the moment the block was authored. ## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Decompose the `binder-model-parse-rule` paragraph into the five obligations above and give each a REQ-ID, continuing the page's existing `BNDR-N` sequence (the current maximum is BNDR-11, so the new rules take BNDR-12…BNDR-16). Suggested mapping:

- **BNDR-12** — two-step load-time resolution of the free-form binder-model string via loom's own exact-match resolution over `ctx.modelRegistry.getAvailable()`.
- **BNDR-13** — reference-form matching (bare `modelId` → `Model<Api>.id`; `provider/modelId` → short-provider-id `Model<Api>.provider` + `Model<Api>.id`).
- **BNDR-14** — ambiguity / no-match resolves to no model (not pick-first) and fails the load with `loom/load/binder-model-unresolved`.
- **BNDR-15** — the three-valued `strictCapable` probe, with page-local sub-anchors for the three outcome branches (`bndr-15a` `true`/admit, `bndr-15b` `false`/`binder-model-not-strict-capable` E, `bndr-15c` `undefined`/`binder-model-strict-capability-unknown` W) plus the no-model short-circuit MUST. Use the same page-local sub-letter scheme BNDR-7 already uses (and that GOV-23 describes) for the branch-level identifiers, since they are sub-cases of one probe rule rather than independent rules.
- **BNDR-16** — the bypass exemption (bypass-eligible looms skip both checks).

Retain the existing `binder-model-parse-rule` umbrella anchor on the paragraph for the seven inbound links that cite the rule as a unit (exactly as BNDR-7 keeps its umbrella `bndr-7` anchor alongside the sub-letters), so those citations keep resolving; repoint only the citations that target a single obligation to the most-specific new ID. Fold the probe-field-name-constant / rename-enforcement clause into BNDR-15's body rather than giving it a sixth ID — it is a property of the probe, not an independent obligation. Once the IDs land, tighten the `V11a` / `V11a-T` acceptance bullets to cite them instead of re-paraphrasing the prose.

Edge cases for the implementer:

- The page already distinguishes REQ-IDs (independent rules: BNDR-8/9/10/11) from page-local sub-letter anchors (sub-obligations of one rule: BNDR-7a…); keep that two-tier discipline — independent obligations get BNDR-N, the probe's three branches get sub-letters under BNDR-15.
- Do not renumber or remove the existing BNDR IDs or the BNDR-7 sub-letters; append BNDR-12…BNDR-16 after BNDR-11.
- Register the new BNDR IDs in the governance REQ-ID prefix table so GOV-8 lifecycle tracking and the ID-uniqueness gate pick them up.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Behaviour is fully pinned in prose, so two implementers will not diverge on what to build; the gap is in traceability. Per-obligation test results, coverage-matrix cells, and failure reports cannot cite a stable identifier for any one of the five obligations, and GOV-8 lifecycle tracking sees them as a single unit — so a regression in (say) the ambiguity rule or one `strictCapable` branch cannot be tied to an ID the way every neighbouring BNDR rule's failures can.

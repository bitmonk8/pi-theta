## Problem

The paragraph anchored by `binder-model-parse-rule` in the *Binder model* section bundles five independently-conformance-testable obligations — two-step load-time model resolution, reference-form matching, ambiguity/no-match → no model, the three-valued `strictCapable` probe, and the bypass exemption — under one anchor that carries no per-obligation REQ-ID. A conformance test, coverage-matrix cell, or failure report that exercises one obligation can only cite the whole `#binder-model-parse-rule` anchor, which equally names the other four, so GOV-8 ID-lifecycle tracking sees the block as a single unit and cannot record the individual obligations' status. The neighbouring rules BNDR-8 through BNDR-11 each carry their own REQ-ID and BNDR-7's renderings carry page-local sub-letter anchors, making the asymmetry conspicuous on this very page.

**History:** `git log -S 'id="binder-model-parse-rule"'` shows the anchor and its paragraph first appear in `54c4e39`. Inspecting that commit's diff, the paragraph already bundled all the obligations present today — two-step resolution, bare-vs-`provider/modelId` matching, the ambiguity→no-model rule, the three-valued `strictCapable` probe with its short-circuit, and the bypass exemption — under the single `binder-model-parse-rule` anchor with no per-obligation REQ-ID; the missing-identifiers defect was therefore present in full at the moment the block was authored. Later commits refined the block's content without ever assigning IDs: `2424003` (2026-06-24, fix F-1311) re-specified the matcher as loom's own exact-match resolution over `ctx.modelRegistry.getAvailable()`, and the strict-capability-absence cluster (`6829a79`, 2026-06-06) tightened the probe wording. Over the same window the neighbouring rules received identifiers — BNDR-10 (`bc77cf6`), BNDR-11 (`1b6d2f1`), and the BNDR-7 sub-letter anchors (`72eace5`, `1a41db2`) — while this paragraph stayed un-numbered, so the asymmetry is the accumulated result of every binder edit skipping it rather than a single regression.

## Solution approach

Split the `binder-model-parse-rule` paragraph into its independently-testable obligations and add a REQ-ID anchor to each, continuing the page's `BNDR-N` sequence. Apply the page's existing two-tier discipline: independent obligations take `BNDR-N` IDs, and the `strictCapable` probe's outcome branches take page-local sub-letter anchors using the same scheme BNDR-7 already uses. Retain the existing umbrella anchor on the paragraph for the inbound links that cite the rule as a unit, and repoint only the citations that target a single obligation to the most-specific new ID.

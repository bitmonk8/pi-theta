## Finding

`H6a`'s end-of-`Deps` parenthetical carries three distinct pieces of prose. One is load-bearing: the explanation of why the `H7a` edge is pinned directly on `H6a` (because `H7a` closes no new spec REQ-ID and so is not a member of `H5b`'s coverage-producing set). The other two are plan-structural rationale that duplicates content already authored on a sibling leaf: the restatement that `H5b` owns the complete coverage-producing dependency set and that `H6a` inherits it transitively, and the claim that the warn-only and hard-fail footings "cover an identical set of leaves by construction" and "cannot drift."

Those last two assertions already appear verbatim-in-substance in `H5b`'s own end-of-`Deps` parenthetical (which states the canonical coverage-producing set and the transitive-completeness inheritance) and in `plan.md` §Release gate (which states the `H5b`→`H6a` sequencing and the inherited set). Holding two copies of the same rationale is a maintenance-drift surface: an edit to the canonical statement on `H5b` will not be mirrored here automatically. None of the duplicated prose imposes an implementation or acceptance constraint on the `H6a` implementer.

The `H7a`-edge justification is the exception and must survive any trim — it guards against a future maintainer who, seeing `H7a` in `H6a`'s `Deps` but absent from `H5b`'s coverage-producing set, removes the edge as apparently inconsistent. A separate finding (`Undeclared dependency on H7a…`) actively wants that edge documented, so a wholesale deletion of the parenthetical would regress it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/H6a-live-corpus-activation.md`, in the end-of-`Deps` parenthetical, strike the prose that restates `H5b`'s coverage-producing set and the cross-footing equivalence claim — specifically the sentence beginning "`H5b` owns the complete coverage-producing dependency set (`H1a`, `H5a`, `M`, `V1a`–`V18d`)… so the warn-only and hard-fail footings cover an identical set of leaves by construction." and the trailing sentence "The live surfaces this leaf hard-fails on are exactly the surfaces `H5b` warn-only-reconciles immediately prior… so the two footings cannot drift." Both already appear on `H5b`'s own end-of-`Deps` parenthetical and in `plan.md` §Release gate.

Retain the `H7a`-edge justification — the clause explaining that the `H7a` edge is pinned directly on `H6a` because `H7a` closes no new spec REQ-ID and so is not a member of `H5b`'s coverage-producing set. That rationale is load-bearing at the point of use (it sits next to the `Deps. H5b, H7a` line a maintainer reads) and the `Undeclared dependency on H7a…` finding requires the edge to stay documented; do not delete it as part of this cruft trim.

Also retain the opening clause that grounds what the two `Deps` entries (`H5b`, `H7a`) are, so the `Deps` field keeps its inline gloss.

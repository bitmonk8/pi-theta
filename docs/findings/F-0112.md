## Finding

V18a's **Adds** describes `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` as "the full Pi-side surface set." Read literally, that phrasing asserts the inventory captures *every* Pi-side surface the SDK exposes — including members loom never touches. No mechanism in the plan delivers that property. The only closure check is V18b's inventory-closure audit, a negative-direction gate that proves every Pi-side surface reference under `src/**` *resolves into* `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` (or a typebox allow-list / `// allow-pi-surface:` exemption); V18c separately proves the inventory's members exist on the SDK. Neither direction asserts the inventory is complete with respect to the SDK's total surface.

The spec backs only the weaker claim: `inventory-audit-intro.md` defines *surface-set closure* as "every Pi-side surface reference in the audited source tree resolves to an `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` entry," and states the inventory is "strictly broader than the seven capabilities" — broader than the capability subset, not exhaustive of the SDK. The plan's adjacent clause ("strictly broader than the seven capabilities") recovers the intended meaning, but the leading phrase "the full Pi-side surface set" still reads as an SDK-completeness claim the mechanics do not support.

The accurate statement is that the inventory is the full set of Pi-side surfaces *loom references* (those reachable by the V18b audit), which is strictly broader than the seven capabilities yet bounded by loom's own usage.

The overclaim is therefore the residue of resolving the prior "Non-capability SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY rows have no owning leaf" finding: the closing edit legitimately broadened V18a's ownership but chose "the full Pi-side surface set" to convey breadth, which reads as SDK-exhaustive. The phrase was not present at the initial plan build (`c6a664e`), and no later commit on the file (`git log --follow`) altered it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V18a-capability-inventory.md`'s **Adds** field, replace the phrase `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` is the full Pi-side surface set with wording that bounds the inventory to loom-referenced surfaces — e.g. ``SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` is the full set of Pi-side surfaces loom references`. Keep the existing trailing qualifier "strictly broader than the seven capabilities per [`inventory-audit-intro.md` §SDK capability inventory]" and the rest of the sentence unchanged. The fix is confined to V18a's Adds prose; the spec is read-only for this finding (its wording — "strictly broader than the seven capabilities" — already states the correct bound and needs no edit), and V18b/V18c are read-only.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer reading "the full Pi-side surface set" as an SDK-completeness obligation could over-scope V18a — attempting to enumerate every SDK member rather than only loom-referenced surfaces — an unbounded task the V18b audit does not require. The intended bound is recoverable from the adjacent clause and the spec, so a working leaf is still reachable, but the wording undercuts the inventory's actual (loom-reference-bounded) contract.

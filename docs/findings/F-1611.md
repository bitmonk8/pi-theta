## Finding

`docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-wire-and-canary.md` is structured as three peer normatively-dense paragraphs, each anchored independently and each carrying its own cluster of MUST/MUST NOT obligations: *Wire serialisation* (the line-delimited stdout record grammar), *Infrastructure-failure handling* (anchor `audit-infrastructure-failure-handling` — fail-closed behaviour, the two-category infrastructure-failure surface, the wall-clock budget, the pre-emission termination carve-out, partial-evaluation semantics), and *Non-empty-scan canary* (anchor `audit-non-empty-scan-canary` — the two-counter floor, the green-path emission contract, the infra-aborted-run carve-out, the canary-red contributor-facing resolution arm). The page title names only two of the three (`wire` and `canary`) and silently drops the middle paragraph.

That dropped paragraph is the largest of the three and is the home for ~half a dozen cross-references from elsewhere in PIC (the *Three-class partition* paragraph in `audit-failures.md` deep-links into `#audit-infrastructure-failure-handling`, step 5's TS230x routing carve-out points at it, and the *Non-empty-scan canary*'s *Infra-aborted-run carve-out* leans on it by name). A reader navigating from the parent TOC entry `[Audit wire and canary]` cannot tell from the link text that infrastructure-failure handling lives behind it; a reader grepping the corpus for a title-shaped anchor on the topic finds nothing; and the audit-cluster sibling files (`audit-failures.md`, `audit-recognised-shapes.md`, `audit-target-categories.md`, `audit-resolution.md`) all advertise their full scope in their titles, so this page is the odd one out in its own cluster.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception (at the file's creation as a standalone page)
**Introducing commits:** `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04, *"docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set"*)
**History:** The file `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-wire-and-canary.md` did not exist before `f5e89f4`; `git log --follow --diff-filter=A` returns that single commit. `f5e89f4` lifted lines 877–884 of the then-monolithic `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` into the new standalone page and gave it the H1 `# Audit wire and canary`. At that point the slice already contained all three peer paragraphs (*Wire serialisation*, *Infrastructure-failure handling*, *Non-empty-scan canary*) and the omission of "Infrastructure-failure handling" from the title is therefore baked into the split itself. `git log -S 'Infrastructure-failure handling'` confirms no earlier or later commit altered the H1 to reflect the third paragraph's presence. The follow-on `81bf52a` (2026-06-11, *"docs: collapse spec/ section pages into spec_topics/"*) only renamed the parent directory and did not touch the H1.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Rename the heading to enumerate all three paragraphs

**Approach.** Change the H1 of `audit-wire-and-canary.md` from `# Audit wire and canary` to a title naming all three peers, e.g. `# Audit wire, infrastructure failures, and canary`, and update the matching child-list entry in `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` (line 32) to the same text in lock-step.

**Spec edits.**
- `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-wire-and-canary.md` line 1: replace `# Audit wire and canary` with `# Audit wire, infrastructure failures, and canary`.
- `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` line 32: replace `- [Audit wire and canary](./pi-integration-contract/audit-wire-and-canary.md)` with `- [Audit wire, infrastructure failures, and canary](./pi-integration-contract/audit-wire-and-canary.md)`.
- No other edits — the file's basename, the two paragraph anchors (`audit-infrastructure-failure-handling`, `audit-non-empty-scan-canary`), and every inbound deep-link (in `audit-failures.md`, `coverage-matrix.md`, `V18b-inventory-audit.md`, `V18b-T-inventory-audit.md`) are unchanged.

**Pros.**
- Single one-line edit per file; trivially reviewable.
- Title now matches the file's actual scope; readers scanning the TOC see all three sub-topics.
- File basename `audit-wire-and-canary.md` stays put, so every existing path reference (plan leaves, coverage matrix, deep-link anchors, split-script line ranges in `docs/_tools/split_spec.py`) continues to resolve.

**Cons.**
- The title is longer than its cluster-siblings (`audit-failures`, `audit-recognised-shapes`, `audit-target-categories`, `audit-resolution`), each of which names a single topic.
- Mild drift between the H1 text and the file basename (basename still says `wire-and-canary`); future readers may notice.

**Risks.** None of substance. The single inconsistency to watch is that `docs/_tools/split_spec.py` has a hard-coded slug entry `("audit-wire-and-canary", 877, 884)`; that script is a one-shot historical tool for the original split and is not re-run, but a reviewer should leave the slug alone (renaming it would falsely imply the file's basename had changed).

### Option B — Split the file into three sibling pages, one per peer paragraph

**Approach.** Carve `audit-wire-and-canary.md` into three pages — `audit-wire-serialisation.md`, `audit-infrastructure-failures.md`, `audit-non-empty-scan-canary.md` — each owning one of the existing peer paragraphs, and update the cluster TOC plus every deep-link.

**Spec edits.**
- Create three new files under `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/`, distributing the paragraphs and their `<a id>` anchors (`audit-infrastructure-failure-handling` moves to `audit-infrastructure-failures.md`; `audit-non-empty-scan-canary` moves to `audit-non-empty-scan-canary.md`).
- Move the final step-2(b) wiring epilogue into one of the new files (almost certainly `audit-wire-serialisation.md`, since it pins the harness wiring for the wire contract).
- Delete `audit-wire-and-canary.md`.
- Re-point every deep-link in `audit-failures.md` (the `[Infrastructure-failure handling]` and `[Non-empty-scan canary]` cross-references) to the new file paths.
- Re-point the three plan citations (`V18b-inventory-audit.md`, `V18b-T-inventory-audit.md`, `coverage-matrix.md`) to enumerate the three new files instead of the one.
- Update the cluster TOC in `pi-integration-contract.md` to list all three new entries in place of `[Audit wire and canary]`.

**Pros.**
- Each page's H1 unambiguously names its single topic, matching the sibling-naming convention.
- Each peer paragraph becomes individually citable by file path, not just by anchor.

**Cons.**
- Multi-file edit touching at least six existing files plus three new files; far larger review surface than Option A.
- The three paragraphs are mutually entangled (the canary's *Infra-aborted-run carve-out* refers back to *Infrastructure-failure handling*; both lean on *Wire serialisation*'s record grammar; the step-2(b) wiring epilogue governs all three), so the deep-link density across the new files would be higher than within the present single file.
- Risks breaking inbound references the grep above did not surface (e.g. anchor-less prose citations such as "the *Infrastructure-failure handling* sub-paragraph below" sprinkled across the existing text).

**Risks.** Material — splitting the file is a structural edit on a load-bearing audit page; a missed inbound reference produces a dangling cross-link that fingerprint/check-link tooling would surface but only after the edit lands.

### Recommendation

Take **Option A**. The finding is cosmetic-severity and the defect is a title-vs-content mismatch, not a structural problem with the file's contents; a one-line H1 rename plus a matching TOC-entry rename resolves it without disturbing the file's anchors, basename, or any inbound deep-link. Adopt `# Audit wire, infrastructure failures, and canary` as the new title (and the identical TOC text) — it enumerates the three peers in source-order to match the file body. Watch one edge case: keep the file basename `audit-wire-and-canary.md` unchanged so the plan leaves, the coverage matrix row, and the `audit-failures.md` deep-links continue to resolve against unchanged paths.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader scanning the TOC or arriving via a cross-reference cannot see that infrastructure-failure handling lives on this page, increasing the chance that an implementer or reviewer misses the middle paragraph when working through the cluster. No normative obligation changes and no implementation diverges; the cost is navigational only.

## Finding

The `extensions/index.ts` entry shim authored by `H4a` sits outside `src/**` and is therefore invisible to every `src/**`-scoped mechanical gate. Its purity is instead asserted by a human at the loom 1.0 release-time residue inspection, item 8 (*Entry-shim purity*) in `conventions.md`. Both the `H4a` Adds prose and residue-inspection item 8 require the shim to carry "no `Promise` combinator of its own", but neither site defines what counts as a `Promise` combinator.

The authoritative enumeration exists elsewhere on the same page: the *Sequential by default* cross-cutting rule names exactly four forms (`Promise.all`, `Promise.race`, `Promise.allSettled`, `Promise.any`) and the rule's own enforcement-posture text calls them "the four Promise-combinator forms". The two entry-shim sites never point to that set, so the manual reviewer running item 8 has no anchored definition. A reviewer left to interpret the bare term may disagree on borderline constructs — `new Promise(...)`, `Promise.resolve(...)`, `Promise.reject(...)` are not in the four-form set yet read as "Promise combinators" to some auditors — producing a non-reproducible item-8 verdict for exactly the construct class the item exists to police. The two named carry-paths (the `conventions.md` cross-cutting-scope intro paragraph and item 8) repeat the bare phrasing, so the ambiguity surfaces wherever the reviewer enters the gate.

The *Sequential by default* rule's four-form enumeration predates both and was never linked from the shim-purity wording.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Anchor the bare term to the existing authoritative enumeration in the *Sequential by default* rule rather than defining it anew. At each site that reads "no `Promise` combinator of its own", tie the phrase to the four-form set so the manual reviewer resolves it deterministically:

- In `docs/plan_topics/conventions.md` residue-inspection item 8 (*Entry-shim purity*) and the cross-cutting-rules scope intro, change "no `Promise` combinator of its own" to name the set inline, e.g. "no `Promise` combinator of its own (the `Promise.all` / `Promise.race` / `Promise.allSettled` / `Promise.any` forms enumerated in *Sequential by default*)".
- Apply the same anchoring to the entry-shim purity clause in `docs/plan_topics/H4a-factory-shell-and-harness.md`'s Adds field.

Cross-referencing the *Sequential by default* rule (already the single authoritative home of the four forms) keeps one definition; the reviewer then treats constructs outside the four — `new Promise(...)`, `Promise.resolve/reject(...)`, `Promise.withResolvers(...)` — as out of scope for item 8 unless the same edit chooses to enumerate them explicitly. Edge case to watch: keep the anchored wording identical at all three sites so the term cannot drift back apart on a future edit.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The shim-purity check is a manual gate, not a mechanical one, so an undefined term degrades reproducibility: two reviewers running residue-inspection item 8 could reach different go/no-go verdicts on a borderline `Promise.*` construct in the entry shim. The shim is a thin re-export, so the realistic blast radius is small, but the gate's whole purpose is to catch the rule-subject constructs the mechanical gates miss for this one out-of-`src/**` file.

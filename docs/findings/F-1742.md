## Problem

The `mode: subagent` bullet under `### Scope of a loom file` in `docs/spec_topics/overview.md` ends with "the intermediate transcript is private to the loom and is not retained by the runtime after the loom returns." "Not retained by the runtime" reads two ways — narrowly, an unobservable GC-lifetime claim that no reference is held after the per-invocation `finally`; broadly, an assertion that the spawned `AgentSession`'s transcript is actively torn down. Neither reading is testable: the conformance harness can witness `dispose()`-in-`finally` behaviour but not a retention-after-return predicate. The disposal obligation is actually owned by PIC-9 (`docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/subagent.md`), which the overview clause neither names nor points at.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit regression
**Introducing commits:** `8eea523` (2026-05-04, "spec: fill implementation-blocking gaps"); migrated verbatim into `spec_topics/overview.md` by `fecb504` (2026-05-04, "Split spec.md and plan.md into per-topic / per-phase files").
**History:** `git log -S "is not retained by the runtime"` returns exactly the two commits above. Before `8eea523`, the subagent-mode bullet in `spec.md` ended at "the intermediate transcript is private to the loom." `8eea523`'s commit message records "Document subagent conversation lifecycle (discarded after return)" as one of its bullet points, and its diff appends "and is not retained by the runtime after the loom returns. Surfacing it for testing, replay, or observability is a future consideration (see `loom test` in [Future Considerations](#future-considerations))." The same commit elsewhere strengthens the conformance pins, but in the overview it introduced the ambiguous retention clause without anchoring it to the dispose-in-`finally` obligation that PIC-9 owns. `fecb504` then carried the clause unchanged into `spec_topics/overview.md` during the file split; no later commit has revised it.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the closing clause of the `mode: subagent` bullet so it states the observable propagation-and-isolation property — only the loom's return value crosses back to the caller, no intermediate turn appears in the caller's session, and the parent observes the result only after the spawned `AgentSession` is disposed — and forward-link the disposal obligation to PIC-9 in `pi-integration-contract/subagent.md` (anchor `pic-9`). Drop the retention claim the rewrite replaces.

## Solution constraints

- Add no new normative obligation in the overview; forward-link to PIC-9 and state no requirement of the overview's own.
- Out of scope: removal of the `loom test` future-work parenthetical, owned by the sibling `mode: subagent` future-work-parenthetical finding — do not re-introduce that aside.

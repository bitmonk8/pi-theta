## Finding

The "Caching and reload" paragraph in `package-and-settings.md` mandates "A file-watcher on each of the two paths" that invalidates the settings cache on change, debounced over a 250 ms window via the injected `Clock` seam. But the paragraph never names the mechanism that *watches* the two settings files. The only filesystem seams it cites are `FileSystem` (PIC-13) for reading and `Clock` (PIC-12) for the debounce — and the `FileSystem` seam exposes no watch member (its surface is `readText` / `readBytes` / `readdir` / `lstat` / `realpath` / `homedir` / `cwd`).

A separate `FileWatcher` seam does exist (PIC-14, `watch(roots, handler): Unsubscribe`, production-wired to chokidar), but it is introduced and scoped to the **discovery roots**: registration-steps step 5 registers "a chokidar file watcher over the discovered roots", and PIC-14's per-member prose ties the seam to the discovery-root event stream, the structural-vs-content split, and the watcher-termination posture. Nothing in the corpus states that the *settings-file* watcher attaches through PIC-14 (or through any seam at all). The settings watcher is treated as a distinct object elsewhere — session-shutdown teardown step 4 calls `settingsWatcher.close()` separately from `discoveryWatcher.close()` — yet its construction mechanism is never pinned.

The result is that an implementer cannot tell whether to (a) route the two settings paths through the existing PIC-14 `FileWatcher` seam, (b) construct a second, independent `FileWatcher`/chokidar instance, or (c) reach for Node's `fs.watch` directly. Option (c) would violate the seam discipline the rest of the spec enforces (the `FileSystem` and `Clock` seams carry explicit no-direct-Node bans precisely so the `FakeFileSystem` / `FakeClock` test seams can drive behaviour deterministically), and would leave the settings-watcher debounce/coalesce behaviour — which the spec asserts is deterministically assertable through the `Clock` seam — untestable through any injected fake.

(The original finding also raised a second concern — that the `looms.binderModel` strict-capability check "runs at loom-load time" presupposes an unstated model-registry-populated-at-load precondition. That concern does not hold: `host-prerequisites.md#model-registry-population-presupposition` explicitly states the load-time-population presupposition, `binder-model-and-context.md#binder-model-parse-rule` cites it and pins the `ctx.modelRegistry.getAvailable()` surface, and the `strictCapable` probe is fully specified there as a three-valued duck-typed check. Only the settings-watcher seam gap is a real defect.)

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** 043762e — pi-loom spec: resolve "looms.binderModel is an extension-owned settings key, not a Pi-recognised setting" (2026-05-04, Thomas Andersen); 8a6d15f — pi-loom spec: resolve "FileSystem seam interface signature lives outside the spec corpus" (2026-05-06, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The "Caching and reload" paragraph — "A file-watcher on each of the two paths invalidates the cache on change" — was authored in 043762e (then in `spec_topics/discovery.md`) with no watch seam, because no watcher seam existed in the corpus yet. The `FileWatcher` seam (PIC-14, `FakeFileWatcher`) was introduced two days later in 8a6d15f, but scoped to the discovery-root watcher; the pre-existing settings-watcher prose was never updated to bind to it. The gap is the interaction: the settings watcher predates the seam, and the seam's later arrival covered only the discovery roots, leaving the settings watcher unanchored to any seam through the f5e89f4 topic split and every edit since.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In the "Caching and reload" paragraph of `package-and-settings.md`, state that the settings-file watcher attaches through the PIC-14 `FileWatcher` seam — the same injected seam (`watch(roots, handler): Unsubscribe`) the discovery-root watcher uses — passing the two resolved settings-file paths as its roots, so the production chokidar adapter watches them and the `FakeFileWatcher` test seam drives the debounce/coalesce assertions deterministically. Make explicit that the `settingsWatcher.close()` call in session-shutdown teardown step 4 is the `Unsubscribe` this `watch(...)` call returns.

Clarify that the settings-watcher path consumes only the seam's raw `add`/`change`/`unlink` event stream feeding cache invalidation and the debounced re-merge; the structural-change-note machinery and the structural-vs-content split that PIC-14's discovery-root consumer layers on top do not apply here (settings edits route to cache re-merge, and a `loomPaths` change is separately routed as a structural change per registration-steps). Whether the implementation shares one `FileWatcher` instance with the discovery-root watcher or constructs a second dedicated instance is left to the implementer; the observable contract is that the watch goes through the injected seam, not direct Node `fs.watch`.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two reasonable implementers diverge on how the settings files are watched: one wires them through the PIC-14 `FileWatcher` seam (deterministically drivable by `FakeFileWatcher`), another uses a raw `fs.watch`, which both breaks the injected-seam test discipline the spec relies on and contradicts the no-direct-Node posture the sibling seams enforce. The asserted "deterministic via `Clock`" debounce/coalesce behaviour cannot be exercised through a fake on the raw-`fs.watch` path.

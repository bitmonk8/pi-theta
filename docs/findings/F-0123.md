## Finding

`V3d` and `V3d-T` carry a final-value-contract Tests bullet asserting that the function body's produced value is its final value at the function-result seam *on success* and is **absent on fail/cancel**. The success and fail arms are reachable from the leaf's declared dependencies: `V3a` supplies the evaluator and `V4a` supplies `?`/`Err` propagation, so a fail outcome can be induced and asserted. The **cancel** arm has no such backing. A cancelled function-result outcome is the `Err(QueryError { kind: "cancelled" })` representation defined by `errors-and-results/error-model.md` §Terminal outcomes and produced by the cancellation runtime owned by `V17a`; the cancelled trichotomy and its caller-surface map are owned by `V4c`. Neither `V17a` nor `V4c` appears in `V3d`'s Deps (`V3d-T, V3a, V4a`) or `V3d-T`'s Deps (`V3a, V4a`).

The leaf never states how the cancel case is induced or represented at the function-result seam — whether `V3d` constructs the cancelled outcome directly as its own test fixture, or whether it consumes the cancelled-outcome representation owned by `V4c`/`V17a` (which would require a declared dependency edge). The coverage matrix maps `functions.md (FN), return.md (RET)` to `V3d`, so the final-value contract — including its "no final value flows on cancel" clause — is genuinely `V3d`'s obligation to close; the gap is in *how* the obligation is exercised, not in *which* leaf owns it.

An implementer authoring `V3d-T` (whose exit gate is "tests exist, compile, and fail red for the intended reason") must invent a means of constructing a cancelled function-result outcome. Two reasonable implementers will diverge: one fabricates a synthetic cancel state local to `V3d`, another pulls in `V17a`'s real cancellation machinery via an undeclared dependency. The first risks asserting the contract against a representation that later contradicts `V17a`'s actual `cancelled` envelope.

The defect predates and survives that edit.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — V3d-owned, directly-constructed cancel state

**Approach.** State that the cancel arm is witnessed by `V3d`/`V3d-T` constructing a cancelled function-result-seam outcome directly within the test, without consuming the cancellation runtime; keep Deps at `V3a, V4a`.

**Plan edits.** In the final-value-contract Tests bullet of `V3d-T-functions-and-return.md` and `V3d-functions-and-return.md`, state that the cancel case is exercised by the test directly constructing a cancelled function-result-seam outcome owned by `V3d`, not the live cancellation runtime. Reflect the same in each leaf's Ships when. Leave Deps unchanged.

**Spec edits.** None.

**Pros.** Keeps `V3d` at its early DAG position; introduces no cross-slice edge into the heavy `V17a` subtree (`V17a` transitively depends on `H4a`/`H4b`/`V8a`/`V9a`).

**Cons.** A cancel state constructed locally can drift from `V17a`/`V4c`'s actual cancelled representation; lower fidelity to the runtime envelope.

**Risks.** If `V3d`'s synthetic representation later diverges from `V17a`'s `Err(QueryError { kind: "cancelled" })` envelope, the contract is witnessed against a representation the runtime contradicts.

### Option B — declare a dependency on the cancelled-outcome representation

**Approach.** Treat the cancel arm as consuming `V4c`'s cancelled trichotomy / `V17a`'s cancellation seam, and declare the owning leaf in Deps.

**Plan edits.** Add `V17a` (and/or `V4c`) to the Deps of `V3d-functions-and-return.md` and `V3d-T-functions-and-return.md`, and state in the final-value-contract Tests bullet that the cancel case is exercised through `V4c`/`V17a`'s cancelled-outcome representation.

**Spec edits.** None.

**Pros.** The cancel arm is witnessed against the real cancelled-outcome representation; full fidelity to the runtime envelope.

**Cons.** `V17a` sits far down the DAG (transitive deps on `H4a`/`H4b`/`V8a`/`V9a`), so adding it as a `V3d` dep resequences an early V3 leaf to land much later, for a language-level contract.

**Risks.** Significant reordering of an early leaf; V3 function work becomes idle-blocked behind the V17/H4 subtrees.

### Recommendation

Take **Option A**. The final-value contract is a language-level property the coverage matrix already assigns to `V3d` (`functions.md (FN), return.md (RET) | V3d`); witnessing "no final value flows on cancel" at the function-result seam does not require the cancellation runtime, and keeping Deps at `V3a, V4a` preserves `V3d`'s early DAG position. Edge case the implementer must watch: the directly-constructed cancel state must match the "no final value flows; caller observes `Err(QueryError { kind: "cancelled" })`" shape defined in `error-model.md` §Terminal outcomes and `cancellation.md`, so the `V3d` test does not assert against a representation `V17a` later contradicts.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

The `V3d-T` cancel-arm assertion has no enumerated means of constructing a cancelled function-result outcome, so two implementers will resolve it differently — one with a synthetic state local to `V3d`, another by reaching into `V17a`/`V4c`'s representation through an undeclared edge. A synthetic cancel state authored in isolation can pass `V3d`'s exit gate while asserting the contract against a representation that diverges from the `Err(QueryError { kind: "cancelled" })` envelope `error-model.md`/`cancellation.md` actually define.

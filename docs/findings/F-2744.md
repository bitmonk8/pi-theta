## Finding

`version-bump-step2.md` item (e) (per-session slash-dispatch serialisation) is not a single-shot pass/fail audit. When the first-pass audit records a `fail` on sub-outcome (e.i) or (e.ii) — including any non-positive verdict the conservative-posture rule coerces to `fail` — the spec requires the contributor to add a defensive per-extension-instance recovery serialisation of the snapshot → swap → body → restore window *in the same edit as the bump*, re-run (e.i)/(e.ii) against the loom-side source the bump lands, and record a **second-pass outcome** alongside the original `fail`. The spec is explicit that "the bump MAY merge only when that second-pass outcome is `pass`" and that a recovery serialisation which does not re-establish the precondition-(b) non-overlap property leaves the second-pass at `fail` and does not clear the merge gate.

V18c's editorial-review acceptance criterion (`Ships when`, arm (2)) encodes only a flat final-state check: "no MUST-level item `(a)`–`(e)` carries a `fail` outcome … a `fail` on any MUST-level item `(a)`–`(e)` blocks the bump." For item (e) this is the wrong operand. Under the literal wording a legitimately-recovered bump — first-pass `fail`, recovery serialisation landed, recorded second-pass `pass` — still "carries a `fail` outcome" on item (e) and is wrongly blocked; conversely an implementer who reads past the literal wording has no plan text telling them the second-pass `pass` (not the first-pass result) is the operand the gate reads, nor that the recovery serialisation must actually land in the same edit. The acceptance gate as authored neither asserts the recovery-serialisation obligation nor names the second-pass outcome as the merge operand for item (e).

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

A faithful implementer of V18c's exit gate reads "a `fail` on any MUST-level item `(a)`–`(e)` blocks the bump" and rejects a bump the spec explicitly permits (item (e) first-pass `fail` recovered by a recorded second-pass `pass`), while another implementer invents the second-pass handling the plan never states — two reasonable implementers diverge, and in the worst case a bump merges without the recovery serialisation actually landing.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V18c-version-bump-checklist.md`, extend the `Ships when` editorial-review acceptance criterion (arm (2)) so it encodes item (e)'s recovery second-pass semantics rather than treating any item-(e) `fail` as terminal. The criterion must require that, when item (e) records a first-pass `fail` on (e.i) or (e.ii) (including a conservative-posture-coerced `fail`), the bump commit also records the recovery serialisation landing in the same edit and a **second-pass** (e.i)/(e.ii) outcome, and that the operand the merge gate reads for item (e) is that recorded second-pass outcome under the pass-evidence rule — the bump may merge only when the second-pass outcome is `pass`. A first-pass `fail` with no recorded passing second-pass (or with a second-pass still at `fail`) blocks the bump; a first-pass `fail` followed by a recorded second-pass `pass` is a conformant merge-eligible outcome.

Cite `version-bump-step2.md` item (e)'s second-pass paragraph (the "the bump MAY merge only when that second-pass outcome is `pass`" sentence) and sub-outcomes (e.i)/(e.ii) as the source obligation. The recovery serialisation's runtime contract is already pinned spec-side at `tool-registration-lifetime.md#recovery-mutex-runtime-contract`; V18c need not restate it, only require that the merge gate reads the second-pass outcome. The fix is internal to the V18c plan leaf — the spec is read-only for this change.

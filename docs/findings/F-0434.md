## Finding

The terminal loom 1.0 release-gate operation ‚Äî the transition of the `H5a` closing gate from its seeded-fixture footing to its live-corpus footing, owned by `H6a` ‚Äî is named with two different nouns across the plan corpus. `plan.md` How-to-use step 5 calls it the "non-gating‚Üígating **activation**". `coverage-matrix.md`'s intro paragraph and `H6a`'s own second `Tests.` bullet call the same construct the "non-gating‚Üígating **flip**".

The `H6a` leaf title ("Live-corpus closing-gate **activation** (loom 1.0 release gate)") and the leaf filename (`H6a-live-corpus-activation.md`) anchor "activation" as the canonical operation noun. "flip" is the divergent variant for this construct, and it appears twice: in `coverage-matrix.md`'s intro and in `H6a`'s `Tests.` bullet 2. The original finding flagged only the `coverage-matrix.md` occurrence; the construct in fact diverges in two places.

This is a pure naming inconsistency over synonymous terms; "flip" remains acceptable as an ordinary verb elsewhere in `H6a`'s `Adds.`/`Rollback.` prose ("flipping the closing gate", "a bad flip"). The defect is confined to the named noun-phrase construct "non-gating‚Üígating X".

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Harmonise the construct on the title-anchored noun "activation":

- In `docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md` intro paragraph, replace "non-gating‚Üígating flip" with "non-gating‚Üígating activation".
- In `docs/plan_topics/H6a-live-corpus-activation.md` `Tests.` bullet 2, replace "non-gating‚Üígating flip is active" with "non-gating‚Üígating activation is active".

This aligns all three occurrences of the construct with the `H6a` leaf title and the `H6a-live-corpus-activation.md` filename. Scope is the named construct "non-gating‚Üígating X"; the ordinary verb "flip" elsewhere in `H6a`'s `Adds.`/`Rollback.` prose is out of scope and need not change.

## Consequence

A reader meets the same operation under two names ("activation" / "flip") within the release-gate prose and must infer they denote one operation. No implementer would diverge behaviourally; the inconsistency is organisational/wording only.

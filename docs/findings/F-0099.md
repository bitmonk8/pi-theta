## Finding

`V15a` (Invocation core) carries fourteen `Tests` bullets that span eight largely independent concern groups: (1) `INV-1` load-time/invocation-time `realpath` containment on both channels, (2) the `INV-2`/`INV-3` arg-list-style and open-options-struct contracts, (3) the static-resolution transitive parse/lower pass, (4) `return.md` final-value propagation, (5) the fresh-vs-attach cross-mode matrix selection, (6) the six `invoke` parse/load diagnostic codes (`loom/parse/invoke-arg-type-mismatch`, `-return-type-mismatch`, `-arity-too-few`, `-arity-too-many`, `-non-loom-extension`, `loom/load/callee-has-errors`), (7) swallowing-handler attachment with three-channel late-settlement suppression at the `Checkpoint` seam, and (8) the ceiling-#4 `params`/`invoke<T>`-return delegation plus the `SLSH-5` per-frame invocation-record provenance seam.

Even after `V15b` (depth/cycle), `V15c` (imports), and `V15d` (promptâ†’prompt suspend) were peeled off into their own leaves, `V15a` remains wide enough that a reviewer cannot verify every Class-1 and Class-2 obligation it carries in a single focused pass. The static-resolution recognition path (parse cache, six diagnostics, static arg/return checks) and the runtime-execution path (cross-mode matrix, final-value propagation, swallowing-handler suppression, ceiling-#4 delegation, `SLSH-5` record) read against different data sources and exercise different machinery, yet they share one acceptance gate.

This is a step-atomicity defect: the leaf is implementable, but its breadth degrades the reviewability of its `Ships when` gate and inflates the surface a single `-T` suite must establish before the implementation leaf can land.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

Split `V15a` along the static-resolution / runtime-execution boundary, keeping the spec obligations themselves unchanged and only re-partitioning their closing leaf:

- Keep on the static-resolution facet (retain `V15a`): the static-resolution transitive parse/lower pass, the six `invoke` parse/load diagnostic-code bullets (`loom/parse/invoke-arg-type-mismatch`, `loom/parse/invoke-return-type-mismatch`, `loom/parse/invoke-arity-too-few`, `loom/parse/invoke-arity-too-many`, `loom/parse/invoke-non-loom-extension`, `loom/load/callee-has-errors`), and the `INV-2`/`INV-3` arg-list-style and open-options-struct bullets, plus the static-resolution parse-cache portion of the `Adds`.
- Move to a new runtime-execution leaf (`<new>`, depending on the static-resolution facet): the fresh-vs-attach cross-mode matrix bullet, the `return.md` final-value propagation bullet, the `INV-1` load-time/invocation-time `realpath` containment bullet, the swallowing-handler attachment / three-channel late-settlement suppression bullet, the ceiling-#4 `params`/`invoke<T>`-return delegation bullet, and the `SLSH-5` per-frame invocation-record provenance bullet, with the matching `Adds` provenance text and the `Ships when` clauses for those assertions.

Carry each leaf's `-T` partner alongside it (`V15a-T` stays with the static facet; add a `-T` partner for the new runtime leaf). Re-point downstream `Deps` edges to whichever facet they consume: the `SLSH-5` provenance consumer `V12b`/`V12b-T` and the ceiling-#4 routing reference in `V5e`/`V5e-T` point at the runtime leaf; static-resolution consumers (`V15c`/`V15c-T`) stay on `V15a`; re-evaluate `V14a`, `V15b`, `V15d`, `V6c`, `V18d` against the facet each actually uses. Add the new leaf to `H5b`'s coverage-producing `Deps` set and to the V15 slice listing in `docs/plan.md`. In `coverage-matrix.md`, the `INV-1` portion of the `INV-1, INV-2, INV-3` row and the `implementation-notes.md` static-resolution row may need their closing-leaf cell updated to name the correct facet (the `INV-2`/`INV-3` and six-diagnostic-code closures stay on the static facet). The `<new>` ID is the implementer's to allocate per the plan's leaf-ID scheme.

Resolve the `INV-1`-containment-versus-`V8b` Deps decision (see Related Findings) on whichever facet ends up owning the `realpath` containment bullet.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

If V15a ships unsplit the leaf is still buildable, but its `Ships when` gate forces one implementation pass to satisfy fourteen heterogeneous assertions spanning static recognition and runtime execution, so a reviewer cannot confirm each Class-1/Class-2 obligation in a focused pass and a weak or mis-asserting bullet is more likely to slip through. The leaf's `-T` partner must also stand up the entire surface before any of it can land, enlarging the critique surface for downstream review passes.

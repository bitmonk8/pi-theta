## Finding

`imports.md` carries one numbered REQ-ID, `IMP-1` (the `Resolver` failure contract / `loom/load/unresolvable-warp-path`), which is mapped in the Numbered REQ-IDs table to `V15c`. But the page also owns a set of obligations that carry **no** numbered `PREFIX-N` REQ-ID:

- Five code-keyed parse/load diagnostics — `loom/parse/warp-top-level-statement`, `loom/parse/import-non-warp-extension`, `loom/parse/import-unknown-symbol`, `loom/parse/import-name-collision`, and `loom/load/import-cycle` (tested by `V15c`).
- The un-anchored **Visibility** and **Re-exports** normative rules — implicit auto-export of every top-level `schema`/`enum`/`fn`, the aliased `export … from` re-export form, and the negative rule that a plain `import` is not re-exported downstream (tested by `V15i`).

None of these appear in the Code-keyed obligation areas table. That table is the traceability and closing-gate surface for exactly this class of obligation — "Several non-narrative pages own their obligations through `loom/parse/*`, `loom/load/*`, and `loom/runtime/*` diagnostic codes rather than numbered `PREFIX-N` REQ-IDs … [plus] every normative MUST/MUST-NOT … that carries no numbered `PREFIX-N` REQ-ID and no `loom/...` registry code." `invocation.md` is the structurally identical case (one numbered family `INV-1…4` plus un-anchored invoke diagnostics) and **is** tracked, at `cka-14` → `V15f`. `imports.md` is the asymmetric omission: its code-keyed and visibility obligations have no `cka-<n>` row at all.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add a new appended row (taking the next free, append-only `cka-<n>` token) to the Code-keyed obligation areas table in `docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md` covering `imports.md` (IMP):

- The code-keyed parse/load diagnostics `loom/parse/warp-top-level-statement`, `loom/parse/import-non-warp-extension`, `loom/parse/import-unknown-symbol`, `loom/parse/import-name-collision`, and `loom/load/import-cycle` → closing leaf `V15c`.
- The un-anchored Visibility / Re-exports MUSTs (implicit auto-export of top-level `schema`/`enum`/`fn`; the aliased `export … from` re-export form; the negative plain-`import` non-re-export rule) → closing leaf `V15i`.

`IMP-1` / `loom/load/unresolvable-warp-path` is already mapped to `V15c` in the Numbered REQ-IDs table and needs no new entry. The diagnostics half and the visibility half may be carried as one combined row or as two separate rows. Every leaf-ID in the new closing-leaf cell(s) must be backtick-delimited per §Closing-leaf column authoring, or the `H5d` tokeniser drops it. The fix is internal to the plan corpus — no edit to `docs/spec_topics/imports.md` is required.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

`imports.md`'s code-keyed diagnostics and visibility/re-export MUSTs have no traceability path in the Code-keyed obligation areas table, asymmetric with the tracked `invocation.md`/`cka-14` analogue. `H5d`/`H5e` therefore offer no `cka`-backed gate for them, and `H5e` scans MUST-tokens rather than diagnostic-code-keyed obligations, so a regression — a dropped `V15c` diagnostic test or `V15i` visibility test — would not redden any coverage gate. The obligations are tested today by `V15c`/`V15i`, so nothing ships untested; the gap is the missing mechanical traceability, not a present coverage hole.

## Finding

`V9b` is the owner of the registration steps. Its **Adds** enumerates step 1–5 and names the accumulator built during the `session_start` walk as the "pending list" (`registerFlag('loom')` → walk; **pending list**; `session_start` collision + `registerCommand`; …).

`V9k` and `V9k-T` describe the failure behaviour of that same accumulator — a `session_start`-time `pi.getCommands()` read failure "drops the **pending-registration list** for that pass (no `pi.registerCommand` calls issue)" — but call it "pending-registration list".

These are the same object: the per-`session_start` collection of discovered looms staged for `registerCommand`. No equivalence statement links the two spellings, so a reader searching the corpus for either term misses the other half of the contract — the producer (`V9b`) and the failure-path consumer (`V9k`) do not cross-reference under a shared name.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-by-specific-commit
**Introducing commit:** `6df646a` — "pi-loom plan: resolve \"extension-bootstrap SDK-failure rule has no closing leaf\"" (2026-06-12)
**History:** `V9b`'s "pending list" wording has been present since the plan's initial build commit `c6a664e` ("build/update plan for spec.md + review", 2026-06-10) — at that point there was a single name and no defect. The divergence was introduced when the `V9k` leaf was first authored in `6df646a`, which named the same accumulator "pending-registration list" rather than reusing `V9b`'s existing term. The two-name conflict therefore dates to `6df646a`, against the inception-era `V9b` term.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Standardise on "pending-registration list" — the more descriptive of the two spellings, already used at both `V9k` sites. The only edit needed is in `V9b`'s **Adds** bullet in `docs/plan_topics/V9b-registration-drain-state.md`: change the step-1–5 enumeration's "pending list" to "pending-registration list". `V9k` and `V9k-T` already use the target term and need no change.

`V9b` remains the owning/declaration site of the accumulator, so the canonical term lives there once aligned. No spec edit is required; the term is internal to the plan's registration-step description.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A cross-search for "pending-registration list" misses `V9b`'s producer site and vice versa, so an implementer wiring `V9k`'s `getCommands()` read-failure branch cannot trace the accumulator back to its construction in `V9b` by name. The concept is recoverable from surrounding context, so no implementer is blocked, but the producer/consumer link is weaker than the plan's naming discipline intends.

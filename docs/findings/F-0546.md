## Problem

`V9a`'s **Adds** field enumerates the consumers of the pinned-constants-block constant `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS` as "`V9g`, `V9i`, and `V17a` import rather than redeclare" it. `V17a`'s **Adds** field contradicts this: it frames `V17a` as a conceptual consumer of the constant's *semantics* only, naming `V9g` and `V9i` as the sole leaves that consume the block constant and stating no leaf redeclares it. The two pages disagree on whether `V17a` is a runtime importer of the literal. The corpus resolves against `V17a` importing the literal — its **Tests** (`CNCL-1`…`CNCL-6`) exercise no site that reads the numeric cap, which is awaited only at `V9g`'s session-shutdown teardown and `V9i`'s subagent-disposal bound; `V17a` owns the cap's semantics, not a runtime read.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 622d0d3 — pi-loom plan: resolve "SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS physical home contradicts pinned-constants-block placement" (2026-06-11, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** Commit 622d0d3 authored both halves of the contradiction in the same change: it rewrote `V9a`'s Adds to read "`V9g`, `V9i`, and `V17a` consume it without redeclaring it" while simultaneously writing `V17a`'s Adds to enumerate only `V9g` and `V9i` as block-constant consumers and frame `V17a` itself as a "conceptual consumer." A later commit 2f4ecea (2026-06-17) sharpened `V9a`'s "consume it without redeclaring" to "import rather than redeclare," making the already-present contradiction starker but not introducing it.

## Solution approach

In `docs/plan_topics/V9a-capability-probe.md`, edit the **Adds** field's consumer enumeration so it names only `V9g` and `V9i` as the leaves that import the block constant, matching `V17a`'s variant, and state that `V17a` owns the cancellation-runtime *semantics* of the constant without importing the literal. The clause currently reading "`V9g`, `V9i`, and `V17a` import rather than redeclare" should drop `V17a` from the importer list.

## Finding

`docs/spec_topics/session-model-and-appendix.md` carries, on line 7, a stand-alone HTML comment:

> `<!-- Anchor-scheme stability and orientation-vs-obligation citation rules for the sm-N-… anchors relocated to [Governance — GOV-23](./governance/anchor-scheme-and-retired.md#gov-23). -->`

The comment is invisible in rendered Markdown, contributes nothing to either reader-facing prose or the normative contract, and exists solely to record a past content-relocation event (the move that became GOV-23 in `docs/spec_topics/governance/anchor-scheme-and-retired.md`). The normative anchor-scheme stability rule it points at is already self-contained at its destination, and is correctly cross-referenced by other sites in the corpus (`binder/defaulting-system-note-echo.md`, `binder/binder-model-and-context.md`, `glossary.md`) without needing a redirect comment at the origin. Nothing in the SM-1…SM-8 list relies on the comment for context, and a reader who lands on the Session Model section gains nothing from learning where the rule used to live.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** af9288d — pi-loom spec: resolve "editorial and governance meta-commentary misplaced in orientation" (2026-06-03, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The comment was added in af9288d on 2026-06-03 as part of the same change that lifted the inline `sm-anchor-scheme-stability` paragraph out of the Session Model section and re-homed its normative content under GOV-23 in `governance.md`. The relocation itself was sound; the redirect comment was kept behind as a courtesy breadcrumb. Two later refactors only moved the comment around without altering its semantics: f5e89f4 (2026-06-04) split the oversized spec set into the subdirectory layout, carrying the comment from `docs/spec/session-model-and-appendix.md` to its present path, and 81bf52a (2026-06-11) collapsed `spec/` into `spec_topics/` and repointed the GOV-23 link from `./spec_topics/governance.md#gov-23` to the current `./governance/anchor-scheme-and-retired.md#gov-23` after governance.md was itself split. The defect therefore traces cleanly to a single introducing commit.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Delete line 7 of `docs/spec_topics/session-model-and-appendix.md` (the entire HTML comment, including its blank-line padding so the umbrella paragraph and the SM-1 bullet end up separated by a single blank line). Make no other edits — GOV-23 already owns the normative rule and is cited from the sites that need to find it, so no replacement breadcrumb is required at the relocation origin. Verify after deletion that `grep -n "anchor-scheme\|GOV-23\|gov-23" docs/spec_topics/session-model-and-appendix.md` returns nothing; the other corpus citations of GOV-23 are not affected and need no edits.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The defect is invisible to rendered output and to any conforming implementation. It is pure editorial residue and harms only the spec's own hygiene: every future maintainer who reads `session-model-and-appendix.md` must skip past a comment that exists only to apologise for a refactor that has long since settled.

## Finding

The closed `details.call` label set is owned by the **Per-step isolation** paragraph of `session-shutdown-semantics.md`, which enumerates exactly **eleven** labels: sub-step 1 (`"loomRegistry.drain"`, `"loomRegistry.initDrainStateTag"`), sub-step 3 (`"Clock.now()"`, `"Clock.setTimeout(awaitCap)"`, `"Clock.clearTimeout(awaitCap)"`), sub-step 4 (`"discoveryWatcher.close"`, `"settingsWatcher.close"`, `"Clock.clearTimeout(debounce)"`), and sub-step 5 (three `removeEventListener` sites). Sub-step 3's three bounded-await `Clock` calls **do** carry `details.call` labels and **do** emit `loom/host/session-shutdown-teardown-step-failed` (`details.step: 3`); only sub-step 2 and sub-step 3's per-entry `Promise.allSettled` settle are label-free.

`patch-skew-degradation.md` contradicts this. Its (P-a)/(P-b-1)/(P-c-1) fixture obligations assert that "each of the **eight** call sites enumerated in the closed `details.call` label set of the **Per-step isolation** paragraph below was invoked", and immediately add that "**Sub-steps 2 and 3** carry no `details.call` label and are witnessed separately" — folding sub-step 3 wholesale into the label-free bucket alongside sub-step 2. That undercounts the set by exactly the three sub-step-3 `Clock` labels and mis-states sub-step 3's labelling status.

`session-only-degraded-state.md` is already correct and corroborates the authoritative count: its acceptance criterion (b) enumerates "the two sub-step-1, three sub-step-3, three sub-step-4, and three sub-step-5 sites = **3 × 11 = 33 fixtures**", and its co-emission rule lists all eleven sites including the three sub-step-3 `Clock` calls. The defect is confined to `patch-skew-degradation.md`: an implementer following its fixture prose asserts eight invoked sites and never spies the sub-step-3 `Clock` calls, while an implementer following `session-only-degraded-state.md` writes 33 fixtures covering eleven sites — the two pages cannot both be satisfied.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `patch-skew-degradation.md`, align the (P-a)/(P-b-1)/(P-c-1) "full five-sub-step teardown still runs" fixture obligations with the eleven-member closed set owned by `session-shutdown-semantics.md`:

- Change "each of the **eight** call sites enumerated in the closed `details.call` label set" to "each of the **eleven** call sites" (or replace the hard count with a by-reference phrase — "each call site in the closed `details.call` label set of the **Per-step isolation** paragraph" — so the count cannot drift again on a future set widening).
- Correct the follow-on sentence: only **sub-step 2** (per-entry `ActiveInvocationRegistry` iteration) and **sub-step 3's per-entry `Promise.allSettled` await over `disposeBarrier`s** are label-free and witnessed separately via the registry-iteration / `disposeBarrier`-or-`Clock.setTimeout` spies. Sub-step 3's three bounded-await `Clock` calls (`Clock.now()`, `Clock.setTimeout(awaitCap)`, `Clock.clearTimeout(awaitCap)`) carry `details.call` labels and are part of the eleven-member spy assertion, not the separately-witnessed bucket.

No edit to `session-only-degraded-state.md` is required — its `3 × 11 = 33` count and eleven-site co-emission list already match the authoritative set. Watch the spy-seam list when editing: the eleven spies fan across the injected `LoomRegistry`, `chokidar`-watcher, `Clock`, and signal-listener seams, and the three new sub-step-3 sites are all `Clock`-seam spies.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two implementers diverge on the conformance fixtures for the wire-contract `details.call` set: one following `patch-skew-degradation.md` spies eight sites and omits the three sub-step-3 `Clock` calls entirely; one following `session-only-degraded-state.md` writes 33 fixtures covering all eleven sites. The patch-skew (P-a)/(P-b-1)/(P-c-1) fixtures would not witness sub-step-3 teardown-step-failed emission at all, leaving that emission path unverified under one reading.

## Problem

In the PIC corpus "seam" is a load-bearing term of art naming a DI-injected interface the runtime reads through and tests substitute against (`Checkpoint`, `SchemaValidator`, `Clock`, `FileSystem`, `FileWatcher`, `TokenEstimator`, `IdSource`, `Resolver`). In `patch-skew-degradation.md` §**Per-step isolation** the phrase "the interstitial seam between sub-steps 4 and 5" — and the anchor `interstitial-seam-tag-write-swallow` it carries — instead labels a control-flow transition point at which the runtime performs the `"shutting-down" → "degraded-needs-reload"` drain-state tag write (`LoomRegistry.markRuntimeDegraded`); no DI interface is introduced, nothing is injected, and tests do not substitute against it. The collision propagates to `session-only-degraded-state.md`, `drain-state-contract.md`, and `code-registry-host.md`, each of which would, under the corpus-wide meaning, imply a phantom DI surface the implementation neither exposes nor intends.

## Solution approach

Rename the misused term where it labels the post-sub-step-4 tag write: at the definition site in `patch-skew-degradation.md` §Per-step isolation, replace "interstitial seam between sub-steps 4 and 5" with a tag-write phrasing (e.g. "interstitial tag-write between sub-steps 4 and 5") and rename the anchor `interstitial-seam-tag-write-swallow` → `interstitial-tag-write-swallow`. Propagate the de-seamed term to every cross-reference site that spells "seam" for this transition: the swallow-rule links and `try`/`catch` references in `session-only-degraded-state.md` (§Isolation, §Compound-failure case, acceptance (f), acceptance (k)), the `markRuntimeDegraded`-call and transition-reference phrasings in `drain-state-contract.md`, and the "seam-tag-write-throw recovery path" / "same handler-scoped seam" phrasings in `code-registry-host.md`. Preserve the legacy anchor's resolvability so existing links and external bookmarks still resolve after the rename.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the legitimate DI-seam references in the same files (`Clock`, `Checkpoint`, `FileWatcher` seams, *Seam minimality (normative)* in `session-only-degraded-state.md`) — rename only the sites labelling the post-sub-step-4 tag write.
- Do not change the wire-contract call-site label `"loomRegistry.markRuntimeDegraded"` in the **Per-step isolation** `details.call` set; it is carried for prose cross-reference only.

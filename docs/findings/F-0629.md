## Finding

The glossary entry for `caller` (`docs/spec_topics/glossary.md`, line 13) explicitly tells the reader that the term is used in two distinct senses — *slash caller* (the user whose slash-command turn dispatched a loom) and *invoke parent* (the loom or `.warp` `fn` whose `invoke(...)` or `.loom`-callable call spawned the current loom) — and then defers resolution to an author-side discipline: "Authors and implementers SHOULD use the disambiguated terms `slash caller` and `invoke parent` whenever the bare word `caller` is ambiguous in context." The collision is acknowledged on the canonical page and pushed back into prose, not resolved.

The bare word `caller` is then used across the spec corpus in both senses without enforcement. `invocation.md` uses `caller` consistently for invoke-parent contexts (the cross-mode matrix rows; "the caller's current conversation"; "the caller's settings are not inherited"); `slash-invocation.md` introduces the variant `slash caller` (per the SHOULD); `errors-and-results/error-model.md` mixes both — the failure-cause table uses the column header *Slash caller (user-visible)* alongside *invoke parent*, but neighbouring prose says "the caller observes only the `Err` envelope" where `caller` is whichever of the two the cause routes to. `binder-model-and-context.md` uses `caller-session` for the user session, `frontmatter-fields-a.md` says "the loom inherits the caller's model" (which means the *Pi session* — neither of the two glossary senses). The glossary entry's own *See* points at the two normative pages, but neither of those pages enforces the disambiguated forms either, so a reader resolving `caller` by following the link does not land somewhere stricter.

A glossary entry whose body is a recommendation is upside-down: the glossary is the canonical-term page, so an entry that says "use *these other* terms instead" is admitting that the head term should not be a glossary entry at all but a disambiguation pointer to two glossary entries that do exist on their own (`slash caller`, `invoke parent`). The current entry achieves the worst of both shapes — it keeps `caller` admissible as a glossary term while undermining its own authority.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single
**State:** shaped

### Recommendation

Replace the entry with a pure disambiguation pointer and sweep the call sites. Add standalone glossary entries `slash caller` and `invoke parent`, each with its own canonical definition and `See:` link. Replace the current `caller` entry's body with one sentence stating that `caller` is not a coined term: it is shorthand for one of the two entries, and spec prose MUST use the disambiguated form except where the context names both (e.g. a sentence true of both, such as "the caller observes the `Err` envelope" in `error-model.md`, which may keep `caller` provided the surrounding paragraph has just enumerated both senses). Sweep `invocation.md`, `overview.md`, `error-model.md`, `subagent.md`, and `binder-model-and-context.md` so every bare `caller` either becomes one of the disambiguated terms or is justified by a both-senses-apply context. This makes the SHOULD enforceable as a MUST, gives a future grep gate (`grep -nE '\bcaller\b' docs/spec_topics`) something to check against, and aligns with the existing precedent in `runtime-event-channel.md` and `slash-invocation.md` SLSH-3 that already write `slash caller and no invoke parent` in full, and the `error-model.md` failure-table column header that already uses *Slash caller*.

Spec edits: three glossary entries (`caller` becomes a disambiguator; two new entries `slash caller`, `invoke parent` are added); approximately 15–25 prose rewrites across the five files above; the `operator` entry's parenthetical loses its embedded redefinition.

Edge cases for the implementer:
- The sentences "the caller observes only the `Err` envelope" under *Failure* and *Cancelled* in `error-model.md` are true under both senses; rewriting them as "the *slash caller* and the *invoke parent* each observe only the `Err` envelope" is more accurate than collapsing to one term. Leaving bare `caller` here is defensible **iff** the surrounding `Per-cause caller surfaces` table has just enumerated both — the carve-out covers exactly this case.
- The bare `caller` in `frontmatter-fields-a.md` `model` row ("the loom inherits the caller's model") and the `caller-session` token in `binder-model-and-context.md` denote the *Pi session* (the slash caller's session, but at a layer below the user), not either glossary sense; these are a third quasi-sense and are best resolved by replacing them with *Pi session* / *Pi session's model* rather than by inventing a third glossary entry. Flag these in the sweep but resolve them as the obvious local rewrite.
- The `operator` entry's parenthetical "(which may be another loom via `invoke(...)` or a Pi prompt-mode user turn)" embeds a definition of `caller` that the standalone entries will subsume; reduce it to a `[caller](#caller)` cross-reference.
- A sweep that misses one ambiguous site leaves the corpus in a worse place than today (a MUST with admitted violations). Run a one-shot grep over `docs/spec_topics/**` after the sweep.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The spec is internally coherent in context — at every concrete use site one of the two senses is determinable by the surrounding paragraph — so an implementer does not actually diverge. The cost is reader friction: the canonical-term page admits ambiguity and then defers, which both weakens the glossary's authority and makes spec-prose drift (the `caller-session` / `caller's model` third-sense uses) hard to catch with a grep gate. Left in place, every future spec edit that adds a `caller` use perpetuates the SHOULD-level workaround.

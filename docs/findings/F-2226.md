## Problem

The top-of-file *Implementation gate* banner in `session-only-degraded-state.md` scopes by clause: it declares two clauses non-gated (the *Drain-state tag write (state-independent)* section and the *Isolation* sub-clause under *Diagnostic emission*) and every other clause gated pending the host-prerequisites clause-(a) resolution, with a default-to-gated rule for clauses added later. The body carries no heading, badge, callout, or section break that distinguishes the non-gated clauses from the gated remainder — the non-gated *Drain-state tag write* section sits as an ordinary `##` section between gated sections, and the non-gated *Isolation* clause is buried mid-way inside the otherwise-gated *Diagnostic emission* section. An implementer who reads past the gate preamble, or who jumps directly to a section, gets no in-body signal of which clauses are buildable now and may implement gated behaviour before the dependency resolves.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** single-commit-introduction
**Introducing commits:** `8bcd52a` (F-1163 — "session-only-degraded-state gate scopes by clause not page")
**History:** The file was first created by a spec-size-cap split (`f5e89f4`). Its *Implementation gate* banner originally blocked the **entire page** (`b8f004f`, F-0719: "The obligations on this page are blocked …") — with no non-gated subset, gated and non-gated content could not interleave, so the defect did not yet exist. Commit `8bcd52a` (F-1163) re-scoped the gate "by clause, not by file," carving the *Drain-state tag write* paragraph and the *Isolation* clause out as non-gated while leaving everything else gated, but added no heading, callout, or section break to separate the two classes in the body. The later relocation commit `c3de47a` (F-1602) touched the file without adding any separation. The interleaving therefore entered exactly with the clause-scoping change.

## Solution approach

Add a uniform gate-status badge at each `##` section heading and at the start of the *Isolation* sub-clause under *Diagnostic emission*, marking each clause as buildable-now or gated and cross-linking back to the *Implementation gate* banner, so the partition the banner declares is observable at every reading entry point. Mark the *Drain-state tag write (state-independent)* section and the *Isolation* sub-clause as non-gated; mark every other section, including the acceptance-criteria fixtures (a)–(m), as gated.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: moving, relocating, or renaming any section — the *Isolation* clause stays beside the *Diagnostic emission* contract, and existing section names cited by `V9l` stay valid.
- Badge wording reuses the gate banner's existing partition vocabulary (non-gated / buildable-now vs gated); do not coin a third naming for the same partition.

## Finding

The top-of-file *Implementation gate* banner in `session-only-degraded-state.md` scopes by clause rather than by page: it declares exactly two clauses **non-gated (buildable now)** — the *Drain-state tag write (state-independent)* paragraph (the `LoomRegistry.markRuntimeDegraded` setter contract) and the *Isolation* sub-clause under *Diagnostic emission* — and marks **every other clause** gated pending resolution of the host-prerequisites clause-(a) open contradiction, with a default-to-gated rule for any clause added later.

The body, however, carries no heading, callout, badge, or section break that distinguishes the two non-gated clauses from the gated remainder. The non-gated *Drain-state tag write* section sits as an ordinary `##` section between gated sections, and the non-gated *Isolation* clause is buried mid-way inside the otherwise-gated *Diagnostic emission* section. The branch-behaviour normative text (predicate split, co-emission, idempotent re-entry, residual gaps) and acceptance criteria (a)–(m) share the same flat structure. An implementer who reads linearly past the gate preamble — or who jumps to a section directly — encounters an unbroken normative document with no in-body signal of which clauses are buildable now, and may implement gated behaviour before the dependency resolves.

The gate preamble itself is complete and unambiguous; the defect is purely that the gated/non-gated boundary it declares is not observable anywhere in the body that the declaration governs.

Its *Implementation gate* banner originally blocked the **entire page** (`b8f004f`, F-0719: "The obligations on this page are blocked …") — with no non-gated subset, gated and non-gated content could not interleave, so the defect did not yet exist. Commit `8bcd52a` (F-1163) re-scoped the gate "by clause, not by file," carving the *Drain-state tag write* paragraph and the *Isolation* clause out as non-gated while leaving everything else gated, but added no heading, callout, or section break to separate the two classes in the body. The later relocation commit `c3de47a` (F-1602) touched the file without adding any separation. The interleaving therefore entered exactly with the clause-scoping change.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Keep the existing section order in `session-only-degraded-state.md` and add an explicit, uniform gate badge to each `##` section and to the *Isolation* sub-clause, so the gated/non-gated boundary declared by the *Implementation gate* banner is observable at every reading entry point.

Immediately under each section heading (and at the start of the *Isolation* sub-clause inside *Diagnostic emission*), add a single leading bold badge line cross-linking back to the gate banner:

- Mark the *Drain-state tag write (state-independent)* section and the *Isolation* sub-clause with `**[buildable now]**`.
- Mark every other section — the branch-behaviour normative text (predicate split, co-emission, idempotent re-entry, residual gaps) and acceptance criteria (a)–(m) — with `**[gated — clause (a)]**`.

Add one short badge line per section/sub-clause; do not move or relocate any content, and do not rename any section (so the Spec citations in `V9l` remain valid and the *Isolation* clause stays beside the *Diagnostic emission* contract it isolates).

Use the gate banner's exact vocabulary verbatim — "non-gated (buildable now)" / "gated" — in the badge wording so the body does not introduce a third naming for the same partition. Restate the default-to-gated rule at the section level so that a newly added clause carrying no badge still reads as gated.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer of the degraded-state branch who skips the gate preamble, or who navigates straight to a section, gets no in-body signal that all branch-behaviour text and fixtures (a)–(m) are gated. They may build gated behaviour early and incur rework when the host-prerequisites clause-(a) verdict lands (which may declare the branch unreachable as written). The gate banner is fully stated at the top, so a careful reader can still produce a correct build; the gap is wayfinding, not missing information.

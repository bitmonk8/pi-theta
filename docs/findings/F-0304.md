## Problem

`docs/plan.md` §How to use this plan, step 2 restates the transitive-completeness trigger inline using the phrase "can introduce", which admits two incompatible readings: a factual one (the leaf *will* introduce a coverage-producing artefact) and a modal one (the leaf merely *has the potential to*). At the borderline the two readings partition new leaves differently — a leaf an author judges "might add a REQ-ID but hasn't yet" lands inside the obligation under the modal reading and outside it under the factual reading. Step 2 names `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline as the authoritative trigger, but that clause carries the identical wording, so the deferral does not resolve the ambiguity. Under the narrow reading a genuinely coverage-producing leaf can be omitted from `H5b`'s **Deps**, and because `H5d`'s transitive-completeness arm is a per-cell at-least-one check it stays green when a sibling closer covers the same row.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced by a later fix (single introducing commit)
**Introducing commit:** `ab8e297` — "pi-loom plan: resolve \"Transitive-completeness obligation invisible at leaf authoring\"" (2026-06-11)
**History:** The plan corpus is git-tracked. `git log -S "can introduce an executable REQ-ID" -- docs/plan.md` and `git log -G` both return exactly one commit, `ab8e297`, which added the entire trigger sentence to step 2 (previously step 2 ended at "Link the new leaf into the appropriate section below."). That fix resolved a prior finding about the transitive-completeness obligation being invisible at leaf-authoring time by surfacing the trigger inline — and in doing so imported the ambiguous "can introduce" wording. The wording was inherited from the §REQ-ID discipline *Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance* clause first authored one commit earlier in `37733fd` ("resolve \"H6a transitive-completeness rule parked in Deps, not conventions\"", 2026-06-11). The defect is therefore fix-introduced at the `plan.md` surface by `ab8e297`, propagating phrasing established by `37733fd`; it was not present at plan inception.

## Solution approach

Strike step 2's inline re-listing of the trigger and cue the author to the authoritative `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline (*Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance*) clause instead of restating it. This removes the second ambiguous surface outright rather than maintaining a disambiguated copy, and matches step 2 already declaring `conventions.md` the single owning statement of the trigger.

## Solution constraints

- `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline remains the authoritative trigger surface; do not introduce a new or competing trigger definition in `plan.md`.

## Finding

GOV-16 (`spec_topics/governance/stable-inline-labels.md`) registers exactly three stable-inline-label prefixes as a second governed identifier class: `HC3` (`HC3-a … HC3-e`) and `NOCEIL` (`NOCEIL-1 … NOCEIL-4`) on `hard-ceilings.md`, and `SNK` (`SNK-a … SNK-k`, the per-`kind` top-level `Err` note templates under SLSH-4) on `slash-invocation.md`. The coverage matrix's `## Governance REQ-IDs (GOV-*)` paragraph accounts for two of the three: it states `HC3-a … HC3-e` close in `V11f` and that the `NOCEIL-1 … NOCEIL-4` non-existence claims are distributed across `V6a`/`V4d`/`V4b`/`V15b`. `SNK` is the only registered GOV-16 inline-label prefix with no closure note anywhere in the matrix.

The underlying obligation is in fact closed: `SNK-a … SNK-k` are sub-rows of `SLSH-4`, and the *Numbered REQ-IDs* table maps `SLSH-3, SLSH-4, SLSH-5 | V12b`; `V12b` (and `V12b-T`) carry a `SLSH-4` Tests bullet asserting the per-kind note templates (`SNK-a..k`) render verbatim with a catch-all. So the defect is not an uncovered obligation — it is an incomplete inline-label accounting. An auditor reconciling GOV-16's three registered prefixes against the matrix finds `HC3` and `NOCEIL` explicitly accounted for and `SNK` silently absent, with nothing recording that `SNK` closure is subsumed by `SLSH-4 → V12b`.

Note that the H5a closing gate does not audit the inline-label class as a distinct surface (its arms cover numbered REQ-IDs, registry codes, and un-anchored MUSTs), so adding the note does not arm a mechanical gate; its value is the matrix's self-consistency as the traceability artefact the release process reasons from.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `27b2576` (pi-loom spec: resolve "SLSH-4 covers 11 independently normative template rows under one REQ-ID", 2026-06-05); `2565ddd` (pi-loom plan: resolve "V16a NOCEIL Adds. claim with no backing Tests", 2026-06-10)
**History:** `git log -S "SNK"` shows the `SNK` inline-label class was coined in the spec at `27b2576` (decomposing SLSH-4's eleven note-template rows into `SNK-a … SNK-k` on `slash-invocation.md` and registering the `SNK` prefix in GOV-16); that commit touched only spec files and `spec-review.md`, leaving `coverage-matrix.md` untouched. `git log -S "HC3-a"`/`-S "NOCEIL-1"` on `docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md` shows the inline-label closure accounting (the `HC3-a … HC3-e close in V11f` / `NOCEIL-1 … NOCEIL-4` distributed paragraph) was added later at `2565ddd`. At that point `SNK` had been a live registered prefix for five days, yet `git show 2565ddd -- docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md` confirms the added paragraph names `CEIL`/`CIO`/`HC3`/`NOCEIL` but not `SNK`. The gap is the interaction of the two: `SNK` entered the corpus at `27b2576` without a matrix touch, and the matrix's parallel inline-label accounting authored at `2565ddd` omitted the already-live `SNK` prefix.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md`, add to the `## Governance REQ-IDs (GOV-*)` paragraph — alongside the existing `HC3`/`NOCEIL` closure sentences — a sentence accounting for the third GOV-16 inline-label prefix, recording that `SNK-a … SNK-k` are sub-rows of `SLSH-4` and are closed where `SLSH-4` already maps. Candidate text:

> The `SNK-a … SNK-k` inline labels on `slash-invocation.md` (the per-`kind` top-level `Err` note templates, registered under GOV-16) are sub-obligations of `SLSH-4` and close at [`V12b`](./V12b-top-level-err-chain.md), where the *Numbered REQ-IDs* row `SLSH-3, SLSH-4, SLSH-5 | V12b` already maps.

This makes all three registered GOV-16 inline-label prefixes (`HC3`, `NOCEIL`, `SNK`) accounted for in the matrix. Edge case for the implementer: do not add `SNK` to the *Code-keyed obligation areas (no numbered REQ-IDs)* table — that table is for obligations carrying no numbered `PREFIX-N` REQ-ID, and `SNK` rides on the numbered `SLSH-4`; the closure note belongs in the Governance prose to mirror how `HC3`/`NOCEIL` are handled.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The coverage matrix's GOV-16 inline-label accounting is incomplete: of the three registered inline-label prefixes, only `SNK` lacks a closure note, so a reader auditing inline-label coverage cannot confirm `SNK` is closed and has no matrix anchor recording that `V12b` owns it. No implementer is blocked — `V12b`/`V12b-T` already exercise `SNK-a..k` — but a future `SNK` row addition or an `SLSH-4` re-scope would have no matrix entry to keep it traceable.

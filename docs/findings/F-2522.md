## Finding

`frontmatter-fields-a.md#frnt-2` and `#frnt-3` are normative REQ-IDs reading "Authors and implementers MUST use the term `callable set` / `.loom callable` … synonyms to be avoided." GOV-18 (`#gov-18-binding-prohibition`) forbids binding a third party such as an implementer's own prose vocabulary, and the MUSTs have no mechanical conformance oracle, so they cannot be falsified.

**History:** The "Authors and implementers MUST use the term … synonyms to be avoided" terminology obligation was first authored in `fec0870` (2026-05-06), before any binding-scope rule existed. `64cdc60` (2026-05-26) added GOV-18, whose `#gov-18-binding-prohibition` clause retroactively reclassified third-party-binding obligations as out-of-scope — at which point the wording became a standing defect. `5569ceb` (2026-06-28, F-1640) then relocated the terminology MUSTs out of the glossary and promoted them into the numbered REQ-IDs FRNT-2/FRNT-3 (and the sibling SUBS-2), carrying the "Authors and implementers MUST" framing verbatim rather than conforming it to GOV-18, hardening the pre-existing defect into the normative body. The second, independent site — the `system:` seam blockquote — entered separately and earlier in `09c42c0` (2026-05-05, "resolve 'Surface-extension Seam annotations carry V1 obligations from non-goals page'") and has duplicated its neighbouring *Path grammar* / *Parser entry point* / *Parse errors* prose since that authoring.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Reframe both REQ-IDs in place on `frontmatter-fields-a.md` as properties of the spec corpus's own prose rather than directives to people, keeping the identifiers and anchors. For example: *"**FRNT-2.** *Callable-set terminology.* Within the spec corpus, the per-loom resolved tool list is named `callable set` (or, with an explicit scoping modifier, `frontmatter callable set` / `loom's declared callable set`); the bare phrases `tools: set`, `tool set`, `loom's tools`, and `available tools` do not appear as its name, because they erode the no-inheritance invariant. The host Pi session's tools are the distinct `ambient tool set`, which a callable set excludes."* Apply the parallel restatement to FRNT-3 (`.loom callable` is the corpus name; `registered loom` / `registered subagent loom` / `registered-loom call` / `loom callee` do not appear as its name; the rename touches no code identifiers, extensions, diagnostic codes, frontmatter fields, or wire formats). Remove the "Authors and implementers MUST use" subject from both. Because `FRNT` is a REQ-ID prefix bound to `frontmatter/` by the [prefix table](../governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-a.md#req-id-prefix-table), keep both on this page; this is a GOV-8 *Pure rewording* (modal restatement of the same obligation), not a split or deletion — the identifiers and anchors stay.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

FRNT-2/FRNT-3 are normative MUSTs the corpus's own GOV-18 forbids (they bind "implementers"), so they stand as internal self-consistency defects a GOV-18 audit flags, and they give the false impression of an enforceable conformance check that no runtime can discharge. The `system:` seam blockquote is redundant prose that enlarges the maintenance surface (a future wording change must be kept in sync across the blockquote and the paragraphs it restates). Neither defect changes any runtime behaviour or diagnostic; implementers can still produce a working system.

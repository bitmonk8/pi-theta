## Problem

`V14a` (`docs/plan_topics/V14a-tool-calls.md`) carries five structurally distinct
sub-concerns in a single leaf — the parse-time argument checks, the closed
`CodeToolError` enum, the accepted-path return-type table, the `.loom`-callable failure
surface, and the five byte-exact accepted-path `execute()` envelope-lowering rules — plus
the `cka-47` checkpoint facet and the `ERR-13` live-carrier witness, behind an
eleven-assertion Ships-when gate. The envelope-lowering mechanics are a self-contained,
independently-testable block: the coverage matrix already isolates them as their own
obligation row `cka-46`, and they can be cycled red-green against a stub `execute()`
without any of the other concerns. Bundling them into `V14a` forces unrelated concerns
through one red-green cycle, against the §3 convention that each leaf be "the smallest
feature that can ship and be tested independently."

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** `77f2e88` — "pi-loom plan: resolve \"code-side tool-call envelope-lowering MUSTs unanchored\"" (2026-06-18)
**History:** The plan corpus is git-tracked. `git log --follow` over `docs/plan_topics/V14a-tool-calls.md` shows that commit `5a1915d` (2026-06-17, "resolve \"V14a bundles eight independently-testable concerns\"") had reduced `V14a` to a **parse-only** leaf — peeling the off-surface routing, host-denial, ceiling-#4, and swallowing-handler concerns into `V14c`–`V14f` — leaving a **Ships when** that asserted only the argument codes, the closed enum, the return-type rows, and the `.loom`-callable failure surface. The runtime `execute()` envelope-lowering phase was then re-introduced into `V14a` by commit `77f2e88` (`git show 77f2e88` shows the five-sub-case `host-interfaces-core.md` bullet and the matching **Ships when** clause added), which anchored the previously un-anchored envelope-lowering MUSTs onto the parse leaf rather than a dedicated lowering leaf — re-creating the two-phase coupling. The later `cka-47` facet (`849d926`) and ERR-13 witness (`7bd65a7`) added load but are not the source of the compile-time/runtime split; that coupling entered solely at `77f2e88`.

## Solution approach

Split the accepted-path `execute()` envelope-lowering mechanics (the `cka-46` obligation
block — the five byte-exact rules) out of `V14a` into a new `V14<letter>` / `V14<letter>-T`
leaf pair owning that block and testable against a stub `execute()`. Remove the
envelope-lowering Tests bullet and Ships-when clause from `docs/plan_topics/V14a-tool-calls.md`
and `docs/plan_topics/V14a-T-tool-calls.md`. Retarget the `cka-46` closing-leaf cell in
`docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md` from `V14a` to the new impl leaf, update
`docs/plan_topics/V14b-tool-calls-parallel-batch.md` / `…V14b-T…` Deps so the parallel
batch's per-call lowering depends on the new leaf, and add the new pair to the V14 slice
listing in `docs/plan.md`.

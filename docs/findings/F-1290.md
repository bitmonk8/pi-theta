## Problem

The bullet anchored `#strict-capability-absence-pin` in `audit-target-categories.md` carries a MUST that extends the SDK surface-inventory literal-read assertion — the *absence-under-the-probed-name* arm that completes the rename-detection arm owned by `version-bump-triggers.md` step 7. That assertion is owned by `version-bump-step2.md` step 2(a) (`#bump-step-2-positive`) and consumed by step 7; it never participates in surface-categorisation, so it is subject-matter foreign to `audit-target-categories.md`, whose remit is the closed enumeration of category-(1)/(2)/(3) surfaces. The two plan leaves that consume the arm (V18c, V18c-T) cite a categories page as the spec source for their absence fixture, and step 2(a)'s literal-read assertion description never names the absence arm at all.

## Solution approach

Move the literal-read assertion extension out of `audit-target-categories.md` into the inventory hub `inventory-audit-intro.md`, alongside the *Inventory-closure audit* section (`#sdk-cap-inventory-closure-audit`) that already pins `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY`'s relationship to the step-2(a) literal-read assertion; co-locating on `capability-probe.md` step (d)/(e) is an acceptable alternative home. Add a forward cross-reference from `version-bump-step2.md` step 2(a)'s literal-read assertion description (`#bump-step-2-positive`) to the relocated absence arm so both arms are enumerated at the assertion's own definition rather than only via the binder absence-pin chain. Retain on `audit-target-categories.md` the on-topic note that the `strict-capability-probe` inventory entry kind is `path`-exempt.

## Problem

The `#concurrency` paragraph in `tool-calls.md` and its mirror in `implementation-notes.md`'s "Per-invocation single-threaded execution" bullet justify sharing the per-query compiled-validator cache across concurrent sibling invocations by asserting that compiled validators are immutable post-compile. That justification is false: a compiled validator mutates its own per-call state (notably `.errors`) on every call, so immutability is not what makes sharing safe. The actual safety property — that the synchronous `validate()` call runs to completion without yielding the event loop, so sibling invocations serialise rather than interleave — is left unstated, which leaves future changes (async validation, stateful-iterator validators, worker-thread sharing) without the real load-bearing invariant to check against.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit-introduction
**Introducing commits:** `7ce0ffa` (2026-05-04, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Tool registry mutation during execution and model-driven parallel tool calls\"")
**History:** Walked `git log -L` over the `<a id="concurrency"></a>` paragraph of `docs/spec_topics/tool-calls.md` (predecessor `spec_topics/tool-calls.md` before the `docs/`-relocation commit `31ff060`) and over the "single-threaded execution" bullet of `docs/spec_topics/implementation-notes.md`. The pre-7ce0ffa Concurrency paragraph (originating in the split commit `fecb504`) acknowledged Pi's parallel-tool mode but said nothing about the schema-validator cache or its concurrency safety; the implementation-notes bullet at the same time only stated "Single-threaded execution … no shared mutable state across loom invocations". Commit `7ce0ffa` resolved the "Tool registry mutation … parallel tool calls" finding and, in the same diff, added the two parallel sentences — to `tool-calls.md` ("The schema cache … is read-mostly and safe to share across concurrent invocations; compiled validators are immutable post-compile…") and to `implementation-notes.md` ("The schema-validator cache and the file-watcher are shared across invocations but are read-mostly: compiled validators are immutable post-compile…"). No later commit revised the justification clause; subsequent edits to the paragraph (`a6d82db`, `0e12140`, `7159420`, `f5e89f4`) only renamed `V1` → `loom 1.0`, added the parallel-batch settle-all sentences, and reflowed cross-references. The defect therefore entered the corpus at the moment a concurrency-safety claim was first authored — the resolution that needed to say "validators may be shared" reached for the convenient but inaccurate "they're immutable" framing instead of naming run-to-completion of the synchronous call as the load-bearing fact.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the validator-sharing justification in `tool-calls.md`'s `#concurrency` paragraph so it grounds cross-invocation cache sharing on the run-to-completion of the synchronous `validate(value)` call — which never yields the event loop, so sibling invocations cannot interleave inside a single call and observe each other's per-call mutable state — rather than on validator immutability post-compile. Mirror the same correction in `implementation-notes.md`'s "Per-invocation single-threaded execution" bullet, keeping its existing cross-link to `tool-calls.md#concurrency`.

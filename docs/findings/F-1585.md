## Finding

The normative constraint that none of the three transient-toast paths (chokidar watcher throw, unrecoverable settings I/O exception, internal extension invariant violation) writes the `LoomRegistry` drain-state tag lives only in `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md`, inside the **Transient toasts (auxiliary)** paragraph. The rule is load-bearing for the `session_shutdown` handler-entry short-circuit predicate: a toast-surface tag write would cause a subsequent `session_shutdown` to short-circuit on arm (b) or (c) of `readDrainState` and skip teardown entirely.

The canonical write-surface contract for the tag ŌĆö `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/drain-state-contract.md` ŌĆö enumerates the four-method call surface (`drain`, `initDrainStateTag`, `markRuntimeDegraded`, `readDrainState`) and carries a *Non-normative editorial convention* that "no other writer or reader of either field is permitted", but never names the three toast paths and never restates the MUST NOT as a normative obligation on the toast surface. An implementer reading `drain-state-contract.md` to audit every site that may write the tag therefore sees only the four-method surface plus the editorial convention; they do not see the normative carve-out forbidding writes from the three concrete toast-emission sites, nor the consequence (a later `session_shutdown` short-circuiting and skipping teardown) that justifies it. The same gap appears the other direction: `diagnostic-shape.md` already cross-links to the drain-state contract for the short-circuit predicate, but the drain-state contract carries no reciprocal link back to the toast-paths enumeration.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Move the normative MUST NOT to `drain-state-contract.md` (the canonical contract page for who may write the tag) and leave only a forward-link in `diagnostic-shape.md`'s **Transient toasts (auxiliary)** paragraph. Concretely:

- In `drain-state-contract.md`, immediately after the four-method enumeration and the *Non-normative editorial convention* paragraph that already pins the closed write surface, add a normative sub-paragraph (e.g. *Transient-toast write prohibition*) stating that none of the three transient-toast emission paths enumerated in `diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md` ┬¦ Transient toasts (auxiliary) ŌĆö the chokidar watcher throw, the unrecoverable settings I/O exception not covered by a `loom/load/settings-*` code, and the internal extension invariant violation ŌĆö writes `drainStateTag`. State the rationale inline (a toast-surface write would set the tag to one of the two arms of `readDrainState`'s enumeration and cause the next `session_shutdown` invocation to short-circuit via the handler-entry predicate `snapshot.drained === true || snapshot.tag !== undefined`, skipping the full five-sub-step teardown sequence) and cite the **idempotent** clause's predicate as the consuming site.
- In `diagnostic-shape.md`'s **Transient toasts (auxiliary)** paragraph, replace the existing "None of the three arms writes the `LoomRegistry` drain-state tag ŌĆ”" sentence with a forward-link to the new `drain-state-contract.md` sub-paragraph, keeping the three per-mode post-failure-subsystem-state enumerations (the chokidar/watcher-terminated, settings-cache-retained, and invariant-violation arms) on this page since they describe the toast surface's own observable behaviour and belong with the mode enumeration.

This placement makes the MUST NOT discoverable from the contract page (the audit-surface a write-side implementer reads first) while keeping a single source of truth, satisfying the existing closed-write-surface convention on the same page and avoiding the dual-authority hazard that a duplicate-with-link would create. The implementer-relevant edge case to flag in the new sub-paragraph: the rule is uniform across both arms of the closed two-arm tag set (`"shutting-down"` and `"degraded-needs-reload"`), since the short-circuit predicate keys on tag-presence rather than tag value.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer auditing the drain-state write surface from `drain-state-contract.md` alone does not encounter the toast-paths prohibition and may believe the four-method enumeration is the only constraint on tag writes. The normative MUST NOT does exist in the corpus, so a careful implementer reading `diagnostic-shape.md` will find it; the risk is a discoverability gap rather than an outright omission, and the four-method closed surface plus the editorial convention together still steer a conformant implementation away from toast-surface writes.

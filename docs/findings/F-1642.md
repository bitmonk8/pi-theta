## Finding

The **Trust boundary** bullet under *Non-functional requirements* in `docs/spec_topics/overview-and-orientation.md` is declared *informative orientation* by both the section preamble ("The bullets are *informative orientation*: each one forward-links the topic page that owns the normative contract") and by GOV-30, which treats `spec.md`-style aggregator paragraphs as carrying no normative weight in their own right. In practice the bullet runs roughly 700 words and reproduces the substance of `pi-integration-contract/trust-boundary.md` (PIC-52) inline rather than forwarding to it:

- "The runtime itself interposes no additional access channels (sandbox, capability filter, mediated proxy) …" — the PIC-52 MUST NOT, restated in the orientation bullet.
- "Any callable a loom declares exposes the full underlying capability of that callable to the loom's model; the runtime applies no privilege classification or filtering. `bash` and `read` are illustrative — the rule does not depend on a callable being deemed 'privileged.'" — the PIC-52 model-reachable-set rule plus normative-flavoured editorial commentary, neither anchor-cited.
- "The host-side denial-surface rule — how denials of filesystem, network, or Pi-API access reach loom code through the tool that issued the request, and that silent success on denial is forbidden — is owned by [Pi Integration Contract — No additional access channels]" — names the PIC-52 owner but restates the rule in the same sentence, so a reader who never follows the link still receives the constraint.
- A several-sentence sub-paragraph on the build-time SDK surface-inventory presupposition (the "presence check on the seven named SDK capabilities … detection of any such facet is routed to the per-bump editorial-review item") imports detail from `pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md` and `capability-probe.md` that no other Scope/NFR bullet on this page imports for its own topic.

An implementer who reads only this bullet can build a conforming runtime without ever visiting `trust-boundary.md`. That is the failure mode the *informative orientation* declaration exists to rule out. Beyond the labelling contradiction, the duplicated prose creates a dual-authority surface: PIC-52's wording on `trust-boundary.md` and the bullet's wording on `overview-and-orientation.md` must be edited in lock-step under GOV-30, but GOV-30 reserves no mechanical witness for semantic drift between the two (the *Pure-rewording* undecidability disclaimer applies), so any future amendment to PIC-52 risks silently leaving the orientation bullet stale.

Sibling NFR bullets on the same page show the intended shape: **Source-language stability** authors its normative content as a forwarding paragraph naming GOV-15 + carve-out owners; **Runtime observability** is a tight three-sentence pointer to the channel owner, the always-log set, and the deferred telemetry slot. The Trust boundary bullet is an outlier.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Reduce the Trust boundary bullet to an orientation pointer of roughly the same weight as **Runtime observability**: name the privilege model, forward-link the canonical owner, and forward-link the non-goal — without restating the constraints. Concretely:

- Keep the opening sentence ("loom 1.0 looms execute inside the Pi extension-host process at full Node host-process privilege; loom 1.0 imposes no loom-level sandbox") as the orientation kernel.
- Replace the body — the access-channel prohibition, the `tools:` callable-set framing, the privilege-classification clause, the `bash`/`read` editorial commentary, and the denial-surface sentence — with one forwarding sentence: "The runtime's *No additional access channels* contract — the prohibition on inserting a privilege layer, the `tools:` callable-set scoping, and the host-side denial-surface rule — is owned by [Pi Integration Contract — Trust boundary](./pi-integration-contract/trust-boundary.md#pic-52)."
- Keep the closing pointer to the non-goal: "A per-loom capability model is **out of scope for loom 1.0**; the disposition is owned by the **No per-loom sandbox or capability model** bullet under [Future Considerations — loom 1.0 non-goals](./future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md#loom-1-0-non-goals)."
- Excise the SDK surface-inventory presupposition sub-paragraph entirely from this bullet. Before deletion, confirm `pi-integration-contract/trust-boundary.md` already states the *absence of per-extension privilege scoping* presupposition and routes detection of any new facet to the per-bump editorial-review item on `version-bump-step2.md`; if it does not, migrate that one sentence to `trust-boundary.md` (option-dependent edit on that file) and replace the sub-paragraph here with the same `trust-boundary.md` forward-link rather than restating it.

Edge cases for the fixer:

- The `tools:` callable-set scoping language overlaps with PIC-17 (active-set allowlist gating) and with the **No per-loom sandbox or capability model** non-goal. Forward to `trust-boundary.md#pic-52`; do not re-cite PIC-17 from the orientation bullet — `trust-boundary.md` already links to it.
- The "`bash` and `read` are illustrative — the rule does not depend on a callable being deemed 'privileged'" disclaimer is editorial commentary on PIC-52 framing, not a separate rule. Drop it from the bullet; if it carries reviewer value, fold it into `trust-boundary.md` as a parenthetical on PIC-52 itself.
- GOV-30 registers this NFR cluster under the *multi-source-page* shape (the "three cross-cutting non-functional-requirement dispositions"); trimming the bullet does not change the count or the source set, so no GOV-30 / GOV-31 lock-step edit is triggered.
- Sibling **Source-language stability** is a longer bullet than the proposed Trust boundary shape; that is acceptable — Source-language stability's length is driven by the equivalence-relation definition (observables a/b/c, byte-equivalence, placeholder-rendering normalisation) that has no single canonical owner outside GOV-15, whereas Trust boundary *does* have a canonical owner at PIC-52. The asymmetry is intended.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The duplication does not block implementation — PIC-52 already exists on `trust-boundary.md` and is the only site the plan leaves cite — but it weakens the orientation-vs-normative discipline GOV-30 is meant to enforce: future PRs that amend PIC-52 are not mechanically prompted to update the orientation bullet, so the two surfaces will drift. A reader using the orientation bullet as a self-contained summary may also miss subsequent PIC-52 refinements (e.g. the `cka-18` denial-surface mapping, the `kind: "code_tool"` envelope) that the bullet does not currently restate.

## Problem

The `H5a` standing transitive-completeness arm reconciles every closing-leaf cell in `coverage-matrix.md` against `H5b`'s `Deps.`, and its `Adds.` text says it "excludes … the *IMPL* row's pure back-references." But the IMPL row embeds leaf-ID tokens in both its left-cell obligation-area prose and its right-cell closing-leaf column, with no machine-readable marker distinguishing a back-reference token from a forward-closure token; `V7a` is dual-classed, appearing both as a pure back-reference in the left cell and as a legitimate closing leaf in the right cell. The exclusion clause's own example list (`H3a`, `H1a`) is narrower than the IMPL row's actual "Pure back-references" list (it omits `multi-error batching → V7a`), so an implementer cannot reconstruct the suppression set — and if the arm reads only the right-hand column, the exclusion clause has no referent at all. The arm's behaviour on the one row the plan singles out for exclusion is therefore not determinable from the plan text, and the same ambiguity is duplicated in `conventions.md` *Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance*.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commit:** `08bd641` (2026-06-11) — "pi-loom plan: resolve \"H5b coverage Deps completeness has no mechanical backstop\""
**History:** The plan corpus is git-tracked. The transitive-completeness arm and its "excludes … the *IMPL* row's pure back-references" exclusion clause entered the corpus wholesale in `08bd641`, which authored the arm in `H5a` and the lockstep restatement in `conventions.md`. The clause carried the same under-specified `H3a`/`H1a` example list from inception (`git show 08bd641 -- H5a-closing-gate-automation.md`). The IMPL row whose two-cell layout the clause references predates it by ten hours: `249cec5` (2026-06-11, "resolve \"implementation-notes.md (IMPL) unmapped in coverage matrix\"") is an ancestor of `08bd641` and introduced the IMPL row with leaf-ID tokens in both cells. Later edits to the arm — `3a84938` (range expansion) and `c0a983a` (named-singleton example) — left the IMPL-row exclusion clause byte-unchanged, so the defect has been present in every revision of the arm since `08bd641`.

## Solution approach

In `H5a`'s `Adds.` transitive-completeness paragraph, pin the arm's read scope so it reconciles only the right-hand closing-leaf column of each `coverage-matrix.md` row and never reads leaf-ID tokens out of the left-cell obligation-area prose. With the read scope pinned, strike the "and the *IMPL* row's pure back-references (e.g. …)" clause, since it then names a suppression the arm does not perform. Apply the identical change to the lockstep restatement in `conventions.md` *REQ-ID discipline* → *Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance*.

## Solution constraints

- Retain the `<new>` placeholder-row exclusion in both `H5a` and `conventions.md`; only the IMPL-row pure-back-references clause is the defect — the `<new>` rows have no real leaf in their closing-leaf column and remain a genuine exclusion.
- Do not introduce a per-token back-reference annotation scheme on `coverage-matrix.md`.

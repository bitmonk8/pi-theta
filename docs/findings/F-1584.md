## Problem

`LoomRegistry.readDrainState` exposes a single closed three-arm dispatch enumeration (PIC-29: (a) steady-state dispatch, (b) `"shutting-down"` note, (c) `"degraded-needs-reload"` note), but its conformance fixtures are split across two files with no top-level map tying them to the arm enumeration. `drain-state-contract.md` § "Acceptance criteria — `(drained: true, tag: undefined)` → arm (b)" owns the arm-(b) routing fixtures, while `session-only-degraded-state.md` § "Acceptance criteria" owns fixtures (a)–(m); the two sections cross-reference each other only at the level of individual fixtures. A test author opening either file alone cannot determine whether PIC-29's three arms are fully covered, and arm (a) (steady-state dispatch) has no dedicated routing fixture in either section. The split is partly load-bearing: the session-only fixtures (a)–(m) are gated pending the host-prerequisites clause-(a) contradiction while the arm-(b) fixtures are non-gated, and the page gate scopes by clause, not by file.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** f5e89f4 (`docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set`, 2026-06-04), 8008b91 (`pi-loom spec-plan: fix F-1217 — add acceptance-criteria/fixture blocks for four Shard-08 MUSTs`, 2026-06-25)
**History:** Both `drain-state-contract.md` and `session-only-degraded-state.md` were created by the spec-split commit f5e89f4. The session-only acceptance set was established on the new page shortly after (its throwing-`readDrainState` fixtures (l)/(m) were added by 2775382, 2026-06-05, resolving "Two throwing-readDrainState MUSTs have no acceptance criteria"). The cross-file partition was created much later by 8008b91 (`git show 8008b91 -- drain-state-contract.md` confirms the `## Acceptance criteria — \`(drained: true, tag: undefined)\` → arm (b)` heading was net-new in that commit), which added a second acceptance-criteria section in `drain-state-contract.md` for the arm-(b) MUST without consolidating it with, or cross-indexing it against, the pre-existing session-only set. The defect is the interaction: neither commit is wrong in isolation, but together they leave the single PIC-29 arm enumeration with two unconnected fixture homes and no index.

## Solution approach

Add a consolidating acceptance index for the `readDrainState` arm enumeration adjacent to PIC-29 in `drain-state-contract.md` (the non-gated owner of the arm enumeration), mapping each PIC-29 arm to its covering fixture IDs, owning file, and gating status, while leaving the fixtures themselves where they live. For arm (a), the index records that steady-state-dispatch routing is currently asserted only indirectly and forward-links to the dedicated arm-(a) routing fixture obligation owned by F-1685 (PIC-30). Add a reciprocal pointer from the `session-only-degraded-state.md` § "Acceptance criteria" preamble back to this index.

## Solution constraints

- The index MUST restate, not relax, the per-clause implementation gate: rows covering the session-only fixtures (a)–(m) carry the gated marker even though the index lives on the non-gated page; do not collapse gated and non-gated fixtures into one undifferentiated set.
- Out of scope: the arm-(a) / all-three-throw routing fixture owned by F-1685 — point at it, do not author it here.

## Finding

The `Deps.` field of both `V4e` and `V4e-T` carries the explanatory parenthetical:

> (`V5e` owns the loom-owned depth walk whose ceiling-#4 slash-load `params` routing decision and depth-6 breach `ERR-16` wraps)

The trailing relative clause "whose … `ERR-16` wraps" is structurally ambiguous. The intended reading is *`V5e` owns the depth walk; `ERR-16` wraps that walk's routing decision and breach*. But "whose … `ERR-16` wraps" can also be parsed with the ownership inverted — as though `ERR-16` (or its wrapping) is the antecedent of "whose", i.e. that the walk belongs to / is produced by the ERR-16 path. The two parses describe opposite producer/consumer relationships between the `V5e` depth walk and the `ERR-16` slash-load `params` routing.

The `Adds.` field states the relationship unambiguously ("the depth-6 breach it wraps is detected by `V5e`'s live loom-owned depth walk … binding `V5e` via its `Deps`"), so the canonical intent is recoverable; the defect is confined to the condensed `Deps` restatement, which reads in isolation as a Deps-line annotation.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-by-later-edit
**Introducing commit:** `887c440` (2026-06-17) — "pi-loom plan: resolve \"V4e declares no dependency on V5e depth-walk producer for ERR-16\""
**History:** The `V5e` dependency edge and its explanatory parenthetical were added together in `887c440`, which closed a prior finding that `V4e` declared no dependency on the `V5e` depth-walk producer. `git show 887c440` confirms the ambiguous relative clause "`V5e` owns the loom-owned depth walk whose … `ERR-16` wraps" entered the `Deps` field in that single commit; it was not present at file inception (`c6a664e`). The later commit `324813d` (the `ERR-7` reload split) trimmed the co-located `V9b`/`V10d` clauses from the same parenthetical but left the `V5e` clause and its ambiguity intact. The defect is a wording artefact of the producer-dependency fix, not a pre-existing condition.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V4e-pre-evaluation-failures.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V4e-T-pre-evaluation-failures.md`, replace the `Deps` parenthetical clause

`(`V5e` owns the loom-owned depth walk whose ceiling-#4 slash-load `params` routing decision and depth-6 breach `ERR-16` wraps)`

with the split-sentence form that pins the ownership direction:

`(`V5e` owns the loom-owned depth walk; `ERR-16` wraps that walk's ceiling-#4 slash-load `params` routing decision and depth-6 breach)`

Apply the identical edit to both leaves so the paired pages stay in lockstep.

Edge case: the related placement finding (see Related Findings) proposes deleting this `Deps` parenthetical outright in favour of a bare ID list; if that fix is taken, the parenthetical disappears and this rewrite is moot. Resolve the placement question first — if the parenthetical survives in any form, the clause must use the split-sentence wording above.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader who takes the inverted parse mis-models which leaf produces the depth walk and which consumes it, but the bare `Deps` identifier list (`… V5e`) and the `Adds.` prose both remain correct, so an implementer can still wire the dependency and ship a working leaf. The cost is reader friction, not divergent implementations.

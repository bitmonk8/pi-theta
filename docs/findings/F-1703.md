## Problem

The *Failure-surface contract* (`audit-failure-surface-contract`) closes the mandatory negative-test fixture-assertion list at exactly two assertions — (a) the discriminator token matches the `audit/<class>/<family>/<symptom>` shape, and (b) the emitted `<family>` token routes correctly — and never names the `proposed-resolution` field. The adjacent *Per-family record-shape table* pins normative per-family `proposed-resolution` value ranges and declares that a record carrying a value outside its family range violates the rule. The two clauses do not meet: a conformant audit can emit the literal `<n/a>` sentinel as `proposed-resolution` on every violation record and still pass its own pinned fixtures, leaving the per-family-range rule with no test surface. Two implementers honouring only the pinned fixtures diverge on `proposed-resolution`, and CI parsers a contributor relies on to route a violation to the right step-2(b) arm break silently against the `<n/a>` variant.

The fixture-assertion list was closed at (a)/(b) at first authoring and the per-family content ranges were never folded into it.

## Solution approach

Extend the mandatory fixture-assertion list in the *Failure-surface contract* to add a third assertion binding the emitted `proposed-resolution` value to the per-family range pinned by the *Per-family record-shape table*, and update the closing "PIC pins these two fixture assertions" pin to match. Update the **Tests** bullets of plan leaves `V18b-T` and `V18b` to require the off-inventory-reference fixture and the family-(4)-shape fixture each assert the new predicate on the emitted `proposed-resolution`.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the per-`<symptom>` fixture-floor clauses (owned by F-1597 and F-1635) and the family-(4) `symbol` range (owned by F-1599) in the same paragraph.
- The new assertion MUST NOT forbid the `<n/a>` sentinel on infrastructure-failure or non-empty-scan-canary records, where it remains the pinned `proposed-resolution` value.
- The assertion MUST bind only per-family range membership, not the exact `proposed-resolution` paraphrase text, which remains leaf-owned per the *Wire serialisation* clause.

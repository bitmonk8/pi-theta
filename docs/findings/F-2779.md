## Finding

`V9g` ("Session-shutdown teardown and emission isolation") carries two concern clusters in one leaf. The first is the five-sub-step fixed teardown sequence: abort each in-flight `loomAbort`, await every in-flight entry's `disposeBarrier` via `Promise.allSettled` bounded by `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS` (`cka-31`), emit `loom/runtime/reload-teardown-timeout` at the cap, and synthesise the `CNCL-4` session-shutdown abort reason. The second is the diagnostic-emission-isolation contract `PIC-24…PIC-28`: per-emission `try`/`catch` wrap (`PIC-24`), the bare-`code` / two-token / three-token serialiser-throw fallback forms (`PIC-25`), the construction-site catch-arm self-wrap (`PIC-26`), the handler-isolation swallow of a throw out of `console.error` (`PIC-27`), and invocation-site count semantics (`PIC-28`) — a sub-surface that `diagnostic-emission-isolation.md` carries its own `## Acceptance criteria` block for (fixtures (a)/(b)/(c)).

These are distinct, independently red-testable failure modes: the teardown sequence is about ordering, await-cap, and cancellation-reason fidelity; the emission isolation is about hardening the handler's `console.error` sites against serialiser and stdio throws so they never unwind teardown. The coupling is real but the per-emission resilience paths can be exercised in isolation from the sequence proper.

This is a sizing/atomicity observation, not a build break. The leaf is implementable as written; the concern is that the two clusters give a `plan-diff` reviewer (and an implementer) a larger, two-themed surface than a single coherent leaf.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

If shipped unchanged the leaf is still buildable, but the two unrelated concern clusters present a single oversized critique surface: a `plan-diff-fix-loop` pass against `V9g` sees both the teardown-ordering theme and the five-clause emission-isolation theme at once, enlarging the diff any one fix produces and the surface the next pass's lenses attack. No spec rule goes unclosed and no gate misfires.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Forward-dependent emission-isolation leaf

- **Approach.** Keep `V9g`/`V9g-T` as the teardown-sequence core (five sub-steps, the `Promise.allSettled` await cap `cka-31`, `loom/runtime/reload-teardown-timeout`, the `loom/runtime/cancelled-by-session-shutdown` per-invocation emission, and `CNCL-4`), with the handler emitting its diagnostics. Carve the `PIC-24…PIC-28` hardening of those `console.error` sites into a new `<new>` leaf (with paired `<new>-T`) that lists `V9g` in its `Deps`.
- **Plan edits.** Move the `diagnostic-emission-isolation.md (PIC-24, PIC-25, PIC-26, PIC-27, PIC-28)` Tests bullet (and the `DIAG-1` serialiser-throw clause) from `V9g`/`V9g-T` to the new leaf; add `<new>` to `docs/plan.md` §V9 listing; retarget the `PIC-24 … PIC-28` closing-leaf cell at `coverage-matrix.md:62` from `V9g` to `<new>`; update the `cka-29` retirement note (`coverage-matrix.md:133`) so its "mapped to `V9g`" phrase names the new leaf.
- **Spec edits.** None.
- **Pros.** Clean per-cluster atomicity; each leaf reds for one theme.
- **Cons.** The `PIC-24…PIC-28` emission sites are physically interior to the `session_shutdown` handler `V9g` owns, so the new leaf draws a leaf boundary through one handler and must re-enter `V9g`'s internal emission points to harden them. Touches four files including the coverage matrix.
- **Risks.** Mis-retargeting the coverage-matrix row or the `cka-29` note could leave `PIC-24…PIC-28` mapped to a leaf not in `H5b.Deps`, reddening H5d.

### Option B — In-leaf cluster labelling

- **Approach.** Keep `V9g`/`V9g-T` as one leaf but structure its Adds and Tests into two clearly labelled clusters — a teardown-sequence cluster and an emission-isolation (`PIC-24…PIC-28`) cluster — so the implementer can land and red-test them in sequence without a structural split.
- **Plan edits.** Group the existing Adds prose and Tests bullets under two cluster labels within `V9g-session-shutdown.md` (and mirror in `V9g-T`); no coverage-matrix or plan.md listing changes.
- **Spec edits.** None.
- **Pros.** Zero structural churn; preserves the natural coupling (the emissions live inside the handler); no risk to the closing gates. Two-file touch.
- **Cons.** The leaf stays a two-themed unit; the atomicity improvement is documentary rather than structural.
- **Risks.** Minimal.

### Recommendation

Take **Option B**. The `PIC-24…PIC-28` obligations govern the `console.error` calls emitted from inside the very `session_shutdown` handler `V9g` builds, so a structural split forces the emission-isolation leaf to reach into `V9g`'s internals and gains little real separation. Given the advisory severity and the present-since-inception coupling, label the two clusters within `V9g`/`V9g-T` (a teardown-sequence cluster and an emission-isolation `PIC-24…PIC-28` cluster) so the implementer sequences them, and leave the coverage-matrix `PIC-24…PIC-28 → V9g` mapping intact. Watch that the `CNCL-4` and `cka-31` Ships-when criteria stay attached to the teardown cluster.

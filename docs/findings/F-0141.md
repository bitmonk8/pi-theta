## Finding

`V8a`'s Adds requires the `Checkpoint` seam to perform "macrotask yield for `loop-iter`, microtask otherwise," and `PIC-10` (in both `V8a` and `V8a-T`) asserts "the correct yield kind." Neither leaf states *how* the single macrotask turn is scheduled. The spec (`host-interfaces-services.md` PIC-10) likewise leaves the realisation to a conforming implementation, noting only that the seam is wired through the same constructor-injection skeleton as the other host seams. So the scheduling primitive is unpinned at the one place an implementer builds it.

The plan's surrounding constraints make this gap load-bearing rather than cosmetic. The *No globals, statics, singletons* ambient-primitive ban (`conventions.md`, enforced by the `H3a` scan) forbids any direct `setTimeout`/`clearTimeout` outside the `WallClock` adapter, so a raw `setTimeout(0)` macrotask yield in the checkpoint code is a banned ambient reference. PIC-10's stated rationale is *deterministic testability without depending on JS microtask scheduling*, which the rest of the corpus realises by routing time through the injected `Clock` seam and driving it with the `FakeClock` double (e.g. `V10b`/`V10c` debounce and per-read-deadline tests). An implementer who instead reaches for `setImmediate` produces a macrotask yield that is not ambient-banned but is not `FakeClock`-observable (so the `PIC-10` conformance assertion cannot drive it deterministically); one who reaches for `queueMicrotask` produces a microtask — the wrong kind entirely.

The only mechanism that satisfies all three constraints at once — macrotask kind, not ambient-banned, and `FakeClock`-observable — is routing the `loop-iter` yield through the injected `Clock` seam (`Clock.setTimeout`, owned by `V8d`, already present in `V8a`'s Deps). A prior review pass added the `V8d` Deps edge so the substrate is reachable, but neither `V8a` nor `V8a-T` states that the yield must travel through it, leaving the decisive detail to invention.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `V8a`'s Adds, state that the `loop-iter` macrotask yield is scheduled through the injected `Clock` seam (`Clock.setTimeout`, owned by `V8d`, already in `V8a` Deps) rather than any ambient or `setImmediate`/`queueMicrotask` primitive — e.g. replace "macrotask yield for `loop-iter`" with "macrotask yield for `loop-iter` scheduled via the injected `Clock` seam (`Clock.setTimeout`, `V8d`)". In the `PIC-10` Tests bullet of both `V8a` and `V8a-T`, state that the conformance test drives and observes the `loop-iter` macrotask turn through the `FakeClock` test double (mirroring the `FakeClock`-driven pattern in `V10b`/`V10c`), so "the correct yield kind" is deterministically assertable. Update `V8a`'s Ships-when to reference the `Clock`/`FakeClock`-observed macrotask yield. No new Deps edge is required (`V8d` is already declared); `V8d` and the `host-interfaces-services.md` PIC-10 contract are read-only for this fix.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two reasonable implementers diverge: one routes the `loop-iter` yield through the `Clock`/`FakeClock` seam and produces a deterministically-testable macrotask turn; another picks `setImmediate` (untestable under `FakeClock`) or `queueMicrotask` (wrong, microtask) — or `setTimeout` directly and trips the `H3a` ambient ban. `V17a`/`V17a-T` inherit the problem: their loop-iteration macrotask-yield assertion ("a signal flipped during a synchronous compute-bound loop body is observed before the next iteration") can only be written deterministically once the `V8a` scheduling substrate is pinned.

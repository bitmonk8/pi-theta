## Finding

On `pi-integration-contract/runtime-event-channel.md` the bare word "cascade" is used as a load-bearing term for three independent mechanisms, with no statement reconciling them:

1. **Top-level cascade** — a failure path: an unhandled `Err` reaching the slash-dispatch boundary (the failing loom has a slash caller and no invoke parent). Used in the display-rule prose ("when the failure cascades out as a top-level `Err` at the slash-dispatch boundary"), the `display`/content table row ("top-level cascade at the slash-dispatch boundary"), and the dedup/lifetime rules ("When a top-level cascade applies in prompt mode…").
2. **Cascade twin / cascade-twin re-emission** — a *payload-duplication* mechanism: the originating `RuntimeEvent` instance is emitted twice (once at origin, once at the boundary) under one `occurred_at`, collapsing under the dedup tuple. Named "cascade-twin re-emission" in PIC-1 clause (f) and cross-referenced from `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md`.
3. **Subagent-mode `display: false` cascades** — a routing case: a subagent-mode loom's top-level `Err` reached via `invoke(...)`/`.loom`-callable that lands in the subagent's private transcript. Used as an italicised noun phrase in the Delivery-surface paragraph, as "subagent-mode invoke-reached cascade" in the content table, and as "subagent-private cascade" in the fallback-chain step.

The same word thus denotes a failure *path*, a payload *duplication*, and a private-transcript *routing* case. A reader cross-referencing "the cascade" between sections (or following the `ceilings-3-and-4.md` cross-reference into clause (f)) must re-infer which sense applies at each site, and the italicised "Subagent-mode `display: false` cascades" reads ambiguously as a verb participle rather than a defined noun. There is no glossary entry for any "cascade" sense.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `4ccc072` (2026-05-07, "Masked ceilings…") and `7598f50` (2026-05-07, "CIO-6 hard-ceiling co-fire…") coined "cascade twin" in the hard-ceiling/PIC content; `78a2f94` (2026-06-04, "Misplaced sections within the pi-integration-contract pages") introduced both "top-level cascade" and the "`display: false` cascades" phrasing; `d75f2a8` (2026-06-06, "Runtime-event channel occurrence/origin, observable PIC-1 (e), mask-domain placement") consolidated all three senses onto `runtime-event-channel.md` and added the hyphenated "cascade-twin re-emission"; `0033898` (2026-06-09, "subagent-mode top-level Err at slash boundary unsurfaced") elaborated the subagent-private cascade prose.
**History:** No single commit introduced the overload. Three "cascade"-flavoured terms were coined in separately-authored fixes (2026-05-07 → 2026-06-09), each addressing its own concern without reconciling the word against the others; the collision became durable once `d75f2a8` brought all three senses onto one page. The fix therefore addresses the term-system on the page, not any single commit.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Disambiguate the three senses on `runtime-event-channel.md` in one pass:

- Rename **"cascade twin" / "cascade-twin re-emission"** to **"boundary re-emission"** everywhere it appears (PIC-1 clause (f), the Deduplication-and-lifetime bullet, and the `ceilings-3-and-4.md` cross-reference text). This names the mechanism by what it does (re-emitting the originating `RuntimeEvent` at the boundary) rather than by a metaphor that collides with the failure-path sense.
- Keep **"top-level cascade"** for the failure path reaching the slash-dispatch boundary; it is already the most widely used sense and reads clearly.
- Reword the italicised **"Subagent-mode `display: false` cascades"** noun phrase so it does not read as a participle — e.g. "Subagent-mode `display: false` routing" or "Subagent-private delivery" — and use the same chosen label for the table row ("subagent-mode invoke-reached cascade") and the fallback-chain step ("subagent-private cascade").
- Add a single glossary entry that names the failure-path sense ("top-level cascade") and explicitly states that boundary re-emission and the subagent-private routing case are distinct mechanisms, not synonyms, with cross-links to PIC-1 clause (f) and the Delivery-surface paragraph.

Edge case for the implementer: `ceilings-3-and-4.md` cross-references the obligation by the old "cascade-twin re-emission" name; update that cross-reference text in the same change so the anchor and the prose stay aligned.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Each mechanism is independently and precisely defined (the failure path by the display rule, the re-emission by clause (f), the subagent routing by the Delivery-surface paragraph), so implementers are unlikely to diverge on behaviour. The cost is comprehension: the shared word raises reading and cross-reference friction on a central PIC page, and the participial "cascades" usage invites a misread.

## Problem

On `runtime-event-channel.md` the bare word "cascade" is load-bearing for three independent mechanisms with no statement reconciling them: a failure *path* ("top-level cascade" — an unhandled `Err` reaching the slash-dispatch boundary), a payload *duplication* ("cascade twin" / "cascade-twin re-emission" in PIC-1 clause (f) — the originating `RuntimeEvent` emitted twice under one `occurred_at`), and a private-transcript *routing* case ("Subagent-mode `display: false` cascades" in the Delivery-surface paragraph). A reader cross-referencing "the cascade" between sections — or following the `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md` cross-reference into clause (f) — must re-infer which sense applies at each site, and the italicised "Subagent-mode `display: false` cascades" reads as a verb participle rather than a defined noun. No glossary entry exists for any "cascade" sense.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `4ccc072` (2026-05-07, "Masked ceilings…") and `7598f50` (2026-05-07, "CIO-6 hard-ceiling co-fire…") coined "cascade twin" in the hard-ceiling/PIC content; `78a2f94` (2026-06-04, "Misplaced sections within the pi-integration-contract pages") introduced both "top-level cascade" and the "`display: false` cascades" phrasing; `d75f2a8` (2026-06-06, "Runtime-event channel occurrence/origin, observable PIC-1 (e), mask-domain placement") consolidated all three senses onto `runtime-event-channel.md` and added the hyphenated "cascade-twin re-emission"; `0033898` (2026-06-09, "subagent-mode top-level Err at slash boundary unsurfaced") elaborated the subagent-private cascade prose.
**History:** No single commit introduced the overload. Three "cascade"-flavoured terms were coined in separately-authored fixes (2026-05-07 → 2026-06-09), each addressing its own concern without reconciling the word against the others; the collision became durable once `d75f2a8` brought all three senses onto one page. The fix therefore addresses the term-system on the page, not any single commit.

## Solution approach

Rename the payload-duplication sense ("cascade twin" / "cascade-twin re-emission") to a name that does not collide with the failure-path sense, everywhere it appears — PIC-1 clause (f) (`#pic-1`), the Deduplication-and-lifetime-rules bullet, and the cross-reference text on `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md`. Reword the italicised "Subagent-mode `display: false` cascades" noun phrase in the Delivery-surface paragraph so it no longer reads as a participle, and apply the same chosen label to the display/content table row and the fallback-chain step. Keep "top-level cascade" for the failure-path sense. Add a glossary entry on `glossary.md` distinguishing the three senses, cross-linked to PIC-1 clause (f) and the Delivery-surface paragraph.

## Solution constraints

- Rename and reword only; do not alter the verbatim-copy / copy-depth semantics of PIC-1 clause (f), which F-1714 owns.

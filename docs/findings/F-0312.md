## Finding

The closing sentence of the *No globals, statics, singletons* bullet in `conventions.md` asserts: "The mechanical scans (the `H2a` architectural test and the `H3a` identifier-keyed scan) and those two enumerated checklist items partition the space with no third gap between them." "Partition the space with no third gap" is a completeness claim — it states that every violation form of the rule is either statically caught by the two mechanical scans or covered by release-time residue-inspection checklist items 1 (closure-captured / lazy-cache / DI-container singletons) and 2 (indirect ambient reads).

The non-mechanical half of that claimed partition is a human reviewer reading `src/**`, and both checklist items open with an *enumeration of forms* — item 1 names closure capture, lazy module cache, and DI-container registration; item 2 names alias, destructure, computed access, helper wrapper, and re-export indirection. A reviewer who treats those enumerations as the closed catalogue of what to look for will miss a violation form that is neither statically detectable nor on either list — for example a global/ambient hazard reached by a route not enumerated under item 2, or a statefulness form outside item 1's three named singleton patterns. Best-effort manual review against an example list cannot deliver the gap-free partition the sentence asserts; the claim over-states the guarantee the release-time inspection actually provides.

(Each item's **Clean when** condition is in fact broader than its example list — item 1's "no module-level binding retains cross-invocation mutable state outside an injected seam" is a catch-all — but the sentence's "no third gap" framing rests on the example enumerations, not on the **Clean when** conditions, and presents human review as exhaustive rather than best-effort.)

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/conventions.md`, edit the closing sentence of the *No globals, statics, singletons* bullet so it no longer asserts an exhaustive gap-free partition over best-effort human review. State instead that the two mechanical scans (`H2a` architectural test, `H3a` identifier-keyed scan) catch the violation forms they can statically detect, and that release-time residue-inspection checklist items 1 and 2 are the best-effort human review of the residue those scans concede — governed by each item's **Clean when** condition rather than by its illustrative example list, so the items function as open-ended catch-alls over the rule's conceded residue rather than as a closed enumeration.

Concretely, replace the final sentence

> "The mechanical scans (the `H2a` architectural test and the `H3a` identifier-keyed scan) and those two enumerated checklist items partition the space with no third gap between them."

with text to the effect of

> "Between them, the mechanical scans (the `H2a` architectural test and the `H3a` identifier-keyed scan) catch the violation forms they can statically detect, and checklist items 1 and 2 are the best-effort human review of the residue they concede — each governed by its **Clean when** condition, not by its illustrative form list, so a residue form outside both the scans' reach and an item's named examples is still caught when it breaches that item's **Clean when** condition."

Edge case for the fixer: the over-claim recurs in checklist items 1 and 2's own **Clean when** phrasing only if those conditions are re-narrowed to the example lists; leave them as the broad catch-all conditions they already are. The lockstep risk of the *banned-primitive list itself* drifting out of date (a hazard outside the enumerated set entirely) is a separate concern owned by the sibling finding below, not this wording fix.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The over-claim can lead the loom 1.0 release-time reviewer to treat checklist items 1–2 as the exhaustive catalogue of violation forms and skip a hazard that is neither statically detected nor among the enumerated examples, letting a global/static/singleton or indirect ambient read reach `main` while the plan reports the space gap-free. No leaf is blocked and implementers still produce working leaves; the defect is a guidance gap in the review backstop, not a divergence in any leaf.

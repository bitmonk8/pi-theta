## Finding

GOV-30 (`governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md#gov-30`) opens "[`spec.md`](../../spec.md) carries no per-page REQ-ID prefix … Several `spec.md` paragraphs are *aggregators*" and pins its binding MUST as "a PR that adds, removes, or renames an item on a source page … MUST update the corresponding `spec.md` aggregator in the same commit." The bare link `[`spec.md`](../../spec.md)` resolves to `docs/spec.md`, which is a three-link table-of-contents hub containing only forward links to `overview-and-orientation.md`, `language-and-architecture.md`, and `session-model-and-appendix.md`. It hosts no aggregator paragraphs.

The aggregators GOV-30 governs actually live on the orientation topic pages split out of the former monolithic `spec.md`: the two Scope bullets, the three Host runtime preconditions, and the four-item hard-runtime-ceilings list are on `overview-and-orientation.md` (`#scope`, `#prerequisites`, `#hard-runtime-ceilings`, plus the `terminal-outcomes-aggregator`); the five `.warp` permitted top-level forms are on `imports.md#permitted-top-level-forms`; the eight loom 1.0 non-goals are on `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md`. The corpus already relies on a tacit convention in which the link text "`spec.md` — X" carries a URL that resolves to one of these topic pages (used at 40+ sites, e.g. `[`spec.md` — Hard ceilings](../overview-and-orientation.md#hard-runtime-ceilings)`), but that convention is nowhere defined, and GOV-30's own bare `../../spec.md` link does not follow it.

A maintainer who edits a source page and consults GOV-30 to find "the corresponding `spec.md` aggregator" to update in lock-step follows the link, lands on the three-link hub, and finds nothing to update — leaving the normative MUST literally undischarge­able as written and the actual lock-step target ambiguous between `spec.md` and the orientation pages.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** f5e89f4 (`docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set`, 2026-06-04), fe8aa5c (`pi-loom spec: resolve "GOV-12 bundles ≥3 independently-testable sub-obligations"`, 2026-06-05)
**History:** Before f5e89f4, `docs/spec.md` was a ~182-line monolith that carried the aggregator paragraphs directly (e.g. the `.warp` permitted-top-level-forms list tagged "*orientation aggregator per [Governance — GOV-12]*"); at that time the aggregator-lock-step rule (then GOV-12) correctly named `spec.md` as the host. f5e89f4 collapsed `spec.md` to a nine-line table-of-contents hub and relocated the aggregator content to `docs/spec/` section pages, but did not reconcile GOV-12's "`spec.md` aggregator" framing to the new split layout — this is where label and reality diverged. One day later, fe8aa5c retired GOV-12 and authored its successor GOV-30, re-introducing the stale "`spec.md` aggregator paragraphs" framing and the bare `[`spec.md`](../../spec.md)` link verbatim against the already-collapsed hub rather than the new host pages. 81bf52a (2026-06-11) subsequently renamed `docs/spec/` → `docs/spec_topics/` and left the framing untouched. `git log -S 'id="gov-30"'` and `git log -S 'spec.md\` aggregator'` confirm the framing has carried the defect since fe8aa5c; `git log -L '1,20:docs/spec.md'` confirms the f5e89f4 collapse.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add one normative definition in `governance/` stating that, in spec-corpus cross-references, "`spec.md`" (and the "`spec.md` — X" label form) denotes the orientation topic pages split out of the former monolithic `spec.md` — i.e. `overview-and-orientation.md` and its siblings — not the present three-link table-of-contents hub at `docs/spec.md`. This legitimizes the labelling convention already used at 40+ corpus sites.

Point GOV-30 at this definition instead of carrying a bare link: add a one-line back-reference from GOV-30 to the definition so that "the corresponding `spec.md` aggregator" resolves through it to the actual host pages (the Scope, Host-precondition, and hard-ceiling aggregators on `overview-and-orientation.md`; the `.warp` permitted top-level forms on `imports.md`; the loom 1.0 non-goals on `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md`). The siblings GOV-31 and GOV-23 reference the same definition.

In the definition, state explicitly whether the bare `[`spec.md`](../../spec.md)` link in GOV-30 (and GOV-23) is an intentional orientation entry point or must be repointed to its specific host page, so the link does not silently resolve to the aggregator-free hub. Place the definition where readers of the governance rules will find it rather than burying it.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

GOV-30's binding MUST is unsatisfiable as written: the link a maintainer follows to find the aggregator to update lands on a hub with no aggregators, and "the corresponding `spec.md` aggregator" is ambiguous between the hub and the orientation pages. Two maintainers will diverge on where (and whether) to apply the lock-step update, defeating the aggregator-vs-source parity the rule exists to maintain.

## Problem

GOV-30 (`#gov-30`) binds a maintainer who edits a source page to "update the corresponding `spec.md` aggregator in the same commit," and its bare link `[`spec.md`](../../spec.md)` resolves to `docs/spec.md` — a three-link table-of-contents hub that holds no aggregator paragraphs. The aggregators GOV-30 governs actually live on the orientation topic pages (the Scope/Host-precondition/hard-ceiling aggregators on `overview-and-orientation.md`, the permitted top-level forms on `imports.md#permitted-top-level-forms`, the loom 1.0 non-goals on `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md`). A maintainer following the link lands on the hub and finds nothing to update, leaving the MUST undischargeable as written and the lock-step target ambiguous between `spec.md` and the orientation pages.

## Issue introduction
**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** f5e89f4 (`docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set`, 2026-06-04), fe8aa5c (`pi-loom spec: resolve "GOV-12 bundles ≥3 independently-testable sub-obligations"`, 2026-06-05)
**History:** Before f5e89f4, `docs/spec.md` was a ~182-line monolith that carried the aggregator paragraphs directly (e.g. the `.warp` permitted-top-level-forms list tagged "*orientation aggregator per [Governance — GOV-12]*"); at that time the aggregator-lock-step rule (then GOV-12) correctly named `spec.md` as the host. f5e89f4 collapsed `spec.md` to a nine-line table-of-contents hub and relocated the aggregator content to `docs/spec/` section pages, but did not reconcile GOV-12's "`spec.md` aggregator" framing to the new split layout — this is where label and reality diverged. One day later, fe8aa5c retired GOV-12 and authored its successor GOV-30, re-introducing the stale "`spec.md` aggregator paragraphs" framing and the bare `[`spec.md`](../../spec.md)` link verbatim against the already-collapsed hub rather than the new host pages. 81bf52a (2026-06-11) subsequently renamed `docs/spec/` → `docs/spec_topics/` and left the framing untouched. `git log -S 'id="gov-30"'` and `git log -S 'spec.md\` aggregator'` confirm the framing has carried the defect since fe8aa5c; `git log -L '1,20:docs/spec.md'` confirms the f5e89f4 collapse.

## Solution approach

Rewrite GOV-30's `spec.md`-aggregator reference so "the corresponding `spec.md` aggregator" resolves to the topic pages that actually host the aggregators (`overview-and-orientation.md`, `imports.md#permitted-top-level-forms`, and `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md`) rather than the aggregator-free `docs/spec.md` hub. Because F-2064 and F-2216 carry the same `spec.md` mislabel, the natural mechanism is a shared governance definition naming what `spec.md` denotes in spec-corpus cross-references, with GOV-30 forward-linking to it.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: GOV-23's and GOV-31's prose, owned by F-2064 and F-2216 respectively — this finding repoints only GOV-30 (the shared `spec.md`-denotes definition may be co-authored).

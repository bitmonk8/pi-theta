## Finding

H1a's `capability-probe.md §(a)` Tests bullet (the build-time `package.json#engines.node` literal-read assertion) carries two explanatory passages that state no implementation constraint:

1. **Sequencing rationale** — "At `H1a` sequencing (`Deps: -`) no in-tree single-source constant exists for the test to read: neither `V9a`'s module-resident pinned-constants block nor `V18a`'s `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` `pi-engines-node` row has been authored yet. The test therefore asserts `package.json#engines.node` equals this transcribed literal rather than reading `package.json` against itself — the latter would be a vacuous tautology that never catches the manifest typo this bullet exists to catch."

2. **Version-bump maintenance note** — "This transcribed literal is an `H1a`-resident copy of the spec floor; a Node-floor move during the version-bump procedure ([`version-bump-step2b.md` step 3]) keeps it aligned with `capability-probe.md` §(a)."

Both passages are justification, not instruction. The normative content the bullet must carry is independent and already present in its surrounding sentences: the assertion target and floor owner, the transcribed-inline comparison operand (`>=22.19.0`, sourced from `capability-probe.md §(a)`), the failure condition, and the closing `MUST NOT read V9a … nor duplicate V18c's three-way gate` ownership pin. Stripping the two passages removes no constraint — in particular the "transcribe inline rather than read `package.json` against itself" rule survives in the retained operand sentence and in the `MUST NOT` pin.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/H1a-scaffold-and-toolchain.md`, in the `capability-probe.md §(a)` `engines.node` literal-read Tests bullet, strike the two explanatory passages and retain the normative sentences.

Strike (sequencing rationale): "At `H1a` sequencing (`Deps: -`) no in-tree single-source constant exists for the test to read: neither `V9a`'s module-resident pinned-constants block nor `V18a`'s `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` `pi-engines-node` row has been authored yet. The test therefore asserts `package.json#engines.node` equals this transcribed literal rather than reading `package.json` against itself — the latter would be a vacuous tautology that never catches the manifest typo this bullet exists to catch."

Strike (version-bump maintenance note): "This transcribed literal is an `H1a`-resident copy of the spec floor; a Node-floor move during the version-bump procedure ([`version-bump-step2b.md` step 3](../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2b.md)) keeps it aligned with `capability-probe.md` §(a)."

Retain: the opening assertion (reads `package.json#engines.node`, asserts equality with the `capability-probe.md §(a)` floor); the transcribed-inline operand sentence ("The comparison operand is the Node-floor literal (currently `>=22.19.0`) transcribed inline into this `H1a` test directly from `capability-probe.md` §(a) — an independently-sourced copy resident in the test itself."); the population-check and failure sentences; and the closing `MUST NOT` ownership pin against `V9a`/`V18a`/`V18c`.

Edge case to preserve: the "transcribe inline, not read `package.json` against itself" rule must remain observable after the cut — it is already carried by the retained operand sentence and the `MUST NOT` pin, so no separate replacement text is required.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The passages duplicate constraints and cross-references already carried by the bullet's normative sentences, by the Adds field's version-bump alignment note, and by V18c's gate ownership. Leaving them in adds reviewer/maintenance surface and invites a future editor to treat the rationale as load-bearing, but no implementer diverges and no gate misfires.

## Finding

The opening definition of the always-log set in `runtime-event-channel.md` reads: "The **always-log set** — `QueryError` `kind` values, binder failure causes, and `loom/runtime/*` panic codes — emit a structured note … exactly once per occurrence." The phrase `` `QueryError` `kind` values `` is unqualified and reads as *all* nine `QueryError` kinds. It is not: a paragraph further down ("`validation`, `context_overflow`, `cancelled`, and `invoke_callee` are deliberately **not** in the always-log set") excludes four of the nine kinds, and the Group A enumeration above it lists only the five included kinds (`transport`, `model_tool`, `code_tool`, `tool_loop_exhausted`, `invoke_infra`) plus binder failures.

The membership is therefore stated twice with conflicting breadth: the definition sentence implies all kinds; the exclusion paragraph plus the Group A list scope it to five. A reader who consumes the definition sentence as authoritative and skips (or skims past) the exclusion paragraph will wire the four excluded kinds into the always-log emission path, producing `loom-system-note` events that the spec specifically forbids for them — including at discard sites, where the *Discard-site disposition* paragraph requires the four kinds to emit nothing.

The defect is purely the unqualified breadth of the first mention; the actual membership is unambiguously pinned downstream (Group A / Group B lists + the four-kind exclusion). Resolving it requires qualifying the opening sentence so the `QueryError` component is read as a *subset*, while preserving that binder failures and `loom/runtime/*` panics are full members (they are not `QueryError` kinds).

That commit, resolving the sibling finding that the definition was *narrower* than its true membership (it named only `QueryError`, omitting binder failures and `loom/runtime/*` panics), rewrote the opening to "The **always-log set** — `QueryError` `kind` values, binder failure causes, and `loom/runtime/*` panic codes — emit …". In broadening the definition to cover all three member categories, the edit dropped the "subset" qualifier on the `QueryError` component, converting a correctly-scoped phrase into the unqualified "`QueryError` `kind` values" reading. The four-kind exclusion paragraph already existed at `038dab3` (confirmed via `git show 038dab3:…`), so the contradiction went live in that same commit. A later edit (`d75f2a8`, 2026-06-06) reworked the surrounding occurrence/origin prose but left the unqualified phrase intact. The defect is a regression introduced by a prior fix, not present since inception.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Qualify the `QueryError` component at first use so the opening reads as a subset, while leaving binder failures and `loom/runtime/*` panics as full members. Concretely, change "`QueryError` `kind` values" to a phrasing such as "a subset of `QueryError` `kind` values (the five enumerated in Group A below; `validation`, `context_overflow`, `cancelled`, and `invoke_callee` are excluded — see below)", keeping "binder failure causes, and `loom/runtime/*` panic codes" as the other two member categories.

Edge cases the edit must respect:
- Do **not** re-collapse the set to "`QueryError` failures only" — that re-introduces the prior "narrower than membership" defect (`038dab3` exists precisely to keep binder failures and runtime panics as members; they are not `QueryError` kinds).
- Keep the qualifier on the `QueryError` component only; binder failures and runtime panics are wholesale members and must not be described as subsets.
- The downstream exclusion paragraph and Group A / Group B lists stay authoritative; the edit only makes the opening sentence consistent with them, it does not move or duplicate the enumeration.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two reasonable implementers diverge: one reads `` `QueryError` `kind` values `` as all nine kinds and emits always-log `loom-system-note` events for `validation`, `context_overflow`, `cancelled`, and `invoke_callee`; the other honours the exclusion and emits for five. The first produces spurious operator-facing events (and breaks the discard-site contract), with no diagnostic flagging the divergence.

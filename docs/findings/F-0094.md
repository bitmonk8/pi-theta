## Finding

`V13c` (Query tool loop and typed two-phase) carries Tests/Adds spanning four distinct spec topics and four unrelated obligations:

1. Free-phase round counting and the `max_rounds:0` / forced-respond boundary (`query-tool-loop.md`, CIO-4 / QRY).
2. Ceiling-#2 exhaustion and the ceiling-#4 co-fire `masked:["ceiling#2"]` surface, consulting the `V16a` cross-ceiling arbitration seam (`ceilings-3-and-4.md`).
3. Model-driven parallel tool-call batch concurrency — the `Promise.allSettled` settle-all-then-construct-next-turn rule with independent per-sibling lowering (`tool-calls.md` §Concurrency, TOOL).
4. The cancellation swallowing-handler three-channel suppression at the `@`-query provider abandonable-Promise site (`cancellation.md`, the per-site routing witness named by `V17a`).

Obligations 3 and 4 are independently assertable at the session-double level and have no dependency on round counting; they cite separate spec topics and pull two extra Deps edges (`V8a`, `V8c`) onto the leaf. The leaf now requires a reviewer to verify free-phase loop semantics, two-axis ceiling arbitration, batch concurrency, and cross-channel cancellation suppression in a single commit — four orthogonal recognition paths.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

This finding is bimodal: it asks the plan to relocate two independent obligations (a `tool-calls.md` concurrency assertion and a `cancellation.md` suppression assertion) and to retarget coverage gating for each. The two halves cannot be resolved by one leaf edit, so they are decomposed below; resolving them in order keeps each diff bounded.

### Option A — Relocate the parallel-batch concurrency assertion (TOOL co-witness)

**Approach.** Move the `tool-calls.md — Concurrency` (TOOL) Tests bullet — the settle-all `Promise.allSettled` batch with independent per-sibling lowering — out of `V13c`/`V13c-T` into a new leaf pair `<new>`/`<new>-T`, leaving the free-phase loop and ceiling obligations in V13c.
**Plan edits.** Strike the `tool-calls.md — Concurrency` Tests bullet from `V13c` and `V13c-T`; add it to `<new>`/`<new>-T` with Deps including `V13c`; change the coverage-matrix `tool-calls.md (TOOL)` row from `` `V14a`, `V13c` `` to `` `V14a`, `<new>` ``; link `<new>` into the plan.md V13 slice; add `<new>` to `H7a` Deps and its permitted-code / golden-derivation reference set wherever it currently names `V13c` for the TOOL-emitted codes. Keep the "a parallel batch counts as one slot" clause in V13c's `CIO-4` bullet — it is round-counting, not batch concurrency.
**Spec edits.** None.
**Pros.** Removes an entire spec-topic concern from V13c; the TOOL row gains a precise single-purpose closing leaf.
**Cons.** Adds a leaf pair; `H7a` Deps grows.
**Risks.** Moving the "counts as one slot" wording out of V13c would break the CIO-4 free-phase assertion; it must stay.

### Option B — Relocate the swallowing-handler `@`-query site routing assertion (cancellation)

**Approach.** Move the swallowing-handler three-channel suppression Tests bullet (plus its mirroring Ships-when clause and the `V8a` Deps it requires) out of `V13c`/`V13c-T`, then retarget `V17a`/`V17a-T`'s per-site routing cross-reference for the `@`-query provider site.
**Plan edits.** Strike the swallowing-handler Tests bullet from `V13c`/`V13c-T` and the matching Ships-when clause; drop `V8a` from V13c Deps if nothing else references it; add the bullet to `<new>`/`<new>-T` with `V8a` in Deps; in `V17a-cancellation-core.md` and `V17a-T`, replace `V13c` with `<new>` in the four-site routing list (`` `V14a`, `V13c`, `V15a`, `V9i` ``); retarget any cancellation-site coverage gating that cites V13c.
**Spec edits.** None.
**Pros.** Detaches the `cancellation.md` concern from the query-tool-loop leaf; the `@`-query routing witness becomes single-purpose.
**Cons.** Propagates into `V17a`/`V17a-T` cross-references; `V8a` Deps moves with it.
**Risks.** V17a's prose asserts the four-site routing is witnessed by `V14a`/`V13c`/`V15a`/`V9i`; the cross-reference must be updated in lockstep or the suppression-coverage claim goes stale.

### Recommendation

Resolve **Option A first** — it is the narrower, scope-bounding obligation (touches `V13c`/`V13c-T`, the TOOL row, and `H7a`) — then land **Option B** on top, since B propagates into the `V17a`/`V17a-T` cross-references and benefits from a stable baseline. The two split-out obligations may co-locate in one new leaf pair or split into two; the implementer picks the real ID(s) for `<new>` (do not reuse the source's speculative `V13e`). The free-phase "parallel batch counts as one slot" round-counting assertion and the ceiling-#2/#4 arbitration stay in V13c.

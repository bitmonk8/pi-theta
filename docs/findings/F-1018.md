## Finding

`schema-subset.md` is the home for the JSON Schema subset, the lowering pass, and the canonical-hash → schema-slug recipe. Two of its sub-paragraphs in *Canonical schema hash* reach beyond that surface into the Pi Integration Contract and binder layers and assert normative authority over their tool-registration vocabulary:

- Step 5 (*Synthesised names*) enumerates four `<slug>`-bearing name forms and declares "this list is the source of truth for the full set". Two of those four — `__loom_callee_<slug>__<post-rename-name>` (the prompt-mode registered tool for a `.loom` callee) and `__loom_bind_<slug>` (the binder's structured-output tool) — are registration-protocol names whose lifetime, registration mechanism, and disambiguation policy are specified in `pi-integration-contract/tool-registration-lifetime.md` and `pi-integration-contract/binder-inference.md` respectively. By naming `schema-subset.md` "source of truth" for them, the spec forces PIC/binder implementers to read a schema-lowering page to learn the authoritative spelling of names whose semantics live on their own pages.
- The *Schema-slug collision posture* paragraph's third bullet normatively binds the prompt-mode `pi.registerTool` registration-cache collision rule: it both names the diagnostic (`loom/runtime/registration-cache-collision`) and asserts that counter-suffixed disambiguation applies, on a page whose remit is the on-disk/on-wire schema contract rather than the registration cache. The same rule is then fully restated by its true owner in `tool-registration-lifetime.md`; the schema-subset bullet is a duplicate normative claim, not a pure cross-reference.

The problem is not internal contradiction — the two pages currently agree — but ownership drift: a future edit to the registration-cache disambiguation rule on `tool-registration-lifetime.md` will silently desync schema-subset's restatement, and the "source of truth" assertion makes that desync ambiguous to resolve.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**
- `c577ab3` (2026-06-06, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Glossary schema slug name enumeration cannot be complete\"") — replaced step 5 with the four-form enumeration and added the *"this list is the source of truth for the full set"* phrasing, pulling `__loom_callee_<slug>__<post-rename-name>` (a PIC name form) and reaffirming `__loom_bind_<slug>` (a binder name form) into the schema-subset page as its authoritative inventory.
- `2128e5f` (2026-06-07, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Schema-slug collision posture pinned only for registerTool cache\"") — added the *Schema-slug collision posture* paragraph and its three-bullet enumeration including the prompt-mode `pi.registerTool` registration-cache bullet, restating the `loom/runtime/registration-cache-collision` diagnostic and counter-suffix rule whose authoritative pinning already lived in `tool-registration-lifetime.md`.

**History:** Each commit individually resolved a real upstream review concern (incomplete name enumeration; collision posture covering only one of several slug-keyed sites). The scope-ownership defect emerges from the *combination*: c577ab3 promoted schema-subset to authoritative registry of all slug-bearing names, and 2128e5f then attached normative collision rules to the registration-layer rows of that registry. Neither commit introduced incorrect content; together they relocated PIC/binder normative surface onto a schema-lowering page. No subsequent commit has revisited this scope boundary, and the spec corpus moves under `docs/spec_topics/` (`81bf52a`) and the binder-rendering pin (`6892aa3`) are unrelated.

## Solution Space

### Recommendation

Reframe `schema-subset.md` step 5 (*Synthesised names*) as a non-normative convenience index rather than an authoritative registry, and demote the registration-cache bullet in *Schema-slug collision posture* to a bare cross-reference.

In step 5, keep all four name forms in the enumeration but change the framing language: replace the "this list is the source of truth for the full set" phrasing with wording that labels the paragraph a convenience index — e.g. "convenience index; each form's authoritative pinning is on the linked owner page" — and add an explicit note that the authoritative pinning of each name form's lifetime and registration mechanism lives at the linked owner. The schema-subset index uses backticks and cross-references for `__loom_callee_<slug>__<post-rename-name>` and `__loom_bind_<slug>` but must not introduce or claim authority over their spelling; their normative definitions stay solely in `tool-registration-lifetime.md` and `binder-inference.md` respectively.

In *Schema-slug collision posture*, keep the load-time `__inline_<slug>` `$defs`-dedup bullet and the per-query AJV compiled-validator-cache bullet normative on this page. Rewrite the prompt-mode `pi.registerTool` bullet as a pure cross-reference to the registration-cache section in `tool-registration-lifetime.md`: do not name the `loom/runtime/registration-cache-collision` diagnostic and do not restate the counter-suffixed disambiguation policy. That diagnostic name must appear normatively only in `tool-registration-lifetime.md` (and in the diagnostic-code registry); the schema-subset bullet refers to it only via cross-reference, not by quoting the code string. Removing this duplicate normative restatement is the load-bearing fix.

Watch these edges:

- The *Schema-slug collision posture* lead-in still says "three slug-keyed sites" — verify the count remains accurate after the edit (it does: `__inline_<slug>` `$defs` dedup, per-query AJV cache, prompt-mode registration cache).
- `loom/load/schema-slug-collision` (the `__inline_<slug>` load-time diagnostic) remains owned by `schema-subset.md` — only the *runtime*-cache diagnostics move out.
- `tool-registration-lifetime.md` and `binder-inference.md` require no normative change; their existing pinnings become the sole authoritative source.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A scope/ownership defect with no current observable behaviour change: the spec is internally consistent today. The risk is forward — a future edit to `pi.registerTool` registration semantics or the binder envelope tool name will land on `tool-registration-lifetime.md` / `binder-inference.md` (their natural homes) and silently leave `schema-subset.md`'s "source of truth" enumeration and registration-cache bullet stale, with no automatic gate to catch the drift. Implementers reading `schema-subset.md` for the slug recipe are also forced to absorb registration-layer obligations to discover whether they live here authoritatively or only by cross-reference.

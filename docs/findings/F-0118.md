## Finding

`V18d` (and its `-T` partner) own one gate — output (c) of the version-bump procedure, an `H4a`-harness end-to-end run of a representative integrated `.loom` against the bumped Pi-SDK pin. The leaf names that single gate with a shifting set of phrases that are never equated to one another:

- **"runtime-evidence acceptance gate"** — title, Adds, Tests (the canonical form).
- **"double-backed runtime-evidence run"** and **"double-backed acceptance gate"** — Adds.
- **"acceptance evidence"** and **"build-time runtime-evidence / `H4a`-harness gate"** — Ships-when.
- **"double-backed acceptance evidence"** — `V18d-T` Ships-when.

The most confusing variant is "build-time runtime-evidence / `H4a`-harness gate" in V18d Ships-when: it appears where the reader is being told the gate's red-result obligation, reads as a *different* gate from the "runtime-evidence acceptance gate" of the title/Tests, and the phrase "build-time runtime-evidence" is itself internally odd (the run is a build-time-triggered *runtime* evidence run). Because no inline equivalence ties the variants together, a reader cannot be sure the Ships-when exit condition and the Tests gate are the same object.

The most confusing "build-time runtime-evidence / `H4a`-harness gate" form was added later at `4f4d85e` ("resolve 'V18d Ships-when conflates pre-merge gate and post-merge smoke'") while reworking Ships-when, and further "double-backed … acceptance evidence" wording (including in V18d-T) entered at `15eb44c` ("resolve 'real-host divergence pre-merge gate (posture i)'"). The variant set accreted across three separate Ships-when/Adds edits rather than entering whole at inception.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Standardise on **"runtime-evidence acceptance gate"** as the single name for output (c) throughout `V18d-version-bump-acceptance.md` and `V18d-T-version-bump-acceptance.md`. Concretely:

- In V18d Ships-when, strike the appositive "the build-time runtime-evidence / [`H4a`](./H4a-factory-shell-and-harness.md)-harness gate" and the standalone "the bump's acceptance evidence is red"; refer to the gate as "the runtime-evidence acceptance gate" in both places.
- In V18d Adds, where the `H4a`-double basis is being contrasted with real-host coverage, keep "double-backed" only as a qualifier on the canonical noun phrase (e.g. "the double-backed runtime-evidence acceptance gate"), not as a standalone alternate name ("double-backed runtime-evidence run", "double-backed acceptance gate").
- In V18d-T Ships-when, change "the bump's double-backed acceptance evidence is red" to refer to "the runtime-evidence acceptance gate" (with the "double-backed" qualifier if the double-vs-real-host contrast is wanted).

If any single deviation is retained deliberately, equate it inline on first use (e.g. "the runtime-evidence acceptance gate (the `H4a`-harness-backed run)"). The fix is internal to the two V18d leaf files; the spec's "runtime-evidence run" term and the coverage-matrix row are the canonical anchors and stay unchanged.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader of V18d's Ships-when may take "build-time runtime-evidence / `H4a`-harness gate" as a gate distinct from the "runtime-evidence acceptance gate" of the title and Tests, and treat the exit condition as referencing a second mechanism. The cross-references to `H4a` and output (c) let a careful implementer reconcile them, so a working leaf is still reachable, but the inconsistency invites mis-reading and weakens the single-source intent.

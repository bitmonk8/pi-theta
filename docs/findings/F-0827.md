## Finding

Every SHOULD-level item (f)–(an) in `version-bump-step2.md` ends with the same closing sentence: "This obligation is SHOULD-level only at loom 1.0; it escalates to a build-time pin (on the same footing as the seven-capability literal-read assertion under step 2(a) above) once Pi exposes a typed `<X>` contract that step 2(a)'s surface-inventory probe can mechanically verify, at which point the editorial audit prescribed here is superseded by the mechanical gate." The clause is ~56 words and recurs verbatim 34 times across items (f) through (an); only the typed-contract slot `<X>` varies (`session-lifetime`, `handler-promise-lifecycle`, `ordering`, `turn-liveness`, `registry-population-ordering`, `command-registration-ordering`, etc.). That is roughly 1,900 words of templated boilerplate inside a single ~11,200-word file — the largest single source of inflation on the page, and a measurable share of the corpus-level word budget the §size-cap guidance targets.

Two problems follow. First, the per-item information density collapses to a single varying token: a reader who has parsed the clause once gains nothing from the next 33 repetitions, while every per-item context (the cited paragraph, the recording shape, what falsification looks like, why no SDK signal exists) gets pushed under that uniform tail. Second, the 34 escalation statements describe a single coherent post-loom-1.0 work item — convert this whole class of editorial audits to mechanical step-2(a) gates as Pi's typed surface grows — but that work item lives nowhere as a collected scope statement. `future-considerations/surface-extensions.md` already carries a `Tooling deferrals (no loom 1.0 impact)` section that is the natural home for it; today the post-loom-1.0 plan is reconstructable only by reading 34 inline clauses and noticing they say the same thing.

The two halves are independent: collapsing the per-item clause is an editorial refactor confined to `version-bump-step2.md`; surfacing the collected post-loom-1.0 scope is a new entry in `future-considerations/surface-extensions.md` (or sibling). Either lands without the other, and both improve readability and discoverability on their own.

## Solution Space

### Recommendation

Apply two edits, in this order.

First, in `version-bump-step2.md`, collapse the repeated escalation clause into one lead note. Insert a SHOULD-level heading `#### SHOULD-level items (f)–(an)` directly before item (f), and place the escalation rule in a single paragraph immediately under it: "Each item below is SHOULD-level only at loom 1.0; each escalates to a build-time pin on the same footing as the seven-capability literal-read assertion under step 2(a) above once Pi exposes a typed contract of the kind named in the item's tail (e.g. `session-lifetime`, `handler-promise-lifecycle`, `turn-liveness`, ...) that step 2(a)'s surface-inventory probe can mechanically verify, at which point the editorial audit prescribed here is superseded by the mechanical gate." Then trim every (f)–(an) item's closing sentence down to its unique typed-contract slot (e.g. item (f) ends "Escalation contract: `session-lifetime`."); items whose typed-contract token matches the lead-note default omit the marker and inherit the note, and any item whose escalation predicate genuinely differs keeps its full closing sentence as an explicit override. This removes ~1,800 words from the page and supplies the missing tier-boundary marker between the MUST (a)–(e) and SHOULD (f)–(an) blocks.

Three edge cases govern this trim: (i) items whose closing sentence already varies from the template (items (i)/(u)/(aa)/(ab)/(af), which carry corpus-currency fixture obligations and additionally name "and whenever a provider publishes an error-format or API-version change") must keep their item-specific second sentence even after the generic escalation tail is removed; (ii) the lead note must not weaken the per-item "recorded `pass` / `fail` / `N/A` / `not-performed`" obligation, which is set in the preamble paragraph and the item bodies, not in the escalation tail being collapsed; (iii) the `V18c` plan leaf's `(a)`–`(al)` range citation is independently stale and is not a blocker on this edit, but a touching commit should refresh it to `(a)`–`(an)`.

Second, in `future-considerations/surface-extensions.md`, add a bullet (or short sub-section) under the existing `Tooling deferrals (no loom 1.0 impact)` block (anchor `tooling-deferrals-no-loom-1-0-impact`) naming the collected post-loom-1.0 scope: "Convert `version-bump-step2.md` items (f)–(an) from editorial-review audits to mechanical step-2(a) build-time gates, item by item, as Pi exposes typed contracts for each named behavioural surface (`session-lifetime`, `handler-promise-lifecycle`, `turn-liveness`, `messages-ordering`, `registry-population-ordering`, `command-registration-ordering`, ...). Each escalation lands as a co-edit of one `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` entry plus deletion of the corresponding (f)–(an) item." Add one cross-reference from the lead-note paragraph (or the bottom of the SHOULD-level block) in `version-bump-step2.md` back to this entry so a reader of either page sees the connection. Sequencing the `version-bump-step2.md` edit first means this cross-reference lands against the stable lead-note anchor rather than against 34 individual item tails that no longer exist.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The spec is conformant and the per-item audit obligations are unambiguous — every item still states its own escalation behaviour, just verbosely. The cost is page weight and the implementer's ability to see the post-loom-1.0 mechanical-gate roadmap as a single planned scope rather than as 34 distributed footnotes. Left as-is, the file remains a discoverability tax on every contributor who reads it, and the corpus carries ~1,800 words of boilerplate that earn nothing.

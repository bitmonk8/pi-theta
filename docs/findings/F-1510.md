## Problem

Arm (1) of the *Lookup-failure-to-discriminator routing* paragraph in `unknown-reason-rule.md` mandates a specific guard form — the handler MUST guard the lookup with an explicit `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY === undefined` check before invoking the iteration primitive. The only spec-observable property of this arm is the discriminator value: an inventory that resolves to `undefined` must route to `details.failure === "missing-entry"`, not to any `"throw:TypeError: …"` arm. Several equally-conformant guard shapes deliver that routing (explicit `=== undefined`, a nullish guard, a `typeof` check, a catch-arm reclassification, or a fused predicate); naming one by MUST forecloses the others without serving the operator-deduplication invariant the paragraph cites as the reason for spec ownership. The form pin also contradicts the paragraph's own discipline, which explicitly marks the iteration primitive leaf-owned and only the routing spec-owned.

## Solution approach

Narrow arm (1) of the *Lookup-failure-to-discriminator routing* paragraph in `unknown-reason-rule.md` so it pins only the behavioural routing — an `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` that resolves to `undefined` routes to discriminator `"missing-entry"`, not to any `"throw:TypeError: …"` arm — and demote the explicit `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY === undefined`-check-before-the-iteration-primitive requirement from a MUST to one example guard among equivalents. Retain the existing `?.find` anti-pattern call-out as the rationale for why some guard is mandatory.

## Solution constraints

- The relaxation MUST NOT weaken the catch-arm discriminator's existing pin that a thrown `TypeError` from the undefined access routes to `"throw:…"` rather than `"missing-entry"`; the four-arm routing stays fixed regardless of the guard form chosen.
- Out of scope: the ESM/TDZ module-model assumption question owned by F-1744.

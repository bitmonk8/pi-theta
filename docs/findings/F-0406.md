## Finding

`V16a`'s **Adds.** field carries two sub-bullets — `**Verified at the seam in isolation:**` and `**Not witnessable at the stateless seam — owned by downstream sites:**` — that enumerate which CIO sub-properties the stateless seam can witness and which are deferred to downstream enforcement-site leaves (`V4e`/`V11f`/`V5e`/`V13c`/`V15b`) and `H7a`. That same scoping is already stated per-test in the **Tests.** field (the CIO-1 bullet's "witnessed downstream at the load-time-vs-runtime consult split … not at this seam" clause and the CIO-5 bullet's "witnessed end-to-end by `H7a` … not by this stateless seam" clause) and once more in the closing **Ships when** sentence ("CIO-1's slash-load-before-runtime placement and CIO-5's never-interleaves cross-site relations are not asserted here; they are witnessed downstream …").

The result is the same seam-decidable-vs-downstream split written in three sections of one leaf. The content is test-strategy context, not implementation scope: `conventions.md` §Leaf format pins **Adds.** as "One sentence — what the leaf introduces", descriptive by default, with the binding obligations living in the **Tests.** REQ-ID bullets and the **Ships when** gate. The two Adds sub-bullets restate scope that those binding fields already own.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single introducing commit (fix-introduced redundancy)
**Introducing commits:** `8ef7055` (2026-06-11) — "pi-loom plan: resolve \"V16a over-claims all CIO bullets provable at stateless seam\""
**History:** The two Adds sub-bullets were absent at the leaf's inception (`c6a664e`, 2026-06-10); `git log -S` for both `"Verified at the seam in isolation"` and `"Not witnessable at the stateless seam"` returns only `8ef7055`. That commit — the remediation of the prior finding about V16a over-claiming all CIO bullets at the stateless seam — added the seam-decidable-vs-downstream scope split simultaneously to three locations in one diff: the two new Adds sub-bullets, the per-test clauses appended to the CIO-1 and CIO-5 Tests bullets, and the closing Ships-when sentence. The triplication this finding flags entered as that single over-correction; the Tests and Ships-when copies are the binding ones, leaving the Adds copy redundant.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V16a-ceiling-order-masked.md`, delete from the **Adds.** field the two sub-bullets `**Verified at the seam in isolation:** …` and `**Not witnessable at the stateless seam — owned by downstream sites:** …`, together with the clause that exists only to introduce them: `The stateless single-candidate seam witnesses only the CIO sub-properties that are decidable from co-present candidates; the temporal cross-site relations are not observable here and are owned by the downstream enforcement-site leaves and the \`H7a\` integration run:`. Terminate the preceding sentence with a period after "… do not exist when `V16a` is picked up." so the synthesised-candidate test-strategy sentence still stands, and keep the following `Each downstream leaf's \`Deps\` on \`V16a\` binds the seam it consults.` line.

The scope split is preserved where it binds: the CIO-1 and CIO-5 **Tests.** bullets already carry the per-test "witnessed downstream / not at this seam" clauses, and the closing **Ships when** sentence already states the same boundary. No Tests or Ships-when text changes.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The seam-decidable-vs-downstream split is duplicated across Adds, Tests, and Ships-when, bloating the descriptive Adds field beyond the one-sentence convention and creating three copies that must stay in lockstep. A future edit that adjusts the witness boundary in one location and not the others would leave the leaf internally inconsistent. No implementer is blocked or misled today — the three statements currently agree.

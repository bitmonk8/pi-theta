Routing arm (1) on `unknown-reason-rule.md` pins the exact guard expression `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY === undefined` before the iteration primitive, alongside the already-stated observable requirement.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Keep the observable obligation (an `undefined` `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` MUST yield discriminator `"missing-entry"`, never a `"throw:…"` value). Demote the explicit-`=== undefined`-check mandate to a non-normative note, retaining the existing parenthetical warning that a naive `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY.find(…)?.literals` pattern misroutes the case to the throw arm.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Implementers reading the clauses as MUSTs are needlessly forbidden from using equivalent, conforming alternatives (a different undefined-guard expression, a re-read from a stable source instead of a hoisted local, other variable names, a different constant layout), and the over-pinned mechanism grows the normative surface future maintainers and conformance fixtures must track. No two conforming implementations diverge in observable behaviour, so this is a guidance/quality gap rather than a correctness hazard.

## Problem

`H4a`'s third `Tests` bullet co-locates two concerns of different kind: the four-axis session-double fidelity-contract self-check (assertions (i)–(iv)), which is automated `npm test` work observable at H4a's exit gate, and an extensive manual real-host smoke **governance protocol** that never runs under `npm test`. The governance protocol is policy design — a live-host precondition, a pass/fail criterion (a)–(e), two named triggers with per-owner run-and-record obligations, an evidence-recording convention, and merge-blocker / revert implications — whose audience is the bump/merge contributor and the H6a release-gate auditor, not the H4a implementer. Because the bullet's heading implies automated coverage, a reviewer cannot verify factory/double/self-check correctness and the coherence of the smoke governance policy in one pass, and an implementer can mis-read the manual protocol as automated coverage.

## Solution approach

Move the manual real-host smoke **governance protocol** (from "The concrete pre-merge gate for a double-vs-real divergence is a manual real-host smoke run…" through the merge-blocker paragraph) out of `H4a`'s third `Tests` bullet into a non-test field — `Adds` or `Ships when` — leaving only the four fidelity-contract self-check assertions (i)–(iv) in `Tests`. Keep the smoke run's reuse of `H7a`'s committed multi-feature fixture `.loom` and committed permitted-code list explicit so the smoke and the H7a integration gate never fork into separate fixtures.

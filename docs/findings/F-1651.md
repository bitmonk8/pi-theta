## Problem

The "**Reference encoding (non-normative).**" paragraph in `docs/spec_topics/runtime-value-model.md` ends with a normative `MUST` (the "revisit before that surface ships" clause) even though its heading disclaims normativity. A reader scanning for normative keywords either skips the paragraph and misses the revisit obligation, or honours the `MUST` and is left unsure how much of the surrounding `__loomEnum` / `{ ok, value }` description also binds. The two readings produce different conformance pictures and the page offers no way to choose between them.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit-introduction
**Introducing commit:** `0a7884a` — "pi-loom spec: resolve 'JS representation of Result and enum brand is over-prescribed'" (2026-05-04)
**History:** `git log -S 'MUST be revisited' --all -- spec_topics/runtime-value-model.md` returns exactly `0a7884a`; the file move under `docs/` (`31ff060`) carried the wording forward unchanged, and `git log -L '/Reference encoding/,+1' -- docs/spec_topics/runtime-value-model.md` confirms only `0a7884a` (and the rename) ever touched this paragraph. The diff for `0a7884a` shows the entire "Reference encoding (non-normative)." paragraph — including the `MUST be revisited` clause — being added in one hunk while resolving a separate review finding about over-prescribing the JS representation; the normativity collision was introduced by that one edit and has been latent ever since.

## Solution approach

Split the revisit obligation out of the "**Reference encoding (non-normative).**" paragraph and promote it to a peer normative paragraph on `runtime-value-model.md`, leaving the "(non-normative)" label attached only to the `__loomEnum` / `{ ok, value }` reference-shape description it was meant to cover.

## Solution constraints

- Do not weaken the revisit `MUST` to `SHOULD` or delete it — the revisit obligation is a real spec-maintenance gate, not part of the non-normative collision being removed.
- Out of scope: relocating the obligation off `runtime-value-model.md` (e.g. to a governance or host-interop page); the trigger condition is phrased in terms of this page's own contract.

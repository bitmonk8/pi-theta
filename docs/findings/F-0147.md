## Finding

`V8e` bundles two host seams — `FileWatcher` (`watch(roots,handler) → Unsubscribe`, three change kinds; `PIC-14`) and `TokenEstimator` (`estimate(message)` delegating `estimateTokens`; `PIC-16`) — that serve unrelated subsystems and share no infrastructure constraint. The leaf's Adds names both seams and disclaims the seams owned by V8b and V8d, but never says *why* `FileWatcher` and `TokenEstimator` belong in the same leaf.

The contrast with its sibling is direct. `V8d` (`Clock` + `IdSource`) opens its Adds with an explicit grouping justification — "the two ambient-wrapping host seams kept together so the `PIC-12`/`PIC-20` `// allow-ambient:` allow-list contract is not split" — so a reader immediately understands the coupling is load-bearing. `V8e` carries no equivalent sentence, so a future reader cannot tell whether the pairing reflects a real shared constraint or is incidental editorial batching.

The seam definitions themselves are unambiguous and an implementer can build the leaf as written; the gap is purely one of stated rationale at the V8-seam-split boundary.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add a grouping-rationale clause to `V8e`'s **Adds** field in `docs/plan_topics/V8e-watch-token-seams.md`, mirroring the explanatory role V8d's Adds already plays. Because these two seams share no allow-list or ambient-wrapping constraint (unlike V8d's), the rationale must state the *actual* reason for the pairing — they are the residual host service seams with no shared contract, grouped editorially as a single small seam leaf rather than spawning two single-seam leaves. Concretely, prepend a clause such as "The two remaining host service seams, grouped as one leaf because each is a single self-contained seam with no shared contract that would warrant a separate leaf:" ahead of the existing `FileWatcher`/`TokenEstimator` enumeration.

If on inspection the fixer judges the two seams genuinely warrant separate leaves, splitting `V8e` into one leaf per seam (with `V8e-T` split to match) is an acceptable alternative that also closes the finding; the rationale sentence is the lighter resolution and is sufficient.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader of `V8e` cannot tell whether the `FileWatcher`+`TokenEstimator` pairing is constraint-driven (as V8d's is) or incidental, so the leaf is harder to review and a future editor has no basis to decide whether the two seams may be split or must stay together. No implementer is blocked and no spec obligation goes uncovered.

## Finding

`V2b` (`docs/plan_topics/V2b-type-compat-engine.md`) declares in its **Adds**: "a deferral marker for statically-unresolvable operands, consumed by the downstream runtime-validator sites (`V6b`, `V11g`)", and its **Ships when** commits to "marks unresolved operands for downstream runtime validation". This names a cross-leaf producer→consumer seam with two explicit consumers.

Neither named consumer fulfils the contract. `V6b` (`docs/plan_topics/V6b-params-defaults.md`) carries `Deps. V6b-T, V6a, V5d, V5f, V8c` and never names the deferral marker (or any synonym). `V11g` (`docs/plan_topics/V11g-defaulting-revalidation.md`) carries `Deps. V11g-T, V11a, V5d, V5f, V8c` and likewise never names it. A repository-wide search finds the term "deferral marker" only in `V2b`'s Adds.

Per `conventions.md` §Leaf format (Adds bullet), a Class-2 seam binds "only when … a leaf listing this leaf in its `Deps.` names the seam in its own `Adds.` or `Tests.`"; "a seam named in `Adds.` with no such consumer remains illustrative (non-binding)". V2b's deferral marker therefore is not the Class-2 seam its prose presents — no consumer binds it. The contract is one-sided: V2b ships a marker whose only documented readers do not reference it, declare a `V2b` Deps edge, or sequence after V2b.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

Determine which leaf's current text is authoritative first: whether statically-unresolvable operands genuinely require V6b and V11g to consult V2b's deferral marker, or whether that marker is fully subsumed by V6b/V11g's existing AJV re-validation and the consumer claim is the inconsistent element.

### Option A — Drop the consumer claim (marker stays illustrative)

- **Approach:** Treat V2b's deferral marker as a leaf-internal mechanism with no cross-leaf consumer.
- **Plan edits:** In `docs/plan_topics/V2b-type-compat-engine.md` Adds, strike "consumed by the downstream runtime-validator sites (`V6b`, `V11g`)", leaving the marker described without naming consumers; adjust the Ships-when phrasing if it still implies a named downstream reader.
- **Spec edits:** None.
- **Pros:** Minimal diff confined to one leaf; no new Deps edges; matches the conventions rule that a consumerless seam is illustrative.
- **Cons:** If the static→runtime handoff is real, this discards the seam intent and leaves the producer→consumer relationship undocumented.
- **Risks:** Mis-classifying a real handoff as illustrative could let statically-unresolvable operands skip targeted runtime validation if a future implementer relies on the marker existing but unwired.

### Option B — Wire the seam at both consumers

- **Approach:** Make V6b and V11g genuine Class-2 consumers of V2b's deferral marker.
- **Plan edits:** In `docs/plan_topics/V6b-params-defaults.md`, name the deferral marker verbatim in Adds (or a Tests bullet) at the AJV validation site and add `V2b` to its Deps; in `docs/plan_topics/V11g-defaulting-revalidation.md`, name the deferral marker verbatim at the post-merge AJV-validation site and add `V2b` to its Deps. The deferral-marker term then carries through to the regenerated terms index.
- **Spec edits:** None.
- **Pros:** Makes the producer→consumer contract binding and observable; sequences the runtime-validation sites after V2b; satisfies the conventions Class-2 binding rule.
- **Cons:** Larger diff across three leaves; introduces new Deps edges.
- **Risks:** Adding `V2b` to V6b/V11g Deps is a forward edge (V2 precedes V6/V11 in build order; V2b deps only on V2a/V5d) and introduces no cycle — confirm before landing.

### Recommendation

First establish authoritativeness: inspect whether `type-system.md` / `schema-subset.md` make the marker a required input to the runtime AJV sites, or whether V6b/V11g's existing `SchemaValidator`/AJV re-validation already closes the obligation without it. If the handoff is required, take Option B and wire both consumers; if the marker is subsumed by the existing AJV re-validation, take Option A and drop the consumer claim. Whichever direction is chosen, V2b's Adds and the two consumers must end consistent — no consumer named that does not bind, and no binding consumer left unnamed.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

V2b builds a deferral marker (a binding obligation under its **Ships when**) whose only declared readers, V6b and V11g, have no `Deps` edge to V2b and never name the marker, so two reasonable implementers diverge: one wires the static→runtime handoff V2b's prose implies, while another treats the marker as illustrative and relies on V6b/V11g's independent AJV re-validation alone. The marker can therefore ship with no documented consumer, leaving the producer→consumer intent unverifiable and the sequencing of the runtime-validation sites after V2b undeclared.

## Finding

The `diagnostics.md` hub intro says of the diagnostics defined in the diagnostics shard: "the diagnostics defined here all fire after the extension is already live (during scan, watcher reload, or slash-command execution)." The very next sentence of the same paragraph forward-links `loom/load/extension-bootstrap-failed` (defined in this shard, in `diagnostics/code-registry-load.md`), and that code's *Trigger* is "A factory-time or `session_start`-time SDK registration or subscription call … threw or rejected" — i.e. it fires *at* extension bootstrap, before the factory has returned, and is precisely the "same class of factory-time bootstrap registration / subscription failures Pi would otherwise push into `LoadExtensionsResult.errors`" the immediately preceding clause acknowledges.

The contradiction matters because the universally-quantified intro clause is the only place that tells an implementer when the shard's diagnostics may be expected to fire. A reader who trusts it will plan one delivery posture (renderer is live, persistent channel is available) for every row in the shard, then hit `extension-bootstrap-failed` — a row whose own trigger text explicitly fires under the opposite precondition (the renderer itself may be the failing capability, hence its mandated routing through the System-notes fallback chain). The same shape applies to `loom/load/host-incompatible`, which fires from the factory-time capability probe before any registration call. The intro therefore mis-states a load-bearing precondition shared by at least two rows in its own shard.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `2d2fc8e` ("pi-loom spec: resolve 'Pi SDK capability calls have no failure contract'", 2026-05-06) and `6829a79` ("pi-loom spec: resolve 'Pi-SDK hidden-assumption cluster …'", 2026-06-06)
**History:** The clause "all fire after the extension is already live (during scan, watcher reload, or slash-command execution)" entered `diagnostics.md` in `ac8c3af` (2026-05-04, "LoadExtensionsResult.errors is the wrong delivery channel for loom diagnostics") as part of a rewrite of the hub intro. At that revision the universal-quantifier reading was true: the diagnostics shard then defined no factory-time bootstrap codes. Two days later `2d2fc8e` added `loom/load/extension-bootstrap-failed` as a new row in the shard's load registry, with a *Trigger* describing factory-time SDK registration calls that threw — making the universal-quantifier intro false against the shard's own contents, without editing the intro. `6829a79` (2026-06-06) then revised the intro paragraph again to forward-link `[\`loom/load/extension-bootstrap-failed\`](./diagnostics/code-registry-load.md)` as the loom-emitted replacement for `LoadExtensionsResult.errors`, placing the forward-link in the same paragraph as — and immediately before — the surviving universal-quantifier clause. The placement made the contradiction acute (the linked row's trigger is factory-time bootstrap; the next clause says all rows in this shard fire after bootstrap), but the introducing condition is the May-6 row addition; the June-6 edit only sharpened the visible contradiction. `bf85092` (2026-06-06) further extended the row's trigger to cover `session_start`-time `pi.getCommands()` failures, broadening the contradiction marginally but not introducing it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Rewrite the universal-quantifier clause in the `diagnostics.md` hub intro to partition the shard's codes by emission phase rather than asserting a single precondition over all of them. Specifically, replace "the diagnostics defined here all fire after the extension is already live (during scan, watcher reload, or slash-command execution)" with a two-part statement that names the exception explicitly:

> Most diagnostics defined here fire after the extension is already live (during scan, watcher reload, or slash-command execution). The two exceptions are `loom/load/extension-bootstrap-failed` and `loom/load/host-incompatible`, which fire at factory-time (or `session_start`-time) extension bootstrap and route through the System-notes fallback chain because the message renderer itself may be the failing or missing capability.

The replacement preserves the orienting purpose of the original clause (telling the reader when, broadly, to expect emission), names every row that violates the broad claim rather than reasoning by example, and ties the bootstrap-phase rows to the fallback-routing rationale already pinned on those rows so the intro reinforces rather than contradicts the row text. The enumeration must include both `extension-bootstrap-failed` and `host-incompatible`; an enumeration covering only the row this finding cites would leave the same contradiction live for the sibling row.

Implementer edge cases to watch: (a) do not collapse the two named codes into a generic "bootstrap-phase codes" reference — the rest of the shard's `loom/load/*` rows are not bootstrap-phase, so the predicate must be by row name, not by code namespace; (b) do not relocate the fallback-routing statement out of the rows themselves — the intro should reference it, not own it (it is the row trigger's load-bearing rationale and stays there).

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer or reviewer who trusts the universally-quantified intro misreads the diagnostics shard's emission preconditions for `loom/load/extension-bootstrap-failed` and `loom/load/host-incompatible`, and may build the persistent-diagnostic channel assuming the renderer is already registered for every row defined here. The fallback-chain routing both rows mandate is the safety net, so this is unlikely to produce a wrong implementation — but the hub's framing is internally inconsistent with the row text it forward-links to, which is the defect.

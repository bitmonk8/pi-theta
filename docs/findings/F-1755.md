## Finding

In `capability-probe.md` the brevity-summary paragraph states: "`(d)` is part of step `(c)`'s pinned-constants execution group and **shares its short-circuit slot**, and `(e)` is the trailing `typebox` host-shape check." A nearly-identical phrase recurs at the intra-(d) site describing the four-package iteration ("the four-package iteration **shares step (c)'s short-circuit slot** and does not aggregate failures across packages").

"Short-circuit slot" is not defined anywhere in the spec corpus — it appears only at these two sites in `capability-probe.md` and nowhere else. The phrase admits at least three readings: (i) (d) shares (c)'s `kind` discriminator value (false — (d) routes to its own `peer-dep-out-of-range` / `peer-dep-malformed-version` kinds); (ii) (d) shares (c)'s position in the short-circuit chain (i.e. runs "as part of" (c) rather than after it); (iii) the intended reading — (d) participates in the same stop-on-first-failure discipline as the other sub-steps and, on failure, prevents (e) from running on equal footing with the other steps. The fixed order paragraph immediately following clarifies (`(c)+(d)` is one step in the `(a)→(b)→(c)+(d)→(e)` order) but doesn't reach back to redefine the "slot" jargon, leaving the prior sentence as the lexically-prior definition site for the relationship.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Rewrite both occurrences of "shares … short-circuit slot" to name the actual shared property — the stop-on-first-failure discipline — and the disposition on failure, dropping the "slot" coinage entirely.

For the brevity-summary paragraph at line 64, replace

> `(d)` is part of step `(c)`'s pinned-constants execution group and shares its short-circuit slot, and `(e)` is the trailing `typebox` host-shape check.

with

> `(d)` is sourced from the same pinned-constants block as step `(c)` and participates in the same stop-on-first-failure discipline: a step `(d)` failure emits `loom/load/host-incompatible` with its own `kind` (`peer-dep-out-of-range` or `peer-dep-malformed-version`) and prevents step `(e)` from running, on equal footing with a step `(c)` failure. `(e)` is the trailing `typebox` host-shape check.

For the intra-(d) iteration paragraph at line 53, replace

> the four-package iteration shares step `(c)`'s short-circuit slot and does not aggregate failures across packages.

with

> the four-package iteration follows the same stop-on-first-failure discipline as the other probe steps and does not aggregate failures across packages.

Edge cases for the implementer:

- The rewrite is wording-only; `V9a` / `V9a-T` tests are unaffected and need no change.
- Preserve the existing per-`kind` routing language exactly — the rewrite must not imply (d) inherits a `kind` value from (c).
- Keep the fixed-order recap (`(a)→(b)→(c)+(d)→(e)`) unchanged; it already conveys the relative positioning correctly.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer reading the brevity-summary paragraph in isolation can plausibly conclude either that step (d) reuses step (c)'s `kind` value (it doesn't) or that step (d) executes as a sub-loop inside step (c) (it doesn't — they share a pinned-constants source-of-truth block but run as sequential probe steps). The fixed-order paragraph immediately below recovers the intent, so the defect is recoverable in context; but at the level of a single-sentence quotation lifted into review, lens, or code-comment material, the wording is misleading.

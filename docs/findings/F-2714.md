## Finding

`H6a` (`docs/plan_topics/H6a-live-corpus-activation.md`) gates its completion on three release-time activities that fail independently, require different execution environments, and produce separate evidence records, yet must all be discharged in one task and recorded in one gate-activation commit message:

1. **The nine-item release-time residue inspection** — a reviewer manually reading `src/**`, the repo-root doc artifacts, the `spec_topics/**` corpus, `coverage-matrix.md`, the test corpus, `extensions/index.ts`, and the `H3a` timing-primitive list (`conventions.md#release-time-residue-inspection`), recording each of nine items clean-or-dispositioned.
2. **The manual real-host smoke run** — driving `H7a`'s committed multi-feature fixture `.loom` against a live Pi host under the shipping SDK pin, scored against the five model-output-invariant criteria in `real-host-smoke-gate.md` (needs live-host credentials and an environment the other two activities do not).
3. **The mechanical gate-activation flip** — reconfiguring the `H5a`/`H5c` closing gate from its seeded-fixture footing to its live-corpus footing, plus the removed-mapping / removed-citation reddening assertions, a code-and-CI act with no live-host or reviewer-reading dependency.

Each activity can fail on its own and recovers differently: a dirty residue item is fixed in `src/**` or docs; a smoke failure is a live-host behavioural divergence; a bad flip is reverted by a single commit. An agent cannot meaningfully drive a live-host smoke run, conduct a nine-item manual corpus sweep, and land the mechanical gate flip as one coherent verify-and-ship pass. Conflating them in one Ships-when also makes partial states (e.g. smoke green but a residue item dirty) impossible to represent at the leaf's completion tag.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The release gate cannot be discharged in one coherent task: a single agent must simultaneously hold live-host credentials, conduct a nine-item manual corpus sweep, and land a mechanical CI flip, then attest all three in one commit message. A partial outcome (one activity green, another dirty) has no clean representation at the leaf's completion tag, and the gate's three independently-failable verifications are entangled where remediation is most expensive.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Split the three activities into separate leaves so each independently-failable obligation lands and records evidence on its own, with the mechanical flip last and confirming the prior records:

- New leaf `<new>` (residue inspection): cites `conventions.md` *Loom 1.0 release-time residue inspection* (`#release-time-residue-inspection`) in its **Convention.** field; **Adds.** executes the nine-item manual sweep and records each item clean-or-dispositioned in its own commit message (the falsifiable-record convention `H6a` currently uses); **Deps.** `H5b` (so it runs after the canary's complete coverage-producing set lands).
- New leaf `<new>` (real-host smoke run): cites `real-host-smoke-gate.md`; **Adds.** drives `H7a`'s committed fixture `.loom` against a live Pi host under the shipping SDK pin and records the run's falsifiable evidence (pin literal, owner, date, observed result against the narrowed model-output-invariant criterion) in its own commit message; **Deps.** `H7a` (the fixture producer).
- Narrowed `H6a`: retains only the mechanical flip of the `H5a`/`H5c` closing gate from seeded-fixture to live-corpus footing and the removed-mapping / removed-citation reddening assertions; its **Ships when** additionally confirms the two new leaves' evidence records are present and clean (replacing the inline smoke and residue text). **Deps.** `H5b, H5c, <new>` (residue), `<new>` (smoke); the `H7a` edge is pulled transitively through the smoke leaf.

`H6a` keeps its ID and remains the terminal release-gate flip, so `plan.md` item 5, the `coverage-matrix.md` intro, `H5b`, and `real-host-smoke-gate.md` references to H6a as the gate-activation owner stay correct; only the two new leaf links need adding to `plan.md`'s Horizontal-phases listing and Release-gate section.

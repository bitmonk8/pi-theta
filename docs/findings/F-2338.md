## Finding

The "Per-boundary routing *decision*" Tests bullet in `V5e` and its paired `V5e-T` ends with two sentences that are not acceptance criteria for this leaf:

> The actual wrapping of a depth-6 breach into each carrier is asserted at the site owner: `ValidationError` at `V13c`, `CodeToolError` at `V14e`, `InvokeInfraError` at `V15j`, the slash-load cross-route at `V4e`. The model-driven and slash-load `params` rows produce no loom-code `Err` (model feedback / load-time respectively) and are decision-only here.

`conventions.md` §Leaf format defines a Tests bullet as one assertion per REQ-ID, citing the ID inline — i.e. the bullet states what *this* leaf reds-out on. The two trailing sentences instead describe how ceiling-#4 enforcement is distributed across the DAG (which downstream leaves assert the destination-surface wrapping, and which rows produce no loom-code `Err`). That is descriptive scope context, not a property `V5e`/`V5e-T` asserts.

The text appears verbatim in both paired files. It is the residue of a prior fix (resolving "V5e per-boundary routing test asserts destination error surfaces its Deps cannot reach") that correctly narrowed `V5e`'s assertion to the in-isolation routing *decision* but parked the explanatory cross-leaf attribution inside the Tests bullet rather than in `Adds`.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Relocate the two trailing cross-leaf sentences out of the "Per-boundary routing *decision*" Tests bullet in both paired files, and house the distribution context in `V5e`'s `Adds`.

- In `docs/plan_topics/V5e-depth-enforcement.md`, strike from the Tests bullet the text: "The actual wrapping of a depth-6 breach into each carrier is asserted at the site owner: `ValidationError` at `V13c`, `CodeToolError` at `V14e`, `InvokeInfraError` at `V15j`, the slash-load cross-route at `V4e`. The model-driven and slash-load `params` rows produce no loom-code `Err` (model feedback / load-time respectively) and are decision-only here." The bullet then retains only the routing-decision assertion (which of the five site-classes maps to which destination surface class, asserted in isolation against the harness).
- Carry the same struck content into `V5e`'s `Adds` as scope context describing how ceiling-#4 enforcement is distributed across the DAG (the site-owner leaves that assert the per-carrier wrapping, and the rows that produce no loom-code `Err`).
- In `docs/plan_topics/V5e-T-depth-enforcement.md`, strike the identical trailing sentences from the mirrored Tests bullet so the test task's bullet states only the routing-decision assertion.

Cite the same site-owner leaf IDs already present (`V13c`, `V14e`, `V15j`, `V4e`) when moving the text; do not introduce new IDs.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer reading the Tests bullet sees cross-leaf scope narrative interleaved with the one assertion the bullet actually mandates, so the boundary between "what `V5e` reds-out on" and "what downstream leaves own" is blurred at the point of authoring the test. The bullet's explicit "asserted at the site owner" disclaimer keeps the reader from over-implementing, so this degrades clarity rather than breaking pickability or coverage.

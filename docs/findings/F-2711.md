## Finding

`H4a` ("Extension factory shell and end-to-end harness") delivers five things in one horizontal leaf: (1) the `extensions/index.ts` thin delegating entry shim; (2) the `src/**` Pi extension factory (synchronous-arm, never-throw boundary); (3) the reusable end-to-end harness loading the extension against an in-process Pi session double and driving a slash dispatch; (4) the in-memory fixture-supply mechanism that feeds `.loom` content to discovery without a `FileSystem` seam; and (5) the **session-double fidelity contract** plus the **minimal response-emission capability** that makes the contract self-checkable, asserted by a harness self-check over four axes (streamed-token order vs `ctx.waitForIdle()`, single-turn append, the `pi.on` cancel-forward subscription, cancellation propagation).

Deliverables (1)–(4) form a coherent scaffold unit: the shim, factory, harness, and fixture supply are the substrate every harness-driven leaf and `M`/`M-T` build on, and `H4a`'s "no-op command dispatches end-to-end" gate exercises them together. Deliverable (5) is a distinct, separately-gateable artefact — it has its own `Tests.` bullet, its own self-check, and its own Ships-when clause ("the session-double fidelity-contract self-check passes"), and it can fail or be re-verified independently of whether the no-op dispatch works. It also has its own downstream coupling: `H4b` "extends this minimal emission capability rather than introducing emission from scratch," so the emission capability — not the bare harness — is `H4b`'s real prerequisite.

`H4a` was already de-bundled once (the scripted-injection response-programming surface was extracted to `H4b`); the residual mixing of the fidelity contract / minimal-emission deliverable with the core scaffold is the remaining atomicity gap.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer can still build all five deliverables in one pass and produce a working leaf, so nothing is blocked or ambiguous. The cost is reviewability and incremental shipping: a fidelity-contract or minimal-emission regression cannot be re-verified without re-running the whole `H4a` gate, and the leaf's diff conflates the core scaffold (which `M`/`M-T` and every harness-driven V-leaf depend on) with a behaviour-modelling artefact that only the fidelity self-check and `H4b`'s emission extension consume.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Keep `H4a` as the core-scaffold leaf and extract the fidelity deliverable into a new horizontal leaf (`<new>`, e.g. an `H4d`-form ID the implementer allocates per the plan's `H<n><letter>` scheme — do not rename `H4a`, which `M`, `M-T`, `H4b`, `H7a`, `V18d`, and the harness-driven V-leaves already reference by Deps).

- In `H4a`: remove from **Adds** the session-double fidelity-contract sentence, the minimal response-emission capability sentence, and the self-check description; retain the shim, `src/**` factory, never-throw boundary, harness, in-process double, and in-memory fixture-supply text. Remove the third `Tests.` bullet (the fidelity-contract self-check). Reduce **Ships when** to: ``npm test`` loads the extension through the harness and dispatches a no-op command end-to-end (strike "and the session-double fidelity-contract self-check passes").
- In the new `<new>` leaf (**Convention.** field, like `H4a`): place the session-double fidelity contract (the four axes with their `conversation-drive.md` / `host-interfaces-core.md` / `cancellation.md` cross-references), the minimal response-emission capability, and the fidelity-contract self-check `Tests.` bullet, currently in `H4a`. Set its **Deps** to `H4a`. Its **Ships when** is that the fidelity-contract self-check passes.
- In `H4b`: retarget **Deps** from `H4a` to `<new>`, because `H4b` extends the minimal-emission capability that moves into `<new>`. `H4c` (Deps `H4b`) carries the edge transitively and needs no edit. `M`/`M-T` keep their `H4a` edge (they consume the harness and fixture supply, not the fidelity self-check).
- In `docs/plan.md` §Horizontal phases, add a list entry linking the new `<new>` leaf after `H4a`.

The new leaf is convention-backed (no spec REQ-ID), so it is not added to `coverage-matrix.md` or `H5b`'s Deps. Edge cases the implementer must watch: any other consumer that relies specifically on the double's emission behaviour (rather than the bare harness) must depend on `<new>`, not `H4a` — audit the harness-driven leaves' Deps for emission use during the split; `M`/`M-T`'s no-op happy-path dispatch does not require emission and stays on `H4a`.

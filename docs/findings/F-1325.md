## Finding

The `V17b-T` tests task carries a single **Tests** bullet for the forwarding-listener throw-trap (coverage-matrix row `cka-32`, `cancellation.md` Â§*Forwarding-listener throw*). That one bullet folds together four independently-failing obligations: a throw injected from each of the three steady-state forwarding listeners (the slash-command `ctx.signal`-aborted trigger, the tool-exposed `signal`-aborted trigger, and the `invoke`-parent derived-controller trigger), the routing of that defect through `loom/runtime/internal-error` (and the `cause: "internal_error"` arm of `InvokeInfraError` at an `invoke` parent), the non-swallowing assertion (`source.signal.aborted` stays `true` and the next `Checkpoint`-seam await still surfaces `Err(QueryError { kind: "cancelled" })`), and â€” listed under "Edge cases" â€” the first-source-wins one-shot guard on `loomAbort.abort()`, where a throw on a re-entrant *second* trigger must not re-stamp the reason.

The three steady-state injection points plus the routing/non-swallow assertions form one coherent harness-driven scenario. The first-source-wins one-shot guard is a different mechanism that requires a distinct fixture: it exercises a re-entrant second `abort()` call rather than the first-source steady-state path. Bundling it into the same bullet means a red on the re-entrant-guard obligation is indistinguishable, at the plan-bullet level, from a red on the steady-state trap â€” the implementer and any later reviewer cannot attribute the failure to the obligation that actually broke.

The convention requires at least one **Tests** bullet per REQ-ID (`conventions.md` Â§Leaf format) and explicitly permits a leaf to carry multiple bullets that cite the same REQ-ID family ("each Tests bullet still cites its REQ-ID"). Splitting this bullet is therefore convention-compliant and improves failure attribution without changing what `cka-32` closes.

## Solution Space

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V17b-T-forwarding-listener-throw-trap.md`, replace the single **Tests** bullet with two bullets that both cite the `cka-32` / *Forwarding-listener throw* obligation:

- A steady-state bullet covering the three forwarding-listener injection points (slash-command `ctx.signal`-aborted, tool-exposed `signal`-aborted, `invoke`-parent derived-controller), the routing through `loom/runtime/internal-error` (including the `cause: "internal_error"` arm of `InvokeInfraError` at an `invoke` parent), and the non-swallow assertion (`source.signal.aborted` stays `true`; the next `Checkpoint`-seam await still surfaces `Err(QueryError { kind: "cancelled" })`).
- A separate bullet for the first-source-wins one-shot guard re-entrant edge case: a throw on a re-entrant second `loomAbort.abort()` must not re-stamp the reason.

Keep the existing parenthetical noting that the `session_shutdown` teardown-iteration swallow is out of scope (governed by its own sub-step-2 rule at `V9g`); attach it to whichever bullet reads most naturally.

Apply the same two-bullet split to the mirror **Tests** bullet in `docs/plan_topics/V17b-forwarding-listener-throw-trap.md` so the paired tests and implementation leaves do not diverge.

No `coverage-matrix.md` change is needed: both bullets continue to close the same `cka-32` row, which maps to `V17b` and is unaffected by bullet granularity.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

If `V17b-T` ships with the merged bullet, a red on the re-entrant first-source-wins guard reads as a generic forwarding-listener-throw failure, so the implementer has no plan-level pointer to which of the four obligations regressed. The leaf is still buildable and convention-compliant; the cost is degraded failure attribution during the TDD red phase, not a coverage gap or behavioural divergence.

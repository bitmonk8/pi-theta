## Finding

The clause-(a) contradiction narrative in `host-prerequisites.md` (the "Clause (a) contradiction narrative" skippable decision-log sub-section under `#degraded-state-host-prerequisites`) grounds its open-contradiction classification on `@earendil-works/pi-coding-agent`'s `docs/extensions.md`, attributing the **entire** session-replacement lifecycle to one named section: *"Session replacement lifecycle and footguns"*. The attributed narrative covers four distinct claims — (1) on `/new`, `/resume`, `/fork` Pi tears the old runtime down and **reloads-and-rebinds extensions for the new session**, (2) then fires `session_start { reason: … }` **followed by `resources_discover`**, (3) it **directs extensions to do cleanup in `session_shutdown` and reestablish in-memory state in `session_start`**, and (4) the old runtime is torn down / the replacement session rebound / the new instance has already received `session_start`, with captured old `pi`/`ctx` objects stale and throwing if used.

Only claims (4) live in the cited section. In the pinned Pi SDK doc, *"Session replacement lifecycle and footguns"* is scoped entirely to the `withSession` callback: it opens "`withSession` receives a fresh `ReplacedSessionContext`…" and every bullet describes `withSession` preconditions and footguns (the teardown/rebind/`session_start` ordering is stated only as the precondition for *when `withSession` runs*; the stale-captured-objects-throw claim is bullet 3). The reload-and-rebind claim (1), the `resources_discover` ordering (2), and the explicit cleanup-in-`session_shutdown`/reestablish-in-`session_start` guidance (3) are not in that section — they are in the **event-handler sections** (`session_before_switch`, which covers `/new` and `/resume`, and `session_before_fork`), each of which ends with the prose "pi emits `session_shutdown` for the old extension instance, reloads and rebinds extensions for the new session, then emits `session_start`… Do cleanup work in `session_shutdown`, then reestablish any in-memory state in `session_start`," and in the lifecycle diagrams that show the `session_start` → `resources_discover` ordering.

The consequence is an auditability gap: an auditor or version-bump contributor who opens only the cited *"Session replacement lifecycle and footguns"* section — the documented evidentiary basis for clause (a)'s open-contradiction classification — finds the teardown/stale-object confirmation but not the reload-and-rebind claim, which is precisely the load-bearing fact the contradiction turns on (a fresh, non-drained `LoomRegistry` after rebind). The narrative also names the new-session event as governed by that single section rather than the `session_before_switch` event handler (note: the actual Pi event covering `/new` and `/resume` is `session_before_switch`, not `session_before_new_session`).

Inspecting `git show 7447d1e:.../host-prerequisites.md` confirms the narrative attributed the full lifecycle (reload-and-rebind, the `resources_discover` ordering, the cleanup-in-`session_shutdown`/reestablish-in-`session_start` guidance, and the teardown/stale-object claims) to the single *"Session replacement lifecycle and footguns"* section from the moment it was written — the mis-scoped citation is original to the narrative, not a later drift. A subsequent refactor (`27a023e`, F-1535) relocated the narrative into the present skippable sub-section but carried the citation wording unchanged (net-zero occurrence count, so `-S` does not surface it). The defect has therefore been present since the narrative's inception.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Widen the citation in the clause-(a) contradiction narrative so it names every `docs/extensions.md` location that carries the attributed claims, rather than a single section:

- The reload-and-rebind claim and the cleanup-in-`session_shutdown`/reestablish-in-`session_start` guidance come from the **`session_before_switch`** event section (covering `/new` and `/resume`) and the **`session_before_fork`** event section — each ends with that exact lifecycle prose. Cite these for claims (1) and (3).
- The `session_start` → `resources_discover` ordering comes from the lifecycle diagrams (and the `resources_discover` "fired after `session_start`" note). Cite these for claim (2).
- The teardown / rebind / "new instance has already received `session_start`" precondition and the "captured old `pi`/`ctx` are stale and throw" footgun come from the **"Session replacement lifecycle and footguns"** section — keep this citation for claim (4).

Correct the event name: the new-session/resume lifecycle is governed by `session_before_switch`, not a `session_before_new_session` event. Keep the narrative's open-contradiction conclusion unchanged — only the attribution of which `docs/extensions.md` location states each fact needs broadening. No normative obligation, REQ-ID, or acceptance criterion changes.

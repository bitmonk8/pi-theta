## Finding

The `masked` field's wire contract is split across two owner pages in two different subtrees. The `## masked field` section in `docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md` owns two clauses — the closed identifier set (`"ceiling#1"` … `"ceiling#4"`) with the canonical-absence rule (omitted, never `[]`), and the wire-location split (`details.masked` for diagnostic-shape surfaces, `details.event.masked` for runtime-event surfaces). Every other element of the same field's wire contract — the per-site reachable mask domain table, the V1 reachable predicate, the pure-read MUST on the masking probe, the verbatim-copy obligation on cascade-twin re-emission, and the dedup-key non-inclusion rule — is owned by PIC-1 in `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/runtime-event-channel.md`.

The split is acknowledged in the corpus: PIC-1 forward-links the closed set and wire-location clauses to `ceilings-3-and-4.md`, the `ceilings-3-and-4.md` `## masked field` section back-links the other clauses to PIC-1, and `diagnostic-shape.md` § `masked` reaches into both to assemble its own restatement. The closed-set + wire-location pair is the smaller, more localised half; PIC-1 is the larger and more central owner already carrying the predicate, the pure-read MUST, the verbatim-copy obligation, and the dedup-key rule. An implementer assembling the on-the-wire shape of `masked` for either `Diagnostic` or `RuntimeEvent` reads both pages; the split exists for ownership-rule reasons (the closed set is a cross-ceiling concept; PIC-1 is the Pi-integration wire owner), but the wire contract itself is a single coherent surface.

PIC-1 is the larger, more wire-shaped half of the contract and already sits on the Pi-integration-contract page that owns every other wire-shaped surface (`sendMessage` call shape, `details` variant matrix, `RuntimeEvent` payload type block, dedup tuple); lifting the closed enum and wire-location prose up into PIC-1 joins them with the predicate and probe rules that co-constrain them.

- `ceilings-3-and-4.md` `## masked field`: collapse to a one-paragraph stub anchored at `#masked-field` that says the field is owned by PIC-1 and forward-links there; keep the section heading and the `<a id="masked-field">` anchor so the existing inbound citations (`diagnostic-shape.md`, `V16a-ceiling-order-masked.md`, the page's own CIO-6 bullet) continue to resolve. ## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer wiring `masked` onto a `Diagnostic` or `RuntimeEvent` payload, or onto the in-memory shape consumed by the cross-ceiling arbitration seam, must read both `ceilings-3-and-4.md` and PIC-1 to obtain the full field contract; missing one half yields either an under-constrained probe (no pure-read MUST) or an under-specified wire shape (no enum, no `details.*` location). The cross-links between the two owners are in place, so a careful reader is not led to an incorrect implementation; the cost is reading time and the structural awkwardness of a single wire contract split across two subtrees.

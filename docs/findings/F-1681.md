## Finding

In `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/tool-registration-lifetime.md`, the PIC-19 paragraph (line 20) reads:

> "…leaves the install uncommitted under the synchronous-completion-and-commit precondition recorded at [snapshot/restore Pi behavioural preconditions](#snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions) **above**…"

The anchor `snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions` is actually defined seven lines later, on line 27 (inside the second bullet of the "Consequences" list that follows PIC-19). PIC-19 is the first rule on the page that names the precondition; the precondition itself lives below it, not above.

The hyperlink resolves regardless, but a linear reader who pauses on PIC-19 to verify the cited precondition will scan upward through PIC-8, PIC-17, and the page intro and find nothing — the same disorientation the other in-page directional pointers ("above"/"below") on this page are deliberately calibrated to avoid (every other PIC-N back-reference on the page that uses "above" cites a target that actually appears above).

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** `19b193b` ("pi-loom spec: resolve 'PIC-17 active-set swap/snapshot throws have no failure-mode rule'", 2026-06-05)
**History:** `git log -S 'snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions' -- docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/tool-registration-lifetime.md` identifies `19b193b` as the commit that introduced both the PIC-19 paragraph and its `[…](#snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions) above` pointer. Inspecting that commit's pre-image (`git show 19b193b^:…`) confirms the `snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions` anchor already existed *below* the PIC-19 insertion point (on the bullet that is now line 27 of the file) before the commit ran — so the directional pointer was authored wrong in the very commit that introduced it, rather than being correct at introduction and broken by a later reflow. Commit `391bcb2` later rephrased the precondition text from "synchronous-and-atomic" to "synchronous-completion-and-commit" but did not touch the `above` token or the anchor's location relative to PIC-19.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/tool-registration-lifetime.md` line 20, change the single word `above` to `below` in the phrase `recorded at [snapshot/restore Pi behavioural preconditions](#snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions) above`. No other edit is required: the anchor target, the link text, and the surrounding clause structure all stay as-is, and no other site on the page paraphrases this directional cue (every other reference to the precondition uses an explicit cross-file link rather than an "above"/"below" cue, so no follow-on flips are owed).

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader who follows the "above" cue scans the wrong direction and either misses the precondition entirely or concludes it isn't pinned on the page (it is — just below). The cited synchronous-completion-and-commit precondition is load-bearing for PIC-19's "no restore owed on step-2 throw" claim, so the misdirection lands on a clause an implementer actually needs to read to justify the rule.

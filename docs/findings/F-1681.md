## Problem

In `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/tool-registration-lifetime.md`, the PIC-19 paragraph cites the precondition at `[snapshot/restore Pi behavioural preconditions](#snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions)` with the directional cue **above**, but the anchor `snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions` is actually defined below PIC-19 (inside the "Consequences" list that follows the rule). A linear reader who pauses on PIC-19 to verify the cited precondition scans upward and finds nothing — the precondition is load-bearing for PIC-19's "no restore owed on step-2 throw" claim, so the misdirection lands on a clause an implementer needs to read.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** `19b193b` ("pi-loom spec: resolve 'PIC-17 active-set swap/snapshot throws have no failure-mode rule'", 2026-06-05)
**History:** `git log -S 'snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions' -- docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/tool-registration-lifetime.md` identifies `19b193b` as the commit that introduced both the PIC-19 paragraph and its `[…](#snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions) above` pointer. Inspecting that commit's pre-image (`git show 19b193b^:…`) confirms the `snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions` anchor already existed *below* the PIC-19 insertion point (on the bullet that is now line 27 of the file) before the commit ran — so the directional pointer was authored wrong in the very commit that introduced it, rather than being correct at introduction and broken by a later reflow. Commit `391bcb2` later rephrased the precondition text from "synchronous-and-atomic" to "synchronous-completion-and-commit" but did not touch the `above` token or the anchor's location relative to PIC-19.

## Solution approach

In the PIC-19 paragraph, change the directional cue on the `#snapshot-restore-pi-behavioural-preconditions` cross-reference from `above` to `below` so it points the reader downward to where the anchor is defined.

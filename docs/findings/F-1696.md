## Finding

PIC-55 in `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/registration-steps.md` (under step 5) splits the post-`error` / post-throw watcher posture into two arms keyed on whether "the watcher keeps delivering events on the watched roots after the error" (transient `ctx.ui.notify` toast, no further action) versus "the error or throw leaves one or more watched roots no longer delivering events" (persistent `loom/runtime/watcher-terminated` `loom-system-note`, watcher torn down, operator runs `/reload`). The diagnostic-table row for `loom/runtime/watcher-terminated` in `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/code-registry-runtime.md` repeats the same predicate verbatim ("its `error` route fired or it threw such that one or more watched roots stop delivering events"), and the toast-channel paragraph in `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md` is now tied to the same two-arm split.

The classifying predicate is not observable. The `FileWatcher` seam (PIC-14, `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/host-interfaces-services.md`) carries only the three event kinds `add | change | unlink` plus the `Unsubscribe` return; the adapter sees chokidar's `error` event (whose payload is an `Error`, with no "is-terminal" / "I have stopped delivering" flag) and any synchronous throw out of the event callback. "No longer delivering events" is an absence-of-future-events condition that cannot be decided at the moment the `error` or throw is observed â€” chokidar publishes no contract that says "after this error I will / will not continue delivering". Two reasonable implementers will therefore diverge: one routes every `error` to the transient toast and never emits `watcher-terminated` (so a dead watcher silently strands hot-reload), the other routes every `error` to the persistent note and unnecessarily prompts `/reload` for benign transient errors.

PIC-55 must either name the observable input the adapter uses to pick an arm (e.g. a closed list of terminal `Error.code` values, or a "the adapter unconditionally calls `close()` on `error` and re-arms only by operator `/reload`" rule), or be collapsed to a single arm whose classifier is the act of observing `error` / a sync throw itself.

## Solution Space

### Recommendation

Treat the post-`error` / post-throw watcher posture as a single terminal disposition; do not split it on whether the watcher keeps delivering events. On any chokidar `error` event or synchronous throw out of an adapter-installed event callback, the adapter closes the underlying chokidar `FSWatcher` and the runtime emits the single persistent `loom/runtime/watcher-terminated` `loom-system-note`. There is no transient-toast arm for the chokidar-error case.

Apply these spec edits:

- PIC-55 (`docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/registration-steps.md`, under step 5): replace the two-case body with a single posture â€” "On any `error` event or synchronous throw out of the adapter's event callback, the adapter closes the watcher and the runtime emits a single persistent `loom/runtime/watcher-terminated` `loom-system-note`." Keep the existing drain-state-tag-MUST-NOT-write paragraph; remove the `(Continues-delivering.)` paragraph.
- `code-registry-runtime.md`, `loom/runtime/watcher-terminated` row: change the trigger clause to "its `error` route fired or it threw, and the adapter consequently closed it" â€” an observable condition with no future-events predicate.
- `diagnostic-shape.md`, `transient-toasts` paragraph case (1): remove the "the terminal stopped-delivery case additionally emitsâ€¦" qualifier and the "transient toast stays reserved for the informational chokidar-error case" carve-out; the chokidar-error case is no longer a toast case. Retain the toast surface for the other two listed origins (settings I/O exceptions outside `loom/load/settings-*`, internal invariant violations).
- `host-interfaces-services.md` PIC-14 bullet on `watch`: no edit required; the cross-reference back to PIC-55 still holds.

Implementer edge cases to honour:

- The adapter's `close()` call must be idempotent against the existing `Unsubscribe` (PIC-14 already requires this).
- The synchronous-throw path must catch out of every adapter-installed event handler â€” not just the three load-bearing kinds â€” so that a throw escaping the filtered-out `addDir` / `unlinkDir` / `ready` handlers also reaches the terminal path.
- The drain-state-tag MUST-NOT-write rule under PIC-55 carries over verbatim.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two implementers building against PIC-55 today will produce materially different observable behaviour: one will silently lose hot-reload after the first chokidar `error` (no `watcher-terminated` note ever fires), the other will demand `/reload` after every transient `error` even when the watcher would have continued. Both can claim conformance, because the spec's bifurcation predicate has no defined observable input the adapter can evaluate at the failure site.

## Finding

The `V9g` `CNCL-4` acceptance text ends with "observed as `loomAbort.signal.reason` at a downstream checkpoint" (Tests bullet and Ships-when in `V9g-session-shutdown.md`, plus the mirrored Tests bullet in `V9g-T-session-shutdown.md`). The lowercase locator "a downstream checkpoint" carries no seam citation, so it is ambiguous: a reader can take it to mean the `Checkpoint` seam owned by `V8a` (`V8a-checkpoint-validator-seams.md` — Adds: "The `Checkpoint` seam"), or merely "some later code location that reads `signal.reason`".

The collision is not hypothetical. `V17a-cancellation-core.md`'s own `CNCL-4` bullet uses the same phrase deliberately to mean the seam — "land an abort via the `Checkpoint` seam (`V8a`) and assert `loomAbort.signal.reason === source.reason` at the downstream checkpoint". Because the identical phrase is seam-anchored in `V17a` and seam-blind in `V9g`, an implementer who reads `V17a` first will reasonably infer that `V9g` also routes its assertion through the `V8a` `Checkpoint` seam — yet `V9g` Deps is `V9g-T, V9e, V9h, V17a`, with no `V8a` edge. The reader is left unsure whether `V9g` has a missing `V8a` dependency or whether the phrase just means "inspect the signal after the abort fires" (the intended reading — the `session_shutdown` handler aborts directly, not via the checkpoint seam).

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** `9081369` ("pi-loom plan: resolve \"CNCL-4 session-shutdown reason facet not authored red or gated\"", Thomas Andersen, 2026-06-10)
**History:** `git log -S 'downstream checkpoint' -- docs/plan_topics/V9g-session-shutdown.md` and `git show 9081369` confirm the phrase "at a downstream checkpoint" entered both `V9g` (Ships-when) and `V9g-T` (new `CNCL-4` Tests bullet) in commit `9081369`. The commit authored the `CNCL-4` session-shutdown reason facet to resolve an earlier review finding; the `V9g-T` `CNCL-4` bullet did not exist before this commit (`git show 9081369^:docs/plan_topics/V9g-T-session-shutdown.md` has no `CNCL-4` or checkpoint reference). The defect is a wording regression introduced while closing a different finding, not a present-since-inception gap.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Disambiguate the locator by replacing the seam-colliding phrase with one that names the observation explicitly. In all three sites — the `CNCL-4` Tests bullet of `V9g-session-shutdown.md`, the Ships-when sentence of `V9g-session-shutdown.md`, and the `CNCL-4` Tests bullet of `V9g-T-session-shutdown.md` — strike "at a downstream checkpoint" and state that the assertion reads `loomAbort.signal.reason` after the `session_shutdown` handler's abort fires (e.g. "… observed as `loomAbort.signal.reason` after the abort fires"). Do not introduce the word "checkpoint" in `V9g`/`V9g-T` for this assertion, since `V9g` aborts directly and does not route through the `V8a` `Checkpoint` seam; this keeps `V9g`'s current Deps (`V9g-T, V9e, V9h, V17a`, no `V8a`) correct rather than implying a missing edge.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Two reasonable implementers diverge on whether `V9g`'s `CNCL-4` assertion must read `loomAbort.signal.reason` through the `V8a` `Checkpoint` seam (prompting a spurious `V8a` Deps edge) or simply inspect the signal after the handler's direct abort. The leaf still produces a working test under either reading, so it is not blocking, but the ambiguity wastes implementer time and risks an unnecessary dependency edge.

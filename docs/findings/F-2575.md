## Finding

The glossary defines a single `caller` entry (`glossary.md`, `<a id="caller">`) that documents two distinct referents: the **slash caller** (the user whose slash-command turn dispatched a loom) and the **invoke parent** (the loom or `.warp` `fn` whose `invoke(...)` / `.loom`-callable call spawned the current loom). The entry explains that the two senses cannot co-occur for one invocation and advises (SHOULD) using the disambiguated terms when bare `caller` is ambiguous, but it gives neither `slash caller` nor `invoke parent` its own anchor, so every cross-reference resolves to the one combined `#caller` target.

Bare "caller" is then used pervasively in normative prose with one sense or the other left to context: `invocation.md` uses it throughout for the invoke parent (the "Caller mode" matrix column, "caller's current conversation", "caller's settings are not inherited"); `errors-and-results/error-model.md` uses "the caller observes…" and "Panics surface to the loom's caller as…" across both senses; `binder/binder-model-and-context.md` uses "caller's session" for the slash caller; `pi-integration-contract/subagent.md` uses "Forwarded from the caller's `ExtensionCommandContext`" for the invoke parent; `overview.md` mixes "an `invoke` caller" and "the caller's current conversation". The disambiguated terms already appear in places (`slash-invocation.md` SLSH-3, the `error-model.md` per-cause table columns), confirming the spec intends the split — but the term remains overloaded at the definition site and across the prose that the entry is meant to govern.

This is a naming-consistency defect rather than a behavioural one: in nearly every site the surrounding context fixes the sense, and the no-co-occurrence rule prevents true contradiction. The cost is reader/author/reviewer load and the absence of anchorable canonical terms.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `53f5831` (bare-"caller" prose, present since inception); `d54b426` (central `caller` glossary entry authored as a single combined entry)
**History:** Bare "caller" entered the corpus at `53f5831` (2026-05-03, "spec: rewrite query primitive, system prompts, and schema syntax") and was used in invocation prose for both senses well before any central glossary existed. No `caller` glossary entry existed until `d54b426` (2026-05-07, resolving "Glossary cluster: six independent defects in spec_topics/glossary.md"), which authored the term directly in its present dual-sense form — both referents under one entry, with the disambiguated terms named only inline and gated by a SHOULD. A later commit (`0033898`) added the `<a id="caller">` anchor but did not split the entry. The finding therefore sits at the interaction of pre-existing pervasive bare usage and the `d54b426` decision to document, rather than separate, the two referents.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Promote the two referents to independently anchorable terms while keeping a single disambiguation hub:

- In `glossary.md`, keep the `caller` entry as the hub that states the no-co-occurrence rule, and give `slash caller` and `invoke parent` their own `<a id="slash-caller">` / `<a id="invoke-parent">` anchors (either as sub-entries or as inline anchors on the bolded terms within the `caller` entry) so cross-references can target the exact sense.
- Replace bare "caller" with the disambiguated term at cross-cutting sites where the local context does not already fix the sense — specifically the `error-model.md` panic/cancellation prose and the `overview.md` / `overview-and-orientation.md` mode descriptions.
- Leave page-local uses where the sense is fixed by the page's subject intact (e.g. the `invocation.md` "Caller mode" matrix and its surrounding prose, where "caller" uniformly means the invoke parent); add a single one-line scoping note at the top of that page stating that bare "caller" there denotes the invoke parent.

Edge cases to watch: the `error-model.md` per-cause table already separates "invoke parent" and "Slash caller" columns — keep those column headers as the canonical phrasing; and "root chain may have a slash caller" wording for nested invokes must stay attached to the chain root, not the immediate invoke parent.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

If shipped unfixed, the spec remains readable — context resolves the sense at almost every site — but cross-references cannot anchor to the precise term, and the SHOULD-only guidance lets new prose reintroduce bare ambiguous "caller". The risk is reviewer/author drift and re-litigation, not divergent implementations.

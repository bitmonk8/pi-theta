## Finding

`coverage-matrix.md` uses the word "retired" for two incompatible states. In the
*Numbered REQ-IDs* preamble, a *retired interior ID* is a number inside a `…`
range that the prefix's `## Retired REQ-IDs` table accounts for: being retired it
"carries no citing test by design" and is excluded from the mapped set. This is the
**removal** sense — the obligation is gone. The same sense is reused in the GOV-*
section ("REQ-ID anchoring, retirement, …") and in the `SNK-j` retirement note.

The ten code-keyed rows (`cka-7`, `cka-12`, `cka-18`, `cka-21`, `cka-22`, `cka-23`,
`cka-24`, `cka-25`, `cka-28`, `cka-29`) use "retired as a code-keyed-area row" to
mean the opposite: the row's obligations were **migrated** to numbered `PREFIX-N`
REQ-IDs (GOV-22 drain) and still exist, mapped to a closing leaf in the *Numbered
REQ-IDs* table. The obligation is preserved, not removed.

A reader scanning the cka table and seeing "retired" can infer the row's
obligations are gone (no closing leaf required, no citing test) when in fact they
remain live under a renumbered ID. The full cell prose ("now coined as numbered …
and mapped to their closing leaf …") disambiguates on a careful read, but the
single overloaded term carries the opposite of its meaning everywhere else in the
file.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader filtering the cka table for live work can mis-read a migrated row as a
removed obligation, concluding (wrongly) that its REQ-IDs need no closing leaf or
citing test. The mechanical gates are unaffected (the row's `*(numbered above)*`
cell tokenises identically regardless of wording), so no implementer ships a broken
leaf, but the term inverts its own meaning between the preamble and the table.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced (multi-commit interaction)
**Introducing commits:** `716a13b` (2026-06-25, F-1286) — first use of "retired as a code-keyed-area row" (migration sense), reusing a word that `7103dca` (2026-06-18) had already given the removal sense.
**History:** `7103dca` introduced "retired interior ID" in the *Numbered REQ-IDs* preamble to mean a range-interior ID accounted for in `## Retired REQ-IDs` — a genuine removal that carries no citing test. One week later `716a13b` (the QRY GOV-22 drain) reused "retired" for the `cka-12` row to mean the row's obligations were migrated to numbered REQ-IDs and still exist; `47915a2` (2026-06-27, F-1890) and `ffe2cc9` (2026-06-28, F-2317) propagated the same "retired as a code-keyed-area row" phrasing to the remaining nine cka rows. The two senses have coexisted in the file since `716a13b`.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md`, change the leading phrase of each of the
ten cka rows (`cka-7`, `cka-12`, `cka-18`, `cka-21`, `cka-22`, `cka-23`, `cka-24`,
`cka-25`, `cka-28`, `cka-29`) from `retired as a code-keyed-area row` to
`superseded as a code-keyed-area row — migrated to numbered REQ-IDs`, leaving the
rest of each cell ("the … obligations are now coined as numbered … and mapped to
their closing leaf … in the *Numbered REQ-IDs* table above; no residual …
obligation remains") unchanged.

Reserve "retired" / "retirement" for the removal sense it already carries in the
*Numbered REQ-IDs* preamble ("retired interior ID" and the `## Retired REQ-IDs`
reference), the GOV-* section, and the `SNK-j` retirement note — do not touch those.

The `*(numbered above)*` closing-leaf cells stay verbatim; this change is prose-only
and does not alter any token the H5d / H5e recognisers read.

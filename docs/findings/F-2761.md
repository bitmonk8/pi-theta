## Finding

The schema-lowering pass produces one artifact that `V5f` owns and `V2e` consumes: a per-`$defs` sidecar holding **two** maps — a wire-name translation map and a named-enum-position map (`schema-subset.md` Lowering Algorithm step 5, "a sidecar with two maps"). The producing leaf names it canonically: `V5f`'s **Adds** calls it "the per-schema two-map sidecar (wire-name translation map plus named-enum-position map)", and `V5f`/`V5f-T` **Tests** and **Ships when** reinforce the count ("a two-map sidecar", "the per-schema sidecar two-map shape").

The consuming leaf `V2e` truncates the name. Its **Adds** refers only to "the `V5f`-produced per-schema sidecar", dropping the "two-map" descriptor. A reader who opens `V2e` in isolation cannot tell from the name that the sidecar carries two distinct maps; the count must be recovered by following the back-reference into `V5f`. The artifact, its owning leaf, and its two-map shape are all correct — only the cross-leaf name is inconsistent.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A `V2e` reader cannot tell from the leaf's own prose that the consumed sidecar holds two maps; the count is only recoverable by following the `V5f` back-reference. `V2e`'s Adds already describes both maps' functions (loom-side rebuild plus enum-tag reattach), so no implementer is misled about behaviour — the cost is purely naming consistency across the producer/consumer pair.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V2e-wire-name-translation.md`, **Adds** field, change the phrase `the `V5f`-produced per-schema sidecar` to `the `V5f`-produced per-schema two-map sidecar`, matching `V5f`'s canonical name. No other field in `V2e` and no `V5f` field needs to change — `V5f` already carries the canonical form, and `V5f`/`V5f-T`'s "two-map sidecar" phrasings already convey the count.

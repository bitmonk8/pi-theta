## Finding

`V11f` (`docs/plan_topics/V11f-binder-retry-taxonomy.md`) carries two units that draw on different spec surfaces and different test infrastructure. Unit 1 is the binder per-class retry budget (hard ceiling #3, `HC3-a`…`HC3-e`), the four-class failure taxonomy with its six verbatim failure-mode templates, and the depth-walk fast-fail `<ajv-summary>` rendering — all statically exercisable against the binder envelope, sourced from `ceilings-3-and-4.md` and `determinism-cancellation-failure.md` (taxonomy/templates). Unit 2 is in-flight binder-call cancellation forwarding: landing an abort *during* the binder's live provider call through the `Checkpoint` seam (the only reason `V11f` carries `V17a` in its Deps) and asserting the `loom /<name>: argument binding cancelled` note surfaces while the loom does not run — sourced from `cancellation.md` (*Granularity*/*Surfacing*) and `determinism-cancellation-failure.md` §Cancellation.

The two units require different fixtures (the cancel assertion needs the `V17a` `Checkpoint`-seam abort plumbing and scripted in-flight timing; the retry/taxonomy unit needs none of it) and verify against different obligation families. A reviewer cannot confirm the retry caps, the six templates, the depth-walk rendering, and the in-flight abort behaviour in a single pass. The coupling is not spurious — the abort is landed into the same binder call the retry budget governs, including during a budgeted retry — so the question is whether to separate the units or to document the coupling, not whether they are unrelated.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Extract the cancellation-forwarding unit into a new leaf

**Approach.** Keep `V11f` as the retry-budget + four-class failure taxonomy + six failure-mode templates + depth-walk fast-fail rendering leaf, and move the in-flight binder-call cancellation-forwarding Tests bullet and its Ships-when clause into a new leaf `<new>` (with its `<new>-T` partner) that depends on `V11f` + `V17a` + `V9j` + `H4b`. Drop `V17a` from `V11f`/`V11f-T` Deps once the cancel unit leaves, and adjust the `V11f` title and Adds to drop the "cancellation"/cancellation-forwarding wording.

**Plan edits.** In `V11f`/`V11f-T`: remove the "Binder-call cancellation forwarding" Tests bullet, strike the cancelled-binder clause from Ships-when, remove `V17a` from Deps, and drop "cancellation" from the title and the "cancellation forwarding into the binder call" phrase from Adds. Add the `<new>`/`<new>-T` leaf pair carrying the cancel bullet and Ships-when. Link `<new>` into the V11 slice list in `docs/plan.md`. Add a `coverage-matrix.md` row closing the cancelled-binder arm (`cancellation.md` *Surfacing* cancelled-binder / `determinism-cancellation-failure.md` §Cancellation) at `<new>`. Add `<new>` to `H5b`'s Deps per `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline transitive-completeness. Add `<new>` to `V18d` and/or `H7a` Deps if their runtime-evidence / golden run observes the cancelled-binder note.

**Spec edits.** None.

**Pros.** Each leaf is reviewable in one pass; the retry/taxonomy leaf becomes statically exercisable and sheds its `V17a` infra dependency; the dependency is linear (the cancel leaf sits on the binder-call machinery the retry leaf builds).

**Cons.** Adds a leaf pair, a coverage-matrix row, and an `H5b` Deps edge; the resulting cancel leaf is thin.

**Risks.** Omitting the `H5b` Deps registration silently drops the new leaf from transitive completeness; the cancelled-binder arm currently has no dedicated coverage-matrix row, so the new leaf could ship untraceable (compare the V17b / V15d untraceable-leaf findings).

### Option B — Keep merged, document the coupling

**Approach.** Leave `V11f` as one leaf and add a rationale to `V11f`.Adds (and a parallel note in `V11f-T`) stating why the cancellation forwarding is co-located: the abort is landed into the same in-flight binder call the retry budget governs — including during a budgeted retry — so the cancel assertion exercises the retry machinery and cannot be reviewed independently of it. This mirrors how `V8d` justifies its Clock+IdSource grouping.

**Plan edits.** One rationale sentence in `V11f`.Adds; a parallel note in `V11f-T`.

**Spec edits.** None.

**Pros.** No new leaf, no coverage-row or `H5b` churn; preserves the genuine coupling between the cancel forwarding and the retry-governed binder call.

**Cons.** `V11f` remains a two-spec-surface, two-infra (`V17a`) leaf; the reviewer still confirms retry caps, six templates, depth-walk rendering, and in-flight abort in one pass.

**Risks.** The rationale can rot; the too-large condition persists.

### Recommendation

Take **Option A**. The retry/taxonomy unit and the cancellation-forwarding unit draw on different spec surfaces (`ceilings-3-and-4.md` / `determinism-cancellation-failure.md` taxonomy vs `cancellation.md` *Surfacing*), and the cancel unit is the only part requiring `V17a` `Checkpoint`-seam infra; extracting it makes the retry leaf statically exercisable and leaves a thin cancel leaf on top. Resolve in order — land `V11f` (retry/taxonomy) first so the cancellation-forwarding leaf lands on a stable baseline. Edge cases the implementer must watch: register `<new>` in `H5b`'s Deps and add its `coverage-matrix.md` row for the cancelled-binder arm (otherwise it ships untraceable), and keep the "an abort during a budgeted retry also surfaces the cancelled-binder note" requirement with `<new>`, which depends on `V11f`'s retry machinery being present.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A working leaf can still be produced as bundled — two reasonable implementers would converge on the same behaviour — so nothing breaks at runtime. The cost is single-pass reviewability: the leaf forces a reviewer to verify a statically-exercisable retry/taxonomy unit and a `V17a`-infra-dependent in-flight-abort unit together, and ties the otherwise-static retry half to the `Checkpoint`-seam dependency it would not otherwise need.

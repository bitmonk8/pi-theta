## Finding

`NOCEIL-1 … NOCEIL-4` are *absence* acceptance criteria — they assert that loom 1.0 imposes **no** per-call timeout field (NOCEIL-1), **no** token-domain cap or cumulative budget beyond `ContextOverflowError` (NOCEIL-2), **no** catchable wrapping of host-fatal uncatchable conditions (NOCEIL-3), and **no** loom-level frame-depth ceiling other than the 32-level `invoke`-chain bound (NOCEIL-4). The coverage matrix's Governance section maps each NOCEIL label to one *positive* observable seam: NOCEIL-1 → V6a's `loom/parse/timeout-field-rejected`, NOCEIL-2 → V4d's `ContextOverflowError`, NOCEIL-3 → V4b's `loom/runtime/internal-error` carve-out, NOCEIL-4 → V15b's `INV-4` invoke-depth bound. Those positive seams are each tested by their owning leaf.

The non-existence property itself — that *no other* ceiling has been introduced anywhere in the corpus — is not closed by any leaf or by the H5a closing gate. The coverage matrix routes that cross-cutting closure to "the spec's four-axis Audit methodology in `hard-ceilings.md` (a GOV-15 release-time corpus-review obligation, not a runtime leaf)." That manual audit is the sole instrument asserting the absence claims; a new ceiling introduced in a later leaf (a new token cap, a new depth bound, a new catchable wrap) is not caught by any mechanical gate and would pass CI green.

Compounding the gap, the GOV-15 release-time reviewer inspection that this closure depends on is not enumerated as a reproducible checklist in any plan file, so the four-axis NOCEIL audit currently has no concrete, repeatable procedure a release reviewer is directed to perform.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add the NOCEIL cross-cutting non-existence audit as an explicit, reproducible review item to the GOV-15 release-time reviewer-inspection surface in `docs/plan_topics/conventions.md`. The item must direct the reviewer to perform the four-axis Audit methodology defined in `docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings.md` across the corpus and confirm that no ceiling exists beyond the four enumerated NOCEIL axes — naming each axis and its owning positive seam so the reviewer can cross-check: NOCEIL-1 (`V6a` `loom/parse/timeout-field-rejected`), NOCEIL-2 (`V4d` `ContextOverflowError`), NOCEIL-3 (`V4b` `loom/runtime/internal-error` carve-out), NOCEIL-4 (`V15b` `INV-4` invoke-depth bound). Keep `coverage-matrix.md`'s existing NOCEIL distribution note as the authoritative seam map and have the new checklist item reference it rather than restating the seam list (avoid a second divergent copy).

This item is one entry in the broader GOV-15 reviewer checklist; it must be authored inside whatever checklist structure that related finding establishes, not as a free-standing surface. The spec's four-axis Audit methodology is the procedure — do not author a new audit definition in the plan; cite the existing `hard-ceilings.md` anchor.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The positive NOCEIL seams are mechanically tested, but the negative-space "no other ceiling exists" property is closed only by a manual GOV-15 review whose procedure is not written down. A ceiling silently introduced by a later leaf would not be caught by any gate, and a release reviewer has no reproducible checklist directing the four-axis audit, so the audit risks being skipped or rubber-stamped.

## Problem

`audit-target-categories.md` is the audit-detection page — its `id="audit-target-surface-categories"` paragraph defines the three target surface categories, the carrier-syntax rules, and the typebox carve-out. Wedged into that same paragraph is a separate cluster of normative MUSTs about extending `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY`'s entry-kind taxonomy: the per-category new entry kinds, the minimum-entry-shape predicate (`kind` + `path`) and its closed exemption list, the literal-read assertion's `literal-read/<symptom>` discriminator family and branch-table arms, and the per-`<symptom>` fixture floor. The authoritative home for the literal-read assertion and these obligations is `version-bump-step2.md` step 2(a) (`id="bump-step-2-positive"`), which already back-references *Target surface categories* for per-kind payload shapes — forming a circular dependency in which detection lives where the assertion is named while the assertion's own obligations live where detection is named. An implementer reading either page gets the other half as implicit scope, and the per-`<symptom>` fixture floor — mentioned only here — is easy to miss when implementing the assertion from its own page.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit

**Introducing commits:** `e5cd514` — *pi-loom spec: resolve "T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit"* (2026-05-09)

**History:** The bundled taxonomy-extension paragraph entered the corpus in `e5cd514` as part of the T35 work that added the negative-direction inventory-closure audit to `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md`. That commit introduced both the *Target surface categories* paragraph and step 2(a)'s *Positive direction — literal-read of the imported namespace* in the same edit and placed the new entry-kind / per-kind payload / branch-table / fixture-floor obligations inside *Target surface categories* while leaving step 2(a) to cite them by reference. Subsequent edits did not reopen the split: `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04) mechanically split the over-limit `pi-integration-contract.md` into the `pi-integration-contract/` sub-directory, carrying `audit-target-categories.md` and `version-bump-step2.md` to their current file paths with text and cross-references repointed; `23d28a1` (F-0958) renormalised the audit-cluster file basenames and swept cross-references; `bf92fe6` (F-1290) relocated the unrelated strict-capability absence-arm MUST out of `audit-target-categories.md` into `inventory-audit-intro.md` but did not touch the taxonomy-extension bundle this finding names; `d5a714a` (F-1123) tightened the category-(1)/(3) carrier annotation rules without altering the bundled obligations. The bundling has therefore stood since the audit was introduced.

## Solution approach

Move the taxonomy-extension, minimum-entry-shape, `literal-read/<symptom>` discriminator-family, branch-table, and per-`<symptom>` fixture-floor obligations out of `audit-target-categories.md`'s `id="audit-target-surface-categories"` paragraph into `version-bump-step2.md` step 2(a) (`id="bump-step-2-positive"`), adjacent to the existing `CAPABILITY_OBLIGATIONS` count-equality and unrecognised-`kind` rules already there. Replace the relocated text in `audit-target-categories.md` with a forward-link to the step-2(a) home, leaving that page focused on detection. Repoint the step-2(a) back-references that currently cite *Target surface categories* to the new local home, and re-point the `path`-exemption cross-reference in `audit-target-categories.md`'s `id="strict-capability-absence-pin"` paragraph to the step-2(a) home.

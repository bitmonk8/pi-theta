## Finding

`audit-target-categories.md` is the audit-detection page: it defines the three target surface categories (`pi.<member>`, peer / `typebox` named imports, canonical-`ctx` member access), the carrier-syntax rules, and the typebox carve-out — i.e. *what the audit walks for and how it recognises an in-scope reference*. Wedged into the same enormous *Target surface categories* paragraph, after the category definitions and the typebox carve-out, is a separate cluster of normative MUSTs that has nothing to do with detection. That cluster says the implementation MUST extend `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY`'s entry-kind taxonomy with new per-category kinds; MUST land those new entry kinds (with `kind` discriminator strings and per-kind payload field shapes) before the first audit run on `main`; pins the minimum entry shape (`kind: string` + `path: string`) every audit-participating kind MUST carry, plus the closed exemption list (`node-floor`, `strict-capability-probe`, `peer-dep-range`, `api-coverage`) for the `path` requirement; mandates that the SDK surface-inventory *literal-read assertion* (per `version-bump-step2.md` step 2(a)) gain a `literal-read/<symptom>` discriminator family enforcing the minimum-entry-shape obligation; mandates that the literal-read assertion's *branch table* gain a recognising arm for every new `kind` under the same precondition; and pins a per-`<symptom>` fixture floor with illustrative `<symptom>` tokens.

The authoritative home for the literal-read assertion, its `CAPABILITY_OBLIGATIONS` count-equality gate, its branch-table failure-routing, and the version-bump co-edit obligations is `version-bump-step2.md` step 2(a). That page already cross-references *Target surface categories* for the per-kind payload field shapes ("consuming the new entry's per-kind payload field shape from the implementation's inventory the same edit lands per *Target surface categories* above"), forming a circular dependency: step 2(a) cites detection rules, while the assertion's own minimum-shape and branch-table obligations are smuggled into the page step 2(a) cites. A reader of `audit-target-categories.md` who is implementing the inventory-closure audit's static AST walker gets the literal-read-assertion obligations as implicit scope; a reader of `version-bump-step2.md` step 2(a) who is implementing the build-time literal-read assertion gets only a back-reference and misses the per-kind discriminator family, the minimum-entry-shape predicate, and the per-`<symptom>` fixture floor that the assertion is actually obligated to surface.

The result is two implementations of one contract pulled apart: detection lives where the assertion is named, and assertion lives where detection is named.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit

**Introducing commits:** `e5cd514` — *pi-loom spec: resolve "T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit"* (2026-05-09)

**History:** The bundled taxonomy-extension paragraph entered the corpus in `e5cd514` as part of the T35 work that added the negative-direction inventory-closure audit to `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md`. That commit introduced both the *Target surface categories* paragraph and step 2(a)'s *Positive direction — literal-read of the imported namespace* in the same edit and placed the new entry-kind / per-kind payload / branch-table / fixture-floor obligations inside *Target surface categories* while leaving step 2(a) to cite them by reference. Subsequent edits did not reopen the split: `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04) mechanically split the over-limit `pi-integration-contract.md` into the `pi-integration-contract/` sub-directory, carrying `audit-target-categories.md` and `version-bump-step2.md` to their current file paths with text and cross-references repointed; `23d28a1` (F-0958) renormalised the audit-cluster file basenames and swept cross-references; `bf92fe6` (F-1290) relocated the unrelated strict-capability absence-arm MUST out of `audit-target-categories.md` into `inventory-audit-intro.md` but did not touch the taxonomy-extension bundle this finding names; `d5a714a` (F-1123) tightened the category-(1)/(3) carrier annotation rules without altering the bundled obligations. The bundling has therefore stood since the audit was introduced.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Relocate the taxonomy-extension / minimum-entry-shape / branch-table / fixture-floor obligations out of `audit-target-categories.md`'s *Target surface categories* paragraph and into `version-bump-step2.md` step 2(a) (*Positive direction — literal-read of the imported namespace*), adjacent to the existing `CAPABILITY_OBLIGATIONS` count-equality and unrecognised-`kind` rules that already live there. Concretely:

1. In `audit-target-categories.md`, delete the run-on sentence beginning "Satisfying the three in-scope categories MUST extend `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY`'s entry-kind taxonomy…" through the per-`<symptom>` fixture-floor sentence and its `namespace-function`-corruption guard. Replace it with a single forward-pointer sentence ("The implementation's per-category entry-kind taxonomy and per-kind payload field shapes are pinned at *Re-run the SDK surface-inventory tests* step 2(a) in `version-bump-step2.md`; this page pins only the detection rule.") that keeps the page focused on detection.
2. In `version-bump-step2.md` step 2(a), append a new sub-paragraph (e.g. `*Entry-kind taxonomy extension and literal-read discriminator family.*`) that hosts the relocated obligations verbatim: the per-category new-kind requirement, the precondition that new kinds (with `kind` discriminator strings and per-kind payload field shapes), recognising branch-table arms, and category-(1)/(2)/(3) detection wiring all land before the first `npm test` run of the audit on `main`; the minimum-entry-shape predicate (`kind: string` + `path: string`) with its closed `node-floor` / `strict-capability-probe` / `peer-dep-range` / `api-coverage` exemption list; the `literal-read/<symptom>` discriminator family enforcing the minimum-entry-shape obligation and the unrecognised-`kind` failure; and the per-`<symptom>` fixture-floor obligation with the illustrative implementation-owned tokens. The `namespace-function`-corruption guard ("The new entries MUST NOT be folded into the existing `namespace-function` kind…") lands in the same sub-paragraph.
3. Update every inbound cross-reference: the existing back-references inside step 2(a) that say "per *Target surface categories* above" / "per the new entry kind enumerated below" become local intra-page references to the new sub-paragraph; the existing references from `audit-target-categories.md`'s sibling paragraphs (the *Strict-capability absence under the pin* paragraph's exemption reference, and any other in-page cross-reference) re-point through `version-bump-step2.md` step 2(a); `V18a`'s plan-side **Spec** citations gain `version-bump-step2.md` step 2(a) as the home for the entry-kind-taxonomy obligation.

Edge cases for the implementer: the `*Category-(3) drift contributor obligation.*` sub-paragraph already in step 2(a) consumes the same `kind` / `path` minimum-entry-shape rule, so the relocation must place the new sub-paragraph *before* the *Category-(3) drift* sub-paragraph so the latter's `path` / `kind` references resolve locally; the strict-capability `path`-exemption note at the end of `audit-target-categories.md` (`The 'strict-capability-probe' kind's 'path'-exemption (per the *Target surface categories* minimum-shape exemption above) is unchanged.`) MUST be re-pointed to the new step-2(a) home in the same edit so the cross-reference does not go stale.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Implementers can still reconstruct the full contract because the two pages cross-reference each other, but each owner gets the other half as implicit scope: an audit-detection implementer reading `audit-target-categories.md` carries assertion-shape, fixture-floor, and discriminator-naming obligations they don't own, and a literal-read-assertion implementer reading `version-bump-step2.md` step 2(a) sees only a back-pointer to a page nominally about category recognition. Risk of under-reading is highest on the per-`<symptom>` fixture-floor obligation, which is mentioned only here and easy to miss when implementing the assertion from its own page.

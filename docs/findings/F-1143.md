## Finding

Five paragraphs in the shard-07 PIC topic cluster currently sit on a topic page that does not own their subject; each was added in a focused fix to the nearest page that mentioned the concept rather than to the page whose TOC scope governs it. The cluster falls under one umbrella (PIC topic-page TOC discipline) but each item resolves independently, so they are mapped as separate obligations rather than a single edit.

The five misplacements are:

1. **`no-extra-mediation` NFR — lead section of `conversation-drive.md`.** The "No additional access channels" paragraph (the runtime's no-sandbox / no-capability-filter / no-mediated-proxy obligation) is a runtime-wide trust-boundary NFR that the `overview-and-orientation.md` Non-functional-requirements §"Trust boundary" bullet already forward-links to as the normative source. It sits as the *first* paragraph of `conversation-drive.md` because the conversation-drive section happens to be where the rule's denial-routing-through-tools sub-clause was first restated; placement under "conversation drive" mis-scopes a runtime-wide NFR as a prompt-mode driver concern.

2. **ESM / entry-point lives on `host-prerequisites.md`.** The two trailing paragraphs of `host-prerequisites.md` — "Extension ES-module declaration" (anchor `#extension-esm-type-module`) and "Extension entry point" (the `extensions/index.ts` + `package.json#pi.extensions = ["./extensions"]` factory pin) — describe loom's *own* extension manifest, not a Pi-side prerequisite. They belong on `pi-integration.md` (the parent integration page that already lists slash-command discovery and the file-watcher contract for the same audience) or on a dedicated extension-manifest topic page. The ESM paragraph was relocated to `host-prerequisites.md` on 2026-06-22 (commit `ce48e09`) to satisfy a previous round's "ESM type:module obligation has no anchored home" finding; the relocation landed it next to the wrong neighbours.

3. **PIC-7 split-definition between `host-prerequisites.md` and `session-shutdown-semantics.md`.** PIC-7 *Session-binding contract* is defined in `host-prerequisites.md` (single-active-session premise + `SessionShutdownEvent['reason']` union pin + cross-link to the unknown-reason rule + cross-link to the degraded-state branch). The session-shutdown handler algorithm whose preconditions PIC-7 sets — the five-sub-step sequence, the `event.reason` partition, the partial-append rule, the `invoke`-parent observation rule — lives on `session-shutdown-semantics.md` (in fact under the *Patch skew degradation* file, per the separate "five-sub-step session-shutdown handler algorithm lives under 'Patch skew degradation'" finding). The reason-set membership pin and the teardown-vs-session-swap partition discriminator should travel with the handler that consumes them, leaving `host-prerequisites.md` with only the single-active-session premise it can defend without forward-linking back into shutdown territory.

4. **Degraded-state open contradiction on `host-prerequisites.md`.** The "Host prerequisites for the degraded-state branch" block — particularly clause (a)'s *open contradiction against Pi's documented teardown-and-rebind extension lifecycle*, which gates implementation of the SM-4/SM-5/SM-6/SM-3b degraded-state obligations — is a high-stakes pre-implementation decision (it currently blocks the `V9l` leaf in `plan.md`'s **Blocked obligations** list). Burying it as sub-bullets of a "Host prerequisites" page hides its blocking status; readers scanning `host-prerequisites.md` for "what Pi must offer" instead encounter a four-paragraph contradiction analysis. The block belongs either on `session-only-degraded-state.md` (which owns the branch the contradiction gates) or on a dedicated `degraded-state-precondition.md` page where its blocked-decision status is the page's headline subject, not an embedded clause.

5. **Named host presuppositions in `registration-steps.md` prose rather than with their peers.** Two named host-behaviour presuppositions — `getcommands-completeness-presupposition` (added 2026-06-04 by `d16c23a`) and `register-tool-post-startup-presupposition` (added 2026-06-06 by `113fb60`) — live inline in `registration-steps.md`'s step-3 and step-5 prose. Every other named host-behaviour presupposition in PIC lives on `host-prerequisites.md` as a sibling of the degraded-state, model-registry-population, settings-write-back, operator-always-present, and `pi.registerFlag` overlap presuppositions. The two `registration-steps.md` presuppositions were added in isolation against the page that named the surface; relocating them to `host-prerequisites.md` with peer framing (and leaving an inline cross-reference at the step-3 / step-5 call site) restores the single-page presupposition inventory the version-bump editorial-review checklist already keys on.

The combined effect is that shard-07's topic-page boundaries are not load-bearing as the reader navigates: which page owns which obligation depends on which page mentioned the concept first, not on TOC scope. None of the five items is individually severe, but together they obscure the contract surface for anyone using TOC navigation to find a specific obligation.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

This finding is bimodal-by-construction (five independent placement obligations) and is mapped per finding-protocol step 7.5 as five separate options. The orchestrator must resolve them sequentially so each move lands on a stable baseline.

### Option A — Relocate the `no-extra-mediation` NFR off `conversation-drive.md`

**Approach.** Move the *No additional access channels* paragraph out of the lead-section position on `conversation-drive.md`. Two candidate homes are (A1) a new lead-section block on `pi-integration.md` (the parent integration page; the trust-boundary content is runtime-wide rather than prompt-mode-specific), or (A2) a dedicated `trust-boundary.md` PIC topic page. Leave a one-sentence cross-reference at the current `#no-extra-mediation` site so existing in-corpus citations resolve until they retarget.

**Spec edits.** Lift the paragraph and its `<a id="no-extra-mediation"></a>` anchor; restate the denial-routing-through-tools sub-clause at the new location; cross-reference from `conversation-drive.md`'s lead. `overview-and-orientation.md`'s Non-functional-requirements §"Trust boundary" bullet's forward link is unchanged in target identity if the anchor name moves with the paragraph.

**Pros.** Restores `conversation-drive.md`'s lead-section scope to prompt-mode driver concerns; collocates the trust-boundary NFR with the surface that orients readers to PIC.

**Cons.** Touches every cross-corpus citation to `#no-extra-mediation` (small but non-zero; the citation set is in `overview-and-orientation.md`, `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md`, `runtime-value-model.md`).

**Risks.** Forgetting to migrate the anchor breaks five-to-eight existing cross-references; the `f5e89f4` shard-split's anchor-rewrite tooling under `docs/_tools/` can be reused.

### Option B — Relocate the extension-manifest paragraphs off `host-prerequisites.md`

**Approach.** Move *Extension ES-module declaration* (anchor `#extension-esm-type-module`) and *Extension entry point* off `host-prerequisites.md` to (B1) `pi-integration.md` as adjacent paragraphs in the parent integration page, or (B2) a dedicated `extension-manifest.md` PIC topic page paired with the existing `package.json#pi.extensions` assertion's build-time gate.

**Spec edits.** Lift both paragraphs verbatim; preserve `#extension-esm-type-module` (cited from `H1a-scaffold-and-toolchain.md` twice); leave a "Loom's extension-manifest pins are owned by …" pointer where the *Extension entry point* paragraph currently sits, because the *Extension entry point* opening sentence is referenced from PIC-18 in `conversation-drive.md` ("the **Extension entry point** above"), from `binder-inference.md`, and from the **System notes** + factory-bind paragraphs that follow.

**Pros.** Lets `host-prerequisites.md` describe *Pi-side* prerequisites only, matching its TOC. Collocates loom's manifest contract with the only paragraph on `pi-integration.md` (or its sibling) that documents loom's own packaging.

**Cons.** *Extension entry point* is named by many "above" / "below" anaphoras inside the same shard; each must be re-pointed in the same commit. The `f5e89f4` shard-split rewrite already established the anaphor-rewrite recipe under `docs/_tools/`, so the cost is mechanical, not editorial.

**Risks.** A miss on a "Extension entry point above" anaphor leaves a dangling self-reference in `conversation-drive.md` or `registration-steps.md`.

### Option C — Split PIC-7 between `host-prerequisites.md` and `session-shutdown-semantics.md`

**Approach.** Keep PIC-7's *single-active-session premise* sentence on `host-prerequisites.md` (it is a Pi-side host prerequisite proper), but relocate the `SessionShutdownEvent['reason']` union pin and the implicit teardown-vs-session-swap partition framing into `session-shutdown-semantics.md` (the shutdown-handler page that already consumes them and that owns the *Patch skew degradation* + factory-ordering pin neighbours). Re-anchor `pic-7` on the relocated half (the handler-side body is the load-bearing surface for V18c's type-equality assertion); leave a one-line "PIC-7's reason-set membership and teardown-vs-session-swap partition are owned at …" pointer at the prerequisites site.

**Spec edits.** Move the second half of PIC-7's current paragraph; update `pic-7` anchor home; ensure V18c's checklist item (f) and the bidirectional `SessionShutdownEvent['reason']` type-equality assertion citation in `version-bump-intro.md` continue to resolve. The session-model aggregator's SM-1 / SM-3b citations are by anchor name only and continue to work.

**Pros.** Eliminates the split-definition risk (the section that defines the closed reason set sits with the handler that branches on it); reduces forward-link density in `host-prerequisites.md`.

**Cons.** PIC-7 currently sits next to `degraded-state-host-prerequisites` because clause (a) builds on PIC-7's instance-survival premise; that adjacency is editorial-only (the cross-link works either way) but the proximity helps the reader currently. If option D also lands, the adjacency goes away regardless, so the cons reduce to "cross-link must be explicit, not adjacent."

**Risks.** Re-anchoring `pic-7` requires updating session-model-and-appendix.md's SM-3b/SM-4 anchor citations in the same commit.

### Option D — Relocate the degraded-state contradiction block off `host-prerequisites.md`

### Option E — Relocate the two named host presuppositions off `registration-steps.md`

**Approach.** Move `getcommands-completeness-presupposition` (currently inline in step 3's prose) and `register-tool-post-startup-presupposition` (inline in step 5's prose) into `host-prerequisites.md`'s presupposition peer set, alongside the existing model-registry-population, settings-write-back, operator-always-present, and `pi.registerFlag` overlap presuppositions. Leave a one-sentence cross-reference at the step-3 and step-5 call sites pointing to the relocated anchors.

### Recommendation

Resolve the five obligations sequentially in this order, each as a separately-loopable fix so the spec-diff-fix-loop does not diverge on a five-way-bundled diff:

1. **Option E first** (named presuppositions) — smallest scope, sets a precedent for the host-prerequisites peer-inventory pattern that option D will then extend.
2. **Option B** (extension-manifest paragraphs) — independent of the other four; resolves the most recently introduced misplacement (June 22), restoring `host-prerequisites.md`'s Pi-side scope.
3. **Option A** (no-extra-mediation NFR) — independent of B / C / D / E; restores `conversation-drive.md`'s scope.
4. **Option C** (PIC-7 split) — must land before option D so the `pic-7` anchor's re-home is settled before the degraded-state block (which forward-links into PIC-7) moves.
5. **Option D last** (degraded-state contradiction) — the highest-stakes move because it touches `plan.md`'s Blocked-obligations citation and V9l's spec anchor; landing it on a stable shard layout (post-A, post-B, post-C, post-E) avoids re-rewriting the same cross-references twice.

The orchestrator should treat each item above as a separate fix-loop iteration, not a single multi-page commit, per the bimodal-decomposition discipline.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The placement bug does not produce divergent implementations — every paragraph cited is normatively unambiguous in its current location, and the cross-references resolve. Cost is paid by readers: scanning a topic page does not surface the obligations the page's TOC scope implies it owns, and high-stakes content (the PIC-7 split, the degraded-state contradiction) is harder to find than its weight warrants. The five-way bundling also raises the editorial cost of any future PIC topic-page restructure, because each move requires sweeping the same cross-reference surface.

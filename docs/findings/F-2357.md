## Finding

The `V16a` leaf's **Adds.** field is, per [`leaf-template.md`](docs/plan_topics/leaf-template.md) and `conventions.md` *Leaf format*, meant to describe what the leaf delivers — the new mechanism it introduces. `V16a`'s Adds correctly opens by declaring the cross-ceiling arbitration seam (`arbitrate(candidate) → { surfaced, masked? }`) and the distributed-enforcement model. It then continues with a test-strategy rationale paragraph that does not describe the delivered seam at all:

> "The synthesised-candidate test strategy drives ceiling-candidates through this seam in isolation … not against the live `invoke` entry / AJV boundary / round-boundary sites … The stateless single-candidate seam witnesses only the CIO sub-properties that are decidable from co-present candidates …"

followed by two bullets — **Verified at the seam in isolation** and **Not witnessable at the stateless seam — owned by downstream sites** — that enumerate which CIO sub-properties the *tests* can and cannot assert at `V16a`'s position.

This is test-scope / witness-boundary content, not a description of what the leaf adds. The same scope limitation is already carried where it belongs: the `V16a` per-CIO **Tests** bullets spell out the downstream-witness caveats for CIO-1 (slash-load-vs-runtime split) and CIO-5 (H7a integration witness), and the `V16a` **Ships when** clause restates that "CIO-1's slash-load-before-runtime placement and CIO-5's never-interleaves cross-site relations are not asserted here; they are witnessed downstream … and by `H7a`'s integration run." The paragraph in Adds is therefore redundant duplication of test-scope reasoning living in the wrong field.

The placement defect is original; the amplifying edits added the witness-boundary bullets but left the content in Adds.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V16a-ceiling-order-masked.md`, within the **Adds.** field, strike the test-strategy rationale paragraph that begins "The synthesised-candidate test strategy drives ceiling-candidates through this seam in isolation …" together with the two bullets that follow it (**Verified at the seam in isolation:** … and **Not witnessable at the stateless seam — owned by downstream sites:** …). Retain the seam declaration and the distributed-enforcement description that precede it, and retain the closing "Each downstream leaf's `Deps` on `V16a` binds the seam it consults." sentence.

The struck content's witness-boundary information is already present and need not be re-homed: the per-CIO downstream-witness caveats live in `V16a`'s **Tests** bullets (CIO-1 and CIO-5), and the seam-decidable-vs-downstream split is stated in `V16a`'s **Ships when** clause. No edit to `V16a-T-ceiling-order-masked.md` or to the spec is required.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader of `V16a`'s Adds must wade through a test-scope rationale that duplicates the per-CIO Tests caveats and the Ships-when clause; no implementer is misled and no leaf diverges. The cost is organisational clutter in a field the template reserves for the delivered mechanism.

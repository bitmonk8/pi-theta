## Problem

DISC-1 (`id="disc-1"` in `docs/spec_topics/discovery/discovery-sources.md`) states that the `~user` form "is **not** honoured — only the bare `~` followed by `/` (or end-of-string)", but never states what *does* happen to a `~user`-prefixed path. The candidate outcomes — verbatim passthrough to the `FileSystem` seam, leading-segment stripping, parse-time rejection, or a load-time diagnostic — are all left open, so two compliant implementations can diverge on what the operator sees for `~alice/looms/foo.loom`. The negative landed without a paired positive, leaving DISC-2's failure-modes path unwired to this case and the corresponding plan test bullet asserting only the negative.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `2793da3` (2026-05-05, "resolve M ~/.pi/agent/looms/ path expansion unspecified for Windows") — added the DISC-1 home-directory paragraph in its current form, including the "`~user` form … is **not** honoured" sentence but without stating the outcome.
**History:** The negative ("not honoured") landed without a paired positive outcome — a "fix-without-observable" pattern shared across the DISC findings.

## Solution approach

Clarify DISC-1 to state the positive outcome of a `~`-prefixed path whose `~` is not followed by `/` or end-of-string: the segment is passed byte-verbatim to the `FileSystem` seam (no `~user` stripping) and surfaces through the standard failure modes, with a forward-link to `id="disc-2"`. Ensure the stated outcome reads as applying to DISC-1's enumerated `~`-eligible sites rather than to the global root alone.

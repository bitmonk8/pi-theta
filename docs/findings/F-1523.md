## Finding

The same hard-ceiling-#3 budget is named two ways across the binder corpus with no equivalence statement linking them:

- *"Binder per-class retry budget"* is used in the ceiling #3 list item (`overview-and-orientation.md`, hard-ceiling-3 anchor), the hard-ceilings aggregator (`hard-ceilings.md`), and the owner section heading and prose in `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md` (`### Per-class retry budget (ceiling #3)`, `CIO-1`, the `HC3-c` "no per-class retry budget" parenthetical).
- *"Per-invocation retry budget"* is the section anchor `#per-invocation-retry-budget` and the surrounding paragraph label in `binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md`; it is the cross-link text used from `pi-integration-contract/binder-inference.md` and from `session-model-and-appendix.md` SM-8 (and the bare *"binder retry budget"* variant appears in SM-8 and in `future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md`).

The two names point at the same object — they are orthogonal facets of one budget (the budget is scoped *per slash invocation*, and within that scope each retry-eligible failure class gets its own slot) — but no page says so. A reviewer reading the ceiling-#3 list sees a "per-class" budget; clicking through to the binder topic page lands on a paragraph that calls itself the "per-invocation retry budget" with no acknowledgement that this is the same object the ceiling list named. The plan compounds this by using the per-class wording in `V11f-binder-retry-taxonomy.md`'s title and the per-invocation wording in `H4b-response-programming-surface.md`'s body. Nothing pins which is canonical or whether either is a synonym.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Pin the equivalence in one place — the canonical owner paragraph at `binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md#per-invocation-retry-budget` — and add a one-time forward equivalence at the ceiling-#3 first-use site. Concretely:

1. In the `#per-invocation-retry-budget` paragraph, prepend a sentence that names both labels and pins them as the same object: *"This budget — also called the **binder per-class retry budget** at [ceiling #3](../hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md#hc3) — is per-invocation in scope and per-class in slot allocation: each retry-eligible failure class (transport, malformed-envelope) has its own slot within a single slash invocation."* No other normative content changes.
2. In `overview-and-orientation.md`'s ceiling-#3 list item (`#hard-ceiling-3`), append a parenthetical at first use: *"(also called the **per-invocation retry budget** on its [owner page](./binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md#per-invocation-retry-budget))"*. The existing forward link stays.
3. In `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md`, leave the `### Per-class retry budget (ceiling #3)` heading and `HC3-c` wording as-is — the section's opening paragraph already forward-links to the binder page, and step 1 makes that page authoritative on the equivalence. No edits required at `CIO-1`, the `HC3-c` parenthetical, or `hard-ceilings.md`.
4. No edits required at `binder-inference.md`, SM-8, or the Future-Considerations bullet — once the equivalence is pinned at the anchor, their existing link text resolves unambiguously.

Pin the equivalence; do not rename either occurrence. Renaming would either invalidate the `#per-invocation-retry-budget` anchor (breaking SM-8 / `binder-inference.md` / V11f references) or invalidate the `HC3-a … HC3-e` "per-class" framing the ceiling list and `HC3-c` parenthetical rely on. Edge case for the implementer: when wording the equivalence at the owner paragraph, do not introduce a new third synonym ("binder retry budget" is already used by SM-8 and the Future-Considerations bullet as an informal short form; leave those as-is rather than coining a fourth term).

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader cannot decide from the text alone whether "per-class retry budget" and "per-invocation retry budget" name the same object or two distinct budgets; the ambiguity is resolved only by following cross-references and inferring identity from context. Implementers and reviewers can still arrive at the correct behaviour because the linked anchor paragraph fully pins the rule, but every grep, every test name, and every diagnostic discussion has to navigate the synonym without a glossary or first-use equivalence statement to fall back on.

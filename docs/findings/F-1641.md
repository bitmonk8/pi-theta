## Finding

The Source-language-stability bullet in `docs/spec_topics/overview-and-orientation.md` (the `#non-functional-requirements` aggregator entry) closes its "release-process goal" sentence with a clause that purely records retirement history: *"the companion review-posture guidance (formerly GOV-14) is now retired and demoted to a non-normative editorial note (see [Retired REQ-IDs](./governance/anchor-scheme-and-retired.md#retired-req-ids))."* The sentence states no constraint a loom author or implementer must satisfy and forwards no live rule that the surrounding bullet does not already forward — `[Governance — GOV-15]` is already cited in the immediately preceding clause. Its sole information content is *what GOV-14 used to be and that it is no longer live*, and that fact is owned canonically by the `Retired REQ-IDs` table on `anchor-scheme-and-retired.md` and by the non-normative editorial note on `req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` (line 40). The NFR aggregator should not duplicate that bookkeeping.

The `(formerly GOV-N)` decision-log pattern is the cross-shard concern flagged on the original heading; the present finding's scope is the one parenthetical-plus-sentence in the `overview-and-orientation.md` aggregator entry.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-by-named-commit
**Introducing commit:** `5a772e3` — *pi-loom spec: resolve "GOV-14 two SHOULD-NOTs admit no mechanical witness"* (2026-06-05)
**History:** Before `5a772e3`, the bullet read *"… are owned by [Governance — GOV-15] with the companion review-posture rule at [GOV-14]."* — a forward-link to a live REQ-ID, not a decision-log trace. Commit `5a772e3` retired GOV-14 and rewrote the clause to its current "*… is now retired and demoted to a non-normative editorial note …*" form in the same edit, also adding the parallel sentence in `source-language-stability.md`'s GOV-15 body and the editorial-note line in `req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md`. The defect therefore did not pre-exist GOV-14's retirement; it is the retirement edit's choice to record bookkeeping in the NFR aggregator entry rather than letting the Retired REQ-IDs table carry it alone. The subsequent `81bf52a` (*docs: collapse spec/ section pages into spec_topics/*, 2026-06-11) only relocated the file and did not touch the prose.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/spec_topics/overview-and-orientation.md`, delete the entire sentence *"the companion review-posture guidance (formerly GOV-14) is now retired and demoted to a non-normative editorial note (see [Retired REQ-IDs](./governance/anchor-scheme-and-retired.md#retired-req-ids))."* from the Source-language-stability bullet. Adjust the punctuation of the preceding clause (which already ends with `… owned by [Governance — GOV-15](./governance/source-language-stability.md#gov-15)`) so the semicolon becomes a sentence-ending period, and the next sentence (`Introducing a new hard runtime ceiling …`) begins cleanly.

The retirement of GOV-14 remains discoverable: the `Retired REQ-IDs` table on `anchor-scheme-and-retired.md` is the canonical record, and the editorial note on `req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` line 40 carries the retired guidance's substance for any reader who follows the table's `Formerly` column back. The aggregator entry's job is to forward to GOV-15 (which it already does); it does not owe readers a "and by the way, GOV-14 is gone" annotation.

Do not, in this finding, edit the parallel sentence in `source-language-stability.md`'s GOV-15 body or the editorial-note line in `req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md`. Each is a separate site with its own dispositional choice (the GOV-15-body sentence is the closest analogue and would warrant the same deletion; the editorial-note line is the canonical home of the retired guidance and should stay). Bundling them here would silently expand scope.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The aggregator entry carries a sentence whose only payload is change-history, mildly inflating an already long NFR bullet and weakening the bullet's "informative orientation" framing. No implementer behaviour changes either way; the cost is that the next reviewer must re-discover that the sentence carries no constraint, and the bullet's signal-to-noise ratio degrades incrementally each time a retired sibling is annotated this way.

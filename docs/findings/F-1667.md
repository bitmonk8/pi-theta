## Problem

The `loom/load/package-read-timeout` row in `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/code-registry-load.md` pins a message template carrying the `<name>` placeholder, but the diagnostic fires precisely because the candidate `package.json` read was abandoned — so the package's authored `name` field is unobservable at the emission site. Neither the row nor DISC-6 (`#disc-6`) in `docs/spec_topics/discovery/package-and-settings.md` states what `<name>` renders in that case, and the `placeholder-rendering-b.md` §5 source-derived rule has no source string and no fallback for this site, leaving a rendering hole. Two conformant implementations can therefore emit non-byte-identical messages (directory entry vs. absolute path vs. a sentinel), violating the registry's byte-identicality contract for non-§8 placeholders.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**
- `25e8a60` (2026-05-07) "pi-loom spec: resolve 'T08 — Per-package.json read timeout: overrun is unbounded'" — added the per-read-deadline mechanism and the message `package '<name>' package.json read exceeded <deadline>ms during package discovery` on the `loom/load/unreadable-source` row, with `<name>` unsourced for the timeout case.
- `65989e2` (2026-06-01) "pi-loom spec: resolve 'unreadable-source row carries two message formats'" — spun the per-read-timeout sub-trigger off into its own `loom/load/package-read-timeout` row, carrying the `<name>` placeholder forward verbatim without specifying its source-at-timeout.

**History:** `git log -S "package '<name>' package.json read exceeded"` against `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/code-registry-load.md` and the predecessor `spec_topics/diagnostics.md` shows the message text first appears at `25e8a60` riding the `unreadable-source` row, and `65989e2` lifts that text into the new `package-read-timeout` row. Neither commit's diff adds a sentence on what `<name>` renders when the read fails, and the candidate-enumeration paragraph in `discovery/package-and-settings.md` (which would be the natural site for the rule) was untouched by both. The defect is the interaction: `25e8a60` introduced an interpolation against a field that is by construction unobservable at the emission site, and `65989e2` preserved that interpolation when separating concerns rather than tightening it. The later split through `f5e89f4` (the 100KB-cap-driven file split that moved the row from `diagnostics.md` into `diagnostics/code-registry-load.md`) is mechanical relocation and not an introducing commit.

## Solution approach

Pin `<name>` on the `loom/load/package-read-timeout` row to render the candidate package's directory-entry name — the immediate-child entry the walker enumerated under one of the **Roots scanned** roots in `docs/spec_topics/discovery/package-and-settings.md`, joined as `@scope/pkg` for a scope-directory child — rather than the unread `package.json` `name` field, which the walker already holds at timeout time without a parse. Mirror that semantics onto the DISC-6 (`#disc-6`) clause that emits the warning "naming the package". Rendering continues through the existing `placeholder-rendering-b.md` §5 source-derived-placeholder rule.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the per-read deadline mechanism and the message surround on the `loom/load/package-read-timeout` row; only `<name>`'s rendering source is pinned.

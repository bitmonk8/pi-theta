## Finding

`docs/spec_topics/imports.md` describes the import-path `Resolver` seam twice with no added content the second time. The **Resolver interface.** paragraph already states the full seam contract: the `resolve(spec: string, fromFile: string): string` shape, the single loom 1.0.0 relative-path implementation, the unresolvable-path treatment of non-relative specs, and the closing non-normative note that single-seam routing is a maintainability convention rather than an observable conformance point. The `> **loom 1.0 seam — `Resolver` interface.**` blockquote a short distance below restates every one of those points in near-identical wording, including its own non-normative-convention disclaimer.

The two blocks carry no distinct normative payload. The blockquote's only unique content is the two anchor ids it hosts — `<a id="loom-1-0-seam-resolver-interface"></a>` and `<a id="v1-seam-resolver-interface"></a>` — one of which (`#v1-seam-resolver-interface`) is deep-linked from `docs/spec_topics/future-considerations/surface-extensions.md`. A naive deletion would break that inbound link, so the redundancy must be removed by merging rather than by dropping the blockquote wholesale.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** `02d7ca5` — "pi-loom spec: resolve \"T02 surface-extension seam obligations not consistently marked\"" (2026-05-08)
**History:** The corpus is git-tracked. The **Resolver interface.** paragraph entered first, in `09c42c0` (2026-05-05, "Surface-extension Seam annotations carry V1 obligations from non-goals page"), as the sole description of the seam. `02d7ca5` then added the `> **V1 seam — `Resolver` interface.**` blockquote immediately after that paragraph — at the time directly adjacent to it — as part of a sweep giving every surface-extension seam a uniform `> **V1 seam — <name>.**` callout; this is the commit that created the duplication. The two blocks have drifted apart only cosmetically since: `9c90227` inserted the IMP-1 resolver-failure paragraph between them, and `8362824` ("Demote over-prescriptive and non-testable MUSTs on unobservable implementation structure") demoted both the paragraph and the blockquote from a normative MUST to the matching "maintainability convention, not an observable conformance point" non-normative note — deepening the redundancy but not introducing it. The defect is attributable to the single seam-callout sweep in `02d7ca5`.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Merge the blockquote into the **Resolver interface.** paragraph and delete the blockquote, preserving link integrity:

- Relocate **both** anchor tags from the blockquote — `<a id="loom-1-0-seam-resolver-interface"></a>` and `<a id="v1-seam-resolver-interface"></a>` — onto the retained **Resolver interface.** paragraph (placing them at the paragraph's start is sufficient; anchor position within a block does not affect fragment resolution). Both must move, not just one: `surface-extensions.md` links `#v1-seam-resolver-interface`, and the `loom-1-0-seam-resolver-interface` spelling matches the visible heading text used elsewhere.
- Ensure the retained paragraph keeps the more specific forward link the blockquote carried — `[Future Considerations — Surface extensions](./future-considerations/surface-extensions.md#surface-extensions-v1-leaves-a-seam)` — rather than the paragraph's current generic `[Future Considerations](./future-considerations.md)` target.
- Delete the `> **loom 1.0 seam — `Resolver` interface.**` blockquote.

Edge case: after the edit, confirm both fragments (`#loom-1-0-seam-resolver-interface`, `#v1-seam-resolver-interface`) still resolve and that no other corpus file links either fragment expecting the blockquote to remain a distinct element.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

Two co-located near-verbatim statements of the same non-normative seam contract are a maintenance hazard: a future edit to one block silently diverges from the other, and a reader cannot tell whether the blockquote adds an obligation the paragraph omits. No implementer-observable behaviour is affected.

## Finding

The canary's strictly-greater-than-zero floor on both counters treats any zero as a misconfigured walker, but never states the precondition that makes "zero ⇒ misconfiguration" valid: that the audited source tree always contains ≥1 in-scope `.ts` file and ≥1 recognised Pi-side reference. The invariant holds for the loom runtime via Orientation — Prerequisites and V18b's land-green sweep, but is not pinned at the floor.

Both are omission/interaction defects present since the apparatus's inception, not introduced by a later edit.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

At the *Non-empty-scan canary* paragraph, state the precondition invariant the `> 0` floor relies on — that the audited source tree, by the loom runtime's construction, always carries at least one in-scope `.ts` and at least one recognised Pi reference — and cross-reference the entry-point obligation that guarantees it (`overview-and-orientation.md#orientation` Prerequisites, which establishes that `src/` references the seven Pi capabilities). Alternatively, narrow the canary's normative statement to "a tree known to contain Pi surface". Do not introduce a separate parse/load diagnostic; the floor itself stays as written, only its governing assumption is made explicit.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two reasonable implementers diverge on both points: one treats the canary's `> 0` floor as universal (red on any Pi-free tree), another assumes the runtime-always-has-Pi-surface precondition silently, so their canary behaviour differs on the same edge tree. And a conforming CI parser following the infra-aborted-run carve-out will raise a spurious canary-obligation violation on a host-killed zero-record run, defeating the carve-out's purpose of distinguishing infra aborts from real canary breakage.

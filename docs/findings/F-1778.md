## Problem

The "Tool execution from loom code" surface (`#tool-execution-from-loom-code` in `host-interfaces-core.md`) and the runtime bullet in `implementation-notes.md` invoke a captured Pi `ToolDefinition.execute(toolCallId, params, signal, onUpdate, ctx)` directly for every code-side tool call, bypassing Pi's dispatcher: they pass a runtime-minted `loom-direct:`-prefixed `toolCallId` that Pi never registered, a no-op `onUpdate`, and a synthesised `ExtensionContext`. The spec narrates what the runtime does but never pins, even informally, the host-behaviour precondition that an arbitrary Pi `ToolDefinition` is safe to drive out-of-band. Every other comparable unpinned Pi behaviour is inventoried as a named presupposition on `host-prerequisites.md` and routed to a re-audit item in the *Editorial-review checklist for unpinned host presuppositions* in `version-bump-step2.md`; the out-of-band-`execute()` precondition is the only tool-dispatch-side precondition of that scope not pulled into the inventory. A Pi minor that correlated progress by `toolCallId`, required `onUpdate` to be a real sink, or expected dispatcher-side per-call setup would misbehave with no loom-side diagnostic.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** `fae85d3` "Tighten spec for implementation-plan readiness" (2026-05-04)
**History:** The "Tool execution from loom code" surface was added in `fae85d3` already in its current shape — direct `ToolDefinition.execute(toolCallId, params, signal, onUpdate, ctx)` invocation, `toolCallId` "synthesised UUID prefixed `loom-direct:`", `onUpdate` "a no-op (V1 does not surface streaming partial results to loom code)", `ctx` a "synthesised `ExtensionContext`". `git log -L` over the paragraph through every subsequent edit (`ec6ab5c`, `a44ad72`, `bf85092`, `81bf52a`, `0c6c347`, plus the `f5e89f4` split into `host-interfaces-core.md`) shows the bullets have only been elaborated — UUID body format pinned, `IdSource` seam wired, `ctx` per-mode overrides described — never wrapped in a host-behaviour presupposition. The named-presupposition inventory on `host-prerequisites.md` grew up around the surface (`register-tool-post-startup-presupposition`, `registry-persistence-presupposition`, etc., several added as late as `fa69d8f`), but no commit ever pulled the out-of-band `execute()` precondition into it. The defect is one of category coverage rather than a regression: the surface was authored before the presupposition pattern existed and was never retrofitted when the pattern was generalised.

## Solution approach

Add a named host-behaviour presupposition entry on `host-prerequisites.md`, sibling to `#register-tool-post-startup-presupposition` and `#registry-persistence-presupposition`, carrying its own anchor, pinning that a captured `ToolDefinition.execute` tolerates a caller-minted `toolCallId` with no dispatcher-side registration, treats a no-op `onUpdate` as a legal sink, and requires no other dispatcher-side per-call setup. Add forward-links to the new entry from the `#tool-execution-from-loom-code` paragraph in `host-interfaces-core.md` and from the runtime bullet in `implementation-notes.md`. Add a corresponding lettered item to the *Editorial-review checklist for unpinned host presuppositions* in `version-bump-step2.md`, matching the recording shape of the neighbouring items.

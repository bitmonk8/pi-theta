## Problem

`V18c`'s **Adds** and **Ships when** both describe the contributor editorial-review checklist as items `(a)`–`(al)` (MUST-level for `(a)`–`(e)`, SHOULD-level-audit-but-MUST-record for `(f)`–`(al)`), but `version-bump-step2.md` enumerates the checklist through `(aq)`. The five items beyond `(al)` — `(am)` `tools`-allowlist suppression, `(an)` parallel-tool-batch delivery, `(ao)` flag-namespace overlap, `(ap)` `registerTool` persistence, `(aq)` `TokenEstimator` determinism — each carry the same MUST-record obligation as `(f)`–`(al)`. The leaf therefore under-counts the spec by five items, omitting `(am)`–`(aq)` from the per-item recording requirement — including `(an)`, the parallel-tool-batch-delivery presupposition `V14b` depends on.

**History:** Commit 1ca13ec authored `V18c`'s `(a)`–`(al)` range, which was correct at that point because `(al)` was then the spec checklist's last item. Five subsequent spec-side commits each appended a new checklist item past `(al)` — `(am)` at 077afb5, `(an)` at 2123dc9, `(ao)` at db69f2b, `(ap)` at bef37f3 (2026-06-25), and `(aq)` at 341bcd9 — and none of them touched `V18c`, so the leaf's stated range drifted out of sync with the spec's growing extent.

## Solution approach

In `docs/plan_topics/V18c-version-bump-checklist.md`, update each `(al)` range bound to `(aq)` so the leaf tracks the spec's checklist extent. In **Adds**, change the overall editorial-review range to `(a)`–`(aq)` and the SHOULD-level-audit-but-MUST-record range to `(f)`–`(aq)`. In **Ships when**, change the per-item recording clause to "every item `(a)`–`(aq)`" and the non-blocking-SHOULD clause to `(f)`–`(aq)`. Preserve the MUST/SHOULD split: `(a)`–`(e)` stay MUST-level and `(f)`–`(aq)` stay SHOULD-level-audit-but-MUST-record, matching `version-bump-step2.md`.

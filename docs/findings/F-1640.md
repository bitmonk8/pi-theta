## Finding

The glossary's opening paragraph (`docs/spec_topics/glossary.md` line 3) classifies the page descriptively: "Each entry is descriptive — a one-paragraph reminder of what a term means and where it is used. The canonical, normative definition lives on the feature page named in the entry's `See:` reference; if a glossary entry and its canonical page disagree, the canonical page wins." The page's own row in the [REQ-ID prefix table](../spec_topics/governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md) matches this framing — it carries the byte-exact `(no IDs — narrative)` cell, so under GOV-3 / GOV-9 it is forbidden to coin `GLOS-N` REQ-ID anchors and therefore cannot satisfy GOV-9's `#prefix-n` cross-link contract for any normative rule it states.

Three entries violate that framing by hosting full naming-convention MUSTs whose subject is corpus-wide terminology rather than the term's meaning:

- **`callable set`** (line 15): "Authors and implementers MUST use the term `callable set` (or, with an explicit scoping modifier, `frontmatter callable set` / `loom's declared callable set`) for the per-loom resolved tool list; the bare phrases `tools: set`, `tool set`, `loom's tools`, and `available tools` are spec-prose synonyms to be avoided…"
- **`schema slug`** (line 53): "Authors and implementers MUST use `schema slug` (or the bare form `slug` when context is unambiguous). The synonyms `schema hash`, `schema-hash`, `sha12`, `lowered-schema hash`, and `lowered-schema content hash` are spec-prose drift to be avoided; *a future grep gate uses this entry as its source of truth.*"
- **`Pi tool` vs. **`.loom` callable** (line 57): "Authors and implementers MUST use the canonical term `.loom callable` (or, in running prose, `` `.loom` callable ``) for a `.loom` entry in `tools:`; the synonyms `registered loom`, `registered subagent loom`, `registered-loom call`, and `loom callee` are spec-prose drift to be avoided…"

Two consequences follow. (1) The `if a glossary entry and its canonical page disagree, the canonical page wins` doctrine is unsatisfiable for these three MUSTs: there is no canonical-page MUST to lose to, so the glossary is in fact the source of truth — explicitly named so for `schema slug`'s grep-gate — while the prose insists it cannot be. The `schema-subset.md` page reinforces the inversion ("the [Glossary](./glossary.md) entry pins the avoid-list"). (2) Because the glossary is a `(no IDs — narrative)` page, each MUST is permanently un-anchored under GOV-9; reviewer-side prefix coining is impossible without first promoting the entire page out of narrative status (forcing a fresh `GLOS-N` prefix for every entry the page coins), and the H5e un-anchored-MUST gate's live-corpus activation will recognise each MUST as an un-enumerated normative obligation.

- `fec0870` (2026-05-06, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"callable-set prose synonyms erode no-inheritance invariant\"") — added the `MUST use the term \`callable set\`` clause and the `MUST use the canonical term \`.loom callable\`` clause inside the existing glossary entries.
- `9d99fe6` (2026-05-07, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Schema-hash identifier is referred to by six surface names\"") — added the new `schema slug` entry already carrying its `Authors and implementers MUST use \`schema slug\`` clause and the "a future grep gate uses this entry as its source of truth" sentence.

**History:** `git log --follow -S "MUST use the term \`callable set\`"`, `-S "MUST use \`schema slug\`"`, and `-S "MUST use the canonical term"` on `docs/spec_topics/glossary.md` each return one commit (the two above plus `b4fbfac` for the page itself). The descriptive disclaimer at line 3 has been unchanged since `b4fbfac`. Neither of the two later commits touched the intro disclaimer or the prefix-table row that pins glossary.md as `(no IDs — narrative)`. The defect is the interaction: each later fix migrated a corpus-wide naming MUST into the entry it considered the canonical home (each subject was already cross-referenced from multiple feature pages), without revisiting whether the glossary's narrative-page framing still held after that MUST landed there. Each commit is locally well-motivated in isolation; the rule that breaks is one neither commit edited.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Add a generalising GOV rule and demote the glossary clauses to forward pointers

**Approach.** Add one new rule on `governance.md` (under a fresh GOV-N allocated per GOV-3) saying, in effect: *Where the glossary names a term as canonical, authors and implementers MUST use that term; the per-entry avoid-list, if present, is normative for the corpus.* In each of the three glossary entries, demote the `Authors and implementers MUST …` sentence to a descriptive form (`This entry's canonical term is X; synonyms Y/Z/… are spec-prose drift to be avoided`) whose normative force flows from the new GOV rule rather than from the glossary itself. Rewrite `schema-subset.md`'s "the Glossary entry pins the avoid-list" sentence to cite the new GOV rule alongside the glossary.

**Spec edits.**
- `governance.md` (or a new sub-page like `governance/canonical-terminology.md`): coin GOV-N (e.g. `GOV-32` if the next free number) stating the meta-rule above; add the row to the per-page prefix-table if relocating to a sub-page.
- `glossary.md`: rewrite the three normative sentences (lines 15, 53, 57) into the descriptive form; keep the avoid-lists.
- `schema-subset.md` §Canonical schema hash: change "(the [Glossary](./glossary.md) entry pins the avoid-list)" to cite the new GOV rule with the glossary as the term's avoid-list registry.

**Pros.**
- Single point of control: future glossary terms automatically pick up the same enforcement contract.
- Avoids fanning the avoid-lists across multiple feature pages.
- Keeps the glossary's `(no IDs — narrative)` classification — no prefix-table churn.

**Cons.**
- The new GOV rule references the glossary as the registry of canonical terms — that's the same "narrative page hosts the avoid-list" inversion in slightly nicer clothes, only now mediated by a meta-rule. The avoid-list itself is still on the narrative page.
- Adds another GOV rule to an already-dense governance surface.

**Risks.** A reviewer unfamiliar with the new meta-rule will still treat the glossary entry as the *de facto* MUST source; the meta-rule's existence does not make `grep`-based change-detection on `glossary.md` any less load-bearing.

### Option B — Move each MUST to the canonical feature page already named in the entry's `See:`

**Approach.** Carry out the relocation the glossary's intro doctrine already presupposes: each entry's `See:` link names a non-narrative canonical owner page that can host a PREFIX-N anchor. Move each MUST + avoid-list onto that page; shrink the glossary entry to the descriptive paragraph the intro promises.

**Spec edits.**
- `frontmatter/frontmatter-fields-a.md` §`tools`: add a new normative sub-paragraph with a fresh `FRNT-N` anchor stating the `callable set` canonical-term MUST and its avoid-list; cite it from `tool-calls.md` if needed.
- `schema-subset.md` §Canonical schema hash: convert the existing parenthetical ("the Glossary entry pins the avoid-list") into a normative paragraph with a fresh `SCHM-N` anchor that hosts the `schema slug` canonical-term MUST and avoid-list itself.
- `tool-calls.md` (or `frontmatter/frontmatter-fields-a.md` §`tools`): add a normative paragraph hosting the `Pi tool` vs. `.loom callable` canonical-term MUST and avoid-list, anchored under the page's prefix.
- `glossary.md` entries `callable set`, `schema slug`, `Pi tool` vs. `.loom callable`: drop the `Authors and implementers MUST …` sentences and the avoid-lists; the descriptive paragraph and the `See:` link remain. The `schema slug` entry's "a future grep gate uses this entry as its source of truth" sentence moves to the new `SCHM-N` anchor on `schema-subset.md`.

**Pros.**
- Honours the glossary's stated doctrine without amending it: every entry remains genuinely descriptive.
- Each MUST gets a real PREFIX-N anchor satisfying GOV-9's cross-link contract and the H5e un-anchored-MUST gate.
- The grep-gate's source of truth lives on a non-narrative page whose REQ-ID lifecycle is governed by GOV-7 / GOV-8.

**Cons.**
- Three distinct edit sites rather than one; the avoid-lists themselves now live on three feature pages.
- The `callable set` term is referenced from both `frontmatter` and `tool-calls`; reviewers must pick one canonical home (the entry's `See:` lists `frontmatter.md` first, so that is the natural choice).

**Risks.** A subsequent glossary-entry author may forget that the MUST belongs on the feature page and re-introduce a MUST on the glossary entry; documenting the doctrine more explicitly in the glossary's intro mitigates this.

### Recommendation

Option B. The glossary's intro disclaimer was set in `b4fbfac` and has not moved since; Option B is the one that delivers what it promises rather than re-framing why it cannot. It also produces real `#prefix-n` anchors for each MUST, satisfying GOV-9 and H5e in their own terms instead of via a meta-rule carve-out. The implementer should pick the leftmost `See:` target as the canonical home (so: `frontmatter/frontmatter-fields-a.md` for `callable set`, `schema-subset.md` for `schema slug`, `frontmatter.md` for `Pi tool` vs. `.loom callable`), allocate one fresh REQ-ID per relocated MUST under the page's existing prefix, and update the single back-pointer in `schema-subset.md` line 94 so it cites the new local anchor rather than the glossary.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader who follows the glossary's stated discipline ("if a glossary entry and its canonical page disagree, the canonical page wins") and goes looking on the canonical feature page to verify a contested term — say, whether `tool set` is admitted alongside `callable set` — finds nothing there, and is left unsure whether the glossary's MUST is binding or aspirational. The `schema slug` entry's claim that "a future grep gate uses this entry as its source of truth" only works as a stable anchor if the entry is normative; under the page's own framing it cannot be. The live-corpus arm of H5e (gated by H6a) will redden on these three sites as un-enumerated normative obligations unless they are relocated or explicitly mapped into the coverage-matrix code-keyed-obligation table.

## Finding

The glossary's opening paragraph (`docs/spec_topics/glossary.md` line 3) classifies the page descriptively: "Each entry is descriptive ÔÇö a one-paragraph reminder of what a term means and where it is used. The canonical, normative definition lives on the feature page named in the entry's `See:` reference; if a glossary entry and its canonical page disagree, the canonical page wins." The page's own row in the [REQ-ID prefix table](../spec_topics/governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md) matches this framing ÔÇö it carries the byte-exact `(no IDs ÔÇö narrative)` cell, so under GOV-3 / GOV-9 it is forbidden to coin `GLOS-N` REQ-ID anchors and therefore cannot satisfy GOV-9's `#prefix-n` cross-link contract for any normative rule it states.

Three entries violate that framing by hosting full naming-convention MUSTs whose subject is corpus-wide terminology rather than the term's meaning:

- **`callable set`** (line 15): "Authors and implementers MUST use the term `callable set` (or, with an explicit scoping modifier, `frontmatter callable set` / `loom's declared callable set`) for the per-loom resolved tool list; the bare phrases `tools: set`, `tool set`, `loom's tools`, and `available tools` are spec-prose synonyms to be avoidedÔÇª"
- **`schema slug`** (line 53): "Authors and implementers MUST use `schema slug` (or the bare form `slug` when context is unambiguous). The synonyms `schema hash`, `schema-hash`, `sha12`, `lowered-schema hash`, and `lowered-schema content hash` are spec-prose drift to be avoided; *a future grep gate uses this entry as its source of truth.*"
- **`Pi tool` vs. **`.loom` callable** (line 57): "Authors and implementers MUST use the canonical term `.loom callable` (or, in running prose, `` `.loom` callable ``) for a `.loom` entry in `tools:`; the synonyms `registered loom`, `registered subagent loom`, `registered-loom call`, and `loom callee` are spec-prose drift to be avoidedÔÇª"

Two consequences follow. (1) The `if a glossary entry and its canonical page disagree, the canonical page wins` doctrine is unsatisfiable for these three MUSTs: there is no canonical-page MUST to lose to, so the glossary is in fact the source of truth ÔÇö explicitly named so for `schema slug`'s grep-gate ÔÇö while the prose insists it cannot be. The `schema-subset.md` page reinforces the inversion ("the [Glossary](./glossary.md) entry pins the avoid-list"). (2) Because the glossary is a `(no IDs ÔÇö narrative)` page, each MUST is permanently un-anchored under GOV-9; reviewer-side prefix coining is impossible without first promoting the entire page out of narrative status (forcing a fresh `GLOS-N` prefix for every entry the page coins), and the H5e un-anchored-MUST gate's live-corpus activation will recognise each MUST as an un-enumerated normative obligation.

## Solution Space

For each relocated MUST, use the leftmost `See:` target as the canonical home and allocate one fresh REQ-ID under that page's existing prefix.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader who follows the glossary's stated discipline ("if a glossary entry and its canonical page disagree, the canonical page wins") and goes looking on the canonical feature page to verify a contested term ÔÇö say, whether `tool set` is admitted alongside `callable set` ÔÇö finds nothing there, and is left unsure whether the glossary's MUST is binding or aspirational. The `schema slug` entry's claim that "a future grep gate uses this entry as its source of truth" only works as a stable anchor if the entry is normative; under the page's own framing it cannot be. The live-corpus arm of H5e (gated by H6a) will redden on these three sites as un-enumerated normative obligations unless they are relocated or explicitly mapped into the coverage-matrix code-keyed-obligation table.

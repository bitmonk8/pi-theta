## Finding

`docs/spec_topics/future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md` ends with a third H2, `## Open contradictions (blocking, not deferrals)`, that hosts a single bullet: the extension-instance-survival open contradiction that gates the SM-3b / SM-4 / SM-5 / SM-6 degraded-state obligations. The bullet itself is just a re-pointer — the normative content lives at `pi-integration-contract/host-prerequisites.md` clause (a) (the authoritative-determination anchor) and the open question is tracked on the version-bump editorial-review checklist at `version-bump-step2.md#bump-checklist-instance-survival`.

The placement contradicts the Future Considerations taxonomy. `future-considerations.md` opens by declaring the page's contents as "Features deliberately deferred from loom 1.0, organised by the kind of decision loom 1.0 must make about each" and enumerates exactly four buckets — tooling deferrals, surface extensions, model-level changes, loom 1.0 non-goals. The host file's name (`model-changes-and-non-goals.md`) covers categories 3 and 4 only. An open contradiction is neither a deferred feature nor a non-goal: it is a blocking obligation whose *resolution* gates implementation, the polar opposite of "deferred from loom 1.0." The `## Open contradictions` H2 is therefore a fifth, undeclared bucket sitting on a page whose name does not name it, inside a hub whose intro explicitly excludes it.

The consequence is a discoverability split. A reader scanning Future Considerations for what loom 1.0 ships without will skim past this H2 as more of the same; a reader hunting for blocking issues will look at `plan.md` §Blocked obligations (which does cite V9l and clause (a)) and at `host-prerequisites.md` clause (a) (which owns the authoritative-determination contract), but has no reason to consult a Future Considerations sub-file. The bullet duplicates information already correctly anchored at its two normative homes while occupying a slot that misclassifies it.

The original F-0719 fix put the index entry where Future Considerations content was being authored at the time, rather than at a dedicated open-contradictions home; the misclassification entered with that single commit.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Delete the misfiled H2 and rely on the existing normative anchors

**Approach.** Remove the `## Open contradictions (blocking, not deferrals)` H2 (lead paragraph and the Extension-instance-survival bullet) from `model-changes-and-non-goals.md` outright. Add one short call-out paragraph at the top of `host-prerequisites.md` §`#degraded-state-host-prerequisites` flagging clause (a) as the spec's open contradiction at the loom 1.0 pin and forward-linking the version-bump checklist item (a) and `plan.md` §Blocked obligations. Future Considerations is restored to a clean four-bucket structure.

**Spec edits.**
- `model-changes-and-non-goals.md`: delete the trailing `---` separator and the `## Open contradictions (blocking, not deferrals)` section.
- `host-prerequisites.md`: at the top of the `#degraded-state-host-prerequisites` block (before clause (a)'s prose), insert a one-sentence call-out — e.g. *"Clause (a) below is the spec's open contradiction at the [loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin](#pi-sdk-pin); its authoritative determination is tracked at [version-bump checklist item (a)](./version-bump-step2.md#bump-checklist-instance-survival) and recorded as a blocked obligation at [`plan.md` §Blocked obligations](../../plan.md#blocked-obligations)."* The existing clause-(a) prose already covers the substance.
- No edit to `future-considerations.md`.

**Pros.** Eliminates the misclassification without inventing a new file. Removes a redundant re-pointer whose content lives at its normative home. Keeps the spec-side surface area small. Future Considerations' four-bucket taxonomy stays uncontradicted.

**Cons.** Spec-side has no single "list of open contradictions" index — the plan-side `## Blocked obligations` becomes the only enumeration. With only one open contradiction at the pin, this is proportionate; with a second, a fresh index would have to be authored.

**Risks.** Minor: anyone with a memory of the old H2 will not find a substitute index in the spec corpus and must look in `plan.md` instead.

### Option B — Promote to a top-level `open-contradictions.md` sibling

**Approach.** Author a new top-level page `docs/spec_topics/open-contradictions.md` whose body is the relocated H2 content (lead paragraph + Extension-instance-survival bullet). Add it to whatever top-level navigation `spec.md` and `future-considerations.md` use, and amend the Future Considerations intro to name the new sibling explicitly so the four-bucket disclaimer holds. Delete the misfiled H2 from `model-changes-and-non-goals.md`.

**Spec edits.**
- New file `docs/spec_topics/open-contradictions.md` with the relocated content and a forward-link to `host-prerequisites.md` clause (a) and `version-bump-step2.md#bump-checklist-instance-survival`.
- `model-changes-and-non-goals.md`: delete the trailing `---` separator and the misfiled H2.
- `future-considerations.md`: add one sentence to the intro distinguishing "deferred-from-loom-1.0 work (this page)" from "open contradictions blocking loom 1.0 work (see [`open-contradictions.md`](./open-contradictions.md))".
- Update any top-level `spec.md` navigation that enumerates topic pages.

**Pros.** Creates a discoverable spec-side index. Scales if a second open contradiction lands. Makes the blocking/deferred distinction visible at the navigation level.

**Cons.** Adds a topic page for a one-bullet list, which the spec's size-cap convention (`f5e89f4`) discourages. Requires a parallel update wherever the topic-page list is enumerated (Future Considerations Contents, `spec.md`, the REQ-ID-prefix table, etc.), each of which is a small surface for drift.

**Risks.** The page risks staying single-bullet indefinitely, which is exactly the case the spec's existing "index at the normative owner" pattern (e.g. `plan.md` §Blocked obligations) is shaped to handle.

### Recommendation

Take **Option A**. The bullet is already a re-pointer to `host-prerequisites.md` clause (a) and `plan.md` §Blocked obligations, so deleting the misclassified H2 loses no normative content. A short call-out at the top of `host-prerequisites.md` §`#degraded-state-host-prerequisites` keeps the "this is the open contradiction" signal where a reader of the normative page will see it. Implementer to watch: the call-out paragraph must use a relative link to `plan.md` (`../../plan.md#blocked-obligations`) and must forward-link the version-bump checklist item by its existing anchor, not restate its content.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader looking for what gates implementation of the degraded-state branch can still reach the answer via `plan.md` §Blocked obligations or via `host-prerequisites.md` clause (a), so no implementer is materially mis-directed. The cost is a misclassified H2 that puts blocking content into a "deferred / non-goal" hub whose intro explicitly excludes it, eroding the four-bucket taxonomy `future-considerations.md` declares.

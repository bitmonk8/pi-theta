## Finding

`docs/spec_topics/future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md` ends with a third H2, `## Open contradictions (blocking, not deferrals)`, that hosts a single bullet: the extension-instance-survival open contradiction that gates the SM-3b / SM-4 / SM-5 / SM-6 degraded-state obligations. The bullet itself is just a re-pointer — the normative content lives at `pi-integration-contract/host-prerequisites.md` clause (a) (the authoritative-determination anchor) and the open question is tracked on the version-bump editorial-review checklist at `version-bump-step2.md#bump-checklist-instance-survival`.

The placement contradicts the Future Considerations taxonomy. `future-considerations.md` opens by declaring the page's contents as "Features deliberately deferred from loom 1.0, organised by the kind of decision loom 1.0 must make about each" and enumerates exactly four buckets — tooling deferrals, surface extensions, model-level changes, loom 1.0 non-goals. The host file's name (`model-changes-and-non-goals.md`) covers categories 3 and 4 only. An open contradiction is neither a deferred feature nor a non-goal: it is a blocking obligation whose *resolution* gates implementation, the polar opposite of "deferred from loom 1.0." The `## Open contradictions` H2 is therefore a fifth, undeclared bucket sitting on a page whose name does not name it, inside a hub whose intro explicitly excludes it.

The consequence is a discoverability split. A reader scanning Future Considerations for what loom 1.0 ships without will skim past this H2 as more of the same; a reader hunting for blocking issues will look at `plan.md` §Blocked obligations (which does cite V9l and clause (a)) and at `host-prerequisites.md` clause (a) (which owns the authoritative-determination contract), but has no reason to consult a Future Considerations sub-file. The bullet duplicates information already correctly anchored at its two normative homes while occupying a slot that misclassifies it.

The original F-0719 fix put the index entry where Future Considerations content was being authored at the time, rather than at a dedicated open-contradictions home; the misclassification entered with that single commit.

## Solution Space

### Recommendation

Delete the `## Open contradictions (blocking, not deferrals)` H2 from `model-changes-and-non-goals.md` and move its open-contradiction signal to the normative page that already owns the contract. The bullet is a re-pointer to `host-prerequisites.md` clause (a) and `plan.md` §Blocked obligations, so removing it loses no normative content and restores Future Considerations to its declared four-bucket structure.

Spec edits:
- `model-changes-and-non-goals.md`: delete the trailing `---` separator and the entire `## Open contradictions (blocking, not deferrals)` section (lead paragraph and the Extension-instance-survival bullet).
- `host-prerequisites.md`: at the top of the `#degraded-state-host-prerequisites` block, before clause (a)'s prose, insert a one-sentence call-out flagging clause (a) as the spec's open contradiction at the loom 1.0 pin — e.g. *"Clause (a) below is the spec's open contradiction at the [loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin](#pi-sdk-pin); its authoritative determination is tracked at [version-bump checklist item (a)](./version-bump-step2.md#bump-checklist-instance-survival) and recorded as a blocked obligation at [`plan.md` §Blocked obligations](../../plan.md#blocked-obligations)."* The existing clause-(a) prose already covers the substance.
- No edit to `future-considerations.md`.

The call-out paragraph must use a relative link to `plan.md` (`../../plan.md#blocked-obligations`) and must forward-link the version-bump checklist item by its existing anchor (`./version-bump-step2.md#bump-checklist-instance-survival`), not restate its content.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader looking for what gates implementation of the degraded-state branch can still reach the answer via `plan.md` §Blocked obligations or via `host-prerequisites.md` clause (a), so no implementer is materially mis-directed. The cost is a misclassified H2 that puts blocking content into a "deferred / non-goal" hub whose intro explicitly excludes it, eroding the four-bucket taxonomy `future-considerations.md` declares.

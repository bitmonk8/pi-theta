## Problem

The verb *cross-route* / *cross-routed* is load-bearing at multiple spec sites — the `terminal-outcomes-aggregator` clause (a) on `overview-and-orientation.md`, the Failure bullet clause (a) and the `ERR-16` line on `errors-and-results/error-model.md`, and the per-boundary reconciliation and ceiling-#1 panic-uniqueness invariant on `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md` — but it is nowhere defined. The glossary lists the two adjacent ceiling descriptors `in-loop` and `query-terminating` but has no `cross-route` entry. Two readings remain available — reclassification (the slash-load `params` arm counts against HC3-d's worst-case budget under HC3-c's no-retry disposition) versus delivery-channel-only — and although the ceiling-#4 per-boundary table at `#ceiling-4-table` entails the reclassification reading, the aggregator sites do not say which is canonical, while a downstream worst-case-budget test rests on that reading being inferred from an undefined verb. The operative content the term names is fully pinned at `#ceiling-4-table`; what is missing is a one-line definition the citing sites can forward-link into.

**Introducing commits:** `e39deae` (2026-05-07, "Ceiling #4 opening classification contradicts its own table and CIO-1") + `a90a58a` (2026-05-10, "T29 pre-evaluation exclusion clause names ceiling #3 only")
**History:** `git log -S 'cross-rout'` against the spec corpus first surfaces `e39deae`, which rewrote the hard-ceilings aggregator one-liner to introduce the present-tense verb (`its slash-load 'params' arm cross-routes through ceiling #3's load-time classification`) and added the same phrasing to the parallel `hard-ceilings.md` page in the same commit, resolving an earlier finding that the opening classification contradicted CIO-1. Three days later `a90a58a` rewrote the terminal-outcomes aggregator paragraph (then on `docs/spec.md`, later relocated to `overview-and-orientation.md` by `81bf52a`'s 2026-06-11 spec/→spec_topics/ collapse) and the parallel `errors-and-results.md` clause to move from "(c) ceiling #4's slash-load `params` arm (which cross-routes through ceiling #3's load-time classification...)" to "(a) binder argument-binding failure — including ceiling #4's slash-load `params` arm cross-routed through ceiling #3 per..." — promoting the past-participle phrasing to the exact form the finding cites and adding the matching `ERR-16` line. Both commits resolved adjacent prior findings about cross-ceiling routing without adding a glossary anchor for the new verb they introduced; the term then propagated into the plan leaves (`V4e`, `V5e`, `V15j`, `V16a`) when the plan was authored against the post-`a90a58a` spec. The defect is the union of those two fix commits: each made the term load-bearing at one more site without ever defining it.

## Solution approach

Add a glossary entry anchored `id="cross-route"` in `glossary.md`, alphabetically between `callable set` and `in-loop`, defining the term and pinning it to the reclassification reading that `#ceiling-4-table` already entails, with a forward-link to `#ceiling-4-table` for the per-arm normative content. Add a forward-link to the new anchor at first use on each citing page: `overview-and-orientation.md`'s `terminal-outcomes-aggregator` clause (a), and `error-model.md`'s Failure bullet clause (a) and `ERR-16` line.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the per-boundary table and reconciliation paragraph at `#ceiling-4-table` (plus CIO-1, HC3-c, HC3-d) — they already own the operative content; do not re-author it.
- The glossary definition MUST NOT contradict the split established at `#ceiling-4-table` whereby the carrier ceiling (#4) owns the bound and canonical message while the routing ceiling (#3) owns the delivery surface and accounting budget.

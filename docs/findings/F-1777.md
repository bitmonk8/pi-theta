## Finding

The terminal-outcomes aggregator on `overview-and-orientation.md` excludes "ceiling #4's slash-load `params` arm cross-routed through ceiling #3" from the Failure-cause enumeration, and the parallel paragraph on `errors-and-results/error-model.md` excludes the same arm in the same words. The verb *cross-route* / *cross-routed* also carries weight on `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md` (where the reconciliation paragraph under the per-boundary table says the slash-load row "is the only row that crosses ceilings" and the ceiling-#1 panic-uniqueness invariant says the arm "cross-routes through ceiling #3 and so resolves at load-time off the trichotomy") and inside the `ERR-16` line of the pre-evaluation failure list ("cross-routed per CIO-1 through ceiling #3's no-retry classification"). The plan picks up the same vocabulary in `V4e`, `V5e`, `V15j`, and `V16a`.

Nowhere in the spec is the verb defined. The glossary covers `in-loop` and `query-terminating`, the two adjacent ceiling descriptors, but does not list `cross-route`. A reader can converge on a reading from the surrounding clauses, but two distinct readings remain available: (a) **reclassification** — ceiling #4's slash-load `params` arm *is* a ceiling-#3 enforcement point for routing purposes, counts against HC3-d's worst-case 3-call budget, and is settled by HC3-c's no-retry disposition; (b) **delivery channel only** — the depth-walk still fires *as* a ceiling-#4 check (per CIO-3 it is the first sub-check at the `params` AJV boundary) and only the *failure-mode template / system-note rendering* is borrowed from ceiling #3. The ceiling-#4 table actually entails reading (a) — it says the row "routes through ceiling #3's no-retry classification" and that the binder LLM call "still counts toward HC3-d's worst-case 3-call budget exactly as a regular AJV-on-`args` failure does" — but the aggregator paragraphs do not say which reading is the canonical one, and a downstream test for the worst-case-budget arithmetic on the slash-load arm currently rests on reading-(a) being inferred from an undefined verb.

The defect is a missing glossary anchor, not a wrong claim: the operative content the term names is fully pinned at `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md` (per-boundary table + CIO-1 + HC3-c/HC3-d). What is missing is a one-line definition the four aggregator/`ERR-16` sites can forward-link into so the term stops being load-bearing-and-undefined.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add a glossary entry that pins the term to reading (a), and link each citing site at first use:

1. **Glossary entry** in `docs/spec_topics/glossary.md`, alphabetically between `callable set` and `in-loop`:

   > <a id="cross-route"></a>**cross-route** — A descriptor for a hard-ceiling enforcement point whose breach is routed and accounted by *a different* ceiling than the one that owns the bound. In loom 1.0 the only cross-routing arm is ceiling #4's slash-load `params` row, whose AJV-boundary depth check fires per CIO-3 but whose surface is the binder's failure-mode-template system note (ceiling #3's load-time classification) and whose producing binder LLM call counts toward HC3-d's worst-case 3-call budget under HC3-c's no-retry disposition. The arm is therefore *off the success / fail / cancelled trichotomy* exactly like other ceiling-#3 breaches. The carrier ceiling (#4) still owns the bound (depth 5) and the canonical message (`JSON document depth exceeds 5`); the routing ceiling (#3) owns the delivery surface and the retry/accounting budget. Per-arm normative content lives at [Hard Runtime Ceilings — Per-boundary destination/surface table (ceiling #4)](./hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md#ceiling-4-table) and the reconciliation paragraph that follows it.

2. **Citing sites** — replace bare uses with glossary-anchored links at first use per page:
   - `overview-and-orientation.md` terminal-outcomes-aggregator clause (a): `… cross-routed through ceiling #3 per …` → `… [cross-routed](./glossary.md#cross-route) through ceiling #3 per …`.
   - `errors-and-results/error-model.md` Failure bullet clause (a) and the `ERR-16` line: same replacement at each first use within the page.
   - `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md` already owns the operative content and need not be edited for the term itself; the reconciliation paragraph's "is the only row that crosses ceilings" wording remains correct.

3. **Plan-side touch-up** — at each of `V4e`, `V5e`, `V15j`, `V16a`, change the first prose use of `cross-routed` / `cross-route` to link the new glossary anchor. No leaf acceptance criterion changes.

Edge cases the editor must watch:
- The reconciliation paragraph on `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md` says "this cross-ceiling handoff is the only *load-time* case where ceiling #4's enforcement point produces a non-`Err` surface". *Handoff* is a one-off rephrasing of the same concept; once `cross-route` is in the glossary, prefer `cross-route` here too so the term-set is closed.
- The ceiling-#1 panic-uniqueness invariant on the same page uses "cross-routes through ceiling #3 and so resolves at load-time off the trichotomy" — that clause already entails reading (a) and need only swap the bare verb for the linked form.
- Do *not* prefer the alternative suggested in the original finding ("reclassified as a ceiling #3 load-time enforcement point"). It collapses to one reading of the term but at the cost of an inaccurate framing — ceiling #4 still *owns the bound and the canonical message* per the per-boundary table, and rewording away the carrier-vs-routing-ceiling split would force a parallel rewrite of CIO-3, HC3-d's budget arithmetic, and the ceiling-#1 panic-uniqueness invariant.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A determined implementer reaches reading (a) by following the per-boundary table from each citing site, so the slash-load arm's budget and template behaviour is recoverable. The cost is paid in re-derivation: every reader of the aggregator paragraphs, the `ERR-16` line, and the four citing plan leaves must walk the cross-file chain to confirm what `cross-routed` denotes, and a future spec edit that touches ceiling #3's accounting cannot rely on the term to mean a single fixed thing.

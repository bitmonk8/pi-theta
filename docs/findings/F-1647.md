## Finding

The *Non-empty-scan canary* requires both the audited-files-walked counter and the in-scope-references-recognised counter to be strictly greater than zero on every invocation, and frames the second counter as an under-walking guard whose canary-red recovery arm is "widen the audit's scope configuration … until both counters clear their floors". This phrasing only makes sense if the audited source tree is guaranteed to contain at least one Pi-side surface reference; otherwise the second floor is unsatisfiable and the only "recovery" is to introduce a sham Pi reference for the sole purpose of pacifying the canary.

The justification for the floor exists in the corpus but is never stated at the canary. The mandatory **Extension entry point** factory (`default function (pi: ExtensionAPI)` under `extensions/index.ts`, per `extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md`) plus the load-bearing factory-time calls it pins (`pi.registerFlag`, `pi.registerMessageRenderer`, and the three factory-time `pi.on(...)` subscriptions) together guarantee, by construction, at least one category-(1) `pi.<member>` reference and at least one category-(2) named import from `@earendil-works/pi-coding-agent` somewhere under `src/**/*.ts` — i.e. at least one *recognised* in-scope reference under the canary's clause-(a)/(b)/(d) definition. The `> 0` floor is therefore safe against any conformant loom extension; the paragraph just never says so, and a reader pattern-matching it against a generic project gets a real false-positive impression.

This is a derivability gap rather than a correctness bug: a reviewer or new contributor reading the canary in isolation has no in-paragraph evidence that `recognised > 0` is a presupposition rather than an aspiration, and no cross-reference to the **Extension entry point** obligations that underwrite it. The fix is one sentence at the floor.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Append a single sentence to the *Non-empty-scan canary* paragraph in `audit-wire-and-canary.md`, immediately after the "MUST surface as `npm test` red rather than as a silent green" clause (i.e. before the discriminator paragraph), stating the precondition that underwrites the `> 0` floor on the second counter: that the **Extension entry point** obligations in `extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md` (the mandatory `pi.registerFlag`, `pi.registerMessageRenderer`, and the three factory-time `pi.on(...)` subscriptions on the `default function (pi: ExtensionAPI)` factory) guarantee, by construction, at least one category-(1) `pi.<member>` reference under `src/**/*.ts`, so any conformant loom extension satisfies the `recognised > 0` floor without the floor itself needing a `>= 0` relaxation arm.

The sentence MUST link to the **Extension entry point** anchor (`extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md#extension-entry-point`) so a reviewer hitting the canary-red recovery arm can navigate directly to the entry-point obligations the floor protects. The existing canary-red recovery arm ("widen the audit's scope configuration … until both counters clear their floors") stays unchanged — with the precondition stated, the recovery arm correctly reads as "restore the entry-point surface the audit was meant to see" rather than "introduce decorative Pi calls".

No edits to `extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md`, `audit-target-categories.md`, or `audit-resolution.md` are required — they already pin the obligations the new sentence cites.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A conformant extension cannot actually trip the floor (the entry-point obligations underwrite it), so no implementer ships broken behaviour. But the canary-red recovery arm reads as a generic "widen your audit scope" instruction with no anchor for *why* the floor must always be satisfiable; a future contributor staring at an unfamiliar canary-red on a refactor or extension-restructure may be misled about the right remediation (e.g. relaxing the floor, or adding decorative `pi.*` calls) rather than restoring the load-bearing entry-point surface that the floor is implicitly guarding.

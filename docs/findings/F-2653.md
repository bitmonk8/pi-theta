## Finding

`session-shutdown-semantics.md` describes the scope of sub-step 2's per-entry `try`/`catch` two ways that an implementer cannot reconcile. The detailed sub-step 2 prose states the per-entry `try`/`catch` "wraps both the stamp and the `abort()` call — on a stamp throw … the per-entry `catch` arm proceeds to the next entry (the `abort()` for this entry is skipped, since the stamp failed) so a per-entry write failure cannot escape sub-step 2's loop and defeat the no-throw-escapes-the-handler invariant." That is the stamp+abort scope.

The **Per-step isolation** paragraph in the same file contradicts it: "Sub-step 2 retains its existing per-entry isolation rule (per-entry `try`/`catch` around `loomAbort.abort()`) and does not emit `teardown-step-failed`." This is abort-only scope. The word "existing" reinforces the abort-only reading, because the genuinely pre-existing rule — the registry contract's per-entry `try`/`catch` (`active-invocation-registry.md`, "**`loomAbort.abort()` throwing inside the sub-step 2 iteration** is swallowed by a per-entry `try`/`catch`") — was authored before the `shutdownReason` stamp was added to sub-step 2 and only ever framed the `abort()`-throw case.

The stamp is not optional: sub-step 2 mandates stamping `entry.shutdownReason` *before* `loomAbort.abort()` on every entry, and that stamp can throw (frozen entry, proxied entry, write-rejecting setter, OOM during property assignment). An implementer who follows the **Per-step isolation** summary places the `try`/`catch` around `abort()` only; a stamp throw then escapes sub-step 2's loop, aborts teardown of the remaining registry entries, and breaks the explicitly-stated no-throw-escapes-the-handler invariant. An implementer who follows the detailed sub-step 2 prose wraps both and is safe. The two readings produce materially different teardown behaviour on a stamp throw.

Commit 75a9626 (2026-05-09) then added the `shutdownReason` stamp to sub-step 2 and authored the detailed "wraps both the stamp and the `abort()` call" prose (`git log -S "wraps both the stamp"`, `git log -S "stamp MUST happen before"` both first appear there), but did not sweep the Per-step-isolation summary's abort-only wording. Later commits c3de47a (relocation of the teardown sequence into `session-shutdown-semantics.md`) and 4e531dc (F-2231, sub-step 3 Clock isolation) reworked the surrounding text but carried the stale abort-only summary forward unchanged.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Update the **Per-step isolation** paragraph in `session-shutdown-semantics.md` so its sub-step 2 sentence matches the detailed sub-step 2 prose: state that sub-step 2's per-entry `try`/`catch` wraps **both** the `shutdownReason` stamp and `loomAbort.abort()`, that a stamp throw skips that entry's `abort()` and continues to the next entry, and that sub-step 2 emits no `teardown-step-failed` on either the stamp-throw or abort-throw arm. Drop the word "existing" (the abort-only rule it points back to no longer fully describes sub-step 2 once the stamp is in scope). Keep the registry contract's `abort()`-throw bullet accurate by leaving it as the abort-arm description; if any cross-reference precision is desired there, note that the same per-entry `try`/`catch` also covers the stamp performed by the `session_shutdown` handler, but no behavioural change to the registry contract is required.

Edge case for the implementer: the no-emit rule must hold on both arms — neither a stamp throw nor an `abort()` throw emits `loom/host/session-shutdown-teardown-step-failed` (sub-step 2 is excluded from the teardown-step-failed surface entirely); only sub-step 3's three bounded-await `Clock` calls and sub-steps 1/4/5 emit it.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two reasonable implementers diverge: one wraps both the stamp and `abort()` per the detailed prose and is safe on a stamp throw; the other wraps only `abort()` per the Per-step-isolation summary, so a stamp throw escapes sub-step 2's loop, halts teardown of the remaining in-flight invocations, and defeats the no-throw-escapes-the-handler invariant the spec itself asserts. The `V9g` "Ships when" per-step-isolation assertion cannot pin a single correct stamp-throw behaviour while the two passages disagree.

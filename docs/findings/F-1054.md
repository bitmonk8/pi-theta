## Finding

Step 2 of the Pi version-bump procedure (split across `version-bump-step2.md` and
`version-bump-step2b.md`) accumulates three independent labelling collisions
inside one short stretch of normative prose. Each collision is small on its
own; together they make the most-cited sub-step of the bump procedure ("step
2(a)") read ambiguously, and the spec corpus references both layers — the
editorial-review checklist items and the SDK-test directions — using the same
`(a)` / `(b)` short form roughly sixty times across the PIC pages and the
plan leaves.

The three collisions are independent — they live in disjoint paragraphs,
have disjoint fix surfaces, and the prose carries no shared mechanism that
would resolve them together:

1. **Letter labels `(a)`/`(b)` overload two distinct enumerations.** The
   step-2 preamble in `version-bump-step2.md` opens an editorial-review
   checklist whose items are labelled `(a)` … `(an)`. Sub-step 2 of the
   same step then opens a second enumeration — the build-time SDK
   surface-inventory test directions, labelled `(a)` (positive) and
   `(b)` (negative). A bare cross-reference to "step 2(a)" therefore
   has two readings, and neither the preamble nor sub-step 2 declares a
   disambiguation convention (e.g. "item (a)" for the checklist vs.
   "step 2(a)" for the test direction). The corpus relies on context to
   pick the right reading, which works in most cases but fails on the
   bump-procedure pages themselves where both enumerations are nearby.

2. **Parent/child anchor stems disagree on the `2b` segment.** The
   parent test-direction anchors live in `version-bump-step2.md` as
   `bump-step-2-positive` and `bump-step-2-negative` (no `2b` segment,
   even though the negative-direction prose lives in
   `version-bump-step2b.md`). The five resolution branches of the
   negative direction, defined in `version-bump-step2b.md`, are anchored
   as `bump-step-2b-delete`, `bump-step-2b-promote`,
   `bump-step-2b-exempt`, `bump-step-2b-stale-rewrite`, and
   `bump-step-2b-rewrite-shape`. So the branches carry a `2b` segment
   their own parent anchor doesn't, and the inventory-closure-audit
   prose elsewhere on the PIC pages cross-references the parent under
   `step2.md#bump-step-2-negative` while citing the children under
   `step2b.md#bump-step-2b-…` — the same anchor space split inconsistently
   across two files.

3. **Three names for the (e.i)/(e.ii) construct.** Item (e)'s body
   under `version-bump-step2.md` calls the two atomic verdicts
   "sub-outcomes" in one paragraph and "sub-check" in another; the
   appendix's *Outcome recording conventions* (anchored at
   `bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions`) carries the section
   heading "two-sub-outcome recording convention" and refers back to
   "two sub-checks against distinct file groups" in the same paragraph.
   Items (a)–(c), separately, call the corresponding host-prerequisite
   bullets "sub-item (a)" / "sub-item (b)" / "sub-item (c)". Three
   names for two structurally identical concepts (independently
   falsifiable atomic verdicts beneath a parent checklist item) leaves
   the appendix's normative MUST ("MUST be recorded as the two atomic
   sub-outcomes [(e.i)] and [(e.ii)]") with no anchored term a future
   "sub-check" or "sub-item" item could re-cite without ambiguity.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Resolve the three labelling collisions in step 2 of the version-bump
procedure with three independent edits. Land them as separate commits in
the order below so each diff lands on a stable baseline; run the
verification greps after each.

**1. Standardise the (e.i)/(e.ii) construct on "sub-item".** In
`version-bump-step2.md`, rewrite the two paragraphs that currently call
the (e.i)/(e.ii) construct "sub-outcome" / "sub-check" to use "sub-item"
verbatim, matching the "sub-item" usage already pinned for items (a)–(c)
of the editorial-review checklist:

- the introductory sentence to (e.i)/(e.ii) at the end of item (e)'s
  body ("two atomic sub-outcomes below … each carrying its own scope"); and
- the appendix paragraph anchored at
  `bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions` ("two atomic sub-outcomes
  [(e.i)] and [(e.ii)] defined as sub-items under item (e)").

Keep the appendix's bold section heading "**Item (e) two-sub-outcome
recording convention.**" unchanged so its slug does not move; only the
running prose under it switches to "sub-item". The appendix's normative
MUST keeps the same propositional content under the renamed term. Update
`V18c-version-bump-checklist.md`'s *Ships when* phrasing ("item (e)
recording its two sub-outcomes" / "either sub-outcome `(e.i)`/`(e.ii)`")
to use "sub-item" verbatim, in lock-step with the spec text it mirrors.
After this edit, run `rg 'sub-outcome|sub-check'
docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/` and confirm no residual
usages remain.

**2. Disambiguate `(a)` / `(b)` between the two enumerations.** Keep the
letter labels intact on both enumerations (items (a)–(an) on the
editorial-review checklist; (a)/(b) on the SDK-test directions) and pin a
one-sentence disambiguation convention in the step-2 preamble of
`version-bump-step2.md`: spec prose cites the editorial-review
enumeration as "item (a)" / "item (e)" / "item (ah)" and the SDK-test
directions as "step 2(a)" / "step 2(b)" / "sub-step 2(a)". Then sweep
every bare "(a)" / "(b)" citation so it carries the right qualifier
verbatim across `version-bump-step2.md`, `version-bump-step2b.md`,
`version-bump-intro.md`, `version-bump-triggers.md`,
`inventory-audit-intro.md`, `audit-recognised-shapes.md`,
`audit-wire-and-canary.md`, `host-interfaces-core.md`,
`overview-and-orientation.md`, `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md`,
`V18c-version-bump-checklist.md`, and `V18c-T-version-bump-checklist.md`.
Leave the editorial-review item labels untouched so the bump-commit
message format documented at `V18c`'s *Ships when* does not move. The
sweep is mechanical (literal grep on `step 2(a)` / `step 2(b)` /
`\(a\)` / `\(b\)` across the spec corpus plus the two `V18c` leaves);
re-run the grep post-edit so no residual ambiguous "(a)" remains on the
page where the convention is pinned.

**3. Re-anchor `bump-step-2-positive` / `bump-step-2-negative` to
`bump-step-2a` / `bump-step-2b`.** Rename the two parent
test-direction `<a id>` attributes in `version-bump-step2.md` to
`bump-step-2a` and `bump-step-2b` so the parent/child anchor stems share
the `2a` / `2b` segment the five `bump-step-2b-…` children already use
and the stem matches the file basename it lives in. Drop the editorial
"positive direction" / "negative direction" label from the anchor and
keep it only in the prose. Update the ~18 inbound cross-references
(~15 `step2.md#bump-step-2-positive`, ~3 `step2.md#bump-step-2-negative`)
across the spec corpus and plan to `step2.md#bump-step-2a` /
`step2b.md#bump-step-2b` — the negative-direction anchor moves with the
prose to `step2b.md`, since that is where the negative-direction prose
lives. No prose changes are required beyond the link-target updates.
Watch these edge cases:

- The audit pages' family→branch routing tables in `audit-resolution.md`
  carry `step2.md#bump-step-2-negative` and `step2b.md#bump-step-2b-…`
  links in adjacent table rows; update both together.
- The *Failure-surface contract* documentation in `audit-resolution.md`
  and `inventory-audit-intro.md` cites the negative-direction anchor by
  name under text like
  "[step 2(b)](./version-bump-step2.md#bump-step-2-negative)"; the
  rename moves both the link's file half (across the page split) and the
  fragment.
- GOV-31 in `governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` cites
  `bump-step-2-positive` by anchor in its in-code-constant carve-out
  prose; that citation is the test for sweep completeness.

After this edit, run `rg 'bump-step-2-(positive|negative)' docs/` and
confirm no residual usages remain (a missed inbound citation breaks the
deep-link silently).

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A practitioner reading the bump procedure can still resolve each
cross-reference by re-reading the cited paragraph, so neither the
editorial-review checklist nor the SDK-test directions misfire in
practice. The cost is recurring reader friction every time
"step 2(a)" or "sub-outcome" is cited — ~60+ occurrences across the
PIC pages and the plan — and a long-running risk that a future spec
edit lands a fix or audit obligation under the wrong reading because
the surface prose admits both.

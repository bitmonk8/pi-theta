## Finding

`V9f` and its paired `V9f-T` both carry the Tests bullet *"The callable set's visibility tracks the invocation."* Unlike every other Tests bullet on the pair, this one names no concrete observable and pins no REQ-ID. The siblings each cite `PIC-8` (restore-failure protocol), `PIC-19` (snapshot/swap-install failure), `PIC-44` (cache-hit byte-equality), and the `ToolDefinition.label` derivation (`cka-17`) — each with a pinned assertion. This bullet alone says nothing about *what* the active set / callable visibility contains during the invocation window, what is true before and after, or what an isolated concurrent invocation observes. An implementer cannot tell what to assert.

The bullet restates the headline design property of `tool-registration-lifetime.md` §Tool-registration lifetime and visibility ("tool visibility tracks the loom's invocation rather than the process lifetime"). But the concrete observables that realise that property are already owned and gated by other leaves, exactly as the coverage matrix records: the prompt-mode per-query active-set snapshot/set/restore-in-`finally` facet of `PIC-17` is closed by `V9c` (with the prompt→prompt-invoke restore facet on `V15d`), and the subagent-mode isolated `AgentSession` (no shared tool table; released at `dispose()`) facet `PIC-9` is closed by `V9i`.

The bullet is also not referenced by `V9f`'s Ships-when, which names only `PIC-8`, `PIC-19`, and the `ToolDefinition.label` derivation — so it gates nothing. An implementer marking the leaf done is not blocked by it, but is left unsure whether and what to assert for it, with the standing risk of re-implementing a redundant copy of the `PIC-17` active-set check that `V9c` already owns.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer reading `V9f`/`V9f-T` cannot tell what this bullet requires them to assert, so it is either skipped or re-implemented as a redundant copy of `V9c`'s `PIC-17` active-set check — duplicating coverage already owned elsewhere. The leaf's gated obligations (`PIC-8`, `PIC-19`, `PIC-44`, `cka-17`) are pinned and unaffected, so the leaf stays pickable and shippable; the cost is wasted or ambiguous effort, not a coverage gap.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Strike the bullet, deferring to the leaves that own the observable

**Approach.** Remove the "The callable set's visibility tracks the invocation." bullet from the Tests sections of both `V9f` and `V9f-T`.
**Plan edits.** Delete the bullet in `docs/plan_topics/V9f-tool-registration-lifetime.md` Tests and the identical bullet in `docs/plan_topics/V9f-T-tool-registration-lifetime.md` Tests. No other field changes.
**Spec edits.** None.
**Pros.** Removes the ambiguity outright with no duplication. The visibility observable stays asserted where the coverage matrix already maps it — `PIC-17` on `V9c` (prompt-mode per-query active-set snapshot/set/restore-in-`finally`) and `V15d` (prompt→prompt restore), plus the isolated subagent session on `V9i` (`PIC-9`). `V9f`'s gated obligations (`PIC-8`, `PIC-19`, `PIC-44`, `cka-17`) are untouched, and Ships-when does not reference the bullet, so no exit gate changes.
**Cons.** `V9f`'s no-`unregisterTool`-but-still-gated design property gains no dedicated test of its own (it carries no REQ-ID; spec documents the registration-accumulation consequence as cosmetic loom 1.0 behaviour).
**Risks.** Minimal.

### Option B — Restate to a `V9f`-distinct observable

**Approach.** Replace the bullet with a concrete observable `V9f` uniquely owns and `V9c`/`V9i` do not: the persistent-registration-vs-per-invocation-visibility property — the loom-synthesised tool's `pi.registerTool` entry persists for the process lifetime (no `unregisterTool`), yet the tool is reachable to the model only during the invocation (prompt mode: only inside the active-set gating window; subagent mode: only inside the spawned `AgentSession`, released at `dispose()`).
**Plan edits.** In `V9f` and `V9f-T` Tests, replace the bullet with that per-mode observable, cross-referencing `V9c` `PIC-17` (prompt-mode active-set facet) and `V9i` `PIC-9` (subagent isolation) for the facets those leaves own rather than re-asserting them.
**Spec edits.** None (the property is the headline of `tool-registration-lifetime.md` §Tool-registration lifetime and visibility).
**Pros.** Gives `V9f` a pinned observable for the no-`unregisterTool` design property it actually owns; also closes the sibling "no-`unregisterTool` rule unasserted" gap.
**Cons.** Larger edit; risks re-asserting the `PIC-17`/`PIC-9` facets already owned by `V9c`/`V9i` unless the new wording is scoped tightly to the persistence-vs-visibility tension.
**Risks.** Duplication of owned coverage if the scoping is loose.

### Recommendation

Take Option A: delete the "The callable set's visibility tracks the invocation." bullet from both `docs/plan_topics/V9f-tool-registration-lifetime.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V9f-T-tool-registration-lifetime.md` Tests. The substantive visibility observable is already pinned and gated elsewhere — `V9c`/`V15d` close the `PIC-17` active-set facet and `V9i` closes the isolated-subagent-session facet (`PIC-9`), as the coverage matrix records — so the bullet adds no observable `V9f` uniquely owns. If the team instead wants `V9f` to carry a pinned observable for its no-`unregisterTool` property, take Option B and scope the new wording strictly to the persistence-vs-per-invocation-visibility tension so it does not duplicate the `PIC-17`/`PIC-9` facets.

## Problem

In `version-bump-step2.md`, every editorial-review checklist item from (f) through (ap) closes with a near-identical ~55-word escalation sentence stating that the obligation is SHOULD-level at loom 1.0 and escalates to a build-time pin once Pi exposes a typed contract that step 2(a)'s surface-inventory probe can mechanically verify. The only token that varies between copies is the contract noun, and many copies use the bare generic "typed contract". This single uniform escalation policy is repeated 36 times, adding roughly two thousand words of duplicated prose that buries each item's substantive per-item obligation. Item (i) is the lone exception: it carries a distinct escalation disposition (post-1.0 maintenance CI-gate follow-up, not a typed-contract supersession).

**History:** Commit 8c930fe (2026-05-17) introduced the SHOULD-level escalation sentence with a single item carrying it. The duplication then accreted incrementally: each later commit that added a new presupposition item to the checklist copied the same closing sentence verbatim (count grew 1→2→4→5 by 2026-06-04 and reached 36 at HEAD as items through (ap) were added one fix at a time). No single commit created the ~35× repetition; it is the cumulative product of the per-item authoring pattern.

## Solution approach

State the escalation rule once at the step-2 preamble that already establishes the (f)–(ap) SHOULD-level band, generalising over the per-item contract noun. Delete the repeated closing escalation sentence from each item body (f)–(ap) that carries the standard boilerplate.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: item (i)'s distinct escalation disposition — do not fold it into the shared rule.

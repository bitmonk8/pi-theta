## Finding

In `version-bump-step2.md`, every editorial-review checklist item from (f) through (ap) closes with a near-identical ~55-word escalation sentence of the form: "This obligation is SHOULD-level only at loom 1.0; it escalates to a build-time pin (on the same footing as the seven-capability literal-read assertion under step 2(a) above) once Pi exposes a typed `<X>` contract that step 2(a)'s surface-inventory probe can mechanically verify, at which point the editorial audit prescribed here is superseded by the mechanical gate." This sentence is repeated 36 times across the page; the only token that varies between copies is the contract noun (e.g. "typed session-lifetime contract", "typed handler-promise-lifecycle contract", "typed turn-liveness contract"), and many copies use the bare generic "typed contract" with no variation at all.

The escalation rule is a single uniform policy applied identically to the entire (f)–(ap) SHOULD-level band — it states *when* any SHOULD-level audit item is promoted to a mechanical gate and *what* supersedes it. Stating it once per item adds roughly two thousand words of duplicated prose, pushing each item's actual per-item obligation (the specific Pi surface to re-read, the specific diagnostic that would otherwise fire) underneath a wall of repeated policy text. A reader scanning for the substantive content of item (ac) or (am) must skip past the same closing sentence they have already read thirty times.

Note that item (i) is the lone exception: it uses a different escalation framing ("wiring the fixture re-run into the bump procedure as a mechanical CI gate is the post-loom 1.0.0 maintenance follow-up…") rather than the standard boilerplate, so the consolidation must preserve (i)'s distinct disposition rather than fold it into the shared rule.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

State the escalation rule once and delete the trailing sentence from each item body.

- Add one escalation clause to the step-2 preamble (or to a short dedicated note adjacent to it) that already establishes the (f)–(ap) SHOULD-level band — e.g.: "Each of items (f)–(ap) is SHOULD-level only at loom 1.0; any such item escalates to a build-time pin (on the same footing as the seven-capability literal-read assertion under step 2(a) above) once Pi exposes a typed contract for the property that item audits and step 2(a)'s surface-inventory probe can mechanically verify it, at which point that item's editorial audit is superseded by the mechanical gate."
- Delete the repeated closing sentence from every item body (f)–(ap) that currently carries the standard boilerplate.
- The genericised preamble clause subsumes the per-item "typed `<X>` contract" noun (session-lifetime, handler-promise-lifecycle, turn-liveness, etc.); these nouns carry no obligation the item body does not already establish, so dropping them loses nothing. If an editor judges a specific contract noun worth keeping for one item, retain only that noun as a short parenthetical rather than the full sentence.
- Leave item (i) untouched: it carries a distinct escalation disposition (post-1.0 maintenance CI-gate follow-up, not a typed-contract supersession) and is not part of the shared rule.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader of the version-bump checklist must wade through 36 copies of the same escalation policy to extract each item's distinct per-item obligation, increasing cognitive load at a page that is already long and dense. No contributor behaviour diverges and no audit outcome changes — the escalation rule is stated identically everywhere — so the defect is purely organisational verbosity.

## Finding

The three expression-stdlib leaves each name the same concept two ways within a single page. Every heading reads `Expression stdlib members: <type>` and every Tests bullet links `expressions.md — <type> stdlib members`, but each leaf's **Adds** field switches to `standard-library member surface` (`The \`string\` standard-library member surface…`, `The \`array<T>\` standard-library member surface…`, `The \`object\` standard-library member surface…`).

The spec uses the short form: `expressions.md` introduces the feature as "A small stdlib is exposed on the primitive composite types." The heading/Tests phrasing is therefore the spec-aligned one, and the **Adds** phrasing is the stray variant. A reader must decide whether "stdlib members" and "standard-library member surface" denote the same set; they do, so the divergence is pure terminology noise on otherwise parallel leaves.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-leaf-creation (propagated by a later split)
**Introducing commits:** cb8737a — `pi-loom plan: resolve "Systemic leaf over-bundling across the plan corpus"` (2026-06-12), which created the predecessor `V3e-expression-stdlib.md` with `Adds` reading "The `string`/`array<T>`/`object` standard-library member surface…" while its heading was "Expression stdlib members" and its Tests used "stdlib members". Propagated by 2a4f797 — `pi-loom plan: resolve "V3e bundles three independent stdlib type namespaces"` (2026-06-18), which split V3e into V3f/V3g/V3h and copied the "standard-library member surface" Adds phrasing verbatim into all three.
**History:** `git log -S "standard-library member surface" -- docs/plan_topics/` returns only cb8737a (origin) and 2a4f797 (propagation); the heading-vs-Adds drift was born with the first stdlib leaf and has never been reconciled. The split preserved rather than introduced it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In each leaf's **Adds** field, replace the phrase `standard-library member surface` with `stdlib members`, matching that leaf's own heading and Tests bullets and the spec's `expressions.md` wording:

- `docs/plan_topics/V3f-expression-stdlib-string.md` — change `The \`string\` standard-library member surface evaluated on top of` to `The \`string\` stdlib members evaluated on top of`.
- `docs/plan_topics/V3g-expression-stdlib-array.md` — change `The \`array<T>\` standard-library member surface evaluated on top of` to `The \`array<T>\` stdlib members evaluated on top of`.
- `docs/plan_topics/V3h-expression-stdlib-object.md` — change `The \`object\` standard-library member surface evaluated on top of` to `The \`object\` stdlib members evaluated on top of`.

The `-T` tests-task partners already use "stdlib members" and need no change.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

Pure wording inconsistency within each leaf; no implementer is misled into a divergent implementation because the two phrasings clearly denote the same stdlib member set. The cost is reader friction and a small drift surface for future edits.

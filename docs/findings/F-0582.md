## Finding

`ceilings-3-and-4.md` CIO-1 reads "Ceiling #3 (binder per-class retry budget) is evaluated at slash-load time, before any *runtime-class* ceiling fires". The phrase *runtime-class* is italicised — the same convention the spec uses to flag a defined descriptor term — but no glossary entry and no local enumeration define it, and CIO-1 carries no cross-reference to where the term resolves. A reader must recover the intended meaning (the runtime-evaluation-time ceilings: #1, #2, and #4's four runtime enforcement points, as opposed to load-time ceiling #3) by inferring it from CIO-5 ("ceiling #3 never interleaves with #1/#2/#4") or by a cross-page lookup the term does not direct.

The asymmetry is visible against the spec's own pattern: the two sibling descriptor terms used the same way for hard-ceiling enforcement points — *in-loop* and *query-terminating* — each have a `docs/spec_topics/glossary.md` entry with an anchor that italicised uses resolve to. *runtime-class* is used identically (and recurs in the glossary `query-terminating` entry, `overview-and-orientation.md`, and `runtime-event-channel.md`) but is the one descriptor of the family with no defining home.

The meaning is recoverable, so no consumer is hard-blocked, but the term is load-bearing for the leaf that codifies CIO ordering: `V16a`'s CIO-1 test constructs "a synthesised candidate set carrying ceiling #3 (binder retry) and a runtime-class ceiling" and asserts the seam arbitrates #3 over it — the test author must already know which ceilings count as runtime-class to build the candidate set.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-by-refactor (single commit)
**Introducing commit:** `e39deae` (2026-05-07, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Ceiling #4 opening classification contradicts its own table and CIO-1\"")
**History:** Before `e39deae`, CIO-1 read "… before any runtime ceiling" (un-italicised, not "runtime-class"), and the Hard-ceilings aggregator preamble carried a crisp enumerated definition: "Three are *runtime-class* (ceilings #1, #2, #4)". Commit `e39deae`, while resolving an unrelated ceiling-#4 misclassification finding, simultaneously (a) reworded CIO-1 to "before any *runtime-class* ceiling fires", introducing the italicised descriptor into CIO-1, and (b) replaced the crisp "Three are *runtime-class* (ceilings #1, #2, #4)" enumeration with the split framing ("Ceilings #1 and #2 are uniformly *runtime-class* … Ceiling #4 is runtime-class at four of its five enforcement points …"), dissolving the nearest local definition the italicised term could resolve to. No glossary entry was added to compensate, so the term has been used in CIO-1 without a resolvable definition since that commit. The content later split to its current path at `f5e89f4` (the 100KB size-cap split) and carried the gap forward. Corroborated by `git log -S "before any *runtime-class* ceiling fires"`, `git show e39deae`, and the absence of a `runtime-class` anchor in `glossary.md`.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Inline parenthetical enumeration in CIO-1

**Approach.** Add an enumeration directly to CIO-1's italicised term, e.g. so it reads "before any *runtime-class* ceiling (#1, #2, and #4's four runtime enforcement points; #4's slash-load `params` arm cross-routes to ceiling #3 per the per-boundary table) fires".
**Plan edits.** None.
**Spec edits.** `ceilings-3-and-4.md` CIO-1 only.
**Pros.** Minimal, local, zero risk to other pages; the reader sees the definition at the point of use.
**Cons.** Leaves the other call sites still leaning on the undefined term (the `query-terminating` glossary entry, `overview-and-orientation.md`, `runtime-event-channel.md`); a future drift can reintroduce the same gap elsewhere.
**Risks.** Low.

### Option B — Glossary entry for `runtime-class`

**Approach.** Add a `runtime-class` descriptor entry to `docs/spec_topics/glossary.md` alongside the sibling `in-loop` and `query-terminating` entries, enumerating the runtime-class ceilings (#1, #2, and #4 at four of its five enforcement points, with #4's slash-load `params` arm cross-routing to ceiling #3 per the per-boundary table), and make CIO-1's italicised *runtime-class* resolve to it.
**Plan edits.** None.
**Spec edits.** `glossary.md` (new entry) + `ceilings-3-and-4.md` CIO-1 (point the italic term at the entry).
**Pros.** Restores parity with the other two descriptor terms; gives the term one authoritative home that every call site (overview, runtime-event-channel, the `query-terminating` entry) resolves to; re-establishes the single enumeration that `e39deae` dissolved, guarding against re-drift.
**Cons.** Slightly larger surface than Option A.
**Risks.** Low.

### Recommendation

Take Option B. The defect arose precisely when the single crisp `runtime-class` enumeration was dissolved from the preamble, and the term recurs across at least four spec locations; a glossary entry re-establishes one authoritative definition and matches the existing treatment of its sibling descriptors *in-loop* and *query-terminating*, which is exactly the pattern CIO-1's italics already invoke. Ensure the entry records that ceiling #4 is runtime-class at four of its five enforcement points (its slash-load `params` arm cross-routes to ceiling #3), so the entry does not contradict the per-boundary table. This is a spec-only edit; no leaf `Tests` / `Ships when` changes.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer or `-T` test author working `V16a` (or any leaf consulting CIO-1, e.g. `V4e`/`V11f`/`V5e`/`V13c`/`V15b`) must infer which ceilings are "runtime-class" from surrounding CIO clauses rather than reading a definition. The intended set (#1, #2, #4) is recoverable, so a working leaf can still be produced; the cost is reader friction and a residual risk of treating ceiling #4 inconsistently, since #4 is runtime-class at only four of its five enforcement points.

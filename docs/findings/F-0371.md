## Finding

`V9a`'s **Adds.** field describes the exported `FACTORY_PROBABLE_CAPABILITIES` constant as "the importable symbol `V18a`/`V18c` reconcile their partition against." The phrase is imprecise in two ways. First, "their partition" has no clear referent: grammatically it reads as if `V18a` and `V18c` each own a partition, inviting the misreading that the two leaves maintain separate capability-identifier subsets whose union is the five factory-probable items. There is in fact a single partition, and it lives in `V18a` (`CAPABILITY_OBLIGATIONS` entries each carry a factory-probed / verified-otherwise flag; `V18a`'s build-time partition assertion checks that the factory-probed-flagged subset equals `FACTORY_PROBABLE_CAPABILITIES`).

Second, conflating `V18a` and `V18c` as co-subjects of "reconcile their partition" mis-describes `V18c`. `V18c` does not reconcile a partition at all — its build-time literal-read assertion *reads* `FACTORY_PROBABLE_CAPABILITIES` (and `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS`) from `V9a`'s single source-of-truth pinned-constants block. The two consumers have distinct relationships to the constant, and the Adds prose collapses them into one verb that fits neither cleanly.

The precise semantics are pinned in the `PIC-5` Tests bullets of `V9a` and `V18a` and in `V18c`'s literal-read Tests bullet, so an implementer cross-reading those bullets would converge on the correct behaviour. The defect is confined to the descriptive Adds prose.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V9a-capability-probe.md`, edit the **Adds.** field's closing clause about `FACTORY_PROBABLE_CAPABILITIES` so it names a single shared constant and distinguishes the two consumers' uses. Replace the trailing clause

> as the importable symbol `V18a`/`V18c` reconcile their partition against.

with text that states `FACTORY_PROBABLE_CAPABILITIES` is the importable symbol that `V18a`'s build-time partition assertion reconciles its `CAPABILITY_OBLIGATIONS` factory-probed flags against, and that `V18c`'s build-time literal-read assertion reads. Avoid any phrasing that implies `V18a` and `V18c` each hold a partition or each contribute a subset (the "respective subsets together exhaust" framing reintroduces the union-of-subsets misreading and must not be used). Watch that the edit stays consistent with the `PIC-5` Tests bullet already in this leaf, which pins the symbol `V18a`/`V18c` import.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader of `V9a`'s Adds. prose can mis-model the capability partition as two per-leaf subsets (union = five) rather than one shared five-element constant owned by `V18a`, and can wrongly infer that `V18c` reconciles a partition rather than performing a literal-read. The `PIC-5` Tests bullets correct both misreadings, so a working leaf is still reachable; the cost is reader confusion about which leaf owns the partition and what each consumer does.

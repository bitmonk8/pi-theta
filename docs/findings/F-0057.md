## Finding

The set of `// allow-broad-catch:` same-line comments across `src/**` is the corpus's single broad-catch exemption registry. `conventions.md` §*Specific exception types only* pins its canonical name: *the lint allow-list* ("the phrase *the lint allow-list* denotes exactly the set of those same-line comments the scan discovers across `src/**`"). That term is used consistently elsewhere in the plan — H2a's Adds ("the set of those comments across `src/**` being the lint allow-list") and H5a's Adds ("the `no-broad-catch` lint allow-list reconciliation arm") both reach for it.

The H5c leaf, however, titles itself ``H5c` — `no-broad-catch` allow-list closing-gate reconciliation`` — in both `plan.md`'s table-of-contents link (line 33) and the leaf file's H1 heading — naming the same registry with the non-canonical synonym "`no-broad-catch` allow-list". No equivalence statement bridges the title's term to the canonical one at the title/TOC surface.

The H5c body does bridge the two terms internally ("scanning the `// allow-broad-catch:` comments across `src/**`, which that rule defines as the lint allow-list"), so an implementer reading the full leaf is not misled about behaviour. The residual cost is discoverability and consistency: a reader or search keyed on the canonical "lint allow-list" does not match the H5c title or its TOC entry, and the title advertises a third surface form of the same concept.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Align H5c's display title with the canonical defined term *the lint allow-list* from `conventions.md` §*Specific exception types only*. Edit the H1 heading at `docs/plan_topics/H5c-broad-catch-allow-list-gate.md` line 1 and the TOC link text at `docs/plan.md` line 33 to both read ``H5c` — lint allow-list closing-gate reconciliation`` (the two must match each other). `conventions.md` is the canonical source and is not edited.

The leaf's ID (`H5c`) and filename (`H5c-broad-catch-allow-list-gate.md`) are out of scope here — a file-slug rename is a separate, heavier change and is not required to resolve the terminology divergence; cross-references that cite the leaf by ID (`H5a`'s Adds, the `H5b`/`H6a` links) are unaffected by the title text. Optionally, normalise the lone "broad-catch allow-list" shorthand occurrences inside the H5c body (Convention line, Tests bullet, Ships-when) to "lint allow-list" while touching the file. No durable index edit is needed: the review's `terms.md` is a transient projection regenerated from the plan.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

Nothing breaks at implementation time: H5c's body resolves the equivalence and the gate behaviour is unambiguous. The defect is a discoverability/consistency gap — the leaf title and its TOC link advertise a synonym the rest of the corpus does not use, so cross-references and searches keyed on the canonical "lint allow-list" silently miss the leaf.

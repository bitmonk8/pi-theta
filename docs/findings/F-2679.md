## Finding

Step 1 lives mid-paragraph inside `version-bump-intro.md` with no TOC entry and no dedicated file, unlike steps 2, 2b, 3–4, and 5–7, so it is not discoverable from the procedure's page index.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** no single commit; organizational residue accreted across the page-split and checklist-growth history. Most identifiable single-defect origins: `a7a172c` ("version-bump-triggers.md H1 mis-scopes page contents", 2026-06-05) re-titled the H1 from "Version bump triggers" to "Version bump steps 5–7" while leaving the filename `version-bump-triggers.md` (the filename/heading mismatch); `11ee11c` (F-2252, "position editorial-review checklist as step 2's editorial arm") introduced the third label alongside the pre-existing "step 2 audit"; `1a59ccf` (F-2264) added the MUST item (ar) among the SHOULD items and demoted the inbound-reference audit gate to post-1.0; `74633aa` folded the inventory-closure post-1.0 framing inline across nine pages.
**History:** `git log --follow` on the five `version-bump-*.md` files shows the procedure originated as a single section, was fragmented into `spec_topics/` topic pages by `81bf52a` ("collapse spec/ section pages into spec_topics/") and the `f5e89f4` size-cap enforcement, then further split (`59819f8` F-1600 relocated steps 3–4 to their own file; `23d28a1` F-0958 realigned audit-cluster sibling files to basenames) — producing the inconsistent filename/heading styles and the step-1 placement. The ~35–40 SHOULD-escalation clauses and items (f)–(as) accreted incrementally over a long series of fix commits (e.g. F-0560, F-0718/F-0823, F-0824, F-0833, F-0981, F-1227, F-1618, F-2264). No commit set out to introduce a defect; the residue is the cumulative byproduct of repeated structural edits, which is why the durable fix is per-site cleanup rather than a revert.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Either give step 1 its own anchored subsection/TOC entry within `version-bump-intro.md` or split it into a dedicated `version-bump-step1.md`, matching the per-step discoverability the other steps have.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The procedure remains implementable, but the residue carries real mis-build risk: the literal `=== 7` and `.assert.ts` MUST invite a brittle literal-mechanism reading inconsistent with the page's own "any equivalent form" stance; the buried item (ar) MUST can be implemented as an optional SHOULD; and the interleaved post-1.0 notes can be mistaken for 1.0 obligations (over-build) or mask a deferred-vs-required boundary. The filename/heading/label/ordinal drift raises navigation and cross-reference cost without changing semantics.

## Finding

In `docs/spec_topics/expressions.md`, the **Grammar disambiguation** section (the `**`match`-arm body.**` bullet) reads:

> An arm body is a single expression; the full rule and the block-expression escape hatch for multi-statement arms are specified at [§ `match` expression — Arm syntax](#match-expression) **above**.

The link target `#match-expression` resolves to the `## `match` expression` heading, which sits *below* the bullet (the Grammar-disambiguation section ends and the `match` expression section opens immediately after). The directional descriptor "above" is therefore wrong — the referenced section is below the reference. The anchor itself is correct, so the hyperlink works; only the prose direction word misleads a reader navigating by the words rather than the link.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-by-commit
**Introducing commits:** 87dfa1d
**History:** The `match`-arm-body cross-reference bullet and the `## `match` expression` heading it targets were both added by commit 87dfa1d ("fix F-2145 — relocate match/? spec from error-model to expressions cluster"). That commit added the bullet in the Grammar-disambiguation section (added line 25 of its diff) *above* the `## `match` expression` heading (added line 27), yet the bullet's cross-reference text reads "above". The directional descriptor was therefore wrong at the moment of introduction — a carry-over from the prior error-model.md layout where the ordering differed. No later commit has touched the token (`git log -S`/`-G` over the cross-reference returns only 87dfa1d).

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Change "above" to "below" in the `match`-arm-body bullet of the Grammar disambiguation section so the directional descriptor matches the within-file ordering (`## `match` expression` follows the bullet). The anchor `#match-expression` is already correct and needs no change.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader following the words "above" scrolls the wrong direction to find the arm-syntax rule; the live anchor still works, so no implementer derives wrong behaviour. Pure navigational wording defect.

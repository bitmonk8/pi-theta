## Finding

The terminal-outcomes-aggregator paragraph in `overview-and-orientation.md` lists exclusions with the phrase "the following hard-ceiling cases are excluded from the **Failure-cause enumeration**: (a) binder argument-binding failure … (b) ceiling #4's in-loop model-driven tool-call args row". The capitalised "Failure-cause enumeration" reads as a defined term-of-art, but it is never introduced, anchored, or glossed anywhere in the corpus. A reader cannot tell whether it names (1) the inline evaluation-Failure arm of the success/fail/cancelled trichotomy stated two clauses earlier ("it fails (by returning `Err`, by panicking, or via a query-terminating enforcement point …)"), or (2) the separately-owned closed list in [Errors and Results — Terminal outcomes](errors-and-results/error-model.md#terminal-outcomes).

The two candidate referents are not interchangeable, and one reading is self-contradictory. Under reading (2), "binder argument-binding failure is excluded from the Failure-cause enumeration" directly contradicts the same page's pre-evaluation failure list, where binder argument-binding failure is item ERR-5 — i.e. it *is* in that enumeration. The intended meaning is reading (1): these cases are *not* evaluation Failures (they are pre-evaluation failures, or they feed a tool-error result back to the model). The actual routing is fully cross-referenced, so behaviour is determinable, but the undefined capitalised term forces the reader to reverse-engineer the antecedent and invites the contradictory reading.

The same undefined term is reused in `error-model.md`'s Terminal-outcomes section (both in the Failure bullet's exclusion sentence and again in "For the Failure-cause enumeration, no final value flows"), so the fix must address both pages to keep them in lock-step.

Later commits f5e89f4 (size-cap split) and 81bf52a (collapse `spec/` into `spec_topics/`) only moved the content mechanically — relocating the paragraph into `overview-and-orientation.md` with no content edits — so the undefined-term defect has been carried verbatim since 8168ea8.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace the bare capitalised "the Failure-cause enumeration" with an explicit referent to the evaluation-Failure arm of the trichotomy at every occurrence:

- In `overview-and-orientation.md` (terminal-outcomes aggregator): change "are excluded from the Failure-cause enumeration" to name the inline fail arm — e.g. "are NOT evaluation Failures (the *fail* arm of this success/fail/cancelled trichotomy)". This makes clear the exclusions are not failure-branch outcomes, and that pre-evaluation failures and the model-fed-back tool-call args row sit outside the trichotomy.
- In `error-model.md` (Terminal outcomes): apply the same rewording in the Failure bullet's exclusion sentence and in the trailing "For the Failure-cause enumeration, no final value flows" sentence, so the page no longer uses an undefined term-of-art for its own fail arm.

Do not introduce a glossary/anchor entry for "Failure-cause enumeration" — the concept is already named (the fail arm of the trichotomy); the defect is the undefined alias, and removing the alias is cleaner than defining it. The implementer must update all three occurrences (one in `overview-and-orientation.md`, two in `error-model.md`) in the same commit to keep the aggregator and its owning page consistent.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader resolving "Failure-cause enumeration" to the error-model pre-evaluation list reaches a self-contradictory statement (binder argument-binding failure being both listed and "excluded"), making the trichotomy/pre-evaluation boundary harder to internalise. Per-case routing is cross-referenced elsewhere, so a careful implementer still produces correct behaviour; the cost is comprehension friction and a latent mis-read of which outcomes count as evaluation Failures.

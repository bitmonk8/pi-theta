## Finding

In `version-bump-step2.md`, step 2's editorial-review checklist runs items `(a)` through `(ap)` — 42 items in all. The **Outcome recording conventions** block, which is the authoritative definition of (i) item (a)'s one-shot authoritative-determination recording shape (`governed-by-rebind` / `provably-differs` verdicts, evidentiary standard, acceptance criterion) and (ii) item (e)'s `N/A — superseded by …` definition and its two-sub-outcome `(e.i)`/`(e.ii)` recording convention, is positioned at the very end of the checklist — after item (ap) and after the "Any future presupposition…" closing paragraph.

The first checklist item, item (a), opens with two anchored forward references into that end-placed block (`#bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions`); item (e), the fifth item, references it three more times for its `N/A` definition and sub-outcome shape. A contributor reading the checklist top-to-bottom therefore hits the obligations that depend on the conventions roughly 40 items before the conventions themselves are stated. The block's own opening sentence ("…collected here so they are discoverable apart from the item bodies' inline prose") confirms the intent was to surface these shapes in one place; placing that place last defeats the discoverability goal for sequential readers of the two items that actually consume it.

The anchored links keep the content reachable, and items (a) and (e) carry enough inline prose to be substantially self-describing, so this is an organisation/reading-order defect rather than a content gap.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Move the entire `<a id="bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions">` **Outcome recording conventions** block from its current position (after item (ap)) to immediately after the step-2 preamble paragraph and before item (a). The block's internal "defined in the step-2 preamble above" reference stays valid since the preamble remains above it, and the anchor id is preserved so every existing `#bump-step-2-outcome-recording-conventions` link continues to resolve. After the move, a sequential reader meets the recording conventions before reaching item (a), eliminating both forward references. Leave the closing "Any future presupposition…" paragraph where it is — it is a checklist-maintenance note, not a recording convention, and belongs after the items.

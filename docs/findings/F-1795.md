## Finding

`imports.md`'s `.warp` rules describe `invoke(...)` from inside a `.warp` `fn` body as: "the invocation executes against the *calling* `.loom`'s conversation (or spawns a fresh isolated one if the callee is subagent-mode), **exactly like a `@`...`` query inside a warp function**." The trailing comparison is intended only to fix the conversation-routing rule (a `.warp` body has no conversation of its own, so its embedded primitives borrow the calling `.loom`'s conversation), but its surface reading is broader: it equates `invoke` from a `.warp` body with a query in all respects.

That over-broad reading is wrong on at least one observable axis. `invoke` starts a fresh loom evaluation and pushes a countable frame onto the invoke-depth chain governed by `INV-4` in `invocation.md` (`Depth is the count of countable frames on the active call chain, where a countable frame is any direct invoke(...) call, …`). Queries do not. A `.warp`-author who reads this sentence in isolation and treats the equivalence as load-bearing can conclude that invokes originating in `.warp` bodies inherit *every* property of queries — including their non-participation in depth accounting — and omit the per-chain counter increment for that arm. Two reasonable implementers will then diverge: one increments the depth counter for invokes regardless of caller file kind (per `INV-4`), the other only for invokes originating in `.loom` bodies (per the most natural reading of "exactly like").

The intended rule has two clauses that should be stated directly rather than smuggled in via the comparison: (1) the conversation against which the embedded `invoke` executes is the calling `.loom`'s conversation (or, for a subagent-mode callee, a fresh isolated one) — the same conversation-routing rule already stated for `@`...`` queries one bullet above; (2) `INV-4`'s invoke-depth accounting still applies to invokes originating in `.warp` `fn` bodies, exactly as it applies to invokes originating in `.loom` bodies.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-the-introducing-feature-commit
**Introducing commit:** `8eea523` (2026-05-04, *spec: fill implementation-blocking gaps*) — the same commit that first allowed `invoke` from inside `.warp` `fn` bodies; its commit message lists "Allow invoke from .warp function bodies" as a bullet, and its diff against the prior `spec.md` adds the bullet verbatim with the "exactly like a `@`...`` query inside a warp function" comparison already in place.
**History:** The "exactly like" wording is the only wording the rule has ever had. The May 4 split (`fecb504`, *Split spec.md and plan.md into per-topic / per-phase files*) moved the bullet into `docs/spec_topics/imports.md` byte-for-byte. The only subsequent edit (`be0dba6`, 2026-06-25, *pi-loom spec-plan: fix F-1280 — forward-ref INV-4 depth-frame rule from functions.md + imports.md*) appended a *cross-file `.warp fn` call* depth-frame sentence to the same bullet; it left the "exactly like" clause untouched and only addressed the orthogonal cross-file `fn` arm, not the direct `invoke` arm whose semantics this finding flags. `git log -L` on the bullet (run via `git log -L /May call \`invoke/,+1:docs/spec_topics/imports.md`) confirms two commits: the June 25 cross-file-frame append and, before the topic split, the original May 4 introduction (`git log -S "exactly like a \`@\`...\`\`" --all` returns exactly `8eea523` and `fecb504`). The defect entered with the feature; no prior shape exists to revert to.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace the trailing comparison in the `May call invoke(...)` bullet. Concretely, edit the bullet in `docs/spec_topics/imports.md` so that the second clause reads (substance, not exact wording):

> The invocation executes against the *calling* `.loom`'s conversation by the same conversation-routing rule that applies to `@`...`` queries inside a `.warp` `fn` body (see the *Inside fn bodies* bullet above), or spawns a fresh isolated conversation when the callee is `subagent`-mode. `INV-4`'s invoke-depth accounting (see [Invocation — Invocation depth bound](./invocation.md#inv-4)) applies to these invokes unchanged: a direct `invoke(...)` from a `.warp` `fn` body pushes a countable frame onto the active call chain on the same terms as a direct `invoke(...)` from a `.loom` body.

Keep the existing trailing sentence about cross-file `.warp` `fn` calls being countable frames (added in `be0dba6`) — it covers the orthogonal `fn`-call arm and is still needed.

Authoring notes for the implementer:

- The rewrite is local to one bullet in `imports.md`; do not touch `invocation.md`'s `INV-4` wording.
- Do not say "behaves identically to a query" or any equivalent — that re-introduces the over-broad reading. State the conversation-routing rule and the `INV-4` applicability independently.
- The subagent-mode parenthetical stays exactly as it was — that arm is the conversation-routing rule for `subagent`-mode callees and is unrelated to the over-broad equivalence.
- Cross-link `INV-4` by anchor (`./invocation.md#inv-4`) so the reciprocal-cross-reference gap separately flagged by "INV-4 cross-file depth rule depends on `imports.md` with no reciprocal cross-reference" gets partial coverage from the same edit. (Full coverage of that finding still requires its own changes.)

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

An implementer who treats `exactly like a @...\`` query` as load-bearing can drop invoke-depth counting on the `.warp`-originating arm — producing a build where `INV-4`'s ceiling is enforced for invokes from `.loom` bodies but silently bypassed for invokes from `.warp` library code. The unboundedness is exploitable by any recursion routed through a `.warp` helper and undermines the "every invoke chain has a finite, observable cap" guarantee `INV-4` is the sole guardian of.

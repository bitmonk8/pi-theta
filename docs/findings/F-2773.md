## Finding

The V9 ("Extension host integration") interleave note in `docs/plan.md` enumerates two ordering facts: the `V11a` mid-V9 interleave (`V9b → V11a → V9c`/`V9i`/`V9j`) and the cross-slice `V9h`/`V9g`/`V9l`→`V18c` edges. Its prose then reads as if those are the only departures from letter order, closing with the blanket "Sequence by **Deps**, not slice number."

But the V9 listing carries three further prerequisite-after-dependent inversions that the note never names:

- `V9m` (`LoomRegistry` drain-state contract) is listed 16th yet is a direct **Deps** entry of `V9h` (`V9h-T, V9a, V9m, V18c`) and `V9p` (`V9p-T, V9m, H4a, V9b, V7d`), and a transitive prerequisite of `V9g` (which depends on `V9h`). A load-bearing prerequisite sits near the very end of the listing.
- `V9h` (9th) is a direct **Deps** entry of `V9g` (8th): `V9g.Deps = V9g-T, V9a, V9e, V9h, V17a`. The dependent is listed before its prerequisite.
- `V9j` (12th) is a direct **Deps** entry of `V9n` (4th): `V9n.Deps = V9n-T, V9c, V9j, V17a`. The prerequisite is listed eight positions after its dependent.

Sibling slices set the precedent of enumerating their non-letter-order edges in the interleave note (V10 names `V10c → V10a → V10b`/`V10d`; V11 names `V11e → V11h`; V12, V14, V15, V17 each call out their cross-slice edges). V9's note is the one slice that flags some of its inversions while silently omitting others, so a reader who trusts the note as the exhaustive set of V9 ordering departures is misled about which leaves carry load-bearing late-listed prerequisites.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The dep-driven pickability predicate (How-to-use step 3) and the note's "Sequence by **Deps**, not slice number" close govern execution, so the build does not break — an implementer following the predicate picks `V9m`/`V9h`/`V9j` before their dependents regardless of listing order. The defect is a guidance gap: V9's interleave note is inconsistent with every sibling slice that enumerates its inversions, so a reader trusting the note as the complete set of V9 ordering departures can mistake the letter-ordered listing for the build order and overlook that `V9m` (16th) and `V9j` (12th) are load-bearing prerequisites.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `b9b427d` (note authored with narrow scope), `0c3a2a2` (added `V9n`→`V9j` inversion), `cb8737a` (added `V9m` prerequisite inversions)
**History:** `b9b427d` ("resolve slice-ordering narrative overpromises DAG order", 2026-06-10) introduced the V9 interleave note enumerating only the `V11a` interleave and the `V9h`/`V9g`→`V18c` edge. At that commit `V9g` was already listed before `V9h` while depending on it (the `V9g`→`V9h` edge dates to `c6a664e`, the initial plan build), so the `V9h`→`V9g` listing inversion has been un-enumerated since the note's inception. `0c3a2a2` ("resolve V9c bundles three concern clusters in one leaf pair") later added the `V9n` leaf depending on `V9j`, introducing the `V9j`→`V9n` inversion without expanding the note; `cb8737a` ("resolve Systemic leaf over-bundling across the plan corpus") split `V9m` out as a separate 16th-listed leaf that is a prerequisite of `V9h`/`V9p`/`V9g`, again without expanding the note. The omission is the joint product of the note's originally narrow scope and two later leaf-restructuring commits that did not reflect their new intra-V9 edges back into it; no single commit is solely at fault.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan.md`, extend the V9 ("Extension host integration") interleave note so it names the three currently-omitted intra-V9 prerequisite edges alongside the `V11a` and `V18c` facts it already carries: `V9m → V9g`/`V9h`/`V9p` (the `LoomRegistry` drain-state contract listed 16th is a prerequisite of those three), `V9h → V9g` (`V9g` depends on `V9h`), and `V9j → V9n` (`V9n` depends on `V9j`). This brings V9 in line with the sibling slices (V10/V11/V12/V14/V15/V17) whose interleave notes enumerate their own non-letter-order edges. Keep the existing "Sequence by **Deps**, not slice number" close. The leaf files are read-only for this fix — their **Deps** fields are already correct; only the plan.md narrative is incomplete. Do not edit the spec.

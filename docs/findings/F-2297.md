## Finding

`V4c`'s `ERR-12` Tests bullet ends with the clause "the live-surface re-assertion `V9i` carries off the `H4a` double." The verb phrase "carries off the H4a double" has no settled meaning here and admits two incompatible readings:

- (a) `V9i` delivers (carries) the delegated live-surface re-assertion using the `H4a` double — i.e. the assertion is still run against the double; or
- (b) `V9i` carries the re-assertion *away from* / supersedes the `H4a` double — i.e. the `H4a` double is no longer the vehicle for it.

The actual design is neither stated cleanly: `V4c`'s `ERR-12` is exercised against the `H4a` harness modelling a subagent-mode callee, and the live-surface confirmation (a mid-stream cancellation does not mutate the real subagent `AgentSession`'s committed turns) is a real-host-only behaviour witnessed at the manual smoke gate and owned by `V9i` — exercised against the **real host**, not the `H4a` session double. `V9i`'s own `ERR-8` bullet and `V4c-T`'s parallel `ERR-12` bullet ("— not the live `V9i` surface") both carry this correct framing; only `V4c`'s trailing clause garbles it, and under reading (b) it can be misread as implying the `H4a` double is obsolete.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V4c-terminal-outcomes.md`, V4c Tests, ERR-12 bullet, replace the trailing clause "the live-surface re-assertion `V9i` carries off the `H4a` double" with wording that states the ownership and surface explicitly — e.g. "the live-surface re-assertion is owned by `V9i`, which exercises it through the real host rather than the `H4a` session double used here." The replacement must (1) name `V9i` as the owner of the live-surface re-assertion, (2) say it runs against the real host, and (3) make clear the `H4a` double is the vehicle for `V4c`'s own offline `ERR-12` assertion (not superseded). Keep the rest of the bullet — the `H4a`-harness subagent-mode framing and the smoke-gate cross-reference to `real-host-smoke-gate.md` criterion (c) — intact.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader who takes reading (b) could conclude `H4a`'s session double is superseded by `V9i` and is no longer the vehicle for the `npm test`-side `ERR-12` assertion, mis-scoping what `V4c` actually verifies offline. The corrective signal in `V4c-T`'s mirror bullet and in `V9i` means a careful implementer still recovers the intended split, so no leaf is blocked.

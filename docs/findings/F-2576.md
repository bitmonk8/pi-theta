## Finding

`invocation.md` uses the bare word "drain" for the diagnostic output channel in three places: the *Resolution* paragraph says an escaping path surfaces "a `loom/load/invoke-path-escape` diagnostic on the diagnostics drain" and that "operators reading the drain can detect escape attempts"; the *Static resolution* paragraph aggregates "each visited file['s] diagnostics … into the entry loom's drain". The `loom 1.0 seam — symlink-resolution hardening` blockquote repeats "diagnostics drain", and `implementation-notes.md`'s *Static-resolution load pass* bullet likewise aggregates diagnostics "into the entry loom's drain".

Independently, the Pi Integration Contract formalises an unrelated concept under the same word: the `LoomRegistry` **drain-state** lifecycle (`pi-integration-contract/drain-state-contract.md`), with a `drained` boolean, a `drainStateTag`, `LoomRegistry.drain()`, `markRuntimeDegraded`, and a closed `"shutting-down" | "degraded-needs-reload"` tag set. There "drain" denotes registry teardown during `session_shutdown`, not a diagnostic sink.

"drain" as the diagnostic-channel name is purely informal: it has no glossary entry and the diagnostics topic (`diagnostics/diagnostic-shape.md` *Multi-error reporting*) never names the aggregation sink "drain" — it speaks of the batched `pi.sendMessage` / persistent-diagnostic channel. The drain-state concept, by contrast, is a load-bearing formalised PIC term. A reader meeting "diagnostics drain" in `invocation.md` after the PIC drain-state machinery cannot tell the two apart, and a corpus-wide grep for the registry lifecycle term is polluted by the diagnostic-channel uses.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `333ba2f` (2026-05-04, "spec: define static resolution for invoke/tools callees (Option C)") and `c8b8790` (2026-05-05, "pi-loom plan: resolve 'invoke-path-escape parse-only TOCTOU'") introduced the informal diagnostic-channel uses of "drain" in `invocation.md`; `52b9101` (2026-05-11, "pi-loom spec: resolve 'T24 — Fork-reason watcher closure …'") introduced the formal `LoomRegistry` drain-state / `LoomRegistry.drain` lifecycle that collides with them.
**History:** `git log -S "diagnostics drain"` / `-S "entry loom's drain"` (with `--follow`) on `invocation.md` traces the diagnostic-channel "drain" wording to the static-resolution and invoke-path-escape commits of 2026-05-04/05; `git log -S "drain-state" --reverse` and `git log -S "LoomRegistry.drain" --reverse` place the first appearance of the registry-lifecycle "drain" concept a week later in the T24 fork-reason watcher-closure commit (`52b9101`). The overload was latent in neither commit alone: the informal channel name pre-existed and was benign until the later PIC commit coined a formal, load-bearing "drain-state" sharing the word. The durable fix therefore addresses the term collision, not any single commit.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Reserve "drain" / "drained" / "drain-state" for the PIC `LoomRegistry` lifecycle concept, and rename the informal diagnostic sink to a non-colliding term — "diagnostic output channel" (or "the loom's diagnostic batch", matching `diagnostic-shape.md`'s *Multi-error reporting* framing). Apply at all four sites:

- `invocation.md` *Resolution* paragraph: "a `loom/load/invoke-path-escape` diagnostic on the **diagnostic output channel**" and "operators reading the **diagnostic output channel** can detect escape attempts".
- `invocation.md` *symlink-resolution-hardening* seam blockquote: "a `loom/load/invoke-path-escape` diagnostic on the **diagnostic output channel**".
- `invocation.md` *Static resolution* paragraph: "aggregated into the entry loom's **diagnostic batch**" (keeping the `(file, line, col)` sort and the *Multi-error reporting* cross-reference).
- `implementation-notes.md` *Static-resolution load pass* bullet: same substitution for "the entry loom's drain".

Pick one replacement term and use it consistently across all four sites. Leave every PIC drain-state usage unchanged. No glossary entry is required for the renamed channel, but if one is added it must not reuse "drain".

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

One word names two unrelated concepts in the same topic neighbourhood — an informal diagnostic output channel and the formalised PIC registry-teardown lifecycle. A reader can conflate them, and a grep for the drain-state term returns spurious hits. No behavioural divergence: the diagnostic-aggregation behaviour is fully specified under its own rules regardless of the label.

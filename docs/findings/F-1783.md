## Problem

The "The loom 1.0 reference implementation:" sub-bullets under §Runtime in `implementation-notes.md` carry three normative behavioural obligations rather than implementation-specific recipe. The `tool_loop.max_rounds` cap-counting clause and the user-visibility clause duplicate obligations already pinned normatively on canonical pages (QRY-16 and `conversation-drive.md`'s typed-query bullet), creating drift surface every time either side is edited. The prohibition on installing a global `before_provider_request` payload-rewrite hook has no normative anchor on any canonical page — it lives only inside the "reference implementation" framing, so an implementer can read it as one acceptable recipe, install a global hook that fingerprints every provider call in every session, and still satisfy every named canonical-page obligation.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-by-commit
**Introducing commits:** 82be125 ("spec: adopt P1 typed-query two-phase loop (Option A)", 2026-05-04)
**History:** The "reference implementation:" parent line and its sub-bullets — including the `before_provider_request` rejection, the cap-counting clause, and the user-visibility clause — were authored together in commit 82be125 when typed queries were re-shaped as the two-phase tool loop. Earlier text (prior to 82be125) housed the typed-query mechanism in a single non-bulleted paragraph that conflated normative behaviour with the synthesised-tool recipe; the rewrite split the recipe out under the "reference implementation:" heading but did not lift the surviving normative obligations to their canonical pages. Subsequent edits — 1ea0455 (2026-06-04, "DI-seam vs reference-implementation placement boundary") and 7f75947 (2026-06-05, "Typed-query forced-respond tool choice delivery channel") — refactored adjacent material and rewrote the user-visibility sub-bullet to route the forced respond turn off-session, but left the placement defect in place: the rewrites added the `complete()`-driven off-session phrasing and the cross-reference scaffolding without moving the binding obligations off the "reference implementation" paragraph. The defect is therefore a residue of the original Option-A adoption rather than the product of a later regression.

## Solution approach

Narrow the "The loom 1.0 reference implementation:" sub-bullets under §Runtime to implementation-specific recipe (synthesised tool name, `Type.Unsafe<unknown>` wrap, label literal, registration-cache and snapshot/restore mechanics, the `complete()` channel choice), removing the embedded behavioural obligations. Promote the prohibition on a global `before_provider_request` payload-rewrite hook, with its cross-session-fingerprinting rationale, into `conversation-drive.md`'s typed-query bullet as a normative clause. Replace the `tool_loop.max_rounds` cap-counting paraphrase with a forward-link to QRY-16, and replace the user-visibility paraphrase with a forward-link to `conversation-drive.md`'s "MUST surface the validated payload exactly once and MUST NOT add user-visible turns" obligation.

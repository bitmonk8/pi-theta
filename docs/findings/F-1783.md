## Finding

`docs/spec_topics/implementation-notes.md` introduces the typed-query mechanism with the sentence "The loom 1.0 reference implementation:" and then enumerates five sub-bullets. Three of those sub-bullets carry obligations that read as normative behavioural constraints — not as recipe choices a reference implementation merely happens to make:

1. The two-phase-loop sub-bullet's clause "The runtime does **not** install a `before_provider_request` payload-rewrite hook in loom 1.0 … the per-call `options.toolChoice` route on `complete()` is scoped to exactly the forced respond turn and avoids the cross-session-fingerprinting machinery a global hook would require."
2. The `tool_loop.max_rounds`-enforcement sub-bullet's "counting free-phase tool-call rounds only; the forced respond turn … is the exempt-routed terminator … and is not counted in the cap. … `tool_loop_exhausted` is unreachable on the typed-query path".
3. The user-facing-layer sub-bullet's "the forced respond turn runs off-session through `complete()` … and is therefore not rendered as a transcript turn, matching the behavioural contract's 'no user-visible turns beyond the natural free-phase turns' clause."

Obligations (2) and (3) are already pinned normatively on the canonical pages — `tool_loop.max_rounds` cap-counting and the unreachable `tool_loop_exhausted` branch in `query/query-tool-loop.md` QRY-16; the user-visibility clause and the off-session forced-respond dispatch in `pi-integration-contract/conversation-drive.md`'s typed-query bullet (the "MUST surface the validated payload exactly once and MUST NOT add user-visible turns to the conversation beyond the natural free-phase turns" sentence). Their duplication inside a paragraph headed "reference implementation" invites either silent drift (two pages saying nearly-but-not-quite the same thing) or, worse, a reading that the implementation-notes paraphrase is the binding text and the canonical pages are summaries — exactly inverting the layering the file's own header advertises.

Obligation (1) is the harder problem: it has no normative anchor on any canonical page. `conversation-drive.md` ends its typed-query bullet by deferring the mechanism — "including the `before_provider_request` payload-rewrite hook loom 1.0 rejects — is described in [Implementation Notes — Runtime]" — so the prohibition on a payload-rewrite hook lives only inside the "reference implementation" framing. An implementer who reads that framing as "one acceptable recipe" can satisfy every named canonical-page obligation while still installing a global `before_provider_request` rewrite hook, defeating the cross-session-fingerprinting argument the spec is trying to make.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-by-commit
**Introducing commits:** 82be125 ("spec: adopt P1 typed-query two-phase loop (Option A)", 2026-05-04)
**History:** The "reference implementation:" parent line and its sub-bullets — including the `before_provider_request` rejection, the cap-counting clause, and the user-visibility clause — were authored together in commit 82be125 when typed queries were re-shaped as the two-phase tool loop. Earlier text (prior to 82be125) housed the typed-query mechanism in a single non-bulleted paragraph that conflated normative behaviour with the synthesised-tool recipe; the rewrite split the recipe out under the "reference implementation:" heading but did not lift the surviving normative obligations to their canonical pages. Subsequent edits — 1ea0455 (2026-06-04, "DI-seam vs reference-implementation placement boundary") and 7f75947 (2026-06-05, "Typed-query forced-respond tool choice delivery channel") — refactored adjacent material and rewrote the user-visibility sub-bullet to route the forced respond turn off-session, but left the placement defect in place: the rewrites added the `complete()`-driven off-session phrasing and the cross-reference scaffolding without moving the binding obligations off the "reference implementation" paragraph. The defect is therefore a residue of the original Option-A adoption rather than the product of a later regression.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Restrict the "The loom 1.0 reference implementation:" sub-bullets to genuinely implementation-specific recipe (the synthesised tool's `__loom_respond_<slug>` name, the `Type.Unsafe<unknown>` wrap, the `"Loom typed-query response"` label literal, the registration-cache and snapshot/restore mechanics, the choice to route the forced respond turn through `complete()`), and migrate every behavioural obligation off them as follows:

- Promote the "no `before_provider_request` payload-rewrite hook" prohibition into `conversation-drive.md`'s typed-query bullet as a numbered normative clause (e.g. a new `PIC-<N>` adjacent to PIC-50/PIC-51), with the cross-session-fingerprinting rationale moved with it. The `conversation-drive.md` sentence currently reading "including the `before_provider_request` payload-rewrite hook loom 1.0 rejects — is described in [Implementation Notes — Runtime]" then becomes the body of the new clause rather than a forward pointer.
- Delete the user-visibility sub-bullet's normative-feeling paraphrase and replace it with a one-sentence cross-reference to `conversation-drive.md`'s "MUST surface the validated payload exactly once and MUST NOT add user-visible turns" obligation. The implementation-specific fact worth keeping — that the forced respond turn runs off-session through `complete()` and therefore attaches no transcript turn — is also normative and already lives in `conversation-drive.md`; the implementation-notes paragraph should not restate it.
- Delete the `tool_loop.max_rounds` enforcement sub-bullet's paraphrase and cross-reference QRY-16 instead. The "free-phase rounds only", "forced respond turn is the exempt-routed terminator", and "`tool_loop_exhausted` unreachable on the typed-query path" claims all live on QRY-16 verbatim; the duplication adds drift surface and no information.
- Resolve the "Option B" decision-log fragment (see the related finding) as part of the same edit: once the prohibition has been lifted to its canonical home, no decision-log shrapnel remains to delete.

The result is an implementation-notes paragraph that names the loom 1.0 implementation choices (one-shot tool name, registration-cache wiring, `complete()` channel selection) and cross-references the binding behaviour to its canonical pages, eliminating the duplicated normative claims and giving the `before_provider_request` prohibition an unambiguous normative anchor. Implementer-relevant edge case: the new `conversation-drive.md` clause must be worded so it binds regardless of the dispatch channel (it is a property of the runtime's interaction with Pi's event surface, not of the typed-query mechanism specifically) — otherwise a future implementer routing some non-typed call through a hooked path could claim the prohibition does not apply.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

A second implementer reading the spec in good faith may install a global `before_provider_request` payload-rewrite hook — satisfying every named canonical-page obligation — because the prohibition is housed inside a paragraph headed "The loom 1.0 reference implementation:" and is plausibly read as one acceptable recipe rather than as a binding constraint. The resulting implementation would silently fingerprint every provider call in every session into which the loom extension is loaded, which is the precise failure mode the spec text intends to forbid. The duplicated user-visibility and cap-counting clauses (sub-bullets 3 and 2) compound the drift surface every time either side is edited.

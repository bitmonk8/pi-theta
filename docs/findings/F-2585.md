## Finding

The automated build-time *inventory-closure audit* is deferred to post-loom-1.0: `inventory-audit-intro.md` pins it as a `MAY` ("PIC does NOT require this automated audit as a loom-1.0 merge gate"), the six audit-cluster pages carry a "post-1.0 hardening" banner, and `overview-and-orientation.md` (Orientation — Prerequisites) states inline that "the PIC — inventory-closure audit is a post-1.0 mechanization of that obligation, not a loom-1.0 merge gate." The deferral is therefore stated, but only at the audit's own normative sites and in one orientation parenthetical.

Future Considerations is the spec's self-described canonical inventory of "Features deliberately deferred from loom 1.0." Its category 1, **Tooling deferrals (no loom 1.0 impact)** (`future-considerations/surface-extensions.md`), already lists post-1.0 build-time/tooling work of exactly this kind — LSP support, a `loom test` command, richer runtime-event telemetry, and an automated cadence for re-importing Pi patches. The inventory-closure audit — a post-1.0 build-time mechanization that ships as tooling and requires no loom 1.0 runtime decision — fits that bucket precisely, yet it has no entry there (nor anywhere else in Future Considerations). A reader enumerating loom 1.0's deferred work from the canonical inventory would not discover it.

Note on classification: this is **not** a loom 1.0 non-goal. The non-goals list (`future-considerations/model-changes-and-non-goals.md`, aggregated as "V1 non-goals" in `language-and-architecture.md`) is reserved for cross-cutting dispositions loom 1.0 ships *without* a forward-compatibility seam and that "will require a migration." The inventory-closure audit is the opposite: it is an anticipated post-1.0 addition with a defined forward path (a `MAY` the implementation may publish, with a same-commit land-green sweep already specified). Its home is the Tooling-deferrals bucket, not the non-goals list.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add a bullet to the **Tooling deferrals (no loom 1.0 impact)** list in `docs/spec_topics/future-considerations/surface-extensions.md`, alongside the existing LSP / `loom test` / telemetry / patch-cadence entries:

- Name it the automated build-time **inventory-closure audit**, describe it as a post-1.0 build-time tooling mechanization of the *surface-set closure* obligation, and state that loom 1.0 enforces that obligation by other means (the positive-direction step 2(a) literal-read assertions plus the editorial-review checklist), not by the automated audit.
- Cross-link the normative owner: `[Pi Integration Contract — Inventory-closure audit](../pi-integration-contract/inventory-audit-intro.md#sdk-cap-inventory-closure-audit)`.

Do not add it to the loom 1.0 non-goals list: a non-goal is a no-seam disposition that requires a migration, whereas the audit is an anticipated post-1.0 addition with a defined forward path, so the tooling-deferrals bucket is the correct home. Optionally, retarget the inline parenthetical in `overview-and-orientation.md` to forward-link the new tooling-deferral entry, keeping the orientation honest against the inventory per GOV-30 lock-step.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader relying on Future Considerations as the complete inventory of deferred loom 1.0 work gets an incomplete list: the inventory-closure audit is invisible from the canonical deferral catalogue and discoverable only by reading the audit cluster itself. No implementer is driven to wrong behaviour — every audit site marks the post-1.0 status clearly — but the deferral inventory's completeness claim is silently violated, and the seam-vs-tooling-vs-non-goal taxonomy the page advertises has a gap.

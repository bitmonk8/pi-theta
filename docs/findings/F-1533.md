## Finding

The step-2 editorial-review checklist preamble in `version-bump-step2.md` states its SHOULD-level audit range three times, and one of the three is stale. The two outer-sentence citations read "SHOULD additionally audit it against items (f) through (ap)" and "for items (f) through (ap), the contributor MUST record one of `pass` / `fail` / `N/A` … or `not-performed` … — omitting the row is non-conformant", but the trailing parenthetical reads "the (f)–(ao) audits stay SHOULD-level, but the per-item recording is mandatory either way".

Item (ap) (`pi.registerTool` registration persistence and reachability) exists in the checklist, is explicitly SHOULD-level, and carries the same per-item recording obligation as every other (f)-series item. The `(f)–(ao)` parenthetical therefore excludes (ap) from the very sentence that declares the recording obligation mandatory, so a reviewer reading the preamble alone cannot tell whether omitting the (ap) recording row is conformant: the outer recording sentence says it is non-conformant to omit it, the parenthetical's range stops one item short of it. The endpoints must agree on the last in-range item.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit-introduction
**Introducing commit:** `bef37f3` (2026-06-25) — "pi-loom spec-plan: fix F-1228 — pin registry-persistence presupposition, narrow PIC-8(b) /tools hint"
**History:** `git log -S` over the cited file shows the parenthetical "(f)–(ao) audits stay SHOULD-level" text entering in `db69f2b` (F-0981, which added item (ao)); at that commit all three range citations in the preamble were consistent at `(ao)`. Commit `bef37f3` then added item (ap) and updated two of the three citations — the "SHOULD additionally audit … (f) through (ap)" sentence and the "for items (f) through (ap) … MUST record" sentence — from `(ao)` to `(ap)`, but left the trailing parenthetical at `(f)–(ao)`. `git show bef37f3` confirms the diff bumps the outer-sentence endpoints while the parenthetical's `(f)–(ao)` is carried through unchanged. The discrepancy is a single missed citation in that one commit's range-widening edit.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In the step-2 preamble's trailing parenthetical, change "the (f)–(ao) audits stay SHOULD-level" to "the (f)–(ap) audits stay SHOULD-level", so all three range citations terminate at the same last in-range item (ap). No clause distinguishing (ap) is needed: item (ap) is SHOULD-level with the standard per-item recording obligation, identical to the rest of the (f)-series.

To keep this endpoint from going stale again as items are appended, the durable form is to state the range against the last enumerated item rather than a hardcoded letter — but a future contributor adding item (aq) must update all three citations regardless, so the minimal correct fix is the `(ao)` → `(ap)` change.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two editorial reviewers applying the preamble diverge on whether omitting the item (ap) recording row is conformant: the outer sentence's `(f)–(ap)` mandate includes it, the parenthetical's `(f)–(ao)` range excludes it. A reviewer who trusts the parenthetical could accept a bump commit that never records an item-(ap) outcome, defeating the "omitting the row is non-conformant" rule for that item.

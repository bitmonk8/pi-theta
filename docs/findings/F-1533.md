## Problem

The step-2 editorial-review checklist preamble in `version-bump-step2.md` states its SHOULD-level audit range three times, and the three disagree on the last in-range item. The two outer-sentence citations read "(f) through (ap)", but the trailing parenthetical reads "the (f)–(ao) audits stay SHOULD-level". Item (ap) (`pi.registerTool` registration persistence and reachability, anchor `bump-checklist-register-tool-persistence`) exists in the checklist, is SHOULD-level, and carries the same per-item recording obligation as every other (f)-series item. A reviewer reading the preamble alone cannot tell whether omitting the (ap) recording row is conformant: the outer recording sentence declares it non-conformant to omit, while the parenthetical's range stops one item short.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit-introduction
**Introducing commit:** `bef37f3` (2026-06-25) — "pi-loom spec-plan: fix F-1228 — pin registry-persistence presupposition, narrow PIC-8(b) /tools hint"
**History:** `git log -S` over the cited file shows the parenthetical "(f)–(ao) audits stay SHOULD-level" text entering in `db69f2b` (F-0981, which added item (ao)); at that commit all three range citations in the preamble were consistent at `(ao)`. Commit `bef37f3` then added item (ap) and updated two of the three citations — the "SHOULD additionally audit … (f) through (ap)" sentence and the "for items (f) through (ap) … MUST record" sentence — from `(ao)` to `(ap)`, but left the trailing parenthetical at `(f)–(ao)`. `git show bef37f3` confirms the diff bumps the outer-sentence endpoints while the parenthetical's `(f)–(ao)` is carried through unchanged. The discrepancy is a single missed citation in that one commit's range-widening edit.

## Solution approach

In the step-2 preamble's trailing parenthetical, change `(f)–(ao)` to `(f)–(ap)` so all three range citations terminate at the same last in-range item (ap).

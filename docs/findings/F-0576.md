## Finding

`V17a` (Cancellation core) closes its **Tests.** field with an italic parenthetical that delegates two surfaces to its sibling leaves: *"The checkpoint-granularity surface … is owned by [`V17c`]. The forwarding-listener throw-trap … is owned by [`V17b`]."* This is a scope/ownership statement — it declares what `V17a` does **not** introduce and which leaves own the excluded mechanisms — not a per-REQ-ID test assertion. The leaf-template (`leaf-template.md`) reserves **Tests.** for "one bullet per REQ-ID this leaf claims to close," while **Adds.** is the field describing what the leaf introduces (and, by extension, the boundary of its scope).

The same delegation note also appears, in a shorter form, in the paired tests leaf `V17a-T` (*"The checkpoint-granularity surface is owned by `V17c`; the forwarding-listener throw-trap is owned by `V17b`."*), again at the foot of its **Tests.** field. Both instances are scope-delineation prose sitting in the assertion-listing field.

The note's content is correct and useful — it explains why checkpoint-granularity and throw-trap assertions are absent from `V17a`'s Tests list after those surfaces were peeled into `V17c`/`V17b`. The defect is purely placement: a "what siblings own" boundary statement is field-appropriate to **Adds.** (the scope-description field), not to **Tests.**.

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction

**History:** The misplacement pattern entered when `cb8737a` peeled the forwarding-listener throw-trap into `V17b` and appended a delegation parenthetical to `V17a`'s **Tests.** field rather than its **Adds.** field. `5b95143` then peeled the checkpoint-granularity surface into `V17c` and extended that same Tests-field parenthetical with the second delegation clause (the `V17a-T` mirror tracks the same edits). The current two-clause scope note in the Tests field is the cumulative product of both leaf-split commits.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Relocate the sibling-ownership delegation note out of the **Tests.** field and into the **Adds.** field on both leaves of the pair; the note's content is unchanged, only its host field changes.

- In `docs/plan_topics/V17a-cancellation-core.md`: strike the trailing Tests-field parenthetical *"(The checkpoint-granularity surface — the fixed five-site checkpoint set with its best-effort negative arm and the loop-iteration macrotask-yield property — is owned by [`V17c`](./V17c-checkpoint-granularity.md). The forwarding-listener throw-trap — a distinctly complex, independently-verifiable sub-mechanism — is owned by [`V17b`](./V17b-forwarding-listener-throw-trap.md).)"* and place that same text in the **Adds.** field, where the leaf's scope is described.
- In `docs/plan_topics/V17a-T-cancellation-core.md`: strike the trailing Tests-field parenthetical *"(The checkpoint-granularity surface is owned by `V17c`; the forwarding-listener throw-trap is owned by `V17b`.)"* and place it in that leaf's **Adds.** field.

The per-REQ-ID and per-facet bullets in each **Tests.** field stay as they are; only the ownership/scope note moves.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader scanning `V17a`'s **Tests.** field for the list of asserted REQ-IDs meets a non-assertion ownership note mixed into the assertion bullets. No implementer is misled (the note is accurate wherever it sits, and the same reader sees both fields), so nothing ships wrong; the cost is field-purpose drift that erodes the Tests/Adds convention the template establishes.

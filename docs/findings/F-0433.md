## Finding

`docs/plan.md` §How to use this plan, step 2 instructs the leaf author: "If the new leaf can introduce an executable REQ-ID, a citing test that *closes* a coverage-matrix-mapped numbered REQ-ID, or an un-anchored normative MUST, add it to `H5b`'s **Deps** per `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline (*Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance*), which states that obligation once and governs its exact triggers." The same sentence both enumerates the three trigger conditions inline **and** explicitly cedes authority over them to conventions.md — "which states that obligation once and governs its exact triggers."

The authoritative list lives in `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline (*Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance*): a new leaf must be added to `H5b`'s `Deps.` whenever its `Tests.` bullets (1) carry an executable REQ-ID, (2) carry the citing test that *closes* a coverage-matrix-mapped numbered REQ-ID, or (3) carry an un-anchored normative MUST. Keeping a second copy of that list in the plan index creates a divergence surface with no mechanical guard: if the authoritative triggers are ever amended, nothing forces step 2 to track them.

The divergence is not hypothetical. The inline copy already uses the capability phrasing "**can** introduce an executable REQ-ID", whereas the authoritative text turns on the actuality test "whose `Tests.` bullets **carry**". The two readings are already out of step, which is exactly the drift this duplication invites.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commit:** `ab8e297` — "pi-loom plan: resolve \"Transitive-completeness obligation invisible at leaf authoring\"" (2026-06-11)
**History:** `git log -S` over `docs/plan.md` for both the inline trigger enumeration ("can introduce an executable REQ-ID") and the deferral clause ("which states that obligation once and governs its exact triggers") resolves to the single commit `ab8e297`. The diff (`git show ab8e297 -- docs/plan.md`) shows step 2 changed from a bare "Link the new leaf into the appropriate section below." to the current sentence carrying both the inline three-trigger list and the conventions.md pointer. The inline restatement was deliberately added there to surface the transitive-completeness obligation at leaf-authoring time; it was not present at inception and was not introduced by a multi-commit interaction.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan.md` §How to use this plan, step 2, replace the inline enumeration of the three trigger conditions with a pointer that preserves the leaf-authoring reminder (the goal of the introducing commit `ab8e297`) while leaving the trigger list owned solely by conventions.md. Strike the clause "can introduce an executable REQ-ID, a citing test that *closes* a coverage-matrix-mapped numbered REQ-ID, or an un-anchored normative MUST" and keep a deferring reminder, e.g.:

> If the new leaf triggers the transitive-completeness obligation, add it to [`H5b`](./plan_topics/H5b-warn-only-canary.md)'s **Deps** per [`conventions.md`](./plan_topics/conventions.md) §REQ-ID discipline (*Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance*), which states that obligation once and governs its exact triggers.

The exact trigger conditions then exist in exactly one place (`conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline). Keep the `H5b` link and the conventions.md pointer; the reminder must still flag that the obligation exists so the visibility win from `ab8e297` is not lost. `conventions.md` is read-only for this fix — do not edit the authoritative trigger list.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A leaf author who reads only step 2 may apply the wrong trigger test for adding a leaf to `H5b`'s `Deps.` — the inline copy already says "can introduce" where the authoritative section requires the `Tests.` bullets to actually carry the obligation. As the corpus evolves the two copies drift further with no gate to catch it, so the index can give guidance that contradicts the section it points to.

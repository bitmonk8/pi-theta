## Finding

`H7a`'s final Tests bullet drives one ceiling co-occurrence through the live pipeline — a binder-retry-class breach (ceiling #3, `V11f`) co-occurring with the `tool_loop.max_rounds` round boundary (runtime-class ceiling #2, `V13c`) — and asserts the live sites' consultation of the `V16a` arbitration seam surfaces a single ceiling in `CIO-5` order with `masked` enumerating the suppressed sibling. The bullet then characterises this as "closing the live-site integration gap that `V16a`'s synthesised-candidate suite leaves open".

`CIO-5` (`hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md#cio-5`) is the property that ceiling #3 *never interleaves with #1, #2, or #4*. The single co-occurrence the run exercises witnesses only the #3/#2 pairing at the live sites that fixture traverses; the #3-vs-#1 and #3-vs-#4 relations are not driven end-to-end anywhere. The unqualified phrase "closing the live-site integration gap" therefore reads as end-to-end closure of the whole cross-site `CIO-5` property, while the run delivers one representative pairing. `V16a`'s Adds compounds the over-credit by delegating `CIO-5`'s entire cross-site witness to "`H7a`'s co-occurring-breach integration assertion".

The remaining `CIO-5` pairings are not actually under-covered — by the binder-bypass design (binder runs only at slash-load; `invoke(...)` does not invoke the binder) ceiling #3 cannot co-occur at runtime with #1/#4, and the seam-local arbitration plus per-leaf gates carry the rest. The defect is purely the wording over-crediting the one exercised pairing as full-gap closure, which can lead a reviewer to over-credit the integration run's coverage.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/H7a-integration-acceptance.md`, scope the final Tests bullet's closing clause to the co-occurrence the run actually exercises. Replace the trailing phrase "closing the live-site integration gap that `V16a`'s synthesised-candidate suite leaves open" with wording that names the #3/#2 live-site co-occurrence specifically and notes the other `CIO-5` pairings do not need an end-to-end witness — e.g. "closing the `CIO-5` #3/#2 live-site co-occurrence gap that `V16a`'s synthesised-candidate suite leaves open; ceiling #3 cannot co-occur with #1 or #4 at runtime per the binder-bypass design (binder runs only at slash-load), so those pairings rest on `V16a`'s seam-local arbitration and the per-leaf gates rather than this run."

The Ships-when sentence already names the "ceiling #3 / runtime-class ceiling #2" pairing explicitly and is accurate as written; it needs no change, but if the two are reconciled keep the Ships-when scoped to the #3/#2 breach. Leave `V16a` for its own finding — do not retitle or re-scope `V16a`'s CIO-5 delegation sentence as part of this fix.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reviewer reading the Tests bullet would credit `H7a` with closing the entire live-site cross-ceiling arbitration gap, when only the #3/#2 co-occurrence is driven end-to-end. No implementer produces a wrong leaf — the per-leaf gates and the binder-bypass design cover the other pairings — but the overclaim masks which `CIO-5` relations the integration run actually witnesses.

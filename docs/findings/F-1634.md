## Finding

The *Malformed-marker discriminator* paragraph — the definitional rule that fixes when an exemption attempt is syntactically invalid via the seven clauses (a)–(g) (missing colon, missing citation, separator-tolerance violation, under-length / punctuation-only justification, off-line placement, non-lowercase `allow-pi-surface` spelling, block-comment shape) — lives in `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-failures.md` (the second paragraph on the page). Every other clause of the exemption-marker grammar lives in `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-resolution.md`: the *Exemption mechanism* itself, *Separator tolerance*, *Minimum justification*, *Citation-token grammar*, and *Stale-marker discriminator* are five consecutive paragraphs there. The malformed-marker rule is the last grammatical clause of that family — it specifies, per construct, exactly which features of the *Exemption mechanism* / *Separator tolerance* / *Minimum justification* / *Citation-token grammar* / *Multi-line surface placement* obligations make a marker malformed when violated — but it is the only one that does not sit beside them.

The separation produces a visible run of dangling intra-page directionals that resolve cross-shard. In `audit-resolution.md`, *Stale-marker discriminator* opens with "well-formed (passes every clause of the *Malformed-marker discriminator* below)" and *Multi-line surface placement* with "the 'offending surface line' the *Malformed-marker discriminator* below clause (e) names" — neither "below" target is on the same page. The *Citation-token grammar* paragraph similarly cites "the *Malformed-marker discriminator* clause (b) below". `audit-failures.md`'s own *Failure-surface contract* then back-references "malformed-marker clauses (a)–(g) per *Malformed-marker discriminator* above" to a paragraph that is in fact above it but only because the page-author placed it there; nothing about the rule's content anchors it to the failures shard.

Routing-table content (which failure family the marker fires under) belongs on the failures shard and is already there independently; the seven clauses themselves are exemption-grammar content and belong on the resolution shard with the other clauses they extend.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `e5cd514` (2026-05-09, *pi-loom spec: resolve "T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit"*) and `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04, *docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set*)
**History:** Commit `e5cd514` introduced the *Malformed-marker discriminator* paragraph in the then-single `docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` file, placed adjacent to the *Exemption mechanism* / *Citation-token grammar* / *Stale-marker discriminator* paragraphs (verified at line 854 of that file pre-split, immediately following the *Stale-citation limitation* sub-bullet at line 852). At that point the paragraph's "above" / "below" directionals to its sibling clauses resolved on-page and the placement was internally consistent. Commit `f5e89f4` then split the 576KB single file into the `pi-integration-contract/<child>.md` subdirectory layout; the splitter placed the *Malformed-marker discriminator* paragraph in `audit-failures.md` (chosen because it interacts with the *Failure-surface contract* sentence immediately following it) while routing the *Exemption mechanism* / *Citation-token grammar* / *Stale-marker discriminator* block to `audit-resolution.md`, severing the on-page adjacency. The cross-shard "below" directionals were carried over textually unchanged. Neither commit individually authored a misplaced paragraph; the defect is the interaction — the original on-page co-location was load-bearing, and the split chose its shard boundary one paragraph too high.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Move the *Malformed-marker discriminator* paragraph from `audit-failures.md` into `audit-resolution.md`, inserting it as a new sibling between *Citation-token grammar* and *Stale-marker discriminator*. This restores the on-page adjacency the rule's referents already assume and groups all six exemption-grammar clauses on one shard.

Mechanical edits:

1. In `audit-resolution.md`, insert the *Malformed-marker discriminator* paragraph verbatim between *Citation-token grammar* (current line 13) and *Stale-marker discriminator* (current line 15). Its "above" referents to *Separator tolerance*, *Minimum justification*, *Citation-token grammar*, *Marker scope is per source line* now resolve on-page, as the original e5cd514 author intended.
2. In `audit-failures.md`, replace the moved paragraph with a one-sentence routing pointer that retains only the failure-surface obligation, e.g.: *"Malformed-marker routing. A malformed exemption marker — as defined by the *Malformed-marker discriminator* in [`audit-resolution.md`](./audit-resolution.md#audit-exemption-mechanism) — MUST surface under family (5)'s discriminator, AND MUST additionally surface the underlying out-of-inventory reference (if any) under its own family (1) / (2) / (3) discriminator on the same audit run; routing the malformed marker only to the underlying-surface discriminator would silently demote the contributor's intended exemption and is prohibited."* This is the load-bearing failure-shard content (the dual-emission obligation and the family-(5) routing); the seven definitional clauses move with the grammar.
3. In the moved paragraph, update the now-cross-shard "*Marker scope is per source line* above" referent inside clause (e) — both *Marker scope* and the moved paragraph now live in `audit-resolution.md`, so this becomes an on-page "above" reference and reads cleanly without an anchor.
4. In `audit-failures.md`, the *Failure-surface contract* parenthetical "malformed-marker clauses (a)–(g) per *Malformed-marker discriminator* above" updates to "per *Malformed-marker discriminator* in [`audit-resolution.md`](./audit-resolution.md#audit-exemption-mechanism)". The *Stale sub-kinds* paragraph at the top of `audit-failures.md` carries no reference to the moved paragraph and is unaffected.
5. In `audit-resolution.md`, the three "below" referents to *Malformed-marker discriminator* (in *Citation-token grammar*, *Stale-marker discriminator*, *Multi-line surface placement*) update to "above" where they now precede the paragraph (the *Multi-line surface placement* paragraph follows the moved paragraph, so its "below" stays "below" if the *Multi-line surface placement* sub-bullet keeps its current relative position; if not, flip accordingly).
6. In `version-bump-step2b.md` branch (4), the link "(any clause (a)–(g) of the *Malformed-marker discriminator* above — currently: unrecognised separator, missing colon, …)" updates to reference `audit-resolution.md` instead of "above" (the version-bump page already cross-references audit-resolution for the *Exemption mechanism* grammar, so this aligns the malformed-marker link with the sibling citation-grammar link there).

Edge case the implementer must watch: the *Malformed-marker discriminator* paragraph closes with a sentence binding to the *Failure-surface contract* ("The audit MUST surface every malformed marker under family (5)'s discriminator, AND MUST additionally surface the underlying out-of-inventory reference (if any) under its own family (1) / (2) / (3) discriminator on the same audit run …"). That sentence is failure-shard content even though it textually trails the grammatical clauses. The move MUST split it off so the seven clauses (definitional, grammar-shard) and the dual-emission obligation (routing, failure-shard) end up on the shards their content belongs to; placing the whole paragraph wholesale on either shard re-creates the cross-shard defect in mirror form.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader who lands on `audit-resolution.md` to understand the exemption-marker grammar finds five of the six clauses in place and the sixth elsewhere; the four intra-page "below" / "above" directionals to *Malformed-marker discriminator* mis-locate by one shard. Implementation is unaffected — both files are obligatory reading for the audit-leaf implementer, and the cross-shard rules already resolve unambiguously by anchor — so no two implementers diverge over this; the cost is reader-orientation friction in the densest paragraph cluster of the PIC audit subsystem.

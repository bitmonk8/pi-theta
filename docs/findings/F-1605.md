## Finding

GOV-22 (in `docs/spec_topics/governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md`, line 57) makes its coinage MUST conditional on the diff adding or modifying a *defining obligation site*, then defines that term as a source line that carries an RFC-2119 normative-modal token, "excluding tokens inside fenced code blocks, inline code spans, and HTML comments (the GOV-3 exclusion list) and excluding back-references to obligations another page owns."

The first two exclusions are operational — they name byte-recognisable constructs and route through GOV-3. The "back-references to obligations another page owns" exclusion is not: the phrase "back-reference" is used elsewhere on the corpus (GOV-1 *Canonical form* contrasts the anchor site `**BNDR-8.**` with the back-reference `per BNDR-8`; GOV-5 talks about REQ-ID anchors and back-references matching `\b<PREFIX>-[1-9][0-9]*\b`) but is never given a definition that a GOV-22 committer can apply. Two reasonable readings collide on common patterns:

- A sentence like "per BNDR-1, implementations MUST surface a `loom/load/...` diagnostic" — does the `MUST` token here count as a defining obligation site (because the sentence adds a new enforcement site or diagnostic for BNDR-1's rule), or as a back-reference (because the sentence cites BNDR-1 by anchor name)?
- A markdown link of the form `[per PIC-12](.../host-interfaces-services.md#pic-12)` whose surrounding sentence carries `MUST NOT` — citation form, but on whose page is the obligation "owned"?
- A summary paragraph that restates another page's MUST verbatim, with no `per …` citation at all — same MUST byte sequence, but no link.

Two committers will reach opposite conclusions on whether GOV-22 fires. Because GOV-22 has no CI gate (it is reviewer-enforced per its own closing sentence), the disagreement surfaces only as a "should we have coined a new REQ-ID here?" PR comment, with no shared test to settle it. The rule's progressive-drain mechanism relies on the same trigger predicate being applied identically at every commit; an under-specified exclusion fragments that predicate.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add an operational definition of *back-reference to an obligation another page owns* immediately after the *defining obligation site* definition in GOV-22, so the exclusion has the same byte-recognisability as the GOV-3 exclusion list it sits beside. A workable form:

> A *back-reference to an obligation another page owns* is a source line whose normative-modal token sits inside a sentence that (1) cites a REQ-ID or inline label whose defining anchor (per GOV-1 *Canonical form* / GOV-16 *Canonical form*) sits on a different `spec_topics/**` page — either by a `per PREFIX-N` / `per PREFIX-<tail>` citation token or by a markdown link whose fragment is the anchor's `#prefix-n` / `#prefix-<tail>` slug — and (2) introduces no new firing condition, no new enforcement site, and no new diagnostic code beyond what the cited rule already pins. A sentence that satisfies (1) but adds an additional MUST/MUST-NOT clause on top of the cited rule is **not** a back-reference and is a defining obligation site on the citing page; its coinage on the citing page is governed by this rule on the same terms as any other defining obligation site.

Implementer-relevant edge cases:

- *Same-page citations* (a `per PREFIX-N` whose anchor sits on the same page as the citing sentence) are out of scope of the exclusion — they are not "another page owns" — and so a same-page citation that carries an additive normative-modal sentence is a defining obligation site on its own page in the usual way; this matches the prevailing convention that a page's own rules cite each other inline by REQ-ID without re-coinage.
- *Glossary entries and the corpus hub `spec.md`* — both are pure-narrative per GOV-3 and GOV-30 respectively, so a back-reference appearing in them is unreachable by GOV-22's trigger (the rule binds only non-narrative pages) regardless of how the exclusion reads; the definition above does not need a glossary/hub carve-out.
- *Restatements with no `per …` citation token and no markdown link* (a paragraph that paraphrases another page's MUST verbatim, with no anchor cite) fall outside clause (1) and so are **not** back-references under the proposed definition — they are defining obligation sites on the restating page. This is the intended outcome: an unannotated restatement is indistinguishable from a freshly-coined rule from the reader's point of view and so should carry its own REQ-ID (or be rewritten as a `per …` citation).
- *Inline labels* (GOV-16 family — `HC3-a`, `NOCEIL-3`, etc.) participate in the definition on identical terms via the GOV-1 dual-form-layout-applies clause GOV-16 *Canonical form* establishes; the proposed wording names them explicitly.
- The cross-references in the definition (`per GOV-1 *Canonical form*`, `per GOV-16 *Canonical form*`) themselves match the definition's clause (1) but add no normative force to GOV-22, so they are well-formed back-references and do not trigger the rule on their own paragraph — the definition is self-consistent.

The edit is confined to the GOV-22 paragraph in `req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` and lands as a GOV-8 *Split* (the rule's normative content is changing: the trigger predicate is narrowing for citation-form sentences and widening for unannotated restatements). Per GOV-8 the in-place edit form is prohibited and the prior GOV-22 retires under the existing `## Retired REQ-IDs` machinery on that page, with a successor `GOV-N` (next free integer) carrying the revised text.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two committers reviewing the same PR can reach opposite conclusions on whether GOV-22's MUST fires for a citation sentence carrying a normative-modal token. With no CI gate to settle the disagreement, the progressive-drain mechanism produces a corpus where some pages coin new REQ-IDs for restatements of upstream rules and others treat the same construct as a back-reference and skip coinage — exactly the divergence GOV-1's lock-step convention is designed to prevent. The defect set GOV-22 is meant to drain monotonically can grow under one reading and shrink under the other.

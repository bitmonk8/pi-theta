## Problem

GOV-22's *defining obligation site* definition (`#gov-22` in `docs/spec_topics/governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md`) excludes "back-references to obligations another page owns", but never defines that phrase, whereas its other two exclusions are byte-recognisable and route through the GOV-3 exclusion list. Common citation patterns admit two opposing readings: a sentence carrying a `per PREFIX-N` cite plus its own normative-modal token, a markdown link to another page's `#prefix-n` anchor in a sentence carrying `MUST NOT`, and an unannotated restatement of another page's MUST. Because GOV-22 is reviewer-enforced with no CI gate, two committers reach opposite conclusions on whether the coinage MUST fires, fragmenting the trigger predicate the rule's progressive-drain mechanism depends on.

The defect is therefore co-extensive with GOV-22's lifetime in the corpus; no later commit introduced or aggravated it.

## Solution approach

Clarify the *back-references to obligations another page owns* exclusion in GOV-22's *defining obligation site* clause (`#gov-22`) so it is byte-recognisable on the same terms as the GOV-3 exclusion list it sits beside. Give an operational test distinguishing a citation sentence — one that names another page's REQ-ID or inline label (per GOV-1 / GOV-16 *Canonical form*) via a `per PREFIX-N` token or a markdown link to its `#prefix-n` anchor and adds no new firing condition, enforcement site, or diagnostic — from a sentence that cites such a rule but adds an additive normative-modal clause, which remains a defining obligation site on the citing page. Resolve the same-page-citation and unannotated-restatement cases the current phrasing leaves ambiguous.

## Solution constraints

- The change narrows and widens GOV-22's normative trigger predicate, so it is substantive: land it as a GOV-8 *Split* (retire GOV-22 under the page's `## Retired REQ-IDs` machinery and coin a successor), not an in-place edit.

## Finding

`H5b`'s `Deps.` field is followed by a large italicised parenthetical block (`_(The dependency set is the complete coverage-producing set …)_`). The block restates, in prose, the standing transitive-completeness maintenance obligation — that every coverage-producing leaf must be kept in `H5b`'s `Deps.` so the warn-only canary and the `H6a` hard-fail gate stay sequenced after all such leaves, and that `H6a` inherits the set by depending on `H5b` rather than restating it.

That obligation is authoritative in `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline under the **Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance** paragraph, which states it once and owns its exact triggers. The H5b parenthetical itself even ends by referring back to that section ("the standing plan-maintenance obligation stated in *REQ-ID discipline*"), so the block is a second copy of an obligation that lives elsewhere. A duplicated obligation is a divergence surface: if the trigger set or the inheritance mechanism is revised in `conventions.md`, the H5b prose silently drifts out of agreement with no mechanical guard.

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `ea6b1da` ("pi-loom plan: resolve \"Live-corpus gate activation has no documented rollback\"", 2026-06-11) authored the parenthetical at file creation; `37733fd` ("pi-loom plan: resolve \"H6a transitive-completeness rule parked in Deps, not conventions\"", 2026-06-11) relocated the obligation's authoritative home into `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline.
**History:** `git log --follow --diff-filter=A` shows `H5b-warn-only-canary.md` was created at `ea6b1da`, where the parenthetical block already existed and was described as "the single statement of the transitive-completeness obligation" — at that point it was the authoritative home, not cruft. `git log -S "standing plan-maintenance obligation"` pins the obligation's move to `conventions.md` at `37733fd`. After that relocation the H5b block became a residual restatement of an obligation owned elsewhere, which is the defect as characterised here. The block has therefore been present since the file's inception, but became redundant cruft specifically at `37733fd`.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/H5b-warn-only-canary.md`, strike the italicised parenthetical block that follows the `Deps.` list and replace it with a one-line forward reference to `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline (*Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance*) as the authoritative statement of the set-completeness and `H6a`-inheritance obligation. Leave the `Deps.` enumeration itself intact. The replacement pointer should carry only what the canary's reader needs in place: that `H5b`'s `Deps.` is the complete coverage-producing set and that keeping it complete is the obligation owned by that conventions.md paragraph. Do not edit `conventions.md` — it is read-only for this fix.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

If `conventions.md`'s transitive-completeness obligation is revised, the H5b prose copy drifts out of agreement with no mechanical guard, leaving two divergent statements of the same plan-maintenance rule. No implementer is blocked and no gate misfires; the cost is a maintenance/divergence hazard on a load-bearing plan rule.

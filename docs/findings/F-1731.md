## Finding

Branch (2) ("Promote the surface to an inventory entry") on `version-bump-step2b.md` is a dense block of contributor obligations — a chain of "the contributor MUST update…" co-edit steps covering the integer literal `7`, the `CAPABILITY_OBLIGATIONS` constant, and the markdown enumeration. The final sentence of the GOV-31 carve-out closes with a reviewer-directed obligation: "its prose restatements are maintained by editorial convention — **a reviewer SHOULD flag a stale cardinality phrasing on the bump-commit diff**."

This is the only soft backstop for the constant-vs-prose drift gap that GOV-31 deliberately leaves un-mechanised, yet it sits at the tail of a contributor-action paragraph with no structural separation marking it as reviewer-facing. A contributor following the branch as an action list reads through a run of MUSTs and reaches a SHOULD addressed to someone else; a reviewer who must actually discharge it has to mine it out of contributor prose, because the page maintains no reviewer checklist. The clause is grammatically attributed ("a reviewer SHOULD…"), so there is no ambiguity about *who* owns it — the defect is discoverability and audience-mixing, not contradiction.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-by-commit
**Introducing commits:** `56b1a11` — "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Branch (2) promote co-edit obligation non-exhaustive\"" (2026-06-07)
**History:** The spec corpus is under git. `git log -S` on the clause "a reviewer SHOULD flag a stale cardinality phrasing" returns `56b1a11` as the sole introducing commit. The diff for that commit shows branch (2) previously carried a hard contributor co-edit obligation requiring *every* natural-language `"seven"`-cardinality restatement to be updated in the same edit. `56b1a11` reversed that: it declared the prose restatements NOT a mechanically-verifiable co-edit obligation, cited GOV-31 as authority for the intentional constant-vs-prose drift gap, and substituted the inline reviewer SHOULD as the soft backstop. The audience-mixed placement therefore entered wholesale with that resolution — there was no reviewer SHOULD on the page before it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Lift the reviewer-directed clause out of the inline contributor prose and present it as a labelled reviewer note. Split the GOV-31 sentence so the contributor-facing statement ends at "…its prose restatements are maintained by editorial convention.", then add a separated callout — e.g. a blockquote `> **Reviewer note:** On the bump-commit diff, flag any stale cardinality phrasing among the prose restatements of the capability count; GOV-31 leaves this constant-vs-prose drift gap intentional, so it is a SHOULD, not a mechanically-gated obligation.` Keep the existing grammatical attribution ("a reviewer…") in the callout so the audience marker survives. Preserve the immediately following sentence ("GOV-31 makes this constant-vs-prose drift gap intentional; branch (2) keeps the bump-time prose update under that editorial-convention treatment…") as contributor-facing rationale, or fold its drift-gap justification into the reviewer note. Do not promote the SHOULD to a MUST and do not re-introduce the per-site mechanical sweep that `56b1a11` deliberately removed — the carve-out itself is correct; only its placement needs separating.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The reviewer SHOULD that backs the GOV-31 editorial-convention drift gap is harder to discover than its load-bearing role warrants: a reviewer who skims branch (2) as contributor procedure can miss the only check guarding cardinality-prose staleness. No implementer behaviour diverges and the obligation itself is unambiguous; the cost is organisational.

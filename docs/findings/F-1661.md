## Finding

`docs/spec_topics/query/query-failure-and-repair.md` opens — before its `# Query failure and repair` H1 has produced any failure or repair content — with `## Options surface`, whose entire body is a single `> **loom 1.0 seam — per-call timeout / per-query overrides.**` blockquote pinning the open-struct shape of the runtime-internal options record. The heading carries no failure/repair/`QueryError` content and is therefore homeless on this page: a reader navigating to "Query failure and repair" expecting respond-repair / `ContextOverflowError` / forced-respond-non-compliance material hits a deferral note about future extensions, and a reader following the surface-extensions hub's `[Query — Options surface](../query.md)` link arrives at `query.md` (a TOC stub) with no `#options-surface` anchor anywhere in the subtree.

Independently, the heading is misleading on its own terms: "Options surface" promises an enumeration of *current* options the primitive accepts, but the body documents only a *deferred* extension surface (per-call timeout, per-query `model`/`tools`/`system` overrides). The two sibling primitives anchor the analogous seam differently — `docs/spec_topics/tool-calls.md` and `docs/spec_topics/invocation.md` (INV-3) carry the `loom 1.0 seam — per-call timeout` blockquote inline next to the primitive's body with **no** "Options surface" wrapper heading. The query primitive's seam was the only one wrapped in a dedicated section, and the spec-set split that landed `query.md` into a subdirectory routed that section onto the page whose subject is the furthest from forward-compatibility seams.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `09c42c0` (2026-05-05, *pi-loom spec: resolve "Surface-extension Seam annotations carry V1 obligations from non-goals page"*); `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04, *docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set*)
**History:** `git log -S "## Options surface" -- spec_topics/query.md docs/spec_topics/query/query-failure-and-repair.md` returns exactly these two commits. `09c42c0` introduced `## Options surface` into the then-monolithic `spec_topics/query.md`, inserted between the "Untyped form" `Ok`-payload paragraph and the `## Failure modes` heading — adjacent to forward-compatibility material, with `## Failure modes` as its natural downstream neighbour. `f5e89f4` then split `query.md` into the subdirectory + index layout (this is the size-cap split that produced the four-page subtree `query-forms.md` / `query-escapes-stringification.md` / `query-tool-loop.md` / `query-failure-and-repair.md`); the `## Options surface` section was routed onto `query-failure-and-repair.md` as its first section rather than onto `query-forms.md` next to the primitive's introduction. The heading text "Options surface" — non-ideal but not misleading while it sat between two query-options-adjacent neighbours — became actively misleading once it was the opening section of a page whose H1 is "Query failure and repair"; the surface-extensions cross-references `[Query — Options surface](../query.md)` were not repointed in `f5e89f4` (they still target `query.md`, where the anchor no longer exists), confirming the split was the routing event that crystallised the placement defect. The sibling primitives' seam pages (`tool-calls.md`, `invocation.md`) were not affected by the size-cap split and retained their inline placement, which is why only the query surface exhibits this defect.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Move the seam blockquote into `docs/spec_topics/query/query-forms.md`, attached to the introduction of the query primitive — natural placement is at the end of `## Untyped form` or as a short trailing subsection of `## Typed form`, matching how `docs/spec_topics/tool-calls.md` and `docs/spec_topics/invocation.md` (INV-3) carry their `loom 1.0 seam — per-call timeout` blockquotes inline next to the primitive's body without a dedicated wrapper heading. Drop the `## Options surface` heading entirely — neither sibling primitive uses it, and the blockquote is self-titled (`> **loom 1.0 seam — per-call timeout / per-query overrides.**`).

When relocating, preserve the existing anchors verbatim on the blockquote so cross-references survive (currently the blockquote has no `<a id>` of its own; once it is the only seam on its new page, the relocation pass should add a `loom-1-0-seam-query-options` / `v1-seam-query-options` anchor pair matching the convention used by the other primitive-seam blockquotes — see e.g. `loom-1-0-seam-pre-flight-token-nullability` / `v1-seam-pre-flight-token-nullability` later in the same file).

Repoint the two `surface-extensions.md` `[Query — Options surface](../query.md)` citations (lines 19 and 25) to `[Query forms — loom 1.0 seam: per-call timeout / per-query overrides](../query/query-forms.md#…)` against the new anchor. Update the two parallel `[Tool Calls — Options surface](../tool-calls.md)` and `[Invocation — Options surface](../invocation.md)` citations on line 19 in the same pass to point at the inline blockquotes the sibling primitives already carry (those pages have never had an `#options-surface` anchor either); this corrects all three "Options surface" citations to genuine anchored targets in one edit and removes the misleading framing that the three primitives have a common "Options surface" surface name.

Edge cases for the implementer: (a) the relocated blockquote co-resolves with the sibling finding *"Options surface" seam bundles two independent deferred features* — if that finding's split lands first, two callouts move rather than one, both into the same location; (b) the surface-extensions citations refer to the relocated content by its seam label, not by section heading, so future renames of the seam blockquote require the same paired update; (c) the dual `loom-1-0-seam-*` / `v1-seam-*` anchor pair convention is the residual subject of the *"loom 1.0 seam" display text vs `v1-seam-*` cross-reference anchors* finding — adopt whichever convention that finding settles on if it lands first, otherwise use the dual-anchor form already established on neighbouring seams in the same file.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader consulting the page whose H1 is "Query failure and repair" first encounters a forward-compatibility deferral note about per-call timeouts and per-query overrides, then has to scroll past it to reach `QRY-8`. Cross-references from the surface-extensions hub resolve to an unanchored hub stub rather than to the normative seam, so the seam's discoverability through the hub is broken in addition to the on-page misplacement.

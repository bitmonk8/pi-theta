## Problem

The closing sentence of the *Emergent fast-path (sub-steps 2–3 only)* section of `session-only-degraded-state.md` attributes the fast-path's gating to `event.reason` ("available only for the five enumerated reasons"), but the triviality of sub-steps 2–3 is driven solely by the `ActiveInvocationRegistry` being empty — which is equally true on the unknown-reason path. The real contrast is whether an empty-registry observation may be used to short-circuit the abort-and-await scaffolding (forbidden on the unknown path by the **Unknown-reason rule**), not which reasons are eligible for a fast-path. The two pages also disagree on the fast-path's extent: this page scopes it to sub-steps 2–3 and pins that sub-step 4 always runs, while `unknown-reason-rule.md` enumerates watcher closure (sub-step 4) and listener detachment (sub-step 5) inside the fast-path it forbids, inviting the inference that the five reasons license skipping those steps.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `5e8b499` (2026-05-06, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Hot-reload teardown contract for the loom extension instance is unspecified\""); `9559e19` (2026-05-07, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"session_shutdown unknown reason: no closed-set validation, no fail-safe\""); `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04, "docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set")
**History:** `git log -S` on the defect tokens against `pi-integration-contract.md` and the post-split `session-only-degraded-state.md` traces the misframing to an interaction of three commits, not a single edit. `5e8b499` first seeded the reason-attributed framing in the `new`/`resume`/`fork` edge-case bullet ("a no-teardown reason makes the sequence a fast-path no-op (no active invocations exist at session boundaries because Pi serialises turns)") — already conflating the fast-path with the reason rather than with registry emptiness. `9559e19`, while adding the closed-set validation and Unknown-reason rule, appended the specific clause this finding targets: "This emergent fast-path is available only for the five enumerated reasons; an unknown `event.reason` … MUST run the full five-sub-step sequence even when the registry is empty." `f5e89f4` (the 30/100 KB split) then restructured the bullet into the dedicated *Emergent fast-path (sub-steps 2–3 only)* section, narrowing the fast-path to sub-steps 2–3 and adding "a session-only reason does **not** skip sub-step 4 or any other sub-step" — which sharpens the contradiction by retaining the reason-gated sentence beside an explicit registry-emptiness scoping. The durable fix addresses the interaction (the retained reason-gated sentence versus the narrowed emptiness-driven scoping), not a revert of any one commit.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the closing sentence of the *Emergent fast-path (sub-steps 2–3 only)* section so the sub-step-2/3 triviality is attributed to registry emptiness rather than to `event.reason`, and so its contrast with the unknown-reason path names what the **Unknown-reason rule** (`#unknown-reason-rule`) actually forbids — using an empty-registry observation to short-circuit the abort-and-await scaffolding — rather than which reasons are eligible. Reconcile the fast-path's extent between this page and `unknown-reason-rule.md` so the two do not disagree on what the fast-path covers.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: do not reintroduce any suggestion that the five session-only reasons skip more than the sub-step-2/3 abort-and-await — the section's existing pin that a session-only reason does **not** skip sub-step 4 or any other sub-step must continue to hold.

## Finding

The *Emergent fast-path (sub-steps 2–3 only)* section of `session-only-degraded-state.md` correctly scopes the fast-path to the abort-and-await over `ActiveInvocationRegistry` (sub-steps 2 and 3) and states that it is *emergent* — a consequence of the registry happening to be empty (e.g. `/new` from an idle prompt) — and that "a session-only reason does **not** skip sub-step 4 or any other sub-step." The closing sentence then undoes that framing: "This fast-path is available only for the five enumerated reasons; an unknown `event.reason` … MUST run the full five-sub-step sequence even when the registry is empty." Read literally, this asserts the fast-path is *gated by reason* — available for `{"quit","reload","new","resume","fork"}` and withheld from unknown reasons.

That attribution is wrong. The triviality of sub-steps 2–3 is driven solely by the registry being empty, and an empty registry makes the abort-and-await equally trivial on the unknown-reason path. The real contrast the sentence is reaching for lives in the **Unknown-reason rule** (`unknown-reason-rule.md`): on the unknown path the runtime MUST NOT short-circuit the abort-and-await scaffolding on observing an empty registry — "the registry drain, the bounded `Promise.allSettled` await, the watcher closure, and the listener detachment all run unconditionally." The distinction is therefore "may the empty-registry observation be used to skip the scaffolding?", not "which reasons are eligible for a fast-path."

The two pages also disagree on the fast-path's extent. `session-only-degraded-state.md` scopes it to sub-steps 2–3 and pins that sub-step 4 always runs for every reason; `unknown-reason-rule.md` enumerates watcher closure (sub-step 4) and listener detachment (sub-step 5) inside the fast-path it forbids on the unknown path. A reader who takes the closing sentence together with the unknown-reason rule's broader enumeration could conclude that for the five reasons the empty-registry observation licenses skipping watcher closure / listener detachment — directly contradicting this page's own "does not skip sub-step 4 or any other sub-step."

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Reframe the closing sentence of the *Emergent fast-path (sub-steps 2–3 only)* section so the fast-path's gating is attributed to registry emptiness, not to the reason. State it as: the sub-step-2/3 abort-and-await is trivial whenever the registry is empty regardless of reason; what the **Unknown-reason rule** forbids is *using* an empty-registry observation to short-circuit that scaffolding on the unknown path, where the drain and bounded await run unconditionally. Make explicit that the full five-sub-step sequence (including sub-steps 1, 4, and 5) runs for *every* reason — known or unknown — so the only reason-dependent behaviour is whether the empty-registry short-circuit of sub-steps 2–3 is permitted.

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- Keep the wording consistent with the section's existing pin that "a session-only reason does **not** skip sub-step 4 or any other sub-step"; the reframed sentence must not reintroduce any suggestion that the five reasons skip more than the sub-step-2/3 abort-and-await.
- Verify the reframed sentence does not contradict `unknown-reason-rule.md`'s "no-active-invocations fast-path … MUST NOT be taken" clause, whose enumeration lists watcher closure and listener detachment. Either cross-reference that clause precisely or scope the fast-path consistently to sub-steps 2–3 on both pages so the two do not disagree on the fast-path's extent.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The normative behaviour is fully pinned in the same section and in the Unknown-reason rule, so an implementer reading the full corpus produces a correct handler. The misleading sentence misattributes the fast-path's gating to the reason rather than to registry emptiness, and — taken with the broader fast-path enumeration in `unknown-reason-rule.md` — invites a careless reader toward the wrong inference that the five reasons license skipping watcher closure / listener detachment on an empty registry.

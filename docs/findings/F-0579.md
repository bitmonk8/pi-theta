## Finding

`V18c`'s **Adds** packs the entire static portion of the version-bump checklist into a single leaf: the SDK surface-inventory tests (step 2(a)/2(b)), the `engines.node` three-way equality gate, the `peerDependencies` pin assertion, two pinned-constant literal-read assertions (`FACTORY_PROBABLE_CAPABILITIES`, `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS`), the `session-shutdown-reason-snapshot` brand-string type-equality gate, the provider seed-field `Api`-coverage + per-provider fixture gate, and the strict-capability probe (two arms). Each has its own triggering condition, failure symptom, and seam.

On top of those mechanical build-time gates, the same leaf also owns the **manual** editorial-review checklist items `(a)`–`(ak)` — which the leaf's own prose flags as "Separately — and *not* a build-time gate" — a per-bump human audit confirmed by editorial review rather than by `npm test`. The leaf therefore mixes two fundamentally different *natures* of verification (CI-reddening mechanical gates vs a manual editorial audit) under one **Ships when**, and the manual half additionally carries the `V9l` clause-(a) resolution ownership.

The runtime-evidence acceptance gate was already peeled out of this leaf into `V18d`; what remains is still a large, multi-seam bundle whose mechanical and manual halves are landed, reviewed, and reverted as one unit.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Separate the manual editorial-review checklist from the mechanical gates

**Approach.** Keep every mechanical build-time gate in `V18c` (they share the `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` / `package.json` seam and all redden one `npm test`), and move the manual per-bump editorial-review checklist `(a)`–`(ak)` — the half the leaf itself labels "not a build-time gate" — plus its clause-(a) resolution ownership into a new leaf.

**Plan edits.** Add `<new>` (+ `<new>-T`). Strike the editorial-review-checklist paragraph from `V18c`'s **Adds** and the editorial-acceptance clause from `V18c`'s **Ships when**; relocate both to `<new>`. Retarget the clause-(a) `Deps` edge on `V9l` and `V9l-T` from `V18c` to `<new>`, and update the §Blocked obligations clause-(a) owner pointer and §V9 interleave note in `docs/plan.md` from `V18c` to `<new>`. `V9h`/`V9h-T` (snapshot brand-string) and `V18d`/`V18d-T` (re-runs the static gates on revert) keep their `V18c` edges.

**Spec edits.** None.

**Pros.** Splits on the verification-nature boundary (mechanical CI gate vs manual editorial audit) the leaf already names; lowest retargeting cost (only the clause-(a) consumers move); the build-time gates stay one cohesive unit; the mechanical and manual halves gain independent revert.

**Cons.** Does not reduce the build-time-gate count within `V18c` (~9 mechanical gates remain in one leaf).

**Risks.** The clause-(a) owner is referenced from `docs/plan.md` and two blocked leaves; a missed reference leaves `V9l` pointing at a stale owner.

### Option B — Split the build-time gates into literal-pin vs structural-probe leaves

**Approach.** `V18c-literal` owns the literal/version-pin gates (surface-inventory 2(a)/2(b), `engines.node` three-way equality, `peerDependencies` pin, the two constant literal-reads, the `session-shutdown-reason-snapshot` brand string); `V18c-probe` owns the SDK structural-probe gates (provider seed-field `Api`-coverage + per-provider fixture, strict-capability probe). Each depends on `V18a`/`V18b` only.

**Plan edits.** Two leaves (+ two `-T` partners) replace `V18c`. Retarget `V9h`/`V9h-T` (snapshot brand-string) to the literal leaf; retarget `V18d`/`V18d-T` (re-runs all static gates on revert) to both. Assign the manual editorial-review checklist a home (it is neither literal nor probe).

**Spec edits.** None.

**Pros.** Reduces each leaf's build-time-gate count; groups gates by failure mode (literal skew vs structural shape).

**Cons.** Both halves share the `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY` seam and one spec checklist document, so the split fragments a cohesive unit; more retargeting (`V9h` and `V18d` go to different targets); doubles the `-T` partners; the editorial checklist still needs a home, so this split alone is incomplete.

**Risks.** `V18d` must depend on both halves to re-run all static gates on revert; missing one leaves the revert path under-gated.

### Recommendation

Take Option A. It splits on the boundary the leaf already draws ("Separately — and *not* a build-time gate"), separates the two genuinely different verification natures, and carries the lowest retargeting cost. Edge cases the implementer must watch: `V9l` and `V9l-T` name `V18c` as the clause-(a) resolution owner while blocked — move both their `Deps` and the `docs/plan.md` §Blocked obligations pointer and §V9 interleave note to `<new>` in the same change; `V9h`/`V9h-T` and `V18d`/`V18d-T` keep their `V18c` edges because they consume mechanical-gate outputs, not the editorial checklist. Honour the no-invented-IDs policy: use `<new>`/`<new>-T` until the implementer allocates real IDs.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A single leaf landing ~9 mechanical build-time gates plus a manual editorial audit is harder to land green incrementally and harder to review than the underlying seams warrant, and its mechanical and manual halves cannot be reverted independently. No gate is blocked and no spec rule goes uncovered, so an implementer can still produce a working leaf; the cost is reviewability and a coarse revert/blast-radius unit.

## Finding

GOV-1 *Canonical form* pins one anchor shape for every REQ-ID: the inline marker `**PREFIX-N.**`, with the bold-with-period decoration declared normative and the dual-form witness regex fixed as `<a id="prefix-n"></a> \*\*PREFIX-N\.\*\*`. GOV-3 further declares "the bold-with-period bytes are the witness pattern for any third-party REQ-ID extractor". The corpus, however, overwhelmingly uses two label shapes that this canonical form and witness regex do not match: the PIC subtree's titled form `**PIC-N. Title.**` (period and descriptive title inside the bold span, e.g. `**PIC-2. Prompt-mode sequential execution.**`) and the governance pages' parenthetical form `**GOV-N (description).**` (e.g. `**GOV-1 (anchor placement).**`). Because the witness regex requires the closing `**` immediately after the period that follows `PREFIX-N`, neither shape matches — the marker `**PIC-2. Prompt-mode sequential execution.**` is not `**PIC-2.**`.

The divergence is systemic, not incidental. Of 55 PIC anchor sites, 37 use the titled form and are invisible to the witness regex; only the 18 that happen to carry the bare `**PIC-N.**` form are extractable. Among the GOV anchors, every one except `**GOV-3.**` uses the parenthetical form, so the canonical-form rule fails to match even its own defining anchor (`**GOV-1 (anchor placement).**`). The run's generated `ids.md` projection corroborates this exactly: it lists only the 18 bare-form PIC IDs and only `GOV-3` at a canonical governance anchor; PIC-1/2/7/8/9/10–23/33–44/49–53/55 and GOV-1/5/6/7/8/9/15–31 are absent at their definition sites. A reviewer auditing conformance through the index therefore cannot confirm that a cited ID has a definition, and a third-party extractor implemented to the spec's own witness pattern silently drops ~55 obligations.

The defect is a single internal contradiction between one normative rule (GOV-1 *Canonical form* / witness regex) and the anchor form the corpus actually uses; either the rule must widen to admit a descriptive remainder inside the bold span, or every titled/parenthetical anchor must be normalised to the bare form. GOV-16 inherits GOV-1's witness regex by reference for the inline-label class, so any change to the rule propagates to inline labels.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Normalise every anchor to the bare canonical form

- **Approach:** Move every title/description out of the bold-with-period span so each anchor reads exactly `**PREFIX-N.**` (e.g. `**PIC-2.** Prompt-mode sequential execution.` and `**GOV-1.** (anchor placement)` or `**GOV-1.** Anchor placement.`), across all ~37 titled PIC sites and all parenthetical GOV sites; regenerate `ids.md`.
- **Spec edits:** Edit ~55 anchor sites in `pi-integration-contract/*.md` and `governance/*.md`; leave GOV-1 *Canonical form* and the witness regex unchanged.
- **Pros:** No change to the normative rule or the witness regex; the bold-with-period property and extractor contract stay exactly as written; preserves the strictest single witness pattern.
- **Cons:** Very wide-surface edit touching every governance and PIC page; reflows readable, navigable titles that the corpus deliberately carries at every anchor; high churn and re-review cost; easy to miss a site.
- **Risks:** A missed site stays invisible to the index; cross-references that rely on rendered heading/anchor text near these markers may shift.

### Option B — Amend GOV-1 *Canonical form* and the witness regex to admit a descriptive remainder

- **Approach:** Widen the canonical form so the bold span may carry an optional descriptive remainder between `PREFIX-N` and the terminating period (covering both `**PIC-N. Title.**` and `**GOV-N (desc).**`), keeping the bold-with-period decoration as the anchor/back-reference discriminator. Update the witness regex accordingly (e.g. `<a id="prefix-n"></a> \*\*PREFIX-N\b[^*]*\.\*\*`), preserving the requirement of a terminating period before the closing `**` so non-bold and period-less bold back-references still fail to match.
- **Spec edits:** Edit GOV-1 *Canonical form* and *Dual-form layout* in `req-id-prefix-table-active-a.md`; GOV-16 inherits the change by reference, so confirm the inline-label anchors (`HC3-a`, `NOCEIL-3`, `SNK-a`) still match under the widened pattern.
- **Pros:** Localised governance edit; preserves the descriptive titles already present at every anchor; reconciles the rule with its own defining anchor; one change covers the whole corpus.
- **Cons:** Loosens the single most-precise witness pattern; the widened regex must be authored carefully to keep the anchor/back-reference distinction intact.
- **Risks:** If the widened regex omits the terminating-period requirement it would start matching bold back-references; the interaction with the sibling back-reference finding must be checked.

### Recommendation

Adopt **Option B**: amend GOV-1 *Canonical form* and the witness regex to admit an optional descriptive remainder inside the bold-with-period span. The descriptive titles are present at essentially every governance and most PIC anchors, including GOV-1's own — the corpus evidently intends them, so the canonical-form rule is the under-specified party. The widened witness regex MUST still require a period immediately before the closing `**` so that period-less bold back-references (e.g. `**GOV-7**`) remain non-matching, and the change MUST be checked against GOV-16's inline-label anchors, which inherit the regex by reference.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

The canonical-form rule is internally contradicted by ~55 of the corpus's own anchor sites, including GOV-1's own. A REQ-ID extractor built to the spec's declared witness pattern omits those IDs at their definition sites, so the projected `ids.md` index cannot confirm that a cited ID is defined, and two reasonable extractor implementers (strict witness regex vs. lenient token scan) produce different ID sets from the same corpus.

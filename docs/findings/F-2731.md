## Finding

`V10d` (`Reload debounce and settings-re-merge failure-injection arm`) carries two
substantively different state machines in one leaf. The first is the stateless
250 ms watcher-event coalescer (`cka-36`): a drop-and-reschedule debounce driven
through the injected `Clock`/`FakeClock` seam, whose only observable is "N events
in one window → exactly one reload; a post-window event → a second reload." The
second is the `PIC-49` cross-window rebuild-serialisation concurrency guard: a
guard that observes whether a prior window's rebuild is still in flight against the
live `LoomRegistry`, AJV validator cache, and prompt-mode registration cache,
*defers* a newly-fired rebuild rather than running it concurrently, and *releases*
the in-flight guard on the prior rebuild's single synchronous publish or its
`loom/runtime/registry-swap-failed` discard (the `PIC-36` completion signal `V9b`
owns).

These are independently red-testable concerns. The coalescer is a pure timer state
machine; the serialisation guard is a concurrency invariant whose fixture must
stage an in-flight rebuild awaiting its asynchronous steps and assert both the
deferral and the guard-release at each of two completion outcomes. They are coupled
only at the debounce→rebuild boundary and via the shared `FakeClock` seam — coalescing
can be implemented and verified without the guard, and vice versa. Bundling them (on
top of the leaf's third concern, the settings-re-merge failure-injection arm
contributed to `V9b`'s `ReloadFailureInjector` seam) forces a single agent to hold
two distinct mental models and two unrelated fixtures in one verification pass, and
makes the leaf larger and harder to red-test in isolation than its `V10a`/`V10b`/`V10c`
siblings.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

If shipped unfixed, `V10d` remains a larger-than-sibling leaf whose two unrelated
state machines (timer coalescing and a cross-window concurrency guard) must be
verified in one pass; an implementer can still produce a working leaf, so this does
not block pickup or open a coverage gap, but it degrades isolatability — a red on
one concern does not localise to the other, and the guard's concurrency fixture is
entangled with the coalescer's timing fixture.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Peel the `PIC-49` cross-window rebuild-serialisation guard off `V10d` into a new
sibling leaf (with its paired `-T`), leaving `V10d` as the reload-debounce coalescer
plus its existing settings-re-merge failure-injection arm.

- In `docs/plan_topics/V10d-reload-debounce.md`: strike the PIC-49 content — the
  `Adds` sentence beginning "It also adds the across-window rebuild-serialization
  guard at the debounce→rebuild boundary (`PIC-49`)…", the second `Tests` bullet
  (`PIC-49` cross-window rebuild serialization), and the trailing PIC-49 clause of
  `Ships when` ("…and a debounce timer firing while a prior window's rebuild is still
  in flight defers… (`PIC-49`)"). Retain the 250 ms coalescing debounce (`cka-36`),
  the settings-re-merge arm against `V9b`'s `ReloadFailureInjector`, and the
  `V10c`/`V8d`/`V9b` Deps that those two concerns require. `V9b` stays in `V10d`'s
  Deps for the settings-re-merge arm even after the PIC-49 coupling moves.
- In `docs/plan_topics/V10d-T-reload-debounce.md`: strike the corresponding PIC-49
  red-first `Tests` bullet, leaving the debounce-coalescing bullet.
- Add the new leaf `<new>` (and `<new>-T`) owning `PIC-49`: the serialisation guard's
  deferral behaviour and guard-release on the in-flight rebuild's synchronous publish
  or its `loom/runtime/registry-swap-failed` discard, driven through the injected
  `Clock`/`FakeClock` seam. Its Deps include `V8d` (the `Clock` seam), `V9b` (the
  `PIC-36` publish/discard completion signal it releases on), and `V10d` (the guard
  sits at the debounce→rebuild boundary the coalescer produces).
- In `docs/plan.md` §V10: add the new leaf link after `V10d` and extend the V10
  interleave note to record the new leaf's Deps edge (it sequences after `V10d`).
- In `docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md`: retarget the `PIC-49` closing-leaf row
  from `V10d` to `<new>`. The `cka-36` reload-debounce row stays `V10d`.
- In `docs/plan_topics/H5b-warn-only-canary.md`: add `<new>` to the Deps range that
  currently enumerates `V10a`–`V10d`, since the new leaf is coverage-producing.

The leaf-ID scheme (`PREFIX-N` with a letter facet, e.g. `V10d`) is the plan's; let
the implementer pick the concrete ID for `<new>` and `<new>-T` rather than minting it
here. `V4g`'s `ERR-7` settings-re-merge dependency on `V10d` is unaffected and needs
no edit.

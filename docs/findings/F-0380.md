## Problem

The `PIC-1` Tests bullet in `V9d` (and the byte-identical bullet in its `V9d-T` twin) describes `masked` as "cascade-copied verbatim with non-inclusion under dedup." That compound clause fuses two independent spec obligations and admits two incompatible readings: (a) `masked` is always copied verbatim onto the cascade-twin re-emission and is merely not a dedup-tuple key (spec-correct), or (b) `masked` is *not copied* when the dedup step collapses a message. The spec separates these into distinct normative clauses — `runtime-event-channel.md` §PIC-1 clause (f) (verbatim-copy obligation, copy the originating instance including `masked`, never re-derive) and clause (g) (consumers MUST NOT include `masked` in the `(kind, query_site, message, occurred_at)` dedup tuple). Reading (b) contradicts clause (f), so a test author could build a PIC-1 witness that drops or re-derives `masked` at the boundary.

**History:** `git log -S"cascade-copied" -- docs/plan_topics/V9d-runtime-event-channel.md` and `git log -S"non-inclusion under dedup" -- docs/plan_topics/` both return `c6a664e` as the sole commit changing the count of either token. `git show c6a664e:` confirms the verbatim phrase was present in both `V9d` and `V9d-T` at the initial plan build, and no later commit on either file's `--follow` history touched it. The ambiguity entered the corpus with the first authored version of these leaves and has never been revised.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the trailing clause of the `PIC-1` Tests bullet in both `V9d` and `V9d-T` so the verbatim-copy obligation and the dedup-key non-inclusion rule read as two separate obligations rather than one fused phrase. Tie the verbatim-copy half to `runtime-event-channel.md` §PIC-1 clause (f) (the boundary copies the originating instance's `masked` unchanged, never re-derives) and the non-inclusion half to clause (g) (`masked` is excluded from the `(kind, query_site, message, occurred_at)` dedup tuple).

## Solution constraints

- Keep the `PIC-1` Tests bullets in `V9d` and `V9d-T` identical to each other; the `-T` twin must continue to mirror the implementation leaf.
- The spec is read-only for this fix; the defect and its correction are internal to the two plan leaves.

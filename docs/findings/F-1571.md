## Finding

The opening orientation paragraph of `governance.md` summarises the page's
REQ-ID inventory inline as: *"the rules (GOV-1, GOV-3 through GOV-9, GOV-15,
GOV-16, GOV-30, GOV-31; GOV-2, GOV-10, GOV-11, GOV-12, GOV-13, GOV-14
retired …)"*. This summary contradicts the authoritative sub-pages it points
to in three ways:

- **Lists a retired ID as live.** The range "GOV-3 through GOV-9" sweeps in
  GOV-4, which is retired (per the *Retired REQ-IDs* table on
  `governance/anchor-scheme-and-retired.md`, retired and re-added as GOV-24).
  A range expression is the wrong shape for an inventory with interior holes.
- **Under-lists the retired set.** The retired sub-list (GOV-2, GOV-10,
  GOV-11, GOV-12, GOV-13, GOV-14) omits the two other retired IDs — GOV-4 and
  GOV-21 (the latter split into GOV-25–GOV-29).
- **Omits live rules entirely.** Live rules GOV-19, GOV-20, GOV-22, GOV-23,
  GOV-24, and GOV-25 through GOV-29 appear nowhere in the enumeration. (GOV-17
  / GOV-18 are accounted for separately earlier in the same sentence.)

The net effect is an unreliable at-a-glance inventory on the very page that
the glossary and other pages name as the authoritative REQ-ID registry. A
maintainer or tool trusting the orientation line concludes that GOV-4 is live
and that no GOV ID above 31 — and several below it — exists, directly
contradicting the page's own authoritative active/retired sub-tables.

**History:** Commit 64cdc60 rewrote the orientation paragraph into the "GOV-3 through GOV-9" range form, at which point GOV-4 was still live and the range was accurate. The summary then drifted as the registry it describes was edited around it: 1d773d6 retired GOV-4 without narrowing the range, 8e3ccb4 retired GOV-21 without adding it to the inline retired sub-list, and fe8aa5c (the most recent edit of the line) added GOV-30/GOV-31 from the GOV-12 split but left the stale range, the missing retired IDs, and the omitted live rules (GOV-19/20/22/23/24/25–29, added incrementally across intervening commits) unreconciled. The defect is the cumulative product of partial updates to a free-prose summary that no lock-step gate covers, not any single commit's error.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Edit the opening orientation paragraph of `governance.md` to remove the
parenthetical REQ-ID enumeration and its inline "… retired" sub-list. In its
place, state that this page owns the live and retired REQ-ID registry and
describe the *classes* of rules it governs (coining, anchoring, retirement,
corpus-direction/binding-scope, source-language stability, stable inline
labels, release-version naming) without naming every individual ID. Add a
one-clause forward-reference to the authoritative active prefix tables
(`req-id-prefix-table-active-a.md`, `req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md`) and the
*Retired REQ-IDs* table on `governance/anchor-scheme-and-retired.md`.

Preserve the existing GOV-16 inline-label paragraph and the GOV-17/GOV-18
corpus-direction clause already present in the same sentence — the edit must
leave those references intact.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A spec maintainer or REQ-ID-mapping tool that trusts the hub's orientation
line builds a wrong mental model of the registry — treating retired GOV-4 as
live and concluding that GOV-19/20/22/23/24/25–29 do not exist. The
authoritative active/retired sub-tables on the same page are correct and
override the summary, so no runtime obligation is mis-derived; the harm is
misdirection on the page meant to be the inventory's source of truth.

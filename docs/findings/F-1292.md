## Problem

`docs/spec_topics/overview-and-orientation.md` and `docs/spec_topics/overview.md` both carry "Overview" in their `# H1` and both open with a standalone single-sentence top-line definition of pi-loom, but the two definitions diverge ("a domain-specific scripting language for prompts and agentic operations" vs. "a purpose-built scripting language for authoring parameterized, programmatic templates that target the code/model boundary") and neither cross-references the other. The two pages also sit at different corpus levels — `overview-and-orientation.md` is a section page linked from `spec.md`'s contents list, while `overview.md` is a topic page reached via the *Reading order* list under `language-and-architecture.md` — but nothing on either page marks which is the canonical definitional entry point. A reader cannot tell which page owns the definition, and a contributor editing the top-line cannot tell which page to keep in sync.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04, size-cap split) compounded by `81bf52a` (2026-06-11, spec/ → spec_topics/ collapse)
**History:** `fecb504` (2026-05-04) split the original monolithic `spec.md` into per-topic files and created `spec_topics/overview.md` titled "Overview and Conceptual Model" with the "purpose-built scripting language … code/model boundary" opening, at which point that page was the sole "Overview" in the corpus. `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04, the 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size-cap enforcement) lifted the root-level opening paragraph of `spec.md` (which had been authored in the older "adds a domain-specific scripting language for prompts and agentic operations" form) into a newly created `spec/overview-and-orientation.md` section page, so the corpus now carried two distinct "Overview"-titled pages with divergent top-line definitions. `81bf52a` (2026-06-11) then collapsed `spec/` into `spec_topics/` to restore the single-delegation-target architecture; the move was content-preserving by design and so did not touch either opening paragraph. The result is two "Overview"-titled pages co-located in the same directory, each carrying its own standalone definitional one-liner, with neither deferring to the other. No single commit authored the conflict.

## Solution approach

Establish `overview-and-orientation.md`'s `## Overview` as the single canonical top-line definition, and rename `overview.md` so its title no longer claims the "Overview" role (its `# H1` is already "Overview and Conceptual Model", so "Conceptual Model" is the natural retitle), applying GOV-30's aggregator-vs-source convention. Drop the renamed file's duplicate definitional one-liner, migrating the forward-links it carried (final-value owner, effects-surface owner, scope-of-a-loom-file cross-link) onto the renamed file's lead paragraph. Repoint every inbound reference to the renamed file in the same commit — `language-and-architecture.md`'s `### Reading order` bullet, the `glossary.md` entries that link it, `pi-integration-contract/subagent.md`'s link, and the plan-leaf **Spec.** fields in `plan_topics/M-minimal-slash-command.md` and `plan_topics/M-T-minimal-slash-command.md`.

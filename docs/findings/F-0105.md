## Finding

`V16a`'s **Adds** field closes with two sub-bullets — `Verified at the seam in isolation:` and `Not witnessable at the stateless seam — owned by downstream sites:` — that partition the CIO-1…CIO-6 properties into seam-decidable versus downstream-witnessed. The same partition is already carried inline by the **Tests** bullets: the `CIO-1` Tests bullet states its temporal "evaluated at slash-load before any runtime-class ceiling fires" arm is witnessed downstream at the load-time-vs-runtime consult split (`V4e`/`V11f` vs `V5e`/`V13c`/`V15b`), and the `CIO-5` Tests bullet states its cross-site "ceiling #3 never interleaves" arm is witnessed by `H7a`'s co-occurring-breach integration run. The **Ships when** field restates the same exclusions a third time.

The Adds sub-bullets therefore duplicate (in a looser, summarised form) normative test-scope content whose authoritative home is the per-CIO Tests bullets. This is a second normative surface for the same scope partition: a future edit to the Tests bullets (e.g. retargeting CIO-5's downstream witness if `H7a` is split) would leave the Adds summary stale, and an implementer reading Adds first would form an inaccurate picture of what the leaf's `npm test` run actually asserts.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-by-single-commit
**Introducing commits:** `8ef7055` ("pi-loom plan: resolve \"V16a over-claims all CIO bullets provable at stateless seam\"", 2026-06-11)
**History:** `git log -S`/`git show` on `docs/plan_topics/V16a-ceiling-order-masked.md` shows the two Adds sub-bullets and the per-CIO inline Tests exclusions (CIO-1 downstream consult-split, CIO-5 `H7a` witness) were added together in commit `8ef7055`. That commit reworked the prior single "All CIO bullets are exercised…" sentence into both (a) the Adds `Verified` / `Not witnessable` partition and (b) the expanded per-CIO Tests bullets — so the over-claim fix that closed an overclaim defect simultaneously introduced the redundant second surface. The defect did not pre-exist that edit; the earlier text carried no such partition.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V16a-ceiling-order-masked.md`, strike the two terminal Adds sub-bullets — `Verified at the seam in isolation:` and `Not witnessable at the stateless seam — owned by downstream sites:` — leaving the per-CIO Tests bullets as the single authoritative carrier of the seam-decidable / downstream-witnessed partition. The preceding Adds sentence ("The stateless single-candidate seam witnesses only the CIO sub-properties that are decidable from co-present candidates; the temporal cross-site relations are not observable here and are owned by the downstream enforcement-site leaves and the `H7a` integration run") already states the partition in prose, so adjust its trailing colon to a full stop when the sub-bullets are removed. Do not weaken or move the inline exclusion clauses in the `CIO-1` and `CIO-5` Tests bullets — those are the surface that stays.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The duplicated scope partition can diverge from the authoritative per-CIO Tests bullets on any future edit, leaving an implementer who reads Adds with a stale view of which CIO properties the leaf actually witnesses at `npm test`. The leaf remains implementable from the Tests bullets alone, so nothing blocks pickup or gating.

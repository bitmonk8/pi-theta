## Problem

`V9b`'s Adds claims sole ownership of the model-reference-matcher production wiring point: at the load pass `V9b` constructs the concrete matcher (`findExactModelReferenceMatch` against `ctx.modelRegistry.getAvailable()`) and injects it into the parser through the injection seam `V6a` defines, so `V6a`'s load-time `loom/load/model-unresolved` resolution and `V11a`'s binder-model resolution bind one matcher. Neither `V9b`'s Tests nor `V9b-T`'s Tests assert any of this, and `V9b`'s Ships-when omits the matcher entirely. A `V9b` implementation could construct no matcher, or inject two divergent instances, and still turn green at its own exit gate, leaving the single-matcher-instance invariant unverified until the H7a terminal integration run.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 5953b18 — pi-loom plan: resolve "V6a model-reference-matcher seam has no declared producer" (2026-06-11, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The model-reference-matcher production wiring point did not exist in `V9b` until commit 5953b18, which resolved the "V6a model-reference-matcher seam has no declared producer" finding by assigning the producer to `V9b` — adding the wiring sentence to `V9b`'s Adds and the `V6a` edge to `V9b`'s Deps. That same commit added no corresponding Tests bullet to `V9b` or `V9b-T`, so the wiring entered the corpus as an owned-but-untested obligation in a single edit.

## Solution approach

Add a paired Tests bullet to `V9b-T` and its `V9b` mirror that asserts the load pass constructs the concrete matcher from `ctx.modelRegistry.getAvailable()`, injects it into the `V6a` injection seam, and that a single matcher instance services both `V6a`'s `loom/load/model-unresolved` resolution and `V11a`'s binder-model resolution. Extend `V9b`'s Ships-when to name this observable so the construction/injection is part of the leaf's exit gate. The assertion should observe single-source-of-construction (instance identity or equivalent), not mere equivalence-of-outcome against a shared fake, since equivalence would pass even when two matchers are wired.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: `V6a` and `V11a` — the matcher contract and the injection seam they declare are read-only for this fix.

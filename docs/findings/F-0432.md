## Problem

`docs/plan.md` §How to use this plan, step 2 triggers adding a new leaf to `H5b`'s **Deps** with the capability modal "If the new leaf **can introduce** an executable REQ-ID …". The authority it explicitly defers to — `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline (*Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance*) — states the same trigger as an actuality test ("Whenever a new leaf is added whose `Tests.` bullets **carry** an executable REQ-ID …"). The two phrasings denote different leaf sets: a leaf merely *capable* of introducing an executable REQ-ID but not actually citing one satisfies step 2 but not conventions.md. A reader following step 2 literally can over-include leaves in `H5b`'s **Deps** relative to the rule that governs.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** ab8e297 — pi-loom plan: resolve "Transitive-completeness obligation invisible at leaf authoring" (2026-06-11, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** Step 2's transitive-completeness pointer did not exist before ab8e297; that commit authored the whole sentence (the `git log -S "can introduce"` pickaxe localises the phrase to this single commit). The defect entered at authoring time: the new pointer paraphrased the conventions.md *Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance* trigger with the capability modal "can introduce" instead of carrying over its actuality test "whose `Tests.` bullets carry".

## Solution approach

In `docs/plan.md` §How to use this plan, step 2, rewrite the trigger clause to use the actuality test from the cited `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline (*Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance*) instead of the capability modal. Replace "can introduce an executable REQ-ID" with phrasing keyed to whether the new leaf's `Tests.` bullets actually carry an executable REQ-ID, so step 2's verb matches the authority it defers to.

## Solution constraints

- `conventions.md` is the authoritative owner of the exact trigger conditions and is out of scope for this fix — align the `plan.md` pointer to it; do not edit `conventions.md`.

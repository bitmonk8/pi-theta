## Finding

`docs/plan.md` §How to use this plan, step 2 instructs the author to add a new leaf to `H5b`'s **Deps** "If the new leaf **can introduce** an executable REQ-ID, a citing test that *closes* a coverage-matrix-mapped numbered REQ-ID, or an un-anchored normative MUST". The authoritative obligation it cites — `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline (*Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance*) — states the same trigger as an **actuality** test: "Whenever a new leaf is added whose `Tests.` bullets **carry** an executable REQ-ID …".

"can introduce" is a capability modal; "whose `Tests.` bullets carry" is an actuality test. The two do not denote the same set of leaves: a leaf that is merely *capable* of introducing an executable REQ-ID but does not actually cite one in its `Tests.` bullets satisfies step 2's reading but not conventions.md's. Step 2 explicitly defers to conventions.md ("which states that obligation once and governs its exact triggers"), so the divergent local wording contradicts the authority it points at. A reader who follows step 2 literally can over-include leaves in `H5b`'s **Deps** relative to the rule that actually governs.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** ab8e297 — pi-loom plan: resolve "Transitive-completeness obligation invisible at leaf authoring" (2026-06-11, Thomas Andersen)

The defect entered at authoring time: the new pointer paraphrased the conventions.md *Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance* trigger with the capability modal "can introduce" instead of carrying over its actuality test "whose `Tests.` bullets carry".

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan.md` §How to use this plan, step 2, replace the capability modal "can introduce" with the actuality phrasing the cited `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline (*Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance*) uses. Concretely, change the clause

> If the new leaf **can introduce** an executable REQ-ID, a citing test that *closes* a coverage-matrix-mapped numbered REQ-ID, or an un-anchored normative MUST, add it to [`H5b`]…

to read

> If the new leaf **introduces an executable REQ-ID (i.e. its `Tests.` bullets carry one)**, a citing test that *closes* a coverage-matrix-mapped numbered REQ-ID, or an un-anchored normative MUST, add it to [`H5b`]…

`conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline remains the authoritative owner of the exact trigger conditions; this edit only aligns the plan.md pointer's verb to it and must not be made by editing conventions.md.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An author following step 2's "can introduce" modal could add leaves to `H5b`'s **Deps** that do not actually carry an executable REQ-ID, diverging from the rule conventions.md governs. The divergence is bounded: step 2 defers to conventions.md for the exact triggers, and `H5d`'s per-cell at-least-one gate enforces the real coverage-producing set mechanically, so the wording mismatch misleads a careful reader without corrupting the plan's gates.

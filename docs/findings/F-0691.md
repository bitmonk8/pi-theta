## Finding

The governance subtree coins two distinct anchor concepts that both lead with the qualifier `dual-`, and neither has a glossary entry or in-prose disambiguator pointing at the other:

- **GOV-1's "dual-form" pair** (`req-id-prefix-table-active-a.md`, also referenced from `stable-inline-labels.md` and `req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md` GOV-22) — a co-located pair *on a single source line*: an `<a id="prefix-n"></a>` HTML token immediately followed by the inline `**PREFIX-N.**` marker. The word "dual" here means *two anchor forms (HTML + inline-bold) for one REQ-ID at one site*.

- **GOV-25–GOV-29's "dual-anchored" heading or paragraph** (`release-version-naming.md`, intensional definition pinned by GOV-27) — *two `<a id>` tags* (a canonical `loom-1-0-…` arm and an alias `v1-…` arm) co-located at one heading or paragraph so that inbound cross-links resolving against either fragment land at the same target. The word "dual" here means *two anchor slugs (canonical + alias) for one heading*.

The two concepts touch disjoint mechanics — REQ-ID anchor *layout* vs. legacy-token alias *back-compat* — but a reader doing `grep dual-` across `governance/`, or skimming the section structure, sees adjacent governance prose that uses overlapping vocabulary for orthogonal rules. The conflict is sharpened by the fact that GOV-27's "dual-anchored" sites *also* satisfy GOV-1, so a single sentence can legitimately need both qualifiers. The glossary defines neither term.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction

**Introducing commits:**
- `ce1af28` (2026-05-06) — first introduced GOV-1's *Dual-form layout* sub-bullet and the term `dual-form`.
- `4a7afbf` (2026-05-28) — introduced GOV-21's "dual-anchor convention" and the predicate "*dual-anchored* iff it carries both a `<a id=\"v1-…\">` anchor and a `<a id=\"loom-1-0-…\">` anchor", overlapping the `dual-` qualifier with the pre-existing GOV-1 vocabulary.
- `8e3ccb4` — split GOV-21 into GOV-25..GOV-29 and pinned the term in GOV-27's official title "dual-anchored intensional definition", elevating the colliding qualifier to a named REQ-ID.

**History:** The collision is a multi-commit interaction. The GOV-1 dual-form terminology landed first; three weeks later GOV-21 was authored out-of-band (the commit message explicitly notes "bypasses the spec-review pipeline") and chose `dual-anchor` for an unrelated alias-pair concept without checking the existing vocabulary. The later GOV-21 split into GOV-25..29 preserved the colliding term in GOV-27's name.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single
**State:** shaped

### Recommendation

Rename GOV-25..29's "dual-anchored" qualifier to **alias-paired** and add a glossary entry. GOV-1's `dual-form` terminology is older, more widely cited (three governance files plus internal back-references on `req-id-prefix-table-active-a.md`), and structurally more central — it is part of the witness regex contracted by GOV-9 and GOV-22 — so renaming it would have a much larger blast radius than renaming the GOV-27 family, which is bounded to one topic file plus one retired-prefix row. "Alias-paired" reads accurately: the canonical arm is not an alias; the *pair* exists *for* the alias.

Spec edits, in order: (1) on `release-version-naming.md`, replace `dual-anchored` (~6 occurrences) and `dual-anchor convention` (2 occurrences) across the preamble + GOV-25..29 rule bodies with **alias-paired**, and update GOV-27's title to "alias-paired intensional definition"; add a one-line "*Not to be confused with GOV-1 dual-form anchor (a single REQ-ID's HTML + inline-marker pair).*" disambiguator under the new term in the preamble; (2) update the retired-prefix row on `anchor-scheme-and-retired.md` (1 occurrence in the GOV-21 retired-prefix row cell); (3) add a **alias-paired heading or paragraph** glossary entry on `glossary.md` citing GOV-27 as canonical home.

Watch for: the GOV-27 REQ-ID identifier itself stays as `GOV-27` — only its descriptive title changes, so no `#gov-27` cross-link breaks. The term "dual-anchored" does not appear in `plan.md`, `plan_topics/`, or any non-governance spec file, so the rename does not propagate beyond the governance subtree plus the one glossary entry.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A future contributor grepping `dual-form` or `dual-anchor` across `governance/` retrieves both rule families and must read each hit's surrounding paragraph to decide which "dual" is at play. Misreading is unlikely to produce wrong runtime behaviour (the two rules' mechanics are syntactically disjoint), but mis-citation in commit messages, review comments, or new spec prose is easy. The defect is a clarity tax on every reader who touches governance, not a behavioural divergence risk.

## Finding

The bullet that pins `pi.setActiveTools` / `pi.getActiveTools` runs the loom 1.0.0 normative snapshot/restore protocol (the synchronous-and-atomic precondition, the cooperative-`await` evidence trail, the SDK surface-inventory coverage, the closed loom 1.0.0 driver set) directly into a multi-clause description of a *defensive per-extension-instance runtime mutex* that loom 1.0.0 does **not** ship on a pass outcome of the [`version-bump-step2.md` item (e)](../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md#bump-checklist-slash-dispatch-serialisation) audit recipe. The contingent material is substantial — it covers the `<a id="recovery-mutex-runtime-contract"></a>` runtime contract (strictly serialising FIFO `await`, no steady-state timeout), the `session_shutdown` teardown interaction (holder cancellation under sub-step 2's `loomAbort.abort()`, queued-waiter fall-through under sub-step 3's `SHUTDOWN_AWAIT_CAP_MS`), the keying granularity (factory-captured `pi: ExtensionAPI`), and the forward-compatibility invariants that govern it — and is written in the same MUST/declarative voice as the surrounding loom 1.0.0 obligations.

The contingency is named only in a parenthetical mid-paragraph (*"the mutex exists only when the recovery path is engaged on a fail outcome of the [item (e)](…) audit recipe — loom 1.0 ships without it on a pass outcome…"*). There is no callout, no sub-heading, no typographic break, and no scope marker delimiting where the loom 1.0.0 MUSTs end and the contingent recovery-path contract begins. A reasonable implementer scanning the bullet for what they must build at loom 1.0.0 has to infer scope from a clause buried two sentences into the contract's keying-granularity discussion; an implementer who skims past that parenthetical will read the entire FIFO/teardown/keying contract as a loom 1.0.0 obligation and build a mutex the spec does not require to ship.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Partition the affected `pi.setActiveTools snapshots are name lists…` bullet into two adjacent blocks. The existing loom 1.0.0 normative material (signatures, surface-inventory coverage, the two behavioural preconditions, the cooperative-`await` evidence trail, the closed loom 1.0.0 driver set, the precondition (b)/Pi-version-bump re-validation hook) stays under the present bullet; the recovery-mutex contract becomes a clearly delimited follow-on block whose opening sentence labels it as a contingent recovery path — e.g. a `**Contingent — recovery path on a fail outcome of item (e); not shipped at loom 1.0.0:**` lead-in, or an indented sub-bullet headed *"Contingent — recovery path on a fail outcome of [item (e)](…), not shipped at loom 1.0.0"*. The `<a id="recovery-mutex-runtime-contract"></a>` anchor stays on the contingent block so existing back-references from `version-bump-step2.md`, `V15d-prompt-suspend-snapshot.md`, and any other linking site continue to resolve. This fix touches `tool-registration-lifetime.md` only.

This in-place partition is preferred over relocating the contract to the version-bump page: the recovery-mutex contract's runtime semantics (what it wraps, the snapshot/restore sequence it serialises, the `Checkpoint` seam its queued waiters land at, the page-pinned one-`pi`-per-extension-instance lifetime its keying depends on) all reference machinery defined on this page, so co-locating the contract with that machinery costs only a one-line scope marker, where a relocation would cost an anchor relocation plus a cross-page inbound-link sweep plus a context split between the mutex and the sequence it wraps.

Edge cases for the implementer:

- The scope marker must explicitly cover the *whole* contingent block (FIFO-`await` runtime contract + teardown-interaction sentence + keying-granularity invariant). The current parenthetical scoping inside the keying-granularity sentence is what creates the ambiguity; pulling that scope statement to a block-leading marker is the load-bearing edit.
- Preserve the `<a id="recovery-mutex-runtime-contract"></a>` anchor on the contingent block. Inbound back-references from `version-bump-step2.md` item (e) and from `V15d-prompt-suspend-snapshot.md` rely on it; moving or renaming the anchor turns this presentational fix into an inbound-link sweep.
- The precondition-(b) re-validation sentence at the end of the bullet (*"If either Pi behavioural precondition above weakens in a future Pi minor…"*) is loom 1.0.0 normative — it stays in the non-contingent block above the marker, not under it.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer building the loom 1.0.0 runtime against this bullet may construct a per-extension-instance mutex with FIFO-await semantics, the teardown interaction, and the keying invariants — none of which loom 1.0.0 actually requires to ship until and unless a future version-bump item (e) audit returns a fail outcome. The wasted effort is bounded, but it is wasted, and the surrounding text gives no signal that the work is contingent. A second observable consequence is review-time: lenses that key on "normative MUSTs in continuous prose" cannot distinguish this block's contingent obligations from the genuine loom 1.0.0 obligations, generating recurring spec-review noise on every pass over this bullet.

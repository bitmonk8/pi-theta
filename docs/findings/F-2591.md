## Finding

`overview.md` coins **query template** in bold in the Query-and-Await section ("The primitive that crosses code → model is the **query template** — an `@`-prefixed backtick template"). Bolding is the corpus's coining marker, yet there is no glossary entry for the term, and the canonical feature page it would point to is titled simply "Query" (`query.md`), whose own opening line uses "query template" in plain prose with no anchor or definition. The glossary's stated admission rule — "Add new entries here when the spec coins a new term that is reused on more than one page" — is squarely met: the bolded form recurs across at least ten pages (`expressions.md`, `grammar.md`, `lexical.md`, `influences.md`, `implementation-notes.md`, the `frontmatter/*` and `query/*` topic files, and the parse-diagnostic registry), so the omission is a glossary-completeness gap, not a one-off.

Compounding the gap, the construct is referred to by three different names with no declared equivalence: **query template** (the bolded coinage and the dominant form), **query** (the page title and much of `overview.md`'s own prose), and **query expression** (`query/query-forms.md`, and the `loom/load/typed-query-unsupported-provider` diagnostic). A reader meeting the bolded coinage cannot tell whether "query template" is the formal name of the `@`...`` construct, an informal descriptor for the back-tick literal that introduces a "query", or a synonym for "query expression". Sibling coinages from the same `overview.md` section — *woven artifact*, *type sink* — both received glossary entries; this one was skipped.

Subsequent commits added many glossary entries to resolve identical coined-term-without-entry findings (e.g. *surface-set closure*, *PIC*, *type sink* refinements) without ever adding this one.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Formalise: add a glossary entry and a canonical anchor

- **Approach:** Treat "query template" as the formal name of the `@`...`` construct. Add a `query template` glossary entry pointing at `query.md`; give `query.md` an anchor at its opening definition the entry can target; standardise on "query template" for the construct and reserve "query" for the act/result of issuing one.
- **Spec edits:** Add a `query template` entry to `glossary.md` (alphabetised), `See:` `query.md`; add an explicit anchor + one-line definition at the top of `query.md`; optionally normalise the stray "query expression" usages in `query/query-forms.md` to "query template" or note them as equivalent.
- **Pros:** Matches the corpus's dominant usage and its uniform "every reused coinage gets a glossary entry" pattern; smallest-surface edit (one entry + one anchor); keeps the bolded coinage meaningful.
- **Cons:** Pins a fourth formal noun where some prose currently gets by with "query".
- **Risks:** Leaving "query expression" un-reconciled would still leave a two-name residue; sweep it in the same edit.

### Option B — Demote: drop the coinage, standardise on plain prose

- **Approach:** Remove the bold from `overview.md`, treating "query template" as an informal descriptor of the `@`...`` back-tick literal, and standardise the construct's name on "query" / "query expression" across the corpus.
- **Spec edits:** Unbold `overview.md`'s "query template"; sweep the ≥10 pages and the diagnostic registry to a single chosen name.
- **Pros:** Removes the formal-term ambiguity by denying formal-term status.
- **Cons:** Wide-surface churn across many pages and the diagnostic registry; fights the corpus's actual dominant usage; risks introducing fresh inconsistency mid-sweep.
- **Risks:** High edit fan-out across registry rows and topic pages raises the chance of a missed occurrence.

### Recommendation

Take Option A. "query template" is already the dominant name across the corpus and the construct meets the glossary's own admission criterion, so adding one glossary entry plus a canonical anchor on `query.md` is both the lighter edit and the one consistent with how every other reused coinage is handled. While doing so, reconcile the stray "query expression" occurrences (`query/query-forms.md`, the typed-query load diagnostic) to the formal term or flag them as equivalent so no third name lingers.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader cannot determine whether "query template" is a formal term, an informal descriptor, or a synonym for "query"/"query expression", and has no single definition site to resolve the three competing names against. Implementers can still build a working construct because its semantics are fully pinned on `query.md`; the cost is reader friction and continued terminology drift, against a corpus that otherwise gives every reused coinage a glossary entry.

## Finding

`V7a` is the canonical home for the system-note diagnostics surface. Its Adds defines `the `loom-system-note` `sendMessage` envelope with its renderer registration, and the fallback chain (`sendSystemNote` → `ctx.ui.notify` → `console.error`)`. The channel name `loom-system-note` and its fallback chain are the spec-owned, canonical terms for this seam.

`V9k`'s `pi.registerMessageRenderer`-failure Tests bullet refers to the same surface with the generic, non-canonical phrasing: "this extension instance's **system notes** permanently degrade to the `ctx.ui.notify` arm of the **System-notes fallback chain**". `V9k-T` carries the byte-identical phrasing. Neither uses the canonical `loom-system-note` channel name, and neither declares an inline equivalence.

Because the consumption site (`V9k`/`V9k-T`) and the owning site (`V7a`) use different terms for the same channel, a cross-search in either direction misses: searching for `loom-system-note` does not surface V9k's degrade-and-proceed behaviour, and searching for "system notes fallback chain" does not surface V7a's definition. `V9k` already declares `V7a` in its Deps, so the relationship is real; only the term is off.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V9k-extension-bootstrap-failures.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V9k-T-extension-bootstrap-failures.md`, rewrite the `pi.registerMessageRenderer`-failure Tests bullet to use V7a's canonical terms:

- Replace the generic "system notes" with the channel name `loom-system-note`.
- Replace "System-notes fallback chain" with a reference to the `loom-system-note` channel's fallback chain (the `sendSystemNote` → `ctx.ui.notify` → `console.error` chain V7a's Adds owns).

For example, the clause "this extension instance's system notes permanently degrade to the `ctx.ui.notify` arm of the System-notes fallback chain" becomes "this extension instance's `loom-system-note` channel permanently degrades to the `ctx.ui.notify` arm of its fallback chain". Keep the rest of the bullet (the `details.capability`/`details.error` assertions) unchanged. V7a remains read-only and authoritative for the channel definition; this fix only aligns the consumer's wording. The projected term index regenerates from the plan once the leaf text is aligned, so a V9k occurrence entry for `loom-system-note` follows from the rename — no separate index hand-edit is required.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader or maintainer cross-referencing the `loom-system-note` channel between its V7a owner and its V9k consumer hits a search miss in both directions, and the divergent term invites further drift as the surface is touched. The behavioural contract is unaffected, so an implementer can still produce a working V9k leaf.

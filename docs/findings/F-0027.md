## Problem

The *REQ-ID discipline* bullet in `docs/plan_topics/conventions.md` uses two distinct phrases for one coverage-gate mechanism: "loom 1.0 closing gate" (the gate exercised against seeded fixtures at `H5a`) and "loom 1.0 release gate" (the same gate on its live-corpus footing, activated by `H6a`). Both names appear inside that one bullet, but no sentence states they denote one gate in two footings. The equivalence exists only in `H6a` (its heading, Convention, and Adds lines), so a reader working from `conventions.md` — the rule's canonical home — cannot tell whether "closing gate" and "release gate" are one gate in two states or two separate gates.

## Solution approach

Add one equivalence statement to the *REQ-ID discipline* bullet in `docs/plan_topics/conventions.md` that ties "loom 1.0 release gate" to "loom 1.0 closing gate" as its live-corpus footing, owned by `H6a`. Place it where the bullet first introduces "release gate" — the un-anchored-MUST / citing-test live-corpus clause — so every reader of the bullet sees it, and forward-link `H6a` ([`H6a-live-corpus-activation.md`](./H6a-live-corpus-activation.md)) as the activation leaf.

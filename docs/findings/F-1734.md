## Finding

The `*Routing obligation for future ceilings*` and `*Release gate*` paragraphs on `ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md` carry exhaustive change-management procedure for future spec-editors: the three-case introduction taxonomy (default-axis, split-axis, new-axis), the four-trajectory retirement rewrite forms (i succeed / ii fall through to a `NOCEIL-N` / iii fall through to another ceiling / iv non-terminating absent cancellation), the SHA-or-tag pointer form, the three-component per-axis-entry composition rule, and the per-operation release-gate acceptance criteria. The page is the in-scope home for the NOCEIL-1…NOCEIL-4 claims, the audit methodology, and the four-axis closure list, but the operational machinery for amending that list is a GOV-30 / `*Operational definitions*` concern that the governance corpus already owns.

GOV-30 (`docs/spec_topics/governance/req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md`) defines the lock-step convention this paragraph invokes, and `*Operational definitions*` (`docs/spec_topics/governance/source-language-stability.md`) already defines the four ceiling-set verbs (`Introduce` / `Retire` / `Relax` / `Tighten`), the in-place retirement-annotated-stub rule, the SHA-or-tag pointer form, the cardinality-after-retirement constraint, and the post-retirement-trajectory enumeration the ceiling page now re-states. The inline expansion is therefore a near-verbatim restatement of the governance rules, with two governance pages and one ceiling page each carrying overlapping prose for the same operational regime — exactly the drift surface GOV-30 was introduced to consolidate. The four-trajectory enumeration in particular appears on both pages with slightly different wording (the ceiling page adds "operator-visible behaviour change" framing on trajectory (iv); `*Operational definitions*` does not), and the SHA-or-tag pointer form is stated twice with the ceiling-page copy chained through a cross-link rather than asserted independently — the canonical risk pattern under GOV-30's own undecidability disclaimer.

The substantive content that genuinely belongs on the ceiling page is the four-axis closure list itself (its membership, its routing destinations for each axis, the four out-of-closure fall-through shapes), the NOCEIL-1…NOCEIL-4 claims, and the high-level routing MUST that any future runtime resource surface either as a new ceiling-list entry or as a documented `NOCEIL-N` fall-through. The per-operation editor checklist (how to amend the per-axis entry on each verb, what the release-gate acceptance criterion is, what pointer form to use) is governance machinery whose stable home is on the governance pages.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Reduce the two ceiling-page paragraphs to the substantive routing rule and a GOV-30 cross-link, and migrate the per-operation editor checklist + release-gate acceptance criteria into a single canonical home on the governance corpus.

Concretely, on `docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings/ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md`:

- Keep the `*Audit methodology*` heading, the `<a id="four-axis-closure-list"></a>` block (its four axes plus the four out-of-closure fall-through shapes), and the NOCEIL-1…NOCEIL-4 claims.
- Replace the `*Routing obligation for future ceilings*` paragraph with a short normative rule: "Any future runtime resource the spec might consider ceilinging MUST surface either as a new entry in the four-item ceiling list at [`spec.md` — Hard ceilings] or as a documented fall-through to one of the four `NOCEIL-1` … `NOCEIL-4` claims above (each `NOCEIL-N` claim names the documented fall-through for its axis); a runtime resource that does neither is a closure defect under this paragraph. The per-operation editor procedure for introducing, retiring, or relaxing a ceiling — including the per-axis-entry rewrite forms, the SHA-or-tag retirement-pointer form, and the lock-step release-gate acceptance criteria — is specified at [Governance — Operational definitions](../governance/source-language-stability.md#operational-definitions) under the GOV-30 lock-step convention." Keep the four-axis closure list update as a same-commit obligation by retaining the existing one-sentence GOV-30 cross-link at the end of the section (the *Five-site list co-edit obligation* paragraph already establishes that authoring pattern on the same page).
- Delete the `*Release gate*` paragraph here; its acceptance criteria are governance machinery that belong adjacent to the verbs they gate.

On `docs/spec_topics/governance/source-language-stability.md`, extend `*Operational definitions*` to carry the migrated procedural content: the three-case `Introduce` taxonomy (default-axis, split-axis, new-axis), the four `Retire` trajectories (succeed / fall-through to `NOCEIL-N` / fall-through to another ceiling / non-terminating absent cancellation), the three required components of each per-axis entry (axis, routing destination, justification), and the per-operation release-gate acceptance criteria. Where this duplicates existing `*Operational definitions*` prose (the SHA-or-tag pointer form, the in-place retirement-annotated-stub rule, the cardinality-after-retirement constraint), reconcile to a single statement rather than restating it. Either site (the governance page or a dedicated sub-page extracted from it) can host the migrated block; the governance page is preferred because it already owns the verbs and the GOV-30 lock-step convention, and a sub-page split would re-create exactly the cross-page drift surface this finding identifies. Author the migration in a single commit so the inline expansion is removed from the ceiling page in the same change that lands the canonical home; otherwise the corpus briefly carries three copies instead of two.

Implementer-visible edge cases:

- Retirement trajectory (iv)'s "operator-visible behaviour change" framing currently lives only on the ceiling-page copy; preserve it on the migrated-to home, since it is the only place this categorisation is recorded.
- The cross-link "the methodology paragraph and the four-axis closure list are themselves under the GOV-30 lock-step co-edit obligation" at the tail of the current paragraph must survive in some form on the ceiling page — it is the hook that brings the closure list itself into GOV-30 scope, independent of what the verbs do.
- GOV-30's "currently:" enumeration names the `hard-ceilings.md` cross-ceiling content (HC3-a…HC3-e, ceiling #4's per-boundary table, CIO-1…CIO-6, NOCEIL-1…NOCEIL-4, worked consequences, four-axis Audit methodology) explicitly; the migration must not change that enumeration's referent set — the four-axis closure list and audit methodology stay on the ceiling page.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A future spec-editor performing an `Introduce` / `Retire` / `Relax` operation must read and reconcile three overlapping prose blocks — the ceiling-page `*Routing obligation*` / `*Release gate*` paragraphs, the GOV-30 lock-step convention, and the `*Operational definitions*` verbs section — that already disagree on framing (the ceiling page adds operator-visibility framing on retirement trajectory (iv); `*Operational definitions*` does not). The duplication is invisible to mechanical checks (per GOV-30's own undecidability disclaimer) so drift accumulates silently across releases and is caught, if at all, only at the GOV-15 reviewer-inspection step the very edit is meant to satisfy.

## Problem

The `*Routing obligation for future ceilings*` and `*Release gate*` paragraphs on `ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md` carry the full per-operation change-management machinery for future ceiling edits: the three-case introduction taxonomy, the four retirement-trajectory rewrite forms, the SHA-or-tag pointer form, the three-component per-axis-entry rule, and the per-operation release-gate acceptance criteria. That machinery is a GOV-30 / Operational-definitions concern the governance corpus already owns — GOV-30 (`req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md#gov-30`) and Operational definitions (`source-language-stability.md#operational-definitions`) already define the four ceiling-set verbs, the in-place retirement-stub rule, the SHA-or-tag pointer form, the cardinality-after-retirement constraint, and the post-retirement trajectories the ceiling page re-states. The result is three overlapping prose blocks for one operational regime that already disagree on framing (the ceiling page adds "operator-visible behaviour change" framing on retirement trajectory (iv); Operational definitions does not) — an undecidable drift surface GOV-30 was introduced to consolidate.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commit:** `2ff03c2` (2026-05-10, *pi-loom spec: resolve "Source-language stability bullet leaves equivalence undefined at the aggregator and silently contradicts the open-set ceiling clause"*)
**History:** The entire `*Audit methodology*` / four-axis closure list / `*Routing obligation for future ceilings*` / `*loom 1.0.0 rejected-candidate record*` / `*Release gate*` block was added wholesale to `docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings.md` in `2ff03c2` (`git log -G "Routing obligation for future ceilings" --all --reverse` first match). The three-case introduction taxonomy, the four-trajectory retirement rewrite forms (i)–(iv), the SHA-or-tag pointer form, and the release-gate acceptance criteria were all present at first introduction — they were never staged in separately. Subsequent commits (`81bf52a` collapsed `spec/` into `spec_topics/` and relocated the block to `hard-ceilings/ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md`; `f5e89f4` enforced the size cap; `7995d3c` and `fe8aa5c` did the GOV-15 / GOV-12→GOV-30 relettering across the corpus) edited cross-references and labels but left the inline expansion in place. The over-broad placement is therefore an inception-time scoping choice in `2ff03c2`, not drift accumulated through later edits, and the parallel content in `*Operational definitions*` on `source-language-stability.md` was added across `2ff03c2` and follow-on commits without ever being deduplicated against the ceiling-page copy.

## Solution approach

Narrow the `***Routing obligation for future ceilings***` paragraph on `ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md` to the substantive routing MUST (every future runtime resource surfaces as a new ceiling-list entry or as a documented `NOCEIL-1` … `NOCEIL-4` fall-through), keeping the `<a id="four-axis-closure-list"></a>` list, the `NOCEIL` claims, and the GOV-30 lock-step co-edit cross-link, and forward-link the per-operation editor procedure to Operational definitions (`source-language-stability.md#operational-definitions`). Delete the `<a id="release-gate"></a>` `***Release gate.***` paragraph. Migrate the per-operation editor machinery — introduction taxonomy, retirement trajectories, per-axis-entry components, and release-gate acceptance criteria — into Operational definitions, reconciling rather than restating the prose it already carries (SHA-or-tag pointer form, retirement-stub rule, cardinality constraint).

## Solution constraints

- The inline-expansion removal from the ceiling page and the canonical-home authoring on the governance page MUST land in a single commit per GOV-30 lock-step; otherwise the corpus transiently carries three copies of the procedural regime.
- Do not change the referent set of GOV-30's "currently:" enumeration (`req-id-prefix-table-active-b.md#gov-30`): the four-axis closure list and audit methodology stay on the ceiling page and are not migrated to governance.
- Preserve retirement trajectory (iv)'s "operator-visible behaviour change" framing on the migration target — it is the only record of that categorisation.

## Finding

`V11e` ("Binder system-note rendering and determinism") closes two unrelated spec surfaces in a single leaf. Its **Spec** field cites both `binder/defaulting-system-note-echo.md` (the five system-note line-discipline rules — single-line collapse, 120-code-point cap, prefix/suffix grammar, empty-content→malformed-envelope classification, `ambiguous.candidates` non-surfacing) and `binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md` (the `temperature:0` + FNV-1a seed-derivation contract). The two concerns share no inputs, no rendering path, and no spec page; the coverage matrix already records them as two separate rows (`defaulting-system-note-echo.md §System-note rendering | V11e` and `determinism-cancellation-failure.md §Determinism | V11e`).

The coupling is mild — both belong to the binder and the leaf remains implementable — but the determinism contract is the odd member: every other obligation in `V11e` derives from `defaulting-system-note-echo.md`, while the determinism MUSTs are the only tenant of `determinism-cancellation-failure.md` that lives outside `V11f`, which already owns that page.

This is an editorial cohesion issue, not a correctness or sequencing defect. The leaf can be implemented and tested as written.

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** `c6a664e` (2026-06-10, "pi-loom plan: build/update plan for spec.md + review")

Later commits (`475d018` expanding the rendering rules from two to five, `cb8737a` an over-bundling sweep) refined the leaf but did not introduce the two-page coupling — it dates to the initial plan build.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Relocate the determinism contract to the leaf that owns its spec page

**Approach:** Move the `temperature:0` + FNV-1a determinism obligation out of `V11e` and into `V11f`, which already cites `determinism-cancellation-failure.md`. `V11e` narrows to the five system-note rendering rules sourced from `defaulting-system-note-echo.md`.

**Plan edits:**
- In `V11e-system-note-determinism.md` and `V11e-T-system-note-determinism.md`: drop `determinism-cancellation-failure.md` from **Spec**, delete the `determinism — binder determinism` Tests bullet, and remove the FNV-1a/`temperature:0` clause from **Adds** and **Ships when**.
- In `V11f-binder-retry-taxonomy.md` and `V11f-T-binder-retry-taxonomy.md`: add a Tests bullet asserting the FNV-1a seed derivation and `temperature:0`-on-every-call MUSTs, and name them in **Adds**/**Ships when**. `V11f` already lists the page in **Spec**.
- In `coverage-matrix.md`: re-point the `binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md §Determinism — temperature: 0 + FNV-1a seed-derivation MUSTs` row's closing-leaf cell from `V11e` to the receiving leaf.

**Pros:** Co-locates the determinism MUSTs with the single leaf owning `determinism-cancellation-failure.md`; `V11e` becomes a clean single-page leaf.
**Cons:** Adds a third concern to `V11f`, which is itself flagged as over-bundled and recommended for splitting; the relocation should target the post-split retry leaf rather than today's monolithic `V11f`.
**Risks:** If `V11f`'s split is not yet decided, the determinism bullet lands on a leaf that will itself be re-divided, forcing a second move.

### Option B — Retain V11e as the binder's deterministic-output leaf

**Approach:** Keep both concerns in `V11e`, treating the leaf as "everything the binder renders deterministically" (the rendered system note plus the seed/temperature that make output reproducible). Make the grouping explicit rather than incidental.

**Plan edits:**
- No structural change. Optionally add one sentence to `V11e`'s **Adds** naming the editorial grouping rationale so the two-page citation reads as intentional.

**Pros:** Zero churn; the leaf remains implementable and the coupling is genuinely mild; avoids loading determinism onto an already-large `V11f`.
**Cons:** The determinism MUSTs stay split from `V11f`'s ownership of the same spec page; review surface unchanged.
**Risks:** None material.

### Recommendation

Adopt **Option A**, but sequence it after the `V11f` split is decided so the determinism bullet lands on the stable retry-side leaf rather than today's monolithic `V11f`. If the `V11f` split is rejected or deferred, **Option B** is acceptable — the coupling is mild enough that retaining it costs little. Either way, keep the `V11e`/`V11e-T` and (under Option A) `V11f`/`V11f-T` Tests bullets aligned 1:1, and re-point the coverage-matrix determinism row to whichever leaf ends up asserting it.

A reviewer of `V11e` must verify two unrelated spec surfaces in one pass, and the `determinism-cancellation-failure.md` obligations are split across two leaves (`V11e` and `V11f`) rather than co-located. Nothing breaks if shipped unfixed; the cost is review surface and weaker spec-page cohesion.

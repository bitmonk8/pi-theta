## Finding

`V9g` implements the `session_shutdown` handler — the five-sub-step fixed teardown sequence per [`patch-skew-degradation.md`](../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/patch-skew-degradation.md). Sub-step 4 ("Close watchers") calls `discoveryWatcher.close()`, `settingsWatcher.close()`, and cancels any pending debounce timer handle via `Clock.clearTimeout`. The 250 ms reload-debounce timer lifecycle (drop-and-reschedule `Clock.setTimeout`/`clearTimeout` handle management) is owned by `V10d`; the discovery (chokidar) watcher is owned by `V9b`'s registration step 5.

`V9g`'s `Deps` are `V9g-T, V9a, V9e, V9h, V17a`. The discovery-watcher owner `V9b` is incidentally reachable through `V9e → V9b`, but `V10d` is reachable from no `V9g` dependency path — and cannot be, since `V10d` is downstream of `V9g` (`V10d`'s own `Deps` are `V10d-T, V10c, V8d, V9b`). So the leaf that owns the debounce-handle lifecycle sub-step 4 cancels is neither named nor reachable.

The plan never states whether `V9g`'s teardown handler is expected to hold real watcher/debounce references (which would require the producing leaves to land first) or whether sub-step 4 operates purely on injected seams (the `FileWatcher` `Unsubscribe` handles and the injected `Clock` seam) supplied through the DI seam set, with the real settings-watcher and 250 ms debounce wiring owned and integrated at `V10d`/`V9b`. The two readings imply different `Deps` graphs and different ship orderings.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** `c6a664e` (pi-loom plan: build/update plan for spec.md + review, 2026-06-10)
**History:** `V9g` was authored at the initial plan build `c6a664e` with `Deps` `V9g-T, V9e, V9h, V17a` and an Adds block already naming the five-sub-step teardown sequence — so the handler has referenced the spec's sub-step-4 watcher/debounce cleanup, without reaching the leaf that owns the debounce lifecycle, since the leaf first existed. At inception the debounce lived in `V10c`, which `V9g` did not reach either; the later `V10c`→`V10d` split (`f14e929`, 2026-06-16) only re-homed the owning leaf from `V10c` to `V10d` and did not introduce the gap. The omission is original to the leaf, not a regression from a specific later edit.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Clarify that sub-step 4 operates on injected seams; no completion-tag Deps edge

**Approach:** Add a sentence to `V9g`'s **Adds** stating that sub-step 4's `discoveryWatcher.close()` / `settingsWatcher.close()` / `Clock.clearTimeout(debounce)` act on the injected `FileWatcher` `Unsubscribe` handle(s) and the injected `Clock` seam, and that the production construction/lifecycle of the settings-file watcher and the 250 ms reload debounce is owned by `V10d` (debounce handle) and the discovery watcher by `V9b`'s registration step 5 — integrated at those leaves, so `V9g` requires no completion-tag `Deps` edge to `V10d`.

**Plan edits:** `V9g` Adds gains the seam-ownership clarification sentence; `Deps` unchanged.

**Spec edits:** None.

**Pros:** Keeps `V9g`'s slice ordering intact; consistent with the DI-seam architecture and with `V9g`'s tests, which drive shutdown against doubles; mirrors how the discovery-watcher reference is handled without a direct `V9g → V9b` edge.

**Cons:** Leaves a cross-leaf integration responsibility (binding the real settings-watcher/debounce handles into the live teardown handler) implicit rather than gated by a `Deps` tag.

**Risks:** If no leaf observably wires the real handles into the production handler, the live teardown could silently no-op against a real settings watcher; mitigated only if the integration owner (`V10d`/`V9b`) is explicitly named.

### Option B — Declare a production-wiring dependency to V10d

**Approach:** Add `V10d` (and, for symmetry, lean on the existing `V9e → V9b` reach) to a path that `V9g` reaches, so the leaf that owns the debounce-handle lifecycle is complete before `V9g` ships, and the teardown handler can bind real references.

**Plan edits:** Add `V10d` to `V9g`'s `Deps`.

**Spec edits:** None.

**Pros:** Makes the resource-ownership dependency explicit and tag-gated.

**Cons:** Creates a backward dependency on a leaf that is itself downstream of `V9g` (`V10d` `Deps` `V10c, V8d, V9b`), re-sequencing `V9g` to land after the entire V10 settings chain; contradicts the seam-injection model under which `V9g` is testable in isolation against doubles.

**Risks:** Pushes `V9g` late in the build order for a reference-binding concern the DI seams already abstract; risks a churned `Deps` graph for no executability gain.

### Recommendation

Take **Option A**. State in `V9g`'s **Adds** that sub-step 4 operates on the injected `FileWatcher`/`Clock` seam handles, and that the production settings-watcher and 250 ms debounce lifecycle is owned by `V10d` and the discovery watcher by `V9b`'s registration step 5 — so `V9g` carries no completion-tag `Deps` edge to `V10d`. This matches the DI-seam architecture (`V9g` ships and is tested against doubles) and avoids inverting the slice order against a downstream leaf. Edge case the author must watch: name `V10d` (debounce handle) and `V9b` (discovery watcher) explicitly as the integration owners so the live-handle wiring is not left unowned.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Two reasonable implementers diverge: one reads sub-step 4's `settingsWatcher.close()` / `Clock.clearTimeout(debounce)` as requiring `V9g` to hold real references and adds a backward `V9g → V10d` completion-tag `Deps` edge, inverting the slice order against a leaf that is itself downstream of `V9g`; the other ships `V9g` against injected doubles with no such edge. Both produce a working leaf, but the wiring ownership of the production settings-watcher/debounce teardown is left unstated and unguarded.

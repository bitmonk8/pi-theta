## Finding

The *Provider-owned-wording presupposition* paragraph in `provider-error-mapping.md` ends with the sentence:

> See *Re-validation gate (loom 1.0.0)* above for the corresponding post-loom 1.0.0 maintenance follow-up.

That sentence carries no normative content — it neither states an obligation, names a presupposition, nor refines the surrounding fixture-rerun guidance. Its sole purpose is to point readers up at the "Wiring this re-validation into the bump procedure as a mechanical gate is a recognised post-loom 1.0.0 maintenance follow-up" sentence in the Re-validation gate. The preceding sentence already routes fixture rerun through item (i) of the editorial-review checklist, which is the load-bearing wire. The back-pointer is pure navigation.

Once the sibling finding ("Re-validation gate — future-work 'post-loom 1.0.0 maintenance follow-up' sentence inline") removes the future-work sentence from the Re-validation gate, this back-pointer references text that no longer exists on the page, becoming dangling. Even with the target left in place, the pointer adds zero new information; it is editorial cruft.

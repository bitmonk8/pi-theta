## Finding

The `loom 1.0 seam — `system:` expression sublanguage` blockquote in `frontmatter-fields-b-and-templates.md` restates the *Path grammar*, *Parser entry point*, and *Parse errors* prose that immediately precede it; its non-normative implementation note explicitly declares itself "not an observable conformance point." The only non-duplicated payload is the blockquote's two anchors.

### Recommendation

Merge, mirroring the resolution applied to the imports.md Resolver seam: place the two anchor ids (`#loom-1-0-seam-system-expression-sublanguage`, `#v1-seam-system-expression-sublanguage`) on the existing *Parser entry point* paragraph (which already states the reuse-the-shared-entry-point-plus-parser-filter mechanism and the deferred-extension seam), then delete the duplicate blockquote. ## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

FRNT-2/FRNT-3 are normative MUSTs the corpus's own GOV-18 forbids (they bind "implementers"), so they stand as internal self-consistency defects a GOV-18 audit flags, and they give the false impression of an enforceable conformance check that no runtime can discharge. The `system:` seam blockquote is redundant prose that enlarges the maintenance surface (a future wording change must be kept in sync across the blockquote and the paragraphs it restates). Neither defect changes any runtime behaviour or diagnostic; implementers can still produce a working system.

## Finding

`V9a`'s **Adds** field describes `FACTORY_PROBABLE_CAPABILITIES` as "the closed five-element list of factory-probable capability identifiers (inventory items 1/2/3/4/6) **the probe iterates**". The relative clause "the probe iterates" attaches to the five-element list, asserting that the probe walks five targets. The spec contradicts this: [`capability-probe.md` step (c)](../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/capability-probe.md) directs the probe to "Check the **nine named function members** listed below â€¦ in the listed order", and states explicitly "five capabilities, nine function members â€” capability 1 contributes one, capability 2 contributes one, capabilities 3 and 6 each contribute two, and capability 4 contributes three". The five-element list is the *capability-identifier partition* that `V18a`/`V18c` reconcile against; it is not the probe's iteration target.

The word "five" is overloaded three ways in this neighbourhood, which is what makes the prose dangerous: (1) the five probe sub-steps (a)â€“(e), which `PIC-5` pins ("there are exactly five checks"); (2) the five factory-probable capabilities (items 1/2/3/4/6), which `FACTORY_PROBABLE_CAPABILITIES` enumerates; and (3) the nine function members iterated within step (c). The Adds clause collapses (2) and (3), telling the implementer the probe iterates the five-element capability list.

An implementer who builds the probe from this clause produces a five-target loop and silently omits four function members (capability 3's second member `AgentSession.prototype.abort`, capability 4's `pi.setActiveTools`/`pi.getActiveTools` and second `register` member, capability 6's `pi.sendMessage`) â€” losing both coverage and the spec's normative first-failure short-circuit determinism, on which the `details.member` conformance contract depends.

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V9a-capability-probe.md`, edit the **Adds** field so the relative clause "the probe iterates" no longer attaches to the five-element identifier list. Separate the two distinct sets:

- The five-element list (`FACTORY_PROBABLE_CAPABILITIES`, inventory items 1/2/3/4/6) is the capability-identifier partition `V18a`/`V18c` reconcile against â€” it does not describe the probe's iteration.
- The probe's step-(c) loop iterates the nine named function members (capability 1 â†’ 1, capability 2 â†’ 1, capabilities 3 and 6 â†’ 2 each, capability 4 â†’ 3) in the spec's fixed normative order.

Concretely, replace the clause "the closed five-element list of factory-probable capability identifiers (inventory items 1/2/3/4/6) the probe iterates" with text that names the five-element list as the reconciliation partition for `V18a`/`V18c` and, separately, states that step (c) iterates the nine function members those five capabilities contribute, in the spec's fixed order. The spec is the canonical source of truth and is read-only for this fix; do not alter `capability-probe.md`.

## Finding

The second paragraph of `## Binder model` in `binder/binder-model-and-context.md` opens with a one-clause statement that `looms.binderModel` hot-reload re-resolves on next load, then — without any sub-heading, callout, or topic break — proceeds to specify a full operational-notification feature: the verbatim `loom watcher: looms.binderModel changed; …; run /reload to retry: <names>` template, the `details: { recovery: { looms: string[] } }` payload, a claim that this is "the fourth shape on the disjoint-by-key `loom-system-note` channel," `recovery.looms` content rules, `<N>` and `<names>` substitution rules, the verbatim-literal `loom(s)` / `looms.binderModel` / `/reload` invariants, the `recovery.looms.length === 0` suppression rule, and the 250 ms settings-file debounce coalescing rule.

That body is a recovery-notification feature in its own right, not a clarifying detail about binder-model resolution. The `## Binder model` subsection is structured around load-time resolution and the strict-capability probe; the surrounding prose gives the reader no signpost that an operator-facing notification surface is about to be defined, and the recovery-note material is too large to fit comfortably as a parenthetical on the resolution path. A reader scanning for "how do I know when settings changes unblock previously-failed looms?" cannot find this content by heading or by cross-reference target name — the only handle is the inline `<a id="binder-model-hot-reload">` anchor.

The shard inherits this from `binder.md` and `spec.md`, which carry no `## Goals` / `## Scope` / `## Non-goals` inventories the omitted feature could have been listed against. The real defect is structural rather than missing-from-an-inventory: a coherent operational feature is wedged into a paragraph whose declared subject is something else, and gives a downstream `runtime-event-channel.md` shape table a back-reference target buried mid-paragraph rather than a discoverable section.

## Solution Space

**State:** shaped

### Recommendation

Promote the recovery-note body to its own `### Hot-reload recovery note` sub-section. Split the current `## Binder model` second paragraph at the existing `<a id="binder-model-hot-reload">` anchor. Keep the first clause ("Hot-reload of Pi settings (`looms.binderModel` changed at runtime) re-resolves on the next loom load; it does not retroactively fix already-failed loads.") in `## Binder model`. Move every byte from "When the change would let a previously…" through "…no `loom/load/*` code is registered for it." into a new `### Hot-reload recovery note` heading immediately after the truncation-rule sub-section, retaining the inline `<a id="binder-model-hot-reload">` anchor verbatim on the new heading.

This is a single-file edit to `binder/binder-model-and-context.md`: no edits to `runtime-event-channel.md` (its `details: { recovery }` row already back-links via the existing anchor, which the move preserves), and no edits to `V11a` or `coverage-matrix.md` (the fragment they cite is unchanged). It preserves locality — recovery-note resolution is conceptually a tail of binder-model resolution — and makes the feature discoverable in the file's heading structure and the `binder.md` Contents-list rendering, with zero plan / coverage churn.

Edge case the implementer must watch: keep the inline `<a id="binder-model-hot-reload">` anchor on the new heading verbatim so `V11a-T-binder-model-resolution.md`, `V11a-binder-model-resolution.md`, `coverage-matrix.md` row `cka-38`, and `runtime-event-channel.md`'s `details: { recovery }` back-link all continue to resolve.

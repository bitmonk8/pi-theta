## Finding

`docs/spec_topics/schema-subset.md` introduces the loom-defined JSON Schema subset with a sentence-then-bullet-list structure. Two of those normative units are interrupted mid-stride by `*(Non-normative provenance.)*` blocks that recount why the subset and its draft were chosen at loom 1.0 authoring time:

- The intro paragraph (line 3) transitions from "It targets a **fixed, loom-defined subset** of JSON Schema, pinned by this spec and listed below" into a multi-clause sentence describing the OpenAI / Anthropic strict-mode origin of that subset, then continues with "The normative subset loom emits and enforces is:" before the bullet list starts.
- The **Draft** bullet (line 14) states the normative rule (Draft 2020-12, evaluation semantics), then opens a `*(Non-normative provenance.)*` clause re-describing the provider-acceptance background within the same bullet.

Both blocks self-disclaim as non-normative, describe historical design intent that the spec has explicitly already removed from the live contract (the surrounding prose pins the subset as "loom-owned … not a live claim about either provider's current requirements"), and constrain nothing. They sit inside the runs of text a reader scans for the next rule, which forces the reader to parse out history while looking for normative content. This is the same `Rationale:` / decision-log embedment pattern flagged elsewhere in the review (QRY-6, non-validation follow-up, frontmatter), but here it appears at the entry point of the most-cited normative inventory in the corpus.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-then-extended
**Introducing commits:**
- `29e4faa` (2026-06-05, "pi-loom spec-review: park T56") — first introduced both `*(Non-normative provenance.)*` blocks at their current sites in the same edit that demoted the previously-normative "lowest common denominator of OpenAI Structured Outputs … and Anthropic tool-use `input_schema`" framing to provenance.
- `7f217a8` (2026-06-07, "pi-loom spec: resolve 'Language-core hidden assumptions: enum-tag sidecar, AJV-config silence, Markdown claim'") — extended the Draft bullet's provenance block with the additional "All occurrences of 'validates' / 'is accepted by the validator' …" sentence, leaving the inline placement of the provenance clause unchanged.

**History:** The defect entered as a *side effect of a correct provenance demotion*: the earlier prose stated the subset was the lowest-common-denominator of two named providers' strict modes, which was a present-tense claim about those providers' live capabilities. The reframing rightly converted that claim into design-context background but parked the background inline at the original sites instead of relocating it to a Background/Rationale block, producing the current shape. Subsequent edits have continued to extend the inline provenance text rather than relocating it, so the pattern is hardening rather than self-resolving.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Lift both `*(Non-normative provenance.)*` clauses out of their current sites and consolidate them into a single `## Background` (or `## Rationale`) section appended after the normative list but before `## Depth Enforcement`. The intro paragraph collapses back to "Loom's `schema` keyword does **not** target the full JSON Schema standard. It targets a **fixed, loom-defined subset** of JSON Schema, pinned by this spec and listed below. The normative subset loom emits and enforces is:"; the **Draft** bullet collapses to "**Draft**: JSON Schema Draft 2020-12. loom fixes this draft for the schemas it emits. All occurrences of 'validates' / 'is accepted by the validator' in normative prose are evaluated against JSON Schema 2020-12 semantics; lowered schemas are evaluated under that draft both by loom and by any conforming validator." The relocated `## Background` section keeps both provenance paragraphs verbatim under sub-bullets ("Subset choice", "Draft choice"), preserving the loom-owned-not-tracking framing the current prose already establishes. Outright deletion is the lower-effort alternative but loses the loom-owned framing that explains *why* the spec is non-tracking; consolidation keeps that context without interrupting the normative scan path.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

Implementers and conformance reviewers are forced to re-parse provider-history clauses every time they re-enter the schema-subset definition; the contract reads the same with or without the provenance, so no observable behaviour changes either way.

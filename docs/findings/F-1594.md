## Finding

The normative section that owns the binder's user-facing system-note row table is named `### Failure-mode templates (normative)` in `docs/spec_topics/binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md` (anchor `#failure-mode-templates-normative`), and that is the name every cross-page link uses (BNDR-2, BNDR-3, BNDR-9, the cancellation-page cross-link, the cross-ceiling routing rules in `hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md`, the `<ajv-summary>` rule in `query/query-failure-and-repair.md`, and the plan leaves V11b / V11b-T / V11f / V11f-T). The in-prose references on the binder pages themselves drift:

- `defaulting-system-note-echo.md` paragraph intro (§System-note rendering opening sentence), rule 4, and rule 5 all call the construct "the failure-modes table".
- `determinism-cancellation-failure.md` §Cancellation says "produces the cancelled-binder system note in the failure-modes table below".
- `binder-bypass-and-envelope.md` envelope-cap paragraph says "exercising the malformed-envelope row in the failure-modes table".
- `cancellation.md` mirrors the same phrase ("the failure-modes table in [Slash-Command Argument Binding]").
- BNDR-3 in `binder-bypass-and-envelope.md` writes `The [failure-mode templates](…) table MUST keep …` — the link text is now the canonical name but the word "table" is tacked on after it, hybridising the two forms in a single sentence.

The collision is amplified by the existence of an unrelated "failure-modes table" elsewhere in the corpus (the DISC-2 table in `discovery/discovery-sources.md`, referenced by `discovery/package-and-settings.md` and the three discovery-source rows in `diagnostics/code-registry-load.md`). A reader chasing "the failure-modes table" inside a binder page has to disambiguate between the binder construct (which is actually named "Failure-mode templates") and the discovery construct that does carry the name "failure-modes table". Anchor-bearing cross-page links resolve unambiguously, but the bare in-prose references force a mapping step the link-bearing references do not.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit-defect
**Introducing commit:** `c1e5d64` — *spec: REQ-ID infrastructure + Phase 12a heading promotion on five named pages (Decision #12, partial)* (Tue May 5 2026)
**History:** Phase 12a of Decision #12 promoted every bold-prefixed paragraph on `spec_topics/binder.md` (among four other pages) to a `##` or `###` sub-heading. In that edit `**Failure modes.**` became `### Failure-mode templates (normative)` — the section was renamed, not merely re-shaped. The three in-prose references calling the construct "the failure-modes table" were already in place at the moment of the rename — `git log -S "failure-modes table" -- spec_topics/binder.md` shows them landing in `0fa81fa` (system-note rendering rule 5, Tue May 5), `989b93e` (system-note rendering paragraph + rule 4, Mon May 4), and `7cb4cc3` (cancelled-binder note paragraph, Mon May 4), all authored against the pre-rename heading where "the failure-modes table" was the literal section name and the reference was self-consistent. The `c1e5d64` promotion changed the heading but did not sweep the in-prose wording, creating the drift. Later edits then compounded rather than introduced it: `1161fa0` (Sun May 31) authored BNDR-3 using the post-rename link text *plus* the legacy word "table" ("`[failure-mode templates](…) table MUST …`"), and `f5e89f4` (Thu Jun 4) split `binder.md` into the four-shard subdirectory layout, distributing the drifted references across `defaulting-system-note-echo.md`, `binder-bypass-and-envelope.md`, and `determinism-cancellation-failure.md` verbatim.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Standardise the binder construct's name to "failure-mode templates" everywhere the binder pages and `cancellation.md` reference it in prose, leaving the heading and anchor (`#failure-mode-templates-normative`) untouched.

Specific edits:

- `docs/spec_topics/binder/defaulting-system-note-echo.md` line 16: change "the failure-modes table reference back here" to "the failure-mode templates reference back here"; line 21 (rule 4): change "the malformed-envelope row in the failure-modes table" to "the malformed-envelope row of the failure-mode templates"; line 22 (rule 5): change "the `ambiguous` row of the failure-modes table renders" to "the `ambiguous` row of the failure-mode templates renders".
- `docs/spec_topics/binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md` line 21 (§Cancellation): change "the cancelled-binder system note in the failure-modes table below" to "the cancelled-binder system note in the failure-mode templates below" — or, if the placement finding ("System-note rendering 'the table below' is a stale cross-file pointer") is resolved first by moving the rendering section, anchor it via the existing `#failure-mode-templates-normative` link instead of "below".
- `docs/spec_topics/binder/binder-bypass-and-envelope.md` line 31 (BNDR-3): drop the trailing word "table" so the sentence reads `The [failure-mode templates](./determinism-cancellation-failure.md#failure-mode-templates-normative) MUST keep distinct …`; the row-prefix obligation is unchanged because "templates" already denotes the rows. Line 75 (envelope-cap rationale): change "the malformed-envelope row in the failure-modes table" to "the malformed-envelope row of the failure-mode templates".
- `docs/spec_topics/cancellation.md` line 67: change "the failure-modes table in [Slash-Command Argument Binding]" to "the failure-mode templates in [Slash-Command Argument Binding]" and point the link at `./binder/determinism-cancellation-failure.md#failure-mode-templates-normative` (it currently points at `./binder.md`, which the shard split made non-canonical for this construct).

Edge cases the implementer must watch:

- The wording "failure-modes table" used in `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/code-registry-load.md`, `docs/spec_topics/discovery/discovery-sources.md`, and `docs/spec_topics/discovery/package-and-settings.md` refers to the **discovery** construct (the DISC-2 table at the top of `discovery-sources.md`), not the binder construct. Do not touch those sites — that table is genuinely a "failure-modes table" on its own page and the term is correct there.
- The shard-split heading text in `determinism-cancellation-failure.md` (`### Failure-mode templates (normative)`) and the slug `#failure-mode-templates-normative` are load-bearing for every external link in the corpus and for the plan-leaf citations in V11b, V11b-T, V11f, V11f-T. Do not rename the heading or change the anchor.
- BNDR-3's normative force depends on naming exactly the two row prefixes whose distinctness is being asserted; the textual content of the row prefixes ("argument binding needs more info" vs "ambiguous arguments") must remain verbatim after the "table" word is dropped.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

Implementers and reviewers can still resolve every reference correctly via the anchor links and the singular table in each binder page, so no behaviour ships wrong. The cost is a reading tax — six in-prose sites force a mental rename from "failure-modes table" to "Failure-mode templates" — and a low-grade collision with the unrelated discovery construct that *is* called "failure-modes table". A future reviewer auditing terminology will keep tripping on the same drift until the in-prose wording matches the heading.

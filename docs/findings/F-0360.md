## Finding

V5e's second `Tests.` bullet ends with two sentences that are design-scope
attribution, not test assertions the leaf can discharge: "The actual wrapping
of a depth-6 breach into each carrier is asserted at the site owner:
`ValidationError` at `V13c`, `CodeToolError` at `V14e`, `InvokeInfraError` at
`V15h`, the slash-load cross-route at `V4e`. The model-driven and slash-load
`params` rows produce no loom-code `Err` (model feedback / load-time
respectively) and are decision-only here."

These sentences describe where the downstream surface-owning leaves assert the
concrete carrier wrapping and which site-classes intentionally produce no
loom-code error. That is ownership/scope information about other leaves, which
V5e's own isolated harness cannot and should not exercise. Placed inside a
`Tests.` bullet it reads as a test obligation on V5e rather than the
boundary-scope note it actually is. The leaf's `Adds.` field is the correct
home for it — it currently states only "with the per-boundary routing,"
omitting the downstream-ownership map entirely.

The site-ownership attribution sentences (`asserted at the site owner: ValidationError at V13c …`, and the "produce no loom-code `Err` … decision-only here" note) were added in a single commit, `3fa39a9`, while resolving a different finding that V5e's routing test asserted destination surfaces its `Deps` cannot reach. That fix correctly pushed the carrier-wrapping assertions downstream but lodged the explanatory attribution inside the `Tests.` bullet rather than `Adds.` (`git log -S 'asserted at the site owner'` and `git log -S 'produce no loom-code'` over the file both resolve to `3fa39a9` only; the carrier IDs were later `V14a`/`V15a` and were renamed to `V14e`/`V15h` in subsequent commits, but the prose's placement is unchanged since `3fa39a9`).

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/V5e-depth-enforcement.md`, relocate the two
site-ownership attribution sentences from the second `Tests.` bullet into the
`Adds.` field. The text to move verbatim is:

> The actual wrapping of a depth-6 breach into each carrier is asserted at the
> site owner: `ValidationError` at `V13c`, `CodeToolError` at `V14e`,
> `InvokeInfraError` at `V15h`, the slash-load cross-route at `V4e`. The
> model-driven and slash-load `params` rows produce no loom-code `Err` (model
> feedback / load-time respectively) and are decision-only here.

After the move, the second `Tests.` bullet retains only the per-boundary
routing *decision* assertion (which five site-classes map to which destination
surface class, exercised in isolation against the harness). `Adds.` already
names "the per-boundary routing"; the relocated sentences extend that into the
downstream-ownership map, so the routing contract and its ownership annotation
live together in `Adds.` where the design-scope information belongs.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The attribution sits where an implementer scanning `Tests.` as a checklist may
read it as a V5e test obligation and attempt to assert downstream-owner
wrapping from within V5e — which its `Deps` cannot reach — or be momentarily
confused about V5e's own test surface. No divergence reaches the shipped
artefact; the defect is purely organisational.

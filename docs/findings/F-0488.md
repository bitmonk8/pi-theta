## Problem

`H6a`'s **Ships when** spells out the full evidence-record *format* for the manual real-host smoke run: it obligates a falsifiable record in the gate-activation commit message with no separate artefact file, and enumerates the four fields the record must capture (the Pi-SDK pin literal, the named owner, the date of the run, and the observed result against the criterion). That is governance procedure — how a smoke run is recorded — not the acceptance condition `H6a`'s gate turns on. The dedicated governance note `real-host-smoke-gate.md` is the documented home of the manual real-host smoke protocol and already states the recording convention, but it does not enumerate the four record fields. The result is one convention stated in two places: the no-separate-artefact rule is duplicated and the four-field enumeration lives only in `H6a`, a maintenance divergence surface on a load-bearing release-gate convention.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** eca63cf, fd22a3d
**History:** eca63cf (2026-06-11) authored the evidence-record format (Pi-SDK pin, date, observed result) directly in `H6a`'s release-gate acceptance text at a time when no dedicated governance document existed — the protocol was then referenced through `H4a`, so housing the format in `H6a` was the only available home. fd22a3d (2026-06-17) extracted the manual real-host smoke governance protocol into the new `real-host-smoke-gate.md`, carrying the recording convention (commit message, no separate artefact) into it but leaving the four-field evidence-record enumeration behind in `H6a`; the placement split crystallised at this commit. Intervening commits 4099d0a and 0bd1978 had moved/refined the format text within `H6a` (defining the "gate-activation commit message" home and relocating it into Ships-when) but predate the dedicated governance note and so did not introduce the misplacement.

## Solution approach

Move the evidence-record field enumeration out of `H6a`'s **Ships when** and into `real-host-smoke-gate.md`, consolidating it with the recording-convention sentence already there so the full format is stated once in the governance note. In `H6a`'s **Ships when**, replace the sentence that enumerates the four fields and the no-separate-artefact rule with the acceptance condition plus a pointer to `real-host-smoke-gate.md` for the record format. Keep `H6a`'s statement that the release does not pass until the gate-activation commit message records the run. The cross-reference from `H6a` to `real-host-smoke-gate.md` already exists, so no new link wiring is required.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the owner-antecedent disambiguation in `real-host-smoke-gate.md`'s Activation window is owned by a sibling finding — reference the owner field without redefining who the owner is.
- Out of scope: the hybrid automated/manual atomicity of `H6a`'s Ships-when is owned by a sibling finding editing the same leaf.

## Finding

The first `SLSH-2` Tests bullet in `V12a-slash-dispatch.md` (and its verbatim twin in `V12a-T-slash-dispatch.md`) closes with a sentence that is not a test assertion: "The real-Pi behaviour this models — Pi's TUI streaming tokens into the transcript before the terminal `agent_end` settles `ctx.waitForIdle()` — is an unpinned host presupposition audited by editorial review per [bump-checklist item (ak)](…version-bump-step2.md#bump-checklist-user-visible-streaming-ordering)." This is a risk/assumption note about a host invariant and its editorial-audit routing, lodged inside the prose of a concrete test case.

A Tests bullet states what the test asserts; the executable content here is the ordering assertion (streamed tokens observable before `ctx.waitForIdle()` resolves, driven through the in-process session double whose ordering is fixed by the H4a fidelity contract). The trailing sentence instead documents *why* that assertion rests on an unpinned presupposition and *where* the presupposition is audited. That substance already has authoritative homes spec-side: `conversation-drive.md#user-visible-streaming-ordering-presupposition` defines the presupposition and `version-bump-step2.md` item (ak) routes it to editorial review. The note in the leaf is therefore a back-reference whose content is accurate but whose placement is wrong — it belongs with the leaf's cross-references (or a plan-level presupposition register), not inside the test-case description.

The defect therefore entered as a placement side-effect of an otherwise-substantive fix, not at plan inception.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Strike from the Tests bullets (cruft)

- **Approach:** Delete the trailing "The real-Pi behaviour this models … item (ak)" sentence from the first SLSH-2 streaming bullet in both `V12a-slash-dispatch.md` and `V12a-T-slash-dispatch.md`, leaving the executable ordering assertion (and the H4a fidelity-contract reference) intact. Optionally re-add the bump-checklist (ak) cross-reference to V12a's `Spec.` field, or as a parenthetical on the `H4a` entry in `Deps.`, so it stays discoverable from the leaf.
- **Plan edits:** Two bullets edited; one optional cross-reference added to V12a's `Spec.`/`Deps.`
- **Spec edits:** None.
- **Pros:** Minimal diff; no new plan surface; the presupposition and its audit routing already live spec-side, so nothing normative is lost.
- **Cons:** The soft risk is no longer surfaced anywhere in the plan index — a plan-level risk reviewer who never opens the spec would not see it (unless the cross-reference is preserved on `Spec.`/`Deps.`).
- **Risks:** Low; if the cross-reference is dropped entirely rather than relocated, plan-side discoverability of the presupposition is lost.

### Option B — Relocate to a plan-index home (placement)

- **Approach:** Strike the sentence from both Tests bullets and re-home it in `plan.md` — either appended to §Blocked obligations or in a new §Unpinned host presuppositions section — recording the SLSH-2 streaming-ordering presupposition and its routing to bump-checklist item (ak).
- **Plan edits:** Two bullets edited; one new/extended section in `plan.md`.
- **Spec edits:** None.
- **Pros:** Surfaces the soft risk to plan-level risk reviewers in a single register; aligns with the plan's existing convention of tracking host-contingent items in §Blocked obligations.
- **Cons:** Duplicates content already owned by `conversation-drive.md` and item (ak), adding a second maintenance surface that can drift; sibling presuppositions (e.g. V14b's parallel-tool-mode reliance) would invite the same treatment, growing the register.
- **Risks:** The plan-side copy and the spec-side owner diverge over time without a mechanical guard.

### Recommendation

Take Option A and preserve discoverability: strike the trailing sentence from the SLSH-2 streaming bullet in both `V12a-slash-dispatch.md` and `V12a-T-slash-dispatch.md`, and relocate the bump-checklist (ak) cross-reference to V12a's `Spec.` field (a parenthetical on the `H4a` `Deps.` entry is an acceptable alternative). The presupposition's normative substance is already owned in `conversation-drive.md#user-visible-streaming-ordering-presupposition` and `version-bump-step2.md` (ak), so a fresh plan.md register (Option B) duplicates spec content. Edit the two leaf files together so they stay identical. Watch one edge: keep the executable ordering assertion and the H4a fidelity-contract reference in the bullet; only the trailing rationale sentence is removed.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The note's content is correct and its load-bearing context survives independently in `conversation-drive.md` and bump-checklist item (ak). Leaving it inside the Tests bullet only blurs the boundary between an executable assertion and an editorial-audit rationale, making the test case harder to read; no implementer is driven to a divergent implementation.

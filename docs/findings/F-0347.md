## Finding

`M` (and its paired tests leaf `M-T`) implement the production prompt-mode conversation drive: their **Adds** issue a single untyped `@`-query "whose assistant response streams as one prompt-mode turn," and their `SLSH-2` tests assert that exactly one prompt-mode turn is appended. The SDK delivery mechanics that behaviour rests on — `pi.sendUserMessage(text)` being `void`-returning (with the `MUST NOT await` / `MUST NOT .catch` rule), `ctx.isIdle()` selecting the bare vs. `{ deliverAs: "steer" }` form, `await ctx.waitForIdle()` as the authoritative completion signal, the prohibition on using a global `pi.on("agent_end", …)` for completion, and the trailing-turn `Ok(string)` extraction from the read-only `ReadonlySessionManager` surface — are normatively pinned in `pi-integration-contract/conversation-drive.md` (with the member surfaces in `host-interfaces-core.md`).

Neither contract is in `M`'s or `M-T`'s `Spec.` field. `M` cites `overview.md`, `slash-invocation.md`, `discovery/discovery-sources.md`, and `frontmatter/frontmatter-fields-a.md`; `M-T` cites the same four. Nor is the contract reachable through `Deps`: `M`'s `Deps` are `M-T, H4a` and `M-T`'s are `H4a`, none of which carries the prompt-mode drive contract (the leaf that owns it, `V9c`, is not a dependency of either). The only path from `M`'s cited set to the mechanics is a single forward-link inside `slash-invocation.md`'s SLSH-2 prose ("Underlying SDK delivery mechanics live in Pi Integration Contract — Conversation drive"). The plan's authoring discipline (How-to-use step 3) directs an implementer to "read only the leaf and the spec topic(s) listed under its **Spec** field," so that hop is outside the set the implementer is told to consult.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** `c6a664e` ("pi-loom plan: build/update plan for spec.md + review")

**History:** `M-minimal-slash-command.md` and `M-T-minimal-slash-command.md` were both created in `c6a664e` (2026-06-10), already carrying the four-topic `Spec.` field (`overview.md`, `slash-invocation.md`, `discovery/discovery-sources.md`, `frontmatter/frontmatter-fields-a.md`) — none of which is `conversation-drive.md`. The two subsequent edits to `M` (`2d52ad1`, resolving the happy-path discovery-source finding; `6773362`, resolving the happy-path-subset qualifier) did not touch the citation gap. `git log -S 'conversation-drive'` over both leaves matches only `c6a664e`, which added the word as an Adds-prose mention ("conversation-drive contracts are deepened by the `V*` slices"), never as a `Spec.` citation. The defect has therefore existed unchanged since the leaves were authored.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add the prompt-mode conversation-drive contract to `M`'s `Spec.` field, citing the link `[`../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/conversation-drive.md`](../spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/conversation-drive.md)` as the source for the untyped-query prompt-mode drive mechanics (`pi.sendUserMessage` void shape and `ctx.isIdle()` form selection, `await ctx.waitForIdle()` completion, and the trailing-turn `Ok(string)` extraction). Mirror the same citation into `M-T`'s `Spec.` field, since `M-T` authors the `SLSH-2` assertions against those same mechanics.

Scope the citation to the untyped-query happy path `M` implements (the prompt-mode drive, error-detection, and `untyped-query-ok-extraction` portions of `conversation-drive.md`); the typed-query two-phase loop and subagent-mode drive in that same topic are deepened by the `V*` slices and are out of `M`'s scope. If the implementer needs the `ExtensionAPI` / `ExtensionCommandContext` member shapes, `host-interfaces-core.md` is the companion citation `conversation-drive.md` already forward-links.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

An implementer following the `Spec.`-field discipline never reaches the prompt-mode drive contract and invents the call sequence, free to violate its load-bearing constraints — `await`ing the `void` `pi.sendUserMessage`, attaching a `.catch`, or resolving completion off a global `pi.on("agent_end", …)` instead of `ctx.waitForIdle()`. The result is a drive that diverges from the H4a fidelity contract `M`'s own **Ships when** depends on; the happy-path turn may still stream, but the per-response single-turn / `waitForIdle`-vs-streaming ordering invariants are spelled out only in the uncited contract.

## Finding

The opening paragraph of `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics.md` carries two distinct defects that a single edit resolves.

**Uncited Pi-behaviour claim + missing invariant cross-reference.** The paragraph claims Pi "would otherwise push" factory-time bootstrap registration / subscription failures into `LoadExtensionsResult.errors`, and that loom emits its own `loom/load/extension-bootstrap-failed` for that class instead — with no anchor citation. Every other concrete Pi-shape claim in the corpus is backed by a `host-interfaces-core.md` or `audit-recognised-shapes.md` pin; this one stands alone, leaving no anchor to re-validate on a Pi minor bump. The interplay depends on a no-double-report invariant — Pi must not also write the same failure into `errors`. That invariant is already pinned in `pi-integration-contract/extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md` (the factory MUST NOT throw out of `default function (pi: ExtensionAPI)`; per-call `try`/`catch` keeps each failure local, and the underlying-error coercion clause guards against a synchronous `TypeError` escaping), so Pi's `errors` stays empty by construction — but `diagnostics.md` never says so, forcing a reader to derive it from a different topic.

**Self-contradictory timing window.** The same intro asserts "the diagnostics defined here all fire after the extension is already live (during scan, watcher reload, or slash-command execution)." Two rows contradict it: `loom/load/extension-bootstrap-failed` is triggered by "a factory-time or `session_start`-time SDK registration or subscription call", and `loom/load/host-incompatible` is emitted from the extension-factory capability probe (Step 0), strictly before any `pi.register*` / `pi.on` call. The intro's qualifier ("Such bootstrap failures surface on Pi startup") does not retract the universal clause that immediately follows.

Both gaps live in the one intro paragraph and resolve through one coherent edit.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Rewrite the opening paragraph to (a) cite the Pi-side surface, (b) name the swallow contract that makes the no-double-report invariant hold, and (c) replace the universal timing clause with a two-window statement.

For (a)/(b), cite `loadExtensions(...)` in `@earendil-works/pi-coding-agent`'s `dist/core/extensions/loader.js` at the [loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin](../pi-integration-contract/host-prerequisites.md#pi-sdk-pin), state that it only populates an entry when the extension's `import()` or factory `await` throws past loom's per-call `try`/`catch` wrappers, and cross-link the never-throw boundary (per [Pi Integration Contract — Extension-bootstrap SDK failures](./pi-integration-contract/extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md)) so Pi's `errors` array stays empty for this class by construction with no double-reporting hazard.

For (c), replace the universal clause with: "The diagnostics defined here fire in two windows: a small set of **factory-time / `session_start`-time** codes — `loom/load/host-incompatible` and `loom/load/extension-bootstrap-failed` — surface during extension bootstrap, before the first scan; all other codes fire **after the extension is live** (during scan, watcher reload, or slash-command execution)."

Notes: the factory-time exception set is exactly those two `load`-phase codes emitted from the capability probe or the bootstrap `try`/`catch` — do not extend it to `invalid-encoding` / `unreadable-source` / `discovery-slow` / `package-read-timeout` (post-bootstrap). If a future audit prefers all Pi-shape pins in one anchor file, move the `loadExtensions` shape claim into `audit-recognised-shapes.md` and cite that. Add a one-line `LoadExtensionsResult` shape item to the bump checklist at `pi-integration-contract/version-bump-step2.md`. Do not duplicate the per-code Trigger detail (it lives at the owner row in `code-registry-load.md`); this is an intro-only edit. Do not demote the channel-ownership sentence to non-normative — that re-opens the ambiguity `ac8c3af` resolved; fix is to cite, not weaken.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

The Pi-shape claim and the per-code triggers are correct, so an implementer ships right behaviour. The costs are re-validation cost on each Pi minor bump (no anchor for "does Pi still populate `errors` only on factory throw?", no signpost to the swallow contract) and reader-orientation churn: a contributor scanning the intro gets a self-contradictory window, and successive reviewers keep re-finding the drifting "all"-claim.

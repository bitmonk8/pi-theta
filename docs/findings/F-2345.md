## Finding

The QRY-11 proximate-propagation Tests bullet in `V13d` and `V13d-T` reads "a non-validation failure propagates the proximate variant and consumes no attempt". The spec's normative phrasing (`query-failure-and-repair.md` §QRY-11) is "the proximate **failure** propagates as the corresponding `QueryError` variant … and terminates respond-repair immediately". The plan's elision to "proximate variant" collapses two distinct things the spec keeps separate: the *proximate failure* (the failure event whose cause "wins") and the *`QueryError` variant* it is mapped to on propagation.

`V13d`'s Ships-when compounds the divergence, asserting "the proximate-**variant** propagation". The spec term "proximate failure" does not appear anywhere in the leaf pair, so a grep-based term audit against the spec finds no match for the canonical noun.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commits:** c6a664e ("pi-loom plan: build/update plan for spec.md + review", 2026-06-10)
**History:** The V13d leaf file was created at c6a664e (`git log --follow --diff-filter=A`), and that first revision already carried "propagates the proximate variant" in the QRY-11 bullet and "proximate-variant propagation" in Ships-when. The phrasing has never matched the spec's "proximate failure" noun; the later QRY-ID coinage commit 716a13b (2026-06-25) repointed the anchor to `#qry-11` but left the divergent noun untouched. The defect is original to the leaf, not introduced by a later edit.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In the QRY-11 proximate-propagation Tests bullet of both `docs/plan_topics/V13d-query-failure-repair.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V13d-T-query-failure-repair.md`, replace "propagates the proximate variant" with "propagates the proximate failure as the corresponding `QueryError` variant" — matching the spec's term and preserving the failure-vs-variant distinction. In `V13d`'s Ships-when, change "the proximate-variant propagation" to "the proximate-failure propagation". Leave the rest of each bullet (the no-attempt-consumed and `ContextOverflowError` short-circuit clauses) unchanged.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The diverging noun is wording only — the linked spec anchor and the surrounding bullet text ("non-validation failure … consumes no attempt") make the intended behaviour unambiguous, so no implementer is misled into a wrong implementation. The cost is a term-consistency gap: a grep audit aligning plan tests to the spec's canonical "proximate failure" noun finds no match in the V13d pair.

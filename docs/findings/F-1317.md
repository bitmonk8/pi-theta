## Finding

`docs/plan_topics/real-host-smoke-gate.md` carries its entire smoke-governance protocol in one `#`-level title followed by continuous prose. The four sub-sections a reader or auditor needs to address individually ŌĆö the activation window, the live-host precondition, the pass/fail criterion, and the two-trigger pre-merge gate ŌĆö are introduced only as bold inline labels (`**Activation window.**`, `**Live-host precondition.**`, `**Pass/fail criterion:**`, and the `(1)`/`(2)` trigger clauses buried in the final paragraph). None is a `##` heading, so none has a stable GitHub-style fragment anchor that another document can deep-link to.

Every reference to the note is therefore whole-document: `docs/plan.md`'s release-gate item, `H4a`'s scope-exclusion preamble, `V18d`'s revert-path `Adds`, and `H6a` all link `real-host-smoke-gate.md` with no fragment. Because there is no anchor for the pass/fail criterion, `H6a`'s `Ships when` restates the criterion inline (no-error completion, binder-envelope validity, observed cancellation, the four fidelity axes, the permitted-code-list subset) rather than deep-linking to it, duplicating the normative text in two places that can drift apart.

This is an additive organizational gap, not a correctness defect: the existing whole-document links remain valid, and the protocol is fully stated. The cost is purely traceability ŌĆö there is no machine-readable target for a sub-section citation, and the inline restatement in `H6a` exists only because no anchor was available to point at.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `docs/plan_topics/real-host-smoke-gate.md`, promote each of the four key clauses from a bold inline label to its own `##` heading so that each gains a stable fragment anchor (GitHub-style anchors are derived automatically from the heading text):

- `## Activation window` (anchor `#activation-window`) ŌĆö covering the deferred-until-`H7a` two-trigger dormancy and deferral-recording text currently introduced by `**Activation window.**`.
- `## Live-host precondition` (anchor `#live-host-precondition`) ŌĆö covering the host-prerequisites / un-runnable-gate text currently introduced by `**Live-host precondition.**`.
- `## Pass/fail criterion` (anchor `#pass-fail-criterion`) ŌĆö covering criteria (a)ŌĆō(e) and the benign-variance / merge-block text currently introduced by `**Pass/fail criterion:**`.
- `## Trigger conditions` (anchor `#trigger-conditions`) ŌĆö covering the two named-owner triggers `(1)` and `(2)` and the contributor-obligation / after-the-fact-detection text currently embedded in the closing paragraph.

The prose body of each clause is preserved verbatim under its new heading. Once `#pass-fail-criterion` exists, `H6a`'s `Ships when` may deep-link to it instead of restating the criterion inline, and `plan.md` / `H4a` / `V18d` may point at the specific clause they reference; those reference updates are optional and may land separately.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

The defect is that a sub-section of the note cannot be cited by anchor, which is why `H6a`'s `Ships when` duplicates the pass/fail criterion inline instead of deep-linking; the two copies can silently diverge over time.

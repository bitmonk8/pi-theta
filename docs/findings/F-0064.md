## Finding

The *Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance* clause in `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline states a categorical, per-leaf obligation: "Whenever a new leaf is added that can introduce an executable REQ-ID, the citing test that *closes* a `coverage-matrix.md`-mapped numbered REQ-ID … or an un-anchored normative MUST, that leaf MUST be added to `H5b`'s `Deps.`" The trigger keys explicitly on coverage-matrix closing-leaf membership ("the test on the leaf the coverage matrix lists as that REQ-ID's closing leaf"), so for a multi-leaf cell every listed closing leaf is in scope of the MUST.

The same paragraph then closes with "This obligation is mechanically backed: `H5a`'s standing transitive-completeness gate arm … fails CI on any closing-leaf cell none of whose listed leaves is in it … so this rule and the gate stay in lockstep." But the gate arm (as authored in both `conventions.md` and `H5a`'s Adds) passes a cell as soon as **at least one** of its listed leaves is in `H5b`'s `Deps.`. A multi-leaf cell such as `CIO-5 | V16a, H7a` stays green when only `V16a` is present. The mechanical arm therefore enforces a *weaker* property (≥1 closing leaf per cell) than the categorical MUST it claims to back (every closing leaf per cell). "Mechanically backed" and "stay in lockstep" assert a full enforcement the arm does not deliver.

The CIO-5/H7a example surfaces the inconsistency directly: the coverage matrix lists `H7a` as a CIO-5 closing leaf — which, by the trigger's own definition, makes it subject to the MUST — yet the prose rationalises its exclusion from `H5b`'s `Deps.` on the ground that it "closes no new spec REQ-ID." The trigger was written to *ignore* that ground ("the trigger turns on the coverage-matrix closing-leaf mapping, not on whether the citation is the REQ-ID's first textual occurrence"). A reader cannot reconcile the categorical trigger, the ≥1-per-cell gate, and the "closes no new REQ-ID" exclusion into one consistent rule.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Align the prose with the gate's actual guarantee and name the residue

**Approach.** Keep the categorical per-leaf MUST, but stop claiming the gate fully backs it. Replace the closing "so this rule and the gate stay in lockstep" with prose stating what the arm actually guarantees (at least one closing leaf per cell) and that full per-leaf co-witness inclusion is a manual obligation carried by the per-phase self-review step and the GOV-15 reviewer inspection — the same residue surface the un-anchored-MUST and blocking-runtime rules already route to.

**Plan edits.**
- `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline: rewrite the final sentence so it claims only that the `H5a` arm guarantees ≥1 listed closing leaf per cell, and add that complete per-leaf co-witness inclusion is a manual plan-maintenance obligation (per-phase self-review / GOV-15). Reconcile the trigger with the H7a exclusion: state that a co-witness which merely re-cites an already-closed REQ-ID does not trigger the MUST, while a co-witness listed as a closing leaf that closes distinct coverage does — and have the CIO-5/H7a example state explicitly that `H7a` does not trigger the MUST because it re-cites CIO-5 (already closed by `V16a`) rather than because the gate stays green.
- `H5a-closing-gate-automation.md` Adds: soften the parallel "so it mechanically backs the *Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance* obligation" clause to match (mechanically backs the ≥1-per-cell guarantee; co-witness completeness is manual residue).

**Spec edits.** None.

**Pros.** Preserves the stronger per-leaf obligation; makes the gate's true guarantee honest; consistent with the plan's established pattern of routing un-mechanizable residue to GOV-15 / self-review.

**Cons.** Leaves a manual obligation whose only enforcement is reviewer discipline.

**Risks.** If the GOV-15 checklist does not enumerate the co-witness-completeness item, the manual obligation is unobserved in practice (see Related Findings).

### Option B — Re-scope the MUST to match the gate

**Approach.** Weaken the obligation itself so the prose and the arm describe the same property: require that at least one closing leaf per cell be in `H5b`'s `Deps.`, dropping the categorical per-leaf requirement.

**Plan edits.**
- `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline: change "that leaf MUST be added to `H5b`'s `Deps.`" to require at least one closing leaf per coverage-matrix cell to be in `H5b`'s `Deps.`, so the MUST and the `H5a` arm coincide; the "stay in lockstep" claim then becomes accurate as written.

**Spec edits.** None.

**Pros.** Eliminates the inconsistency with no residue; "mechanically backed / stay in lockstep" becomes literally true.

**Cons.** Silently weakens a real obligation — a genuine co-witness that closes distinct coverage in a multi-leaf cell could now be omitted from `H5b`'s `Deps.` and be fully compliant, allowing the release gate to activate before that coverage is witnessed.

**Risks.** Re-introduces the very coverage-gap the original `08bd641` backstop was added to close, just below the gate's resolution.

### Recommendation

Take **Option A**. Option B trades the overclaim away by lowering the bar, re-admitting the release-time coverage gap. Option A keeps the per-leaf obligation, makes the gate's guarantee honest, and routes the un-mechanizable remainder to the manual-review surface the plan already uses for scan residue. Edge case the implementer must watch: the trigger and the CIO-5/H7a example must be reconciled so a listed co-witness that closes *distinct* coverage (not a mere re-cite) is still required in `H5b`'s `Deps.`; the example should justify `H7a`'s exclusion by the re-cite ground, not by the gate staying green.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two maintainers adding a genuine co-witness leaf — one that the coverage matrix lists as a closing leaf and that closes a distinct portion of a multi-leaf cell — diverge: one applies the categorical MUST and adds it to `H5b`'s `Deps.`; the other reads the ≥1-per-cell gate plus the H7a precedent and omits it, with no CI signal either way. An omitted genuine co-witness leaves `H5b`/`H6a` sequenced ahead of it, so the release gate can activate before that leaf's coverage is verified against the live corpus — a silent coverage gap the "mechanically backed … stay in lockstep" claim says cannot happen.

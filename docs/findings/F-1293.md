## Problem

The discovery-root containment check in invocation.md *Resolution* (the load-time check and the INV-1 invocation-time re-check) decides symlink escape by the byte-exact output of `FileSystem.realpath` applied to both the resolved callee path and each active discovery root. Correctness depends on two `realpath` properties PIC-13's `realpath` bullet does not pin as normative: full transitive symlink resolution (every symlink on the path, not just the leaf, is followed, so an interior link aiming outside a discovery root is observable), and byte-stable canonical output (two inputs naming the same on-disk target yield byte-identical output). The bullet today pins only its `.code` rejections, a case-canonicalisation guarantee for case-insensitive filesystems, and the inline "DOES follow symlinks" comment — leaving transitive resolution implicit and byte-stability unpinned beyond the case-only carve-out.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:**
- `8a6d15f` (2026-05-06) "resolve 'FileSystem seam interface signature lives outside the spec corpus'" — introduced PIC-13's `readdir` member with the deliberately narrow rejection-only contract; no encoding or normalisation pin.
- `76c649f` (2026-06-04) "resolve 'realpath required by Resolution but absent from FileSystem seam'" — added `realpath` to PIC-13 with the narrow `.code` rejection contract and the inline "DOES follow symlinks" comment, but no normative pin on transitive resolution or byte-stability.
- `9aa73b1` (2026-06-07) "resolve 'Filesystem case-sensitivity for .warp basenames and invoke/tools containment'" — rewrote IMP-1 to decide unresolvability against the byte-for-byte `readdir` return value and rewrote invocation.md *Resolution* to decide containment on the "byte-exact output of `FileSystem.realpath`". This is the commit that lifted both consumption sites onto contract surfaces the seam never pinned.
- `a765f6c` (later, F-0977 fix) — partially closed the `realpath` half by pinning byte-identity across case-variant inputs on case-insensitive filesystems; left full-transitive-resolution and general byte-stability unpinned.

**History:** The defect emerges from the interaction across the seam-introduction commits and the consumption-site sharpening in `9aa73b1`. Each PIC-13 member was added with a narrow rejection-only contract; `9aa73b1` then routed two independent observable rules through properties of those members the narrow contracts never pinned. F-0977's `a765f6c` follow-up closed one corner of the `realpath` half, establishing the template the remaining clauses should follow but not extending it.

## Solution approach

Rewrite PIC-13's `realpath` bullet (anchor `id="pic-13"`, host-interfaces-services.md) to pin full transitive symlink resolution and byte-stable canonical output as normative guarantees binding every conforming seam implementation, modelled on the existing case-canonicalisation clause it generalises. Add a forward-link from invocation.md *Resolution*'s first `FileSystem.realpath` mention to PIC-13's `realpath` bullet.

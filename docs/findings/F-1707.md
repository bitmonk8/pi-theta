## Problem

QRY-19 — the rule requiring an author to pick `?`, `let _ =`, or a binding at a query call site, surfaced as the `loom/parse/discarded-query-result` parse error — is a normative rule about the form of a query call site and is a peer of QRY-1 (untyped form) and QRY-2 (typed form). It currently lives in `query/query-escapes-stringification.md`, whose other rules (QRY-17 escapes, QRY-18 stringification) concern the inside of a template body, not the call-site shape of the surrounding expression. A reader consulting `query/query-forms.md` for the full call-site contract finishes QRY-1 through QRY-7 without ever learning that a bare `@`...`` expression-statement is a parse error, and there is no forward pointer from `query-forms.md` to the rule.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** `f5e89f4` ("docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set", 2026-06-04)
**History:** The "Discarded query results are a parse error" rule first entered the corpus in `53f5831` (2026-05-03, "spec: rewrite query primitive, system prompts, and schema syntax") as a `**Discarded query results are a parse error.**` paragraph inside the monolithic `spec.md`. The split-into-topics commit `fecb504` carried it across into `spec_topics/query.md` unchanged. The misplacement was introduced at `f5e89f4` when `query.md` was split into per-feature subpages — the discard rule was placed into the newly-created `query-escapes-stringification.md` alongside escapes (QRY-17) and stringification (QRY-18) rather than into the sibling `query-forms.md` that received QRY-1/QRY-2. The later REQ-ID assignment in `716a13b` (2026-06-25, "fix F-1286 — coin QRY-1..21 across query subtree") named the block QRY-19 but did not relocate it.

## Solution approach

Move the QRY-19 block — its H2 heading carrying the `loom/parse/discarded-query-result` suffix, the `<a id="qry-19"></a>` block, the diagnostic-message paragraph, and the `let _ =` / `void`-tail-discard paragraph that defines the universe of discard forms — from `query/query-escapes-stringification.md` to `query/query-forms.md` alongside the other call-site form contracts (QRY-1/QRY-2). Carry the `<a id="qry-19"></a>` anchor with the block and repoint the `code-registry-parse.md` "Defined at" reference to `../query/query-forms.md#qry-19`. Add `query-forms.md` to the `Spec` field of the V13g leaf and its `-T` tests leaf.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: the `## Observability of discarded results` (QRY-20) and `### Panics during interpolation are not caught by \`let _ =\`` (QRY-21) blocks on `query/query-escapes-stringification.md` — their relocation is owned by a separate finding.

## Finding

`V7d` (`loom-system-note` delivery channel) carries four separable concerns in one leaf: (1) the producer-facing **diagnostic-emission seam** — the entry point a parse/load/runtime producer hands a constructed `Diagnostic` (and the scan-time batch) to; (2) the per-`.loom` single-`sendMessage` multi-error **batching**; (3) the `PIC-21` renderer-throw **trap**; and (4) the multi-condition `loom/runtime/system-note-delivery-failed` **fallback chain** (`sendSystemNote` → `ctx.ui.notify` → `console.error`, with display/empty-content skips, throwing-`notify` recovery, and the `PIC-54` `try`/`catch`-wrapped terminal `console.error`). Concerns (3) and (4) are independent control flows that red-green cycle on their own, distinct from the seam interface and the batching path.

The seam is not a private implementation detail — it is the `Deps` contract that many upstream producers stand on: `V1a`/`V1a-T` (lexer), `V9a`/`V9a-T`, `V9d`/`V9d-T`, `V9k`/`V9k-T`, `V9p`/`V9p-T`, `V9q`/`V9q-T`, and the `H5b` canary all depend on `V7d` solely to reach the emission seam. Bundling that wide-fan-in seam with the renderer-throw trap and the eight-sub-condition fallback chain means every producer waits for the full error-handling surface to land before its own `Deps` edge is satisfiable, and a reviewer must trace four contracts at once on a single leaf. The project convention (`conventions.md` §Vertical slices: "the smallest feature that can ship and be tested independently") favours separating the independently-testable error/delivery control flows from the seam-plus-batching core.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Keep `V7d` owning the producer-facing diagnostic-emission seam, the `loom-system-note` `sendMessage` envelope with its renderer registration, and the per-`.loom` multi-error batching — i.e. the contract the existing `V7d` `Deps` edges stand for — so no upstream producer's `Deps` reference needs retargeting. Strike the `PIC-21` renderer-throw trap and the `loom/runtime/system-note-delivery-failed` fallback-chain text (the `sendSystemNote` → `ctx.ui.notify` → `console.error` ordering, the display/empty-content skips, the throwing-`notify` recovery, and the `PIC-54` terminal-`console.error` wrapping) from `V7d`/`V7d-T` Adds, Tests, and Ships-when.

Create a new leaf (`<new>`) plus its paired `<new>-T` for the extracted error/delivery-handling control flows, depending on `V7d`, carrying the `PIC-21` trap and the full `system-note-delivery-failed` fallback chain (including the `PIC-54` swallow-and-continue behaviour). Move that Tests content verbatim into the new leaf pair, and add the new leaf to the `### V7 — Diagnostics` listing in `docs/plan.md`.

In `docs/plan_topics/coverage-matrix.md`, retarget the `| PIC-21 | V7d |` and `| PIC-54 | V7d |` rows to cite the new leaf instead of `V7d`. Leave `V7d`'s remaining Tests (batch delivery, re-scan re-emit) as the seam-plus-batching acceptance gate. The implementer chooses the new leaf's real ID per the plan's leaf-ID scheme; do not introduce any spec edit — this reorganisation is internal to the plan corpus.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

If `V7d` ships unsplit, the plan still produces a correct interpreter, but every upstream producer's `Deps: V7d` edge is satisfiable only once the renderer-throw trap and the full delivery-failure fallback chain are also implemented, deferring pickup of the lexer and the V9 runtime producers behind error-handling work they do not consume; a reviewer must also validate four independent contracts on one leaf. No two implementers would diverge and no spec obligation is dropped — this is an organisational/sequencing cost, not a correctness gap.

## Problem

The *Specific exception types only* rule in `conventions.md` permits a `catch (e: unknown)` clause only at an **exempt broad-catch site**, imposing two requirements: (a) the line carries a resolving `// allow-broad-catch:` comment, and (b) the site is one of the five enumerated categories. The two mechanical surfaces — the `no-broad-catch` lint wired in `H2a` and the `H5c` closing-gate token-resolution arm — verify only requirement (a); neither checks category membership. Unlike the sibling *No globals, statics, singletons* and *Doc updates* rules, this rule states no enforcement posture for its mechanically-undetectable residue, so whether category membership is reviewer-enforced or simply unverified is left to inference. A reviewer may assume the closing gate polices membership and skip the manual check, letting a broad catch at a non-enumerated site ship with a valid token and green CI.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception (of the exemption mechanism)
**Introducing commit:** `b8bc4ce` — "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Broad-catch sites lack matching no-broad-catch exemption\"" (2026-06-04)
**History:** The dual-requirement exempt-broad-catch-site model — a mechanical comment/token gate plus membership in one of five enumerated categories — was introduced wholesale in `b8bc4ce`. From that first version the closing gate asserted only that the comment's cited token resolved to a matching REQ-ID; category membership had no mechanical check and no enforcement-posture statement. Later revisions to the rule (`96563f9`, 2026-06-10, widening the token predicate to four arms; `506d71c`, 2026-06-12, defining the lint allow-list as the set of in-source comments) elaborated requirement (a) further but never added a posture statement for requirement (b). The gap is intrinsic to the exemption mechanism and has persisted unaddressed since its inception.

## Solution approach

Extend the *Specific exception types only* rule in `docs/plan_topics/conventions.md` with an enforcement-posture statement following the pattern the sibling *No globals, statics, singletons* and *Doc updates* rules already use. The statement should establish that the mechanical surfaces (`H2a` lint, `H5c` closing gate) verify only the comment/token requirement, and that category membership is verified by the loom 1.0 release-time reviewer-inspection step. Cite the existing GOV-15 anchor (`../spec_topics/governance/source-language-stability.md#gov-15`) that the sibling rules already reference, rather than introducing a new mechanical gate.

## Solution constraints

- The spec is read-only for this fix: GOV-15 is spec-owned governance — cross-reference its existing anchor rather than adding any normative text to the spec corpus.

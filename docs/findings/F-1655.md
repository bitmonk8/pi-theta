## Finding

The runtime-event channel treats `occurred_at` as a distinguishing field in two places. The `discard_site` comment on the `RuntimeEvent` shape asserts that "each discard is a distinct occurrence stamped with its own `occurred_at`, so two discards of the same originating template stay distinct," and the *Deduplication and lifetime rules* state that "two emissions from the same `query_site` with the same `kind` and `message` but distinct `occurred_at` values represent two distinct occurrences." Both rest on the dedup tuple `(kind, query_site, message, occurred_at)`, where `occurred_at` is the only field that can differ between two occurrences sharing a site, kind, and message.

`occurred_at` is stamped via `Clock.wallNow()`, which `host-interfaces-services.md` (PIC-12) defines as Unix epoch milliseconds delegating to `Date.now()` in production. `Date.now()` is millisecond-resolution and non-monotonic: two same-site occurrences within one millisecond receive an identical `occurred_at`, and a backward wall-clock adjustment (NTP step, manual change) can make a later occurrence's stamp equal to or earlier than an earlier one's. Under either condition the two occurrences collapse to one tuple, contradicting the "stay distinct" claim — yet the spec asserts distinctness as a property without stating the precondition that makes it conditional.

The contrast with the sibling member is sharp: PIC-12 deliberately keeps `now()` monotonic for deadline math and forbids the deadline-math callers from migrating to `wallNow()`, but the distinctness claims keyed on `wallNow()`-stamped values carry no analogous caveat. The spec should state the precondition (distinctness holds only to wall-clock millisecond resolution and is not guaranteed under sub-millisecond repetition or backward clock adjustment) or narrow the distinctness claim accordingly. It should not introduce a monotonic counter into `occurred_at`, which would conflate the wall-clock stamp with an ordering token and break the verbatim cascade-twin copy that depends on `occurred_at` being a stable, re-emittable value.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `bc50e69` (2026-05-05, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"H2 Clock seam — watcher debounce, scanPackagesTimeoutMs, RuntimeEvent occurred_at\""), `9183961` (2026-06-04, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"RuntimeEvent.occurred_at source clock contradicts the Clock.now() monotonic pin\""), `6afca02` (2026-06-03, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"discard-site field double-specified on RuntimeEvent\"")
**History:** `bc50e69` introduced the `occurred_at` field and the dedup tuple `(kind, query_site, message, occurred_at)`, together with the "distinct `occurred_at` values represent two distinct occurrences" rule. At that point the stamp came from the **monotonic** `Clock.now()` (the commit explicitly pinned "`Clock.now()` is monotonic (forbids `Date.now()`-style NTP drift)"), so only the millisecond-granularity collision risk was latent — the non-monotonicity vector did not yet exist, and the precondition was never stated. `6afca02` then added the discard-site distinctness clause ("two discards of the same originating template stay distinct"), extending the same unasserted distinctness assumption to a second, discard-specific claim. `9183961` resolved an unrelated contradiction (epoch-ms `occurred_at` vs. a monotonic, `performance.now()`-backed `now()`) by introducing the `wallNow()` member and re-sourcing `occurred_at` from it (delegating to `Date.now()` in production); this swapped the monotonic source for a non-monotonic one without revisiting either distinctness claim, introducing the backward-clock vector on top of the pre-existing granularity vector. The durable defect is the interaction: distinctness was assumed solution-blind under a monotonic clock, and the later clock-source change silently invalidated that assumption.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add a precondition note adjacent to the dedup tuple in *Deduplication and lifetime rules* (and reflected in the `discard_site` comment) stating that occurrence distinctness via `occurred_at` holds only to `wallNow()` millisecond resolution: two same-site, same-kind, same-message occurrences within a single millisecond, or separated by a backward wall-clock adjustment, may carry equal `occurred_at` and collapse under the tuple. Frame this as scoping the already non-normative consumer-side dedup, leaving the normative exactly-once-at-origin emission contract untouched. Do not add a monotonic counter or any ordering token to `occurred_at` — the field must remain the verbatim-copied wall-clock stamp that the cascade-twin re-emission rule and PIC-1 clauses (f)/(g) depend on.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Loom 1.0 conformance (exactly-once emission at the origin) is unaffected, since the emission helper fires per occurrence at the origin site regardless of the tuple, and consumer-side dedup on the tuple is already declared non-normative. The defect is a guidance gap: the spec states an unconditional distinctness property that the chosen clock cannot deliver, so a consumer relying on the "two discards stay distinct" guarantee silently loses an occurrence under same-millisecond repetition or a backward clock step.

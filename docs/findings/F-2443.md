## Finding

The **Forwarding-listener throw** clause in `cancellation.md` enumerates three steady-state forwarding listeners whose `loomAbort.abort(source.reason)` call may throw — the slash-command `ctx.signal`-aborted trigger, the tool-exposed `signal`-aborted trigger, and the `invoke`-parent derived-controller trigger — and routes the throw "through the **runtime-defect surface** … on the `loom/runtime/internal-error` channel, with the `cause: "internal_error"` arm of `InvokeInfraError` at an `invoke` parent." The only observable surface pinned in-line is the `InvokeInfraError` arm, which applies to exactly one of the three enumerated entry points.

The runtime-defect surface defined at `error-model.md#runtime-panics` actually carries two routing channels (a slash-command/prompt-mode system note formatted `"loom /<name> aborted with internal error: <error.message>"`, and `InvokeInfraError` to an `invoke` parent), so the slash-command-entry surface is recoverable by following the cross-reference. The tool-exposed entry path is the genuine gap: a `.loom` callable invoked through another loom's `tools:` reaches its caller via the tool-call mechanism, and its observable surface is determinable only by chaining through `tool-calls.md` ("a `.loom`-callable call's failures cascade … through `InvokeCalleeError` / `InvokeInfraError`" and the operational-equivalence-with-`invoke` rule) — neither `cancellation.md` nor the runtime-defect-surface section draws that mapping for the forwarding-throw case.

The result is that a conformance author implementing the three-path injection cannot enumerate the three observable surfaces from `cancellation.md` (or its linked runtime-defect-surface section) alone. The clause names the surface for the `invoke`-parent path, leaves the slash-command path to a cross-reference, and leaves the tool-exposed path to a two-hop inference, so two reasonable implementers can diverge on what the tool-exposed forwarding-throw observably produces.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `cancellation.md`'s **Forwarding-listener throw** clause, complete the per-path observable-surface mapping so all three enumerated triggers have a named surface, rather than naming only the `invoke`-parent arm:

- **slash-command `ctx.signal`-aborted trigger** (top-level slash/prompt-mode loom) → the slash-command system note `"loom /<name> aborted with internal error: <error.message>"` on the `loom/runtime/internal-error` channel, per `error-model.md#runtime-panics`.
- **tool-exposed `signal`-aborted trigger** (a `.loom` callable invoked through another loom's `tools:`) → surfaces to the calling loom as `Err(QueryError { kind: "invoke_infra", cause: "internal_error", ... })`, because a `.loom`-callable call is operationally equivalent to `invoke` and cascades through `InvokeInfraError` (`tool-calls.md` — Failures / Relationship with `invoke`).
- **`invoke`-parent derived-controller trigger** → `Err(QueryError { kind: "invoke_infra", cause: "internal_error", ... })` to the parent (already stated).

Keep the existing non-swallowing guarantee unchanged (the trap routes only the defect throw; `source.signal.aborted` is unchanged and the next `Checkpoint`-seam await still surfaces `Err(QueryError { kind: "cancelled" })`). State the mapping by reference to the runtime-defect surface's two channels plus the `.loom`-callable operational-equivalence rule rather than restating the system-note string, so the surface definitions remain singly-owned in `error-model.md`. Edge case the implementer must watch: the first-source-wins one-shot guard means a throw on a re-entrant second trigger must not re-stamp the reason or emit a second surface.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

A test author and a runtime implementer cannot determine, from the forwarding-throw clause and its linked runtime-defect surface, what a forwarding-listener throw observably produces on the slash-command and (especially) the tool-exposed entry paths. Two reasonable implementers can diverge on the tool-exposed `.loom`-callable surface — one routing it to a slash-command-style system note, another to `InvokeInfraError` — yielding different observable conformance behaviour for the same defect.

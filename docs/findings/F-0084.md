## Problem

The leaf pair `V11d` / `V11d-T` lives in files `docs/plan_topics/V11d-defaulting-echo.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V11d-T-defaulting-echo.md`, whose slug `defaulting-echo` names two concepts the leaf no longer owns: "defaulting" (fill-if-absent + post-merge validation) is owned by `V11g`, and "echo" (the `(default)` argument annotation) is owned by `V11h`. `V11d`'s actual scope — per its heading "Binder system-prompt builder" and its Adds — is solely the binder system-prompt builder, and its Adds explicitly delegates defaulting to `V11g` and echo to `V11h`. The stale slug misdirects anyone searching by filename for the defaulting or echo seams and invites mis-attribution of those seams to `V11d`. The mismatch propagates to two href reference sites that point at the old slug.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** cb8737a — pi-loom plan: resolve "Systemic leaf over-bundling across the plan corpus" (2026-06-12, Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The leaf pair was created at c6a664e (2026-06-10) titled "System-prompt builder, defaulting, and echo", owning all three concepts — the slug `defaulting-echo` correctly described its scope then. At cb8737a the over-bundling split moved defaulting to the new `V11g` and echo to the new `V11h` and narrowed `V11d`'s heading to "Binder system-prompt builder", but edited the file in place (status `M`, not `R`), leaving the now-stale `defaulting-echo` slug and every href that points at it.

## Solution approach

Rename the `V11d` leaf pair files so the slug aligns with the narrowed scope: `V11d-defaulting-echo.md` → `V11d-binder-system-prompt.md` and `V11d-T-defaulting-echo.md` → `V11d-T-binder-system-prompt.md`. Retarget the two hrefs that reference the old slug to the new path — the `docs/plan.md` line-141 link and the `V11d` cross-link in the `V11g` (`V11g-defaulting-revalidation.md`) Adds — leaving their already-correct display text untouched. After the rename, confirm `grep -rn 'V11d-defaulting-echo' docs/` returns nothing.

## Finding

The "Worked consequences" subsection at the top of `docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings/ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md` (lines 3–9) introduces itself as illustrating "one or more `CIO-N` rules above", and two of its three bullets further appeal to "the per-boundary table above" (the model-driven row in bullet 1; the typed-query response row in bullet 2). Neither anchor is on this page: CIO-1 … CIO-6 are defined in the sibling file `docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings/ceilings-3-and-4.md` under the `## Interaction between ceilings` section (anchors `#cio-1` … `#cio-6`), and the "Per-boundary destination/surface table (ceiling #4)" lives in that same sibling file under anchor `#ceiling-4-table`.

The directional word is wrong across the file boundary, and the bullets carry no anchored cross-references to the rules and table they cite — a reader entering at `ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md` cannot follow "above" to anything, and has to know that the sibling page exists and which sections it owns.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `1c7f550` (2026-05-07, *pi-loom spec: resolve "Hard ceilings block does load-bearing definitional work inside informative orientation"*) + `f5e89f4` (2026-06-04, *docs: enforce 100KB hard / 30KB recommended size cap on spec set*)
**History:** `git log -S "Worked consequences of the interaction order" -- docs/` returns exactly these two commits as the surface-touching pair. Commit `1c7f550` introduced the "Worked consequences" subsection inside a single-file `spec_topics/hard-ceilings.md` whose `## Interaction between ceilings` section (CIO-1 … CIO-6) and `## Per-boundary destination/surface table (ceiling #4)` were on the same page above it; at that point "above" and "per-boundary table above" resolved correctly. The size-cap split commit `f5e89f4` partitioned that single file into the `hard-ceilings/` subdirectory (`ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md` + `ceilings-3-and-4.md`), mechanically relocating "Worked consequences" away from both the CIO rules and the per-boundary table without editing the deictic prose, and `git show f5e89f4 -- docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings/ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md` confirms the bullets were carried across verbatim (the same "rules above" / "per-boundary table above" wording). Neither commit alone is the defect: `1c7f550` was internally consistent, and the `f5e89f4` rationale ("All 307 `<a id>` anchors preserved; 1571 cross-references repointed … no content edits") explicitly disclaimed prose edits, so the broken deictic is the predictable interaction between the layout split and the unrewritten in-page references.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A — Rewrite the deictics as anchored cross-references, keep the layout

**Approach.** Replace each "above" in the three worked-consequence bullets with an anchored link into `ceilings-3-and-4.md`. The intro sentence becomes "…each illustrates one or more `CIO-N` rules in [Interaction between ceilings](./ceilings-3-and-4.md#ceiling-interaction-order)…"; the two "per-boundary table above" mid-bullet phrases become "the [per-boundary destination/surface table](./ceilings-3-and-4.md#ceiling-4-table)" (model-driven row in bullet 1, typed-query response row in bullet 2). The per-bullet `(CIO-3, CIO-4)` / `(CIO-2, CIO-4)` tags can each be re-spelled as anchored links to `#cio-2` / `#cio-3` / `#cio-4` if desired, but that is optional polish — the inline-label form already resolves unambiguously.

**Spec edits.** `docs/spec_topics/hard-ceilings/ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md` lines 5, 7, 8 only. No edits to `ceilings-3-and-4.md`.

**Pros.** Surgical; preserves the existing topic split (the file is named "Ceiling invariants and audit" and the worked consequences function as an audit-of-the-interaction-order exhibit, which sits naturally under that umbrella). No anchor churn, no cross-reference repoint outside this file. Honours the f5e89f4 "no content edits" convention by limiting the change to a deictic rewrite that does not move material.

**Cons.** Leaves the worked consequences three hops from the rules they illustrate (open page, follow link, scroll); a conformance-test author still cross-references rather than reads them adjacent. The deeper structural mismatch flagged by the sibling finding *Page title "Ceiling invariants and audit" mismatches content* (the page hosts CIO illustrations and NOCEIL claims rather than ceiling-set invariants) is not addressed.

**Risks.** Anchor `#ceiling-interaction-order` is also defined as a ghost alias on `overview-and-orientation.md` (per the sibling finding *duplicate anchor `#ceiling-interaction-order`*); pointing the worked consequences at the in-file anchor in `ceilings-3-and-4.md` (the normative owner) is correct, but if the duplicate-anchor finding is resolved by removing the canonical entry rather than the ghost, the cross-reference would need re-pointing.

### Option B — Move the worked consequences adjacent to the CIO rules

**Approach.** Relocate the entire `## Worked consequences` subsection (heading + intro sentence + the three bullets) from `ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md` to `ceilings-3-and-4.md`, inserted immediately after the CIO-6 bullet, before `## Ceiling-set invariants`. Strip the now-redundant "in [Interaction between ceilings]…" hop from the intro sentence; restore the original "rules above" wording (correct again in the new home). The two "per-boundary table above" references are also correct in the new home (the table sits on the same page).

**Spec edits.** Delete the `## Worked consequences` block from `ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md`; insert it into `ceilings-3-and-4.md` between the current line-44 (CIO-6) and the current line-46 (`## Ceiling-set invariants`). Update the TOC in `hard-ceilings.md` if it currently advertises Worked consequences under the audit-and-invariants page (a one-line edit). No anchor changes — neither the subsection nor its bullets carry `<a id="…">` tags today.

**Pros.** Restores adjacency between the rules and their named illustrations, which is what the conformance-test-citation intent in the intro sentence asks for; eliminates the deictic problem rather than working around it. Co-resolves naturally with the sibling finding *Page title "Ceiling invariants and audit" mismatches content* — moving Worked consequences out leaves that page holding NOCEIL claims + audit methodology + release gate, exactly what the suggested rename of that finding targets.

**Cons.** Wider blast radius — two files edited plus an index touch. Adds ~2.3 KB to `ceilings-3-and-4.md` (current 16.8 KB → ~19.1 KB, still comfortably under the 30 KB recommended cap), shrinks `ceiling-invariants-and-audit.md` correspondingly. No public anchors break, but any future reviewer searching for "Worked consequences" within the audit page will not find it there.

**Risks.** None material; the move is mechanical and the relocation budget fits within the size-cap regime that motivated `f5e89f4` in the first place.

### Recommendation

Take **Option B**. The worked consequences exist precisely to illustrate the CIO ordering, the inline label `(CIO-3, CIO-4)` per bullet binds them to specific rules, and the intro sentence's "each illustrates one or more `CIO-N` rules" makes adjacency the natural placement; that adjacency was the original (pre-split) layout in `1c7f550`, and only the mechanical split in `f5e89f4` broke it. Moving the subsection back next to CIO-6 restores the structure the rules-and-illustrations pairing presupposes, eliminates the broken deictics in one move rather than papering over them with cross-references, and aligns with the resolution direction of the sibling page-title finding. The edit must also drop the "in [Interaction between ceilings]…" framing if any was added in interim, restore "rules above" and "per-boundary table above" (both correct on the destination page), and refresh the `hard-ceilings.md` index TOC if it advertises the subsection under the audit-page child.

## Finding

`docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/audit-wire-and-canary.md` is laid out as three normative sub-sections — *Wire serialisation*, *Infrastructure-failure handling*, *Non-empty-scan canary* — followed by an unlabelled trailing paragraph beginning "The audit runs as part of the SDK surface-inventory tests step 2 below names…". That trailing paragraph pins the `npm test` harness-wiring contract: in-process-vs-child-process equivalence, the non-zero-exit-with-zero-records MUST, the `expect(...)` pre-registration MUST for the in-process variant, the empty-stdout-as-no-failures prohibition, and the harness-wrapping-sink-survival rule for the audit's record emission.

These harness-wiring obligations are not specific to any one of the three preceding sub-sections — they govern every record class the page defines (violation records under *Wire serialisation*, infrastructure-failure records under *Infrastructure-failure handling*, and the canary's once-per-invocation green-path record under *Non-empty-scan canary*), plus the zero-record `SIGKILL`-style terminations the *Pre-emission termination carve-out* explicitly admits. Placing them after the three sub-sections inverts the dependency: each preceding sub-section assumes a fail-closed harness whose contract it cannot reach without scrolling past itself, and a reader entering the file at any one of the three sub-headings (a likely entry point via the page's `<a id="audit-…">` anchors, which are linked from elsewhere in PIC) sees the record-shape obligations before the surrounding harness contract that gives those obligations their CI-visible effect.

The trailing paragraph also lacks any heading or italicised sub-section label of its own, so a reader skimming the page's bullet structure can miss it entirely, even though every red-vs-green property the page enumerates ultimately hinges on it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Promote the trailing paragraph into a dedicated, italicised sub-section labelled *Harness wiring* (or equivalent) and place it as the **first** sub-section of `audit-wire-and-canary.md`, ahead of *Wire serialisation*, *Infrastructure-failure handling*, and *Non-empty-scan canary*. The sub-section's content is the existing paragraph verbatim; only its position and the new italic label change. Give it its own `<a id="audit-harness-wiring"></a>` anchor so the contract is independently citeable from elsewhere in PIC.

In the same edit, sweep the three following sub-sections for back-references to "the *Harness wiring* sub-paragraph above" where they currently rely on harness behaviour without naming it — at minimum the *Pre-emission termination carve-out* under *Infrastructure-failure handling*, the *Infra-aborted-run carve-out* under *Non-empty-scan canary*, and the *Wire serialisation* sole-machine-parseable-surface rule, each of which presupposes the harness wiring this paragraph pins — so the new forward-pointing dependency order reads as intentional rather than incidental.

Implementer edge cases to watch:

- The paragraph closes with "an implementation whose `npm test` invocation does not exit non-zero on either record class above, …, violates this section." The phrase "either record class above" currently references *Wire serialisation* and *Infrastructure-failure handling* by their relative position above the paragraph. After the move, rephrase to name the two record classes explicitly (e.g. "violation records per *Wire serialisation* and infrastructure-failure records per *Infrastructure-failure handling*") so the rule survives the reordering. The canary's green-path record is also a record on the same wire; if the intent is that canary records do not gate `npm test` exit code (the canary's red exit is governed by its own MUSTs in *Non-empty-scan canary*), keep that scope distinction explicit in the rewritten phrasing.
- The trailing orphan paragraph that immediately follows (the items 5/7 capability-inventory note, called out by a sibling finding on this page) is unrelated to harness wiring and remains an independent placement defect; do not bundle the two moves.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A diligent implementer who reads the page top-to-bottom still arrives at the harness-wiring contract; the obligations themselves are intact and unambiguous. The defect is organisational: a reader entering at a sub-section anchor sees record-shape MUSTs whose end-to-end fail-closed property is pinned only further down, and the unlabelled epilogue is easier to overlook than the three italicised sub-sections it follows.

## Problem

`V9f` (Tool-registration lifetime and visibility) and its paired tests leaf `V9f-T` are built on the active-set gating window owned by `V9c`: V9f's **Adds.** prose names V9c's snapshot/restore on the prompt path, and both failure-path tests (`PIC-8`, `PIC-19`) perturb that window. But V9f's **Deps.** are `V9f-T`, `V9a`, `V5d` and V9f-T's are `V9a`, `V5d`; neither lists or transitively reaches `V9c` — V9c depends on V9a, so V9a is upstream of V9c, never downstream. Under the pickability predicate both leaves become pickable before V9c's window exists, at which point they cannot be implemented or red-tested as written.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commit:** `c6a664e` (2026-06-10, "pi-loom plan: build/update plan for spec.md + review")
**History:** The plan corpus is git-tracked. `V9f` entered at the inception commit `c6a664e` already carrying "the snapshot/restore on the prompt path" in **Adds** and the `PIC-8`/`PIC-19` window-perturbation tests, with Deps `V9f-T`, `V9a`, `V5d` and no V9c edge — the missing dependency dates to that first commit. A later commit `8bd82be` (2026-06-17, "resolve 'one window concept named three ways across V9c/V9c-T/V9f'") rewrote the **Adds** prose to "the active-set gating window's snapshot/restore on the prompt path (defined in `V9c`)", sharpening the cross-reference and making the omission visible, but did not introduce the undeclared dependency.

## Solution approach

Add `V9c` to the **Deps.** of `docs/plan_topics/V9f-tool-registration-lifetime.md` and to the **Deps.** of `docs/plan_topics/V9f-T-tool-registration-lifetime.md`, so both leaves are unpickable until V9c's active-set gating window exists. The existing explicit `V9a` entry on each leaf is reached transitively through V9c once added; leaving it in place is harmless.

## Problem

The `custom-message-context-entry-presupposition` paragraph in `runtime-event-channel.md` closes with a future-phase behavioural obligation — "the runtime SHOULD set the opt-out on the canonical `sendSystemNote` call" — keyed on a `CustomMessage.excludeFromContext` field that does not exist at the loom 1.0 Pi-SDK pin. The clause is a bare `SHOULD` attached to an action the runtime cannot take at loom 1.0, with no future-phase label demarcating it from the surrounding loom 1.0 contract, so a reader cannot tell whether it is a current obligation, a recommendation, or a deferred action. The consequent "set the opt-out" action is also orphaned from the version-bump checklist item (r) that already owns the per-bump detection of whether such a field has appeared.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit

**Introducing commits:** `044985c` ("pi-loom spec: resolve \"loom-system-note re-enters model context as user-role text\"", 2026-05-07)

**History:** The corpus is git-tracked. `git log -S 'set the opt-out on the canonical'` and `git log -G 'SHOULD set the opt-out'` both bottom out at `044985c` (2026-05-07), which authored the *Custom-message channel persistence and LLM-context entry* paragraph in full while resolving the "loom-system-note re-enters model context as user-role text" finding; `git show 044985c` confirms the closing clause "a future Pi minor … at which point the runtime SHOULD set the opt-out on the canonical `sendSystemNote` call" was present verbatim at that authoring (then keyed to the `^0.72.1` pin and the `@mariozechner` scope). The defect has therefore existed since the paragraph's inception. Subsequent commits only carried it forward without introducing it: `78a2f94` (2026-06-04) relocated the section into `runtime-event-channel.md` per a misplaced-sections fix, `257c545` (2026-06-05) added the `custom-message-context-entry-presupposition` anchor and version-bump re-audit hook, and `158ed09` (2026-06-05) added owning-declaration pins for the bare SDK symbols. None added or materially reshaped the future-phase `SHOULD`.

## Solution approach

In `runtime-event-channel.md`'s `custom-message-context-entry-presupposition` paragraph, rewrite the trailing future-phase `SHOULD` clause as a phase-neutral descriptive statement of the loom 1.0 fact only — that the pinned Pi-SDK exposes no `CustomMessage.excludeFromContext` opt-out, so every `loom-system-note` enters the provider context durably — rather than a normative modal. Keep the forward-link to version-bump checklist item (r). Extend item (r) (`bump-checklist-convert-to-llm-context-entry`) in `version-bump-step2.md` to record the consequent action — adding the opt-out to the canonical `sendSystemNote` call — as the follow-on obligation on a bump where the candidate Pi minor introduces such a field, co-locating detection and consequent action in the procedure that fires on the triggering event.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: do not delete the no-opt-out fact itself or the `BashExecutionMessage.excludeFromContext` analogy referent and its `dist/core/messages.d.ts` declaration pin — item (r)'s no-opt-out claim and the durable-context observability claim are keyed on them.

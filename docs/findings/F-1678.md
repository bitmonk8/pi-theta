## Finding

PIC-1 in `runtime-event-channel.md` is the normative owner of the `masked` hard-ceiling co-fire contract: clauses (a)–(g) carry closed-set, canonical-absence, reachable-mask-domain, V1-reachable-predicate, pure-read, verbatim-copy, and dedup-key-non-inclusion rules, every one of which states a MUST/MUST-NOT. Sandwiched between the normative clause (e) ("Observable pure-read MUST") and clause (f) ("Verbatim-copy obligation") is a paragraph prefixed `*Implementation note (non-normative).*` describing the cheapest implementation mechanism that satisfies clause (e) — treating predicate detection as a pure read, caching the two scalars at event-construction time, and avoiding a re-run of ceiling #4's depth-walk.

The content of the note is correct and useful implementer guidance; the defect is purely positional. A reader skimming PIC-1 for its enforceable obligations must stop and parse a paragraph that constrains nothing, breaking the run of lettered normative clauses precisely where (e)→(f) should read contiguously. The corpus already owns a canonical home for exactly this kind of advisory material (`implementation-notes.md`), and other inline-implementation-note findings in this same review target the same destination.

That commit is itself a spec-review fix that reworked PIC-1 clause (e) into its observable, falsifiable "pure-read MUST" form; the implementation note was authored inline alongside the reworked clause to record the cheapest conforming mechanism. The defect — an advisory paragraph placed between the lettered normative clauses — was therefore born with the clause-(e) rework rather than carried from the page's inception. Subsequent edits to this page (`85daf5a` qualifying PIC-1 (c)(i), `cfb3943` coining PIC-54) touched neighbouring material but left the misplaced note untouched.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Move the `*Implementation note (non-normative).*` paragraph out of PIC-1 and into `implementation-notes.md` under its Runtime sub-section, so clauses (e) and (f) read contiguously. Preserve the note's full content verbatim (pure-read mechanism, the MAY-cache-two-scalars guidance, and the warning that re-running ceiling #4's depth-walk can itself trip an observable second emission and violate clause (e)), and anchor it so it explicitly cites PIC-1 clause (e) as the obligation it advises on. PIC-1 clause (e) is already self-contained and need carry no inline trace of the relocated note; a one-line non-normative pointer from clause (e) to the implementation-notes entry is optional and acceptable but not required.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader scanning PIC-1 for its enforceable clauses encounters a non-normative paragraph mid-run, raising parse cost at a dense normative section. No implementer is misled and no behaviour diverges — the note is correctly labelled non-normative and its guidance is preserved by relocation.

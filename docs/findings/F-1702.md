## Finding

The prompt-mode-only `Map<schemaSlug, registeredToolName>` is referred to inconsistently across the Pi-integration-contract shard set. Some sites pin the mode-qualified form **prompt-mode registration cache** (e.g. `registration-steps.md` PIC-36, PIC-49, and the same-paragraph "prior `LoomRegistry` snapshot, AJV validator cache, and prompt-mode registration cache"; `glossary.md` schema-slug entry; `schema-subset.md` cross-reference; `code-registry-runtime.md` `loom/runtime/registry-swap-failed` row), while sibling sites use the bare form **registration cache** — including the canonical definition site itself in `tool-registration-lifetime.md` (introduces it as "extension-scoped registration cache", then uses bare "registration cache" in the `/reload` cache-drop consequence bullet and the future-`unregisterTool` aside), as well as the step-5 rollback clause in `registration-steps.md` ("the never-published staged registry snapshot and staged registration-cache entries") and the live-state list in `registration-steps.md` PIC-49 and `package-and-settings.md`'s `caching-and-reload` paragraph ("live `LoomRegistry`, validator cache, and registration cache").

The defect is a naming drift, not a missing definition: the canonical-site qualifier "extension-scoped" describes the lifetime envelope, not the calling mode, and is not the qualifier the rest of the corpus uses. Subagent mode never calls `pi.registerTool` for synthesised tools — the tool flows through `customTools` on `createAgentSession` — so a bare "registration cache" reader who has not internalised that explicit subagent-mode disclaimer can plausibly infer a non-existent subagent-mode counterpart, especially when an implementer encounters the term first inside the `registration-steps.md` watcher / rebuild prose (which itself oscillates between bare and qualified within one paragraph).

The fix is uniform qualification at every cross-section site and at the canonical first-use site, with bare "registration cache" remaining acceptable only within a same-paragraph context that has already established the prompt-mode scope (typical mid-paragraph back-reference).

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `27ec53d` ("Resolve Cluster-A: per-mode tool registration", 2026-05-04) seeded the bare form ("extension-scoped registration cache" / "the registration cache"); `6d1a949`, `443ee4e`, and `941e52c` (early-to-mid June 2026, during the spec-shard split and PIC-36/PIC-49 coining) added the **prompt-mode registration cache** qualifier on `registration-steps.md` without back-propagating it to `tool-registration-lifetime.md` or to the per-paragraph bare-form residues that were created in step 5 alongside the qualified mentions.
**History:** `git log -S 'extension-scoped registration cache' -- docs/` shows the bare form entering at `27ec53d` on the pre-split `spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md`; the May 2026 large split (`f5e89f4`) carried it across to `tool-registration-lifetime.md` unchanged. `git log -S 'prompt-mode registration cache' -- docs/` shows the qualified form first appearing in `6d1a949` on `registration-steps.md` and accreting in `443ee4e` (PIC-49) and `941e52c` (PIC-36 / PIC-38). `git log -L /'prompt-mode registration'/,+1:docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract/registration-steps.md` confirms the qualifier was added inside the step-5 paragraph alongside surviving bare references in the same paragraph, never sweeping the canonical site. The drift therefore exists as the residue of two independent edit campaigns that did not share a vocabulary pass.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Pin **prompt-mode registration cache** as the canonical phrase, then sweep the corpus:

1. In `tool-registration-lifetime.md`, replace "extension-scoped registration cache" in the *Prompt mode* arm's first paragraph with "extension-scoped, prompt-mode registration cache" (keeping the lifetime descriptor so the per-extension-instance scope is not lost), and replace bare "registration cache" with "prompt-mode registration cache" in both the `/reload` consequence bullet and the closing future-`unregisterTool` aside.
2. In `registration-steps.md` step 5, replace the two bare residues ("and the registration cache in a single synchronous publish step", "discarding the never-published staged registry snapshot and staged registration-cache entries") with the qualified form; in PIC-49, replace "validator cache, and registration cache" with "validator cache, and prompt-mode registration cache" to match PIC-36's already-qualified parallel list.
3. In `package-and-settings.md`'s `caching-and-reload` paragraph, change the cross-reference's "validator cache, and registration cache" to "validator cache, and prompt-mode registration cache" so it agrees with PIC-49.
4. In `capability-inventory-items.md` ("cosmetic registration-cache accumulation"), `drain-state-contract.md` PIC-30 ("the registration-cache leak"), and `implementation-notes.md` typed-query Runtime bullet ("via the registration cache"), prepend "prompt-mode" once at the first reference in the paragraph; later same-paragraph back-references may stay bare.

Acceptable back-reference rule (worth pinning explicitly in the canonical paragraph as a one-line authoring note, e.g. in the glossary schema-slug entry that already canonicalises the term): once a paragraph or list item has named the **prompt-mode registration cache**, subsequent same-paragraph references may use bare "registration cache" without re-qualifying. This keeps the diagnostics-table cells and the PIC-36 / PIC-49 enumerations readable while removing the cross-section ambiguity.

No new normative rule is introduced; the term carries the same referent at every site after the sweep.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Two reasonable implementers reading the bare form at the canonical site or in the step-5 prose can infer that a parallel subagent-mode registration cache exists, then waste implementation or test effort on it before being corrected by the *Subagent mode* arm's "never calls `pi.registerTool`" disclaimer. The corpus already enforces the qualifier mechanically in the diagnostics-table rows and the glossary schema-slug entry, so the drift is between authoritative definition sites, not just prose flavour.

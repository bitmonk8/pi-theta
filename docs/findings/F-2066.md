## Problem

The *Failure-surface contract* paragraph (anchor `audit-failure-surface-contract`) in `audit-failures.md` is the normative definition of the exhaustive five-family taxonomy, the `audit/<class>/<family>/<symptom>` discriminator shape, the three-class partition, the per-`<symptom>` fixture floor, and the family→step-2(b) routing table that every other substantive section of the file cites by family number or by anchor. It is positioned last in the file, after the four sections that depend on it — *Stale sub-kinds*, *Substitution limitation*, *Stale-citation limitation*, and *Malformed-marker discriminator — family-(5) routing* (anchor `audit-malformed-marker-routing`). A reader working the file top-to-bottom meets the family identifiers and discriminator shape as undefined forward references and must jump to the closing paragraph to resolve them.

**History:** `git show e5cd514:docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md` shows the *Stale sub-kinds* paragraph (with its "per the *Failure-surface contract* below" forward reference) authored at line 708 and the *Failure-surface contract* paragraph at line 715 — the defining section was placed after its dependents in the very commit that introduced the audit. `git log --follow` over `audit-failures.md` confirms `f5e89f4` later split the monolithic `pi-integration-contract.md` into the `audit-*.md` siblings, carrying the same relative order into `audit-failures.md` unchanged; subsequent edits (`b75362a`, `8c5a126`) touched content but never re-ordered the sections. The inverted ordering is therefore original to the audit's authoring.

## Solution approach

Move the *Failure-surface contract* block — together with its co-located *Three-class partition*, the family→step-2(b) routing table, and the *Per-family record-shape table* (anchor `audit-per-family-record-shape-table`) — to the head of `audit-failures.md`, ahead of the *Stale sub-kinds* section, so the five-family taxonomy and discriminator shape are established before any section references them. Update the now-stale directional words in the dependent sections, changing each *Failure-surface contract* **below** reference to **above**.

## Solution constraints

- Keep the `audit-failure-surface-contract` and `audit-per-family-record-shape-table` anchors with their paragraphs; do not rename or drop them — cross-page citations resolve to these anchor targets.

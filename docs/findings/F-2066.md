## Finding

The *Failure-surface contract* paragraph (`<a id="audit-failure-surface-contract">`) in `audit-failures.md` is the normative anchor for the entire inventory-closure audit: it establishes the **exhaustive five-family taxonomy** — (1) unauthorised `pi.<member>`, (2) unauthorised peer-package / typebox surface, (3) unauthorised `ctx.<member>`, (4) out-of-scope import/access shape, (5) stale or malformed exemption marker — together with the `audit/<class>/<family>/<symptom>` discriminator shape, the three-class partition, the per-`<symptom>` fixture floor, and the family→step-2(b) routing table. Every downstream discriminator obligation, fixture floor, and routing rule in the cluster cites this taxonomy by family number or by anchor.

Yet this paragraph is positioned **last** among the substantive sections of `audit-failures.md`. The four sections that precede it — *Stale sub-kinds*, *Substitution limitation*, *Stale-citation limitation*, and *Malformed-marker discriminator — family-(5) routing* — all reference the families before they are defined. *Stale sub-kinds* opens with "The audit MUST surface every stale marker under **family (5)** … (per the *Failure-surface contract* **below**)"; the malformed-marker routing section asserts "family (5)" and "family (1) / (2) / (3) discriminator" routing; both lean on the family numbering and the discriminator shape that only appear further down the file. A reader working the file (or the broader six-file cluster) top-to-bottom encounters the family identifiers as undefined forward references and must jump ahead to the closing paragraph to resolve them. The arrangement is inverted: the definition that all dependent rules cite is the terminal section in reading order rather than the preamble.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Move the *Failure-surface contract* paragraph (`<a id="audit-failure-surface-contract">`) in `audit-failures.md`, together with its co-located *Three-class partition*, the family→step-2(b) routing table, and the *Per-family record-shape table*, to the top of `audit-failures.md`, ahead of the *Stale sub-kinds* section. This establishes the five-family taxonomy and the `audit/<class>/<family>/<symptom>` discriminator shape before any section references them. Keep the `<a id="audit-failure-surface-contract">` anchor with the paragraph; it stays in the same file, so no cross-page anchor target changes and no other file needs editing.

After relocating the block to the head of the file, update the now-stale directional words in the *Stale sub-kinds* and *Malformed-marker discriminator* sections: change each "*Failure-surface contract* **below**" reference to "**above**". Sweep both sections for any remaining "below" directional that should now read "above".

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

No implementer is misled into a divergent implementation — the taxonomy is complete and unambiguous once read, and the cross-page anchors resolve regardless of position. The cost is readability and orientation: every reader of the cluster meets `family (N)` tokens and discriminator-shape references before the section that defines them, forcing a forward jump to the file's last paragraph to establish the vocabulary the dependent rules already assume.

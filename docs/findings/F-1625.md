## Finding

Inside item (e)'s fail-outcome recovery prose, `version-bump-step2.md` states that the per-extension-instance keying granularity is well-defined only while both the one-`pi`-per-extension-instance lifetime and the one-active-user-session-per-extension-instance presupposition hold — "together, on the recovery path, the keyed recovery serialisation is the unique serialisation point per user session". Two phrases in that consequence clause read more than one way.

First, "together" has an ambiguous antecedent. Read against the two-invariant precondition it just stated ("while **both** … AND … hold"), it most naturally means "when those two invariants hold together"; but its position immediately before "on the recovery path" also admits a reading where it conjoins the recovery-path subject rather than the preconditions. Second, "unique serialisation point per user session" is two-way readable: (a) the keyed serialisation is the *only* synchronisation mechanism serialising the recovery path (a sole-mechanism claim that would, on a strict reading, forbid any additional layered serialisation), versus (b) the keying granularity resolves to *exactly one* serialisation point per active user session — one instance per session, neither shared across sessions nor duplicated within one (a one-per-session keying claim).

The surrounding clause already pins the keying mechanism unambiguously ("keyed on the factory-captured `pi: ExtensionAPI` reference", "per-extension-instance keying granularity"), so reading (b) is the intended one: given one `pi` per extension instance and one active user session per extension instance, per-`pi` keying collapses to exactly one serialisation point per user session. The sentence should say that directly rather than leaving the "unique" sense and the "together" antecedent for the reader to reconstruct.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Replace the clause "together, on the recovery path, the keyed recovery serialisation is the unique serialisation point per user session" with a statement that names the intended keying-granularity meaning and binds "together" to the two invariants explicitly, e.g.:

> When both invariants hold, the per-`pi` keying granularity collapses to exactly one recovery-serialisation point per active user session — precisely one such point per session, neither shared across sessions nor duplicated within one — and on the recovery path that point is the construct that serialises the snapshot → swap → body → restore sequence.

Keep the existing parenthetical that the serialisation exists only when the recovery path is engaged on a fail outcome. Do not introduce a sole-mechanism prohibition: the intended claim is one-per-session keying granularity, not that no other serialisation may coexist.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader who takes "unique serialisation point" as a sole-mechanism claim may believe additional layered serialisation on the recovery path is prohibited; a reader who takes it as a keying-granularity claim builds one lock per active session. Because the keying mechanism is pinned explicitly in the same clause, divergence is unlikely to reach wrong code, but the dense double-ambiguity slows comprehension of an already long contingency paragraph and leaves the derived guarantee weaker than its surrounding pins.

## Finding

The Wire-name renaming prose in `schemas.md` states that, after an `as "WireName"` rename, "every other corner of the language sees only that identifier, and the lowercase-first rule still applies to it." The phrase "the lowercase-first rule" is a bare reference: it carries no hyperlink and no anchor. The rule it names is the identifier-casing constraint in `lexical.md` ("**lowercase-first** … is required for: `let` and `let mut` bindings, function parameters, function names, and schema field names"), but `schemas.md` contains no link to `lexical.md` at all.

The target rule is also un-anchored on the owning page. The `lexical.md` Identifiers bullet that defines lowercase-first has no `<a id="…">` (the anchors on that page are `rest-of-line`, `stray-backslash`, `extension-matching`, and `string-literals`). So even a reader who knows to look in `lexical.md` cannot deep-link to the constraint, and a reader of `schemas.md` alone cannot determine what the loom-side field identifier must satisfy or which diagnostic (`loom/parse/binding-case-mismatch`) fires on violation.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Add an anchor to the owning rule and link to it from the citing site:

- In `docs/spec_topics/lexical.md`, attach an `<a id="lowercase-first"></a>` to the **lowercase-first** identifier bullet that defines the rule.
- In `docs/spec_topics/schemas.md`, replace the bare phrase "the lowercase-first rule still applies to it" with a link to that anchor, e.g. "the [lowercase-first rule](./lexical.md#lowercase-first) still applies to it."

Keep the anchor slug stable; other pages that mention lowercase-first casing can adopt the same link target. No prose beyond the link text needs to change, and no diagnostic restatement is required since `lexical.md` already names `loom/parse/binding-case-mismatch`.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reader confined to `schemas.md` cannot resolve the constraint or its diagnostic without already knowing it lives in `lexical.md`; this is a navigation gap, not a behavioural divergence. The rule itself is fully specified on its owning page, so implementers can still produce a working system.

## Finding

Step 0 (d) ("Peer-dep version (lock-step, four packages)") in `capability-probe.md` writes the per-package version read as a normative algorithm: "resolve the package's main entry via `createRequire(import.meta.url).resolve("<pkg>")` … then walk parent directories from the resolved entry path up to the first `package.json` whose `name` field equals the iteration's `<pkg>` and read its `version` field." Two of the four enumerated `peer-dep-malformed-version` routing conditions are then defined in terms of that exact mechanic's failure modes — condition (1) "a `MODULE_NOT_FOUND` thrown by the main-entry resolution call" and condition (2) "a parent-directory walk that reaches the filesystem root without finding a `package.json` whose `name` field equals the iteration's `<pkg>`" — rather than in terms of an observable property of the host installation (the named package's `package.json` is unresolvable / its parsed object has no `version` field / its `version` is not valid SemVer / its `version` is out of the pinned range).

What the runtime contract actually owes its downstream consumers is the four-failure-category routing into `peer-dep-out-of-range` / `peer-dep-malformed-version` / `probe-failed` plus the `details.observed` / `details.required` / `details.package` payload shape — `code-registry-load.md`, `placeholder-rendering-b.md`, and the orchestration of (c)+(d) all key off those, not off `createRequire` semantics. The same step's SemVer comparison is treated as a *Recommended recipe (non-normative)* explicitly to leave room for a future swap, and the immediate parent step (a) Node-floor uses the same recipe / contract split. Step (d)'s algorithm is the same kind of implementation detail but is written into the normative body, with the failure taxonomy bound to its internal symbols. A future implementation that locates the four peers by a different mechanism cannot be conformant by reproducing the observable contract — it has to either reproduce the `createRequire` + walk algorithm itself or invent a re-mapping from its native errors into the spec's algorithm-internal failure tokens, which makes the spec brittle against tooling drift in a way the rest of Step 0 deliberately avoids.

Compounding the over-prescription, the deployment shape the algorithm presupposes is never stated as a precondition: that each of the four `@earendil-works/*` peers exists as a *discrete*, *on-disk* package carrying a synchronously-readable `package.json` reachable by Node's CommonJS resolver from the loom factory's module. That shape is load-bearing for the probe's diagnostic semantics. A loom build that inlines the four peers (a future repackaging through any bundler that traverses `peerDependencies` and ships their JS into the extension's own `dist/`, or any vendored layout in which the peers exist as in-tree source rather than as resolved package roots) makes `createRequire(...).resolve("<pkg>")` throw `MODULE_NOT_FOUND` even though the runtime is fully conformant — the four peers are present, just not as resolvable packages. The probe then emits `loom/load/host-incompatible` with `details.kind = "peer-dep-malformed-version"` on every peer, a false-negative that misclassifies the deployment rather than the host. The loom 1.0 packaging model the rest of the spec describes (peer dependencies declared in `package.json#peerDependencies` for skew detection by `pnpm` / `yarn`, the extension auto-discovered from `package.json#pi.extensions`) is the deployment shape in which (d) is well-defined; the probe contract should make that scope explicit so the four routing conditions read as gating that precondition rather than as a packaging-shape policy in disguise.

The fix is to state the deployment-shape precondition once at the head of (d), restate (d)'s normative body in observable terms, and demote the read algorithm to a recommended-recipe callout on the same footing as (a) and (d)'s own SemVer recipe; the four-category routing, the iteration order, the short-circuit, and the payload-field shape all stay normative.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** multi-commit-interaction
**Introducing commits:** `126ff8a` (2026-05-06, "pi-loom spec: Step 0 capability probe rewrite + V1.0 deferrals + Choice B", Thomas Andersen); `d2a6d6f` (2026-05-07, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Capability probe verifies only one of four lock-step peer-dep versions\"", Thomas Andersen); `4c27f32` (2026-06-03, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"Step 0 (d) peer-dep version probe relies on a subpath export\"", Thomas Andersen); `9371309` (2026-06-05, "pi-loom spec: resolve \"foreseeable shape failure clause leaves kind discriminator under-specified\"", Thomas Andersen)
**History:** The Step-0 rewrite under `126ff8a` introduced (d) as a single-package read with `createRequire(...).resolve("@mariozechner/pi-coding-agent/package.json")` already written into the normative body, paired with a separated non-normative SemVer recipe — and baked the discrete-on-disk-install assumption into the mechanic from the moment it landed, without stating it as a precondition. `d2a6d6f` generalised (d) to the four-package lock-step iteration but propagated the `createRequire`-subpath form into the per-iteration text unchanged, carrying the unstated assumption forward. `4c27f32` then changed the mechanic itself — main-entry resolution + parent-directory walk — to avoid `ERR_PACKAGE_PATH_NOT_EXPORTED`, embedding the new algorithm and its specific throw sites into the normative routing prose and adding the root-bound-walk failure condition (still presupposing discrete on-disk packages). `9371309` closed the discriminator gap by enumerating the four `peer-dep-malformed-version` conditions explicitly, but two of those four are written as failures of the `4c27f32` mechanic rather than as observable properties of the host installation. The defect is the *interaction*: the SemVer-recipe split established alongside the algorithm in `126ff8a` was never extended to the read-mechanic itself, each subsequent revision tightened the algorithm-and-taxonomy coupling, and the discrete-on-disk deployment shape the algorithm presupposes was never promoted from an implicit assumption to a stated precondition.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Restate Step 0 (d)'s per-package read in observable terms, state the deployment-shape precondition it presupposes, and move the `createRequire` + parent-walk algorithm into a non-normative recipe callout on the same footing as the existing SemVer recipe at `#step-0-d-recommended-recipe`.

Concretely, in `capability-probe.md`:

- Add a one-sentence deployment-shape precondition immediately before the four-package iteration in Step 0 (d): the four `@earendil-works/*` peer packages are presupposed to be installed as discrete, on-disk packages each carrying a synchronously-readable `package.json` reachable by Node's CommonJS resolver from the loom factory's module, and the four `peer-dep-malformed-version` routing conditions below gate that precondition. Reference the [Pi SDK pin / Manifest lock-step](./host-prerequisites.md#pi-sdk-pin-manifest-lock-step) framing as the corpus-wide source of the packaging model; do not invent a new packaging contract here. Bundled-or-vendored loom builds in which the four peers are inlined rather than installed as discrete packages are explicitly out-of-scope of the loom 1.0 probe and not host-conformance failures.
- Replace the normative sentence "resolve the package's main entry via `createRequire(...).resolve("<pkg>")` … then walk parent directories … and read its `version` field" with an observable statement: *"For each package in turn, the probe MUST read the installed `version` field from the named peer package's own `package.json` by any `exports`-independent mechanic that, for an iteration whose target package name is `<pkg>`, returns the `version` field of the `package.json` whose `name` field equals `<pkg>` and which sits in the dependency tree resolvable from the loom factory's module."* Keep the "resolution context is the loom factory's module, since the extension may be installed globally" clause as a normative precondition on the mechanic.
- Re-cast the four `peer-dep-malformed-version` conditions in installation-observable terms: (1) the named peer's own `package.json` is not resolvable from the loom factory's module; (2) no `package.json` in the resolution path has a `name` field equal to the iteration's `<pkg>`; (3) the located `package.json` parses but has no own `version` field; (4) the located `version` field is not a syntactically valid SemVer string. Keep (3) and (4) as-is; only (1) and (2) need rephrasing off their algorithm-internal symbols. The `kind` set, iteration order, short-circuit, and `details.observed` / `details.required` / `details.package` payload shape stay normative and unchanged.
- Add a *Recommended recipe (non-normative)* callout (or extend the existing `#step-0-d-recommended-recipe` callout) giving the `createRequire(import.meta.url).resolve("<pkg>")` + parent-directory-walk algorithm as the recommended implementation, with the existing `ERR_PACKAGE_PATH_NOT_EXPORTED` rationale and the existing "swap permitted as long as the contract is preserved" clause.
- In *Self-failure*, keep the existing carve-out as-is — "every other throw inside step (d) … routes to `kind: "probe-failed"`" already keys off "is none of the four conditions above", so no edit is needed once the four conditions are re-stated observably.

In `docs/spec_topics/diagnostics/code-registry-load.md`, mirror the wording change in the `peer-dep-malformed-version` clause: replace "the `package.json` is unresolvable via `MODULE_NOT_FOUND`, or the parent-directory walk reaches the filesystem root without a matching `package.json`" with the same observable phrasing the spec uses ("the named peer's `package.json` is not resolvable from the loom factory's module, or no resolved `package.json` carries a `name` field equal to the iteration's package"). `placeholder-rendering-b.md` does not need an edit.

Implementer-relevant edge cases:

- The deployment-shape precondition is what makes "`MODULE_NOT_FOUND` on the main-entry resolution call" a `peer-dep-malformed-version` rather than a packaging-shape branch in (d); stating it once at the head of (d) lets the four routing conditions read as gating that precondition, and any future repackaging is recognised as out-of-scope rather than as a host-conformance bug.
- The recommended recipe MUST stay textually present, because the family-(4) CJS-reach carve-out in `audit-target-categories.md` is currently scoped to `createRequire(...).resolve(<peer-specifier>)`; dropping the recommended mechanic entirely would either widen that carve-out or strand the only currently-documented conformant recipe.
- The observable phrasing of condition (2) MUST still pin the `name`-field equality check, not a path-shape heuristic — a vendored install where four peers share one `node_modules/<pkg>` directory with a wrapper `package.json` whose `name` does not match must still route to condition (2).

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A second-source implementation that locates the four peers by an `exports`-independent mechanic other than `createRequire` + parent-walk would have to either reproduce the algorithm or invent a re-mapping from its native error surface into the spec's algorithm-internal failure tokens. Separately, the unstated discrete-on-disk precondition leaves the probe's failure semantics ambiguous for any future repackaging: a bundled-or-vendored build of loom would have its four peers silently misclassified as `peer-dep-malformed-version` on conformant hosts, with no in-spec hook explaining why or recording that the deployment shape is out of scope. The current text is also internally asymmetric with (a) and with (d)'s own SemVer-comparator recipe split, which is the precedent contributors will reach for when revisiting (d). Nothing breaks at loom 1.0 with the recommended mechanic on the deployment shape loom 1.0 actually ships under; the cost is paid by future drift.

## Finding

`audit-recognised-shapes.md`'s "Recognised import/access shapes" paragraph enumerates a wide set of prohibited shapes that all route to family (4) of the inventory-closure audit's *Failure-surface contract*. The paragraph closes each one with the same clause — "the prohibition is in family (4) under its own discriminator" — and that clause appears eight times across structurally-distinct shape groups (CJS-reach forms, computed access, namespace destructuring, aliased rebindings (value-binding + aliased-import specifiers + symmetric re-export), captured rebindings, off-canonical-name `ctx`/`pi` parameters, off-canonical-annotation `ctx`/`pi` parameters, destructured carrier parameters, wrapped/intersected/union/generic-applied/aliased carrier annotations, generic-laundering via type-parameter constraint, subtype-creation via `extends`/`implements`/`&`, non-parameter bindings carrying a carrier annotation, plus the bare-paragraph prohibitions on `import * as pi`, default/dynamic/side-effect-only peer imports, `Object.assign({}, pi)`, `keyof typeof pi`, and barrel-module re-exports). Expanded to the leaf sub-kinds an implementer would write distinct AST matchers for, that is ≥18 distinct prohibited shapes.

`audit-failures.md`'s *Failure-surface contract* requires "at least one negative-test fixture per stable `<symptom>` token enumerated under each family". For family (5) the contract enumerates the `<symptom>` set inline ((s1), (s2), plus malformed-marker clauses (a)–(g)) so the per-`<symptom>` floor multiplies to nine fixtures. Family (4) carries no analogous enumeration: PIC leaves the `<family>` and `<symptom>` strings implementation-owned and the family-(4) shape inventory is buried in the recognised-shapes paragraph without being lifted into a named sub-kind list. A conformant implementation may therefore pick exactly one `<symptom>` token for the whole of family (4), publish a single fixture exercising (say) `import * as pi`, and satisfy the floor while leaving 17+ shapes — including the audit-critical generic-laundering and subtype-creation cases the recognised-shapes paragraph explicitly calls out as silently bypassing the carrier-detection branch — entirely untested.

The asymmetry is not stylistic. The carrier-laundering and subtype-creation prohibitions are precisely the shapes that would cause Pi-side surface to escape inventory coverage without firing any other family's discriminator; an audit that compiles, runs green, and ships without ever exercising them in a negative test is the failure mode the per-`<symptom>` floor was added to family (5) to prevent.

The asymmetry is therefore original to the audit's authoring, not a regression from later editing.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In `audit-recognised-shapes.md`, lift the family-(4) prohibited shapes out of the run-on paragraph into a named sub-kind list — one entry per AST-matcher-distinct shape group, each tagged with a stable spec-side sub-kind identifier (e.g. `f4-import-star`, `f4-default-peer-import`, `f4-dynamic-import`, `f4-side-effect-only-import`, `f4-cjs-require`, `f4-create-require-indirection`, `f4-other-cjs-indirection`, `f4-computed-access`, `f4-namespace-destructuring`, `f4-value-binding-alias`, `f4-aliased-import-specifier`, `f4-aliased-re-export`, `f4-captured-rebinding`, `f4-off-canonical-name-ctx`, `f4-off-canonical-annotation-ctx`, `f4-off-canonical-name-pi`, `f4-off-canonical-annotation-pi`, `f4-destructured-carrier-param`, `f4-wrapped-carrier-annotation`, `f4-generic-laundering`, `f4-subtype-creation`, `f4-non-parameter-carrier-binding`, `f4-object-assign-pi`, `f4-keyof-typeof-pi`, `f4-barrel-module-reexport`, `f4-export-star-peer`). The exact partition is spec-owned; the load-bearing property is that every clause currently closing with "the prohibition is in family (4) under its own discriminator" — and every bare prohibition in the same paragraph (`import * as pi`, default/dynamic/side-effect-only, `Object.assign({}, pi)`, `keyof typeof pi`, barrel-module / `export *`) — becomes a named sub-kind.

In `audit-failures.md`'s *Failure-surface contract*, extend the per-`<symptom>` fixture floor obligation to name family (4) alongside family (5) explicitly: "families with multiple sub-kinds — notably family (4) with sub-kinds enumerated under *Recognised import/access shapes* and family (5) with stale sub-kinds (s1)/(s2) and malformed-marker clauses (a)–(g) — get one fixture per sub-kind". The implementation retains ownership of the literal `<symptom>` token strings (consistent with the rest of the contract) but MUST publish one stable token plus one negative-test fixture per family-(4) sub-kind, and MUST NOT collapse multiple sub-kinds onto a single `<symptom>`.

Update the leaf-side family→step-2(b) routing-table obligation in the same paragraph so the per-row branch subset for family (4) is asserted once per sub-kind in the leaf's test invariants (the routing target — branches (1) / (2) / (5) — is uniform across sub-kinds and remains pinned by the existing row).

Edge cases for the implementer: (a) the sub-kind list is closed by PIC at authoring; if a future bump uncovers a new laundering shape the audit must recognise, adding it requires extending both the recognised-shapes enumeration and the implementation's `<symptom>` set in the same commit (the same co-edit discipline step 2(b) branch (2) already pins for `SDK_SURFACE_INVENTORY`). (b) The dual-emission obligation on a family-(4) line carrying an `// allow-pi-surface:` marker (the marker fires family (5), the shape fires family (4)) continues to apply per sub-kind — the malformed-marker dual-emission rule keys on the *underlying* surface's family, not on a per-`<symptom>` decomposition. (c) The fixture corpus grows roughly fourfold for the audit leaf; account for it in `V18b-T`'s scope.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

Two implementers of the audit can ship conformant builds with materially different family-(4) coverage; the audit's load-bearing prohibitions against generic-laundering, subtype-creation, captured rebindings, and aliased-import specifiers can be left without any negative-test fixture, so a regression that re-introduces one of those laundering shapes on a future bump would not fire a red. The leaf-side family→step-2(b) routing table the *Failure-surface contract* requires becomes a paper guarantee: the wire token for family (4) is asserted via fixtures, but the family's behavioural surface is not.

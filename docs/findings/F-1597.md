## Problem

Family (4) (out-of-scope import/access shape) of the inventory-closure audit's *Failure-surface contract* covers 18+ structurally-distinct prohibited shapes — CJS reach, computed access, namespace destructuring, aliased rebindings, captured rebindings, off-canonical `ctx`/`pi` carriers, generic-laundering, subtype-creation, and more — but they are buried in the run-on *Recognised import/access shapes* paragraph and never lifted into a named sub-kind enumeration. The per-`<symptom>` fixture-floor obligation in `audit-failures.md` is calibrated only to family (5)'s enumerated tokens; family (4) carries no analogous sub-kind list. A conformant implementation may therefore pick a single `<symptom>` token for the whole of family (4), publish one fixture, and satisfy the floor while leaving the audit-critical generic-laundering and subtype-creation shapes — the shapes that escape every other family's discriminator — without any negative-test fixture.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception
**Introducing commit:** `e5cd514` (2026-05-09) — *pi-loom spec: resolve "T35 SDK capability inventory closed-set negative-direction audit"*
**History:** The T35 commit established both halves of the defect in one motion: the recognised-shapes paragraph already enumerated the eight `"the prohibition is in family (4) under its own discriminator"` clusters (`git show e5cd514:docs/spec_topics/pi-integration-contract.md | grep -c …` returns 8, matching the live count), and the *Failure-surface contract* already carried the per-`<symptom>` fixture floor calibrated to family (5)'s nine tokens. No subsequent edit (the file-split at `f5e89f4`, the audit-cluster realign at `23d28a1`, the createRequire / aliased-import / subtype-creation / non-parameter-binding tightenings at `81b0e67` / `ce48e09` / `4b4382f`) lifted family (4)'s shape list into a parallel `<symptom>` enumeration. The asymmetry is therefore original to the audit's authoring, not a regression from later editing.

## Solution approach

In `audit-recognised-shapes.md`, lift the family-(4) prohibited shapes out of the run-on *Recognised import/access shapes* paragraph into a named sub-kind list, one entry per AST-matcher-distinct shape group, each carrying a stable spec-side sub-kind identifier. In `audit-failures.md`'s *Failure-surface contract*, extend the per-`<symptom>` fixture-floor obligation to name family (4) alongside family (5), requiring one stable `<symptom>` token plus one negative-test fixture per family-(4) sub-kind. Update the leaf-side family→step-2(b) routing-table obligation so its per-row branch subset for family (4) is asserted per sub-kind.

## Solution constraints

- The spec keeps the literal `<symptom>` / `<family>` token strings implementation-owned — name spec-side sub-kinds, not the implementation's wire tokens.
- Do not pull the `createRequire(...).resolve("<peer>")` path-resolution shape into family (4); it remains the Step 0(d) peer-dep version-read carve-out, outside family (4).

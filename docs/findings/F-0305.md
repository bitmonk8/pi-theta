## Problem

The *Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance* clause in `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline is the authoritative trigger for when a new leaf must be added to `H5b`'s `Deps.`, and its verb "can introduce" admits two incompatible readings: a factual *will-in-fact-introduce* reading (the leaf's `Tests.` bullets carry one of the three trigger kinds) and a speculative *has-the-potential-to-introduce* reading. The clause's own parenthetical disambiguates only the closing-test trigger, leaving the "executable REQ-ID" and "un-anchored normative MUST" triggers governed by the ambiguous verb. A narrow reader can omit a genuine coverage-producing leaf, so the `H5b` canary and the `H6a` hard-fail release gate sequence ahead of it and activate against incomplete coverage; the `H5d` per-cell at-least-one arm backstops only the wholly-omitted-cell case, not every-facet or un-anchored-MUST under-inclusion.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** present-since-inception (of the clause)
**Introducing commit:** `37733fd` — "pi-loom plan: resolve \"H6a transitive-completeness rule parked in Deps, not conventions\"" (2026-06-11)
**History:** The corpus is git-tracked. `git log -S"Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance" -- docs/plan_topics/conventions.md` and `git log -G"can introduce an executable"` both place the clause's first appearance at `37733fd`, which moved the rule into `conventions.md` and authored it as "Whenever a new leaf is added that **can introduce** an executable REQ-ID, a numbered-REQ-ID citing test, or an un-anchored normative MUST …". Later commits `d7173b3` and `ab8e297` (both 2026-06-11) refined the three triggers and the surrounding prose but preserved the "can introduce" verb. The ambiguity has been present continuously since the clause was first written.

## Solution approach

Rewrite the *Transitive-completeness plan-maintenance* trigger in `conventions.md` §REQ-ID discipline so it turns on what the leaf's `Tests.` bullets actually carry rather than the speculative "can introduce" verb, replacing that verb with a factual present-tense formulation across all three triggers (executable REQ-ID, closing test, un-anchored normative MUST). Retain the existing closing-test parenthetical verbatim, since it disambiguates the orthogonal mapping-vs-first-occurrence question. Mirror the same factual phrasing in the §Leaf format `Deps.` field bullet of the same file, which restates the trigger and must stay in lockstep with the owner clause.

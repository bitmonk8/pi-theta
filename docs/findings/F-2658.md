## Finding

In `tool-calls.md`, the paragraph distinguishing `CodeToolError` from `ModelToolError` opens:

> `CodeToolError` is distinct from `ModelToolError`, and the two surfaces dispose of the *same* `execute()` outcomes differently because the contexts differ …

The topic-sentence phrase "the two surfaces dispose of the *same* `execute()` outcomes" is loose. A given `execute()` invocation runs in exactly one context — either a code-side `<name>(args)` call or the model-driven `@`-query tool-call loop — so a literal reading ("a single call's outcome flows to both surfaces") is nonsensical. The intended meaning is that the *same categories* of `execute()` outcome (a throw, or an `{ content, isError: true }` return) are routed to different surfaces depending on which context the call occurs in.

The elaboration after the colon ("a code-side `<name>(args)` call lowers an `execute()` throw … whereas the model-driven `@`-query tool-call loop feeds that same outcome back …") does resolve the intended reading on a careful pass, so the defect is confined to the topic sentence rather than the normative behaviour. Aligning the lead-in with the elaboration removes the momentary misread.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Reword the topic sentence so the lead-in matches the elaboration: replace

> the two surfaces dispose of the *same* `execute()` outcomes differently because the contexts differ

with

> the two surfaces route the same *categories* of `execute()` outcome to different surfaces depending on call context

(or equivalent), keeping the existing "a code-side … whereas the model-driven …" elaboration unchanged. Emphasising *categories* and "depending on call context" forecloses the single-call-to-both-surfaces misreading while preserving the existing meaning.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader who stops at the topic sentence may momentarily entertain the nonsensical "one outcome flows to both surfaces" reading, but the same paragraph's elaboration disambiguates it before any implementation decision is made. No implementer diverges and no observable behaviour is affected; this is pure wording.

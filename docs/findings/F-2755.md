## Finding

The `ERR-11` test bullet in `V4c` (and the identical bullet in its paired test leaf `V4c-T`) reads: "the non-mutation window binds between the cancelled turn and the next driver send." The verb construction "window binds between X and Y" is ambiguous between two readings: (a) *definitional* — the bullet is naming an interval (the window that spans the cancelled turn to the next driver send), or (b) *constraint* — some property "binds" (holds) across that interval. Under reading (a) there is no asserted behaviour at all; under reading (b) the bullet still does not say *which* property must hold. A test author cannot determine what observable to assert.

The spec rule the bullet paraphrases is unambiguous: `errors-and-results/error-model.md` ERR-11 ("Mid-stream cancellation, respond-repair scope window") states that *for typed queries with respond-repair follow-ups, the non-mutation and no-compensating-injection obligations bind the runtime between the cancelled streaming turn and the next driver send*, while the respond-repair loop's own conversation appends remain governed by the query failure-and-repair page. The plan's paraphrase drops both the subject (which obligations bind) and the respond-repair scoping that is the entire point of ERR-11 — it is the rule that bounds the non-mutation window for respond-repair queries so the loop's own appends are not mistaken for prohibited mutation.

Because the bullet is the gate-relevant test description and its paired `-T` leaf is what an implementer writes the failing test from, the lost detail forces the author back to the spec to recover the assertion.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A test author working only from the `V4c-T` ERR-11 bullet cannot tell whether to assert an interval definition or a non-mutation constraint, and the respond-repair scoping is absent. The leaf cites `error-model.md`, so a diligent author recovers the intended assertion from spec ERR-11, but the bullet on its own does not ground the test it gates.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Rewrite the `ERR-11` bullet in both `docs/plan_topics/V4c-terminal-outcomes.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V4c-T-terminal-outcomes.md` (the wording is currently identical in both Tests sections) so it names the obligation that holds and the interval it holds across, restoring the respond-repair scoping from spec ERR-11.

Replace the existing bullet text:

`- `ERR-11`: the non-mutation window binds between the cancelled turn and the next driver send.`

with:

`- `ERR-11`: for a typed query with a respond-repair follow-up, the non-mutation and no-compensating-injection obligations hold throughout the interval from the cancelled streaming turn to the next driver send — committed surfaces are not mutated and no compensating turn is injected anywhere in that interval (the respond-repair loop's own appends are out of scope).`

Keep the two leaves' bullets identical to each other (the `-T` leaf authors the failing test the implementation leaf later satisfies). The spec page `errors-and-results/error-model.md` ERR-11 is the authoritative source and is read-only for this fix.

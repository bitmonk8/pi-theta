## Finding

The closing paragraph of `### File-extension namespace` in `docs/spec_topics/discovery/discovery-sources.md` reads:

> This check is a point-in-time observation, not a guarantee. The check has no REQ-ID, no per-leaf test obligation, and emits no diagnostic; it exists to record the namespace-clearance decision alongside the parallel `pi` manifest-namespace decision in the framing paragraph.

The paragraph itself names every property that disqualifies it as a normative spec statement: no REQ-ID, no per-leaf test obligation, no diagnostic — and then states its purpose explicitly as recording a past decision. Sitting under a `### ` heading inside a normative topic file, it imports decision-log discipline (history of why the namespace clearance was performed) into the implementation-target corpus, which per [GOV-18 arm (b)](../spec_topics/governance/corpus-direction-and-scope.md#gov-18-arm-b) is reserved for spec invariants. The corpus has no general convention for embedded ADR-shaped prose, and no other normative `###` subsection in `discovery-sources.md` carries a self-declared no-obligation disclaimer of this form.

The pattern is not isolated to this one paragraph: the two paragraphs above it (the `.loom`/`.warp` clearance assertion, the `--loom` flag-name clearance assertion, and the "Pi has no central file-extension registry" observation) are also point-in-time clearance observations rather than implementation obligations — the flagged paragraph is just the one that admits it. The fix therefore has to decide whether to remove the self-declaration alone or to recategorise the surrounding text it covers.

## Solution Space

### Recommendation

In `docs/spec_topics/discovery/discovery-sources.md`, delete the closing paragraph of `### File-extension namespace` in its entirety — the paragraph beginning "This check is a point-in-time observation, not a guarantee." Leave the three preceding paragraphs (`.loom`/`.warp` clearance, `--loom` flag clearance, "no central file-extension registry" observation) unchanged.

Make no anchor changes and no cross-reference updates. The `#file-extension-namespace` anchor sits on the preceding `### ` heading line and is preserved, so the inbound cross-references from `overview-and-orientation.md` and `host-interfaces-services.md` remain valid. After deletion, verify that no other spec file cites the deleted paragraph by anchor — only the section anchor `#file-extension-namespace` is referenced externally, and it is preserved.

The cleared-namespace observation that the deleted paragraph recorded remains encoded structurally in two stronger places: the `pi.looms` manifest-key list under `host-interfaces-services.md` and the `pi.registerFlag('loom', …)` registration step under `pi-integration-contract/registration-steps.md`. Implementers needing to know whether `.loom` is namespace-clear reach the answer through those normative anchors.

## Consequence

**Severity:** cosmetic

A reader encounters a paragraph that announces it carries no obligation while sitting inside a `###` subsection in a normative topic file, which trains them to scan for similar disclaimers elsewhere and weakens the default "everything under `spec_topics/` is normative unless marked otherwise" reading rule. No implementation behaviour or test is affected.

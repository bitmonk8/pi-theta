## Finding

`V7a` (Diagnostics primitive) owns the multi-error `Diagnostic[]` assembly — the `(file, line, col)` ordering/aggregation over an entry `.loom` and its transitively-imported `.warp` modules, with no fast-fail and no per-error loss. Its multi-error assembly Tests bullet (verbatim in both `V7a` and `V7a-T`) reads "a file with several parse errors (plus transitive `.warp` import errors) assembles into a single `Diagnostic[]` … ordered by `(file, line, col)` across an entry `.loom` and ≥2 transitively-imported `.warp` modules". That wording reads as real parsing of real files.

But `V7a`/`V7a-T` depend only on `V7a-T, H4a` — there is no lexer/parser leaf, no imports (`.warp`) leaf, and no static-resolution load-pass leaf in scope at this build position. The leaves that actually produce per-file parse/import diagnostics (`V1a` lexer, `V15c` imports, and the `V15a` static-resolution load pass that walks transitively and aggregates each visited file's diagnostics into the entry loom's drain) all land far downstream and many of them depend transitively on the diagnostics primitive itself. `V7a` is foundational and cannot parse anything.

The input source of the test is therefore unstated. If the assembler is exercised as a pure ordering/aggregation primitive over an in-test synthesised `Diagnostic[]` (the only interpretation consistent with `V7a`'s DAG position), the `V7a-T, H4a`-only Deps are correct but the prose misleads. If a reader takes the prose literally and tries to drive the test by parsing real files, the dependency set is under-declared and the leaf is not constructible at its position.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In the multi-error assembly Tests bullet of `docs/plan_topics/V7a-T-diagnostics-primitive.md` and `docs/plan_topics/V7a-diagnostics-primitive.md` (and the corresponding Adds sentence in `V7a`), state that the test's input is an in-test synthesised `Diagnostic[]` and that the assembler under test is the ordering/aggregation primitive, independent of the parse/load passes that produce diagnostics in production.

Concretely, replace the "a file with several parse errors (plus transitive `.warp` import errors) assembles into …" phrasing with wording to the effect of: the test constructs a synthesised `Diagnostic[]` whose entries carry `(file, line, col)` positions spanning an entry `.loom` and ≥2 transitively-imported `.warp` modules, and asserts the assembler produces a single `Diagnostic[]` ordered by `(file, line, col)` with no fast-fail and no per-error loss; the diagnostics are constructed in-test rather than produced by parsing real files. Keep the existing `implementation-notes.md — Static-resolution load pass` cross-reference as the source of the production ordering contract.

This keeps the `V7a-T, H4a` Deps correct (no parser/import/load-pass leaves are added). The downstream leaves that produce real diagnostics already exercise the ordering against actual parsed corpora at their own positions (`V15a` static-resolution load pass, `V15f` invoke parse/load diagnostics); `V7a` is the primitive they feed, not a parse driver.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A fresh implementer reading the multi-error bullet literally would either attempt to wire a parser/import/load-pass into `V7a` (none exists at its build position and none is in its Deps) or conclude `V7a`'s Deps are under-declared and stall. The DAG position constrains the correct reading to a synthesised-input sorter, so a working leaf is still reachable, but the ambiguity wastes the implementer's effort and invites a spurious dependency edit.

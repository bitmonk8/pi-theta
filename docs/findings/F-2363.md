## Problem

`real-host-smoke-gate.md` makes the manual real-host smoke run a required pre-merge gate on two triggers. Trigger (2) fires on "any merge whose diff touches the four fidelity-contract axes" and elaborates this as a commit editing "the production seam realising [axis X]", then concedes the condition "has no automatic diff detector". But the gate gives the contributor no axis-to-seam map: nothing tells them which leaves realise, e.g., the cancellation-propagation seam or the cancel-forward subscription. With no behavioural test and no seam list, two contributors facing the same diff can reach opposite conclusions about whether the smoke must run, and the gate degrades to ad-hoc judgement.

## Solution approach

In `real-host-smoke-gate.md`, rewrite trigger (2)'s "touches the four fidelity-contract axes" wording and its "no automatic diff detector" concession into a recognition criterion a contributor can apply to their own diff. Add the axis-to-seam map the note omits, resolving "the production seam realising [axis X]" to concrete leaves: axes (i)/(ii) and the `pi.on` cancel-forward subscription (iii) are realised by `V9c` (with the streaming-ordering surface of `V12a`), cancellation propagation (iv) by `V17a`, and the double's modelling of all four by `H4a`'s self-check. Correct the note's own seam imprecision — axis (iii) is `V9c`/PIC-18, not `V9b`.

## Solution constraints

- Out of scope: adding a CI hook or automatic diff detector — the gate remains a manual contributor obligation, auditable after the fact at `H6a`.
- Out of scope: criterion (e)'s permitted-code-list machinery (F-2340) and the `H6a` audit-record requirement (F-2346); this fix changes only trigger (2)'s recognition step.

## Problem

The **Failure** bullet of the closed terminal-outcomes trichotomy in `error-model.md` carves out `Err`s "not discarded via `let _ = …` (or the equivalent `void`-tail-expression form)", but the phrase **`void`-tail-expression form** is neither defined nor linked anywhere on the page or in the corpus — there is no anchor, glossary entry, or in-shard explanation. The concept is defined at QRY-20 in `query/query-escapes-stringification.md` and derives from the explicit-`void`-return rule in `functions.md` (`#empty-tail-body`), but a reader at this canonical Failure definition cannot determine which `Err`s the carve-out reaches without a cross-shard hunt. The term is also spelled inconsistently across the shards that use it (`void`-tail form vs. `void`-tail-expression form vs. void-tail-expression discard vs. void-tail-function form), compounding the lookup. Because Failure-vs-Success decides whether a final value flows at the `invoke` boundary, the ambiguity is observable to callers.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** `8168ea8` (2026-05-10, *pi-loom spec: resolve "T30 ceiling #4 model-driven row trichotomy carve-out"*)
**History:** `git log -G 'tail.expression form' -- spec/ spec_topics/` shows `8168ea8` is the earliest commit to add the phrase **`void`-tail-expression form** to the Failure bullet of the terminal-outcomes trichotomy. Before that commit the bullet read *"or exhausted a runtime-class hard ceiling (per … the load-time binder cap is excluded …)"* with no discard clause at all; `8168ea8` rewrote the bullet to add the `match`/`let _ =` carve-out and, in doing so, coined the parenthetical *"(or the equivalent `void`-tail-expression form)"* without an anchor or cross-reference. The underlying concept was already defined in `query.md` since `b3bc4ce` (2026-05-04, *"spec: pin failure-observability surface (Cluster A, Option C)"*) under the spelling **`void`-tail form**, so the defect is precisely a missing cross-shard link plus a spelling drift introduced when the term migrated into a new home — not a fundamental gap in the language semantics.

## Solution approach

Add a forward-link from the error-model.md Failure bullet's `void`-tail parenthetical to QRY-20 (`query/query-escapes-stringification.md#qry-20`, the discard-observability rule that already enumerates both discard shapes as one class) and to `functions.md#empty-tail-body` (the explicit-`void`-return rule the form derives from), and name the concept there by a single canonical spelling. Rename the term's existing call sites in `pi-integration-contract/runtime-event-channel.md` and at QRY-20 to that same spelling so it is greppable as one string.

## Problem

`V2a`'s **Adds** packs two independent grammars into one paired leaf: the type-expression parser (closed by `loom/parse/generic-arity-mismatch`, `loom/parse/void-in-non-return-position`, `loom/parse/result-in-schema-position`) plus `array<T>` type-sink resolution (closed by `loom/parse/array-no-common-type`), and the loom literal sublanguage (closed by `loom/parse/default-not-literal`, `loom/parse/tool-arg-not-literal`, `loom/parse/missing-object-field`). The literal sublanguage is normatively a standalone subset (`grammar.md` §"Loom literal sublanguage") admitted only in `params:` defaults and Pi-tool call arguments, and shares nothing with the type grammar at the test level. Because the two halves share no closing codes, a red assertion in either blocks completing the other, forcing a coarser red-green increment and a larger single-leaf diff than the work warrants. No downstream consumer couples them — `V6b` and `V14a` cite literal-sublanguage codes yet carry no `V2a` Deps edge.

The bundling has existed since the leaf was first authored; no later edit introduced or widened it.

## Solution approach

Split the loom literal sublanguage into its own paired leaf, leaving the type-expression parser and `array<T>` sink resolution on `V2a`. Strike the literal-sublanguage clause from `V2a`'s **Adds**, move the `loom/parse/default-not-literal` / `loom/parse/tool-arg-not-literal` / `loom/parse/missing-object-field` test bullet off `V2a-T` onto the new `-T` leaf, and retitle `V2a`/`V2a-T` to drop "and loom literal sublanguage". Author the new paired leaf spec'd against `grammar.md` (§Loom literal sublanguage) and `type-system.md`, link it into `docs/plan.md` under "V2 — Type system and values", add its ID to `coverage-matrix.md` `cka-1`'s closing-leaf cell, and add it to `H5b-warn-only-canary.md` Deps for transitive completeness.

## Solution constraints

- The type-expression parser and `array<T>` sink resolution (and `loom/parse/array-no-common-type`) stay on `V2a`; `V2b`, `V2c`, `V2d`, `V2e`, `V5a`, and `V11d` keep their `V2a` Deps edge unchanged.
- Allocate the new leaf ID per the plan's `V<n><letter>` scheme and the no-invented-ids rule; do not reuse a retired ID.

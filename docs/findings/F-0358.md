## Finding

`V5d` (schema-subset reject gate) and its paired `V5d-T` both list `V2d` (the canonical integer/number renderer) in their **Deps**, but nothing in `V5d` consumes it. `V5d`'s `Adds.`, `Tests.`, and `Ships when.` are scoped entirely to JSON-Schema-keyword rejection: the allowlist (reject-by-default) gate, `loom/parse/unsupported-feature` for out-of-subset constructs, and `loom/parse/result-in-schema-position`. `V5d`'s own `Adds.` explicitly hands the lowering pass and the canonical-hash recipe ‚Äî the only machinery that composes the number renderer ‚Äî to `V5f`. `V2d`'s real consumers are `V5f` (canonical-hash canonical form), `V11h` (argument echo), and `V11d` (system prompt); the coverage matrix maps `BNDR-4, BNDR-5 ‚Üí V2d` only.

The `V2d` edge is a residue from a leaf split. At plan inception `V5d` was `V5d-subset-lowering.md` ‚Äî a single leaf owning the reject gate, the lowering pass, and the canonical hash ‚Äî so depending on `V2d` was correct. The over-bundling refactor carved lowering and hashing out into the new `V5f` leaf and rewrote `V5d`'s `Adds.` accordingly, but did not prune `V2d` from either `V5d` or `V5d-T`'s `Deps.`. The edge now over-constrains build order with no behavioural need: it serialises `V5d` after `V2d` without any data or seam flowing between them. There is no dependency cycle; the only effect is lost parallelism plus a misleading signal that an implementer may try to "use."

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** introduced-by-later-edit (not present-since-inception)
**Introducing commit:** `cb8737a4d19856053efa4ac7acc6902409908904` ‚Äî "pi-loom plan: resolve \"Systemic leaf over-bundling across the plan corpus\"" (2026-06-12)
**History:** At inception (`c6a664e`) the leaf was `V5d-subset-lowering.md` ‚Äî "Schema-subset gate, lowering, and canonical hash" ‚Äî and owned the lowering pass and the canonical-hash recipe, both of which compose the `V2d` number renderer; the `V2d` dependency was justified there. Commit `cb8737a` split the lowering pass and canonical-hash recipe out into the new `V5f` leaf and rewrote `V5d`'s `Adds.` to delegate them to `V5f`, but left `V2d` in both `V5d`'s and `V5d-T`'s `Deps.` lines. The dependency became dead at exactly that commit; the later slug rename to `V5d-reject-gate.md` (`09293d2`) did not touch it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

Strike `V2d` from the **Deps** field of both leaves:

- `docs/plan_topics/V5d-reject-gate.md` ‚Äî change `**Deps.** `V5d-T`, `V5a`, `V5b`, `V2d`` to drop the trailing `V2d`, leaving `**Deps.** `V5d-T`, `V5a`, `V5b``.
- `docs/plan_topics/V5d-T-reject-gate.md` ‚Äî change `**Deps.** `V5a`, `V5b`, `V2d`` to drop the trailing `V2d`, leaving `**Deps.** `V5a`, `V5b``.

`V2d`'s lowering/hash consumer is `V5f`, which already declares `V2d` in its own Deps; the reject gate touches none of the renderer's surface, so no replacement edge or rationale note is warranted.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

Shipping unfixed serialises `V5d`/`V5d-T` after `V2d` with no consuming behaviour, removing parallelism in the dependency DAG. The dead edge also misleads: an implementer reading `V5d`'s `Deps` may hunt for the renderer's intended use or wire a spurious number-rendering call into the reject gate, where the spec contributes none. No shipped behaviour diverges and the leaf remains pickable.

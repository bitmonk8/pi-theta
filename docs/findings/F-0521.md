## Finding

`V9b` carries three independently-verifiable concerns in a single leaf:

1. **Registration steps 1ﻗ5** ﻗ `registerFlag('loom')` ﻗ walk, the pending-registration list, the `session_start` collision pass + `registerCommand`, the `session_shutdown` subscribe, and the watcher + `LoomRegistry` build-aside-publish swap with its step-5 structural-change `loom-system-note`. This alone spans four facet sub-labels (`cka-23.swap`, `cka-23.getcommands`, `cka-23.note-empty`, `cka-23.note-rename`).
2. **The test-only `ReloadFailureInjector` interface** ﻗ `V9b` is the single declaration site of the `injectReloadFailure` seam for the whole watcher-time reload failure-injection mechanism, wiring the registry-swap and `.loom`/`.warp` re-parse arms (the settings-re-merge arm is contributed by `V10d`, and `V4g` imports the interface for `ERR-7`).
3. **The model-reference-matcher production wiring** ﻗ at the load pass `V9b` constructs the concrete `findExactModelReferenceMatch` matcher from `ctx.modelRegistry.getAvailable()` and injects it into the injection seam `V6a` defines, so `V6a`'s `loom/load/model-unresolved` resolution binds that instance. This crosses into `V6a`'s frontmatter-parser domain and carries its own instance-identity test, and the same instance is also witnessed by `V11a`'s binder-model resolution.

The third concern is the costly one: it is a cross-module wiring point whose verification (single-source-of-construction / instance identity) lives partly in `V6a`'s and `V11a`'s domains, so a reviewer of `V9b` must hold the registration pipeline and the `V6a`/`V11a` model-matcher reconciliation in one pass. The leaf is the smallest-shippable-unit boundary the plan's atomicity discipline targets, and `V9b` exceeds it.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** multiple

### Option A ﻗ Extract the model-reference-matcher wiring into a thin new leaf

**Approach.** Move the model-reference-matcher production wiring (load-pass construction of `findExactModelReferenceMatch` from `ctx.modelRegistry.getAvailable()` + injection into `V6a`'s seam, plus the instance-identity test) out of `V9b` into a new `<new>` leaf.
**Plan edits.** Create the `<new>` leaf owning the wiring; its `Deps.` name `V6a` (the seam owner) and `V9b` (the load pass the wiring threads into). Strike the model-reference-matcher paragraph from `V9b`'s `Adds.`, the `model-reference-matcher production wiring` bullet from `V9b`/`V9b-T` `Tests.`, and the model-matcher clause from `V9b`'s `Ships when`. Retarget `V11a`'s Adds/Tests/Ships-when "the matcher instance `V9b` constructs" references and `V11a`/`V11a-T` `Deps.` to the `<new>` leaf.
**Spec edits.** None.
**Pros.** Preserves `V6a`'s parser purity (no host-context `ctx.modelRegistry` dependency, no forward `Deps` onto binder machinery); each leaf's domain stays single-concern.
**Cons.** Adds a small leaf that still depends on `V9b`'s load-pass internals; one more node in the DAG.
**Risks.** If the `<new>` leaf's instance-identity test re-cites a coverage-matrix-mapped REQ-ID's closing test it could trip the transitive-completeness `H5b`-Deps obligation; it should not, because `cka-37` closes via `V6a`'s `loom/load/model-unresolved`, so confirm no new closing-leaf mapping is created.

### Option B ﻗ Absorb the wiring into V6a

**Approach.** `V6a` itself constructs the concrete matcher at the load pass and injects it through the seam it already defines, removing the cross-module split.
**Plan edits.** Move the construction + injection prose and the instance-identity test from `V9b` to `V6a`/`V6a-T`; strike them from `V9b`/`V9b-T`; give `V6a` access to `ctx.modelRegistry`; retarget `V11a`'s references and `Deps.` to `V6a`.
**Spec edits.** None.
**Pros.** Removes the cross-module wiring entirely; the seam owner is also its producer; no new leaf.
**Cons.** Couples `V6a` (a frontmatter parser the plan deliberately keeps free of host context and forward binder `Deps`) to `ctx.modelRegistry`, breaking the parser-purity invariant `V6a`'s Adds states explicitly.
**Risks.** Introduces a host-context dependency into a leaf intended to read only its `Spec.` topics; may cascade into other `V6a` consumers.

### Recommendation

Take **Option A**. `V6a`'s `Adds.` documents a deliberate invariant ﻗ the parser "carries no forward `Deps` edge onto the downstream binder-model machinery" and the production wiring is "owned by `V9b`" ﻗ which Option B would breach by pulling `ctx.modelRegistry` into the parser. Option A keeps that invariant, removes the cross-module concern from `V9b`, and leaves `V9b` with the registration pipeline plus the `ReloadFailureInjector` seam (which is tightly coupled to the watcher/reload registration steps and stays). Edge cases the implementer must watch: retarget every "matcher instance `V9b` constructs" reference in `V11a`/`V11a-T` and the `V11a`/`V11a-T` `Deps.` to the new leaf; keep `cka-37`'s closing leaf as `V6a` (the wiring is not a coverage-matrix row of its own); and confirm the new leaf does not create a coverage-matrix closing-leaf mapping that would pull it into `H5b`'s `Deps.`.

## Consequence

**Severity:** advisory

A reviewer (and an implementer) of `V9b` must verify the registration pipeline and the cross-module model-matcher reconciliation together, widening the review surface for a leaf the plan intends to be the smallest shippable unit. The leaf remains pickable and an implementer can still produce a working result, so the defect degrades reviewability and increases rework blast radius (the `V6a`/`V11a` instance-identity contract is entangled with registration) rather than blocking work.

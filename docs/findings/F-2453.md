## Finding

The `loom/load/extension-bootstrap-failed` trigger row in `code-registry-load.md` states two whole-extension abort consequences side by side: a `pi.registerFlag` failure is "fatal to the whole extension (**steps 2–5 do not execute**)", and a `pi.on(...)` subscription failure is "likewise fatal to the whole extension (**the remaining steps do not execute**)". The `registerFlag` clause is determinate — `registerFlag` is registration step 1, so "steps 2–5" names every subsequent step. The `pi.on(...)` clause is not: `pi.on(...)` is the call used at **three distinct registration sites** — the step-1 `resources_discover` subscription, the step-3 `session_start` handler, and the step-4 `session_shutdown` handler (per `pi-integration-contract/registration-steps.md` and the `details.event ∈ {"resources_discover" | "session_start" | "session_shutdown"}` payload the same row carries). "The remaining steps" is therefore relative to an unstated current step, and the surrounding asymmetry invites a reader to transfer the sibling's "steps 2–5" range onto it — which would be wrong for a `session_start` (step 3) or `session_shutdown` (step 4) subscription failure, where steps 2/3 have already run.

The canonical owner page, `pi-integration-contract/extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md`, already resolves this precisely: a `pi.on(...)` failure "skips every subsequent `pi.register*` and `pi.on` call" — a step-position-relative rule keyed off the failing subscription's own site, not a fixed range. The diagnostic-registry row paraphrases that owner rule down to the bare "the remaining steps do not execute", dropping the "subsequent … after the failing subscription" qualifier that makes it determinate. The fix is to bring the row's `pi.on(...)` consequence into line with the owner page (e.g. "no subsequent `pi.register*` / `pi.on` call executes after the failing subscription"), so the registry row is self-determining without the reader having to follow the link and without the false parallel to the fixed "steps 2–5" range.

## Issue introduction

**Verdict:** single-commit
**Introducing commits:** 79ea4e8 — pi-loom spec: resolve "throw from pi.on subscription has no granularity rule" (2026-06-06, spec-plan)
**History:** Before `79ea4e8` the `extension-bootstrap-failed` trigger row covered only `registerMessageRenderer` / `registerCommand` / `registerFlag`, with the single determinate fatal clause "(steps 2–5 do not execute)" for `registerFlag`. `79ea4e8` added the `pi.on(...)` subscription arm (and its `details.event` payload) to close the pi.on granularity gap, but phrased the new arm's consequence as the vague "the remaining steps do not execute" alongside the unchanged determinate `registerFlag` range — introducing the step-relative ambiguity in this row even though the owner page added in the same area carries the precise "every subsequent `pi.register*` / `pi.on` call" rule.

## Solution Space

**Shape:** single

### Recommendation

In the `code-registry-load.md` `loom/load/extension-bootstrap-failed` trigger row, replace the `pi.on(...)` consequence "(the remaining steps do not execute)" with the determinate, step-position-relative phrasing already used by the owner page — e.g. "(no subsequent `pi.register*` / `pi.on` call executes after the failing subscription)". Leave the `registerFlag` "(steps 2–5 do not execute)" clause unchanged: `registerFlag` is step 1, so its fixed range is correct. The edit is purely to the registry row; the owner page `extension-bootstrap-and-per-loom.md` already states the rule correctly and needs no change. Watch that the wording stays keyed to "the failing subscription" rather than any fixed numeric range, so it remains correct for all three `pi.on` sites (steps 1/3/4) that the row's `details.event` enumerates.

## Consequence

**Severity:** correctness

A reader who consults the closed diagnostic-registry row standalone — its intended use — cannot determine which steps are skipped on a `pi.on(...)` failure, and the adjacent determinate "steps 2–5" clause actively suggests the wrong answer. An implementer who maps "the remaining steps" onto "steps 2–5" would, for a `session_start`/`session_shutdown` subscription throw, claim steps that already executed are skipped, diverging from the owner page and from two reasonable implementers' readings of the abort scope.
